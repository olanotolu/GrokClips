Sara Pennypacker

Sara Pennypacker (born December 9, 1951) is an American author of children's books. Her books, some of which have featured on the New York Times bestselling list, include Leeva at Last, Pax, Summer of the Gypsy Moths, the Clementine series, the Waylon series, and the Stuart books.

Sara Pennypacker Career

Career

Sara Pennypacker initially pursued a career as a watercolor artist. In the early 1990s, while homeschooling her three children, she began writing children's picture books, with her debut novel Dumbstruck published in 1994 under the pseudonym Sara Lewis. In 1997, she transitioned to writing full-time.[1] Pennypacker has authored over 20 children's books, primarily published by Hyperion Books for Children (now Balzer + Bray, an imprint of HarperCollins). Her works include the New York Times bestselling Clementine series (2006â€“2015), Pax (2016) and its sequel Pax, Journey Home (2021), as well as Here in the Real World (2020), Summer of the Gypsy Moths (2012), and Leeva at Last (2024).[2]

Critical reception

Pax was one of ten books on the longlist for the National Book Award for Young People's Literature in 2016.[3] It was on the New York Times bestseller list for 54 weeks, reaching number 1.[4][5] She received a Christopher Award for Clementine's Letter and the Golden Kite Award for Pierre in Love. She received a Boston Globeâ€“Horn Book Honor for Clementine in 2007.[6] Clementine,[7] Clementine and the Family Meeting,[8] Clementine's Letter,[9] The Talented Clementine,[10] Stuart Goes to School,[11] Pax,[12] Summer of the Gypsy Moths,[13] and The Mount Rushmore Calamity[14] received starred reviews from Kirkus Reviews. Summer of the Gypsy Moths,[15] Clementine,[16] Pax,[17] Dumbstruck[18] and Here in the Real World[19] received starred reviews from Publishers Weekly. Pax,[20] Meet the Dullards,[21] and Summer of the Gypsy Moths[22] received starred reviews from School Library Journal. Pax, Journey Home (2021) spent more than 40 weeks on the New York Times Children's Middle Grade Hardcover bestseller list as of September 2022.[23] Leeva at Last (2023) received starred reviews and positive critical acclaim.[24]
