Queen Creek, Arizona

Queen Creek is a town in Maricopa and Pinal counties, Arizona, United States. The population was 59,519 at the 2020 census, and an estimated 83,781 as of July 1, 2024.[1] It is a suburb of Phoenix, located in the far southeast area of the Phoenix Metropolitan Area.

Queen Creek

History

Early settlers arrived in the area in the late 19th century, establishing farms and ranches in the fertile valley below the San Tan Mountains along the Queen Creek wash. They grew crops such as citrus, cotton, pecans, and vegetables. Based on the post office form on file at the National Archives, the community was originally known as Rittenhouse, after C. H. Rittenhouse, who formed the Queen Creek Farms Company in 1919 and constructed a railroad stop near Rittenhouse and Ellsworth roads as a shipping point for produce. Residents would flag down trains for rides to Phoenix. As the railroad stop declined, the community adopted the name Queen Creek. The eponymous creek (typically more of a dry arroyo), originally called Picket Post Creek after a nearby oddly shaped mountain, was renamed for the Silver Queen Mine near Superior when the mine opened for production. The creek flows from the mountains through Queen Creek Canyon to the town area. By the time Arizona became a state in 1912, a true community had formed with traditions of neighborliness, street dances, and gatherings at the local general store, church, and post office. In the 1920s, an influx of Mexican immigrants arrived to work as miners in southern Arizona and pick cotton by hand in Queen Creek; a cotton gin was later introduced. During the 1940s, German prisoners of war from a local camp and Philippine immigrants joined the farm laborers. In 1946, early residents Raymond and Thora Schnepf hosted a notable 4th of July celebration featuring swimming, barbecue, and fireworks, reflecting the community's rural traditions. The area remained primarily agricultural, producing dairy, beef, carrots, cauliflower, broccoli, lettuce, potatoes, cotton, and grains. Queen Creek was incorporated as a town on September 5, 1989, to preserve its rural lifestyle while managing rapid growth.

Geography

The town of Queen Creek is primarily within Maricopa County, but the town limits extend into Pinal County on the eastern and southern borders. The town is bordered to the south and east in Pinal County by the town of San Tan Valley, Arizona (population 105,231 as of 2023).[2][3] According to the United States Census Bureau, the town of Queen Creek has a total area of 42.130 square miles (109.12 km2), of which 0.028 square miles (0.07 km2), or 0.07%, are water.

As of the 2020 census, there were 59,519 people, 17,965 households, and 15,549 families residing in the town. The population density was 1,476.3 inhabitants per square mile (570.0/km2). There were 19,628 housing units. The racial makeup of the town was 77.2% White, 3.3% African American, 0.9% Native American, 2.5% Asian, 0.2% Pacific Islander, 4.2% from some other races and 11.7% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 16.2% of the population.[5] 31.2% of residents were under the age of 18, 6.3% were under 5 years of age, and 12.9% were 65 and older. [4]

2010 census

As of the 2010 census, there were 26,361 people, 7,720 households, 6,557 families residing in the town. The population density was 940.1 inhabitants per square mile (363.0/km2). There were 8,557 housing units at an average density of 305.1 per square mile (117.8/km2). The racial makeup of the town was 83.6% White, 3.4% African American, 0.7% Native American, 2.8% Asian, 0.1% Pacific Islander, 5.8% from some other races and 3.6% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 17.3% of the population. There were 7,720 households, out of which 50.7% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 75.9% were married couples living together, 7.2% had a female householder with no husband present, and 11.8% were non-families. 8.0% of all households were made up of individuals, and 2.8% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 3.54 and the average family size was 3.77. In the town, the population age spread was: 35.4% under the age of 18, 8.7% from 18 to 24, 30.1% from 25 to 44, 20.9% from 45 to 64, and 4.8% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 31 years. For every 100 females, there were 104.8 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 105.3 males. The median income for a household in the town was $63,702, and the median income for a family was $65,679. Males had a median income of $45,000 versus $31,447 for females. The per capita income for the town was $21,592. About 6.0% of families and 9.2% of the population were below the poverty line, including 10.0% of those under age 18 and 6.5% of those age 65 or over.

Education

The town of Queen Creek is served by five public school districts, as well as public charter schools and private schools. The portion in Maricopa County is mostly in the Queen Creek Unified School District, with portions in the Higley Unified School District and the Chandler Unified School District. The portion in Pinal County is divided between the Florence Unified School District and the J. O. Combs Unified School District.

Transportation in Queen Creek

Transportation in Queen Creek primarily occurs by personal vehicle. Major roads include State Route 24 (also known as the Superstition Freeway), which provides east-west access to the Phoenix metropolitan area, and north-south arterials such as Ellsworth Road and Signal Butte Road.[6] The town is also planning extensions and improvements, including connections to the proposed North-South Corridor (SR 505).[7] Queen Creek is a member of Valley Metro.[8] As of 2025, Queen Creek is not served by fixed-route scheduled bus service.[9] However, Valley Metro provides ADA paratransit Dial-a-Ride service for eligible residents within Maricopa County portions of the town.[10] Valley Metro also offers vanpools for commuters in Queen Creek.[11]
