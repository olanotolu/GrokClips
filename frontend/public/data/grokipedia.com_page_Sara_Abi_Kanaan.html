Sara Abi Kanaan

Sara Abi Kanaan (born February 2, 1990) is a Lebanese actress.[1][2][3] She began her acting career at the age of 11 in the Lebanese series Bent El Hay. She won the Murex d'Or award twice: first in 2013 as Best Promising Actress for her roles in the Lebanese series Al Kinaa and Awwel Marra and the movie 24 Hours of Love, and second in 2015 as Best Supporting Actress for her roles in the pan-Arab series Laow, Ittiham, and Ishq El Nesaa.[4][5][6] She was nominated for Best Supporting Actress in 2017 for her role in the series Samra.[7] She studied medical laboratory sciences at the University of Balamand and pharmacy at the Lebanese American University.[1][2][3] In 2023, she starred in the film Broken Keys, which received international acclaim and a nomination for the FIFOG d'Or. In 2024, she married actor Wissam Fares.

Achievements and awards

  • 2013, won 'Best Promising Actress' award at the Murex d'Or.[55][56]
  • 2015, won 'Best Supporting Actress' award at Murex d'Or.[57]
  • 2017, nominated as 'Best Actress' award at Murex d'Or.[58][59]
  • 2017, was chosen by the iEmmys Academy as the youngest and only Lebanese juror for the semi-final rounds of the iEmmys awards that took place in Abu Dhabi.[60][61][62]
  • 2018, hosted the fourth "Asian World Film Festival" that took place in Los Angeles, California[63][64][65][66]
  • 2018, participated in the JCS International Young Creatives Award competition organized by the iEmmys Academy, representing Lebanon's first entry.[67][68][69]
  • 2023, nominated for FIFOG d'Or Best Feature Film (shared) for Broken Keys.[]
