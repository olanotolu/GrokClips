Al Del Greco

Albert Louis Del Greco (born March 2, 1962) is an American former professional football placekicker. After eight years as golf coach at Spain Park High School in Hoover, Alabama, Del Greco has been the head coach of the men's golf team at Samford University since May 2, 2014.

Albert Louis Del Greco

Early life

Albert Louis Del Greco was born on March 2, 1962, in Providence, Rhode Island, U.S. Del Greco moved with his family to Miami, Florida, at the age of four. He attended Coral Gables Senior High School in Coral Gables, Florida, where he played football and soccer as a kicker from 1977 to 1980.

College career

A four-year letterman at Auburn, Del Greco completed 110 out of 111 PATs in his college career. He also set the SEC record for field goal attempts in a single game and field goals made in a single game in a 1982 game versus Kentucky where he made six out of his seven attempts. He and Daniel Carlson tied for the most field goals in a single game for Auburn at 6 in 1982 and 2016, respectively; as of 2025, this is shared with Alex McPherson. His 236 career points place him ninth on the Auburn career scoring list as of 2025.

Del Greco began his NFL career as an undrafted free agent, signing with the Miami Dolphins in 1984 but spending time on their practice squad. He then played for the Green Bay Packers from 1984 to 1987, the St. Louis/Phoenix Cardinals from 1987 to 1990, and the Houston Oilers/Tennessee Oilers/Tennessee Titans from 1991 to 2000, where he spent the latter part of his 17-season career. Del Greco finished his 17 NFL seasons with 347 of 449 (77.3%) field goals and 543 of 551 (98.5%) extra points, for a total of 1,584 points. As of October 2025, he is ranked 25th on the NFL's list of all-time leading scorers. He was inducted into the Alabama Sports Hall of Fame in 2003.

Del Greco's Post-Playing Career

Post-playing career

At Spain Park High School in Hoover, Alabama, Del Greco was the boys golf coach and the football team's kicking coach. His son, Trey was the football team's place kicker prior to graduating after the 2007 season. Trey went on to play golf on an athletic scholarship at Vanderbilt University. In 2003, Del Greco became Birmingham Steeldogs' kickers coach. He co-hosted "The Opening Drive" on WJOX in Birmingham, Alabama, with Jay Barker and Tony Kurre. The program ended in early 2018. He is now the Men's Golf Coach at Samford University in Homewood, AL.

Celebrity golf

Del Greco has competed at the American Century Championship, an annual competition to determine the best golfers among American sports and entertainment celebrities. He won the tournament in 2000 with a final-round 7-under-par 65 and has a total of ten top ten finishes. The tournament, televised nationally in July, is played at Edgewood Tahoe Golf Course in Lake Tahoe, Nevada.
