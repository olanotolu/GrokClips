Sarah Hassan

Sarah Hassan, O.G.W. (born 5 September 1988) is a Kenyan actress, producer, director and former TV host. She is notable for her acting roles in television series such as Crime and Justice, Tahidi High, and Zora [1], as host of The Wedding Show, and films that include the Nigerian-Kenyan romantic comedy Plan B and Family Vacation.[2][3] Sarah Hassan also features in DStv's show, Zari, as Nina.[4] Her awards include Best Actress (2014) for the short film Now That You're Here; Best TV Host (2014) at the Kalasha Awards (Discovery+ 254); Best East African Journalist (2015) at the Swahili Fashion Week (Mashariki Mix and Maisha Max); Best Actress at the Kalasha Awards in 2019 (Plan B);[5] Best Lead Actress in a TV Drama (2021) at the Kalasha Awards (Crime and Justice);[6] and Entertainer of the Year (2025) at the Forbes Woman Africa Awards.[7] In 2022, alongside Willy Paul, she was a Brand Ambassador for OPPO's Reno 8 phone series.[8][9] As of April 2024, she is the Brand Ambassador for Dettol Skincare.[10]

Early life

Sarah Hassan was born on September 5, 1988, in Mombasa, Kenya, to parents Lucian and Hassan; she is their only child. [11] She started acting at the age of five.[6] For her secondary school education, she attended Machakos Girls' High School [12] and later pursued a Bachelor of Science in Actuarial Science at Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology.[6] She later studied Television and Film at the New York Film Academy, starting in 2016.[13]

Career

Hassan made her debut on TV in 2009 as Tanya, on the Kenyan high-school drama series Tahidi High and became one of the lead characters at the time. She also starred in several series such as Demigods, Saints, and Changes, and even went on to host the East African Dance show Sakata Mashariki. In 2013 she took over Noni Gathoni's role in The Wedding Show as the main host until December 2014. Sarah Hassan was a lead producer for 40 Sticks which won several awards.[13] Through her own Production Company, Alfajiri Productions, she has produced Plan B in 2019, Reflections - a short film and Just In Time.[14][13] In 2018, while in Los Angeles after her studies, she also wrote, directed and produced a film, The Company You Keep.[13] In August 2024, Netflix released Family Vacation, a Kenyan drama co-directed by Voline Ogutu and Edwin Kamau, in which Hassan stars as an internet influencer who tries to save her marriage and family.[2] In March 2025, she was honored with the Forbes Woman Africa Entertainer of the Year award.[15] She is currently based in Nairobi, Kenya. During the 61st Jamhuri Day celebrations in December 2024, Sarah Hassan received a Presidential Award for her contributions to the film industry. She was awarded the Order of the Grand Warrior (O.G.W.) of Kenya recognition. She has added the initials of the honor after her name.[16]

Personal life

Sarah Hassan married Martin Dale on 25 February 2017.[1] They have one son, born in March 2019.[11] The couple separated around 2024 after seven years of marriage.[17]
