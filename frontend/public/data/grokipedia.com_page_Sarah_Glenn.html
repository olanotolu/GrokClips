Sarah Glenn

Sarah Glenn (born 27 August 1999) is an English cricketer who plays for the England women's cricket team as a leg break bowler. She also plays for The Blaze, London Spirit and Brisbane Heat.[1] After making her England debut in 2019, she played in the 2020 ICC Women's T20 World Cup and broke into the top 10 of the ICC T20I Bowling Rankings later that year.[2] In September 2022, she rose to second in the Women's Twenty20 International (WT20I) bowling rankings.[3] Glenn was part of England's squads for the 2025 Women's Ashes and the 2025 ICC Women's Cricket World Cup, taking 5/32 in a warm-up match against Australia.[4]

Early life

Glenn was born on 27 August 1999 in Derby. She was home-schooled for much of her early education, and later attended Trent College, where she played boys' first XI cricket.[1] Glenn grew up playing cricket in her garden and at Denby Cricket Club, influenced by her family, including her father and uncle who played Premier League cricket there.[5] Her sister, Hannah, played cricket for Derbyshire between 2008 and 2011, whilst her uncle Michael played for Derbyshire CCC in 1975 and 1976.[6] Glenn played hockey at junior international level for England and has played for Belper Hockey Club.[5]

Domestic career

Glenn made her debut for Derbyshire in 2013, in a Women's County Championship match against Gloucestershire. She bowled four overs for 22 runs and did not bat.[7] Glenn became a regular for Derbyshire in the Women's Twenty20 Cup over the next few seasons, and was selected for the England Development Programme Under-15s competition in 2015, in which she took 7 wickets in two games, including taking 5/8 against Junior Rubies.[8][9] After further appearances for Derbyshire and the England Academy, Glenn was selected for the regional team Loughborough Lightning for the 2017 Women's Cricket Super League. She bowled five overs in the season and went for just 13 runs at an economy of 2.60, as well as taking 4 wickets at an average of 3.25.[10] She also scored 58 runs at an average of 19.33, opening the batting in two matches.[11] Glenn moved to Worcestershire ahead of the 2018 Women's Twenty20 Cup, taking three wickets in her first tournament with her new side.[12] Glenn appeared for Loughborough Lightning again in 2018, taking three wickets with an economy of 6.87.[13] 2019 was her breakthrough year, however, as she took 11 wickets at an average of 18.72, with an economy of 6.05: the joint-fifth best return for a spin bowler in the competition.[5][14] Her performances in the KSL lead to her international selection in December 2019.[5] Glenn played two matches for Central Sparks in the new Rachael Heyhoe Flint Trophy in 2020, taking one wicket.[15] She was signed by Perth Scorchers for the 2020â€“21 Women's Big Bash, in which she took 17 wickets with an economy of 6.25, ending the tournament as her side's leading wicket-taker, and joint-sixth across the tournament.[16] In 2021, she was drafted by Trent Rockets for the inaugural season of The Hundred.[17] In April 2022, she was bought by the Trent Rockets for the 2022 season of The Hundred.[18] In November 2022, it was announced that Glenn had re-joined Derbyshire from Worcestershire for the 2023 county season.[19] In February 2023, it was announced that Glenn had joined The Blaze from Central Sparks.[20] She played for The Blaze in the Rachael Heyhoe Flint Trophy and Charlotte Edwards Cup from 2023 to 2025. Glenn was retained by London Spirit for the 2023 season of The Hundred, having switched from Trent Rockets. She took 9 wickets in 7 matches that year. In the 2024 season, Glenn was instrumental in London Spirit's championship win, claiming 13 wickets â€“ the second-most in the competition â€“ including 2/17 in the final. She continued with London Spirit in 2025.[21][22] In September 2023, Glenn signed with Brisbane Heat as an international player for the 2023/24 Women's Big Bash League season. She played 19 matches, taking 23 wickets at an average of 18.52, with best figures of 4/18.[23][6] In September 2025, Glenn signed a two-year contract with Yorkshire ahead of the 2026 season, leaving The Blaze.[25]

International career

In November 2019, she was named in England's squads for their series against Pakistan in Malaysia.[26][27] She made her Women's One Day International (WODI) debut for England, against Pakistan, on 9 December 2019, in which she took two wickets, including the winning wicket.[28] She took 8 wickets in the three-game series, including 4/18 in the 3rd ODI.[29] She made her Women's Twenty20 International (WT20I) for England, also against Pakistan, on 17 December 2019, taking one wicket.[30] At the conclusion of the two series, she was singled out for special praise by her captain, Heather Knight, who said that "she definitely deserved to be player of the series in the ODIs."[31] Glenn was selected for the 2020 Australia women's Tri-Nation Series in January and February, and took five wickets in three matches, the joint-highest for England.[32] In January 2020, Glenn was named in England's squad for the 2020 ICC Women's T20 World Cup in Australia.[33] England reached the semi-finals of the tournament, with Glenn taking 6 wickets, with an economy of 4.25.[34] Glenn took 3â€“15 in England's group stage victory over Pakistan.[35] On 18 June 2020, she was named in a squad of 24 players to begin training ahead of international women's fixtures starting in England following the COVID-19 pandemic.[36][37] In September, she played against the West Indies in 5 T20Is, in which she named Player of the Series. Glenn took 7 wickets in the series, as well as contributing a "valuable" 26 off 19 balls with the bat in the 2nd T20I, as England won 5â€“0.[38][39] After the series, she moved up to ninth in the ICC T20I Bowling Rankings.[2] In 2021, Glenn was named in the squad for England's tour of New Zealand.[40] Glenn played all six matches on the tour, and was the joint-leading wicket-taker in the three match T20I series.[41] In June 2021, Glenn was named as in England's Test squad for their one-off match against India.[42][43] However, she was later released from the squad, allowing her to play in the 2021 Rachael Heyhoe Flint Trophy ahead of England's one-day matches.[44] In December 2021, Glenn was named in England's squad for their tour to Australia to contest the Women's Ashes.[45] In July 2022, she was named in England's team for the cricket tournament at the 2022 Commonwealth Games in Birmingham, England.[46] In September 2022, she moved to second position in the ICC women's T20I bowling rankings, her career best rank, after taking a four wicket haul against India.[3] She was named in the England squad for the 2024 ICC Women's T20 World Cup in the UAE.[47] Glenn played in four matches, taking 4 wickets at an average of 25.50, including 1/18 against South Africa and 1/22 against Bangladesh, as England reached the semi-finals.[48] She was also selected for the multi-format tour of South Africa in Novemberâ€“December 2024, consisting of three T20Is, three ODIs, and one Test.[49] In the T20I series, she took 6 wickets, highlighted by 4/20 in the second match, earning Player of the Match and helping England win the series 2â€“1. She claimed 5 wickets in the ODIs and played in the Test, contributing to England's series victory.[50][51] Glenn was named in the England squad for the 2024â€“25 Women's Ashes in Australia in Januaryâ€“February 2025.[52] She featured in the T20I and ODI legs, taking 8 wickets across the white-ball formats despite economical figures in some T20Is (including 0/39 and 0/40), and was dropped for one T20I. England retained the Ashes.[53] In Mayâ€“June 2025, during the home series against West Indies, she played limited white-ball matches (two fixtures), taking 4 wickets.[54] In September 2025, Glenn was selected for the 2025 ICC Women's ODI World Cup in India.[55] As of October 2025, she has taken 7 wickets in the tournament at an average of 22.00, including a five-wicket haul (5/32) against Australia in a group match.[56]
