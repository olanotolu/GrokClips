Sarah Bouhaddi Club Career

Club career

Bouhaddi began her youth career at SC Mouans-Sartoux (1994–1999), followed by FC Mougins (1999–2001) and OS Monaco (2001–2002). She made her senior debut with CNFE Clairefontaine in 2002, where she played until 2005, scoring one goal in 51 appearances.[5] She then moved to Toulouse FC for the 2005–06 season, making 22 appearances. In 2006, Bouhaddi joined FCF Juvisy, playing there until 2009 with 46 appearances. In 2009, Bouhaddi signed with Olympique Lyonnais, spending 13 seasons with the club until 2022. During this period, she made 199 appearances and scored one goal. In 2021, she was loaned to OL Reign in the American NWSL, where she played 19 matches. After leaving Lyon, Bouhaddi joined Paris Saint-Germain on 16 September 2022 on a one-year deal, making 15 appearances in the 2022–23 season.[6] On 30 January 2024, Bouhaddi joined Arsenal on a short-term contract, but did not make any appearances.[7] In September 2024, Bouhaddi signed with Al-Ula in the Saudi Women's Premier League, where she remains as of October 2025, having made 10 appearances in the 2024–25 season.[8]

International career

Bouhaddi is of Algerian descent.[9] Born in France, she made her debut for the France national team on 21 February 2004 against Scotland.[10] She represented her nation at the 2012 Summer Olympics, playing in all six of France's matches as they reached fourth place.[11] Bouhaddi also featured in the 2016 Olympics (four matches), the 2015 and 2019 FIFA Women's World Cups (six and five matches respectively), and the UEFA Women's Euro tournaments in 2005, 2009, 2013, and 2017.[10] With 149 caps, she is France's all-time most capped goalkeeper.[10] In 2020, she retired from international football amid reported conflicts with national team coach Corinne Diacre.[12]
