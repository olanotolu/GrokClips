Hozon Auto

Hozon New Energy Automobile Co., Ltd. is a Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer founded on October 16, 2014, and headquartered at 452 Daduhe Road, Putuo District, Shanghai.[1] The company specializes in the research, development, production, and sale of new energy vehicles, primarily affordable passenger electric cars marketed under the Neta brand, incorporating intelligent driving and connectivity features.[2][3] Initially focused on electric logistics vehicles, Hozon pivoted to consumer EVs with the launch of its first Neta model in 2018, achieving rapid domestic market penetration through low pricing and volume sales strategies.[4] Key models include the compact Neta Aya, mid-size Neta S sedan, and SUVs such as the Neta L and Neta GT, with production centered in facilities in Zhejiang province.[2] The firm expanded internationally to markets including Thailand, Indonesia, Mexico, and Laos, recording overseas sales exceeding 20,000 units in 2023, a 567% year-on-year increase.[5] Hozon experienced peak domestic sales of approximately 152,000 vehicles in 2022, positioning it among China's top new EV startups, but deliveries declined sharply thereafter amid fierce price competition and overcapacity in the sector.[6] In 2023, annual sales totaled 124,189 units with revenue of RMB 13.555 billion, yet the company faced mounting financial pressures, culminating in Zhejiang Hozon entering bankruptcy proceedings on June 20, 2025.[7][8] Reports have highlighted practices such as bulk insurance purchases to boost reported sales figures, contributing to scrutiny over the sustainability of its growth model.[6] Despite these challenges, founder Fang Yunzhou indicated in late 2024 prospects for recovery through new model launches and cost optimizations.[9]

History

Founding and Early Years (2014â€“2018)

Hozon New Energy Automobile Co., Ltd. (Hozon Auto) was established in October 2014 in Tongxiang City, Zhejiang Province, China, initially as Zhejiang Hezhong New Energy Automobile.[10] The company was founded by Fang Yunzhou, a former Chery Automobile executive who had spent 16 years there, including 13 focused on new energy vehicle (NEV) development, before departing in early 2014 to pursue independent ambitions in electric and smart vehicle technologies.[10] Fang, who conducted postdoctoral research at Tsinghua University under battery expert Ouyang Minggao, assembled a team of former Chery colleagues and collaborated with Tsinghua-affiliated research institutes to emphasize intelligent networking and connectivity solutions for the automotive sector.[10] Initial operations received backing from the Tongxiang local government, positioning Hozon as an innovative technology firm integrating hardware, software, and IoT applications for NEVs rather than immediate mass production.[10] During its formative period, Hozon prioritized research and development (R&D) in smart vehicle architectures, drawing on Fang's expertise in hybrid and electric powertrains. The company developed early prototypes, including the E-Take concept vehicle, and established a mature R&D framework that enabled advancements in autonomous driving, culminating in an L4-level test vehicle by early 2018.[10][11] In April 2018, Hozon expanded its global R&D footprint by opening a research center in Silicon Valley, California, aimed at pioneering "third-way" approaches to autonomous driving beyond traditional sensor-heavy or map-reliant methods.[11] Funding progressed with a Series A round in 2017, followed by a majority stake transfer in October of that year to Lhasa Zhixing Innovation Technology, a subsidiary of China Fortune Land Development, which provided capital for scaling R&D and factory preparations.[10] By mid-2018, Hozon shifted toward vehicle branding and production readiness, officially launching the Neta sub-brand in April to target affordable, networked electric vehicles.[10] In July 2018, the first Neta-branded vehicles rolled off the assembly line at its Tongxiang smart factory, marking the transition from pure R&D to initial manufacturing capabilities, though commercial sales began the following year.[10] Co-founder and CEO Zhang Yong played a key role in operational execution during this phase.[12] These efforts laid the groundwork for Hozon's focus on cost-effective EVs with advanced digital features, amid China's burgeoning NEV incentives and market growth.[10]

Growth Phase and Product Introductions (2019â€“2023)

In 2019, Hozon Auto began scaling production and sales of its inaugural model, the Neta N01 subcompact crossover SUV, which entered mass production in 2018 but drove initial market penetration with deliveries totaling approximately 10,000 units that year amid China's burgeoning EV incentives.[13] The company's focus shifted toward direct sales channels and technological refinements, laying groundwork for expansion as domestic EV demand surged. By 2020, Hozon reported deliveries of 15,296 vehicles, reflecting a modest yet foundational growth trajectory fueled by policy support and urban consumer adoption of affordable electric models.[14] The introduction of the Neta U compact SUV in March 2020 marked a pivotal product diversification, targeting family-oriented buyers with a longer-range battery electric powertrain and features like advanced driver assistance systems. Priced starting at around RMB 100,000 after subsidies, the Neta U contributed to Hozon's sales climbing to 69,674 units in 2021, a more than fourfold increase, as the firm expanded its dealer network and optimized supply chains amid competitive price wars.[15] This period saw Hozon achieve revenues of RMB 5.1 billion in 2021, underscoring operational maturation through vertical integration in battery and chassis technologies.[14] Further growth accelerated in 2022 with the launch of the Neta V compact crossover in late 2020â€”expanded with Pro variants by November 2021â€”offering competitive pricing under RMB 70,000 and a CLTC range up to 401 km, which propelled it to become a sales mainstay comprising over 65% of annual volume. The flagship Neta S sedan followed in July 2022, debuting with dimensions of 4,980 mm length and options for up to 715 km range in pure EV form, priced from RMB 189,800, aiming at premium segments with enhanced autonomous driving capabilities. Deliveries reached 152,073 units that year, a 118% year-over-year rise, as Hozon invested in R&D for its HPA platform to support modular scaling.[16][17][18] By early 2023, the Neta GT two-door sports coupe was unveiled in April, with deliveries commencing in the first half, featuring dual-motor AWD options delivering up to 340 kW and a 0-100 km/h acceleration under 4 seconds, positioned as an accessible performance EV starting at RMB 169,800 to attract younger demographics. This lineup expansion, coupled with overseas market scouting including Thailand factory announcements, positioned Hozon for projected 2023 deliveries nearing 300,000 units, though actual figures fell short at around 127,500 amid intensifying domestic competition. Revenues hit approximately $1.92 billion, reflecting peak growth before market saturation pressures emerged.[19][20][21]

Operational Decline and Restructuring Efforts (2024â€“2025)

In November 2024, Hozon Auto halted production at its primary manufacturing facility in Tongxiang, Zhejiang province, amid declining sales and cash flow shortages, leading to salary cuts for remaining employees.[22] [23] The company reportedly laid off up to 70% of its workforce during this period, exacerbating operational disruptions.[24] Co-founder and CEO Zhang Yong resigned on December 6, 2024, as part of broader cost-cutting measures, including unpaid supplier invoices and showroom closures in international markets like Thailand, where hundreds of staff were dismissed and 20 of 60 outlets shuttered.[25] [26] These issues stemmed from cumulative losses totaling 18.3 billion yuan (approximately US$2.6 billion) between 2021 and 2023, compounded by overexpansion, thin margins in China's competitive EV sector, and allegations of sales inflation via insurance schemes.[27] [28] By early 2025, Hozon began restructuring its sales channels, planning to phase out directly operated stores in favor of dealer networks to reduce overhead.[29] A failed 600 million USD funding round in March 2025 further strained liquidity, prompting supplier disputes and a liquidation lawsuit filed by Shanghai Yuxing Advertising in June 2025 over unpaid dues.[30] [31] On June 12, 2025, Hozon entered judicial reorganization proceedings following a creditor petition, aiming to avert full bankruptcy while maintaining production lines in standby mode.[32] [33] The company actively sought new investors, securing involvement from 47 parties by July 2025 to support debt resolution and operational revival.[34] Production resumed in August 2025, with full salary payments reinstated at the Tongxiang plant starting in July, signaling tentative stabilization.[35] However, challenges persisted, including a September 2025 service disconnection for Neta vehicles by data provider Lenovo Connect over unpaid fees, which administrators pledged to negotiate.[36] Hozon committed to sustaining after-sales support and parts availability during reorganization, particularly in export markets.[32]

Corporate Structure and Operations

Manufacturing and Supply Chain

Hozon Auto's primary manufacturing operations are based in China, with key facilities in Tongxiang, Zhejiang Province, and Nanning, Guangxi Zhuang Autonomous Region. The Tongxiang plant, serving as the main production hub, was planned to reach an annual capacity of 200,000 vehicles following upgrades completed by the end of 2024, though overall Chinese production capacity stood at approximately 150,000 units per year as of 2023 across two primary sites. The Nanning facility, located in the Lingli Industrial Park of Qingxiu District, functions primarily as an export-oriented base for right-hand-drive vehicles, with a capacity of up to 100,000 units annually. However, amid escalating financial pressures, Hozon halted domestic production in November 2024, with lines idling for extended periods despite remaining operational, reflecting broader operational challenges including supplier payment delays.[37][38][39] Overseas expansion included establishing production capabilities to support exports and right-hand-drive markets. In Thailand, Hozon's first foreign plant near Bangkok, developed in partnership with Bangchan General Assembly, commenced trial production in November 2023 and mass production in March 2024, targeting an annual output of 20,000 vehicles, primarily the Neta V model. Construction began on a third overseas facility in Malaysia in January 2024, while localized assembly in Indonesia was planned to start in the second quarter of 2024 to facilitate regional sales. These initiatives aimed to diversify production away from China but faced scalability limitations compared to domestic bases.[40][41][39] Hozon's supply chain relies heavily on domestic partners, particularly for batteries and core components, with Contemporary Amperex Technology Co. Limited (CATL) as its primary supplier under a 10-year strategic agreement signed in May 2024, designating CATL as the preferred provider for power batteries from 2025 to 2034 and incorporating advanced Shenxing LFP cells. Earlier collaborations included joint development of the CIIC skateboard chassis platform in 2023. Financial distress strained these relationships, prompting Hozon in March 2025 to propose converting 70% of supplier debts into equity in its parent company, a plan supported by CATL and 133 other vendors to enable production restarts amid bankruptcy proceedings filed in June 2025. This restructuring highlights vulnerabilities in the supply chain, exacerbated by unpaid obligations and the competitive pressures of China's EV sector.[42][43][44]

Funding and Investments

Hozon New Energy Automobile Co., Ltd., operating as Hozon Auto, has secured over 26.4 billion yuan (approximately US$3.74 billion) across 11 funding rounds from 19 investors since its founding in 2014.[45] Early investments focused on research and development for electric vehicle platforms, with later rounds supporting production scaling and international expansion amid intensifying competition in China's EV market.[3] Key funding milestones include a Series D round on October 27, 2021, raising approximately US$626 million to advance intelligent EV technologies.[46] In February 2022, Hozon closed an additional tranche exceeding 2 billion yuan (US$316 million) as part of its extended Series D, attracting backers including Tencent to bolster manufacturing capacity.[47] [48] A pre-IPO venture round on August 30, 2023, brought in US$960 million, aimed at domestic market dominance and overseas growth.[49]

Products and Technology

Passenger Vehicle Lineup

Hozon Auto markets its passenger vehicles under the Neta brand, focusing on battery electric vehicles (BEVs) and range-extended electric vehicles (REEVs) across sedans, SUVs, and MPVs. The lineup emphasizes affordable urban mobility with features like LFP batteries and ADAS integration, targeting price-sensitive consumers in China and emerging export markets. As of 2025, the portfolio includes six active models, spanning compact to mid-size segments, though production continuity has been affected by the company's financial challenges.[54][55] The Neta Aya, a compact crossover SUV launched in 2022, serves as an entry-level model with a 31.9 kWh LFP battery offering up to 301 km CLTC range in base variants. It features a single rear-mounted electric motor producing around 40-65 kW, positioning it for city commuting with dimensions of approximately 4,055 mm length and seating for five.[54] The Neta V, introduced in 2021 as a small five-seat MPV/SUV hybrid in form, provides flexible interior space with sliding doors in some trims and battery options from 31.73 kWh to 55.5 kWh, delivering 301-401 km CLTC range. Powered by motors up to 70 kW, it caters to family buyers with a wheelbase of 2,710 mm.[55][56] Neta S, a midsize sedan debuted in 2021 and updated with REEV options by 2024, combines a 1.5L range extender engine (70 kW) with electric motors up to 200 kW for total system outputs exceeding 230 kW. BEV versions use 65-77 kWh batteries for 500+ km range, available in sedan and wagon bodies with a 2,850 mm wheelbase.[57][56] The Neta GT, a midsize sports sedan launched in 2023, emphasizes performance with dual-motor all-wheel drive setups delivering up to 320 kW and 0-100 km/h acceleration under 4 seconds. It features a 77 kWh battery for around 600 km CLTC range and a low-slung coupe-like design measuring 4,810 mm long.[54][55] Neta X (formerly Neta U, refreshed in 2023-2025), a compact crossover SUV, offers 60-80 kWh LFP batteries for 400-600 km CLTC range and single/dual motors up to 200 kW. At 4,599 mm long with a 2,775 mm wheelbase, it includes advanced driver aids and competes in the family SUV segment.[58][59] The Neta L, a midsize SUV introduced in 2024, targets premium buyers with 60-90 kWh batteries, rear/all-wheel drive options up to 230 kW, and up to 700 km range in top trims. Measuring 4,770 mm long, it incorporates high-speed electric architecture and Level 2+ autonomy features.[60][61]

Key Technological Features

Hozon Auto's core technological foundation is the Shanhai platform, a scalable electric vehicle architecture introduced in 2023, which integrates cell-to-chassis (CTC) battery design to enhance structural efficiency, reduce weight, and optimize interior space utilization.[62] This platform supports modular vehicle development across sedans and SUVs, with features like the EEI central electrical/electronic architecture for streamlined wiring and the XPC supercomputing platform for high-performance data processing.[62] The architecture employs service-oriented architecture (SOA) principles, enabling over-the-air updates for software-defined functionalities such as infotainment and driver assistance systems.[63] In intelligent driving, Hozon integrates advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) via partnerships, including NVIDIA's DRIVE Orin system for AI-enabled perception, path planning, and automated maneuvers, deployable in models like the Neta S with capabilities for highway pilot and urban navigation upgradable remotely.[64] Additional collaborations, such as with Horizon Robotics' Journey 3 solution, provide computing power for features like adaptive cruise control, lane keeping, and collision avoidance in vehicles equipped with multiple cameras and radars.[65] The self-developed NETA PILOT 4.0 system, built on the Shanhai platform, combines these elements for Level 2+ autonomy, emphasizing sensor fusion and real-time decision-making.[66] Battery technology relies on ternary lithium-ion cells supplied through a 10-year agreement with CATL, offering capacities up to 91 kWh in models like the Neta S for ranges exceeding 600 km under CLTC standards, with fast-charging times around 0.58 hours from 30% to 80%.[67] [68] Hozon Intelligent Technology Strategy 2.0 prioritizes four attributesâ€”high efficiency, extended range, comfort, and reliabilityâ€”achieved via optimized powertrains and thermal management systems that support bidirectional charging and vehicle-to-grid integration in select configurations.[69] The Haozhi Skateboard Chassis further modularizes components like motors and suspensions for adaptability across vehicle sizes while maintaining low centers of gravity for stability.[70]

Concept and Future Developments

Hozon Auto has presented concept vehicles under the Neta brand to preview innovative electric vehicle designs, with the Eureka 03 serving as a prominent example. Unveiled at the 2020 Beijing Auto Show on September 29, the Eureka 03 concept features a sleek, Art Deco-inspired sedan body intended to rival mid-size electric sedans such as the Tesla Model 3 and Xpeng P7.[71][72] The design emphasizes aerodynamic efficiency and futuristic aesthetics, signaling Hozon's ambitions in the competitive EV sedan market.[73] Future developments at Hozon have centered on expanding the product lineup and advancing electrification technologies, though execution has been constrained by financial distress. In September 2023, the company announced plans to introduce three new models by 2025, alongside investments in fast-charging systems, high-efficiency motors, and additional EV architectures.[74][75] In August 2024, Hozon revealed the Neta S Hunting Edition, the first passenger vehicle to incorporate CATL's CIIC skateboard chassis, aiming to enhance modularity and battery integration for future platforms.[76] The HOZON Intelligent Strategy 2025 outlined upgrades to intelligent technology version 2.0, focusing on autonomous driving and connectivity enhancements.[77] These initiatives faced significant setbacks following operational halts in November 2024 and bankruptcy proceedings in 2025, which have cast doubt on the timely realization of announced projects.[78][79] Prior to these events, Hozon targeted 50% of sales from overseas markets by 2026, including a planned factory in Brazil for 2025 to support South American expansion.[80] However, the company's collapse has disrupted these strategies, contributing to broader consolidation in China's EV sector.[81]

Market Performance

Domestic Sales Trends

Hozon Auto, operating under the Neta brand, achieved rapid domestic sales growth in China during its initial years, rising from 10,006 units in 2019 to 147,838 units in 2022, driven by expanding model lineups and government incentives for new energy vehicles.[13] This expansion positioned Neta as a notable player in the budget EV segment, with sales benefiting from models like the Neta V and increasing consumer adoption of affordable electric crossovers.[82] Sales peaked in 2022 before declining sharply thereafter, reflecting intensified price competition, supply chain pressures, and market saturation in China's NEV sector. In 2023, domestic deliveries fell 16% year-over-year to 105,300 units, capturing just 1.2% of the overall NEV market share amid a broader industry slowdown for smaller brands.[7] The trend accelerated in 2024, with domestic sales estimated at 64,549 units (total sales of 87,948 minus 23,399 exports), a further contraction as Hozon struggled against dominant players like BYD and Tesla.[28] Early 2025 data underscores ongoing weakness, with reported domestic sales of 474 units in January alone, down 2.27% from the prior year, and combined January-February figures around 487 units, signaling diminished demand and operational challenges.[83][84] Monthly figures from Gasgoo indicate sporadic volumes, such as 9,403 units in July 2024, but overall trajectories point to Hozon's reduced competitiveness in the hyper-competitive domestic market.[85]

International Expansion and Exports

Hozon Auto began its international expansion under the Neta brand in 2023, focusing initially on Southeast Asia with entries into Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Myanmar, alongside initial forays into the Middle East (UAE and Jordan) and Latin America (Costa Rica).[39][87] The strategy emphasized building local manufacturing to circumvent tariffs and support regional exports, starting with a plant in Thailand that broke ground earlier and commenced trial production in November 2023, followed by mass production certification for 20,000 units annually by March 2024.[88][89] Similar facilities were planned or contracted in Indonesia and Malaysia to localize production and target ASEAN markets.[90] Overseas exports accelerated, reaching 6,970 units in the first half of 2023 and scaling to nearly 30,000 vehicles for the full year 2024, primarily comprising models like the Neta X and V-II shipped to Southeast Asia and Latin America.[39][91] This growth supported expansion of sales channels to 184 locations across key regions, including shipments of 1,000 Neta X units to Southeast Asia and Latin America by August 2024.[91][92] In Latin America, partnerships enabled market entry in Mexico starting in Q4 2024, with plans for over 10 stores in Brazil by Q1 2025; further Middle Eastern penetration targeted Jordan and UAE distribution.[93][94] Preparations for Europe included financing partnerships announced in October 2023, positioning Thailand as a potential export hub for the region, though actual sales volumes remained limited amid competitive pressures and domestic financial strains.[95] Hozon aimed for 50 global markets by end-2025 and overseas sales doubling to around 60,000 units, leveraging localized production to achieve 100,000 units in Southeast Asia alone by 2026, but these targets faced challenges from slowing domestic momentum and supply chain dependencies.[96][91][97]

Financial Status

Revenue, Losses, and Economic Pressures

Hozon Auto reported revenue of RMB 5.1 billion in 2021, increasing to RMB 13 billion in 2022 and RMB 13.6 billion in 2023, driven by sales of its Neta-branded electric vehicles totaling 124,189 units in the latter year.[14][7] However, revenue growth stalled amid declining vehicle deliveries, with sales dropping 40.2% year-over-year to 61,592 units in 2024 and further plummeting to just 487 units in January and February 2025 combined.[98][99] The company incurred substantial operating losses, accumulating RMB 18.3 billion from 2021 through 2023, including a RMB 6.7 billion net loss in 2022 and RMB 4.84 billion in 2023.[78][25] Losses continued into 2024 at RMB 4.5 billion, exacerbating total liabilities approaching RMB 10 billion, of which approximately 60% were owed to suppliers.[44] Unpaid supplier claims exceeded RMB 6 billion by mid-2025, contributing to production halts and creditor actions, including a liquidation petition filed by Shanghai Yuxing Advertising in June 2025.[35][31] Economic pressures stemmed primarily from China's oversaturated electric vehicle market, characterized by intense price competition and overcapacity, which drove average discounts to 16.8% by April 2025â€”more than double earlier levelsâ€”and eroded margins for smaller players like Hozon.[100] Heavy reliance on commercial sales, such as ride-hailing fleets that comprised 68% of 2023 volume, proved vulnerable as that segment contracted sharply post-2023.[78] Overexpansion and thin profitability, compounded by slowing domestic demand despite overall battery electric vehicle market growth, led to a 27% sales decline to around 100,000 units in 2024 and prompted Hozon to pursue exports targeting 50% of output overseas by 2026, though financial distress culminated in bankruptcy reorganization proceedings initiated in June 2025.[53][101][102]

Bankruptcy Proceedings and Creditor Claims

Zhejiang Hozon New Energy Automobile Co., Ltd., the parent company of the Neta electric vehicle brand, officially entered bankruptcy reorganization proceedings on June 20, 2025, following a petition filed by a creditor in May 2025 for unpaid debts.[8][103] The proceedings were initiated in response to the company's mounting financial pressures, including significant operational losses reported from 2021 to 2023 totaling 18.3 billion yuan.[104] An administration team, appointed by the Jiaxing Intermediate People's Court and managed by Zhejiang Haihao Law Firm, was established to oversee the restructuring process.[104] The first creditors' meeting convened on September 12, 2025, where 1,631 creditors submitted claims aggregating approximately 26.58 billion yuan (about USD 3.7 billion at prevailing exchange rates).[104][105] Of these, confirmed claims totaled 5.18 billion yuan, reflecting verified debts amid the company's acute liquidity crisis, with reported cash reserves as low as 15 million yuan (about USD 2.1 million) at the time.[106][107] Creditors included suppliers, financial institutions, and employees with unpaid wages, highlighting systemic payment delays that precipitated the filing.[106] As part of the reorganization, Hozon sought investor participation, attracting 47 potential investors by August 2025, which enabled a temporary resumption of production at its facilities after a pause.[35] By late September 2025, a single primary investor emerged in the restructuring talks, aiming to inject capital for operational continuity while addressing creditor repayments under court supervision.[107] The process emphasizes restructuring over liquidation, with priorities on verifying claims, settling employee obligations, and ensuring after-sales support for existing vehicles to mitigate broader market disruptions.[32]

Controversies and Criticisms

Alleged Sales Inflation Practices

In July 2025, a Reuters investigation revealed that Hozon Auto, operating under the Neta brand, along with Geely's Zeekr, had inflated sales figures through a scheme involving the pre-insurance of unsold vehicles.[28] Under China's regulatory framework, vehicles insured by dealers are recorded as sold, enabling manufacturers to recognize revenue prematurely even if the cars remain unsold and undistributed to end customers.[28] This practice allowed Hozon to meet aggressive internal sales targets amid intensifying domestic price competition in the electric vehicle sector.[108] Documents shared with dealers indicated that Hozon inflated sales for at least 64,719 Neta vehicles between January 2023 and March 2024, representing a significant portion of reported volumes during that period.[28] The scheme exploited a loophole where insurance policies were purchased en masse for inventory vehicles, artificially boosting quarterly and annual sales data submitted to industry associations and used for performance evaluations.[109] Hozon executives reportedly pressured dealerships to participate, with some records showing batches of dozens of vehicles insured simultaneously without corresponding retail deliveries.[110] Such tactics emerged in response to China's cutthroat EV market dynamics, where overcapacity and subsidy dependencies drove manufacturers to fabricate growth metrics for investor confidence and government incentives.[111] Independent analyses, including consumer complaints aggregated on platforms like China.com, corroborated similar padding across the industry, though Hozon's scale stood out due to its reliance on fleet sales to ride-hailing firms, which comprised up to 68% of its 2023 totals before market saturation reduced demand.[112] No official regulatory penalties had been imposed on Hozon as of October 2025, but the revelations contributed to broader scrutiny of sales authenticity in the sector.[113]

Insurance and After-Sales Service Disputes

In the context of Hozon Auto's financial deterioration leading to bankruptcy proceedings in June 2025, Neta vehicle owners in China reported sharp increases in insurance premiums or outright rejections by insurers, attributed to heightened perceived risks from the company's instability and declining resale values.[106] These issues exacerbated owner dissatisfaction, as insurers viewed Neta models as higher-risk due to potential service disruptions and parts shortages amid Hozon's cash constraints.[114] After-sales service disputes intensified following the bankruptcy filing, with customers facing prolonged delays in repairs, warranty fulfillments, and insurance claim processing, particularly in export markets like Thailand where operational dealerships dropped from 60 to around 40 by mid-2025.[79] Thai owners reported waits of up to 10 months for resolution of repair or claim issues, prompting widespread complaints on social media platforms about inadequate support and prompting some to seek unauthorized repair services.[26][115] In China, similar maintenance challenges arose as dealerships shuttered or scaled back operations, leaving owners reliant on informal repair networks and raising concerns over vehicle longevity for models like the Neta U and Neta V.[116] Hozon's administrators pledged negotiations with service providers to restore connectivity and parts supply during restructuring, but persistent disputes highlighted systemic vulnerabilities in after-sales infrastructure for financially strained EV startups, with over 1,600 creditorsâ€”including potentially service-related claimantsâ€”filing demands totaling billions in yuan.[36][104] Consumer advocacy groups noted that these service lapses eroded trust in Neta's brand, contributing to reduced demand and further financial pressure.[26]

Business Practices and Supplier Relations

Hozon Auto's supplier relations have been strained by persistent payment delays and financial pressures, prompting structured negotiations for debt resolution. In March 2025, the company convened a supplier conference to address outstanding obligations, proposing a restructuring plan that converted 70% of claims from 134 suppliersâ€”including battery manufacturer CATLâ€”into equity in its parent entity, Zhejiang Hozon New Energy Automobile Co., Ltd., to enable production restarts.[44][117][118] These efforts followed reports of supplier payment disputes dating back to 2024, exacerbated by declining domestic sales and intense competition in China's electric vehicle sector.[44] Upon entering bankruptcy proceedings in June 2025, triggered by creditor petitions over unpaid dues, Hozon disclosed confirmed claims totaling approximately RMB 5.1 billion (USD 718 million) from over 1,600 creditors, predominantly suppliers, with aggregate claimed debts reaching RMB 26.58 billion at the first creditors' meeting on September 12, 2025.[104][105][106] To preserve operational continuity for its Neta-branded vehicles, Hozon has engaged in ongoing discussions with key suppliers to sustain essential services, such as in-vehicle infotainment systems, amid risks of service disruptions.[104] Prior to these crises, Hozon maintained partnerships with established component providers like CATL and Gotion High-Tech for battery systems, integral to its vehicle manufacturing, though these ties have been tested by the firm's liquidity constraints.[119]
