Maximum Effort

Maximum Effort Productions, Inc.[2] is an American film production company and digital marketing agency founded by Canadian actor Ryan Reynolds alongside George Dewey in 2018. In March 2025, Reynolds and Dewey repurchased the marketing division from MNTN and merged it with the production arm.[3] The company's name is a reference to a catchphrase from Reynolds' 2016 film Deadpool.[4]

History

Reynolds and Dewey launched Maximum Effort in 2018, after having collaborated on the marketing campaign for the first two Deadpool films.[5][6] They worked on a series of successful low-budget ads to promote the 2016 film and its 2018 sequel, which at the time were the highest-grossing R-rated films ever.[4] Dewey, formerly senior vice president of digital marketing at 20th Century Fox and president of digital marketing at Annapurna Pictures, was named president of the company,[7] and Reynolds is creative director.[8] Maximum Effort's marketing arm was acquired by advertising software company MNTN in June 2021, with Maximum Effort Marketing keeping its name while operating as an agency within MNTN. Reynolds was named MNTN's chief creative officer, and Dewey MNTN's chief brand officer.[9][10] MNTN was founded in 2009 as Steelhouse, rebranding itself in 2021.[11] In September 2024, Bloomberg News reported that MNTN was planning to go public in 2025, with Morgan Stanley to organize its initial public offering.[12] MNTN completed its initial public offering in early 2025.[13] In MNTN's IPO filing (i.e. Form S-1) with the Securities and Exchange Commission, which was filed February 28, 2025, MNTN disclosed that it had agreed to divest "its interest in Maximum Effort Marketing to an affiliate of its original owner" and in connection with the transaction, MNTN provided a line of credit of up to $5.0 million, which was drawn upon.[14][15] The sale closed on April 1, 2025, recombining the marketing arm with Maximum Effort's film and television production operations as an independent entity under Reynolds and Dewey.[14][16] In connection with the divestment, MNTN and Maximum Effort entered into a creative services agreement on April 1, 2025, to continue their collaboration.[14][17]

Film and television production

Maximum Effort was one of the production companies on the 2018 film Deadpool 2, directed by David Leitch and starring Reynolds and Josh Brolin,[7] on 2021's Free Guy, directed by Shawn Levy and starring Reynolds and Jodie Comer,[18] on 2022's The Adam Project, directed by Levy and starring Reynolds, Mark Ruffalo, Jennifer Garner and Walker Scobell,[19] and on 2024's Deadpool & Wolverine, directed by Levy and starring Reynolds, Hugh Jackman, Morena Baccarin, Brianna Hildebrand and Garner.[20] Maximum Effort Productions projects include the musical Spirited, directed by Sean Anders and starring Reynolds and Will Ferrell.[21] The company also produces the FX docuseries Welcome to Wrexham.[22] In May 2021, Maximum Effort Productions closed a first-look development deal with Paramount Pictures for all their feature films, which was extended in February 2024 for an additional three years through December 2026.[6][23] In August 2022, it was announced Maximum Effort would launch a free ad-supported streaming TV channel, which launched in June 2023 on FuboTV and other platforms.[24][25] The first original program on the channel was entitled Bedtime Stories with Ryan and premiered June 20, 2023.[26] In June 2023, it was announced that Maximum Effort had made a deal with Welsh language channel S4C to show six hours of Welsh language programming per week which Reynolds has referred to as "Welsh Wednesdays".[27] The channel shut down in January 2025.[28] In February 2023, Maximum Effort announced the development of a 1.2 million-square-foot studio to be built in Markham, Ontario, Canada, just outside of Toronto.[29] In March 2025, the company signed a first-look deal with 20th Television.[30] In 2024, the company produced the family film IF directed by Shawn Levy.[31] Announced in 2025 were projects including the comedy Close Personal Friends starring Brie Larson for Amazon MGM Studios, the animated I Eat Poop: A Dung Beetle Story for Paramount Animation, and the National Geographic series Underdogs narrated by Ryan Reynolds.[32][33][34]

Marketing

Maximum Effort Marketing has created ads for Reynolds' brands Aviation Gin and Mint Mobile, [8] films such as Deadpool 2 [8] and Free Guy, [35] and for Match.com, [36] R.M. Williams, [37] One of the Aviation Gin spots went viral, as a spoof on a Peloton holiday commercial that had received significant backlash, with both ads starring the same actress, Monica Ruiz. [38][39] A spot for Mint Mobile featured Rick Moranis, in a rare public appearance for the actor, [38] and an advertising stunt for Aviation Gin and Laughing Man Coffee had a faux Twitter war between Reynolds and Hugh Jackman. [8][39] A video for Match.com went viral at the end of 2020, as it featured Satan dating the year 2020 and debuted a snippet of Taylor Swift's "Love Story (Taylor's Version)". [36] In December 2021, following the release of the Sex and the City revival series And Just Like That..., and the death of Mr. Big on a Peloton bike, shares in Peloton fell. Maximum Effort quickly devised an advertisement for Peloton featuring Chris Noth. Reynolds provided the voiceover promoting the health benefits that cycling can have, and noting that Chris Noth/Mr. Big was still alive. [40] In June 2021, Maximum Effort Marketing was acquired by adtech company MNTN, with Reynolds serving as chief creative officer. [9][10] In March 2025, Reynolds and co-founder George Dewey bought back the marketing arm, recombining it with the production side as an independent entity under Maximum Effort. [3] The company has gained recognition for its ability to produce ads quickly, an approach Reynolds calls "Fastvertising." [41] In 2025, it was named one of TIME's 100 Most Influential Companies for its innovative advertising strategies. [42] Recent campaigns include a September 2025 Visa ad twisting the slogan "Everywhere you want to be... and some places you really don't," [43] and a July 2025 Astronomer ad featuring Gwyneth Paltrow capitalizing on a viral kiss cam moment. [44]
