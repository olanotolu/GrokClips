Sarah Jayne Dunn

Sarah Jayne Dunn (born 25 September 1981) is an English actress and model primarily recognised for her portrayal of Mandy Richardson in the Channel 4 soap opera Hollyoaks.[1] Born in Leigh, Greater Manchester, she began her acting career with the role of Mandy in 1996, appearing in over 1,300 episodes across multiple stints until 2021, including continuous runs from 1996 to 2006 and 2017 to 2021, with sporadic returns in between.[2] Her performance in the series, which depicted Mandy's evolving storylines involving relationships, business ventures, and family dramas, established her as a staple of British television soap opera during that period.[1] Dunn expanded her credits with a minor role as Sal Maroni's mistress in the 2008 film The Dark Knight, directed by Christopher Nolan, and guest appearances in other programmes such as Brookside in 2003.[1] In late 2021, she departed Hollyoaks after producers terminated her contract due to her refusal to cease posting explicit content on OnlyFans, a subscription-based platform she joined for financial autonomy, arguing that such material aligned with prior promotional calendars she had done for the show targeting similar audiences.[3][4] Following her exit, Dunn has continued modelling and content creation, while diversifying into teaching pole dancing classes as of 2025, reflecting a shift toward entrepreneurial pursuits outside traditional acting roles.[5]

Early life and education

Childhood and family background

Sarah Jayne Dunn was born on 25 September 1981 in Leigh, Greater Manchester, England.[1][6] She grew up in the Leigh area and attended Lowton High School, where she demonstrated early aptitude for acting.[6] Dunn's initial foray into performance-related activities came through modelling, which she began at age 13.[7] This provided her first exposure to media and public-facing work prior to formal acting pursuits.[6]

Acting training

Sarah Jayne Dunn did not pursue formal acting training at a drama school. In a 2013 interview, she stated that she learned her craft "on the job," developing skills through practical experience rather than structured academic programs.[8] Her entry into professional acting occurred early, with her debut role as Mandy Richardson in the Channel 4 soap opera Hollyoaks at age 14 in September 1996, providing immediate immersion in set work, script rehearsal, and performance under production demands. This on-the-job apprenticeship allowed her to acquire foundational techniques such as line delivery, character interpretation, and collaboration with directors and co-stars without prior institutional preparation.[8]

Acting career

Hollyoaks roles

Sarah Jayne Dunn portrayed Mandy Richardson, a central figure in the Richardson family, from the soap opera's early years, debuting in the episode aired on 7 October 1996.[9] Mandy, introduced as the daughter of Diane and Dennis Richardson, quickly became involved in dramatic family dynamics, including a pivotal 1998 storyline depicting her sexual abuse by her father, which highlighted themes of incest and trauma within the household. This arc contributed to Hollyoaks' reputation for tackling controversial social issues in its narrative. Dunn's initial tenure spanned a decade, from 1996 to 2006, during which Mandy navigated multiple tumultuous relationships, including marriages to Luke Morganâ€”father of her daughter Ellaâ€”and Tony Hutchinson, marked by infidelity, family conflicts, and custody battles.[10] Brief returns followed in 2007â€“2008 and a regular stint from 2010 to 2011, featuring storylines such as Mandy's involvement in blackmail schemes and affairs, including a year-long liaison with Darren Osborne that strained alliances within the show's Chester community.[11] In July 2017, Dunn reprised the role for another extended period until late 2021, with Mandy's arcs focusing on protecting Ella amid threats like kidnapping attempts and legal disputes over paternity and custody, underscoring the character's enduring maternal instincts and resilience amid personal betrayals.[12] Over her combined appearances in 1,339 main episodes plus spin-offs, Mandy evolved from a vulnerable teen to a complex matriarch, anchoring family-centric plots that sustained viewer engagement through recurring themes of love, loss, and redemption.

Other television, film, and theatre

Dunn portrayed Sal Maroni's mistress in the 2008 superhero film The Dark Knight, directed by Christopher Nolan.[1] [13] She also appeared as Victoria in the 2012 short film After Death: A Steampunk Ghost Story.[14] [15] On television, she guest-starred as Metzler's personal assistant in the 2011 BBC One miniseries Exile.[1] [16] Dunn made appearances in the BBC soap Doctors, including as Renee Dooley and Adele Pearson in separate episodes.[17] In theatre, Dunn debuted on stage in the farce Boeing Boeing at the Comedy Theatre in London in 2007.[18] She starred as Sally Albright opposite Rupert Hill's Harry Burns in a 2010 touring production of When Harry Met Sally..., adapted from the film, which received positive reviews for the leads' chemistry.[19] [20] Dunn played Mrs. Darling in the pantomime Peter Pan: A Musical Adventure at The Lowry in Salford during the 2006 Christmas season.[21] She has also performed in productions of The Vagina Monologues.[22]

Awards and nominations

Sarah Jayne Dunn garnered recognition primarily from the British Soap Awards for her role as Mandy Richardson in Hollyoaks, an annual event honoring UK soap opera achievements through public and industry votes focused on dramatic impact, appeal, and storyline execution.[23] In 2002, she won the Most Spectacular Scene award, shared with co-star David Brown, for the high-stakes car crash involving their characters Adam and Mandy, praised for its intense portrayal of peril and emotional fallout amid competition from other soaps' explosive narratives.[23][9] Subsequent nominations highlighted her character's allure and departure arcs, though no further wins followed.

OnlyFans venture and professional controversies

Launch of OnlyFans account

Conflict with Hollyoaks producers

Post-Hollyoaks endeavors

Pole dancing and fitness pursuits

Following her departure from Hollyoaks, Sarah Jayne Dunn pursued pole dancing as a fitness discipline, achieving advanced-level proficiency and qualifying as an instructor by early 2025.[20] She Podcasting and media engagements

In March 2025, Dunn appeared on the Made by Mammas podcast, hosted by Zoe Hardman and Georgia Dayton, where she discussed raising her son without imposing gender stereotypes and emphasized allowing children to pursue interests freely.[56] She reflected on her Hollyoaks exit as a pivotal moment for personal autonomy, stating that prioritizing her family's needs over contractual obligations enabled her to "find herself" beyond scripted roles.[57] Dunn featured on Charlotte Dawson's Naughty Corner in September 2025, addressing parenting dynamics post-soap stardom and her OnlyFans reflections as a means of reclaiming narrative control after years of producers dictating her image.[58] She described the platform as empowering amid industry hypocrisy, contrasting it with the "sexualized" portrayals she endured on television.[59] Earlier appearances include the Confidence Clinic podcast, where Dunn elaborated on self-belief and using OnlyFans to assert independence following her dismissal.[60] In a 2023 YouTube interview with Matt Haycox, she outlined her post-Hollyoaks pivot to direct-to-audience content, citing creative frustrations and financial incentives as drivers for diversification.[61] Dunn's podcast discussions consistently frame her career shift as a rejection of external judgments, with recurring themes of motherhood's demands and the liberating aspects of unfiltered self-expression, often attributing her resilience to first-hand experiences rather than mediated advice.[62] These engagements have positioned her as a vocal commentator on work-life autonomy in entertainment.

Personal life

Relationships and marriage

Sarah Jayne Dunn met Jonathan Smith, a personal trainer, in 2013 during an audition for a theater adaptation of Birdsong.[22] The couple began dating shortly thereafter and became engaged in December 2016.[63] Dunn and Smith married on May 6, 2018, at the Holford Estate in Plumley, Cheshire, during the early May bank holiday weekend.[64] [65] In November 2019, the couple moved into a shared home in Cheshire, which they subsequently renovated.[66] [67] No prior public romantic relationships for Dunn have been documented.[68]

Motherhood and parenting perspectives

Sarah Jayne Dunn gave birth to her son Stanley on August 7, 2016.[69][70] The delivery was difficult, leading to prolonged physical recovery challenges, including frequent pain that persisted for over a year and impacted her body confidence.[71][72] She has publicly stated that postpartum issues, such as pelvic floor problems, delayed her ability to resume sexual intimacy for several months, contributing to emotional strain during early motherhood.[73][74] In discussions on parenting, Dunn has emphasized allowing children to pursue interests free from rigid gender stereotypes, particularly in raising boys, advocating for activities based on personal preference rather than societal expectations.[56][75] She views her role as a mother to a son as an opportunity to model female strength and independence, aiming to foster respect for women through direct example rather than prescriptive lessons. Dunn has also critiqued traditional gender roles in family responsibilities, noting taboos around mothers prioritizing career demands, such as missing school drop-offs, while still fulfilling parental duties.[76] Dunn integrates her commitment to fitness into her parenting philosophy, crediting the birth of her son with motivating a sustained focus on physical health as a means of modeling resilience and self-care for her family.[77] She describes breaking from conventional parental molds by pursuing empowering personal choices that enhance her well-being, which she believes benefits her child's upbringing by demonstrating autonomy and vitality.[78] This approach, she argues, counters post-birth physical and mental tolls by prioritizing recovery and strength-building activities.[79]
