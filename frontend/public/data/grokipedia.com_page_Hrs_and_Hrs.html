Hrs and Hrs

"Hrs and Hrs" (pronounced "hours and hours") is a song by American singer Muni Long from her EP Public Displays of Affection (2021), and later included on her debut studio album Public Displays of Affection: The Album (2022) as the third single. The song gained recognition through TikTok in late 2021,[1] and was sent to rhythmic contemporary radio on February 1, 2022, as the third single from Public Displays of Affection. "Hrs and Hrs" is an R&B song detailing her love for someone. It became Long's first song to chart on the Billboard Hot 100, peaking at number 16.[2] The song also topped the R&B Songs chart, becoming the first single by an independent female artist to top the chart.[3] The song's success earned her a record deal from Def Jam Recordings in March 2022.[4] "Hrs and Hrs" won the Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance, and received a nomination for Best R&B Song.[5]

Background

Muni Long came up with the song one night when she was washing dishes. To pass time, she searched for beats on YouTube and played them. She came across an R&B beat which she immediately started freestyling to and began writing the song to the beat. According to Long, it took about 20 minutes. The next day, she went to the recording studio with songwriter Kuk Harrell, and recorded the song there.[6][7] Initially, Long did not plan to include the song on her EP Public Displays of Affection , but it became a last-minute addition to the project.[6] Shortly after, actress Bre-Z (a friend of Long's) and her fiancÃ© Chris made a video of their relationship, accompanied by the song. Weeks later, in December 2021, Long posted the video on TikTok. It quickly went viral and the "Hrs and Hrs Challenge" was soon launched; the challenge was popular among the LGBT community, who thought the song meant "Hers and Hers".[6] In January 2022, the song entered multiple Billboard charts, and helped Long move to number one on the Billboard Emerging Artists chart.[7]

Composition

"Hrs and Hrs" is an R&B ballad containing "swoon-worthy, bass-thumping production" by Dylan Graham and Ralph Tiller.[7] It finds Muni Long singing about her willingness to do certain things with her loved one for hours.[8]

Critical reception

Elias Leight of Rolling Stone wrote, "There's not much that's dry and bland about Renea's 'Hrs and Hrs', a decadent R&B ballad â€” shot through with honeyed, high-wire runs, acrobatic ad-libs, and references to three different kinds of showers (champagne, thunder, traditional) â€” that went viral over the holidays."[1] Mike Wass of Variety described "Hrs and Hrs" as a "slinky, loved-up R&B ballad",[9] while in a review of Long's album, the publication called it a "haunting, quirky hit."[10]

Remix

A remix of the song featuring American singer August Alsina was released on December 30, 2021.[11] Another remix featuring American singer Usher was released on June 23, 2023.[12]

Commercial performance

"Hrs and Hrs" debuted at number 83 on the Billboard Hot 100. In the week ending January 6, the song reached number 34 on the Hot 100, with 11.5 million streams in the United States (up 89%) and 3,500 downloads sold (up 81%).[13] The song later peaked at number 16 on the Hot 100 for one week and spent 20 weeks on the chart.[2] It also reached number one on the Hot R&B Songs chart for three weeks, becoming the first song by an independent female artist to top the tally,[3] and peaked at number five on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart.[14] Internationally, the song charted in several countries, including number 57 on the Canadian Hot 100,[15] number 24 on the New Zealand Hot Singles chart,[16] and number 94 on the UK Singles Chart.[17] On Billboard's 2022 year-end charts, it ranked number 57 on the Hot 100,[18] number 14 on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart,[19] and number 12 on the Rhythmic Songs chart.[20] "Hrs and Hrs" has been certified 3Ã— Platinum by the RIAA in the United States for sales and streaming equivalent to three million units as of February 2025.[21] It also received a Platinum certification in Brazil,[22] Gold in New Zealand,[23] and Silver in the United Kingdom.[24]
