Montville, Queensland

Montville is a rural town and locality in the Sunshine Coast Region, Queensland, Australia. In the 2021 census, the locality of Montville had a population of 1,092 people.

Geography

Montville is in the Sunshine Coast hinterland, on the Blackall Range, around 400 metres (1,300 ft) above sea level in the town area. Maleny–Montville Road enters from the south, Montville–Mapleton Road exits to the north, and Woombye–Montville Road enters from the east.

History

Prior to European settlement, the Montville area was part of the traditional lands of the Gubbi Gubbi (also known as Kabi Kabi) people, the First Nations custodians of much of South East Queensland including the Sunshine Coast hinterland. The Gubbi Gubbi have inhabited the region for tens of thousands of years and used the Blackall Range for important cultural gatherings, such as festivals centered around the bunya nut.[2] European settlers arrived in the area in 1887. The town was originally called Razorback, but was renamed following a community meeting. The name Montville was proposed by storekeeper Henry Smith (who bought a selection of land there in 1893) after a suggestion by his mother, as it reminded her of their early years in Montville, Connecticut, USA. Alternatively it is a coined French word from mont meaning mountain and ville meaning village. The name was officially changed to Montville on 30 November 1897. Razorback Provisional School opened on 2 October 1896. In 1904, it was renamed Montville Provisional School. On 1 January 1909, it became Montville State School. From about 1900, the town became a popular mountain resort. Montville Post Office opened by February 1910 (a receiving office had been open from 1897). Montville Methodist Church was built in 1912. It could seat 110 people. It was built from timber at a cost of £230. In 1977 as part of the amalgamation that created the Uniting Church in Australia, it became Montville Uniting Church. St Mary's Anglican Church was built in 1914. The timber church could seat 80 people. The Montville branch of the Queensland Country Women's Association was formed in 1924. Montville was predominantly a logging then farming community (dairy, citrus, avocados, pineapples and macadamia nuts) until a tourism boom which commenced during the 1970s, taking advantage of the scenic views of the Sunshine Coast and the nearby rainforest walks.

Demographics of Montville

Demographics

At the 2011 census, Montville and the surrounding area had a population of 886 people. In the 2016 census, the locality of Montville had a population of 970 people. In the 2021 census, the locality of Montville had a population of 1,092 people.

Heritage listings

Montville has a number of heritage-listed sites, including:
  • Razorback Road and Main Street (Montville–Mapleton Road): Montville Memorial Precinct

Education

Montville State School is a government primary (Prep–6) school for boys and girls at Main Street (26°41′19″S 152°53′35″E / 26.6886°S 152.8930°E / -26.6886; 152.8930 (Montville State School)). In 2024, the school had an enrolment of 95 students with 11 teachers (7 full-time equivalent) and 15 non-teaching staff (5 full-time equivalent).[3] It includes a special education program. There are no secondary schools in Montville. The nearest government secondary schools are Maleny State High School in Maleny to the south, Burnside State High School in Burnside to the north, and Nambour State College in Nambour to the north-east.

Amenities

The town is well served with facilities, including a multi-denominational wedding chapel, a village green and community hall and a large sportsground featuring a football field, tennis courts and a wood working centre. St Mary's Anglican Church is at 9 Memorial Close (26°41′17″S 152°53′34″E / 26.6881°S 152.8928°E / -26.6881; 152.8928 (St Mary's Anglican Church)). Montville Uniting Church is at 152 Main Street (26°41′22″S 152°53′33″E / 26.6894°S 152.8926°E / -26.6894; 152.8926 (Montville Uniting Church)). The Sunshine Coast Regional Council operates a mobile library service which visits Main Street. There are a number of parks in the area:

Crime and safety

As of November 2022, Montville has a low non-violent crime rate of 1 incident per 550 residents, ranking in the bottom 1% for the Sunshine Coast region. The violent crime rate is higher at 1 incident per 78.5 residents, ranking in the bottom 11%. Overall crime in Montville decreased by 24% from 2023 to 2024.[4]

Attractions

The village is now a popular short break tourist destination for the people of Brisbane and is famous for its parks and walks including the Sunshine Coast Hinterland Great Walks. Popular attractions include galleries, restaurants & cafes, wineries, clock shop, hand-made chocolate shop & cafe, and craft and clothing boutiques. There are a number of parks in the area:
  • Baroon Pocket Fauna Sanctuary (26°40′38″S 152°52′03″E / 26.6772°S 152.8674°E / -26.6772; 152.8674 (Baroon Pocket Fauna Sanctuary))
  • Crawford & Foster Lookout (26°41′48″S 152°54′21″E / 26.6966°S 152.9059°E / -26.6966; 152.9059 (Crawford & Foster Lookout))
  • Razorback Lookout (George Carpenter Lookout) (26°41′18″S 152°53′37″E / 26.6884°S 152.8937°E / -26.6884; 152.8937 (Razorback Lookout (George Carpenter)))
There are two waterfalls:
  • Elston Falls (26°41′57″S 152°53′48″E / 26.6991°S 152.8966°E / -26.6991; 152.8966 (Elston Falls)), south of the town
  • Kondalilla Falls, formerly known as Bon Accord Falls, in within the Kondalilla National Park in the north-west of the locality (26°40′24″S 152°51′55″E / 26.6733°S 152.8652°E / -26.6733; 152.8652 (Kondalilla Falls))
There are many places to stay in and around Montville, with accommodation ranging from private cottages to resorts. Montville is becoming a very popular location for destination weddings at its many resorts, chapels, b&bs, wineries and restaurants. Nearby attractions include:
  • The Baroon Pocket Dam and Lake, which provides water for Caloundra and Maroochydore, as well as being a recreational facility, for boating, fishing and picnicking.
  • The world-renowned Australia Zoo which is a 20-minute drive down the range at nearby Beerwah.

In popular culture

Montville was the setting for Eleanor Dark's 1959 novel Lantana Lane.[5]
