Al Corley

Alford Corley is an American actor, singer, and producer best known as Steven Carrington in Dynasty and for his 1984 hit single "Square Rooms".

Career

In the late 1970s, he worked as a doorman at Studio 54 to pay for his studies at the Actors Studio. He would later appear in a VH1 Behind the Music special on Studio 54 to recount his experiences. Al Corley was the first actor to play Steven Carrington on the 1980s soap opera Dynasty. Corley subsequently appeared in numerous films and later transitioned into producing several projects. Al Corley left Dynasty at the end of the second season in 1982[3][4] after complaining publicly in Interview that "Steven doesn't have any fun... He doesn't laugh; he has no humor".[5] He also lamented Steven's "ever-shifting sexual preferences",[6] and stated that he wanted "to do other things".[4] The character was recast in 1983 with Jack Coleman; the change in appearance attributed to plastic surgery after an oil rig explosion.[3][4] Coleman remained on the show until 1988, but Corley returned to the role of Steven for the 1991 miniseries Dynasty: The Reunion when Coleman was unavailable due to scheduling conflicts.[6] Corley later appeared in the short-lived soap opera Bare Essence in 1983, playing Genie Francis' love interest,[7] and co-starred opposite his former Dynasty co-star Pamela Sue Martin in the film Torchlight (1985). In recent years, he has produced films including The Ice Road (2021) and The Mongoose (2024).[8] He was also known as a singer in the 1980s. His 1984 single "Square Rooms", from his debut album of the same name became a number one hit in France (in 1985), also reaching No. 6 in Switzerland, No. 12 in Italy (in 1985), No. 13 in Germany, No. 15 in Austria and No. 80 in the U.S. The same year, he released "Cold Dresses", which was also a big hit in France, reaching No. 5. His second album, Riot of Color was released in 1986, and a third album, Big Picture followed in 1988.

Personal life

He was married from 1989 to 1999 to German actress Jessika Cardinahl. [9] They have three children: Sophie Elena Corley, Ruby Cardinahl Corley, and Clyde Nikolai Corley. [10] Their eldest daughter, Sophie, has Wolf-Hirschhorn syndrome, a genetic disorder causing severe intellectual and physical disabilities. [11] Before his marriage, he had a brief romance with pop star Carly Simon.[12] It was Corley (with his back to the camera) who appeared with Simon on the cover art shot for her 1981 album Torch. [13] As of 2012, he resided in Topanga, California. [14]
