Sarah Lahbati

Sarah Lahbati (born 9 October 1993) is a Filipino actress of Filipino and Moroccan descent. She gained media attention as a finalist on the 5th season of reality show StarStruck, a Philippine talent show broadcast on GMA Network. Lahbati was declared StarStruck V's Ultimate Female Survivor after 15 weeks of the competition. Together with Steven Silva, they each won ₱1.5 million cash prize and an exclusive contract with GMA Network, with a minimum guarantee of ₱3.5 million.[2]

Early life and education

Lahbati was born in Geneva, Switzerland to a Filipina mother and a Moroccan father. She began her studies in Switzerland before moving to the Philippines, where she studied at the Immaculate Conception Academy in Cavite.

Career

Lahbati made her screen debut as Bong Revilla's daughter in Hokus Pokus.[3] After joining and winning the fifth season of hit reality show StarStruck , Lahbati topbilled GMA Network's drama series, Kokak (2011) and Makapiling Kang Muli (2012) along with her real-life partner Richard Gutierrez.[4][5][6] She also starred in different GMA shows such as Love Bug Presents: Exchange of Hearts (2011) , Little Star , Koreana and Time of My Life. She also performed in GMA Network's variety show Party Pilipinas and Sunday All Stars as a regular artist. In 2015, Lahbati transferred to the rival network, ABS-CBN, after her contract under GMA Artist Center expired. She officially joined Viva Artists Agency in June 2015.[7] Tagged as Dance Goddess, Lahbati is a regular performer on ABS-CBN's Sunday variety show, ASAP.[8][9][10] Lahbati was cast in the ABS-CBN soap opera, Written in Our Stars , starring Toni Gonzaga, Piolo Pascual, Jolina Magdangal and Sam Milby which started early 2017.[11] Lahbati appears in E! Philippines reality show It Takes Gutz To Be a Gutierrez.[12] Following Written in Our Stars, Lahbati took a hiatus from acting. She made a cameo appearance in ABS-CBN's The Iron Heart in 2023.[13] In 2024, she returned to lead the TV5 action-drama series Lumuhod Ka Sa Lupa.[14] As of October 2025, no major acting projects have been announced.

Personal life

On March 14, 2020, Lahbati married actor Richard Gutierrez in Taguig. They have two sons together, Zion (born April 29, 2013) and Kai (born March 21, 2018). Lahbati confirmed on March 1, 2024, that she and Gutierrez were separated but stated that they are coparenting their sons, Zion and Kai.[15] In January 2025, Gutierrez said that they were seeking an annulment.[1]

Lawsuit

In late 2012, Lahbati used her Twitter account to express her dissatisfaction with her network, GMA and expressed her desire to be released from her contract. On January 10, 2013, GMA Network and GMA Films president, Annette Gozon-Abrogar, sued Lahbati for libel,[16] and on the following day, January 11, 2013, the network filed a formal complaint against Sarah Lahbati for breach of contract. She was facing multiple charges.[17] In a related article published in PEP on 9 January 2013, Felipe Gozon, the head of GMA Network, released a statement indicating he did not guarantee protection that Sarah Lahbati said she was due.[18] A few days later, Lahbati left the Philippines for Switzerland "to study".[19] It was later revealed that Lahbati left for Switzerland to give birth to her son with Richard Gutierrez in May 2013 at the age of 19.[20] In July 2013, Lahbati returned to the Philippines to face the charges against her. [21] Upon her return, she filed countersuits against GMA executives, including Annette Gozon-Abrogar, for grave coercion and libel, though these were dismissed by prosecutors in 2013 and 2014. [22] In 2018, the Court of Appeals denied GMA Network's appeal, resulting in the dismissal of all cases against Lahbati. [23]
