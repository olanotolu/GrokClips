Sarah Reeves

Sarah Elizabeth Reeves (born April 29, 1989) is an American musician and singer who primarily plays a Christian pop and worship style of music. She has released five extended plays and six studio albums (some of which are independently released).

Early and personal life

Reeves was born Sarah Elizabeth Reeves[1] on April 29, 1989,[2][3] in Athens, Alabama.[4][5] She is the daughter of John Mark "Hershey" Reeves[6][7][8] and Tina Key Reeves[9][10], with an older brother[11] and younger sisters, including Mary Reeves[12], who is in the band Only in Stories.[13] Her father is a music producer and songwriter[7][14] who helped to establish Sound Cell Studios in Huntsville, Alabama, where Sarah recorded her first album.[15] Reeves says that when she was 15 years old, she was called by God to become a worship leader.[15] She was married to Philip Kothlow until their divorce in 2021.[16][17] In December of 2024 she married Bill McAdams Jr.[18]

Music career

Reeves signed her first record deal with Sparrow Records around 2007 and released her debut extended play, Sweet Sweet Sound, on April 21, 2009.[19] This EP marked her breakthrough on the Billboard charts, reaching No. 19 on the Christian Albums chart and No. 9 on the Heatseekers Albums chart.[20] The title track "Sweet Sweet Sound" peaked at No. 27 on the Christian Songs chart[21] and No. 24 on the Christian AC Songs chart.[22] Her follow-up EP, God of the Impossible, was released on April 20, 2010, also with Sparrow Records.[23][24] Reeves' first studio album, Broken Things, followed independently on July 26, 2011.[25][26] She released the independent Christmas EP in 2013[27] and her second studio album, Acoustic Worship Covers, Vol. 1, on March 24, 2014.[27][28] On May 14, 2021, the Belonging Co released "Eyes on You" featuring Reeves as a promotional single from See the Light.[29] In 2018, Reeves signed with Curb Records/Word and released the EP Easy Never Needed You followed by its full-length studio album version later that year.[30] Her third studio album, Life Love & Madness, arrived in 2020.[31] She issued the Christmas-themed EP More the Merrier in 2021 and her fourth studio album, Best Days, in 2023.[32] Recent singles such as "Get Back Your Fight" (2023), "More Than Enough" (2024), and "Cloud Nine" (2025) have charted on Billboard's Adult Contemporary and Adult Pop Airplay charts.
