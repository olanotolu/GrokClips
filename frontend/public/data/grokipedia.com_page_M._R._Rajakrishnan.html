M. R. Rajakrishnan

In this Indian name, the name Medayil Radhakrishnan is a patronymic, and the person should be referred to by the given name, Rajakrishnan. Medayil Radhakrishnan Rajakrishnan (born 25 May 1977) is an Indian sound designer. He won the National Film Award for Best Audiography in 2019 for the film Rangasthalam (2018), and a Special Mention at the 71st National Film Awards for re-recording mixing in Animal (2023). He won Kerala State Film Award for Best Sound Recordist in 2011 for the films Urumi, Chaappa Kurish and in 2012 for the film Manjadikuru. He has worked across Tamil, Hindi, Kannada, Telugu, Marathi and Malayalam films.

Early life

Rajakrishnan was born on 25 May 1977 in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, India.[1] His father, M. G. Radhakrishnan, was one of the most renowned music directors of the Malayalam film industry and a maestro of Carnatic Music.[1] His mother, Padmaja Radhakrishnan, had set her stage in the field of Art and Literature.[1] His grandparents, Malabar Gopalan Nair and Kamalakshi Amma were also musicians.[2] His uncle M. G. Sreekumar is a famous singer in Malayalam.[3] He practiced Carnatic music under his aunt, a renowned Carnatic vocalist, Dr. K. Omanakutty and also studied mridangam under the guidance of Mavelikara Krishnankutty Nair and Thripunithura Radhakrishnan. He completed his bachelor's degree in economics.[1] He later did his course in sound designing from Chethana Studio, Thrissur.

Career

Rajakrishnan began his career at age 23 in 2000, working as an assistant sound engineer under Deepan Chatterjee at Four Frames Sound Company in Chennai. He assisted on multiple films before becoming independent with Aparichithan (2005). He continues to serve as a sound engineer at Four Frames. He has worked with many directors in Malayalam cinema and has done sound designing and mixing for over 300 films in various languages, including Malayalam, Hindi, Tamil, Kannada, Marathi, Telugu, and Bengali.[1] Rajakrishnan has also composed music for the film Winter (2009, directed by Dipu Karunakaran), with his uncle M.G. Sreekumar providing vocals. He has done jingles for directors such as Priyadarshan and Major Ravi. His recent works include sound design for Animal (2023), which received a special mention for re-recording mixing at the 71st National Film Awards.[4]
