Sarah Drew

Sarah Drew (born October 1, 1980)[1][2] is an American actress and director.[3] She played Hannah Rogers in The WB family drama series Everwood (2004–2006) and Dr. April Kepner in the ABC medical drama series Grey's Anatomy (2009–18; 2021–22).

Early life

Drew was born in Charlottesville, Virginia, and raised in Stony Brook, New York, where she attended The Stony Brook School.[4] Her mother, Dr. Jeannie Drew, taught biology at The Brearley School, an independent private school for girls in Manhattan. Her father, the Rev. Charles Drew, was the senior pastor at Emmanuel Presbyterian Church in New York City. Her brother, Allen Drew, was a pastor at Mt. Airy Community Church in Philadelphia from 2009 to 2019 and is the director of an a cappella group at Germantown Friends School. She received a bachelor's degree in drama from the University of Virginia in 2002.[5] Her second cousin is actor Ben McKenzie.[6]

Career

In 1997, while in high school, Drew voiced Stacy Rowe on the animated series Daria. She also voiced that character in the Daria television films Is It Fall Yet? and Is It College Yet?. In 2001, she made her professional stage debut as Juliet in Romeo and Juliet at the McCarter Theatre in Princeton, New Jersey. She made her Broadway debut in 2003 in Vincent in Brixton, which later took her to London's West End. She made the move to television with a guest role in the series Wonderfalls, and was in the film Radio. She appeared as Katie Burrell, the daughter of a Japanese relocation camp sergeant, in the 2007 film American Pastime. From 2004 to 2006, Drew starred as Hannah Rogers in The WB drama series Everwood. She later guest-starred on Cold Case, Law & Order: Special Victims Unit, Medium, Castle, Glee, Privileged, Supernatural, and Private Practice.[7][8] She starred in the Hallmark Hall of Fame film Front of the Class (2008), and from 2008 to 2009, she had a recurring role as Kitty Romano in the AMC drama series Mad Men.[9] In 2014, she starred in the film Moms' Night Out.[10] Drew made her directorial debut in 2018 with the web series Grey's Anatomy: B-Team, directing multiple episodes; the series was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Short Form Comedy or Drama Series.[11]

Grey's Anatomy

In 2009, Drew was cast as Dr. April Kepner in the medical drama series Grey's Anatomy.[12][13][14] Drew was cast in late September and first appeared in the sixth season episode "Invasion" as one of the residents from Mercy West Hospital after its merger with Seattle Grace Hospital.[15] Drew was brought aboard Grey's Anatomy after former collaborations with series creator Shonda Rhimes;[16] she was featured as a guest in 2 episodes of Private Practice in 2008 and was a main cast member in Rhimes' 2009 television pilot Inside the Box, which ABC passed on.[16] In 2010, she was promoted to a series-regular for the seventh season.[14] In March 2018, it was announced that Drew, along with Jessica Capshaw, would be exiting the series. Showrunner Krista Vernoff stated that the decision was purely creative, not budgetary.[17][18] However, it was announced in March 2021 that Drew would be returning during season 17, and in April 2022 that Drew would be returning for the 400th episode of the 18th season, along with Jesse Williams.

2018–present

Shortly following the announcement of her Grey's Anatomy exit, Drew was cast as Detective Cagney in CBS' Cagney & Lacey reboot pilot.[19][20] CBS passed on the pilot in May 2018, deciding not to produce the series.[21] In July 2018, Drew took on the role of Lucille Ball, starring in the world premiere production of I Love Lucy: A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Sitcom, a behind-the-scenes stage comedy about I Love Lucy by Gregg Oppenheimer (son of series creator Jess Oppenheimer). Recorded before a live audience at the UCLA's James Bridges Theater, the L.A. Theatre Works production aired on public radio and has been released on Audio CD and as a downloadable MP3.[22][23] In 2019, Drew was once again cast as the title character in a CBS pilot, this time as Sarah Cooper in The Republic of Sarah, but the project was ultimately passed on by the network.[24][25] A different leading cast, led by Stella Baker, was chosen when the series was rebooted by The CW network for the 2020–21 season.[26] On October 30, 2020, Drew was cast in a recurring role as Cindy Turner (Jeanette's mother) in the Freeform (ABC Family) series Cruel Summer.[27] In September 2021, Drew was cast in Apple TV+ comedy series Amber Brown based on the bestselling books by Paula Danziger.[28] In 2022, Drew starred as Lizbeth Meredith in the Lifetime movie Stolen By Their Father as part of its "Ripped from the Headlines" feature films; it had Elizabeth Smart as an executive producer.[29] In 2023, Drew appeared in the film Birthright Outlaw.[30] In 2024, she starred as Amelia in the Hallmark Channel movie Branching Out and as Emily in the Hallmark+ mystery series Mistletoe Murders, which she also executive produced. That year, she wrote the Lifetime holiday movie A Carpenter Christmas Romance.[31] [32] [33] In 2025, Drew is set to star as Meg in the film Such a Pretty Girl.[34]

Personal life

Drew married Peter Lanfer, a lecturer at Dartmouth College, on June 17, 2002.[35] They have two children.[36] She is Christian. This matched her character's beliefs from the movie Mom's Night Out.[37]
