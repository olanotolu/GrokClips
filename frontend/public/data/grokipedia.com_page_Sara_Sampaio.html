Sara Sampaio

Sara Pinto Sampaio (born 21 July 1991) is a Portuguese model and actress recognized for her work in high-fashion campaigns and film roles.[1] She began her modeling career after winning the Cabelos Pantene contest in Portugal in 2007 at age 16, which led to representation by an agency and subsequent international bookings.[2] Sampaio achieved prominence as a Victoria's Secret Angel, walking in multiple fashion shows and embodying the brand's aesthetic of elaborate lingerie and themed wings.[3] She serves as a Giorgio Armani beauty ambassador and was the face of Michael Kors's "Gorgeous!" fragrance, while also becoming the first Portuguese model featured in Sports Illustrated Swimsuit.[3] In Portugal, she has won the Golden Globe for Best Female Model six times, highlighting her domestic influence in the industry.[3] Transitioning to acting, Sampaio has appeared in projects including the Showtime series Billions, the film Carga (2018), and At Midnight (2023); her most notable role to date is Eve Teschmacher in James Gunn's Superman (2025).[3]

Early Life

Family Background and Childhood

Sara Pinto Sampaio was born on July 21, 1991, in Porto, Portugal, a coastal city in the northern region of the country.[4] Her parents are Armando Sampaio, who works for a professional scuba diving company, and Cristina Sampaio, employed in an import/export business.[5] She has a younger brother named AndrÃ©.[6] Sampaio grew up on the outskirts of Porto, experiencing a typical childhood near the beach in a working environment shaped by her parents' professional commitments.[7] Her family emphasized education and practicality, with her parents insisting she pursue a university degree before fully committing to creative pursuits, reflecting a disciplined household approach to ambition and self-reliance common in Portuguese coastal communities.[8] From an early age, Sampaio displayed an interest in performance arts, describing herself as a "very dramatic child" with aspirations toward acting long before modeling entered her life, unaware even of the modeling profession's existence in her youth.[7] This early exposure to expressive interests, nurtured within Portugal's culturally rich northern setting, laid foundational discipline that influenced her later career trajectory without formal relocation or upheaval during her formative years.[9]

Entry into Modeling

At age 16, Sara Sampaio won the Cabelos Pantene 2007 modeling contest in Portugal, a nationwide competition focused on hair modeling that served as her entry point into the industry.[10] [11] The victory earned her a contract as the face of the Pantene hair care brand, including participation in a promotional commercial aired in Portugal.[12] Despite initial parental reservations due to her ongoing high school studies, Sampaio balanced early opportunities with education, demonstrating persistence in pursuing professional commitments.[10] Following the contest, Sampaio relocated to Lisbon to attend university, where she took on more regular modeling assignments while studying.[13] This move facilitated agency representation in Portugal and initial international exposure, including trips to London for castings with agencies such as Models 1.[14] By age 19, she had established a base in Paris for further development, transitioning to New York at age 20 in 2011 to pursue modeling full-time after signing with prominent agencies.[5] [13] Early experiences involved foundational photoshoots and castings that demanded physical preparation, such as maintaining strict fitness and grooming standards, alongside professional adaptability amid frequent rejections common in competitive agency scouting.[5] These steps highlighted the merit-driven nature of her progression, reliant on contest performance and consistent effort rather than established connections, as she navigated from local opportunities to global markets.[15]

Modeling Career

Breakthrough in Fashion

Sampaio's entry into international modeling accelerated around 2009-2010, following her 2007 win in the Cabelos Pantene contest at age 16, which secured her initial commercial work in Portugal.[11] [5] This local success facilitated contracts with agencies including Central Models in Lisbon and Elite Model Management in Paris, enabling her relocation to Paris by age 19 in 2010 and to New York the following year.[16] [17] In the pre-2010s fashion industry, characterized by fierce competition favoring versatile looks and reliability over celebrity ties, Sampaio's brunette features and disciplined approach distinguished her amid a field dominated by Eastern European and Scandinavian talents.[5] Her ascent gained momentum through editorial features, beginning with a spread in Vogue Portugal's November 2011 issue photographed by Luis Monteiro, which highlighted her poised versatility in a merit-based sector where editorial placements often preceded major bookings. By 2012, additional Vogue Portugal appearances solidified her domestic prominence, transitioning to broader European catalog and campaign work that underscored her adaptability to commercial demands.[18] From 2010 to 2015, Sampaio's portfolio expanded chronologically with increasing runway participationâ€”initially limited but growing to include high-profile shows by mid-decadeâ€”and frequent catalog appearances for mid-tier international brands, marking her shift from regional talent to globally booked model. This period reflected the era's emphasis on proven performers, as agencies prioritized bookers with consistent availability over transient hype, contributing to her recognition as Portugal's leading export in modeling by the early 2010s.[19] [20]

Victoria's Secret and Major Campaigns

Sara Sampaio debuted at the Victoria's Secret Fashion Show on November 13, 2013, walking in the Pink segment after multiple audition attempts.[21] In 2015, she was promoted to full Victoria's Secret Angel status, joining an elite group contracted to represent the brand's core lingerie and apparel lines.[22] She participated in the annual shows through 2018, appearing in segments featuring elaborate wings and fantasy bras that underscored the brand's focus on idealized, athletic femininity achieved via rigorous physical preparation.[23] To meet Victoria's Secret's standards, Sampaio followed a disciplined regimen including three weekly workouts and moderated intake of favorite foods like pizza, prioritizing lean muscle tone and endurance for runway demands. Her Angel tenure aligned with the brand's traditional emphasis on biological markers of attractivenessâ€”such as proportional figures and vitalityâ€”before its 2019-2020 pivot amid declining sales and cultural shifts toward broader body inclusivity, though sales data from peak VSFS years (e.g., 2015's $9.4 billion revenue) indicated strong consumer draw to these standards.[24] Outside Victoria's Secret, Sampaio featured in the 2014 Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue, marking her as the first Portuguese model in the publication's history, with a Jersey Shore photoshoot highlighting her in bikinis against coastal backdrops.[25] She repeated for the 2015 issue, reinforcing her appeal in swimwear emphasizing fitness and poise.[26] Sampaio also served as the face of Giorgio Armani Beauty, starring in campaigns for the SÃ¬ fragrance line from 2018 onward, including SÃ¬ Passione ads depicting confident, multifaceted femininity in urban settings.[27] These efforts contributed to her portfolio of luxury endorsements, where her disciplined aesthetic drove visibility for brands prioritizing aspirational, attainable beauty ideals.

Acting Career

Initial Roles and Transition

Following her peak modeling years in the mid-2010s, Sampaio began pursuing acting in earnest during her mid-20s, starting with classes fitted around her demanding runway and campaign schedule.[38] She expressed interest in auditions while based in New York, where she had relocated earlier in her career, though opportunities remained limited amid the competitive nature of securing roles without established industry connections.[5] These initial efforts included short films and minor television appearances starting around 2017, allowing her to accumulate credits while balancing the physical and travel rigors of modeling.[7] By the late 2010s, Sampaio relocated to Los Angeles to focus more intensively on acting, a shift prompted by longstanding personal aspirations deferred for professional modeling commitments.[8] This transition highlighted the hurdles of entering film and television as an outsider reliant on self-driven training and persistence rather than preferential pathways, with early progress slowed by the need to adapt modeling-honed poise to nuanced performance demands.[39] The COVID-19 pandemic further shaped her path, forcing virtual auditions and classes that tested remote adaptability but sustained momentum.[40] Amid growing fatigue from modeling's intensity, she deliberately scaled back runway work by the early 2020s, redirecting energy toward acting to avoid career stagnation in a field prone to typecasting models in superficial roles.[7] This prioritization evidenced a strategic pivot, as her agency bookings trended toward selective editorial and commercial projects over frequent fashion week appearances.[17]

Film and Television Appearances

Sampaio entered acting with supporting roles that drew on her modeling-honed poise and physical presence in ensemble settings. In 2017, she appeared in the Showtime series Billions as Prianca, a character in a single episode of the financial drama's second season, where her brief portrayal in elite social circles left a notable impression on audiences despite minimal dialogue.[41][3] The series itself achieved strong viewership, with its early seasons averaging around 1 million live viewers per episode.[1] That same year, Sampaio made her feature film debut in The Clapper, a comedy directed by Dito Montiel starring Ed Helms as a professional audience clapper, in which she contributed to the supporting cast amid scenes emphasizing visual and performative flair. The low-budget indie premiered at the Tribeca Film Festival on April 22, 2017, before a limited theatrical and VOD release on December 14, 2018, grossing modest returns under $100,000 domestically.[42] Subsequent credits included the 2018 Portuguese drama Carga, where she portrayed a central figure in a narrative exploring migration and exploitation, marking an early showcase of dramatic depth independent of her fashion persona. The film, directed by JosÃ© Neves, screened at festivals including the 2018 Fantasporto International Film Festival.[42] In 2020, Sampaio guest-starred in the Showtime series Homeland during its eighth and final season, appearing in an episode that utilized her composed screen presence within the espionage thriller's tense interpersonal dynamics; the season finale drew 1.3 million viewers. She also featured in the short film Sombra, a Portuguese production emphasizing atmospheric tension and subtle physicality.[42] By 2022, her role in the sci-fi thriller Wifelike as the android Meredith highlighted transferable skills from modeling, including emotive physicality in synthetic-human interactions opposite Jonathan Rhys Meyers and Elena Kampouris. Released directly to digital platforms on June 14, 2022, the film focused on themes of artificial companionship but garnered limited audience metrics amid niche distribution.[1][42]

Recent Projects and Developments

In 2025, Sara Sampaio portrayed Eve Teschmacher, Lex Luthor's associate and girlfriend, in James Gunn's Superman, marking her most prominent Hollywood role to date.[43] The film, released on July 11, featured Sampaio in scenes emphasizing the character's quirky, self-absorbed demeanor, drawing from the original 1978 portrayal while updating elements for contemporary audiences.[44] To immerse herself, Sampaio took approximately 1,000 selfies in character, using them to refine Teschmacher's selfie-obsessed personality and mannerisms during filming.[45] She also underwent a temporary blonde hair transformation to align with the role's visual requirements, which she described as aiding her disconnection from her natural look for deeper embodiment.[40] Sampaio has articulated a deliberate pivot from modeling toward acting, citing its greater creative fulfillment amid her evolving career priorities. In a July 2025 interview, she explained that while modeling provided early success, it ultimately felt insufficient for long-term satisfaction, with acting representing a longstanding aspiration predating her fashion breakthroughs.[39] This shift gained personal clarity following her ADHD diagnosis, which she discussed in mid-2025 podcasts as resolving prior struggles with focus and emotional regulation, particularly in modeling's repetitive demands versus acting's narrative depth.[46] She noted the diagnosis prompted therapy and lifestyle adjustments, enabling sustained commitment to roles like Teschmacher that demand immersive character work.[47] Bridging her modeling heritage and acting pursuits, Sampaio attended the Fashion Trust U.S. Awards on April 8, 2025, in West Hollywood, where she appeared in couture attire amid industry peers.[48] The event underscored her dual-industry presence post-Superman filming but pre-release, highlighting ongoing fashion engagements as she advances in film.[49]

Personal Life

Health Challenges and Management

In 2025, Sampaio disclosed her adult diagnosis of attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), which she described as providing clarity on longstanding challenges in focus and emotional regulation, ultimately influencing her transition from modeling to acting.[47] The diagnosis, shared during a July podcast appearance, highlighted retrospective symptoms including difficulty sustaining routines amid high-pressure schedules, with Sampaio noting it "made everything click" without prior awareness of the condition.[50] Post-diagnosis, she reported enhanced self-understanding and decision-making, attributing reduced panic attacks and meltdowns to therapeutic interventions that addressed ADHD-related executive function deficits. Sampaio has managed ADHD through structured therapy and intentional routine-building, emphasizing practical strategies like consistent scheduling to counteract impulsivity and sustain professional output in acting roles, such as her preparation for the 2025 film Superman.[51] These approaches yielded verifiable improvements in mental resilience, as evidenced by her completion of demanding projects without the prior "full-on meltdowns" she experienced pre-diagnosis.[52] She avoids pharmacological details in public accounts, prioritizing behavioral adjustments over unverified treatments. Earlier, Sampaio revealed struggles with trichotillomania, a body-focused repetitive behavior disorder involving compulsive hair-pulling, which manifested as gaps in her eyebrows and was publicly discussed in 2018.[53] Affecting approximately 4% of women, the condition intensified under modeling's aesthetic demands, prompting her to seek professional evaluation. Management included cognitive behavioral therapy variants like habit reversal training, alongside consultations for potential adjuncts such as N-acetylcysteine supplementation, though she stressed individualized medical guidance to tailor interventions effectively.[54] Amid fashion industry expectations for physique maintenance, Sampaio advocated disciplined nutritionâ€”centered on balanced macros and caloric controlâ€”and regular resistance training, crediting these for enabling sustained performance without endorsing extremes like restrictive dieting.[55] This regimen, honed over a decade, supported empirical outcomes like consistent bookings for Victoria's Secret campaigns, framing physical discipline as a causal factor in career longevity rather than a health detriment.[56]

Relationships and Privacy

Sara Sampaio has maintained a relatively private personal life, with limited public details on her romantic history emerging primarily from confirmed sightings and her own statements. As of October 2025, Sampaio and Nicholson have no children.[63]

Recognition and Awards

Modeling Honors

Sampaio was promoted to Victoria's Secret Angel status in April 2015, an elite designation for the lingerie brand's most prominent models, following her debut runway appearances in the annual fashion show from 2013 onward.[63] In the 2014 Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue, her rookie feature shoot in Chile earned her the fan-voted Rookie of the Year title with 32% of votes, making her the first Portuguese model to appear in the edition and receive this honor; she returned for a second feature in 2015 shot along Route 66.[26][64] Sampaio received the Best Female Model award at Portugal's Globos de Ouro (Golden Globes) in 2011, 2012, 2014, 2015, and 2016, reflecting consistent national recognition for her modeling achievements.[65] In 2019, she won the Globos de Ouro's Best Fashion Influence category. In September 2018, Giorgio Armani selected Sampaio as one of five global beauty ambassadors, alongside models like Barbara Palvin, to represent the brand's campaigns including the SÃ¬ Passione fragrance.[66]
