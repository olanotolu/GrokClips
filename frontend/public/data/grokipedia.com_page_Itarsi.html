Itarsi

Itarsi is a city and municipality in Narmadapuram in Madhya Pradesh, India.[1] Itarsi is a key hub for agricultural goods and Itarsi Junction railway station is the biggest railway junction in Madhya Pradesh. Rail services from all 4 major metropolitan cities of India namely Mumbai to Kolkata and Delhi to Chennai pass through Itarsi. Itarsi has large number of agro-based industries and warehouses. It has an Ordnance Factory.[2] The Bori Wildlife Sanctuary and Tawa Dam are nearby.

History

During the British colonial era, Itarsi gained prominence as a strategic railway junction connecting major cities in Central India. In 1870, the Great Indian Peninsula Railway (GIPR) extended its line over the Thull Ghat to reach Jabalpur from Itarsi, linking Bombay to Calcutta via the East Indian Railway. IRFCA The Bhopal-Itarsi line, including the Budni-Burkhera ghat section, opened in 1884 under the Begum of Bhopal. IRFCA Established by the British, the railway station played a crucial role in facilitating transportation across the country. Itarsi evolved into a significant transportation hub, serving as a key center for the movement of goods and passengers, which contributed to its economic growth. The city's strategic transportation links fostered industrial development, particularly in transportation and logistics.

Itarsi Tehsil Population Statistics

Itarsi is a sub district in the Narmadapuram district, in the state of Madhya Pradesh. The total population in Itarsi sub district is 240,719 as per the 2011 Census of India. Of this, 117,394 people live in the urban (towns and cities) area and 123,325 live in villages (rural areas) CityPopulation. There are 49,297 households in this sub district. VillageInfo There are 124,898 males (52%) and 115,821 females (48%).

Demographics

As of the 2011 Census of India, [4] Itarsi Urban Agglomeration had a population of 114,495, of which males constituted 52% (59,438) and females 48% (55,057), for a sex ratio of 926 females per 1,000 males. The average literacy rate was 88.9%, higher than the national average of 74.0%: male literacy was 93.3% and female literacy 84.2%. About 11% of the population (12,096 persons) was under 6 years of age. Scheduled Castes made up 11.4% and Scheduled Tribes 3.9% of the population. Hindus comprised approximately 90% of the population.[4] The 2021 census was postponed and has not been conducted as of 2025; the population is estimated at around 164,000.[4] Itarsi Urban Agglomeration consists of Itarsi (M), Pathrauta (part) (OG), Bhilakhedi (CT), and Meharagaon (CT).[4]

Administration

The city is a tehsil under Narmadapuram District. It is a part of Hoshangabad Vidhan Sabha (legislative assembly) and comes under Hoshangabad Lok Sabha (parliamentary) constituency. It has a municipality with 34 wards and is headed by a municipality chairperson.

Itarsi Diesel Loco Shed

Itarsi Diesel Loco Shed is an engine shed located in Itarsi. It comes under the Jabalpur railway division of the West Central Railway zone.[5]

History

As one of the oldest Diesel Loco Sheds, it was opened in the year 1964 with a total number of 40 locomotives.[6]

Operations
