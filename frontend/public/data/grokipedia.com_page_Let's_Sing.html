Let's Sing

Let's Sing is a music video game series developed by Voxler and published primarily by Deep Silver and its parent company PLAION (under the Ravenscourt label). In 2020, Koch Media acquired developer Voxler. Koch Media rebranded to PLAION in 2023.[1][2]

Gameplay

Let's Sing is a karaoke game. Music videos play on the screen, while the lyrics appear and light up as the player is singing them.[3] Singing notes at the right pitch scores points.[4] The series supports USB microphones, and smartphones can be used as microphones via a companion app in entries from Let's Sing 2018 onward.[3][5][6]

Let's Sing

The first game in the series was released on 19 October 2012 for the Wii.

Let's Sing and Dance

The second game in the series was released on 9 October 2013 for the Xbox 360.[14] It differs from the rest of the series since it uses the Kinect motion controller for dancing gameplay.[15] It features songs by Far East Movement, Lady Gaga, LMFAO, and Maroon 5. Brittany Vincent of Hardcore Gamer gave the game three out of ten and said: "[...] the game itself isnâ€™t terrible â€” itâ€™s just derivative and samey. Itâ€™s an accurate representation of how music gaming is viewed these days. Itâ€™s a shadow of its former self, with resources poured entirely into the songs that developers think will attract an audience."[16]

Let's Sing 2014

A sequel in the series was released on 8 November 2013, for the Wii. It features songs by James Arthur, Carly Rae Jepsen, Village People, and Simple Minds.[17]

Let's Sing Windows

The PC port of the original Let's Sing was released on 13 February 2014 for Windows. It was published by Plug in Digital.[18] It was originally announced for release on 12 December 2013.[19] It features songs by Lady Gaga, Maroon 5, Lana Del Rey, Flo Rida, Bruno Mars, Avril Lavigne, and LMFAO.[18][20]

Let's Sing 2015

Announced on 18 September 2014, for release on 24 October 2014, for the Wii. It features songs by Clean Bandit, Avicii, Robin Schulz, Mr. Probz, John Newman, Coldplay, Queen, and Village People.[21][22][23]

Let's Sing 2016

Known in Europe as Now That's What I Call Sing. Released on 23 October 2015 in Europe, 10 November 2015 in North America for the PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Wii, and Windows, developed by Voxler and published by Koch Media.[24][25] Announced on 9 July 2015.[26] It includes tracks by Jessie J, Ariana Grande, Nicki Minaj, Calvin Harris, and Demi Lovato. Sam Brooke of Push Square gave the game seven out of ten and summarized: "NOW That's What I Call Sing is a solid buy for anyone in need of a karaoke game on the PS4. Its wide variety of modes make it a hugely entertaining release to play with friends or by yourself, and the vast range of songs ensures that you'll always have something to sing."[3] Brooke also gave the same score for Let's Sing 2016 and said: "Let's Sing 2016 is practically the same game as Now That's What I Call Sing adapted for the North American audience, which means that it's a pretty solid singing game."[4]

Let's Sing 2017

Known in Europe as Now That's What I Call Sing 2. Released on 14 October 2016 for the Wii, PlayStation 4, and Xbox One.[27][28] It includes tracks by LMFAO, Justin Bieber, Jason Derulo, Meghan Trainor, John Newman, Calvin Harris, Coldplay, and Twenty One Pilots.[29] Sam Brooke of Push Square gave the game seven out of ten and summarized: "NOW That's What I Call Sing 2 isn't anything special, but it's a decent karaoke game that you'll have fun playing with friends. The tracklist may only appeal to a certain audience, but singing along to cheesy pop songs is part of the fun, and the variety of modes does just enough to prevent it from getting boring."[29]

Let's Sing 2018

Announced on 19 August 2017,[30] released on 23 October 2017 for the Wii and PlayStation 4.[31][6] Switch version was released on 24 November 2017.[32] The game has a new "Mix Tape" mode. It includes tracks by Coldplay, Alan Walker, Gnash, and Clean Bandit.[33] Michael Krosta of 4Players gave the game 70 out of 100 and said that it's " rudimentary karaoke package with a fairly recent song selection and an interesting option for four vocalists."[34][35]

Let's Sing 2019

Announced on 21 August 2018 at Gamescom 2018 for the Wii, PlayStation 4, Xbox One and Switch by Ravenscourt and Voxler.[36][37][38][39] It was released on 25 October 2018 in Europe and on 11 July 2019 in North America.[40][41] Francesca Sirtori of IGN gave the game 6.7 out of 10 and said: "Let's Sing 2019 features new mechanics and will let you sing many pop songs with your friends or other players online. Although the graphics and the sound is pretty good, we expected a more various playlist, along with the disappointment of the unavailability to catch screenshots."[42][43] Simone Pettine of Multiplayer.it gave the game 6.5 out of 10 and said: "Let's Sing 2019 is a nice karaoke for the whole family, but the contents are not so many and not too tidy."[44][45] Michael Krosta of 4Players gave the game 73 out of 100 and said: "The disputable song selection aside Letâ€™s Sing shows promise: With four possible vocalists, the original music videos in the background and asynchronous online sing-offs it offers a lot. Unfortunately the voice recognition leaves something to be desired."[46][47]

Announced on 13 August 2019, released on 25 October 2019 for the PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and Switch.[48][49] It includes tracks by Ava Max, Lewis Capaldi, Rita Ora, Robbie Williams, Spice Girls, and Queen.[50] Britt Roberts of Nintendo Life gave the game seven out of ten and summarized: "Letâ€™s Sing 2020 works as a karaoke video game and the point-focused levelling-up system and subsequent unlockables add a sense of satisfaction as you beat your score, unlock avatars and the use of songs in other play modes. It doesnâ€™t need to be more complicated than that."[56] David Chapman of Common Sense Media gave the game two stars out of five, criticizing its song selection as well as its visuals, noting that the music videos included with each song have video quality issues, and expressed that "the game's interface looks like its hokey, generic avatars got carried away with a PowerPoint presentation".[57]

Let's Sing Queen

Announced on 29 July 2020, released on 2 October 2020 for the PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and Switch.[58][59] It includes tracks by Queen like "Bicycle Race", "We Will Rock You", "Another One Bites the Dust", "I Want to Break Free", and "We Are the Champions". Let's Sing Queen received "generally positive" reviews, according to review aggregator Metacritic for the PlayStation 4 and Xbox One versions.[8][10] The Switch version received "mixed or average" reviews.[9] PJ O'Reilly of Nintendo Life summarized: "Let's Sing Queen is a straight-up, no-frills entry in the series that throws you into the legendary super groupâ€™s awesome back-catalogue of hits across the same selection of modes you'll be accustomed to from previous titles in the series. Let's Sing 2021

Announced on 21 August 2020, released on 12 November 2020 for the PlayStation 4, Xbox One, and Switch.[62][63] A new "Legends" mode was added to the game. It features singing challenges against computer controlled opponents.[64] The game includes tracks by Billie Eilish, Tones and I, Jonas Brothers, Panic! at the Disco, Ariana Grande, and Genesis.[65] Let's Sing 2021 received "mixed or average" reviews, according to review aggregator Metacritic.[11] Michael Krosta of 4Players said: "Technically Letâ€™s Sing is an entertaining karaoke game with interesting modes, but also well-known strengths and weaknesses from yesteryearsâ€™ iterations. And the song choice is rather disappointing."[66][11] Stefania Netti of Eurogamer gave a positive review and said: "Letâ€™s Sing 2021 is beautiful musical experience that makes the player enjoy and improve at the same time."[67][11]

Let's Sing 2022

Announced 23 August 2021, released on 23 November 2021.[68][69] It includes tracks from Lady Gaga, Shawn Mendes, Justin Bieber, Ariana Grande, The White Stripes, and Backstreet Boys.[68] Ollie Reynolds of Nintendo Life gave the game seven out of ten and summarized: "Letâ€™s Sing 2022 is a reasonably fun, reliable new entry to a series that continues to play it safe. Thereâ€™s a decent selection of songs on offer, but with a distinct lack of variety, the only other option is to purchase additional five-song packs, which feels a little bit icky."[5] Chad Sapieha of Common Sense Media gave the game three out of five stars.[70]

Let's Sing ABBA

Released on 4 October 2022.[71] It includes tracks from ABBA like "Gimme! Gimme! Gimme!", "Waterloo", "Dancing Queen", "Mamma Mia", and "I Still Have Faith in You".[71] Let's Sing ABBA received "mixed or average" reviews, according to review aggregator Metacritic.[12] Gavin Lane of Nintendo Life gave the game eight out of ten and summarized: "We'd wager even non-aficionados will be drawn in by the sheer strength of this most familiar and indestructible of pop music songbooks, and the variety of modes here, machine-tooled over many years of iteration and repetition for the long-running Let's Sing series, offer enough variety to engage just about anyone who's ever tapped their foot to any of these tracks. Developer Voxler took absolutely no chances here; Let's Sing Abba is exactly what you think it is. And for that, we were thankful."[72] Giulia Martino of Multiplayer.it gave the game 7.0 out of 10 and said "Letâ€™s Sing Abba is the same good old Let's Sing. If you love this iconic band's songs you'll love it, if you are looking for some fresh air or you hate hits like "Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! (A Man After Midnight)" and "Dancing Queen" it's better if you look somewhere else."[73]

Let's Sing 2023

Announced on 16 August 2022, released on 15 November 2022.[74][75] It includes tracks from Avril Lavigne, Ed Sheeran, Jason Derulo, Billie Eilish, Lil Nas X, Rita Ora, Charlie Puth. Dwayne Jenkins of Common Sense Media gave the game two out of five stars.[76]

Let's Sing 2024

Announced on 7 September 2023, released on 7 November 2023 for PlayStation 4/5, Xbox One/Series X|S, and Nintendo Switch.[77] It features tracks by artists such as Dua Lipa, Harry Styles, and David Guetta. The game introduces a career mode and supports up to four players. It received mixed reviews, with a Metacritic score of 70.[13]

Let's Sing 2025

Announced on 12 September 2024, released on 5 November 2024 for PlayStation 4/5, Xbox One/Series X|S, and Nintendo Switch.[78] It includes tracks by Billie Eilish, The Weeknd, and Sabrina Carpenter, along with a VIP pass for additional songs. Reception is mixed, focusing on fun party gameplay but limited base song count.[79]
