Sarah Hildebrandt

Sarah Ann Hildebrandt (/ˈhɪldəbrænt/ HIL -də-brant; born September 23, 1993)[1][2] is an American freestyle wrestler. She won the gold medal in the women's 50 kg event at the 2024 Summer Olympics in Paris, France, and a bronze at the 2020 Summer Olympics in Tokyo, Japan.[3] Hildebrandt is a four-time medalist at the World Wrestling Championships and a gold medalist at the Pan American Games. She is also a seven-time gold medalist at the Pan American Wrestling Championships. In February 2025, she was named assistant national women's coach by USA Wrestling.[4] In June 2025, she signed with the professional wrestling promotion Real American Freestyle.[5]

Career

In 2013, Hildebrandt represented the United States at the Summer Universiade in Kazan, Russia without winning a medal.[2] In 2016, she competed in the 55 kg event at the World Wrestling Championships in Budapest, Hungary without winning a medal.[6] She lost her first match against Mayu Mukaida of Japan and she entered the repechage where she lost her match against Ramóna Galambos of Hungary. In 2017, at the Golden Grand Prix Ivan Yarygin 2017 held in Krasnoyarsk, Russia, she won the silver medal in the women's 55 kg event. In 2018, Hildebrandt won one of the bronze medals in the women's 53 kg event at the Klippan Lady Open in Klippan, Sweden.[7] She also competed in the women's freestyle competition of the 2018 Wrestling World Cup. At the 2018 Pan American Wrestling Championships held in Lima, Peru, she won the gold medal in the women's 53 kg event.[8][9] In the final, she defeated Luisa Valverde of Ecuador.[8] At the 2018 World Wrestling Championships held in Budapest, Hungary, she won the silver medal in the women's 53 kg event.[10][11] In 2019, at the Golden Grand Prix Ivan Yarygin held in Krasnoyarsk, Russia, she won the gold medal in the women's 53 kg event.[12] At the 2019 Pan American Wrestling Championships held in Buenos Aires, Argentina, she won the gold medal in the same event. In June 2019, she won against Katherine Shai at the Final X: Lincoln event held in Lincoln, Nebraska. Later that year, she won the gold medal in the women's 53 kg event at the 2019 Pan American Games held in Lima, Peru.[13][14] In the final, she defeated Betzabeth Argüello of Venezuela.[13] At the 2019 World Wrestling Championships held in Nur-Sultan, Kazakhstan, she competed in the women's 53 kg event but did not advance to the medals.[15] In the same year, she also competed in the women's freestyle competition of the 2019 Wrestling World Cup. In 2021, Hildebrandt won the gold medal in the women's 50 kg event at the Grand Prix de France Henri Deglane 2021 held in Nice, France.[16] She also won the gold medal in this event at the Pan American Wrestling Championships held in Guatemala City, Guatemala.[17][18] At the 2020 Pan American Wrestling Olympic Qualification Tournament held in March 2020, she earned a quota place for the United States, and in April 2021 at the U.S. Team Trials, she secured her place to compete at the 2020 Summer Olympics in Tokyo, Japan.[19][20] She lost her semi-final in a close match after leading 7–2 mid-way through her bout and 7–6 with only 12 seconds to go, but her opponent Sun Yanan of China won a 4-point throw at the death and Hildebrandt lost.[3][21] In her bronze medal match she defeated Oksana Livach of Ukraine.[3] Two months after the Olympics, Hildebrandt won the silver medal in the women's 50 kg event at the 2021 World Wrestling Championships held in Oslo, Norway.[22][23] She won the gold medal in her event at the 2022 Pan American Wrestling Championships held in Acapulco, Mexico.[24][25] She also won the gold medal in her event at the 2022 Tunis Ranking Series event held in Tunis, Tunisia.[26] Hildebrandt won one of the bronze medals in the women's 50 kg event at the 2022 World Wrestling Championships held in Belgrade, Serbia.[27][28] She defeated Alina Vuc of Romania in her bronze medal match.[28] In 2023, Hildebrandt won the gold medal in her event at the Pan American Wrestling Championships held in Buenos Aires, Argentina.[29][30] She won one of the bronze medals in the women's 50 kg event at the 2023 World Wrestling Championships held in Belgrade, Serbia.[31] As a result, Hildebrandt earned a quota place for the United States for the 2024 Summer Olympics in Paris, France.[32] She qualified for the Olympics at the 2024 United States Olympic trials held in State College, Pennsylvania.[33] Hildebrandt won the gold medal in the women's 50 kg event at the 2024 Olympics.[34][35] She defeated Yusneylys Guzmán of Cuba in the gold medal match, after Guzmán advanced to the final when Vinesh Phogat of India, who had initially qualified, was disqualified for being above the stipulated weight.[35] In 2025, Hildebrandt won the women's 50 kg title at the US Nationals, defeating all opponents by a combined score of 38–0.[36] She was named the 2024 USA Wrestling Women's Freestyle Wrestler of the Year in May 2025.[37] In the same year, she won gold at the Pan American Wrestling Championships in the 50 kg event, marking her eighth continental title.[5] In February 2025, she was appointed assistant national women's coach for USA Wrestling while transitioning to a coaching role.[4] Hildebrandt signed with Real American Freestyle in June 2025 and competed in their inaugural event RAF 01 in August 2025 in Cleveland, Ohio, where she won her match 11–0.[5][38]

Personal life

Hildebrandt was born and raised in Granger, Indiana, to parents Chris and Nancy Hildebrandt. She has three siblings: an older brother Cory, a younger sister Amy, and a younger brother Drew, all of whom also participated in wrestling.[39][40] Hildebrandt graduated from King University in 2015.[41]
