Sara Doorsoun

Sara Doorsoun-Khajeh (Persian: سارا دورسون خواجه, Turkish: Sara Dursun ; born 17 November 1991) is a German professional footballer who plays as a defender for Angel City FC of the National Women's Soccer League. She represented the Germany women's national team from 2016 to 2025, retiring in June 2025.[1]

Early life

Doorsoun-Khajeh was born in Cologne to an Iranian father and a Turkish mother.[2] Doorsoun was introduced to football by her older brother and began playing at the age of three, initially joining boys' teams such as SV Wesseling, where she overcame initial skepticism from male teammates by demonstrating her skills.[] [3]

Club career

VfL Wolfsburg

Doorsoun joined VfL Wolfsburg from SGS Essen in April 2018. In her debut season, she won the 2018–19 Frauen-Bundesliga and the DFB-Pokal.[4] She won the Frauen-Bundesliga and DFB-Pokal again the following season with Wolfsburg.[5] In the 2020–21 season, she won the Frauen-Bundesliga with Wolfsburg. The next season, 2021–22, Wolfsburg won both the Frauen-Bundesliga and DFB-Pokal. Doorsoun featured for Wolfsburg during the 2019–20 UEFA Women's Champions League knockout phase, including playing in the 2020 UEFA Women's Champions League Final against Olympique Lyonnais.[6]

Eintracht Frankfurt

Doorsoun joined Eintracht Frankfurt from VfL Wolfsburg on 11 January 2022, signing a contract until 30 June 2023. On 25 April 2023, she extended her contract until 2025.[7] On 7 March 2025, she signed a one-year extension until 30 June 2026.[8] However, in July 2025, her contract was terminated early via transfer to Angel City FC.

Angel City

On 22 July 2025, Angel City FC of the National Women's Soccer League announced that the club had signed Doorsoun to a contract through 2026.[9] She made her NWSL debut on 7 September 2025 as a second-half substitute in a 3–1 loss to Orlando Pride.[10]

International career

Having represented Germany from the under-15 level up, Doorsoun made her debut for the senior team on 3 March 2016 in a 1–0 win over France at the SheBelieves Cup. She has been called up for the 2016, 2017, and 2018 editions of the SheBelieves Cup, as well as the UEFA Women's Euro 2017 where she played in their 2–0 win over Russia and quarter-final match against Denmark.[11][12] After providing two assists during Germany's World Cup qualification campaign, she was called into their squad for the 2019 FIFA Women's World Cup.[13][14] Doorsoun scored her first goal for the senior national team on 31 August 2019 in a UEFA Women's Euro 2022 qualifying match against Montenegro.[15] On 18 June 2022, she was named in Germany's final UEFA Women's Euro 2022 squad.[16] Doorsoun helped Germany reach the UEFA Women's Euro 2022 Final against England with a 2–1 win over France in the semi-final on 27 July 2022.[17] On 31 May 2023, Doorsoun was named in Germany's preliminary squad for the 2023 FIFA Women's World Cup,[18] and was named in their final 23-player squad on 8 July.[19] Doorsoun also featured in the 2023–24 UEFA Women's Nations League, helping Germany win bronze with a 2–0 victory over the Netherlands in the third-place match on 28 February 2024.[20] Doorsoun was named in Germany's squad for the 2024 Summer Olympics and contributed to their bronze medal win, defeating Spain 1–0 in the third-place match on 10 August 2024.[21][22] In June 2025, she announced her retirement from international football.[1]

Personal life

Doorsoun was born in Cologne to an Iranian father, Reza Parviz, and a Turkish mother. Her father immigrated to Germany at age 25 and passed away in 2024, shortly before the Paris Olympics; she has described him as a significant influence, calling her "his princess."[23] She has two older siblings. Doorsoun lives with her dog, Peanut, whom she considers a constant companion.[24] Doorsoun is openly lesbian. In 2021, she began dating Princess Charming winner Lou Schaaf.[25] The couple separated in late 2022, as Doorsoun confirmed in a 2023 television documentary.[26]
