Finnley Hugh Allen

Finnley Hugh Allen (born 22 April 1999) is a New Zealand International cricketer, who has played for the New Zealand cricket team since March 2021.[1] He plays domestic cricket for Auckland, having previously played for Wellington, and has played in a variety of T20 franchise leagues.

Domestic and franchise career

Allen made his Twenty20 debut for Auckland in the 2016–17 Super Smash on 3 January 2017.[2] Prior to his Twenty20 debut, he was named in New Zealand's squad for the 2016 Under-19 Cricket World Cup.[3] Allen made his List A debut for Auckland in the 2017–18 Ford Trophy on 17 February 2018.[4] He made his first-class debut for Auckland in the 2017–18 Plunket Shield season on 9 March 2018.[5] In September 2018, he was named in the Auckland Aces' squad for the 2018 Abu Dhabi T20 Trophy.[6] In November 2019, in a tour match for the New Zealand XI against England, Allen scored an unbeaten century.[7] In June 2020, Allen was offered a contract by Wellington ahead of the 2020–21 domestic cricket season,[8][9] coming into his own as the tournament leading run-scorer (512, SR 194) as Wellington defended their Super Smash title. Opening partner Devon Conway (455) was second.[10] In March 2021, he was signed by Royal Challengers Bangalore as Josh Philippe's replacement for the 2021 Indian Premier League.[11] In February 2022, Allen was bought by the Royal Challengers Bangalore in the auction for the 2022 Indian Premier League tournament.[12] In April 2022, he was signed by Yorkshire to play in the T20 Blast in England.[13] In June 2023, Allen returned to Auckland for the 2023–24 domestic season.[14] In July 2023, he joined the San Francisco Unicorns for the inaugural Major League Cricket season, scoring 381 runs.[15] In 2023, Allen played for Southern Brave in The Hundred, scoring 240 runs in nine innings as they won the title.[16] In August 2024, Allen signed a two-year deal with the Perth Scorchers in the Big Bash League ahead of the 2024-25 season.[17] He started his stint poorly, with a string of single-figure scores, before finding form mid-tournament with a couple of hard-hitting fifties.[18] In January 2025, Allen was selected as a platinum category player by Quetta Gladiators in the PSL draft and played 11 matches for them.[19] In June 2025 while playing for the San Francisco Unicorns of Major League Cricket, Allen hit a league-record 151 runs off 51 balls against the Washington Freedom, also breaking the MLC record for fastest century and a world T20 record for most sixes with 19.[20]

International career

In December 2017, Allen was named in New Zealand's squad for the 2018 Under-19 Cricket World Cup.[21] He scored the first century of the tournament, with 115 not out against the West Indies on the opening day of the competition.[22] In New Zealand's second game of the tournament, against Kenya, Allen scored a half-century off just 19 balls, the joint-second quickest in Under 19 ODI history.[23] He was the leading run-scorer for New Zealand in the tournament, with 338 runs.[24] In March 2021, Allen was named in New Zealand's Twenty20 International (T20I) squad for their series against Bangladesh.[25] He made his T20I debut for New Zealand on 28 March 2021, against Bangladesh,[26] scoring 71 in 29 balls in his 3rd game, opening with Martin Guptill.[27] In August 2021, Allen was named in New Zealand's One Day International (ODI) squad for their tour of Pakistan.[28] In June 2022, Allen was named in New Zealand's ODI squads for their tours of Ireland and Scotland.[29] He made his ODI debut on 10 July 2022, for New Zealand against Ireland.[30] On 27 July, in New Zealand's first match against Scotland, Allen scored his first century in T20I cricket.[31] In January 2024, Allen scored 137 from 62 balls against Pakistan to surpass Brendon McCullum's New Zealand T20I record.[32] With 16 sixes, Allen also drew level with Afghan Hazratullah Zazai to become the joint record holder for the most sixes scored by one player in an innings[33] He was declared player of the match and player of the series for his efforts.[34] In May 2024, he was named in New Zealand's squad for the 2024 ICC Men's T20 World Cup tournament.[35] In August 2024, Allen declined a New Zealand Cricket central contract in order to pursue international franchise opportunities.[36] Despite this, he remained available for international selection and featured in New Zealand's five-match T20I series against Pakistan in March 2025, where he scored a rapid 50 off 19 balls in the fourth match to earn the player of the match award.[37]
