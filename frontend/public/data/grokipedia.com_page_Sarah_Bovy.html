Sarah Bovy

Sarah Bovy (born 15 May 1989 in Liège) is a racing driver from Belgium. She competes with the [Iron Dames](Iron Dames) team in multiple series, including the [FIA World Endurance Championship](FIA World Endurance Championship) and the [24 Hours of Le Mans](24 Hours of Le Mans).

Biography

Following two years in karting, Bovy began her racing career as a Formula Renault Academy driver in [Formula Renault 1.6](Formula Renault 1.6) Belgium. Former [Formula One](Formula One) driver [Thierry Boutsen](Thierry Boutsen) took her under his wing as part of his team, and began an association with the Boutsen-Ginion squad that saw her race in GT, touring car and silhouette racing disciplines before taking a year's break in 2014 due to a lack of funding and undertook a Bachelor of Marketing course.[2] Bovy returned to the sport in 2015, gaining the funding to complete a full season in the Renault Sport Trophy. She yielded a podium in her home race at [Spa-Francorchamps](Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps) before falling back into a part-time endurance campaign in 2016.[3] The next two seasons would see her align with Lamborghini, contesting the 2017 Super Trofeo Europe Championship – finishing 14th in the Pro-Am class – and various endurance races in 2018, finishing second in class at the [2018 24 Hours of Spa](2018 24 Hours of Spa).[4][5] In 2019, Bovy applied for entry into the women's only [Formula Regional](Formula Regional) championship [W Series](W Series (championship)). She was accepted as one of the series' reserve drivers, and would be called upon on three occasions – the first time in her home race at [Zolder](Circuit Zolder) where she failed to start with technical issues; again in [Misano](2019 Misano W Series round) following injury for regular driver [Emma Kimiläinen](Emma Kimiläinen), finishing 12th; and also in the final round of the championship at [Brands Hatch](Brands Hatch), ending the race 19th after sustaining front-wing damage in the early laps. She scored no points and was the penultimate driver in the standings.[6]

Iron Dames

In 2021, Bovy joined the [Iron Dames](Iron Dames) to compete in the opening rounds of the [Michelin Le Mans Cup](2021 Le Mans Cup) alongside [Doriane Pin](Doriane Pin).[7] The same year, she would also drive in two rounds of the [GT World Challenge Europe Endurance Cup](2021 GT World Challenge Europe Endurance Cup), before being introduced into the team's [WEC](2021 FIA World Endurance Championship) roster for the final four rounds of the campaign, replacing [Manuela Gostner](Manuela Gostner).[8][9] The following year saw the Belgian team up with [Rahel Frey](Rahel Frey) and [Michelle Gatting](Michelle Gatting) to contest a full season in the LMGTE Am class of the [World Endurance Championship](2022 FIA World Endurance Championship), as well as racing in the [European Le Mans Series](2022 European Le Mans Series) and [GTWC Europe Endurance Cup](2022 GT World Challenge Europe Endurance Cup).[10][11] During the WEC campaign, Bovy became the first woman to claim a pole position in the series' history, qualifying first at both [Monza](2022 6 Hours of Monza) and [Bahrain](2022 8 Hours of Bahrain).[12][13] Additionally, podiums in the final three races of the season helped the #85 Dames crew to finish third in the teams' championship. Adding to Bovy's successful year, the Dames would win the Gold Cup at the [24 Hours of Spa](2022 24 Hours of Spa) as well as finishing third overall in the ELMS, as a Bovy pole position was converted to victory at the [Algarve](Algarve International Circuit).[14][15] Bovy returned to the Iron Dames roster for another [WEC](2023 FIA World Endurance Championship) season in 2023, this time switching from the [Ferrari 488 GTE Evo](Ferrari 488 GTE) to a [Porsche 911 RSR-19](Porsche 911 RSR-19).[16] The season opener at [Sebring](2023 1000 Miles of Sebring) began strongly, with Bovy taking pole position, though an off by Frey which damaged the car's diffuser and floor would curtail any hopes of a maiden victory.[17] Despite not scoring pole in [Portugal](2023 6 Hours of Portimão), a clean race enabled the #85 team to take their first podium of the year, finishing third.[18] Further top five finishes followed at [Spa](2023 6 Hours of Spa-Francorchamps) and [Le Mans](2023 24 Hours of Le Mans), where Bovy and her teammates narrowly missed out on a podium due to a slow final stop.[19] Bovy scored her second pole of the year in [Monza](2023 6 Hours of Monza) and led the race during the opening stint, before the team fell back to fifth by the checkered flag.[20] At Fuji, the team achieved another top-five class finish. The season concluded with a historic class victory at Bahrain—the first for an all-female lineup in WEC history—securing second place overall in the LMGTE Am drivers' and teams' championships.[21][22] In 2024, Bovy, Frey, and Gatting continued with Iron Dames in the inaugural LMGT3 class of the WEC, driving a Lamborghini Huracán GT3 Evo2. The team secured two pole positions and notable results, including fifth place at the 24 Hours of Le Mans and a top-five finish at Fuji, before ending the season eighth in the class standings with 54 points.[23][24] Following her promotion to FIA Silver driver status in late 2024, Bovy shifted focus in 2025 to the European Le Mans Series with Iron Dames alongside Gatting and Célia Martin in a Porsche 911 GT3 R, achieving a podium at the season-ending 4 Hours of Portimão and fourth place overall in the LMGT3 class. She also substituted for the injured Gatting in the #85 Iron Dames Porsche at the 2025 24 Hours of Le Mans, joining Frey and Martin.[25][26]
