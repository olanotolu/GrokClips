Al Daayen

Al Daayen (Arabic: الضعاين [ALA-LC: al-Ḍaʻāyin ; Wehr: aḍ-ḍaʿāyin]; also spelled as الظعاين [ALA-LC: al-Ẓaʻāyin ; Wehr: aẓ-ẓaʿāyin]) is a municipality in Qatar. Most of the urban landscape can be found in the southern zone of the municipality, particularly in the city of Lusail, while the northern and central sections are primarily rural. It is one of the fastest growing municipalities in Qatar due to its close proximity to the capital Doha.[1] Umm Qarn hosts the municipal office and serves as the municipality's administrative seat.[2]

Al Daayen Municipality

Etymology

Al Daayen Municipality is named after the village of the same name. Two theories are offered for the name. According to the Ministry of Municipality and Environment, the village derives its name from the Arabic word dhaayen, which roughly translates to "travel". It was given this name in reference to the Qatari tribes who abandoned the village and traveled elsewhere in search of water and suitable pasture. The second theory, given in the Encyclopedia of Qatari Information, states that the name is derived from an Arabic term used to denote a place for camels to rest.[3]

History

The demographic and urban growth of Qatar in the early 2000s necessitated the creation of Al Daayen Municipality. Prior to its establishment, the area was primarily rural, consisting of fishing and pearling villages, and was carved out from parts of Umm Salal and Al Khor municipalities. Al Daayen Municipality was established in 2005.[4] Initial development centered on the southern sector of Al Daayen, which is a part of the Doha Metropolitan Area. Early construction was scattered, often leaving developed and undeveloped sections adjacent to each other.[4]

Geography

Situated alongside Qatar's eastern coastline, the municipality is bordered by Doha to the south, Al Khor to the north, and Umm Salal to the west. An overwhelming portion of the municipality is located within Doha Metropolitan Area boundaries. Three main divisions exist within the municipality between the northern, central and southern parts.[5] The municipality accounts for roughly 2.5% of Qatar's total area.[4] According to the Ministry of Municipality and Environment (MME), the municipality accommodates 19 rawdas, which were historically popular spots for settlements as they were typically rich in vegetation due to water and sediment run-off. No less than eight of Al Daayen's villages were established near or on rawdas, including Al Rehayya, Al Sakhama and Al Masrouhiya. Upwards of 15 wadis were recorded, a notable one being Wadi Al Banat. Other geographic features listed include four jeris (places where water flows) one plain, four hills, two sabkhas, and two capes. Two natural islands are found off its shores, Al Jazira (literally meaning 'The Island') and Al Aaliya Island.[6] In the central portion, where most of the municipality's coastline is found, an open desert landscape is predominant. Large swathes of the central-east are dedicated to the Al Wusail Environmental Protected Area.[5] Northern Al Daayen is typified by agriculture, being dotted by several farms and a few small settlements. Among the most important rural settlements are Tenbek, Jarian, Umm Swaya and Umm Qarn.[5] Umm Qarn is being developed to serve as the nerve center for the northern section,[7] mainly because it is the most developed northern village, already accommodating the municipal office, a primary health center and two primary schools.[2] Metropolitan Doha infringes into the highly urbanized southern section of Al Daayen. This area serves as the residential and economic hub of the municipality, with the planned city of Lusail being the focal point,[5] having a prospective population capacity of 200,000. Rawdat Al Hamama, Al Ebb and Leabaib are also being developed as major mixed use centers for the southern area.[7]

Administration

The municipality is divided into 2 zones which are then divided into 385 blocks. The villages of Simaisma and Al Jeryan are geographically located in Al Daayen but serve as administrative zones for Al Khor. Rashid Alhajri is the mayor of Al Daayen Municipality.[8]

Infrastructure

Infrastructure in Al Daayen has witnessed massive improvements, with the number of completed buildings increasing from 3,151 in the 2010 census to 13,040 in the 2020 census.[12]

Government offices

The public services complex at Al Daayen was initiated in 2007 with a planned completion date of 2009.[13] This complex includes the main municipal office, archives, health and emergency centers, Ministry of Interior buildings including a police station and traffic patrol, and a mosque. Centered in Umm Qarn on a plot area of 7,500 square meters, the complex was designed with traditional elements to reflect Qatari architecture, incorporating local tree species such as palm trees, neems, and cedars. It won the architecture prize for municipal projects at the 2010 conference of the Organization of Islamic Capitals & Cities.[14] Recent developments include the establishment of the Al Daayen Government Center, providing centralized public services.[15] Ongoing infrastructure projects, such as roads and utilities in Al Kheesa (tendered in 2024) and Al Masrouhiya (2025), continue to support growth.[16][17]

Education

Public schools in Al Daayen amounted to 16 as recorded in the 2016 education census – 9 were exclusively for girls and 7 were for boys. More than twice as many students were female, at 4,261 compared to 2,034 male students.[18] As part of Lusail's master plan, the city is planned to contain 36 schools with a capacity for 26,000 students. Upwards of 75,000 square meters has been reserved for school buildings by the Lusail City Real Estate Development Company.[19] As of 2025, new agreements continue to support school development in the city, including a partnership for a new world-class school set to open in 2027.[20]

Healthcare

The Primary Health Care Corporation (PHCC) operates two primary healthcare centers in Al Daayen: the Al Daayen Health Center in Umm Qarn's public services complex and the Leabaib Wellness Centre in Leabaib.[21] A Pediatric Emergency Center (PEC) operated by Hamad Medical Corporation was previously located in the Al Daayen Health Center complex but was relocated to Aisha Bint Hamad Al Attiyah Hospital in Doha on 8 September 2024.[22] Lusail, within Al Daayen, hosts several hospitals, including The View Hospital (affiliated with Cedars-Sinai), which opened in 2023, and the Korean Medical Center.[23] Al Daayen is the planned site for the Al Daayan Health District, Qatar's second major medical hub after Hamad Medical City, with a masterplan finalized in 2021 encompassing a tertiary teaching hospital, women's and children's hospital, and ambulatory center with 1,400 beds total. As of 2025, the project remains in development.[24]

Economy

In 2010, 81.7% of the population was economically active.[25] Construction was the primary industry worked in by most of the working population at 21,702 employees, followed by public administration and defense (2,336) and agriculture and fishing (1,294).[26] Note: These figures are from 2010; more recent municipality-specific employment data is not publicly detailed, though Al Daayen's economy has grown significantly with Lusail's development post-2022 FIFA World Cup. The MEEZA Data Center is an IT center south of Umm Qarn.[27] Inaugurated in 2014, it spans an area of 10,000 square meters, making it MEEZA's largest data center.[28] Energy City in Lusail, designed to be a focal point for oil and gas companies, is currently being developed in Al Daayen.[27] First unveiled in March 2006, the $2.6 billion city will host as many as 92 company offices as well as corporate housing.[29] In July 2013, CEO of Energy City Hesham Al Emadi revealed that, as a result of many requests for office space by non energy-related companies, the city has been converted to a mixed-use facility.[30] Numerous non-energy related companies are also headquartered in Lusail. Hotel operator and developer Katara Hospitality is based in the city,[31] as is Qatari Diar Real Estate Investment Company and its subsidiaries,[32] such as Lusail Real Estate Development Company[33] and utilities company Marafeq Qatar.[34] Qatari Diar was responsible for launching the Lusail City Development Project in 2005.[35] In 2002, Lusail and its suburbs of Al Kharayej and Jabal Thuaileb became the first three areas of Qatar where foreigners could own real estate.[36] The Lusail Industrial Area hosts many construction companies. In the Lusail Ready-Mix Batching Plant Zone, two ready-mix batching plants are maintained by Qatar Alpha Beton Ready Mix,[37] one ready-mix batching plant is maintained by SMEET,[38] REDCO owns a precast plant,[39] Qatar Concrete has 1 ready-mix batching plant,[40] and HBK ReMIX operates a ready-mix factory.[41] Agriculture plays a relatively insignificant role in local economy compared to Qatar's other municipalities. Al Daayen's farms accounted for roughly 5% of Qatar's total farms in 2010.[42] Municipal statistics indicate that there were 52 farms at the time of the 2010 census, whereas the Ministry of Municipality & Planning recorded 47 farms.[43] The majority of farms are centered near Umm Qarn, along with small amounts of farmland being scattered around Rawdat Al Hamama, Wadi Al Wasaah and Jeryan Jenaihat.[42]

Transportation

Public transportation

Public transportation in Al Daayen has improved significantly with the completion of major projects. The Doha Metro Red Line and Lusail Tram provide key services, particularly in Lusail, while bus routes connect various areas. However, northern rural settlements may still lack extensive coverage.[44][45] Lusail plans for water taxis to become a primary mode of public transportation along its 30 km of developed coastline. Infrastructure for the first phase, including the Lusail Ferry Terminal and stops at The Pearl and Corniche, was completed in November 2024, with service expected soon.[46]

Roads

Al Shamal Road runs through the western portion of Al Daayen, while Al Khor Coastal Road runs through the central and eastern sections. The latter is the arterial road that connects the coastal settlements, such as Lusail, with Doha.[5]

Rail

Doha Metro's Red Line runs through Lusail, providing its residents with convenient access to Doha and Al Wakrah.[47] Qatar Rail is involved in the construction of the Lusail LRT. First designed in August 2007, the planned Lusail LRT network spans 33.1 km, of which 10.4 km is underground and the remaining 22.7 km is overground. The network connects to the Doha Metro Red Line through the Lusail Main Station and Lusail Marina Station.[48] As of 2025, parts of the Lusail Tram are operational, including the Turquoise Line which opened in January 2025, extending the network to 19 km with 25 stations across four lines.[49] Private transportation remains the preferred method for the municipality's pastoral settlements in the north due to limited rail infrastructure in this region.[50]

Sports

Lusail International Circuit, a motor racing circuit, is centered in the municipality.[51] It hosts major events such as the annual MotoGP and Formula 1 Qatar Grand Prix, including the 2025 edition.[52] Other sporting venues include Lusail Shooting Club and Lusail Sports Arena. Lusail Iconic Stadium was completed in 2022 and hosted the final of the 2022 FIFA World Cup. Planned sporting projects include the Lusail Sports Precinct, a large complex with sporting venues and residential units.[27]

Visitor attractions

According to a Ministry of Municipality and Environment report, the municipality hosted 7 public parks as of 2018. [53] The Lusail Museum, a large-scale facility housing the world's largest collection of Orientalist paintings and photography, is under construction on Al Maha Island (also known as Entertainment Island) in Lusail and is expected to open in 2029. [54]

Major attractions

Al Daayen features several prominent visitor destinations, particularly in Lusail. Lusail Marina offers waterfront dining, yachting, and promenades. The Lusail Iconic Stadium, a FIFA World Cup 2022 venue, hosts sports events and concerts. Al Maha Island includes Lusail Winter Wonderland, an annual seasonal park with rides and entertainment, and luxury dining options. Simaisma North Beach provides recreational facilities and water sports. Lusail International Circuit is a key site for motorsport events. [55] [56] Historic sites of interest can be found in the traditional villages of Umm Qarn, Tenbek, and Al Rehayya, as well as in deserted fishing villages north of Lusail. [2]

Demographics

Al Daayen has seen a rapid increase in population in recent years. The population grew from 43,175 in the 2010 census to 100,083 in the 2020 census. This increase was attributed to the development of Lusail and the numerous housing plots being awarded in the city as part of the Qatari National Housing Program. Most residents work outside of the municipality's borders.[2] In the 2020 census, males accounted for 55.4% of the population (55,466) while females made up 44.6% (44,617). Age-wise, 24.0% of the population (24,035) was under 15 and the remaining 76.0% was aged 15 and over.[58]
