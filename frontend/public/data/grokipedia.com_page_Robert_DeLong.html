Robert DeLong

Robert Charles Edward DeLong (born February 18, 1986)[3] is an American electronic musician from Bothell, Washington and currently residing in Los Angeles, California.[4] With a background in percussion and influences from a number of indie rock bands, DeLong's primary genres include house, dubstep, and moombahton. He has released three full-length albums on Glassnote Records: Just Movement in January 2013, In the Cards in September 2015, and Walk Like Me in November 2021.[5] His fourth studio album, Playlist of Doom, was released in September 2024 on Round Hill Records.[6] In June 2025, he released the deluxe edition Playlist of Doom: After DARK.[7]

Early life

DeLong grew up in the Seattle suburb of Bothell.[8] His father was a drummer.[9] Robert played in various bands while he was in high school. He studied drums at Azusa Pacific University,[10] partially supported by a scholarship.[11] While in college, he played in the band The Denouement[12][13] and settled in Los Angeles after graduation.

Career

DeLong started as a drummer for indie rock bands but added more electronic elements to his compositions following his exposure to their use at raves. He also released remixes on the Owsla label around 2013. DeLong is notable for using video-game peripherals, such as a Wii remote and a joystick, connected to a MIDI interface, to modify his sound. He cites as influences Death Cab for Cutie, Modest Mouse, Dreamers, and Boards of Canada, among others.[14] He released his first full-length album, Just Movement, on Glassnote Records on January 22, 2013.[15] His sound has been described by Wired Magazine as a merger of "electronic dance music, alt rock, and vocals into tracks that are poppy enough to make you move but dissonant enough to be interesting."[16] At live performances, facepaint is offered to fans; volunteer local artists organized and led by DeLong's longtime partner, visual artist Heidi Callaway, do the actual facepainting. Facepaint is a regular fixture of DeLong's performances and creates a sense of community amongst fans. His equipment and clothing feature an orange "X" logo; this same logo, or a variant of it, is often painted on his face and those in attendance.[9] His 2018 single "My Favorite Color is Blue", featuring K. Flay, is included in the videogame soundtrack of NHL 19. DeLong continued releasing music, including the albums In the Cards (2015) and Walk Like Me (2021) on Glassnote Records, followed by Playlist of Doom (2024) on Round Hill Records.[17][18][19]
