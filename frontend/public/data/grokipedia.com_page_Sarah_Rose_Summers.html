Sarah Rose Summers


Sarah Rose Summers (born November 4, 1994) is an American beauty pageant titleholder who was crowned Miss USA 2018, becoming the first woman from Nebraska to win the national title.[1][2] Representing the United States at Miss Universe 2018, she advanced to the Top 20 but did not place further.[3] A graduate of Texas Christian University with dual bachelor's degrees in child development and communication studies earned cum laude, Summers previously worked as a certified child life specialist while competing in pageants since age 10.[4][5] During her reign, Summers drew controversy after a video surfaced in which she commented on the limited English proficiency of Miss Vietnam and Miss Cambodia, prompting online backlash and a public apology from her for any perceived insensitivity.[6][7] Post-pageantry, she married Conner Combs in 2019, with whom she has one child, relocated to Dallas, Texas, and transitioned into podcasting and content creation focused on beauty, style, and family life.[8][9]

Early Life and Background

Childhood and Family Origins

Sarah Rose Summers was born on November 4, 1994, in Omaha, Nebraska, and raised in the adjacent suburb of Papillion, where she experienced a conventional Midwestern upbringing characterized by close community ties and familial proximity.[10][11] She grew up in a tight-knit nuclear and extended family, with grandparents, cousins, and second cousins residing within a 30-minute drive, fostering a supportive environment that emphasized rootedness and neighborly familiarity.[11] Her father, Mike Summers, and mother provided encouragement for her pursuits, though they initially expressed skepticism toward her interest in pageants, believing it might be a passing phase when she was around age 12.[12][11] Summers has one sibling, a brother named Scott, who accompanied family to her pageant events, including the 2018 Miss USA competition.[11] As a child, Summers was notably shy, often clinging to her mother and shying away from eye contact with strangers, traits that contrasted with her later public persona.[11] A pivotal early experience occurred at age 5, when she was hospitalized in Omaha's children's hospital for an unspecified medical issue; while her memories of the event itself were vague, she vividly recalled her parents' distressâ€”particularly her father's fearâ€”and the compassionate care from a nurse, which later influenced her professional path in child life specialization.[13][11]

Education and Early Influences

Summers' interest in child development and healthcare advocacy stemmed from a personal experience with illness during her early childhood. At age four, she was hospitalized due to idiopathic thrombocytopenic purpura (ITP), a blood disorder characterized by low platelet counts that impairs clotting.[14][5] This episode, which involved a brief but impactful hospital stay, motivated her later pursuit of a career supporting hospitalized children, leading to her certification as a child life specialist after completing clinical rotations post-graduation.[14] She attended Texas Christian University (TCU) in Fort Worth, Texas, where she earned dual Bachelor of Science degrees in child development and strategic communication, graduating cum laude in 2017.[15][16][5] During her time at TCU, Summers was a member of the Zeta Tau Alpha sorority and balanced her studies with part-time employment, including roles as a Pilates instructor and promotional work with the Dallas Cowboys cheerleaders.[17][18] These academic and extracurricular experiences reinforced her focus on communication skills and child welfare, aligning with her emerging professional goals in advocacy and public engagement.[4]

Pageantry Career

Early Pageant Involvement (2004â€“2017)

Sarah Rose Summers initiated her involvement in beauty pageants in 2005 at the age of 10, participating in the National American Miss (NAM) system after convincing her parents through a promotional flyer.[3] She competed in NAM events for four consecutive years without securing a title before winning the National American Miss Nebraska Junior Teen crown in 2009.[3] [12] Following her state victory, Summers advanced to the national level, capturing the National American Miss Junior Teen title in 2010 and becoming the first contestant from Nebraska to achieve this honor.[11] These early successes in the NAM circuit, which emphasizes personal development alongside competition, laid the foundation for her subsequent pursuits in more prominent pageant systems.[3] Summers transitioned to the Miss Teen USA franchise during her high school years, representing Sarpy County and earning the Miss Nebraska Teen USA 2012 title.[3] She competed at the national Miss Teen USA 2012 pageant but did not place among the finalists.[3] [19] After a period focused on education at Texas Christian University, Summers returned to competition in the Miss USA system, entering Miss Nebraska USA 2016 and finishing as second runner-up.[14] This placement marked her entry into adult division pageants, building on her prior teen experiences ahead of her eventual state win in 2018.[14]

Miss Nebraska USA 2018 and Miss USA Victory

Sarah Rose Summers was crowned Miss Nebraska USA 2018 on January 7, 2018, at the Rose Theater in Omaha, Nebraska, succeeding the previous titleholder, Aleah Johnson.[20] This victory qualified her to represent Nebraska at the national Miss USA competition, marking a significant milestone in her pageant career that began in her youth.[20] Summers competed in the Miss USA 2018 pageant held on May 21, 2018, at the Hirsch Memorial Coliseum in Shreveport, Louisiana, alongside 50 other contestants from U.S. states and the District of Columbia.[21] The event, hosted by Nick Lachey and Vanessa Lachey with performances by 98 Degrees and Lee Brice, featured preliminary competitions in swimsuit, evening gown, and interview segments earlier that day.[22] During the finals, Summers advanced through the initial swimsuit and evening gown rounds, followed by a top 10 interview segment where contestants addressed topics such as social media's impact on youth.[23] She progressed to the top five, answering a question on bridging political divides by emphasizing active listening and understanding perspectives. Ultimately, Summers was announced as the winner, becoming the first woman from Nebraska to claim the Miss USA title, with Miss North Carolina Caelynn Miller-Keyes as first runner-up and Miss Nevada Carolina Urzua as second runner-up.[23] [14] Outgoing Miss USA KÃ¡ra McCullough of the District of Columbia crowned her successor at the event's conclusion.[21]

Miss Universe 2018 Participation and Top Placement

Sarah Rose Summers represented the United States as Miss USA 2018 at the Miss Universe 2018 pageant, held on December 16, 2018, at the Impact Arena in Bangkok, Thailand.[24] The competition featured contestants from 94 countries and territories, with Summers advancing through the preliminary rounds to the final swimsuit and evening gown segments.[3] During the finals, Summers was selected as one of the Top 20 semifinalists, competing in the evening gown competition before the field was narrowed further to the Top 10.[3] This placement marked the extent of her progression in the event, which ultimately crowned Catriona Gray of the Philippines as the winner.[24] Summers' participation highlighted her as the tenth American titleholder to compete in Miss Teen USA, Miss USA, and Miss Universe.[25]

Professional Pursuits

Pre-Pageantry Employment

Prior to achieving national recognition in pageantry, Sarah Rose Summers pursued employment in fitness and pediatric healthcare. While studying at Texas Christian University, where she earned two degrees and graduated cum laude, she worked part-time as a Pilates instructor.[4] She also held a position at Lululemon Athletica during this period.[13] Following her graduation, Summers became a certified child life specialist, a role focused on supporting children and families in medical settings through therapeutic play and coping strategies.[14][26] This certification, which she had recently obtained prior to the 2018 Miss USA competition, aligned with her academic background in child development and informed her advocacy during pageant appearances.[13] Although she planned to seek full-time opportunities in the field immediately after the competition, her pre-existing experience in child life work was highlighted in contemporary profiles as her primary professional pursuit at the time.[27]

Post-Pageantry Ventures and Advocacy

Following the conclusion of her Miss USA reign on May 2, 2019, Sarah Rose Summers transitioned into independent media and content creation. She launched the podcast More Than A Crown 2.0 in 2019, which features discussions on personal identity, mental health, faith, and lifestyle topics, emphasizing that individuals are defined beyond singular achievements.[28] [29] The podcast, available on platforms including Apple Podcasts and Spotify, includes episodes addressing challenges like pausing production for mental health recovery and broader themes of resilience.[30] Summers has built a personal brand through her website So Sarah Rose and social media, where she shares content on beauty, skincare, fitness routines, and faith-based encouragement.[31] [32] With approximately 331,000 Instagram followers as of 2025, she posts regularly on style, family life, and wellness, positioning herself as a lifestyle influencer and event host.[9] She also pursues speaking engagements, such as those with YoungLife, to inspire audiences on personal growth and purpose.[4] In advocacy, Summers draws from her certification as a child life specialist, completed prior to her pageantry success, to promote support for hospitalized children, including through hospital visits and awareness efforts extended beyond her reign.[32] [33] She emphasizes healthy relationships with food and fitness, advocating against body shaming and unrealistic beauty standards, informed by her experiences with eating disorders during clinical training.[32] [22] Her platform integrates these themes with faith-driven messages, encouraging balanced self-image and resilience.[32]

Controversies and Public Backlash

2018 Instagram Remarks on Contestant Language Skills

In December 2018, during the Miss Universe competition in Bangkok, Thailand, Sarah Rose Summers posted a video to her Instagram Stories expressing frustration over communication barriers with fellow contestants Miss Cambodia Nat Rern and Miss Vietnam H'Hen NiÃª.[6][34] In the footage, Summers stated, "Miss Cambodia is here and doesn't speak any English, and not a single other person speaks her language. Can you imagine?" while gesturing toward the contestants, highlighting the challenges of interacting without a common language.[35][36] The remarks, captured amid the pageant's international setting where English serves as the primary working language, drew immediate online criticism for appearing to mock the contestants' limited English proficiency.[7][37] The video quickly went viral, prompting accusations of xenophobia and insensitivity from social media users and commentators, who argued it demeaned non-native English speakers in a global event.[38][39] Critics, including some in mainstream outlets, labeled the comments as racially tinged bullying, amplifying backlash through shares on platforms like Twitter and Facebook.[40][41] However, Summers' defenders noted the practical difficulties of coordinating 94 contestants from diverse linguistic backgrounds, where translation apps and interpreters were often relied upon but proved inefficient.[42] On December 13, 2018, Summers issued a public apology via Instagram, stating, "My words could have been chosen better... There was no intention to be hurtful to anyone," and emphasizing her respect for the contestants' cultures and efforts.[6][7] H'Hen NiÃª, Miss Vietnam, publicly defended Summers on December 14, 2018, revealing that Summers had previously offered personal encouragement and language assistance during their interactions, describing her as supportive rather than derogatory.[43] Similarly, Catriona Gray, the eventual Miss Universe 2018 winner from the Philippines, later vouched for Summers' character in a January 2019 interview, asserting no malicious intent after knowing her personally.[44] The incident subsided following these responses, with no formal pageant sanctions reported, though it highlighted tensions around cultural expectations in international competitions.[45]

Personal Life and Values

Marriage and Family

Sarah Rose Summers became engaged to her longtime boyfriend, Conner Combs, on December 17, 2018, in Bangkok, Thailand, shortly after her participation in the Miss Universe 2018 pageant.[46] The couple married on October 20, 2019, in an outdoor ceremony at the Anthem Golf & Country Club in Anthem, Arizona.[47] [48] Summers and Combs welcomed their first child, a son named Caden Wyatt Combs, in early 2023; he was born prematurely at 32 weeks gestation and spent time in the neonatal intensive care unit (NICU).[49] The family resides in Dallas, Texas, where Summers has described balancing her roles as a wife, mother, and advocate amid her son's medical needs. No public details are available regarding Summers' extended family or prior relationships.

Faith and Worldview

Sarah Rose Summers professes a Christian faith, drawing on biblical teachings to articulate her views on personal identity and purpose. In personal reflections published on her website, she asserts that "we are each uniquely and purposefully created," directly referencing Psalm 139:13-14, which describes human formation in the womb as a divine act.[50] This scriptural invocation underscores a worldview centered on the inherent value bestowed by a creator God, rejecting self-comparison in favor of embracing one's divinely ordained story.[50] Summers integrates her faith into accounts of significant life events, such as her engagement, where she describes her partner's gesture of washing her feet as emblematic of "the humble sacrificial love of Christ" and references a shared Bible received at a Christian conference, quoting Mark 10:9 on marital unity.[51] She has publicly affirmed belief in core Christian doctrines, including the resurrection of Jesus, stating on social media during Easter that "the battle has been won" and "the grave has been defeated" through Christ's sacrifice.[52] Such expressions align with evangelical emphases on redemption and victory over death via atonement. In interviews, Summers attributes resilience against adversity to faith, listing it alongside family and personal discipline as anchors for handling challenges, including difficult interpersonal dynamics.[27] She has invoked divine faithfulness in recounting her pageant successes, declaring "our God is faithful" as a foundational element of her perseverance.[53] Her involvement with Christian organizations, such as Compassion International for child sponsorship, further reflects a commitment to faith-driven service, prioritizing spiritual guidance through local churches to address poverty and fear.[54] Summers' worldview thus manifests as one oriented toward trust in God's sovereignty, personal transformation through Christ, and ethical living informed by scripture, without evident affiliation to a specific denomination beyond broad Protestant influences evident in her education at Texas Christian University.[16]

Filmography and Interviews

She also featured as a guest on Celebrity Page during her reign, discussing pageantry and advocacy work. Additional appearances encompass Home & Family in 2018 and LIVE with Kelly and Mark (formerly Live with Kelly and Ryan), both as herself promoting her title.[56] In 2019, she participated in the Miss USA 2019 telecast as an outgoing titleholder.
