Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir (born 29 September 1990) is an Icelandic professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Al-Qadsiah in the Saudi Women's Premier League.[1][2] She captained the Iceland women's national team until her retirement from international duty in 2023, earning 145 caps during her career.[3] Gunnarsdóttir won two UEFA Women's Champions League titles with Olympique Lyonnais in 2020 and 2022, becoming the first Icelandic player to claim the competition.[4][5] She is also noted for her landmark 2022 victory in a FIFA dispute resolution against Lyon, where she became the first player to successfully enforce the organization's maternity regulations, receiving over €82,000 in owed salary for the period she was unable to play due to pregnancy.[6][7] This case established a precedent for financial protections for pregnant athletes in professional football.[8]

Club Career

FC Malmö

Gunnarsdóttir signed a three-year contract with LdB FC Malmö, the women's team affiliated with Malmö FF, in 2011, marking her move abroad from Icelandic club Stjarnan.[9] In her first season, she recorded 12 goals across all competitions, contributing to the team's Damallsvenskan title win as defending champions from 2010.[9] [10] During the 2011–12 UEFA Women's Champions League, LdB FC Malmö advanced to the quarter-finals, where Gunnarsdóttir scored the decisive goal in a 1–0 first-leg victory over 1. FFC Frankfurt on 15 March 2012 under interim coach Peter Moberg.[11] The team ultimately exited the competition after losing the second leg 2–0, resulting in a 2–1 aggregate defeat.[11] LdB FC Malmö secured another Damallsvenskan title in 2013, defeating Umeå IK 2–0 on 13 October to clinch the championship.[12] Gunnarsdóttir's consistent performances as a midfielder helped establish the club as a dominant force in Swedish women's football during this period, with the team winning two league titles in her first three seasons.[10] The club, sponsored by LdB since 2007, transitioned to FC Rosengård in 2014 following a takeover, but Gunnarsdóttir remained until announcing her departure in May 2016 to join VfL Wolfsburg.[13]

VfL Wolfsburg

Gunnarsdóttir joined VfL Wolfsburg in the summer of 2016, transferring from FC Rosengård to bolster the club's midfield.[14] During her tenure from 2016 to 2020, she operated primarily as a defensive midfielder, providing stability and leadership in the engine room.[15] Wolfsburg dominated German women's football in this period, securing the Frauen-Bundesliga title for four consecutive seasons: 2016–17, 2017–18, 2018–19, and 2019–20.[16] The club also claimed the DFB-Pokal Frauen in each of those campaigns, completing domestic doubles. Gunnarsdóttir contributed to these successes, appearing in key matches including the 2018 UEFA Women's Champions League final, where Wolfsburg fell 4–1 to Olympique Lyonnais after extra time.[17] In January 2018, amid ongoing team success, she extended her contract until June 2020, with club officials praising her as a vital leader.[15] Approaching the 2018–19 season's end, Gunnarsdóttir highlighted the squad's target of another double, reflecting her integral role in sustaining the team's competitive edge.[18] She left Wolfsburg in July 2020, signing with Olympique Lyonnais.[17]

Olympique Lyonnais

Gunnarsdóttir transferred to Olympique Lyonnais from VfL Wolfsburg in the summer of 2020.[19][20] In the delayed 2019–20 UEFA Women's Champions League final on 30 August 2020, she scored the third goal in a 3–1 victory over Wolfsburg, securing Lyon's seventh European title and her first with the club.[21][5] During the 2020–21 season, Lyon won the Division 1 Féminine title, extending their domestic dominance. Gunnarsdóttir contributed in midfield before taking a maternity leave announced on 22 April 2021, following a pregnancy that began earlier in the year; she had ceased playing in March 2021.[22][23] She gave birth in late 2021 and returned to training and matches in the subsequent season.[24] In 2021–22, Lyon again claimed the Division 1 Féminine championship and reached the UEFA Women's Champions League final, though they lost 3–1 to Barcelona. Gunnarsdóttir departed the club in July 2022, signing with Juventus.[20]

Juventus FC

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir transferred to Juventus FC Women from Olympique Lyonnais on 1 July 2022, signing a two-year contract expiring in June 2024.[25] The 31-year-old Icelandic midfielder joined as a key addition to the squad, bringing prior successes including two UEFA Women's Champions League titles and domestic honors from clubs in Sweden, Germany, and France.[25] Positioned primarily as a central midfielder, Gunnarsdóttir featured regularly in Serie A Femminile and UEFA Women's Champions League matches during the 2022–23 and 2023–24 seasons.[26] She made 49 appearances across all competitions, contributing to the team's defensive solidity and midfield control in a side aiming to dominate Italian women's football and compete in Europe.[26] Her experience helped Juventus secure two trophies over her tenure, bolstering their status as perennial contenders in the league.[26] Gunnarsdóttir's time at Juventus concluded at the end of the 2023–24 season on 21 May 2024, after which she departed the club.[26] The club acknowledged her professional impact in an official farewell, highlighting her role in the Bianconere's achievements during a period of transition following her high-profile arrival.[26]

Al Qadsiah FC

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir joined Al Qadsiah FC, a team in the Saudi Women's Premier League, on a one-year contract announced on August 2, 2024.[2][27] The signing followed the expiration of her previous contract with Juventus FC and aimed to strengthen the squad for the 2024–25 season, with the club having finished fourth in the prior campaign among eight teams.[28] Gunnarsdóttir debuted for Al Qadsiah on September 28, 2024.[29] In the 2024–25 Saudi Women's Premier League, she recorded 11 goals in 19 appearances as a defensive midfielder, helping the team achieve a third-place finish.[30] Her contributions included goals such as one scored in the 15th minute during a 2–0 victory.[31] The player's initial contract was set to expire on June 30, 2025.[29] Following the season, Gunnarsdóttir extended her deal with Al Qadsiah, committing to the club for the 2025–26 campaign amid ongoing participation in the league.[30] As of October 2025, she remains active with the team, which competes in Saudi Arabia's top-tier women's professional league established to professionalize domestic football.[32]

International Career

Senior Debut and Early Years

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir made her senior international debut for the Iceland women's national team on 26 August 2007, aged 16, during a UEFA Women's Euro 2009 qualifying match against Slovenia in Dravograd, which Slovenia won 2–1.[33] The appearance came shortly after her recovery from an anterior cruciate ligament injury sustained earlier in her youth career. Iceland qualified for their first UEFA Women's Euro finals in 2009, with Gunnarsdóttir featuring in the successful campaign that secured second place in Group 6 behind France. Selected for the tournament squad at age 18, she appeared in all three group stage matches as Iceland finished bottom of their group, losing 1–0 to Norway, 3–1 to Germany, and 2–0 to France. Prior to the finals, she contributed offensively in preparatory competitions, including two goals in a 3–1 win over Norway at the 2009 Algarve Cup. In her initial years, Gunnarsdóttir established herself as a defensive midfielder, accumulating over 50 caps by mid-2013 while helping Iceland maintain competitive form in qualifiers and friendlies.[34] Her early international record included participation in UEFA Women's Euro 2013 qualifying, where Iceland advanced to the playoffs but fell short of qualification.

Captaincy and Key Milestones

Gunnarsdóttir succeeded Katrín Jónsdóttir as captain of the Iceland women's national football team around 2013–2014, a role she held for nearly a decade until her retirement.[13] Under new head coach Freyr Alexandersson, she assumed the armband amid transitions in team leadership, providing continuity as a experienced midfielder with prior international exposure.[35] As captain, Gunnarsdóttir led Iceland to qualification for UEFA Women's Euro 2017, where the team advanced through playoffs against Switzerland before exiting the group stage. She captained the side again at UEFA Women's Euro 2022, shortly after returning from maternity leave, though Iceland finished third in their group with draws and losses against Norway, France, and England. These tournaments marked Iceland's consistent presence in major European competitions during her tenure, despite the nation's limited resources compared to larger footballing nations. A key personal milestone came when Gunnarsdóttir surpassed Jónsdóttir to become Iceland's most capped women's player, achieving this record as a regular starter and leader with over 132 appearances by the late 2010s.[36] By her international retirement on January 13, 2023, she had amassed 145 caps and 20 goals for Iceland, contributing to the team's defensive solidity and midfield control in qualifying campaigns.[1][13] Her longevity as captain underscored Iceland's emphasis on experienced leadership in women's football, where she earned recognition as the nation's player of the year multiple times for her on-field influence.[37]

Participation in Major Tournaments

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir participated in four editions of the UEFA Women's Euro with the Iceland national team, appearing in a record 13 matches for her country across the tournaments held in 2009, 2013, 2017, and 2022.[38][39] Her debut came in the 2009 edition in Finland, where at age 18 she started all three group stage fixtures as Iceland exited with defeats to Norway (1-0 loss on 24 August), Germany (1-0 loss on 28 August), and France (1-0 loss on 1 September), recording zero goals.[34][40] In 2013, hosted by Sweden, Gunnarsdóttir played in the group stage matches, contributing to Iceland's campaign that included a 1-1 draw against Switzerland before losses to Denmark (2-0) and Germany (4-0), with no goals scored by her.[34] During the 2017 tournament in the Netherlands, she featured in three matches, scoring one goal, as Iceland drew 0-0 with Finland, lost 1-0 to Austria, and fell 1-0 to Sweden, failing to advance from the group.[41] As captain in the 2022 edition in England, Gunnarsdóttir led Iceland in group stage draws against Belgium (1-1 on 10 July) and France (1-1 on 18 July), followed by a 4-0 loss to Germany, with her defensive block notable in the France match; Iceland did not progress due to goal difference.[42][43] Iceland did not qualify for any FIFA Women's World Cup during Gunnarsdóttir's international career, thus she has no appearances in that competition.[13]

Retirement from National Team

On 13 January 2023, Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir announced her retirement from the Iceland women's national football team via an Instagram post, ending a 16-year international career during which she earned 145 caps and scored 24 goals.[13] As the team's captain since 2013, she had been a central figure in Iceland's midfield, contributing to consistent qualifications for major tournaments including three UEFA Women's EURO appearances (2013, 2017, 2022) and the 2013 FIFA Women's World Cup.[13][7] The Icelandic Football Association acknowledged the decision as difficult, expressing respect and gratitude for her leadership and contributions, which included helping elevate the team's international profile despite Iceland's small population.[44] Gunnarsdóttir's final international appearance occurred in a UEFA Women's Nations League match against Germany on 15 November 2022, marking her return after maternity leave earlier that year.[13] Her retirement came amid ongoing recovery from pregnancy-related challenges, including a high-profile maternity rights dispute with her club, though she cited personal priorities and family considerations as key factors without elaborating on specific injuries or team dynamics.[7] Gunnarsdóttir's departure prompted tributes from peers and officials, highlighting her role in inspiring female footballers in Iceland and advocating for better player protections, particularly for mothers balancing elite sport and family.[13] She remained active at the club level post-retirement, signing with Al-Qadsiah FC in Saudi Arabia in 2024, but confirmed no plans to reverse her international decision as of that year.[45]

Maternity Dispute with Olympique Lyonnais

Background and Pregnancy During Contract

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir, captain of the Iceland women's national team and a midfielder known for her contributions in European club football, joined Olympique Lyonnais from VfL Wolfsburg in the summer of 2020.[19][20] Prior to this transfer, she had spent four seasons at Wolfsburg, where she helped secure multiple domestic titles and competed at a high level in the UEFA Women's Champions League.[19] Her move to Lyon, a dominant force in women's football with a history of seven consecutive Champions League victories entering 2020, aligned with her ambition to play for one of the sport's elite clubs.[19] Gunnarsdóttir's contract with Lyon extended into 2021, during which she participated in team activities amid the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.[8] In March 2021, she discovered she was pregnant and subsequently stopped playing competitive matches for the club.[23] She and Lyon agreed that she would relocate to Iceland to be with her partner and family for the remainder of her pregnancy, rendering her unavailable for the 2020–21 season's conclusion.[22][8] On April 22, 2021, Olympique Lyonnais issued a public statement congratulating Gunnarsdóttir and her husband on the expected arrival of their child by the end of 2021.[22] The announcement confirmed her withdrawal from club duties to prioritize her health and pregnancy in Iceland.[22] Gunnarsdóttir gave birth to a son in November 2021, marking the start of her maternity period while still contractually bound to Lyon until July 2022.[46][20]

Club's Withholding of Salary and Player's Claim

Olympique Lyonnais placed Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir on sick leave from 26 March 2021 to 23 September 2021 upon learning of her pregnancy, during which the club withheld her full contractual salary and instead provided partial compensation totaling €27,427.39 for the period from April to September 2021, despite her entitlement to approximately €111,000 under her employment agreement.[47] The club justified this reduction by asserting that Gunnarsdóttir was unable to provide sporting or alternative services due to her condition and that payments aligned with French labor law and the club's internal company agreement on sick leave remuneration, which capped benefits below full salary levels.[47] [48] Gunnarsdóttir, who had returned to Iceland for her pregnancy, contested the withholding, arguing that her contract—specifying €17,000 per month for the 2020–2021 season, €18,000 per month for 2021–2022, and an additional €1,000 monthly housing allowance—obligated the club to pay her full salary uninterrupted during maternity-related absence, in line with FIFA's maternity regulations introduced in 2020 to protect players' remuneration.[47] [7] On 10 September 2021, she formally filed a claim with FIFA's Dispute Resolution Chamber, seeking €83,472.61 in outstanding salaries and housing allowances specifically for April to September 2021, marking the first such invocation of FIFA's maternity pay protections against a club.[47] [6] The player emphasized that the dispute centered on enforceable contractual rights rather than discretionary club policy, highlighting the gap between national labor frameworks and international sports employment standards.[20]

FIFA Ruling and Precedent Set

In May 2022, FIFA's Dispute Resolution Chamber ruled in favor of Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir in her claim against Olympique Lyonnais, determining that the club violated FIFA's maternity regulations by failing to pay her full salary during her pregnancy from April to September 2021.[7][6] The decision enforced FIFA's policy requiring clubs to provide full remuneration to female players throughout pregnancy until the onset of maternity leave, distinct from the minimum 14 weeks of maternity leave at two-thirds salary, rejecting Lyon's argument that French labor law—which allowed reduced pay during pregnancy—superseded international football regulations.[48][49] The tribunal ordered Lyon to pay Gunnarsdóttir €82,094.80 in outstanding salary, equivalent to approximately $88,867 at the time, plus 5% annual interest from the due dates until full settlement.[49][50] This marked the first successful claim under FIFA's dedicated maternity provisions, introduced in 2020 to address gaps in player protections amid rising pregnancies in professional women's football.[7][6] The ruling established a key precedent by affirming FIFA regulations' primacy over national laws in employment disputes for international transfers, compelling clubs to integrate full pregnancy pay into contracts and highlighting vulnerabilities even at elite teams like Lyon, a seven-time UEFA Women's Champions League winner.[8][23] It prompted broader industry scrutiny, with FIFPro citing it as evidence that maternity policies require robust enforcement to prevent deterrence of family planning among players, influencing subsequent contract negotiations and collective bargaining in women's leagues.[7][50]

Criticisms and Broader Debate on Maternity Policies

Gunnarsdóttir's dispute with Olympique Lyonnais exposed significant shortcomings in maternity policies within elite women's football, even at dominant clubs like Lyon, which had won the UEFA Women's Champions League eight times between 2011 and 2020.[7] The club's decision to withhold her full salary—paying only a portion during her pregnancy despite her contract running until June 2021—drew criticism for treating pregnancy as a de facto penalty on earning potential, contravening FIFA's mandatory regulations introduced in May 2020, which require full remuneration until the onset of maternity leave.[50] Critics, including FIFPro, argued that such practices undermine player welfare and deter family planning, with Gunnarsdóttir's case illustrating how even high-profile athletes at resource-rich teams lack adequate safeguards, prompting calls for stricter enforcement mechanisms beyond FIFA's Dispute Resolution Chamber.[7] The FIFA ruling on May 19, 2022, which awarded Gunnarsdóttir her full €82,000 salary plus 5% interest, set a precedent as the first successful claim under the new rules, but it also fueled debate on the sufficiency of minimum standards like 14 weeks of paid leave at two-thirds salary post-birth. Advocacy groups highlighted that European clubs often lag behind U.S. leagues, such as the NWSL, where collective bargaining agreements since 2020 mandate full pay for 12 weeks plus extended health benefits, contrasting with fragmented protections in UEFA nations that rely on national labor laws varying widely in generosity.[51] Gunnarsdóttir herself described the issue as a "wake-up call" for the sport, emphasizing that non-compliance risks perpetuating discrimination, as clubs may view pregnancy as a financial or roster burden rather than a protected right.[50] Broader discussions post-ruling have centered on causal factors like the youth of women's professional football, where contracts are shorter and less secure than in men's game equivalents, exacerbating vulnerabilities during maternity.[8] While FIFA's policies aim to align with International Labour Organization conventions, skeptics note enforcement gaps, as players must litigate individually, deterring lower-profile athletes from challenging violations due to fear of retaliation or contract non-renewal.[52] This has spurred FIFPro campaigns for standardized global contracts including parental provisions, arguing that without them, the sport's growth—evidenced by rising attendance and revenue—will falter if motherhood remains a career obstacle, potentially limiting talent pools and sustainability.[7]

Personal Life

Family and Relationships

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir is married to Árni Vilhjálmsson, an Icelandic professional footballer who plays as a striker for Breiðablik in the Úrvalsdeild karla.[53][54] The couple welcomed their first child, a son named Ragnar, in November 2021.[54][55] Limited public information exists regarding Gunnarsdóttir's extended family, such as parents or siblings, as she has primarily discussed her immediate household in interviews focused on balancing motherhood with her athletic career.[19]

Motherhood and Return to Professional Play

Gunnarsdóttir gave birth to her first child, a son named Ragnar, on November 16, 2021, in Iceland following the later stages of her pregnancy spent there.[56][57] In January 2022, she returned to Lyon with her partner Árni and infant son to resume training, marking the beginning of her postpartum athletic reintegration.[19] She encountered physical challenges, including initial mobility limitations, and logistical hurdles such as club restrictions on bringing her baby on away trips, which contributed to feelings of isolation amid team dynamics.[19][57] Gunnarsdóttir balanced breastfeeding with intensive conditioning, crediting the process for building resilience, though she noted the emotional toll of motherhood intersecting with professional demands.[19] By March 2022, approximately four months postpartum, she returned to competitive matches, achieving a milestone as the first Lyon player to resume elite-level play after childbirth.[1][24] Her partnership with PUMA documented this phase, emphasizing the rigorous recovery required to regain match fitness.[24] In July 2022, she captained Iceland at UEFA Women's Euro 2022, competing effectively eight months after birth and underscoring her ability to sustain high performance post-motherhood.[54] After departing Lyon in 2022 for sporting reasons, Gunnarsdóttir joined Juventus, where she continued her club career while maintaining family priorities, reflecting on the dual roles as mutually reinforcing despite inherent tensions.[20][48][19]

Off-Field Interests and Sponsorships

Gunnarsdóttir maintains sponsorship agreements with Puma, under which the brand has described her as one of its top female athletes, particularly highlighting her role with Olympique Lyonnais, a leading women's football club.[58] In collaboration with Puma, she featured in the 2022 documentary Do Both, which chronicled her pregnancy, childbirth, and return to professional training after five months postpartum, emphasizing the compatibility of elite athletics and motherhood.[59] She also serves as a member of Team Visa, noted as the program's sole footballer who is a mother, aligning her endorsement with themes of resilience in balancing family and career.[1] These partnerships have involved content creation to promote women's professional opportunities in sports, including editorial series on her post-pregnancy athletic process.[60]

International Caps and Goals

Gunnarsdóttir debuted for the Iceland women's national team in 2007.[63] Over the course of her international career spanning 16 years, she accumulated 145 caps and scored 24 goals, establishing herself as Iceland's most-capped female footballer.[1][64] She captained the side, including at UEFA Women's Euro 2022.[57] Gunnarsdóttir announced her retirement from international duty on 13 January 2023.[13][44]

Honours

Club Honours

With FC Rosengård (2011–2016), Gunnarsdóttir contributed to three Damallsvenskan titles in 2013, 2014, and 2015, along with Svenska Cupen victories in 2016 and Svenska Supercupen wins in 2014 and 2015.[2] During her tenure at VfL Wolfsburg (2016–2020), she secured four consecutive Frauen-Bundesliga championships in the 2016–17, 2017–18, 2018–19, and 2019–20 seasons, as well as four DFB-Pokal der Frauen titles in the same periods.[16][65] At Olympique Lyonnais (2020–2022), Gunnarsdóttir won two Division 1 Féminine titles in 2020–21 and 2021–22, the 2021–22 UEFA Women's Champions League (scoring in key matches en route to the final victory over Barcelona), and the Coupe de France Féminine in 2020.[19][66] With Juventus FC (2022–2024), she claimed two Serie A Femminile championships in the 2022–23 and 2023–24 seasons.[67]

International Honours

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir has not won any major international titles with the Iceland women's national team. The team, under her captaincy from 2013 onward, qualified for the UEFA Women's EURO in 2013, 2017, and 2022, achieving its best result of quarter-finalists in 2013.[68] Iceland has also participated in minor invitational tournaments such as the Algarve Cup, where Gunnarsdóttir featured prominently, including scoring twice in a 2009 group stage victory over Norway, though without securing championships during her active years. Prior to major tournament qualifications, she contributed to Iceland's first appearance at the UEFA Women's EURO 2009 qualifiers and debut in the competition itself, marking historic milestones for Icelandic women's football.[69]

Individual Recognitions

Sara Björk Gunnarsdóttir has been recognized multiple times as Iceland's premier female footballer, winning the Icelandic Footballer of the Year award six times, including five consecutive victories culminating in 2019.[70] She also earned the title in 2017.[71] In broader Icelandic sports honors, Gunnarsdóttir became the only woman to win Sportsperson of the Year twice, first in 2018 for her contributions with VfL Wolfsburg and the national team, and again in 2020 following her UEFA Women's Champions League triumph with Olympique Lyonnais, where she received a record 600 voting points.[72][25] Internationally, she ranked 43rd on the 2017 list of the world's top 100 women footballers, as selected by experts.[71] Gunnarsdóttir was nominated for the UEFA Women's Champions League Midfielder of the Season award for the 2019/20 campaign, one of three midfielders shortlisted alongside teammates from Lyon.[73] She also featured in the UEFA.com fans' Women's Team of the Year for 2020, based on her performances across club and international matches that year.[74]
