M. Patrick Carroll

M. Patrick Carroll is an American real estate investor and entrepreneur who founded CARROLL, a vertically integrated multifamily platform specializing in apartment acquisitions, development, and management along the U.S. East Coast.[1] Without a college degree, Carroll built the firm from initial investments into a portfolio controlling over 28,000 units valued at billions, overseeing more than $12 billion in transactions before selling the company to The RMR Group for $80 million in 2023.[2][3] Post-sale, he founded Carroll Holdings as a private investment vehicle focused on real estate opportunities.[4] Carroll's career highlights include multiple nine-figure exits and a reputation for hands-on operational leadership in value-add strategies, though he has faced recent personal controversies involving a reported bipolar episode leading to a police chase and a lawsuit alleging threats against security personnel.[5][6] Additionally, he has engaged in philanthropy, notably donating $1 million to the Friends of the Israel Defense Forces in 2023.[7]

Early Life and Education

Childhood and Upbringing

M. Patrick Carroll was born in 1979 in Richmond, Virginia, and relocated to Tampa, Florida, during his early childhood, where he spent much of his formative years.[8][9] Carroll grew up in humble circumstances in Tampa and participated in basketball activities at a local Boys & Girls Club, fostering early community ties that later influenced his philanthropic efforts.[10] He has described his upbringing as atypical for a success story, stating that he "got in some trouble growing up," though specific details remain undisclosed in public accounts.[10][11] He attended Jesuit High School in Tampa, graduating in 1998, and has maintained strong connections to the institution through ongoing support and board involvement.[9][8]

Formal Education and Early Ambitions

M. Patrick Carroll attended Jesuit High School in Tampa, Florida, where he developed an early interest in real estate influenced by family background.[4][12] Following his graduation from high school around 1997, Carroll relocated to Atlanta, Georgia, forgoing higher education to pursue entrepreneurial opportunities directly.[8] At age 18, he purchased his first investment property in Atlanta, marking the inception of his focus on real estate acquisition and value enhancement without a college degree.[8][13] Carroll's early ambitions centered on identifying undervalued assets and leveraging personal initiative over traditional academic paths, a strategy that propelled his subsequent ventures in multifamily real estate.[14] This self-directed approach, bypassing formal university training, underscored his emphasis on practical experience and market acumen from the outset of his career.[13]

Professional Career

Founding and Early Development of CARROLL

CARROLL was established in 2004 by M. Patrick Carroll, with headquarters in Atlanta, Georgia, and an initial emphasis on acquiring and managing multifamily properties in Florida and Georgia.[8] The company's early operations centered on residential investments, including single-family homes and developments, before pivoting toward multifamily assets in response to the 2008 financial crisis, which increased demand for rental housing.[15] This strategic shift enabled CARROLL to target opportunities in the Sun Belt region, leveraging undervalued properties for long-term value creation.[15] In its formative years, CARROLL expanded through targeted acquisitions of property management firms to build integrated operations. By 2009, the company had acquired three such entities, including Affordable Realty Management, which oversaw 5,000 units, thereby scaling its asset management capabilities.[16][8] This infrastructure supported rapid portfolio growth, with further purchases in 2010 of Hediger Enterprises (managing 9,000 units) and Miles Properties Management (8,000 units), enhancing control over acquisitions, development, and operations.[8] By 2011, these efforts had positioned CARROLL to oversee more than 20,000 units nationwide, marking a key milestone in its transition from a regional player to a broader real estate operator focused on the Southeast and expanding Sun Belt markets.[8] Early development also incorporated retail assets alongside multifamily holdings, reflecting Carroll's approach to diversified residential and commercial investments.[16]

Expansion, Investments, and Key Milestones

Under M. Patrick Carroll's leadership, CARROLL expanded its multifamily real estate portfolio significantly, growing from initial acquisitions to managing assets valued at over $10 billion by 2021, encompassing more than 75 properties primarily along the East Coast.[15] The firm focused on value-add strategies, including renovations and repositioning of underperforming assets, which contributed to annual returns exceeding 20% for investors in early funds.[17] Key investments included a $200 million co-investment in Las Vegas multifamily assets with GFH Financial Group in September 2021, marking CARROLL's strategic push into western U.S. markets beyond its East Coast core.[18] In November 2021, CARROLL partnered with PGIM Real Estate to sell a multifamily portfolio, realizing gains from assets acquired and improved under its management.[19] By 2022, the company had raised over $340 million for its seventh investment fund through closings in July 2022 and December 2021, providing $5.5 billion in total buying power for further multifamily acquisitions and developments.[17] Milestones in CARROLL's growth included surpassing $6.9 billion in total real estate acquisitions by 2020, with over $3.3 billion in successful exits and more than $1.2 billion in developed or construction-managed projects.[20] The firm's cumulative transaction volume exceeded $12 billion by mid-2023, reflecting disciplined capital deployment and operational efficiencies in property management.[21] These achievements positioned CARROLL as a vertically integrated operator, handling acquisitions, asset management, and third-party services for institutional partners.[22]

Sale of CARROLL and Subsequent Ventures

In July 2023, M. Patrick Carroll agreed to sell CARROLL, the multifamily real estate platform he founded, to The RMR Group in an all-cash transaction valued at $80 million for 100% of the equity interests in MPC Partnership Holdings LLC, the entity operating as CARROLL.[21][1] The deal encompassed CARROLL's management of approximately 28,000 multifamily units across the southeastern United States, along with its third-party property management operations.[1][23] The acquisition closed on December 19, 2023, after which Carroll departed the firm, while CARROLL's approximately 700 employees and executives remained in place under RMR's oversight.[24][25][23] Following the sale, Carroll established Carroll Holdings, Inc., a Miami-based private investment firm specializing in real estate, private equity, and strategic opportunities.[4][26] The firm leverages Carroll's prior experience in acquiring over $12 billion in assets and executing nine-figure exits through platforms like CARROLL.[27] Details on specific investments or portfolio size under Carroll Holdings remain limited, as the entity operates privately with a focus on opportunistic deals rather than public disclosures.[4]

Philanthropic Activities

Establishment of Charitable Initiatives

In 2022, M. Patrick Carroll co-founded the Kickz for Kids initiative in partnership with Boys & Girls Clubs of America, aimed at distributing high-end sneakers to children in underserved communities.[28] The program targeted donations of Nike and Adidas footwear, with an initial $500,000 commitment providing shoes to over 1,000 children across locations including Miami, Tampa, Atlanta, New York, and Chicago, as part of a broader goal to reach $1 million in value through 10 affiliated clubs.[28] This effort built on Carroll's prior involvement as a board member of the Boys & Girls Clubs of Tampa Bay since 2018, where he contributed volunteer time and funds to youth development programs.[28] The following year, in 2023, Carroll established the Patrick Carroll Foundation as a dedicated entity to centralize his philanthropic efforts, focusing on financial aid for underprivileged children and families facing various needs.[28] The foundation serves as a vehicle for targeted support in areas such as health, education, and welfare, reflecting Carroll's shift toward structured giving following the sale of his real estate firm.[28] Through this initiative, Carroll has channeled significant donations, including $1 million to the Friends of the Israel Defense Forces in November 2023, underscoring its role in amplifying aid to specific global causes.[29] These establishments represent Carroll's transition from ad-hoc corporate donationsâ€”such as CARROLL's $250,000 contribution to HOPE Atlanta in September 2021 for homelessness preventionâ€”to formalized personal foundations and partnerships, prioritizing direct impact on youth and community resilience.[28]

Major Donations and Supported Causes

In September 2021, CARROLL, under Carroll's leadership, donated $250,000 to HOPE Atlanta, a nonprofit organization dedicated to preventing and alleviating homelessness in the Atlanta metropolitan area, with a commitment to provide an additional $250,000 over the subsequent five years.[30] Carroll established the Patrick Carroll Foundation in 2023, which focuses on delivering financial aid to underprivileged children and their families for needs including education, healthcare, and basic necessities, aligning with his emphasis on early childhood development.[28] A key initiative involved distributing sneakers to underprivileged children nationwide through the "Kickz for Kids" campaign, culminating in the donation of shoes valued at $1 million by October 2023, following events such as a $100,000 sneaker giveaway to Boys & Girls Clubs in November 2022.[31][32] In November 2023, Carroll donated $1 million to Friends of the Israel Defense Forces, supporting the organization's efforts to aid Israeli soldiers and their families amid ongoing conflicts.[29] Reports from 2022 indicated contributions exceeding $4 million overall, including $2.1 million to C.A.R.E. for Special Children, a nonprofit assisting families of children with special needs; however, a pledged $1.5 million auction bid to UNICEF for Ukrainian children was later withdrawn and not fulfilled.[33][34]

Legal Matters and Controversies

Criminal Arrests and Charges

In October 2023, Carroll was arrested in Miami, Florida, on felony battery charges after two employees at Gold Rush Cabaret accused him of assaulting them. The charges stemmed from an alleged physical altercation at the venue, though specific outcomes such as plea or trial details remain unresolved in public records.[35] On March 21, 2024, Miami Beach police responded to reports of gunshots at Carroll's waterfront property, where he had fired a shotgun from his boat; officers took him into custody for a mental health evaluation under Florida's Baker Act, but no immediate criminal charges were filed at the scene.[36] A related charge was later reduced to a misdemeanor, to which Carroll entered a not guilty plea, with a trial scheduled for August 2024.[36] Carroll faced a criminal contempt charge in Hillsborough County, Florida, for allegedly violating a 2019 divorce settlement agreement with his ex-wife, Lindsey Truex; in February 2023, a court found him guilty of indirect criminal contempt and imposed a sentence including time served.[37] [38] In June 2024, Truex obtained a 15-day temporary restraining order against Carroll from a Hillsborough County family court; he was subsequently charged with felony aggravated stalking for alleged violations, including harassing phone calls reported on July 16, 2024.[38] On October 19, 2024, Carroll was arrested in Lincoln County, Wyoming, on the outstanding warrant issued by Hillsborough County authorities.[38] The stalking charge was dismissed on November 1, 2024, after the state attorney determined the alleged calls occurred after the restraining order expired on July 12, 2024.[39] On July 1, 2024, Carroll was arrested in Los Angeles, California, by the LAPD Hollywood Division after leading officers on a vehicle pursuit, exiting his car, and attempting to flee on foot; he faced felony charges of evading a police officer and carrying a loaded firearm in public, with weapons recovered at the scene and possible DUI elements noted in reports.[35] [38] He appeared for a preliminary hearing on July 30, 2024.[6] In April 2025, during a pretrial hearing, the Los Angeles County District Attorney's Office agreed to drop the charges in exchange for Carroll entering a two-year mental health diversion program, requiring counseling compliance to avoid prosecution.[40]

Civil Lawsuits and Allegations

In 2021, Michael Lundy, the divorce attorney for Carroll's ex-wife Lindsey Truex, filed a defamation lawsuit against Carroll in Florida's Hillsborough County Circuit Court, alleging multiple counts of libel stemming from derogatory Instagram posts and other public statements that accused Lundy of professional misconduct and harassment during divorce proceedings.[41] The suit, amended in 2023 to add claims and seek punitive damages, claims Carroll's actions caused reputational harm and emotional distress; Carroll has denied the allegations, characterizing them as baseless attempts to interfere with his family matters.[42] Divorce proceedings between Carroll and Truex, finalized amid contentious claims, included civil allegations of domestic violence, with Truex citing a 2019 audio recording in which Carroll admitted to striking her while holding their child and choking her during an argument.[42] Truex obtained a domestic violence injunction in Florida Circuit Court in 2020, which merged into the divorce case; the court later found Carroll in violation of the order and child support obligations, though civil aspects focused on asset division and protective measures rather than standalone damages.[37] Separate allegations emerged of Carroll posting a video depicting Truex in a sexual act on Instagram in 2022, described by witnesses as revenge porn, potentially violating Florida's sexual cyberharassment laws; no independent civil suit on this claim was filed, but it factored into ongoing family court disputes, with Carroll securing a 2023 judicial order to remove the domain realmpatrickcarroll.com, which hosted related accusations.[37] In June 2023, Miguel Angel Weill, manager of the Miami restaurant Hiyakawa, sued Carroll for defamation in connection with an April 2023 incident captured on surveillance video, where Carroll allegedly spat on Weill after being confronted for aggressive behavior toward a patron.[43] Weill claimed Carroll's spokesperson falsely accused him of extortion by suggesting he provoked the altercation for financial gain; Carroll's attorney stated he would contest the claims in court, and the suit remains pending without service at the time of filing.[43] This marked the second defamation action against Carroll in two years. On January 24, 2025, former security guards Filberto Clemente and Jessie Davis filed a civil suit against Carroll in Los Angeles County Superior Court, alleging assault, battery, false imprisonment, and wrongful termination arising from a July 30, 2024, incident at Carroll's residence.[6] The complaint claims Carroll held them at gunpoint, threatened to shoot them, demanded their cell phones, and used racial slurs, creating a hostile environment; the suit seeks unspecified damages, while Carroll's representatives have denied the accusations and anticipate dismissal.[6] Earlier business-related litigation included Stanger v. Loetzerich (Texas, 2021), where receiver Derek Loetzerich named Carroll among defendants in claims of fraudulent conveyance, fraud, and civil conspiracy tied to asset transfers involving investor Rita Mullins; the case centered on alleged improper dealings in real estate entities, with appellate review upholding prior rulings but limited public details on Carroll's specific liability.[44] Carroll was represented in related matters by firms handling asset sales and disputes, though outcomes emphasized recovery for creditors over personal penalties.[45]

Mental Health Disclosures and Responses

In November 2024, M. Patrick Carroll publicly disclosed on Instagram that he had been diagnosed with bipolar disorder, stating that the condition had gone undiagnosed for years and contributed to behaviors that were out of character for him, particularly on social media.[46] He elaborated that he was receiving treatment for the illness following incidents that drew public and legal attention.[47] This disclosure came amid multiple episodes involving law enforcement scrutiny of his mental state. In March 2024, Carroll was ordered by police to undergo a mental health evaluation after posting social media videos depicting erratic behavior.[5] Similarly, following a November 2024 altercation at Miami Beach's LIV nightclubâ€”where he berated co-owner David Grutmanâ€”Miami Beach police subjected him to a three-day involuntary mental health evaluation under Florida's Baker Act provisions.[47] Carroll's bipolar diagnosis factored into responses to criminal charges stemming from a July 2024 arrest in Los Angeles, where he faced felony counts of carrying a loaded firearm in public and evading police during a high-speed chase described by media as reminiscent of the O.J. Simpson pursuit.[5] In April 2025, he pleaded not guilty and entered a pretrial diversion program, agreeing to two years of mental health counseling in lieu of prosecution, with charges to be dismissed upon successful completion.[48][40] Prosecutors cited his ongoing treatment as a mitigating factor, avoiding a trial that could have highlighted the role of his manic episode in the incident.[49] Subsequent civil litigation, including a February 2025 lawsuit alleging Carroll held security guards at gunpoint in July 2024, referenced his mental health disclosure as context for prior erratic actions, though it did not directly challenge the diagnosis.[6] Carroll has maintained that treatment is addressing the underlying issues, with no further public disclosures of additional diagnoses or relapses reported as of October 2025.[47]
