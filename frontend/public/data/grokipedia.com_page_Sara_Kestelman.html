Sara Kestelman

Sara Kestelman (born 12 May 1944) is an English actress,[1] known for her extensive theatre career, including Olivier Award-winning performances, as well as roles such as Lady Frances Brandon, Lady Jane Grey's mother, in the 1986 film Lady Jane, providing the voice of Kreia in Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic II: The Sith Lords (2004),[2] and recent television appearances in series like Slow Horses (2022) and The Chelsea Detective (2022).

Life and career

Kestelman was born in London, the daughter of Dorothy Mary (nÃ©e Creagh), a dress designer, and Morris Kestelman, an artist.[3][4] Her father was Jewish, from a family from Russia.[5] In 1994, she won a Laurence Olivier Theatre Award for her performance as Fraulein Schneider in Cabaret in the London revival of the show. She has performed with the Royal National Theatre and the Royal Shakespeare Company. Kestelman joined the latter in 1968 but left in 1973 when she had her first film role in Zardoz. In 1982, she played Lady Macbeth. Kestelman wrote a book of poetry, A Two Hander, with Susan Penhaligon. It was published by The Do-Not Press in 1996.[6] In recent years, she has appeared in the Apple TV+ series Slow Horses (2022â€“present) and is set to star in the 2025 film The Physician II.
