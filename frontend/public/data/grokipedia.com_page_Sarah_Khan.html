Sarah Khan

Sarah Falak (سارہ فلک), née Sarah Zafar Khan (سارہ ظفر خان) (born 14 July 1992), popularly known as Sarah Khan (سارہ خان), is a Pakistani actress who appears in Urdu-language television series. She made her acting debut with a supporting role in 2012 serial Badi Aapa and followed by other brief roles in several series. Khan is best known for her portrayal as Miraal in drama Sabaat and Zohra in drama Raqs e Bismil. She gained further acclaim for her roles in Hum Tum (2022) and the web series Abdullahpur Ka Devdas (2024). She also played the role of Farisa in the romantic Alvida (2015) and later appeared as Saba in the mystery drama Mohabbat Aag Si (2015). She subsequently portrayed leading roles in the romantic drama Tum Meri Ho (2016), Tumhare Hain and the black magic-based Nazr-e-Bad (both 2017), Belapur Ki Dayan (2018).

Early life

Khan was born on 14 July 1992, in Medina, Saudi Arabia to a Lebanese mother and a Pakistani Pashtun father of Yusufzai tribe.[1] The family moved to Pakistan when she was 10 years old.[2] She has two sisters and one brother, including Noor Zafar Khan who is also an actress, Aisha Zafar Khan, and Hamza Zafar Khan.[3]

Career

Khan made her acting debut in a supporting role in the 2012 serial Badi Aapa, aired on Hum TV, where she played the daughter of the main characters. In the same year, she appeared in the telenovela serial Mirat-ul-Uroos, broadcast on Geo TV. She played the role of Javeria a.k.a. Joey alongside Mikaal Zulfiqar, Ahsan Khan, Samina Ahmad, and Ayesha Khan. She followed with supporting roles in successful television series, including the 2014 soap series Bhool alongside Sanam Chaudhry, Behroze Sabzwari, and Fazila Qazi. Her breakthrough came with the negative role of the selfish opportunist Farisa in the 2015 romantic drama Alvida alongside Sanam Jung and Imran Abbas Naqvi. The drama aired on Hum TV, earning her a nomination for Best Supporting Actress at the 4th Hum Awards. In the same year, she portrayed the brave housewife Saba in the drama Mohabbat Aag Si alongside Azfar Rehman, winning the Hum Award for Best Supporting Actress. She subsequently took on leading roles, including in the romantic drama Tumhare Hain Tum Meri Ho (2016) and the black magic-based Nazr-e-Bad (2017). In 2018, Khan played the lead role of Tasha in Belapur Ki Dayan alongside Adnan Siddiqui and Amar Khan. She received praise for portraying Zohra in Raqs e Bismil (2020). Khan earned further recognition for playing the arrogant Miraal in Sabaat (2020) alongside Usman Mukhtar.[4][5] For her performance, she won the PISA Award for Best Television Actress (Popular).[6] In 2022, Khan played the psychology student Maha Qutub ud-Din in the Ramadan special Hum Tum opposite Junaid Khan.[7][8] She then played Anum in Wabaal alongside Talha Chahour.[9] Subsequent roles include Namak Haram (2023), the web series Abdullahpur Ka Devdas (2024), and the title role of Fajar in Sher (2025) opposite Danish Taimoor.[10][11][12]

Personal life

Khan married singer and songwriter, Falak Shabir in July 2020,[13] a few weeks after their engagement.[14][15] On 8 October 2021, Shabir announced the birth of their daughter, Alyana Falak.[16]

Other work and media image

Khan has also appeared as a guest on various shows. In 2015, she played Saman in Maana Ka Gharana. She appeared in an episode of Kitni Girhain Baaki Hain: Part 2 (2017) and in an episode of Ustani Jee (2018).[17] In 2018, she appeared as a guest in the second season of the talkshow, The After Moon Show. Khan became the tenth most searched person on Google in Pakistan in 2020.[18] In 2022, Khan was placed in "Best Pakistani Actresses" list, for her performance in Hum Tum.[19] Khan was placed in Hello Pakistan's HOT100 list, in the "Trailblazers" category, in the same year.[20] In 2025, she received a nomination at the 10th HUM Awards and appeared on the red carpet, enhancing her media presence.[21]
