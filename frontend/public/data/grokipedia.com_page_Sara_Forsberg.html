Sara Forsberg


Sara Maria Forsberg (born 2 May 1994) is a Finnish singer, songwriter, YouTuber, and television presenter recognized for her viral online videos demonstrating vocal impressions of multiple languages and musical styles.[1][2] Born in Jakobstad, Finland, to a Swedish-born father, Forsberg gained international attention in 2014 with a YouTube video titled "Total Eclipse of the Heart," in which she performed the song using invented linguistic sounds mimicking various languages, amassing millions of views and leading to appearances on shows like The Ellen DeGeneres Show.[3][4] Her YouTube channel, featuring original music, covers, and personal content, has surpassed one million subscribers, earning her Silver and Gold Play Buttons from the platform.[5] A notable achievement in her career includes developing the "Jablogian" alien language spoken by the Kanjiklub gang in the 2015 film Star Wars: The Force Awakens, showcasing her linguistic creativity beyond music.[6][7] Forsberg has also hosted Finnish award ceremonies and television programs, while releasing pop and R&B music under stage names like SAARA.[8] Her work emphasizes multilingual vocal experimentation and self-taught language skills, drawing from her bilingual upbringing in Finland's Swedish-speaking community.[9]

Early life

Family and upbringing

Sara Forsberg was born Sara Maria Forsberg on May 2, 1994, in Pietarsaari (also known as Jakobstad), Finland.[2] Her mother is Finnish and her father Swedish, the latter fluent in Finnish despite his origins.[2] Her parents were Baptist missionaries, resulting in an upbringing marked by frequent international travel during her early years, including a period living in Texas.[2] [10] The family eventually settled in Finland when Forsberg was six, after which they made additional moves within the country.[11] This nomadic lifestyle, driven by her parents' missionary commitments, exposed her to multiple languages and cultures from a young age, contributing to her multilingual proficiency in Finnish (her native tongue), Swedish, and English.[12]

Education and initial talents

Forsberg was born on May 2, 1994, in Jakobstad, Finland, to a Swedish-speaking family, growing up trilingual in Swedish, Finnish, and English. She displayed early musical aptitude, initially expressing interest in learning the double bass before her father introduced her to the cello, an instrument she quickly adopted with enthusiasm.[2] During her youth, Forsberg attended a music academy in Jakobstad for several years, honing her skills in performance and composition. She graduated from Finnish high school circa 2013â€“2014, after which she took a gap year working as a waitress while studying culinary arts at chef school.[10][13][14] Her innate talents extended beyond formal training to include exceptional mimicry of languages, accents, and speech patternsâ€”such as Japanese, Estuary English, and California-accented Americanâ€”often demonstrated through improvised vocal imitations without fluency in those tongues. She also exhibited versatility in replicating diverse musical genres and sounds using her voice and minimal instrumentation, skills rooted in self-directed experimentation rather than structured pedagogy.[15]

Rise to prominence

YouTube breakthrough

Forsberg uploaded her breakthrough YouTube video, titled "What Languages Sound Like To Foreigners," on March 3, 2014, to her channel originally operated under the username Smoukahontas.[16][17] In the roughly three-minute clip, recorded impromptu during a day off from work, she delivered improvised gibberish monologues imitating the phonetic patterns, intonation, and rhythms of 11 languagesâ€”French, Spanish, Italian, Russian, Chinese, Japanese, Swedish, Arabic, German, Finnish, and Englishâ€”while claiming fluency in only Swedish, Finnish, and English, with phrases mostly fabricated except for select authentic examples.[16][13][14] The video rapidly gained traction, accumulating over 8 million views within ten days of upload.[13] By March 20, 2014, viewership surpassed 9 million, propelling Forsberg to overnight online fame as viewers praised her mimicry skills despite the non-literal content.[18] A follow-up video, "What Languages Sound Like To Foreigners #2," released on March 5, 2014, addressed viewer questions and expanded on the concept, sustaining the momentum.[19] This viral success represented Forsberg's pivotal entry into YouTube stardom, shifting her from prior low-engagement contentâ€”such as her channel's debut video "From ugly to pretty" in August 2013â€”to a platform where her linguistic and performative talents resonated globally, setting the stage for subsequent professional opportunities.[4][6]

Initial media attention

Forsberg's YouTube video "What Languages Sound Like to Foreigners", uploaded on March 3, 2014, quickly amassed over 8 million views by March 13, drawing early coverage from Finnish public broadcaster Yle, which highlighted her improvised gibberish imitations mimicking various languages as a spontaneous vlog that unexpectedly went viral.[13] The video's rapid spread transformed Forsberg, then 19, into a local celebrity in her hometown of Jakobstad (Pietarsaari), with media noting the organic, unpolished nature of the content produced on a whim.[13] By March 19, 2014, the video had surpassed 9 million views in just two weeks, prompting international wire service coverage from the Associated Press, which detailed her rising fame and impending move to the United States to capitalize on the buzz, including potential music industry interest.[20] Similar reports appeared in outlets like Global News and SBS, emphasizing the video's appeal through Forsberg's multilingual impressions despite her not being fluent in most depicted languages.[21][22] This momentum led to her first major U.S. television exposure on The Ellen DeGeneres Show in April 2014, where she performed live language and musical genre parodies, further amplifying her profile as Yle reported on the baffling yet entertaining nature of her acts to American audiences.[4] Early radio interviews, including on BBC Radio around March 12 and Finnish Yle on March 14, focused on her background and the viral mechanics, marking the onset of broader media scrutiny into her talents beyond YouTube.[23]

Music career

Signing with Capitol and early releases

In July 2014, at the age of 20, Forsberg signed a worldwide recording contract with Capitol Records, a subsidiary of Universal Music Group.[24] The deal positioned her for international exposure, with acclaimed producer Rodney Jerkins appointed as executive producer to oversee her debut material.[24] To align with production demands, she relocated from Finland to Los Angeles, California.[1] Forsberg's first release under the contract came in 2015, adopting the mononymic stage name SAARA for her music career. Her debut single, "Ur Cool"â€”a pop track co-written with Jerkins and othersâ€”premiered on April 21, 2015.[25] The song, characterized by its upbeat, ironic lyrics targeting online admirers, quickly gained traction in Finland, debuting at number one on the national iTunes singles chart and Spotify Top 50 while peaking in the top ten on broader download and airplay charts.[10] Internationally, it received modest streaming attention but marked her transition from viral YouTube content to professional recordings, though no full-length album followed during this period.[26]

Departure from label and independent phase

In February 2016, Forsberg departed from Capitol Records following the exit of her primary producer, Rodney Jerkins, from the label, which left her in a contractual limbo without ongoing support for her projects.[10] She detailed the circumstances in a YouTube video titled "I Left My Record Deal," uploaded on March 6, 2016, explaining that the lack of alignment with the label's direction and the producer's departure prompted her decision to seek greater creative control.[27] This move allowed her to avoid prolonged constraints under the existing agreement, though it meant forgoing major-label resources.[28] Post-departure, Forsberg transitioned to independent music production, maintaining an affiliation with Universal Music Group for distribution while handling much of her creative and business aspects autonomously.[29] In 2017, she released several singles, including "Permission to Love" in February, "Superpowers" in March, and "All the Love" featuring Jillionaire in October, which showcased her shift toward self-directed pop tracks emphasizing personal empowerment and relationships.[3] Additional independent outputs included "California," "The Urge," and "Sangria," reflecting a continued focus on upbeat, multilingual-infused pop without the oversight of a full-service label.[30] She also issued her debut EP, Hello I'm Saara, compiling select tracks and affirming her pivot to indie operations.[31] This phase emphasized Forsberg's versatility as a multi-instrumentalist and songwriter, enabling quicker releases tied to her YouTube audience rather than traditional promotional cycles.[30]

Acting and media appearances

Film and voice work

Forsberg debuted in film acting with a supporting role as Natalya in the 2016 action-comedy Skiptrace, directed by Renny Harlin and starring Jackie Chan and Johnny Knoxville.[1][32] In the same year, she provided the voice for the alien character Kalara in Star Trek Beyond, contributing to the Universal Translator sequences spoken by the extraterrestrial figure.[1][33][34] These roles marked her initial forays into on-screen and voice performance, leveraging her multilingual abilities developed through her YouTube content.[35] No further film or voice acting credits have been documented as of 2025.[1]

Television roles and presenting

Forsberg served as a judge on the fifth season of Talent Suomi, the Finnish version of the Got Talent franchise, which aired in 2016 alongside judges Sami Hedberg and Jari SillanpÃ¤Ã¤.[10][36] In February 2017, she began hosting Remotely Funny, a British children's game show on CBBC in which contestants participated remotely via video link to complete challenges for prizes, with Forsberg directing tasks and awarding "Samojis" for humorous efforts; the program aired its first season through April 2017 and a second in 2018.[37][10] Forsberg also co-hosted the Finnish program Kaikki Vastaan 1 during its 2017 season, a competitive format pitting teams against a single opponent.[27]

Other professional endeavors

Writing contributions

Forsberg serves as a songwriter and composer for her own releases and collaborations with other artists. As SAARA, she wrote and composed tracks including the single "Ur Cool," released on April 21, 2015, and material on her debut EP Hello I'm Saara (May 27, 2016), which reached the top ten in Finland.[38][25] She also penned songs for her follow-up EP Almost Acoustic (May 2018).[38] In collaborations, Forsberg co-wrote "My Heart Wants Me Dead," performed by Lisa Ajax for the Swedish selection competition Melodifestivalen 2016, sharing credits with Linnea Deb, Joy Deb, Anton HÃ¥rd af Segerstad, and Nikki Flores; the track placed seventh in the final.[39][40] She received writing credit for "You Think" by Girls' Generation, featured on their fifth Korean studio album Lion Heart (August 2015).[41] Forsberg has extended her writing to K-pop and international projects, contributing lyrics and composition to tracks that achieved commercial success, including platinum certification in South Korea, as detailed in her personal account of entering the industry.[42] Beyond music, she invented an original alien language for use in Star Wars: The Force Awakens (2015), developing vocabulary and phonetic elements spoken by extraterrestrial characters.[6]

Collaborations and side projects

Forsberg has collaborated as a songwriter and composer on tracks for various artists, including several K-pop releases. She received co-writing credit for "You Think" by Girls' Generation, featured on their fourth studio album Lion Heart, released on August 19, 2015.[43] She also co-wrote "The 7th Sense" for NCT U, a single debut released on April 9, 2016, which she discussed in a 2020 YouTube video detailing her entry into K-pop songwriting.[44][42] Additional credits include composing NCT Dream's "Quiet Down," from their first full-length album Reload, released on April 29, 2020.[45] In the Eurovision context, Forsberg co-wrote "My Heart Wants Me Dead," performed by Lisa Ajax as Sweden's entry in Melodifestivalen 2016, held on February 27, 2016.[43] She contributed lyrics and composition to Taemin's "Drip Drop," from his second studio album Move, released on October 16, 2017.[43] As a featured vocalist, she appeared on "Vauvoja" by Finnish artist Kasmir, a single released on May 15, 2015, which peaked at number one on the Finnish charts.[45] Another feature credit is on "Can't Buy Love" by THRDL!FE, released in 2017.[45] Beyond music, Forsberg launched the clothing line FRSBRG in May 2015, promoting it through videos targeted at her Finnish audience and involving endorsements from local celebrities.[3] This venture represented an early side project diversifying her professional output during her transition to Los Angeles-based work.

Public reception and controversies

Achievements and acclaim

Forsberg's YouTube video "What Languages Sound Like to Foreigners," uploaded on March 3, 2014, achieved viral success, accumulating over 22 million views and establishing her reputation for multilingual mimicry and vocal versatility.[16] This exposure prompted a worldwide recording contract with Capitol Records in July 2014, marking a significant milestone in her transition from online content creator to professional artist.[24] Her debut single "Ur Cool," released on April 21, 2015, peaked at number 10 on the Finnish singles chart, demonstrating early commercial viability in her home market.[10] Beyond music, Forsberg contributed to Star Wars: The Force Awakens (2015) by devising an original alien language, earning recognition for her linguistic creativity in a major film production.[6] Forsberg's main YouTube channel reached 1 million subscribers by November 2017, qualifying her for YouTube's Gold Play Button award for creator milestones, with the channel sustaining approximately 1.13 million subscribers as of recent data.[46] She received acclaim for her judging role on the fifth season of Talent Suomi (Finland's Got Talent) in 2016, where her expertise in performance and languages was highlighted by producers.[27] These accomplishments underscore her acclaim as a multifaceted entertainer, though she has not received major formal music industry awards such as Grammys or equivalent Finnish honors.

Criticisms and personal struggles

Forsberg has encountered few public criticisms in her career, with most commentary focusing positively on her creativity and multilingual talents rather than substantive professional detractors. Her work has occasionally drawn minor online discourse, such as debates over tattoo choices, but these lack organized backlash or credible institutional critique. A primary personal struggle occurred after her 2017 departure from Capitol Records, during which Forsberg gained approximately 50 pounds (23 kg) amid emotional turmoil. She attributed this to "crippling social anxiety that led to loneliness that led to depression," describing a cascade where professional setbacks exacerbated isolation and mental health decline, prompting a shift to independent pursuits.[47] In December 2023, Forsberg detailed regrets over her tattoos in a YouTube video, highlighting dissatisfaction with their appearance, poor healingâ€”particularly fine-line designs fading or blurringâ€”and an overall sense that they no longer aligned with her self-image, leading her to consider removal.[48] She has also navigated challenges from Swyer syndrome, a rare gonadal dysgenesis diagnosed in her teenage years, resulting in a 46,XY karyotype despite female phenotype and requiring lifelong hormone replacement therapy due to non-functional gonads. Forsberg publicly disclosed this in October 2023, stating she is "genetically male" and addressing implications like infertility, while expressing ongoing desires for biological motherhood complicated by the condition's effects on reproductive health.[49] Further personal difficulties surfaced in April 2024, when Forsberg explained a content hiatus in a video titled "Sorry for not uploading, my life fell apart," alluding to undisclosed upheavals that disrupted her routine and output, though she emphasized resilience in resuming creative work.[50]

Discography

Extended plays

Sara Forsberg's debut extended play, Hello I'm Saara, was released on May 27, 2016, comprising three tracks: "California" (3:36), "The Urge", and "Honey" featuring Rich The Kid.[51][31] The EP marked her transition from viral YouTube covers to original pop material, following her 2015 single "Ur Cool".[52] Her second extended play, Almost Acoustic, followed on May 2, 2018â€”Forsberg's birthdayâ€”and features five semi-acoustic tracks emphasizing a laid-back vibe: "Temple" (2:51), "I Do" (3:45), "Sever" (2:33), "Blood" featuring Kirko Bangz, and "Temple (RZY Mix)".[53][54][55] The release totaled 15 minutes in duration and served as a fan-oriented project independent of major label backing.[56]

Singles as lead artist

SAARA released her debut single "Ur Cool" on April 21, 2015, through Capitol Records.[57] The track marked her first release as a signed artist following her deal with the label in 2014.[58] In 2017, SAARA issued several singles independently after parting ways with Capitol. "Permission to Love" appeared on February 3, 2017, via Universal Music Oy.[59] This was followed by "Superpowers" later that year, co-written by SAARA, Teemu Brunila, and Hank Solo.[60] "All the Love", featuring Jillionaire, was released in October 2017.[38] Additional singles include "Sangria" in 2018, which appeared as a standalone release.[61] These tracks reflect SAARA's shift toward self-produced pop material, often shared via digital platforms.

Featured appearances and writing credits

Forsberg provided featured vocals on "Vauvoja" by Finnish singer Kasmir, released as a single on May 15, 2015.[62][63] Among her songwriting credits for other artists, Forsberg co-composed "You Think" for Girls' Generation's fifth studio album Lion Heart, released August 19, 2015.[64] She received co-writing credit for "My Heart Wants Me Dead" by Lisa Ajax, performed at Sweden's Melodifestivalen on February 28, 2016.[40] Forsberg co-wrote "Drip Drop" for Taemin's second studio album Press It, released February 22, 2016. Additional credits include co-composition of "Rookie" from Red Velvet's mini-album Rookie, released February 1, 2017,[65] and "Starlight" (featuring Dean) from Taeyeon's EP Why, released June 25, 2016. She also contributed to Namie Amuro's single "Hero", released August 5, 2016.[66]

Filmography

Film roles

Forsberg's first film appearance was in the 2016 action-comedy Skiptrace, directed by Renny Harlin, in which she played Natalya, the kidnapped daughter of a Russian gangster portrayed by Arnold Schwarzenegger's character. The film features Jackie Chan as a Hong Kong detective partnering with Johnny Knoxville's American gambler to rescue her, blending martial arts sequences with comedic elements during its production in locations including Hong Kong, Mongolia, and Las Vegas. In the same year, Forsberg provided the voice for the Kalara Alien, an antagonistic character who defects to Starfleet but is revealed as a traitor, in Star Trek Beyond, the third installment in the rebooted franchise directed by Justin Lin.[67] Her role involved motion capture and voice work for the CGI alien species, contributing to the film's ensemble cast led by Chris Pine as Captain Kirk amid interstellar conflicts. These credits represent her limited on-screen acting work to date, primarily leveraging her vocal talents developed through music and YouTube performances.[1]
