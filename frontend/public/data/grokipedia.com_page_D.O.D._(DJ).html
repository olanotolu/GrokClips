D.O.D. (DJ)

Daniel O'Donnell (born 5 April 1995), better known as D.O.D, is a British DJ and record producer.[1] He debuted at number 96 on the DJ Mag Top 100 DJs list.2

D.O.D

Biography

Born in Bury, Greater Manchester, D.O.D (Daniel O'Donnell) began DJing at local bars and clubs from the age of 15. He gained his first major break in 2010 by winning a Mixmag DJ competition, which led to an Ibiza residency.[2][3] Began releasing music in the early 2010s, D.O.D was supported in his early career by renowned artists such as Laidback Luke, with Mixmash Records hosting most of D.O.D's productions.[4] A collaboration between him and Laidback Luke, called "Flashing Lights", was released in April 2014.[5] D.O.D performed at Ultra Europe in 2016. D.O.D released "Blip" on 7 November 2016, with which he decided to define his own genre. He dubbed this new sound "Future Jack".[6] The song was primarily characterised by its heavy electronic sounds and repetitive nature. Many of the sounds are reminiscent of a song he released earlier that year called "Taking You Back", which Afrojack worked on as well. His following releases "Don't Wait" with Tommy Trash and "Ghetto" continued this sound. In 2019, D.O.D entered the DJ Mag Top 100 DJs poll at number 96, marking his debut in the ranking.[7][8] Following the Future Jack era, D.O.D evolved his sound towards bass house and piano house, signing releases with labels including Axtone and Spinnin' Records. His 2021 single "Still Sleepless" with Carla Monroe became his biggest hit to date, peaking at number 8 on the UK Singles Chart and earning platinum certification from the BPI.[9][10][11] Subsequent releases like "So Much In Love" (2023, UK #15, platinum) and "Somedays" with Sonny Fodera and Jazzy (2024, UK #5, certified silver) further solidified his commercial success, amassing over 500 million streams across platforms.[9][12][13] As of 2025, D.O.D continues to tour globally, with sold-out headline shows and festival appearances, while maintaining a prolific output of house music productions.[14]
