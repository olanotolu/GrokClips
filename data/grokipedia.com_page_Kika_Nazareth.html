Kika Nazareth

In this Portuguese name, the first or maternal family name is Ramos Ribeiro and the second or paternal family name is Nazareth Sousa. Francisca Ramos Ribeiro Nazareth Sousa (born 17 November 2002), commonly known as Kika or Kika Nazareth ,[1] is a Portuguese professional footballer who plays as a forward or an attacking midfielder for Liga F club Barcelona and the Portugal national team.

Club career

Benfica

Nazareth made her senior debut for Benfica at the age of 16 and signed her first professional contract with the club on 7 October 2020, at the age of 17.[5] On the 2023–24 season, Nazareth was part of the historical Benfica team that won all four domestic competitions: the Supertaça de Portugal (Portuguese Supercup, third in the club's history), the Taça da Liga (Portuguese League Cup, fourth in the club's history), the Campeonato Nacional (Portuguese League, fourth in the club's history), and the Taça de Portugal (Portuguese National Cup, second in the club's history).[6] This was the first time a women's team in Portugal managed this feat, and it is yet to be replicated as of the 2024–25 season. Nazareth was forced to miss two months of play after sustaining an injury against Valadares Gaia in early January 2024.[7] She returned in March[8] and quickly took back her spot in the starting lineup. Across all competitions (including the Champions League), Nazareth played 41 matches, scoring 26 goals and providing 10 assists.[9]

Barcelona

On 4 July 2024,[10] Nazareth completed a move to Spanish giants FC Barcelona for €500,000, a record fee for the women's football scene in Portugal.[11] She made her official debut in a 3–0 win against Deportivo de La Coruña on 8 September 2024, and scored her first official goal in a 4–1 win over Levante on 20 October 2024.[12][13] Nazareth secured her first piece of silverware with Barcelona on 26 January 2025, when the culers defeated their eternal rivals Real Madrid 5–0 to claim their 5th Spanish Supercup.[14] On the second leg of the Copa de la Reina semi-final against Real Madrid on 12 March 2025, Nazareth suffered a serious ankle injury to her left medial collateral ligament, forcing her to be subbed off at half-time.[15] This injury required surgery on 19 March 2025 and sidelined her for approximately four months, marking the end of her 2024–25 club season and initially raising doubts about her participation in UEFA Women's Euro 2025. From the sidelines, however, Nazareth saw Barcelona win a record sixth consecutive Liga F (Primera División) (10th overall)[16] and second consecutive Copa de la Reina (5th in six years, 11th overall),[17] adding two more medals to her personal cabinet. She recovered in time to feature for Portugal at Euro 2025. Nazareth returned to action for Barcelona in the 2025–26 season, making substitute appearances in Liga F and UEFA Women's Champions League matches in August and September 2025. As of October 2025, she has contributed 2 goals in 8 appearances across all competitions.[18]

Kika Nazareth International Career

International career

Nazareth made her debut for the Portugal national team on 4 March 2020, coming on as a substitute for Carolina Mendes against Italy in the 2020 Algarve Cup.[3][19] She scored her first goals for the Portugal national team on 22 June 2022 in a friendly match against Greece, scoring two goals in the first half in a 4-0 win for Portugal. On 30 May 2023, she was included in the 23-player squad for the FIFA Women's World Cup 2023.[20] On 24 June 2025, Nazareth was called up to the Portugal squad for the UEFA Women's Euro 2025.[21] She featured in two group stage matches, logging 174 minutes and providing 1 assist but no goals, as Portugal finished third in Group B with a 0–5 loss to Spain, a 1–1 draw against Italy, and a 1–2 defeat to Belgium, failing to advance to the knockout rounds.
