M-Girls (Chinese: 四个女生) was a Malaysian girl group consisting of Queenzy Cheng, Angeline Khoo, Cass Chin and Crystal Ong. The group was formed in 2001. The group released numerous studio albums, primarily under Starmedia Entertainment from 2009 onward, featuring festive pop arrangements of traditional Mandopop and Hokkien pop songs. Their albums saw distribution across Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, and Chinese diaspora markets.[1] Although M-Girls studio albums have been officially released in many different parts of the world, the group has been a longstanding victim to piracy and unlicensed, illegal distribution significantly impacted revenue, with Starmedia estimating 80% of their albums were illegally reproduced during peak years.[2] As a result, the group's music company, Starmedia, announced in late 2013 that they would cease producing M-Girls albums after the Chinese New Year album True Happiness (真歡喜, released for 2014 CNY), following the October 2013 EP, My Way.[3] However, they released one additional CNY album, New Spring Holiday (新春佳期, for 2015 CNY), through Starmedia in 2014. Afterwards, the group parted ways with Starmedia and established their own company, MG Entertainment. This new company's first album release was entitled The New Year is Coming (年來了, for 2016 CNY), which came out in 2015. They followed with one more album under MG Entertainment in 2016 (for 2017 CNY). Following their 2017 album, the members of M-Girls have been on hiatus from group activities, pursuing solo projects. In November 2023, Cheng died at the age of 37 from a ruptured brain aneurysm while filming content for the Squad Sekawan social media channel.[4] The remaining members paid tribute to her, but the group remains inactive as of 2025.

Career

Pre-debut

Prior to the group's formation, all members originally were under Ya-Ko Enterprise, with Khoo debuting in 7-member girl group known as Seven Fairies (七仙女).

2001–2005: Debut and Chin's departure

M-Girls debuted in 2001 with the Chinese New Year Song, "Happily Welcoming the Harvest Year " (開心迎接豐收年). It set sales records and sold over 50,000 copies, receiving platinum status.[5] In 2004, M-Girls released a Chinese New Year album with Four Golden Princess titled "Flowers Blossom in the Spring" (春風催花開). Their title music video set a new Malaysia Book of Records at the time for the most number of lion dancers in a music video with 108 lion dancers. The former record holding music video had 88 which was shot around two years before. In 2005, with the release of "Happy Year" (開心年) which was filmed in Australia, Chin announced that she would leave the group to concentrate on her studies.[6]

2005–2012: "Nile River" and Cheng's departure

After Chin left, M-Girls released "Nile River " (尼罗河) in 2005,[7] becoming their last mandopop album release until "My Way".[8] After the release of their 2010 Chinese New Year album "Abundant Wealth" (金玉满堂), Cheng left the group following the end of her contract with the company.[6] The group went on hiatus from 2010 to 2013.

2013: Reunion and My Way

In 2013, the four original members reunited and released the EP My Way in November, marking their return to mandopop. They also issued the Chinese New Year album Reunion (團聚).[6]

2014: "True Happiness", "New Spring Holiday", appearance on Nick & Stella & "The New Year Is Coming"

In February 2014, their Chinese New Year album True Happiness (真歡喜) was released by Starmedia. During the release, Starmedia announced plans to cease producing M-Girls albums due to piracy severely impacting sales, though one additional album followed in 2015.[1][2] After mass online speculation, it was revealed that they would release the Chinese New Year 2015 album New Spring Holiday (新春佳期). For the first time, M-Girls covered an English song called Happy CNY, and a Hokkien song called 發大財, which translates to "make a fortune".[9] Production for their 2016 Chinese New Year album began in 2015. Angeline and Crystal made an appearance on Nick and Stella Chung's CNY album, New Year Reunion (新年團圓).[10] The album The New Year is Coming (年來了) was released in 2016 under their new company, MG Entertainment. Crystal Ong departed the group after this release. In 2017, following the release of their final group album Red New Year (過年要紅紅) with Queenzy, Angeline, and Cass, it was announced that M-Girls would take a break to focus on solo activities.[11]
