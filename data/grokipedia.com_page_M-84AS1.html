M-84AS1

The M-84AS1 and M-84AS2[7][8][9] are substantially modernized versions of the M-84 main battle tank designed by the Military Technical Institute and produced by Technical Overhauling Institute "Čačak" in Serbia. The first version designated as M-84AS1 was presented in 2017. Later revisions feature numerous improvements and were presented in 2020 and 2021. Latest revisions of the AS1 and AS2 incorporate newly domestically developed modular explosive reactive armour, a modern battlefield management and fire-control system, a remote controlled weapon station and other updates.[10] A total of 21 subsystems are updated with modern solutions. AS1 and AS2 are developed in parallel and differ in the number of implemented subsystems and some specific turret modification regarding RCWS, the commander hatch and equipment implementation. The AS2 variant is intended for use as a platoon commander's tank in the modernization program, with limited units entering service as of 2025.[2] As of 2025, limited numbers of M-84AS1 and AS2 have entered service with the Serbian Army, with ongoing modernization efforts and the recent unveiling of the M-84AS3 variant.[2] [11]

Design and Development

History

The imperative for modernization of Serbia's aging M-84 fleet presented the Military Technical Institute Belgrade and Serbian Defense Industry with numerous tasks and challenges. Serbia inherited some 212 M-84 from Yugoslavia of which most never experienced any kind of modernization since their adoption. Though a modernization program for the M-84 called M-84AB1 (later renamed to M-84AS) has been presented in 2004 by Serbia featuring several russian weapon systems, it has never seen mass adoption and only one prototype was built, probably due to the financial and political situation of Serbia at that time. Since then tank designs around the world saw a number of fundamental improvements like the incorporation of explosive reactive armour, updated subsystems and optics or even active protection systems. It became evident that even the M-84AS modernization was obsolete and a completely new modernization program was needed to bring the M-84 to a level where it could keep up in terms of effectiveness and protection.

M-84AS1 (2017)

The first version of M-84AS1 from 2017 had several changes and improvements compared to the M-84A and basic M-84 including original model of T-72. Domestic first-generation explosive reactive armour was applied (comparable to Kontakt-5), covering a bigger area of the front turret and a sizeable portion of the front plate, providing better protection. A new fire-control system with domestic laser and radar warning systems was implemented along with a 12.7mm remotely controlled weapon station and a soft active protection suite. The back of the hull and turret is further fitted with cage armour while armour plates attached to the frontal hull remained. Although these changes were an improvement over the previous M-84, this design still had major flaws, especially when it comes to protection. It was criticized for the low reactive armor coverage, bad sensor placements and low situational awareness.[12] The weaknesses of this initial version were recognized and further development of the modernization package commenced. The result was a revised M-84AS1 which was presented in 2020.

M-84AS1 (2020)

The M-84AS1 from 2020 corrected many of the flaws that were found on the previous version from 2017.[13][14] The turret and hull are almost completely covered with domestic second-generation explosive reactive armour M19 (also known as ERO-19), providing increased protection from modern threats like top-attack or tandem-charge missiles according to the designers. A total of nine new subsystems are integrated.[15] Among them an improved fire-control system with an integrated day/night sight and new sensors. New forms of passive and Active protection system are developed. Protection against Top attack[16] missiles like FGM-148 Javelin is increased by using better composition of modular Explosive reactive armour that protects top of turret and soft kill Active protection system that is using Smoke grenade with Infrared smokes to create Smoke screen in order to block missile guidance. Day/night, thermal cameras and commander scope with 6 low light cameras[17] with other sensors increase Situation awareness of tank crew. Early warning sensors for detection of radar,[18] laser designation[19] and launching of rockets are coupled with automatic delivery of Smoke grenade towards threat to achieve protection. Second generation (comparable to third generation in other countries) of domestic Explosive reactive armour ERO-19 better than Kontakt-5 and comparable with Relikt is developed to protect against newer tandem charge HEAT warheads. Additional slat armor for protection against rocket-propelled grenades is also present.[20] Automatic fire extinguisher[21] capable to react in milliseconds with modern UV sensors for fire detection is added to stop explosions in tank compartments. New type of RCWS with 12.7mm machine-gun is added so commander can operate safely inside turret.[10] In total about 21 new subsystems are developed to increase survivability, mobility and precision of M-84AS1 and M-84AS2, with the M-84AS1 incorporating the first nine and the M-84AS2 adding twelve more including specific turret modifications regarding the RCWS, commander hatch, and equipment implementation.[22] CBRN defense is increased with new sensors and automation process.[23][24] New tank gun 125mm[25] with modern thermal sleeve will be produced in Borbeni složeni sistemi factory and new ammunition[26][22] with better penetration and explosive characteristics. The APFSDS rounds issued to the tank feature enhanced penetration capabilities.[27] As of 2024, the modernization program has seen a gradual rollout, with approximately 20 M-84AS2 units entering service.[2]

Production

As of 2025, the Technical Overhauling Institute "Čačak" leads the modernization program under the guidance of the Military Technical Institute in Belgrade, with operational M-84AS1 and AS2 tanks in service and production extending to the M-84AS3 variant.[28] Numerous companies participate in production of many tank subsystems. Effectiveness

Many improvements adopted for the M-84AS1 and M-84AS2 in Serbia's domestic modernization program have resulted in specifications that surpass some other current T-72 upgrade programs. While the basic armor of some T-72 versions is superior to that of the baseline M-84, the additional newer and better-placed modular explosive reactive armor[22] provides enhanced coverage and protection, and new systems offer improved situational awareness along with compatible ammunition types. The new tank variant features an improved engine over a regular T-72 (increased from 780 to 1,200 horsepower)[4], providing an overall mobility edge compared to most other modernized T-72 variants. These enhancements are theoretical, as the M-84AS1 has not entered serial production or combat as of 2025. In terms of protection, it is often considered superior to the Russian T-72B3 counterpart.[41][42]
