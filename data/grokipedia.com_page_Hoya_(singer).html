Lee Ho-won (Korean: 이호원; born Lee Ho-dong (Korean: 이호동) on (1991-03-28)March 28, 1991[3]), better known by his stage name Hoya , is a South Korean singer, rapper, songwriter, actor, and dancer, currently a member of the dance crew Mbitious. He is known to have been a rapper and vocalist in boy band Infinite (and later its sub-group Infinite H) from 2010 until his departure from Woollim Entertainment in 2017.[4][5][6] In 2018, Hoya made his solo debut on March 28 with his first EP Shower and has since released additional singles, including "1AM" (2021), "Deserve" (2023), and "Night in LA" (2023). Hoya made his acting debut in the drama Reply 1997 (2012) and went on to star in the dramas My Lovely Girl (2014), Mask (2015), Radiant Office (2017), Two Cops (2017), Devilish Charm (2018), and Check-in Hanyang (2024), as well as films including A Birth (2022).

Biography

Hoya was born in Busan, South Korea. He dropped out of high school during his first year to pursue his dream as a singer. His father was against the decision thus Hoya had to leave home. He then took the high school qualification exam and passed. Later his father accepted him and supported his music career wholeheartedly.[7] Hoya auditioned after he dropped out and ended up in Woollim Entertainment. On March 10, 2014, he announced that his real birth name is Lee Hodong, not Howon as he had previously told the public, and that he changed it in order not to share a name with comedian Kang Hodong.[8] Hoya graduated from Daekyung University, majoring in Applied Music.[9][10] Hoya enlisted for mandatory military service as a public service worker on February 7, 2019, and was discharged on December 6, 2020.[11]

Career

2010: Debut with Infinite

Hoya debuted as rapper, vocal and main dancer of South Korean boyband Infinite in 2010. The group officially debuted on June 9, 2010.

2012–2014: Infinite H's debut and acting roles

Hoya made his acting debut in the tvN drama Reply 1997. The drama was a major hit and raised his popularity.[12] In September 2012, Hoya and fellow Infinite member Dongwoo formed a subgroup called Infinite H.[13][14] The subgroup released their first mini-album, titled Fly High, in January 2013.[13][14] In 2014, Hoya starred in SBS' music romance drama, My Lovely Girl ; which co-stars his fellow Infinite member Kim Myung-soo.[15] On September 24, 2014, Hoya was cast as the lead role in the film Hiya. He played a high school student who only dreams of becoming a singer. The film premiered on March 10, 2015.[16][17]

2015–2017: Departure from Infinite, solo activities

In 2015, Hoya starred in SBS's melodrama Mask , acting as Soo Ae's brother.[18] In June 2016, Hoya became a contestant in Mnet's dance survival show, Hit The Stage.[19] In December, it was announced that Hoya has been cast in the sitcom Strong Family 2017 , which started airing in February 2017.[20] Hoya was a supporting role in MBC's office drama, Radiant Office.[21] On August 30, it was confirmed that Hoya would not be renewing his contract (which expired June 9, 2017) and would be leaving Infinite as well as Woollim Entertainment.[22] In September, he made his musical debut in Hourglass , a play based on the mega hit Korean drama Sandglass.[23] He then starred in MBC's cop drama, Two Cops.[24]

2018–present: Solo debut

In 2018, Lee made his solo debut on March 28 with his first mini album Shower.[25] He was then cast in the romance comedy drama Devilish Charm.[26] In the midst of all, Lee also appeared on Immortal Song 2 broadcast on August 4 and won the episode with the highest score.[27] Lee also became a dance coach/mentor in KBS variety program, Dancing High that focuses on teaching and mentoring teens in Korea about dance.[28] On September 12, Lee released the digital single 'Baby U', with its music video featuring Kriesha Chu.[29] Hoya enlisted for military service on February 7, 2019, by announcing it through a post two days before the date; he was discharged on December 6, 2020.[30] On June 9, 2021, Hoya made his comeback with the new digital single album 1AM.[31] In July 2022, Hoya joined the dance crew Mbitious as part of the Street Man Fighter project.[32] On April 1, 2022, Hoya signed an exclusive contract with Initial Entertainment.[33] In 2023, he released the digital singles "Night in LA" and "Deserve".[34] In May 2024, he signed with RISE Entertainment.[35] In June 2024, Hoya was cast in the historical drama Check-in Hanyang, which began airing in December 2024.[36]
