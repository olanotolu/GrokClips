List of current heads of government in the United Kingdom and dependencies


The list of current heads of government in the United Kingdom and dependencies catalogs the executive leaders directing policy and administration across the UK's central government, devolved nations, Crown Dependencies, and British Overseas Territories, all operating under the constitutional monarchy where the sovereign serves as head of state.[1][2]
In the unitary state of the United Kingdom, the Prime Minister heads His Majesty's Government, overseeing national matters including foreign policy, defense, and economic regulation, while devolution delegates authority over areas like health, education, and transport to the Scottish Parliament, Senedd Cymru (Welsh Parliament), and Northern Ireland Assembly, each led by a First Minister.[3][1]
The three Crown Dependenciesâ€”Jersey, Guernsey (including Alderney and Sark), and the Isle of Manâ€”possess internal self-government with their own parliaments and executives headed by Chief Ministers or Presidents, though the UK manages their defense and external affairs by convention.[2][4]
The fourteen British Overseas Territories, remnants of the former empire, feature locally elected governments led by Premiers or Chief Ministers handling domestic governance, with the UK retaining ultimate responsibility for security, diplomatic representation, and good governance standards.[5][4]
This decentralized arrangement underscores the UK's retention of parliamentary sovereignty, enabling subnational autonomy without federal division, though tensions arise over fiscal powers and secessionist pressures in devolved regions.[3]

Central Government of the United Kingdom

Prime Minister

Sir Keir Starmer has served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom since 5 July 2024.[6] He leads the Labour Party, which obtained a parliamentary majority of 174 seats in the general election held on 4 July 2024, marking the end of 14 years of Conservative governance.[6] As Prime Minister, Starmer heads His Majesty's Government, overseeing executive functions including policy formulation, national security, and international relations, while chairing the Cabinet and appointing its members.[6] Prior to entering politics, Starmer worked as a barrister and served as Director of Public Prosecutions from 2008 to 2013. Elected to Parliament in 2015 for Holborn and St Pancras, he became Labour leader in 2020 following Jeremy Corbyn's resignation after the 2019 election defeat. His premiership has focused on economic stability, public service reforms, and addressing post-Brexit challenges, though it has encountered criticism over fiscal policies and migration management.[7] As of October 2025, Starmer remains in office, with the next general election not required until 2029.[8]

Devolved Administrations

First Minister of Scotland

The First Minister of Scotland is John Swinney, leader of the Scottish National Party (SNP), who assumed office on 8 May 2024 following the resignation of his predecessor, Humza Yousaf.[9] Swinney was nominated by a vote in the Scottish Parliament, securing the position with support from SNP MSPs after being elected unopposed as party leader on 6 May 2024.[10] As of October 2025, he continues to head the Scottish Government, overseeing devolved matters such as health, education, and justice within the framework of the Scotland Act 1998.[11] Swinney, born on 13 April 1964, represents the Perthshire North constituency as a Member of the Scottish Parliament (MSP) since 2011, having previously served North Tayside from 1999 to 2011.[12] His tenure as First Minister has focused on stabilizing the SNP amid internal challenges and advancing priorities outlined in the 2025 Programme for Government, delivered to Parliament on 6 May 2025.[13] The Scottish Government under Swinney maintains a pro-independence stance, though parliamentary arithmetic requires cross-party cooperation for key legislation given the SNP's minority position post-2021 elections.[14]

First Minister of Wales

Eluned Morgan serves as the First Minister of Wales, having assumed office on 6 August 2024 following her nomination and approval by the Senedd.[15] She leads Welsh Labour, which holds a majority in the Senedd, and is the first woman to hold the position.[16] Morgan was elected as leader of Welsh Labour unopposed after the resignation of her predecessor, Vaughan Gething, in July 2024.[17] Prior to her appointment, Morgan served as Cabinet Secretary for Health and Social Care from March 2024, having previously held the role of Minister for Health and Social Services since May 2021.[18] As a Member of the Senedd for the Mid and West Wales region, she represents Welsh Labour in the devolved legislature.[19] Her tenure as First Minister continues as of October 2025, amid ongoing scrutiny including responses to local election outcomes.[20]

Crown Dependencies

Isle of Man

The Isle of Man is a self-governing Crown Dependency of the British Crown, with internal affairs managed autonomously and the United Kingdom responsible for defense and international representation. The head of government is the Chief Minister, who leads the executive Council of Ministers and is accountable to Tynwald, the island's legislature comprising the House of Keys and the Legislative Council. The Chief Minister is nominated by the House of Keys, the directly elected lower chamber, and formally appointed by the Lieutenant Governor, the monarch's representative.[28] Alfred Cannan MHK has served as Chief Minister since 12 October 2021.[29] [28] He was elected to the position by the House of Keys following the 2021 general election, in which his party, the Manx Independents (though primarily independent members form governments), secured support for his leadership.[29] Cannan, representing the Ayre & Michael constituency, previously held roles as Minister for Financial Services (2018â€“2020) and Minister for the Treasury (2020â€“2021).[28] As of October 2025, he continues in office, overseeing policies on economic development, fiscal matters, and post-COVID recovery, including initiatives like AI regulation announced in September 2025.[28] [30] The Council of Ministers, chaired by the Chief Minister, consists of nine additional ministers appointed from Tynwald members, handling departmental portfolios such as health, education, and infrastructure.[31] This structure emphasizes consensus-driven governance, with the Chief Minister coordinating executive decisions while subject to legislative scrutiny. The next House of Keys election, determining potential changes in leadership, is scheduled for 2026.

Bailiwick of Jersey

The Bailiwick of Jersey, a Crown Dependency of the United Kingdom, has the Chief Minister as its head of government, leading the Council of Ministers and accountable to the States Assembly, the island's legislature.[32] The position, formalized in December 2005, evolved from the earlier role of President of the Council of Ministers to provide clearer executive leadership.[33] Deputy Lyndon Farnham, an independent member representing St. Mary, St. Ouen, and St. Peter, has served as Chief Minister since 30 January 2024, elected by the States Assembly following the appointment process after the 2022 general election.[34] [35] As of October 2025, Farnham continues in office, overseeing priorities including net zero emissions targets by 2050 and inter-ministerial coordination.[36] [37] The Lieutenant Governor, representing the monarch as head of state, performs ceremonial duties but holds no executive authority over government policy.

Bailiwick of Guernsey

The Bailiwick of Guernsey, a Crown Dependency of the British Crown, is led in its domestic affairs by the President of the Policy and Resources Committee of the States of Guernsey, who serves as the Chief Minister and head of government.[38] This position coordinates the executive functions across the Bailiwick, which encompasses Guernsey, Alderney, Sark, and associated smaller islands, while ultimate authority rests with the Lieutenant-Governor as the Monarch's representative.[2] The States of Guernsey, the unicameral parliament, elects the committee's president following general elections.[39] Deputy Lindsay de Sausmarez has held the office of President of the Policy and Resources Committee since 1 July 2025, marking her as the first woman in this role.[40] She was elected by fellow States members with 22 votes, succeeding Deputy Lyndon Trott, and her term extends until 30 June 2029.[41] De Sausmarez, a People's Deputy, has emphasized unifying the assembly, reducing factionalism, and prioritizing financial management and public service improvements.[42] The Policy and Resources Committee, as the senior executive body, oversees policy coordination, budgeting, and resource allocation for the Bailiwick.[38] Deputy Gavin St Pier serves as Vice-President and Deputy Chief Minister, elected on 5 July 2025.[38] While Alderney and Sark maintain separate assemblies with their own presidents for local matters, Bailiwick-wide decisions are channeled through Guernsey's structures.[43]

British Overseas Territories

Caribbean and North Atlantic Territories

The Caribbean and North Atlantic Territories encompass British Overseas Territories including Anguilla, Bermuda, the British Virgin Islands, the Cayman Islands, Montserrat, and the Turks and Caicos Islands, each governed by a premier responsible for executive leadership under a governor appointed by the United Kingdom. These leaders head local cabinets and legislatures, handling domestic affairs such as finance, tourism, and disaster response, while foreign relations and defense remain UK prerogatives.

South Atlantic and Antarctic Territories

The South Atlantic and Antarctic Territories comprise the Falkland Islands, South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands, and the British Antarctic Territory, all British Overseas Territories administered under the sovereignty of the United Kingdom monarch, represented locally by appointed officials. These territories lack unified local governance structures beyond the Falkland Islands, which maintains an elected assembly; the others rely on commissioners for administrative oversight, policy implementation, and compliance with international obligations such as the Antarctic Treaty System. In the Falkland Islands, the Premier serves as head of the local government, leading the eight-member Legislative Assembly elected every four years and chairing the executive policy committees responsible for domestic affairs including health, education, and fisheries. The current Premier is Roger Spink, who assumed the role following the 2021 general election; the next election is scheduled for 11 December 2025. The Governor, who holds executive authority over security, international relations, and constitutional matters, is Colin Martin-Reynolds CMG, sworn in on 29 July 2025.[51] South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands, with no indigenous or permanent residents, operate without a local legislature or elected officials; governance is vested in the Commissioner, concurrently held by the Governor of the Falkland Islands. Colin Martin-Reynolds has served in this capacity since 29 July 2025, managing environmental protection, fisheries enforcement, and limited scientific activities under the territory's Marine Protected Area designation. The British Antarctic Territory, encompassing approximately 1.7 million square kilometers but supporting only transient research personnel at stations like Rothera, has no elected government or permanent inhabitants. Administration falls to the Commissioner for the British Antarctic Territory, Jane Rumble OBE, appointed in February 2025 and operating through the Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office's Polar Regions Department to coordinate UK interests under the Antarctic Treaty, including scientific research and territorial claims.[52]

Indian Ocean and Mediterranean Territories

The British Indian Ocean Territory, comprising the Chagos Archipelago, lacks a permanent civilian population and elected local government, with administration conducted from London by a Commissioner appointed by the British monarch on the advice of the Foreign Secretary. The current Commissioner is Nishi Dholakia, who assumed the role in 2024 and continues to serve as of October 2025.[53] The Commissioner oversees policy, law-making via Orders in Council, and coordination with the US-UK military presence on Diego Garcia, the territory's principal island.[53] Gibraltar, a peninsula at the southern tip of the Iberian Peninsula, operates under a constitution granting significant self-governance, with the Chief Minister as head of the elected Government of Gibraltar, responsible for domestic affairs while the Governor handles defense, external relations, and internal security. Fabian Picardo, leader of the Gibraltar Socialist Labour Party, has served as Chief Minister since December 9, 2011, following his party's victory in the 2011 general election; he was re-elected in subsequent polls in 2015, 2019, and 2023.[54] As of October 2025, Picardo remains in office, addressing issues such as post-Brexit residency policies and self-determination in international forums.[55] The Sovereign Base Areas of Akrotiri and Dhekelia, two enclaves in Cyprus retained by the UK under the 1960 Treaty of Establishment, function primarily as military bases with a civil administration supporting British Forces Cyprus and local communities. The Administrator, appointed by the British monarch, serves as head of the civil government, managing local laws, services, and coordination with Cypriot authorities. Major General Tom Bewick took office as Administrator in April 2025, combining the role with command of British Forces Cyprus.[56] The areas feature elected district councils for local matters but no separate head of government equivalent to premiers in other territories.[57]

Pacific and Other Territories

The Pitcairn Islands, comprising Pitcairn, Henderson, Ducie, and Oeno Islands, constitute the sole British Overseas Territory in the Pacific Ocean, with local governance centered on the Island Council chaired by the Mayor, who functions as the head of local government. The territory lacks formal political parties, and the Island Council handles internal administration, including economic development, community services, and law enforcement, subject to oversight by the UK-appointed Governor resident in New Zealand.[58] [59] Simon Young has served as Mayor and Chairman of the Island Council since 1 January 2023, following his election in November 2022 as the first non-islander to hold the office.[58] [60] He continued in the role through at least September 2025, with council minutes from February and April 2025 confirming his active leadership.[61] [60] [62] Elections for the Mayor, Deputy Mayor, and Island Council occur every two years in November, with the most recent prior to October 2025 held on 8 November 2023; the subsequent election was scheduled for November 2025.[58] The Governor retains reserve powers for defense, foreign affairs, and internal security, but day-to-day executive authority rests with the Mayor and Council.[59] No other inhabited territories fall under this category, reflecting the UK's limited Pacific holdings beyond Pitcairn.[63]
