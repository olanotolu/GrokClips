Phu Quoc International Airport

Sân bay quốc tế Phú Quốc
Cảng hàng không quốc tế Phú Quốc (IATA: PQC , ICAO: VVPQ) is an international airport that serves Phú Quốc Island, in southern Vietnam. It covers nearly 900 hectares of land in the commune of Dương Tơ, about 9 kilometres (5.6 mi) south of the administrative centre of Phú Quốc City. It was built at a cost of around VND 16.2 trillion (US$810 million) and developed in phases. The airport is 10 km from the previous Phu Quoc Airport, which it replaced in December 2012. The airport was initially able to handle about 2.5 million passengers per annum, but expansions are underway to increase capacity to 18 million passengers per annum by 2027. The airport has a single runway, capable of handling aircraft like the Airbus A350 and Boeing 747.[1] The construction was completed in November 2012 and was put into operation on 2 December 2012.[2][3] The Government of Vietnam expects the airport to facilitate the arrival of international tourists who are attracted to the island's beaches. In 2024, it handled over 4.1 million passengers, including 1.8 million international arrivals.[4]

History

Airports Corporation of Vietnam began construction of the airport on 23 November 2008 with an investment of VND 16.2 trillion (US$810 million). It was designed by Singaporean-US design consulting firm CPG-PAE. After four years' work, the airport entered commercial operation on 2 December 2012, with its official inauguration on 15 December 2012. The airport initially handled domestic flights but was granted international status in 2014, enabling scheduled international services. Passenger traffic grew rapidly, reaching 3.7 million in 2019. In May 2025, Sun Group was approved as the primary investor for a major expansion project ahead of the 2027 APEC Summit in Phu Quoc. The upgrade, estimated at VND 22-26 trillion (US$860 million-1 billion), includes a new phoenix-inspired passenger terminal designed by CPG Consultants and a VVIP terminal by Italian architect Marco Casamonti, aiming to increase capacity to 12 million passengers annually by 2030.[6]
