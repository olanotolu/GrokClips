Bare & Rare

Bare & Rare is the second studio album by South Korean singer Chung Ha. The first part was released on July 11, 2022, under MNH Entertainment and 88rising. Although a second part was planned, it was ultimately not released following Chung Ha's departure from MNH Entertainment in 2023.[1]

Chung Ha's Bare & Rare Album Background

Background

On June 27, 2022, Chung Ha revealed a detailed teaser schedule for the first part of her second studio album, titled Bare & Rare. The mood sampler in "Bare" version was uploaded on June 28. On June 30, the official photo of "Bare #1" was released, while the "Bare #2" was released on July 1. The teaser images for the lead single "Sparkling" were released on July 2, which were said to have an "enchanting" atmosphere. The track list for the album was released on July 4, with the highlight medley being released two days later on July 6. The music video teaser was released on July 8 and 9 respectively. Countdown posters were released on July 9 and 10 respectively before the official D-day countdown poster and album was released on July 11, 2022. The album was originally planned to be released in two parts, but Part 2 was ultimately not released following Chung Ha's departure from MNH Entertainment in March 2023.[2]

Composition

The album consists of eight tracks, all of which were co-produced and co-written by Chung Ha. It marks Chung Ha's first full-length release since her debut studio album Querencia, which was released in 2021. The album explores themes of vulnerability and personal stories, diverging from her previous image-focused works to share more authentic emotions. "Louder" is a catchy pop track with bouncy beats and twinkling effects. Chung Ha aims to encourage listeners to stop apologising for the space they take up and start owning their strength. The lead single "Sparkling" was considered a change to Chung Ha's previous music style. It features retro pop elements with modern synths, guitar riffs, and a whistling chorus, capturing the exhilarating dynamics of love.

Conception

At a press conference in Seoul, Chung Ha shared her feelings about the album and reflected on her previous works, citing that she "felt empty for some reason".
"I realized that while I've been a singer for the past few years, I had never really expressed my personal memories through my music. So far, I thought of myself as a performer; someone who performs the stage routine and lyrics that others designed for me. For this album, I wanted to fill it up with my innermost stories. I asked myself what was causing that feeling, and I thought of the times that I sang lyrics that I couldn't relate to, or wanted to change some musical aspects in my songs. So I really wanted to tell my own story this time." â€” Chung Ha explaining her thoughts on the album
Prior to the release of the album, Chung Ha performed "California Dreams" on June 25, 2022, at the Seoul Waterbomb Festival 2022. The album initially was announced as a double album, with a second part of the album being scheduled for release in late 2022. A release of the second part of the project failed to materialize in 2022. In March 2023, MNH Entertainment announced that Chung Ha would not renew her contract, which expired on April 29, 2023. The agency also confirmed that Part 2 would not be released under them due to internal circumstances.[2] Chung Ha addressed the project's future in a letter to fans, expressing hope for eventual release but noting uncertainties.[1] She subsequently signed with More Vision in 2023. As of October 2025, Part 2 remains unreleased.[3]
