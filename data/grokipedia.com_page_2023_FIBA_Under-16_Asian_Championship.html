2023 FIBA Under-16 Asian Championship

The 2023 FIBA U16 Asian Championship was the seventh edition of the FIBA Under-16 Asian Championship in the International Basketball Federation's FIBA Asia zone. The tournament was held at the Al-Gharafa Indoor Hall and the Al-Rayyan Indoor Hall in Doha, Qatar, from 17 to 24 September 2023.[1][2] It served as a qualifier for the 2024 FIBA Under-17 Basketball World Cup in Turkey, where the top four teams qualified.[3] Australia successfully defended their U16 title against first-time semifinalists New Zealand in the thrilling final, 79â€“76, while China clinched the bronze medal after defeating the Philippines in the battle for third place, 87â€“59.[4]

Qualification

The qualification process for the 2023 FIBA Under-16 Asian Championship allocated berths based on subzone performances, with automatic qualifiers from each FIBA Asia subzone and FIBA Oceania. Base automatic berths were: 1 each for Central Asia and South Asia, 2 each for East Asia, Persian Gulf, Southeast Asia, and West Asia (total 10 for Asia), plus 2 for Oceania (total 12). Three additional berths were awarded to the subzones of the top 3 Asian teams from the 2022 FIBA Under-16 Asian Championship (East Asia via Japan, South Asia via India, West Asia via Iran). The host nation (Qatar) received an extra berth for its subzone (Persian Gulf), bringing the total to 16 teams. The host and defending champion (Australia) qualified automatically, drawing from their subzone allocations, with the host adding the extra berth to its subzone.
