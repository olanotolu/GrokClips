Kiiara

Kiara Saulters (born May 24, 1995), known professionally as Kiiara , is an American singer-songwriter from Wilmington, Illinois.[3][4] Signed to Atlantic Records in 2015, her debut single "Gold" peaked at number 13 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart. She released her debut studio album, lil kiiwi, in 2020. She is also known for contributing vocals to Linkin Park's 2017 single "Heavy. Since 2023, she has released music independently, including singles such as "Whoops" (2023) and "This Time" (2025), as well as collaborations like "Never Too Late" with ONLAP (2024) and "Love & Drugs" (2025).[5][6]

Early life

Kiiara was born on May 24, 1995, in Wilmington, Illinois.[7] She attended Wilmington High School where she played for the school's volleyball team.[7] In 2013, she independently released an acoustic pop single titled "Bring Me Back" under her birth name, Kiara Saulters.[8] While recording her breakout EP, she also worked as a hardware store clerk.[3]

Career

In 2013, Kiiara independently released an acoustic pop single called "Bring Me Back" under her real name.[1][8] In June 2015, after signing a deal to Atlantic Records and changing to her stage name from Kiara Saulters to Kiiara, she released her debut single "Gold".[3][9] Later that year, "Gold" was chosen as background music of a 15-second Apple Watch commercial titled "Style".[10] The song was her first entry on the Billboard Hot 100, peaking at number 13. Her debut extended play (EP), Low Kii Savage was released on March 22, 2016.[11][12] The video for "Gold" quickly gained popularity, reaching five million views during mid-May 2016.[13] On September 15, 2016, Kiiara made her television debut, performing "Gold" on The Tonight Show with Jimmy Fallon.[14] Kiiara was featured on Linkin Park's single, "Heavy", which was released digitally on February 16, 2017 and on radio on February 21.[15][16] She later performed "Heavy" with Julia Michaels at the Linkin Park and Friends: Celebrate Life in Honor of Chester Bennington concert at the Hollywood Bowl which was held after the death of Linkin Park's vocalist Chester Bennington.[17] In March 2018, she was featured on the Cheat Codes single "Put Me Back Together".[18] On June 7, 2019, she released the single "Open My Mouth". Kiiara stated that it would serve as the lead single from her debut studio album,[19] along with another single "Bipolar", released on September 6, 2019.[20] However, both were cut from the final track listing, although the first one featured on a deluxe edition of the record.[21] On October 9, 2020, she released her debut studio album Lil Kiiwi , which includes her previously released singles "Gold", "Feels", and "Whippin" featuring Felix Snow, as well as new singles "I Still Do", "Never Let You", and "Numb" featuring DeathbyRomy and Pvris.[22] A deluxe edition of her album was later released, which included two songs "Intention" and "Tennessee" taken from her debut extended play, Low Kii Savage , as well as two previous non-album singles, "Messy" and "Open My Mouth".[21] Following the release of Lil Kiiwi, Kiiara continued issuing singles independently and collaborating with other artists. In 2021, she featured on Felix Cartal's "Happy Hour" and Devault's "Never Felt Like This".[23][24] The deluxe edition of her album was issued in February 2021.[21] In 2022, she collaborated with Rosa Linn on "KING".[25] Throughout 2023, she released a series of independent singles including "Whoops", "The One" (with Limonade), "Minute", "Speed", and "Cum in Peace" .[26] In December 2024, she appeared on "Baila Comigo" with DJ Phenomena and DJ Zangado.[27] Early 2025 saw releases of "This Time" (with Burko and The Archer) in January and "Love & Drugs" (with teamwork. and American Authors) later that month.[28][29]

Influences

Kiiara has cited Eminem, Yelawolf, and Linkin Park as her influences.[30] She has also named Rihanna and Joyner Lucas among her influences.[31]
