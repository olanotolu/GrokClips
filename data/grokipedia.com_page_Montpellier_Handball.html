Montpellier Handball

Montpellier Handball, formerly named Montpellier Agglomération Handball, is a professional handball club from Montpellier, France. Montpellier Handball has won the EHF Champions League twice, in 2003[2] and 2018.[3]

History

The team was founded as Cosmos Montpellier in 1982. In 1992 they were promoted to the top division. From 1994 they were coached by Patrice Canayer who guided them to 14 French championships.[4] In 2003 they became the first French team to win the EHF Champions League, when they beat Spanish Portland San Antonio in the final 27–19 away and 31–19 at home.[2][5] In September 2012 18 people with connections to Montpellier, including 9 players, were accused of match fixing during a match against Cesson.[6] The accusation was that they had lost a match on purpose, in which their relatives had put money on the result worth upwards of 89,000 euros.[6][7][8] As a result several players left the club, including Luka and Nikola Karabatić (to Pays d'Aix UC HB), Erlend Mamelund (to Haslum HK) and Primož Prošt (to Frisch Auf Göppingen).[9] Three years later Nikola Karabatić was found guilty and had to pay a fine of 10,000 euros.[10] The club continued its success under Canayer, winning additional French titles and securing a second EHF Champions League in 2018 by defeating HBC Nantes 32–27 in the final.[11] After the 2023-24 season Patrice Canayer left the club after 30 years in charge.[12] He was replaced by Érick Mathé, who had been the assistant for Canayer from 2015 to 2018.[13]

Crest, colours, supporters

Crest

The club's crest features a shield (blason) as the central visual element. In 2018, two golden stars were added above the logo to commemorate the two EHF Champions League titles won in 2003 and 2018.[14]

Colours

The primary club colours of Montpellier Handball are white and blue.[15]

Supporters

The main supporters' group is the Blue Fox, founded in 1998. The group is known for its mascot Foxy, a blue fox character that has been a fixture at matches since 1998.[16]
