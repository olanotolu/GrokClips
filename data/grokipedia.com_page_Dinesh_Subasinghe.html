Dinesh Subasinghe

Dinesh Subasinghe (born 10 July 1979) is a Sri Lankan composer, violinist, and music producer. He composed Karuna Nadee, a Buddhist oratorio,[1] and re-introduced a lost, ancient musical instrument known as the ravanahatha to Sri Lanka.[2][3][4] He is also a film and television composer whose works integrate western and eastern classical music with electronic music, Buddhist music, folk music, traditional orchestral arrangements, and world music genres.[5] From 2002 to 2009 he led the pop classical band, Dee R Cee Members.[4][6] In 2015â€“2016 Dinesh received Sri Lankan attention for the film score of Ho Gaana Pokuna children movie.[7] For this work, he received eight nominations and won five awards, including Derana Film Awards, Presidential Film Awards, Hiru Golden Film Award, and Signis Awards, in 2016. Subasinghe received the Ten Outstanding Young Persons of the World (TOYP) award in 2016 for his contribution to Sri Lankan arts.[8] In 2018 he received the 'Asia Inspiration Award' at the 2nd South Asian Youth Summit. In 2019 he received the 'India Peace Prize' at the 3rd South Asian Youth Summit in New Delhi, India. He did the music score for Sudu Andagena Kalu Awidin teledrama and directed music of 'Tone Poem'/Baila Sade & Imorich Tunes musical series.[9][10] He continues to compose for film and television, including scores for Meedum Tharanaya (2024) and Sama Asama (2025).

Rohana Subasinghe

Early life

Subasinghe was born in Colombo, Sri Lanka in 1979. He studied at St Peter's College, Colombo and Maris Stella College in Negombo.[4] He began playing music at a very young age, starting with the violin in childhood. He studied music under Samantha Amarajeewa, Janaki Disanayaka, Christal Luduwike, and Patrick Master at his school and in the city. He also studied with B. Victor Perera, Mahinda Senivirathan, Kalani Perera, V. Hemapala Perera and Ananda Perera.[11] In 2001 Subasinghe started learning western classical music under Ashoka Jayawardhana, Hasini Helpe and Annada Dabare. Later they assisted Subasinghe in joining the Sri Lankan Symphony Orchestra.[12] He became a member of composer Stanley Peiris's orchestra.[13][14][15] Subasinghe worked under the Sri Lankan composer Premasiri Khemadasa for eight years and led the orchestra for Khemadasa's opera Agni (2007â€“2008). From 2001 to 2005 he was a member of Sri Lankan musical groups led by Dumindu Kadigamuwa, Harsha Bulathsinghala, Janananth Warakagoda, and Dilup Gabadamudalige.[6][16] He began composing for television and film in 2004 when cinema director Chandrathna Mapitigama selected him to compose music for his television series Hummane.[17] In 2009 he received a scholarship from Sri Lankan President Mahinda Rajapaksa to study music under A. R. Rahman at the KM Music Conservatory in India.[18] At the conservatory, he won the Best Student Award in the instrumental section for violin.[19][20]

Career

Classical music

Subasinghe has composed a Buddhist oratorio, Karuna Nadee,[21] as well as a symphony, trio, sonata, two a cappella pieces, a string quartet, a concerto for trumpet, and a piece for viola, The Dance of Salome.[22] His string quartet, Night Before The Battle, was selected by Joel Thome's Orchestra of Our Time from over 100 compositions worldwide. He has composed music for over 100 Sri Lankan teledramas, more than 20 films, and over 20 stage plays. Subasinghe was the first Sri Lankan to write a cappella pieces with Sri Lankan national themes for SATB choirs, including "The Princes of the Lost Tribe" for Menaka De Shabandu's choir[23] and "Ancient Queen of Somawathee" for Bridget Helpe's choir. The style of these compositions uses harmonies and a sound which is different from Western classical music traditions.[24][25][26]

Alternative rock music

Subasinghe led the alternative rock music band Dee R Cee and introduced rock music into several Sri Lankan television series.[27] He performed at the 2011 Share the Love concert and on Rupavahini TV in February 2013 with the song "Ra Roo".[27]

YouTube record

Subasinghe released an alternative-rock song "E Kale: The One" with Charmika Sirimanne and Ranushka Fernando in 2013, along with its music video created by Ranushka Fernando.[28] The video has garnered over 178,000 views on YouTube as of October 2025.[29]

Musician, music director, actor, tours

Subasinghe has played as a musician for local and international music directors and composers. In 2002, he recorded the violin caprice for the character Musila in the Guthila Upatha ballad play, composed by Diliup Gabadamudalige; the violin for Guthila was performed by his teacher, veteran violinist Kalani Perera.[30] Subasinghe worked for Symphony Orchestra of Sri Lanka from 2003 to 2005 as a violinist.[11] He was the first Sri Lankan invited to join the South Indian Cinema Musicians Union.[31] He has released several CDs, including Rawan Nada, Feel My Heart, the Sihina Wasanthayak soundtrack, and Karuna Nadee.[32] Subasinghe's first professional acting appearance was in Parapura by Cletus Mendis in 2012. He performed a leading role in his music video E Kale: The One and played King Herod Antipas in the 2013 Paasku television series by Prem Fernando.[33] He performed concerts with Sri Lankan pop bands, including Marians in 2020 and Gypsies in later years. As of 2025, Subasinghe continues to compose for films such as Devi Kusumasana and participates in cultural events like AbhirÄva 2025.[34]

  • New Zealand & Australia in 2020 Subasinghe performed with famous Sri Lankan pop band Marians in 'Thaala' concert series along with Sri Lankan rock star Chitral Somapala. Concerts were held in Auckland, Melbourne and Sydney in February and March 2020.[36]
  • Kuwait in 2023 with Commercial Bank for the celebration of 20 years of remittance operations in Kuwait.[37]

Collaborations with International Music

With South Indian Cine Musicians

In 2009, during his academic stay in India, Dinesh was invited to play with the South Indian Cine Musicians Union. He was selected at the KM Music Conservatory and obtained temporary membership of the Union. He played violin for many South Indian and Bollywood movie background scores. During this time, he met veteran string players from the Madras String Quartet, including M. Kalyan, N.S. Narasimhan, and Shekar.[17]

Rhyme Skool with Katrina Kaif in KM Music Conservatory, Chennai

Dinesh worked with his former principal A.R. Rahman and Katrina Kaif on her CD Rhyme Skool with Katrina Kaif, produced by Sa Re Ga Ma Productions in India in 2009â€“2010.

Share the love Concert with Korea (2011)

Subasinghe participated in a joint Sadaham Sevana musical concert with Korea and India on 4 July 2011.[38] He composed Mist of a Mountain, a tribute piece for Eastern Asia, and has participated in many religious music projects around South Asia.[19][39][40]

O'Connor Method in New York (2015/16)

In 2015 and 2016, Dinesh participated in the O'Connor Method Summer Camp in New York, conducted by violinist Mark O'Connor.[41][42][43]

AGBU in Armenia (2016)

Dinesh participated in an Armenian music program in Armenia, organized by the AGBU (Armenian General Benevolent Union) in 2016.[44][45]

He participated and performed with his Tone Poem team at the South Asian Youth Summit held in Colombo, with participation from 35 countries. He was invited by the president of the International Youth Service Society, Dr. K. K. Singh, and was honored with a token of appreciation for his contribution to the event.

Dee R Cee Members

From 2002 to 2009 Subasinghe led the pop classical bands, Dee R Cee and Dinesh and Friends.[4] The other members of Dee R Cee were singers Ranuka Sudam and Chandumal Samapriya.[6] Their music encompassed multiple genres, including pop, alternative rock, cinema music, Hindustani classical, country and western, baila and 1970s music.

Ravanahatha

In 2007, Subasinghe re-introduced the ancient instrument, the ravanahatha, to Sri Lanka on his album Ravana Nada, comprising 12 instrumentals and background vocals.[50][51] The ravanahatha (ravanhatta, rawanhattha, ravanastron, or ravana hasta veena) is a bowed fiddle traditionally associated with the Manganiyar folk musicians of Rajasthan, India. It is believed by some, including Subasinghe, to have originated among the Hela civilisation of Sri Lanka at the time of King Ravana.[52][53] Ravana Nada is the first recording to include music played on the ravanahatha,[54] although it had appeared in films as a visual item. On this CD Subasinghe performed with a slightly different version of the instrument. He also played it on A. R. Rahman's Rhyme Skool with Katrina Kaif, which was narrated by Kaif, and for some Indian Tamil movie music.[55][56] In addition to the ravanahatha, Subasinghe is reviving another ancient bowed instrument called the kingiri.[12][50][57] Since 2007 Subasinghe has tried to discover the similarity between the ravanahatha and the Kingri (string instrument).[54][58] He has mentioned to the media that the instrument is discussed in the book by Abele and Niederheitmann translated by John Broadhouse The Violin: Its History & Construction, Illustrated & described, From Many Sources published by William Reeves in London between 1900 and 1930. He feels that it is important that Sri Lanka claim the rights for this ancient fiddle.[54] Subasinghe's version of the ravanahatha has been discussed by Sir Christopher Ondaatje, in an article on the ravanahatha, who cites it as the mother of today's violin.[59] Subasinghe's Ravana Nada album set a record as the highest selling instrumental music CD in Sri Lanka.[54][60] Released on the Tharanga label, owned by Vijay Ramanayaka, the CD was Dinesh's first solo album and the first solo album using this historical instrument. It sold 16,000 copies between the years 2009 and 2015.[60] Dinesh started using ravanahatha for his musical compositions and became a modern composer who uses this ancient instrument in cinematic and commercial music.[61]

Buddhist oratorio, Karuna Nadee

In 2010, Subasinghe composed Karuna Nadee (River of Kindness) an oratorio for chorus and orchestra based on the life of Buddha.[62] According to the Sri Lankan newspaper The Daily Mirror, it is the first Buddhist oratorio and the first musical work based on Buddha's life and philosophy[21] since Premasiri Khemadasa's cantata Pirinivan Mangalya, a requiem based on Buddha's Parinirvana.[21] However, the American composer Dudley Buck composed an oratorio on the life of Buddha, The Light of Asia, which was first performed in 1887.[63] Buddhist texts have also been incorporated into two other western oratorios, Somei Satoh's 1987 Stabat Mater and Jonathan Harvey's 2011 Weltethos.[64][65] The oratorio consists of 12 pieces of music and, according to the Daily News, is composed in "Far Eastern, Sri Lankan, Indian, and Western classical styles".[62] It also includes a Buddhist chant in the background, as well as elements from Tibetan music and folk and pop genres.[66] It was his first major work since coming home for a break from the A. R. Rahman Academy in India.[5] Buddhist monk Athuraliye Rathana Thero and Negombo astrologer Ranushka Fernando contributed ideas to the conceptualization of the work.[66][67] The work, with Subasinghe playing some of the music has been released on CD.[68][69][70]

Passion plays and other religious music

Subasinghe has composed music for Sri Lankan Passion plays.[71] In 1999, 2000, and 2001, Sri Lankan dramatist Peter Wellambage, Subasinghe, and composer Jayantha Modarage participated in the production of a modern Sri Lankan passion play, Kurusiya Matha Miyadunemi, performed in the Katuwapitiya and Bolawalana villages in Negombo.[72] Subasinghe created the music for the passion play Aho mage senageni performed in editions including 2003 in Halpe, Katana near Negombo. It was directed by Alexius Fernando.[24] In 2008â€“2009, Subasinghe re-orchestrated music for the historic Duwa passion play and composed for the stage drama Katu Otunna, both based on the story of Jesus.[30] In 2012, 18 Sri Lankan cinema and television stars participated in the passion play Sri Kurusawalokanaya Thambakanda, produced by Prem Fernando and directed by Peter Wellambage with music by Subasinghe. Jeewan Kumaranatunga played the role of Jesus.[71] In 2013, Subasinghe composed music for the passion play at St. Anthony's Church in Dalupotha, Negombo.[22] Subasinghe composed the music for the 2013 television film based on the life of Jesus, Jesu Christu Yuga Peraliya, produced by Prem Fernando.[33] He also served as assistant director and script editor, and played the role of Herod Antipas. Jeewan Kumaranatunga played the role of Jesus while Ravindra Randeniya played Pontius Pilate. More than 25 Sri Lankan cinema and television stars participated.[73]

Discography

As of 2017, Subasinghe had composed music for 75 teledramas,[88] 11 movies, 20 stage plays, 10 documentaries, and directed music for nine TV and radio musical programs. He became the youngest composer to write 75 teledrama scores for Sri Lankan television. Subasinghe received the best music director award at the 18th Sumathi Awards (held in 2012) for his work on Pinsara Dosthara, becoming the youngest recipient in the award's history up to that point.
