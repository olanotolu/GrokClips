Polymerization

Carbon suboxide polymerizes spontaneously to a red, yellow, or black solid. The structure is postulated to be poly(Î±-pyronic), similar to the structure in 2-pyrone (Î±-pyrone).[9][10] The number of monomers in the polymers is variable (see Oxocarbon#Polymeric carbon oxides). In 1969, it was hypothesized that the color of the Martian surface was caused by this compound;[11] this was disproved by the Viking Mars probes (the red color is instead due to iron oxide).[11][12]

Carbon Suboxide

Uses

Carbon suboxide is used in the preparation of malonates; and as an auxiliary to improve the dye affinity of furs. In chemical synthesis, carbon suboxide is used mainly for the preparation of five- and six-membered heterocycles with oxo and hydroxy groups at the 1,3-positions. Because it is so unstable, it is a reagent of last resort.[13]

Biological role

Those are 6- or 8-ring macrocyclic polymers of carbon suboxide that were found in living organisms. They are acting as an endogenous digoxin-like Na+/K+\mathrm{Na}^+ / \mathrm{K}^+Na+/K+-ATPase and Ca-dependent ATPase inhibitors, endogenous natriuretics, antioxidants and antihypertensives. According to studies by Kerek and colleagues, small amounts of carbon suboxide (C3O2C_3O_2C3â€‹O2â€‹) may be produced in biochemical processes, such as those involving carbon monoxide. It has been proposed that carbon suboxide can polymerize into macrocyclic structures with the formula (C3O2C_3O_2C3â€‹O2â€‹)_n (primarily n=6 and n=8), derived from plant precursors. These macrocyclic compounds have been shown in vitro to potently inhibit Na+/K+\mathrm{Na}^+ / \mathrm{K}^+Na+/K+-ATPase and Ca2+\mathrm{Ca}^{2+}Ca2+-dependent ATPase, exhibiting digoxin-like properties, natriuretic, and antihypertensive effects.[14][15][16] The researchers suggest these compounds could act as endogenous regulators of ATPases, natriuretics, and antihypertensives, though this remains hypothetical. Additionally, some early work speculated that macrocyclic carbon suboxide compounds might reduce free radical formation, oxidative stress, and contribute to endogenous anticancer mechanisms, such as in the retina.[17]
