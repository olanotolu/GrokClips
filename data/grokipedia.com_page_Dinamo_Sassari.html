Dinamo Sassari

Polisportiva Dinamo , commonly known as Dinamo Sassari and currently known as Dinamo Banco di Sardegna Sassari for sponsorship reasons, is an Italian professional basketball club that is based in Sassari, Sardinia. They are the 2018–19 FIBA Europe Cup champions.[1] The club plays in the Italian LBA, the highest level of professional basketball in Italy, and the FIBA Europe Cup (as of 2025). Dinamo was founded in 1960, and in the past, due to sponsorship deals, has also been known as Banco Popolare Sassari (1989–90). The club has competed in Serie A since 2010. Dinamo Sassari is notable for being the club in Italian professional basketball to have made the impressive score of 158 points in a single game (with no overtimes), during the 1994–95 Serie A2 regular season, versus Pallacanestro Pavia (91);[2] which is the highest score ever made by any club in Italy.

History

Dinamo Sassari was founded on 23 April 1960. The club competed in lower divisions for decades, achieving promotion to Serie B1 as champions in 2003 and reaching the LegaDue playoffs final in 2009. In 2010, Dinamo promoted to Serie A after winning the LegaDue playoffs finals against Prima Veroli.[3] In 1994–95, while in Serie A2, the Dinamo team scored 158 points in a regular season game without overtimes against Pallacanestro Pavia.[2] In 2012, the club made its debut in Europe, playing in the EuroCup regular season. The 2013–14 season was one of the most historic for the club. Banco di Sardegna Sassari won the Italian Cup, the team's first trophy, and later the Supercup. Dinamo's star player Travis Diener was named Italian Cup MVP. Later in the season, Drake Diener—Travis' cousin—was named Italian League MVP. Dinamo also played in the EuroCup again, reaching the eighth-finals. Despite finishing third and elimination in the Italian League semifinals in 2014, Dinamo was invited to the 2014–15 EuroLeague season. After Montepaschi Siena, the Italian league runner-up, resigned, the club received a B license. In the 2014–15 season, Dinamo achieved a treble, winning the Italian Cup, Supercup, and its first Italian League championship. In the Italian Cup final, they defeated EA7 Milano 101–94. In Game 7 of the League Finals, Sassari won 73–75 on the road against Reggio Emilia.[4] Because of this, Dinamo returned to the EuroLeague in 2015, where it was eliminated in the regular season. After transferring to the EuroCup, it was eliminated in the round of 32. In 2017, Dinamo reached the Italian Cup final, losing to Olimpia Milano. That season, the club debuted in the Basketball Champions League, reaching the quarterfinals. In the LBA, the team finished fifth. In the 2018–19 season, Dinamo entered the FIBA Europe Cup in the second qualifying round, beating Benfica of Portugal to advance to the regular season. After advancing through the second round, Dinamo defeated ZZ Leiden, Pınar Karşıyaka, and Hapoel Holon to reach the finals against s.Oliver Würzburg. On 1 May 2019, Sassari won the FIBA Europe Cup after defeating Würzburg in the second leg of the finals.[5] It was the team's first European title. Later that year, in September 2019, Dinamo won the Supercup, defeating Reyer Venezia 83–80 in overtime.[6] In 2019, Sassari reached the LBA Finals for the second time, but was defeated by Umana Reyer Venezia in Game 7, losing the series 3–4.[7] Since 2020, Dinamo has remained competitive in the LBA, reaching semifinals in 2022 and 2023, and runners-up in the 2019–20 regular season (suspended due to COVID-19). The club has continued participating in European competitions, including the Basketball Champions League and FIBA Europe Cup, as of the 2024–25 season.[3]
