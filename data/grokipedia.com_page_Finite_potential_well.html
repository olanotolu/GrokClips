Finite potential well

The finite potential well (also known as the finite square well) is a concept from quantum mechanics. It is an extension of the infinite potential well, in which a particle is confined to a "box", but one which has finite potential "walls". Unlike the infinite potential well, there is a probability associated with the particle being found outside the box. The quantum mechanical interpretation is unlike the classical interpretation, where if the total energy of the particle is less than the potential energy barrier of the walls it cannot be found outside the box. In the quantum interpretation, there is a non-zero probability of the particle being outside the box even when the energy of the particle is less than the potential energy barrier of the walls (cf quantum tunnelling).

Spherically symmetric annular well

The spherically symmetric annular well is defined by the potential U_1 & r < a \\ 0 & a < r < b \\ U_2 & r > b \end{cases} $$ with $ U_1, U_2 > 0 $, and bound states satisfying $ 0 < E < \min(U_1, U_2) $. The number of bound states depends on the well parameters $ U_1, U_2 $ and width $ b - a $, analogous to the one-dimensional finite well. The ground state ($ n = 1 $) has zero orbital angular momentum ($ \ell = 0 $), and the reduced wave function $ \chi(r) \equiv r \psi(r) $ satisfies $$ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{d^2 \chi}{dr^2} + U(r) \chi(r) = E \chi(r), $$ where $ \psi(r) $ is the radial wave function (angular part constant for $ \ell = 0 $). For $ \ell = 0 $, $$ \chi(r) = \begin{cases} c_1 \sinh(k_1 r) & r < a, \quad k_1 = \sqrt{\frac{2m (U_1 - E)}{\hbar^2}} \\ c \sin(kr + \delta) & a < r < b, \quad k = \sqrt{\frac{2m E}{\hbar^2}} \\ c_2 e^{-k_2 r} & r > b, \quad k_2 = \sqrt{\frac{2m (U_2 - E)}{\hbar^2}} \end{cases} $$ with $ E = \frac{k^2 \hbar^2}{2m} $. The constants $ c_1, c, \delta, c_2 $ and energies $ E $ are determined by requiring continuity of $ \chi(r) $ and $ \frac{d\chi}{dr} $ at $ r = a $ and $ r = b $, yielding transcendental equations solved numerically. For $ \ell \neq 0 $, the radial equation is $$ \frac{d^2 \chi}{dr^2} + \left[ k^2 - \frac{\ell(\ell + 1)}{r^2} \right] \chi(r) = 0 $$ in the well region (adjusted for other regions). Solutions involve spherical Bessel functions: $$ \frac{\chi(r)}{r} = \begin{cases} A \, i_\ell (k_1 r) & r < a \\ A' j_\ell (k r) + B' y_\ell (k r) & a < r < b \\ C \, k_\ell (k_2 r) & r > b \end{cases} $$ where $ i_\ell, k_\ell $ are modified spherical Bessel functions of the first and second kind, respectively (ensuring regularity at $ r=0 $ and decay at infinity). Energies are found similarly by matching logarithmic derivatives at the boundaries.
