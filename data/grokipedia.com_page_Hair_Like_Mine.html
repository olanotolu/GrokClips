Hair Like Mine

Hair Like Mine is a photograph captured on May 7, 2009, by Pete Souza, then-White House chief photographer, depicting United States President Barack Obama allowing five-year-old Jacob Philadelphia to touch his hair texture during an Oval Office visit.[1][2] The image originated from Jacob's inquiry to Obama about whether the president's hair resembled his own curly variety, prompting Obama to bend down for the boy to verify firsthand, resulting in Jacob's affirmative confirmation that "it's the same."[2][1] The encounter occurred spontaneously during a family tour of the White House, where Jacob, accompanied by his father and others, met Obama briefly; Souza managed to record the interaction in a single frame amid the fast-paced moment.[1] The photograph later gained widespread recognition after its inclusion in Obama's 2011 children's book Of Thee I Sing: A Letter to My Daughters, which highlighted personal anecdotes of leadership and empathy, amplifying its visibility as a symbol of presidential accessibility.[3] By 2022, as Jacob graduated high school and prepared for college, Obama publicly acknowledged their shared history via social media, congratulating him and reflecting on the enduring image's representation of youthful curiosity met with affirmative engagement.[3][2] The photo has since been referenced in discussions of Obama's public persona, though its primary notability stems from the unscripted authenticity of the exchange rather than contrived optics.[1]

The central figures in the "Hair Like Mine" interaction were President Barack Obama and Jacob Philadelphia, a five-year-old boy from Columbia, Maryland.
