Montana of 300

Walter Anthony "Tony" Bradford (born March 3, 1989), better known by his stage name Montana of 300 (or simply Montana), is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter from Peoria, Illinois. Born in Chicago, Illinois, he moved to Peoria in late childhood. He attended Manual High School. He created his rap group 300 with one of his best friends, Montelle Talley, also known as Talley of 300. His debut studio album, Fire in the Church, was released on May 20, 2016. He is best known for his rap style, incorporating trap, R&B, and drill beats, while also making remixes of hit songs that, like his originals, often include religious subtext. From 2016 to 2019, Montana released several albums on May 20, establishing a brief annual tradition. His second album, Don't Doubt the God, was released on May 20, 2017; his third, Pray 4 the Devil, on May 20, 2018; his fourth, A Gun in the Teacher's Desk, on November 16, 2018; and his fifth, Views from the General's Helmet, on May 20, 2019. His sixth album, A Cold Day in Hell, followed on February 18, 2020. Montana repeatedly announced that his seventh and final solo album, Rap God, would mark the end of his solo releases; after delays dating back to 2019, it was released on January 6, 2022. In 2025, he released the collaborative album Top Flow with No Fatigue.[1]

Early life

Tony Bradford was born on March 3, 1989, in Chicago, Illinois. He moved to Peoria, Illinois, in late childhood and attended Manual High School. He has two brothers and three sisters.[2] He started rapping at age 15, and grew up listening to artists such as Tupac, DMX, Lil Wayne, and Jay-Z.[3][4] His mother suffered from serious substance abuse issues, specifically crack cocaine addiction. After witnessing his mother's struggles, Montana ceased using narcotics, not wanting to "fry his brain."[5] In 2008, Montana, his friend from high school, Montelle Talley (known professionally as "Talley of 300"), as well as several others joined to form the rap group "300". The name for the group was based on the movie of the same name, in which a group of 300 Spartans overcome more than 300,000 Persian Soldiers to hold on to their territory. Montana was particularly inspired by the line, "No Surrender, No Retreat, Only the hard, only the strong, Against all odds".[6]

Career

In 2014, Montana released his first mixtape, titled Cursed with a Blessing. In September 2015, Montana made a cameo appearance as a jamming prisoner in the popular television show, Empire.[7] In December 2015, Montana and Talley of 300 released a collaborative mixtape titled Gunz n Roses. Staples of this tape included "FGE Cypher" and "Mf's Mad". On May 20, 2016, Montana released his debut studio album, titled Fire in the Church. The 18-track release, which only included features from his Fly Guy Entertainment labelmates and Kevin Gates, was lauded by XXL magazine for its "heat-seeking bars and thought-provoking metaphors".[8] The album peaked at #95 on the Billboard 200. On February 10, 2017, Montana released a compilation album with his Fly Guy labelmates, titled No Surrender No Retreat.[9] Following No Surrender No Retreat, Montana continued releasing albums annually on May 20, including Don't Doubt the God (2017) and Pray 4 the Devil (2018), along with A Gun in the Teacher's Desk (November 2018) and Views from the General's Helmet (2019). His sixth and final solo album, Rap God, was released on January 6, 2022, after multiple delays.[2] On February 10, 2025, Montana of 300 (real name Walter Anthony Bradford) was arrested in Peoria, Illinois, on multiple charges including criminal trespass to a building, aggravated domestic battery (great bodily harm), resisting a peace officer, and theft control.[10]
