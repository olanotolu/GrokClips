Max Fewtrell

Maximilian Bradley Fewtrell (born 29 July 1999) is a British content creator and podcaster and former racing driver. He was the 2016 British F4 champion. He is the managing partner of Lando Norris' content and lifestyle company Quadrant and also hosts Quadrant's podcast named Quadcast alongside Izzy Hammond the daughter of former Top Gear and The Grand Tour presenter Richard Hammond.

Early life

Fewtrell was born in Birmingham. His family emigrated to Singapore when he was a toddler due to his father's business. They briefly lived in Malaysia before spending nine years in Singapore. He also has an older brother, Sam, a younger brother, Theo, and an older sister Francesca.[1]

Racing career

Karting

Max began karting in 2009 at the age of ten after watching the Singapore Grand Prix.[2] Fewtrell karted in Singapore but competed mainly in Malaysia.[1] He went on to achieve success in karting during 2013 and 2014, including a win in the WSK Champions Cup.[3]

Lower formulae

In 2015, Fewtrell graduated to single-seaters, partaking in the MRF Challenge where he finished eleventh. The following year, Fewtrell joined Carlin for a campaign in British F4.[4] He took three victories and claimed the championship in the final race at Brands Hatch.[5]

Formula Renault 2.0

In December 2016, Fewtrell was named as part of Tech 1 Racing's driver line-up for the 2017 seasons of Formula Renault 2.0.[6] Around the same time, Fewtrell was named as part of the Renault Sport Academy's 2017 line-up.[7] He won a race at the Red Bull Ring on his way to the rookies' title, and came sixth in the drivers' championship.[8][9] The following year, Fewtrell switched to reigning team champions R-ace GP.[10] He achieved six poles and six wins, including a double victory across a two race weekend at Hockenheim, making him the first driver to do so since Nyck de Vries. Fewtrell claimed the championship title in the season finale after a season-long battle with fellow Renault junior Christian Lundgaard.[11][12]

GP3 Series

After winning the 2018 Formula Renault Eurocup, Fewtrell partook in the post-season test with ART Grand Prix.[13]

FIA Formula 3

In January 2019, Fewtrell joined the championship with ART Grand Prix, alongside Christian Lundgaard and David Beckmann.[14] He finished on the podium twice, first in Austria and then again at the Hungaroring, both being second-place finishes. Fewtrell concluded his debut season in 10th place, after scoring 57 points. Fewtrell subsequently joined Hitech GP for the 2020 season. He left the team in August 2020,[15] and retired from racing.[16][17]

Other ventures

Fewtrell has gained approximately 194,000 followers as of October 2025 on the streaming platform Twitch, where he plays FPS games such as Escape from Tarkov and Call of Duty or races in iRacing on a simulator alongside fellow streamers and occasionally with his close friend Lando Norris, who currently races in Formula One. He took part in Norris' Twitch Rivals Charity Tournament in 2021.[18] After appearing in some earlier content, on 13 June 2021, Fewtrell was announced as a new ambassador and core member of Norris' entertainment, athlete and apparel brand, Team Quadrant.[19] As part of Quadrant's rebrand in March 2025, Fewtrell has been promoted as Quadrant's managing partner.[20] He launched his own merchandise line called Fewtrell Fits in December 2020. The line has since been discontinued. During the Goodwood Festival Of Speed in 2022, Fewtrell took part in two sessions of the hill climb with Veloce Racing's Extreme E car, the Spark Odyssey 21, alongside three-time W Series champion Jamie Chadwick.[21]
