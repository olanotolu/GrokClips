Anwar Ali (cricketer)

Anwar Ali(Urdu: انور علی ; born 25 November 1987) is a Pakistani cricketer who has represented his country in One Day Internationals and T20 Internationals. He was also part of the 2006 Under-19 Cricket World Cup winning team, putting in a man-of-the-match performance in the final against India. He currently represents Sindh in the domestic arena.

Early and domestic career

He was born in Khwazakhela to a Muslim Gujjar family[1][2] and he migrated to Karachi as a child. He spent his early years as a factory labourer, ironing socks.[3] In 2009, he played as a professional for Colne Cricket Club in Lancashire, England, in the Lancashire League. [4] He joined North Down Cricket Club in Northern Ireland for the 2012 season as the club professional. In April 2018, he was named in Balochistan's squad for the 2018 Pakistan Cup.[5][6] In March 2019, he was named in Punjab's squad for the 2019 Pakistan Cup.[7][8] In July 2019, he was selected to play for the Rotterdam Rhinos in the inaugural edition of the Euro T20 Slam cricket tournament.[9][10] However, the following month the tournament was cancelled.[11] In September 2019, he was named in Sindh's squad for the 2019–20 Quaid-e-Azam Trophy tournament.[12][13] In November 2021, he was selected to play for the Galle Gladiators following the players' draft for the 2021 Lanka Premier League.[14] In 2022, he played for Multan Sultans in the eighth season of the Pakistan Super League (PSL). In 2023, he joined Gloucestershire for the Metro Bank One-Day Cup. [15] In 2024, he represented Hyderabad Bahadurs in the Sindh Premier League. [16]

Under-19 World Cup

Anwar Ali played a key role in Pakistan's victory against India in the final of the 2006 Under-19 Cricket World Cup. He took five wickets for 35 runs, including Rohit Sharma, whom he bowled with an inswinger. He also took the wicket of Ravindra Jadeja. His performance earned him the Man of the Match award.[17]

International career

Anwar Ali made his T20I debut for Pakistan on 12 October 2008, against Zimbabwe at King City. He bowled 2 overs and conceded 19 runs without taking a wicket. Pakistan won by 7 wickets.[18] After a long gap, Anwar Ali earned his ODI cap on 24 November 2013, against South Africa at Cape Town. He scored 43 not out and took 2 for 26, earning the Man of the Match award in Pakistan's 1-run victory. Bilawal Bhatti also impressed with 3 for 45.[19] In the subsequent ODI series against Sri Lanka in the UAE, Anwar Ali scored 41 not out in the 5th ODI at Abu Dhabi and took 1 for 36, though Pakistan lost by 113 runs.[20] Anwar Ali returned to T20Is in 2015. In May, he played both matches of the home series against Zimbabwe, which Pakistan won 2–0, though he did not take wickets.[21][22] During the second T20I against Sri Lanka on 1 August 2015 in Colombo, Ali smashed a match-winning knock of unbeaten 46 runs off 17 balls (3 fours, 4 sixes), helping Pakistan chase 173 to win by 1 wicket after being 40 for 5. Shahid Afridi contributed 45 off 22 earlier. Ali was named man of the match, and Pakistan won the series 2–0.[23][24][25][26] Anwar Ali continued playing ODIs and T20Is into 2016, including series against New Zealand and a T20I tri-series in Bangladesh, with his last ODI on 25 January 2016 and last T20I on 2 March 2016.[27]
