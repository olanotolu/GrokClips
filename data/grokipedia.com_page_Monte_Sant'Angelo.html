Monte Sant'Angelo

Monte Sant'Angelo (Foggiano: Mónde) is a town and comune of Apulia, southern Italy, in the province of Foggia, on the southern slopes of Monte Gargano. It is one of I Borghi più belli d'Italia.[2]

History

The Sanctuary of Saint Michael the Archangel originated in the late 5th century, based on legends of the archangel's apparitions in 490, 492, and 493. Under Byzantine rule, a church was established at the site by the 6th century. The Lombards conquered the area in the 7th-8th centuries and adopted the sanctuary as a major pilgrimage center.[3] Monte Sant'Angelo as a town appeared only in the 11th century. Between 1081 and 1103 Monte Sant'Angelo was the capital of a large Norman dominion under the control of Count Henry, who was a vassal of the Byzantine Empire. The grotto which houses the Sanctuary of Saint Michael the Archangel has been the site of many famous pilgrimages and inspired the development of similar sites, such as Mont Saint-Michel in France.[4] Pope John Paul II visited the sanctuary in 1987. Following the Norman conquest, the town came under Swabian rule with Emperor Frederick II in the 13th century, who fortified the castle; it later passed to Angevin and Aragonese control. It became part of the Kingdom of Sicily in the 12th century and, after 1282, the Kingdom of Naples, to which it belonged until the unification of Italy in 1861.[5]

Main sights

The most important attraction of Monte Sant'Angelo is the Sanctuary of Monte Sant'Angelo, originating from the 5th century with apparitions of St. Michael the Archangel in 490, 492, and 493 AD, leading to the construction of the first sanctuary around 493 AD. The site has been a key pilgrimage destination since the early Middle Ages, featuring a Romanesque basilica from the 11th century and later additions, including the bell tower built in the late 13th century by Frederick II and adapted by Charles I of Anjou. On June 25, 2011, the World Heritage Committee added the Sanctuary of San Michele Arcangelo in Monte Sant'Angelo to the UNESCO World Heritage List. The Sanctuary is one of the seven groups of historic buildings included in the World Heritage Site "Longobards in Italy: Places of the Power (568-774 A.D.)".[6] Other sights in Monte Sant'Angelo include:
  • The Castle, with bastions of different ages. The most ancient part, called Torre dei Giganti ("Giants' Tower") is a pentagonal tower 18 metres (59 ft) high, with walls 3.7 metres (12 ft) thick. Its first recorded historical mention dates to 979. Later it was the residence of Rainulf I of Aversa and the Robert Guiscard, who built the Norman Tower and the Treasure Hall. Emperor Frederick II restored the construction to use it as residence for his mistress Bianca Lancia, while under the Angevins it was used mainly as prison. Later, from 1464 to 1485, the fortress was the residence of the exiled Albanian condottiero Skanderbeg. The castle was largely rebuilt in the late 15th century by Ferdinand I. According to legend, the castle is home to the ghost of Bianca Lancia (popularly known as "Biancalancia"), whose sighs can be heard especially in the winter.
  • The Tomb of Rothari (Baptistry of San Giovanni in Tumba), a baptistery dating from the 12th century accessible from the 18th century church of St. Peter. The portal has notable reliefs depicting biblical events.
  • The church of Santa Maria Maggiore (11th and 12th centuries). The façade has blind arcades and a baldachin portal with sculpted frames. The interior has a nave and two aisles, divided by columns with sculpted capitals. The walls have Byzantine-style frescoes.
  • Pulsano Abbey, at 8 kilometres (5 mi) from the city. It was built in 591 over a Pagan temple and was largely destroyed by an earthquake in 1646.

Economy

Monte Sant'Angelo's economy is based on agriculture, including the production of cereals and olive oil, as well as livestock breeding such as goats and sheep in the Gargano area.[7][8] Tourism plays a significant role, driven by the Sanctuary of Saint Michael the Archangel, a UNESCO World Heritage site, with recent growth highlighted by the town's designation as Puglia's Capital of Culture in 2024 and hosting events like Ravello Lab 2025.[9][10]

Monte Sant'Angelo Employment

Employment

Employment in Monte Sant'Angelo is primarily in the services sector (approximately 64%), followed by industry (23%) and agriculture (13%), based on 2011 census data. [11] Many businesses, including stores, bakeries, and restaurants, are family-owned. Agriculture involves farmland ownership and cattle raising, while services are bolstered by tourism related to the Sanctuary of San Michele Arcangelo. The town's population of 11,239 as of 2023 has been declining at an average annual rate of -1.34% since 2018, partly due to limited local job opportunities and high youth unemployment (77.3% for ages 15-29 in 2011). [12] [13] Many young people emigrate to find work elsewhere, contributing to an aging population where 27.6% are aged 65 and over, compared to 61% in working ages 15-64, as of 2024. [14] In Foggia province, the unemployment rate stood at 16% in 2024, higher than Puglia's regional average of 9.3%. [15] [16] Recent developments in sustainable tourism have created new jobs in hospitality, tour guiding, and local crafts. [10]

Transportation

Monte Sant'Angelo can be reached by road through the Foggia-Monte Sant'Angelo SP.55 provincial road. The SS 89 state road (Garganica) passes through the frazione of Macchia. The town is served by regional bus services operated by SITA Sud, with lines connecting to Foggia (journey time approximately 1.5 hours, 3 daily services), Manfredonia, and San Giovanni Rotondo.[17] There is no railway station in Monte Sant'Angelo; the nearest is Foggia railway station, from which bus connections are available.[18] The closest airport is Foggia Gino Lisa Airport (FOG), about 50 km away; Bari Karol Wojtyła Airport (BRI) is approximately 150 km to the southeast.[19]
