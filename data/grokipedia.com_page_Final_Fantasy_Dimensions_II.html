Final Fantasy Dimensions II

Final Fantasy Dimensions II , known in Japan as Final Fantasy Legends II: Toki no Suishō (Japanese: ファイナルファンタジーレジェンズII 時空ノ水晶, Hepburn: Fainaru Fantajī Rejenzu Tsū: Toki no Suishō ; lit. "Crystal of Space-Time"), is a video game developed by Matrix Software and published by Square Enix for Android and iOS devices. It is the second game released in Japan with the "Final Fantasy Legends" title after Final Fantasy Legends: Hikari to Yami no Senshi (which was localized worldwide under the name Final Fantasy Dimensions). It revolves around traveling through time in order to save the world from a god.[1] The game was initially released as a free-to-play title on February 12, 2015, under the name Final Fantasy Legends: Toki no Suishō. Following a massive update, it was re-branded on November 2, 2016, as Final Fantasy Legends II in Japan. On October 31, 2017, the old free-to-play version of the game was shut down, and a paid version was re-launched on November 1, 2017, under the name Final Fantasy Legends II: Toki no Suishō in Japan, which was released worldwide as Final Fantasy Dimensions II. The paid version remains available on iOS and Android as of 2025.[2]

Gameplay

The game uses a turn-based battle system, similar to the one used in Final Fantasy X. Only a maximum of three party members can fight in battle, with the character Mootie taking up a support role. In battle, party member commands are present on a dual-sided command ring, one side with the character's normal battle abilities and the other for summon special attacks. This allows the three members to have up to five actions on each side; a regular physical attack in the ring's middle and four abilities from summon stones equipped to the party members before entering a battle zone. In addition, each character has three consumption meters that can be filled in battle. Characters receive 20%-60% of a consumption meter point for each action they make and can use consumption meter points to summon their equipped summons. Summons play a pivotal role in a character's stats and abilities. Abilities may only be used when equipped with a certain phantom stone, known as signets tied to eidolons. In addition, character stats can only be increased by equipping weapons or accessories, or obtaining memory fragments and upgrading summon phantom stones. Signets are obtained through story missions, subquests, and in-game shops, and provide passive bonuses and abilities that level up with AP earned in battles. Summons can be evolved up to Rank III using specific evolution items. All signets have a maximum rank, with stronger eidolons offering more powerful abilities and summons usable once per battle after filling a three-bar gauge. Mootie can equip any signet and grows stronger as the party levels up, benefiting from buffs and affected by debuffs. Battle encounters are presented in rounds that start once the player enters a battle zone on the world map. Once the player enters a zone, they will have to fight a barrage of enemies until they reach the end. The player can choose to escape a battle, if they find themselves overwhelmed by the enemies, but any loot obtained during the battle will be forfeit. The loot will also be forfeit if they lose the battle. After every battle, the player is rewarded with items, experience, and occasionally signets or evolution materials. Rewards are fixed based on zone completion, with additional items from subquests. The Tower of Babil is an optional multi-floor challenge where players earn Babil points for use in an exchange shop for items and enhancements. Memory fragments obtained from hourglass stations or quests can be exchanged at the memory keeper for permanent stat boosts to characters. By completing quests, the player may obtain weapons, rings, phantom stones, and materials to upgrade their signets.

Final Fantasy Dimensions II

Plot

The story of Final Fantasy Dimensions II centers on Morrow, a young aspiring adventurer from the village of Navos on a small central island.[3] His life changes when a meteorite crashes nearby, from which emerges Aemo, a mysterious amnesiac girl from the future. Together with companions they recruit from various time periods—including the seasoned adventurer Wrieg, the dwarf Parai, the ninja Maina, and the elven princess Jornee—they embark on a journey through time and space across approximately eight historical eras to prevent entities from disrupting the fabric of history and save the world from destruction.[3] [4] Along the way, the protagonists collect Signet Crystals, which enable them to summon powerful eidolons and monsters as allies in battle.[4] The narrative unfolds across two epic story arcs: the main storyline The Crystal of Time and the additional Eureka, World of Primals.[5]

Development

Concept and creation

The game was originally announced in 2014 as Final Fantasy Legends: The Space-Time Crystal, and its two protagonists went by the names Tomorrow and Emo instead of Morrow and Aemo in the Dimensions II version. The game was directed, written, and designed by Takashi Tokita,[1] based in part on his original plans for the cancelled Chrono Break, a proposed sequel to Chrono Trigger.[6] Tokita noted that the balance between the three characters and Aemo's character settings were among the elements from his original plans.[7] The game also took inspiration from classic Super NES titles such as Final Fantasy V and VI to bring players a sense of nostalgia.[1]

Visuals and music

The game's character designs and image illustrations were handled by CyDesignation.[8] CyDesignation is an art design firm founded by former Square Enix artists.[9] The soundtrack was composed and produced by Naoshi Mizuta with over fifty tracks.[1] The game's main theme is Timeless Tomorrow performed by the singer Lia.[10] Mizuta crafted the music to make players feel that when they travel, they have entered a new time, this made sure that all the music was fresh and new.[1]

Release

The game was initially released as a free-to-play title with in-app purchases available.[11] Those who pre-registered got a white chocobo signet within the game.[12] The game also features cameos of characters and props from other games, such as Oersted the knight from the video game Live A Live, and a spell book from The Irregular at Magic High School.[13][14]

Reception

PublicationScore
TouchArcade4/5[15]
TouchArcade praised the game's large amount of content, but noted that the game can drag because it was originally a free-to-play title and is best played in small doses.[15] Kotaku described the time travel story as interesting but presented in the most boring way possible, and noted that the absence of a stamina system allows endless grinding, making the game too easy.[4] On the iOS App Store, it holds a 3.9 out of 5 rating from approximately 100 users (as of October 2025).[16] On Google Play, it has a 3.3 out of 5 rating from about 500 reviews (as of October 2025).[2] A 2023 review on RPG Ranked praised the characters (9.5/10) and battle system (8/10) but criticized the music (5/10).[17] By May 2015, the Japanese version had reached 1.5 million downloads since release.[18]
