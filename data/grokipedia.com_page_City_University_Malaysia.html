City University Malaysia

City University Malaysia is a private higher education institution founded in April 1984 as Petaling Jaya Community College by a group of scholars experienced in local and international academia, initially specializing in preparatory programs for transfer to American universities.[1] It achieved university college status before being granted full university recognition in 2016 by the Malaysian Ministry of Higher Education, following decades of operational excellence.[1][2] The university maintains campuses in Petaling Jaya, Johor Bahru, and other locations, serving a diverse student body that has produced over 38,000 graduates from more than 60 nationalities.[2] It offers over 70 programs spanning foundation, undergraduate, master's, and PhD levels in fields such as business, engineering, information technology, creative arts, and architecture, emphasizing industry-driven curricula to foster employability skills like professionalism, communication, and ethical decision-making.[2][3] Accredited by the Malaysian Qualifications Agency (MQA) and holding a QS 5-star rating, City University Malaysia prioritizes international partnerships with institutions in the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, and Asia, building on its early success in transferring students to over 100 U.S. universities, including Ivy League schools.[1][4][5]

History

Founding and Early Development

City University Malaysia was established in April 1984 as Petaling Jaya Community College (PJCC) by a group of scholars experienced in local and international universities.[1] The institution was registered under the former Education Act 1961 with the Ministry of Education, marking it as one of Malaysia's earliest private higher education providers amid growing demand for accessible post-secondary options.[6] In its initial phase, PJCC focused on providing academic preparation aligned with the American degree system through partnerships with the Consortium of American Universities and Colleges (CAUC), enabling students to transfer credits to U.S. institutions.[1] Programs emphasized foundational studies in fields such as engineering, business, and psychology, with the curriculum designed to facilitate seamless progression to overseas universities.[1] This approach addressed the limited domestic university spots available at the time, positioning PJCC as a pathway for Malaysian students seeking international education.[2] Early growth involved successfully transferring over 20 batches of students to more than 100 American universities, including Ivy League schools, establishing PJCC as a pioneer in student mobility among Malaysian private colleges.[1] Enrollment expanded from modest beginnings, building a reputation for quality preparation despite operational challenges typical of nascent private institutions in 1980s Malaysia.[2] By the late 1980s and early 1990s, the college had laid groundwork for broader international collaborations, though it remained primarily a community college focused on pre-university and diploma-level offerings.[1]

Transition to University Status and Expansion

In 2002, Petaling Jaya Community College was upgraded to university college status by the Malaysian Ministry of Higher Education and rebranded as City University College of Science and Technology, reflecting its expanded academic scope under new management.[7] This transition marked a shift from primarily preparatory programs for American degrees to a broader range of higher education offerings, including diplomas and degrees in fields such as business, engineering, and information technology.[1] The institution continued to grow through enhanced infrastructure and program diversification, forging partnerships with over 100 universities across the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, and Asia to facilitate credit transfers and joint programs.[1] By 2016, following a demonstrated track record of 32 years in education delivery, it was granted full university status by the Ministry of Higher Education, officially becoming City University Malaysia.[1][5] This upgrade enabled the conferral of its own degrees and positioned it among Malaysia's established private universities, with enrollment expanding to support diverse undergraduate and postgraduate programs.[8] Post-2016 expansion included the establishment of additional campuses, notably in Johor Bahru, to extend access in southern Malaysia while maintaining the Petaling Jaya main campus as the administrative hub.[9] The university broadened its international collaborations, signing memoranda of understanding with institutions in Indonesia, Sri Lanka, and beyond, aimed at student exchanges, joint research, and faculty development.[10][11] By 2025, these efforts had resulted in over 70 programs across four campuses, emphasizing industry-relevant curricula in engineering, business, and health sciences.[4]

Campuses and Infrastructure

Petaling Jaya Main Campus

The Petaling Jaya Main Campus, situated at Menara City U, No. 8, Jalan 51A/223, 46100 Petaling Jaya, Selangor Darul Ehsan, Malaysia, functions as the primary hub for City University Malaysia's operations.[12] This location in Petaling Jaya, a suburb adjacent to Kuala Lumpur, provides convenient access via major highways and public transport links, supporting the university's role in delivering foundational and advanced programs since its inception.[12] Originally founded in April 1984 as Petaling Jaya Community College under the Education Act 1961 with registration from the Ministry of Higher Education, the campus initially emphasized preparation for American degree transfers through partnerships with the Consortium of American Universities and Colleges.[1] Over subsequent decades, it evolved to host over 20 batches of students dispatched to more than 100 U.S. institutions, including Ivy League universities across disciplines such as business, engineering, and sciences, marking it as a pioneer among Malaysian private higher education providers in facilitating such international pathways.[1] The campus retained its central status through expansions in international collaborations with universities in the UK, Australia, Indonesia, China, and Malaysian public institutions, culminating in the institution's elevation to full university status in 2016 after 39 years of development.[1] Infrastructure at the campus encompasses modern classrooms, lecture halls, a multipurpose hall for events and assemblies, specialized computer laboratories, science laboratories equipped for practical training, and a comprehensive library resource center.[8] These facilities support a student population drawn from over 60 nationalities, fostering a multicultural academic environment amid the university's broader network of four campuses.[4] The setup aligns with the institution's emphasis on practical, industry-oriented education, though specific capacity metrics for the Petaling Jaya site alone remain integrated within the overall enrollment of approximately 7,500 students across locations.[4]

Johor Bahru Campus

The Johor Bahru Campus of City University Malaysia is situated at G-01, Ground Floor, Menara Selia, Persiaran Southkey 1, Kota Southkey, 80150 Johor Bahru, Johor, serving as one of the university's four regional facilities alongside those in Petaling Jaya, Cyberjaya, and Kota Kinabalu.[9][4] This campus supports the institution's expansion to provide accessible higher education in southern Malaysia, focusing on diploma, bachelor's, master's, Doctor of Business Administration (DBA), and PhD programs tailored to local and international standards.[9][13] Academic offerings at the campus are organized across schools including Business & Management, Education & Liberal Studies, and Information Technology, with curricula emphasizing industry-relevant skills and certifications from professional bodies.[13] Students benefit from the university's established twinning arrangements with institutions in Australia, the United Kingdom, and the United States, enabling credit transfers and dual qualifications, a model pioneered by the parent institution since its origins in American Degree Programs.[9] These programs align with the university's QS 5-Star rating for teaching, facilities, and employability, though specific enrollment figures for Johor Bahru remain undisclosed in public records.[4] Facilities on campus include a dedicated library for research access, a multipurpose hall for events and lectures, equipped classrooms, a campus cafe for student amenities, and free accommodation in two on-site apartments to support residential learners.[14] The setup prioritizes practical learning environments, complementing the university's overall infrastructure rated as competitive under Malaysia's SETARA framework.[4] This configuration facilitates hands-on education in a compact urban setting, distinct from the larger main campus in Petaling Jaya.[4]

Governance and Administration

Leadership Structure

The leadership of City University Malaysia is structured around a governing board and an executive academic team, with the Vice-Chancellor serving as the primary academic and operational head. Prof. Dr. Rosnizah Bt. Shaari was appointed Vice-Chancellor in October 2024, overseeing strategic direction, international partnerships, and campus expansions.[15][16][11] The Board of Governors provides oversight on policy and governance, chaired by Dato' Dr. Husaini Omar as of December 2024.[17] YBhg. Datuk Mohamed Zaini Amran holds the position of Chairman, contributing to leadership vision and student engagement initiatives, as evidenced by his participation in the Chairman Talk Series in February 2025.[18][19] Deputy Vice-Chancellors assist in specialized domains, including research, student affairs, and administration. Assoc. Prof. Dr. Norman Chu serves as a Deputy Vice-Chancellor, while Prof. Dato' Dr. Roselan Baki holds the role for Student Affairs, focusing on welfare and industry relations as of August 2024.[20][21] Earlier records indicate additional deputies, such as James Nga for operations.[22] Academic governance likely involves a senate or equivalent body for curriculum approval and quality assurance, aligned with Malaysian higher education standards, though detailed membership is not publicly specified in recent official communications.[23]

Organizational Policies

City University Malaysia implements a range of governance policies to ensure ethical operations, risk management, and compliance with legal standards, as outlined in its research and administrative frameworks. These policies address areas such as corruption prevention, human rights, and sustainable practices, reflecting the institution's commitment to transparency and accountability.[24] The university's Corruption Risk Management policy focuses on identifying and mitigating bribery and corruption risks across operations, including procurement and partnerships, in alignment with Malaysian anti-corruption laws. Similarly, the Modern Slavery Policy prohibits forced labor, human trafficking, and exploitative practices in supply chains and campus activities, with mechanisms for due diligence and reporting violations. The Student Safety Policy prioritizes the protection of student welfare, encompassing health, emergency response, and campus security protocols to safeguard against physical and psychological harms.[24] In research governance, City University Malaysia adheres to a Research Ethics Handbook that mandates the protection of human participants' dignity, rights, and welfare in all studies conducted under its auspices. This includes obtaining informed consent, minimizing risks, and establishing institutional review processes for ethical approval. The Ethical and Sustainable Investment Policy guides fund management by tracking environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors, such as carbon footprints and UN principle conformity, to avoid investments in unethical sectors.[25][26] Data privacy is governed by the university's Privacy Policy, which requires collection of personal information (e.g., names, identification numbers, health records) only for academic and administrative purposes, with mandatory consent for sensitive data disclosure like race or religion. Data may be shared with government agencies or service providers only with consent or legal permission, and refusal to provide data can impact enrollment. Additionally, the Sustainable Donation Policy ensures contributions align with institutional values, while broader sustainability efforts integrate United Nations Sustainable Development Goals into curricula and operations, emphasizing resource efficiency in energy, water, and waste management.[27][28][24]

Academic Programs

Faculties and Schools

City University Malaysia structures its academic delivery through a network of faculties and schools, encompassing disciplines from health sciences and engineering to business and creative arts. These units offer diploma, bachelor's, master's, and doctoral programs, with a focus on practical skills and industry relevance, supported by the university's QS 5-Star rating for teaching and employability.[4][29] The Faculty of Allied Health Sciences emphasizes training for medical professionals in diagnosing, preventing, and treating illnesses, including programs in nursing, physiotherapy, and medical laboratory technology. It equips students with clinical competencies through hands-on training and partnerships with healthcare providers.[30] The Faculty of Architecture & Built Environment covers urban planning, construction management, and architectural design, fostering skills in sustainable development and project execution via studio-based learning and real-world simulations.[29] The Faculty of Business Management (also referred to as School of Business & Management) delivers curricula in accounting, finance, marketing, and entrepreneurship, integrating case studies and internships to align with global business demands.[29][31] The Faculty of Creative Industries focuses on media production, graphic design, and digital arts, promoting innovation through technology-integrated projects and collaborations with creative sector professionals.[29] The Faculty of Education & Liberal Studies develops expertise in pedagogy, communication, public relations, and advertising, with programs emphasizing language proficiency and ethical leadership for educational and media roles.[32][29] Additional specialized units include the Faculty of Engineering, which offers programs in civil, electrical, and mechanical engineering with laboratory-based research; the Faculty of Sciences, addressing foundational and applied sciences; the Faculty of Information Technology (or School of Information Technology), covering software engineering, cybersecurity, and data analytics; and the School of Hospitality & Tourism, training in hotel management and event planning through experiential learning. These entities collectively support over 70 programs across the university's campuses.[29][31]

Degree Offerings and Curriculum

City University Malaysia offers over 70 academic programs at foundation, diploma, bachelor's, master's, and doctoral levels, spanning disciplines including business management, information technology, nursing and allied health sciences, architecture and built environment, creative industries, education and liberal sciences, engineering, hospitality and tourism, and language studies.[4] These programs are structured to deliver practical, career-oriented education, with a focus on developing industry-relevant skills that support a reported graduate employability rate of 99.9%.[4] Undergraduate offerings include foundation and diploma programs as entry pathways, followed by bachelor's degrees typically spanning 3 to 4 years and requiring 120 to 140 credits.[33] Bachelor's curricula integrate core theoretical coursework, practical applications, and elective modules tailored to specific fields; for instance, the Bachelor of Business Administration (Honours) emphasizes management principles, organizational behavior, and business strategy over 120 credits in full- or part-time modes, while the Bachelor of Nursing (Honours) incorporates clinical training and health sciences fundamentals across 140 credits in 48 months.[33] Other examples encompass the Bachelor in Computer Science (Artificial Intelligence) (Honours), focusing on AI algorithms, machine learning, and cybersecurity within information technology, and programs in engineering management and hospitality management that include project-based learning and internships to align with professional standards.[33] Postgraduate programs at the master's level feature coursework combined with research components, such as the Master of Education, which requires modules in Educational Research Methodology, Development of Curriculum and Instruction, Philosophy of Education, and a research project, selectable from specialized electives.[34] Doctoral offerings emphasize advanced research supervision in fields like business, engineering, and education, building on master's-level inquiry to produce original contributions, though specific credit structures vary by discipline.[4] Across all levels, curricula incorporate continuous assessment, skill-building workshops, and alignment with Malaysian Qualifications Framework standards to ensure competency in real-world applications.[4]

Accreditation, Rankings, and Quality Assurance

National Accreditation and Ratings

City University Malaysia's academic programs, spanning foundation to doctoral levels, are accredited by the Malaysian Qualifications Agency (MQA), the national body responsible for quality assurance in higher education, with accreditations carrying no expiry date for non-professional qualifications since 2007.[35][36] This accreditation verifies alignment with Malaysia's National Qualifications Framework, emphasizing learning outcomes, curriculum design, and assessment standards.[37] In the SETARA 2022 evaluation conducted by the Ministry of Higher Education Malaysia, the university earned a 'Competitive' rating, positioning it among institutions demonstrating solid performance across teaching and learning (weighted at 40%), research (15%), and resources (5%), among other metrics.[35][38] This classification reflects improvements in industry-focused education and graduate employability, though it trails top-tier public universities in overall research output and infrastructure scale.[39] Earlier, in the 2017 SETARA assessment, it received a 4-star (Excellent) rating under the prior star-based system, highlighting strengths in program delivery and student support at that time.[40][8]

International Rankings and Recognitions

City University Malaysia has been awarded a 5-star (Excellent) rating by QS Stars, an international university evaluation system that assesses institutions on criteria such as teaching quality, employability, academic development, and online learning.[4] This rating, achieved for the first time as of recent announcements, underscores the university's performance in targeted areas of higher education delivery.[35] Additionally, specific programs like Occupational Safety and Health have received 5-star QS ratings in categories including teaching, employability, and facilities.[41] The QS Stars framework provides a holistic audit beyond traditional rankings, with City University Malaysia demonstrating strengths in student-focused metrics rather than broad research impact.[42] While the university maintains profiles on platforms like QS and Times Higher Education, it does not hold positions in their flagship world or regional university rankings, which prioritize metrics such as academic reputation, citations, and international outlook among top-tier global institutions.[5][43] No other major international rankings, such as those from CWUR or ARWU, list City University Malaysia in their global tables as of 2025.[44]

Partnerships and Collaborations

Domestic and Regional Partnerships

City University Malaysia has established domestic partnerships primarily with Malaysian public sector institutions to support professional development and training programs. In October 2024, the university signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Institut Tadbiran Awam Negara (INTAN), Malaysia's national institute of public administration, to facilitate higher education access and specialized training for civil servants.[45] This collaboration extends to joint initiatives with INTAN and Jabatan Perkhidmatan Awam (JPA), including the launch of the H.E.M.A.T. framework in April 2025, aimed at public sector reform and skill enhancement.[46] Additional domestic ties include an MoU with Seshan Design, a Malaysian architecture firm, signed in September 2024, focusing on industry-academic integration in design and built environment fields.[47] Regionally, City University Malaysia has prioritized collaborations within ASEAN, particularly with Indonesian institutions, to promote academic exchange and joint programs. In September 2025, the university entered an MoU with ASEAN Accounting Academy (ASEANACA) and 14 Indonesian universities, enabling student and faculty mobility, joint research, and curriculum development.[10] Earlier efforts include a June 2025 MoU with 14 institutions in Cimahi, Indonesia, for cross-border academic and research cooperation, and an August 2025 agreement with HUD Institute Indonesia alongside 22 Bandung-based universities and organizations.[48] These partnerships emphasize practical outcomes such as dual-degree pathways and internships, aligning with ASEAN's regional integration goals, though specific metrics on participant numbers or program enrollments remain undisclosed in public records.[49]

International Engagements and Recent Initiatives

City University Malaysia engages internationally through partnerships with universities in approximately 20 countries, enabling initiatives such as the International Degree Pathways Programme (IPP), a foundation or pre-master's route designed to bridge academic and English proficiency gaps for students from regions including Bangladesh, China, France, India, and Thailand, facilitating progression to bachelor's or master's degrees at the institution.[50] These collaborations emphasize student and staff exchanges, joint programs for curriculum innovation, and increased mobility to foster cross-cultural learning and access to external funding opportunities.[51] Recent initiatives have focused on deepening ties in Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. On June 14, 2025, the university signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with 14 higher education institutions in Cimahi, Bandung, under the Pimpinan Perguruan Tinggi Cimahi coordination, to promote academic exchanges, joint research and publications, staff and student mobility, curriculum development, and collaborative seminars and workshops.[48] In September 2025, City University Malaysia formalized another MoU with the ASEAN Academy of Accounting (ASEANACA) and 14 Indonesian universities, targeting student and faculty exchanges, industry-linked sessions and visits, joint research in technology, entrepreneurship, and architecture, as well as capacity-building workshops to enhance regional academic and cultural integration.[10] Further underscoring its commitment to global outreach, the university hosted a high-impact international forum on sustainable startups on June 23, 2025, convening experts and participants to explore innovation in sustainability-driven entrepreneurship.[52] These efforts align with broader strategies for internationalization, including strategic engagements pursued in London in October 2025 to bolster collaborations and global academic networks.[11]

Research, Innovation, and Achievements

Research Focus Areas

City University Malaysia's Research Management Centre (RMC) oversees research efforts, aiming to foster a robust research culture, enhance multi-disciplinary initiatives, and align outputs with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly emphasizing resource efficiency and sustainable development.[53][28] The university prioritizes ten core research domains, spanning engineering, business, applied sciences, and creative fields, to support collaborative R&D with public agencies, industry partners, and international institutions.[54][53] These areas include:
  • Engineering Disciplines: Mechanical engineering and civil & structural engineering form foundational focuses, targeting practical innovations in design, materials, and infrastructure resilience.[54]
  • Technology and Information Systems: Information technology research explores digital solutions, data management, and computational advancements.[54]
  • Business and Social Sciences: Business, management, accounting, language, and communication studies emphasize strategic decision-making, organizational dynamics, and effective knowledge dissemination.[54]
  • Built Environment and Applied Fields: Architecture, built environment, and applied sciences address urban planning, environmental sustainability, and interdisciplinary applications in health and materials.[54]
  • Education, Creative, and Service Sectors: Education and liberal studies, creative industries, and hospitality & tourism investigate pedagogical innovations, media production, and service sector efficiencies.[54]
Internal research funding, provided through the university's Internal Research Fund (IRF), incentivizes staff-led projects in these areas, offering up to RM25,000 for science and technology endeavors and RM5,000 for non-science initiatives as of the latest policy framework.[55] This support mechanism, established to diversify funding sources and promote consultancy, underscores the institution's commitment to translating research into societal impact via cross-sector partnerships.[53]

Notable Accomplishments and Contributions

The Research Management Centre (RMC) at City University Malaysia oversees research promotion, intellectual property management, and collaborations with government, industry, and academic partners to foster cross-disciplinary research.[53] Internal research grants support faculty and staff projects, offering up to RM25,000 for science and technology fields and up to RM5,000 for non-science fields, enabling targeted investigations in areas such as mechanical engineering, information technology, and business management.[55] The university contributes to scholarly dissemination through the City University eJournal of Academic Research (CUeJAR), a quarterly, double-blind peer-reviewed, open-access publication launched to feature original research across disciplines including allied health sciences, creative industries, and education.[56] In August 2025, City University Malaysia signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with ORS Technologies to advance joint research and innovation in artificial intelligence and cybersecurity, facilitating technology transfer and practical applications for regional challenges. These efforts align with broader initiatives, such as student-led sustainability projects and grant applications through platforms like Trialect, aimed at enhancing research capacity and global engagement.[57]

Student Life and Support Services

Admissions, Scholarships, and Diversity

City University Malaysia admits students through an application process that requires submission of academic transcripts, certificates, and other documents verified by relevant authorities. For undergraduate programs, applicants typically need a minimum of 60% aggregate or a GPA of 2.00 out of 4.00 in secondary qualifications such as SPM or equivalent.[58] Postgraduate admissions require a relevant bachelor's degree with similar minimum thresholds and may include resumes or employment verification.[59] International students must provide a passport valid for at least 12 months, a passport-sized photo, scanned passport pages, and certified transcripts, with non-English documents translated accordingly; an application fee and EMGS visa processing fee of approximately RM3,150 are required.[59] The university processes applications within 6-8 weeks to issue a Letter of Acceptance, followed by a Visa Approval Letter for embassy visa applications, with student pass renewals handled 60 days prior to expiry.[59] Malaysian students submit similar academic proofs via certified copies, often through online forms or direct consultation.[60] The university allocates up to RM20 million annually in scholarships to support student access.[61] These include merit-based awards such as the City University High Achiever Scholarship for top SPM performers, covering foundation or degree levels for one year based on exam results, and the Pro-Chancellor or Vice-Chancellor Scholarships for outstanding academic or leadership performance across degree programs.[61] Additional options like the CityU Outstanding Student Scholarship and sports or industry excellence awards target both Malaysian and international enrollees, with eligibility tied to consistent performance rather than financial need alone.[61] Student diversity at City University Malaysia encompasses over 1,500 international students from nearly 60 countries, speaking more than 50 languages, alongside local Malaysian enrollees from varied ethnic backgrounds including Malay, Chinese, and Indian communities typical of the nation's demographics.[62] This multicultural composition, with examples of students from Egypt, Yemen, Bangladesh, China, and Iran, fosters cross-cultural interactions, though specific ethnic breakdowns for the total student body exceeding 7,000 are not publicly detailed beyond broad international representation.[62][38]

Campus Activities and Facilities

City University Malaysia operates campuses in Petaling Jaya (main campus), Johor Bahru, Kota Kinabalu, and Cyberjaya, each equipped with academic and recreational facilities to support student learning and engagement.[4][63] Key learning facilities across campuses include classrooms, lecture halls, a multi-purpose hall (MPH), an auditorium, computer laboratories, science laboratories, a central library, photography studios, fashion studios, and culinary arts classrooms designed to simulate real-world professional environments.[64] The library system spans multiple locations, with the main campus library situated at Block A, Level 3, providing resources for research and study.[65] Dining options feature on-campus restaurants and cafes such as Aida Brasserie and Table 8, open from 8 a.m. on weekdays, staffed by trained food and beverage personnel to cater to students, faculty, and visitors.[64] Specialized amenities include a state-of-the-art e-sports centre for virtual competitions and collaborative social spaces in cafes to foster interactions.[64] Health services are accessible via the nearby Qualitas Health Clinic, offering primary care less than 1 km from the main campus.[64] Accommodation is provided primarily for Malaysian students residing more than 50 km from campus, with priority allocation; options include apartments like Mentari Court and Damai Court, featuring furnished rooms with security and rates from RM 400 to RM 900 monthly.[66][14][63] Student activities are coordinated through over 20 clubs and societies, overseen by the Studentsâ€™ Representative Council (SRC), a democratic body that represents student interests via elections, campaigns, and media engagement.[67] Sports clubs promote physical fitness and teamwork, including the City U Football Club, City U Chargers Rugby Club, City U Netball Club, City U Badminton Club, City U Taekwondo Club, City U Futsal Club, and City U Bowling Club.[67] Interest-based and cultural groups encompass the Animation, Comics & Games Club (ACGC), Music Club, Photography Club, City U Cultural Club (CUCC), Islamic Society, and Hindu Cultural Society, alongside academic clubs such as Biomedic Club, Business Club, and IT Club, enabling leadership development and skill-building.[67] Support services emphasize inclusivity, with professional counselling, mental health workshops, and crisis intervention available; disability accommodations include wheelchair ramps, assistive technology, sign language interpreters, and academic adjustments like extended exam times.[64] Peer mentoring and tutoring programs further aid academic success, contributing to a collaborative campus atmosphere.[64]
