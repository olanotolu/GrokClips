Phora

Marco Anthony Archer (born October 11, 1994), better known by his stage name Phora, is an American rapper and former tattoo artist from Anaheim, California. In 2011, he founded his own record label, Yours Truly.[1] His debut studio album, Yours Truly Forever (2017), peaked at number 44 on the Billboard 200 chart.[2] After leaving Warner Bros. Records in 2019, Phora continued releasing music independently.[3] In August 2025, he filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection.[4]

Early life

Marco Archer was born in Anaheim, California. Before his career in music, he worked as a tattoo artist which proved to be relatively lucrative and aided in the funding of his rap career. He has survived a stabbing at age 15 and a shooting.[5][6][7][8] On August 25, 2015, while he was driving on the 210 freeway in Pasadena around 2 a.m., a gray Infiniti sedan pulled up alongside Archer, who was driving home with his then-girlfriend. Three bullets from a .45 caliber pistol were fired into his back and neck, narrowly missing his vertebra. The crime remains unsolved.[9]

Career

In February 2017, Phora announced he signed a major label deal with Warner Bros. Records.[10] Phora has released numerous albums and mixtapes, many of which relate to his past struggles with depression. These include Therapy, Still a Kid, One Life to Live, Sincerely Yours, Nights Like These, Angels with Broken Wings, With Love, With Love 2, Heartbreak Hotel, The Butterfly Effect, and his debut studio album Yours Truly Forever, which peaked at number 44 on the Billboard 200.[2] In 2018, he released a follow-up to his debut album, titled Love Is Hell, which peaked at number 87 on the Billboard 200 and featured artists such as Trippie Redd, G-Eazy, Tory Lanez, 6lack, and others. In July 2019, Phora left his record deal with Warner Bros. Records and subsequently released his new album entitled Bury Me With Dead Roses.[3] In 2020, he released his fourth studio album, With Love 2, which peaked at number 160 on the Billboard 200 and featured DaniLeigh, Toosii, Ty Dolla $ign, JhenÃ© Aiko, Kehlani, and Tyla Yaweh. In February 2021, he broke his streak of annual releases with the EP Heartbreak Hotel. In December 2022, he released his fifth studio album The Butterfly Effect. On September 15, 2023, he released his sixth studio album and first R&B album, Lucky Me. In April 2024, he released the EP heartbreak hotel 2. Throughout 2025, Phora has issued several singles, including "Days Before October," "Autumn," "The Lonely Ones," "Sinner Pt. 5," and "Just Like Cole." In August 2025, Phora's company, Phora LLC, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy protection to restructure debt that it owes to Warner Records, amongst several other creditors.[4]

Musical style

Phora's music is characterized by emotional and introspective lyricism, often exploring themes of love, personal struggles, heartbreak, and self-reflection. Blending hip hop and R&B elements, his style draws from 90s hip-hop influences, delivering raw, poetic narratives about life's challenges. Growing up, Archer was strongly influenced by graffiti and his father to pursue music.[1] In his song "The World" from his album Angels with Broken Wings (2015), he names J. Cole, Hopsin, and Logic as the only rappers he respects. His sound is also shaped by longtime collaborator and producer Anthro Beats, with whom he co-developed the "Yours Truly" aesthetic across multiple albums.
