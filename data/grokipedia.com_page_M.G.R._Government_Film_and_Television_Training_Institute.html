M.G.R. Government Film and Television Training Institute

The M.G.R. Government Film and Television Training Institute , popularly known earlier as Madras Film Institute , is Asia's first-ever film and television training institute. Established in 1945 as Adyar Film Institute , it is one of the pioneer film institutes in India.[1] It is in Tharamani, Chennai, and is run by the Department of Information and Public Relations, Government of Tamil Nadu.[2] The diplomas are approved by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), while the certificates are awarded by the Department of Technical Education, Government of Tamil Nadu.[2][3] The incumbent president of the Institute is Trotsky Marudu, who was appointed by the Government on 11 November 2024.[4]

History

The institute was established in 1945 as Adyar Film Institute and was a part of the Central Polytechnic.[1] It grew into a specialized Institute of Film Technology in 1959. In 1965, it moved into its present campus in the Tharamani area of Chennai. At the time, the campus was spread over 54 acres, which has reduced over the following decades due to rapid urbanisation. Parts of land were given to IIT Madras and many IT companies, bringing it down to about 10 acres as of 2009.[5] The institute is located within M.G.R. Film City in Taramani, an integrated film studio complex established by the government in 1994 to provide filmmaking facilities. M.G.R. Film City faced operational issues and was closed around 2006, but revival efforts continue; in August 2025, Chief Minister M. K. Stalin inaugurated a dedicated shooting floor there.[6] The institute was known as the Film and Television Institute of Tamil Nadu before being renamed M.G.R. Film and Television Training Institute in 2006, after former Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu M. G. Ramachandran (1917â€“1987), who was a prominent actor in Tamil cinema.[7] In 2021, it received its current full name, Tamil Nadu Government M.G.R. Film and Television Training Institute.[8] On 16 October 1997, Queen Elizabeth II visited MGR Film City and watched the filming of Kamal Haasan's Tamil movie Marudhanayagam.[9] The acting course that started in 1971, at the behest of M.G.R., was discontinued in 2002.[5] Talks to revive the course have been ongoing for many years.[10]

Courses

The institute offers the following four-year Bachelor of Visual Arts (BVA) degree courses, each with 14 seats:[11]
  1. Cinematography
  2. Digital Intermediate
  3. Audiography
  4. Direction and Screenplay Writing
  5. Film Editing
  6. Animation and Visual Effects
Eligibility requires passing 10+2 or equivalent with at least 50% aggregate marks (45% for reserved categories).[11]
