D'Wan Mathis

D'Wan Terence Mathis (born December 21, 2000) is an American college football quarterback for the Davenport Panthers. He began his career at Georgia Bulldogs football in 2019 and transferred to Temple Owls football in 2021.[1]

Kaleb Mathis

Early life

Mathis attended Oak Park High School in Oak Park, Michigan, where he played quarterback under head coach Greg Carter.[2] He was considered a four-star prospect by major recruiting sites.[3][4][5] Mathis received offers from 11 major programs, including Ohio State, Michigan State, Iowa State, and Oregon.[6] In early December 2018, he verbally committed to Ohio State before flipping his commitment to the University of Georgia on December 19.[7][8] As a senior in 2018, he led the Knights to a 9â€“2 record, completing 62.5% of his passes for 1,509 yards and 20 touchdowns, while adding 305 rushing yards.[9]

College career

Georgia

During Mathis's freshman year he underwent emergency surgery due to a brain cyst.[10] He subsequently received a medical redshirt. After Jake Fromm decided to forgo his senior season to prepare for the NFL Draft and Jamie Newman opted out of the season due to concerns surrounding the COVID-19 pandemic, Mathis was expected to compete for the starting job.[11] He got the start in their opening game at Arkansas.[12] After a disappointing first half, Stetson Bennett came in and took his spot. The next time Mathis got meaningful playing time was against rival Florida.[13] On November 30, 2020, Mathis left the team and entered the transfer portal.[14][15] On December 14, 2020, he announced he would be transferring to Temple.[16][17]

Temple

In his first season with Temple, Mathis was named the day one starter against Rutgers. In the season opener, Mathis went 8 for 24 for 148 yards and one interception in a 61â€“14 loss.[18] Throughout the season, Mathis would share time with fellow Temple quarterback Justin Lynch.[19] In a game against Memphis, Mathis had a record setting day completing 35 passes for 322 yards and three touchdowns.[20] Mathis finished the season with six touchdowns, 1,225 yards, and 116 completions.[21] During the season it was rumored that Mathis had left the Owl's program, but he later publicly announced he would be staying with the team.[22][23] The following season, Mathis was once again named the starting quarterback.[24][25][26] With Mathis at the helm, Temple was shutout in a 30â€“0 loss to Duke, in which he contributed 83 yards on 11 completions.[27][28] The following week, Mathis was benched in favor of true freshman E.J. Warner.[29] Subsequently, Mathis began taking practice reps at wide receiver, before switching positions.[30] In 2023, Mathis appeared in three games as a wide receiver before announcing on October 15 that he would enter the transfer portal for the second time.[6]

Davenport

In 2024, Mathis transferred to Davenport University to play for the Davenport Panthers. He appeared in seven games, completing 45 of 76 passes for 580 yards and three touchdowns, while rushing for 112 yards and one touchdown on 43 carries.[1][8][9]
