Quebec Junior Hockey League

The Ligue de Hockey Junior du Québec (LHJQ) or Quebec Junior Hockey League (QJHL) is a Hockey Québec Canadian Junior A ice hockey league and is a member of Hockey Canada and the Canadian Junior Hockey League. The winner of the QJHL playoffs, determined by the Coupe Québec tournament, competes for the Fred Page Cup against the winners of the Central Canada Hockey League and the Maritime Hockey League and the host team, which is on a three-year cycle between the MHL, CCHL and LHJQ. The winner of the Fred Page Cup then moves on to compete for the Centennial Cup.[1][2][3][4]

History

The Quebec Junior Hockey League is an offshoot of the Quebec Junior A Hockey League that operated from 1972 to 1982. Founded in 1988 as the Quebec Provincial Junior Hockey League, it was renamed the Quebec Junior AAA Hockey League in 1998. The league has been a strong competitor, winning three Central Canadian championships (Dudley Hewitt Cup) in its early years: the Longueuil Collège Français in 1990 and the Châteauguay Elites in 1993 and 1994. In the 1994–95 season, the league was realigned to the Eastern Canadian region to compete for the Fred Page Cup. The Joliette Nationals won the first Fred Page Cup for the league in 1995. As of 2025, the QJHL/LHJAAAQ has won five Fred Page Cups, the others going to the St. Jérôme Panthers (2001), Lennoxville Cougars (2003), Joliette Action (2006), and Terrebonne Cobras (2017). No Quebec team has won the national championship (Royal Bank Cup/Centennial Cup), despite attending the tournament eight times since 1988. In 2002–03, Champlain College Lennoxville added a team to the LHJAAAQ, the Lennoxville Cougars, based on the campus of Champlain College and Bishop's University. Coached by former NHL player Stéphan Lebeau, the Cougars emphasized disciplined play. This approach succeeded, as Lennoxville won the NAPA Cup as league playoff champions and the Fred Page Cup. At the Royal Bank Cup, Lennoxville finished the round robin with a 2–2 record and exited in the semi-finals with a 1–2 loss to the Camrose Kodiaks of the Alberta Junior Hockey League. In 2003–04, the St-Eustache Gladiateurs, ranked No. 5 overall in Canada by the CJHL, lost the league finals to the Valleyfield Braves. As Valleyfield hosted the Fred Page Cup, both teams qualified for the tournament. After round-robin play, where St-Eustache and Valleyfield each had a 2–0 record entering their matchup (which Valleyfield won 4–0), St-Eustache lost to the Nepean Raiders 3–2 in double overtime in the semi-final. The Valleyfield Braves lost the final 4–0 to Nepean. In 2014, after 12 years as president, Richard Morency announced his resignation, remaining until the transition to new leadership. The league rebranded as the Quebec Junior Hockey League, dropping the "AAA" designation, and introduced a new logo. The Valleyfield Braves returned that summer when their owners purchased the La Tuque Wolves franchise, regained rights to the Braves name and logo, and relocated the team to the Aréna Salaberry in Valleyfield. In the 2015 off-season, the Kahnawake Condors, founded in 1999, relocated to Chambly, Quebec, and rebranded as the Chambly Forts. The Gatineau Mustangs of the Eastern Ontario Junior Hockey League joined the league as the Gatineau Flames, becoming the first Hull-area team since the Aylmer Extreme's one season in 2000–01. Shortly after, the Flames purchased the Lachine Maroons and absorbed their franchise rights. The league expelled the Sherbrooke Cougars, viewing them as a collegiate team due to ties with Bishop's University. The Saint-Hyacinthe Lauréats withdrew from the league. For the 2015–16 season, the league divided into two divisions: D1 and D2. In 2016, the league reverted to the Ligue de Hockey Junior AAA du Québec name. Since 2015, the league has continued to compete successfully. The Terrebonne Cobras won the Fred Page Cup in 2017, advancing to the Royal Bank Cup. The Princeville Titans claimed the NAPA Cup in 2019 and reached the Centennial Cup final. Longueuil Collège Français won the league championship in 2023–24, followed by the Valleyfield Braves in 2024–25. As of October 2025, the league operates with 13 teams and remains a key member of the CJHL's Eastern Conference.
