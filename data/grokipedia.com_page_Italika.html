Italika

Comercializadora de Motocicletas de Calidad, S.A. de C.V., commonly known as Italika, is a Mexican motorcycle brand established on November 1, 2004, by Ricardo Salinas Pliego.[1] Italika operates three assembly plants—one in Tlalpan, Mexico City; another in Toluca, State of Mexico; and a third in Guadalajara, Jalisco—and distributes products across Mexico and several Central and South American countries, including Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Panama, and Peru. With over eight million customers as of 2023 and continued growth, Italika is the top-selling motorcycle brand in Mexico. The company markets its products through Elektra stores, its own agencies, self-service channels, Elektra Motos stores, official distributors and its online platform. Italika offers a diverse range of product lines and models, including electric motorcycles launched in 2021,[2] and in 2016, launched Italika Racing, a platform dedicated to promoting new talent in motorcycling sports.

History

Italika began operations in 2004 under the initiative of businessman Ricardo Salinas Pliego, establishing its initial assembly plant in Tlalpan, Mexico City. In 2008, it established an assembly plant in Toluca, State of Mexico, with an initial production capacity of 300,000 motorcycles annually.[3] The company sold its millionth motorcycle on March 23, 2011, reaching two million units by 2014.[1][4] In 2015, Italika earned recognition as a Socially Responsible Company[5] and, the following year, launched its first fully electric model along with the Italika Racing[1] sports platform. The company opened its fifth production line in 2018,[6] and by 2019, had reached five million customers.[7] In 2020, Italika introduced its second generation of electric vehicles,[2] expanded its production capacity to 800,000 units annually by 2021,[8] and surpassed seven million customers by 2022, earning its eighth consecutive Socially Responsible Company award.[9] In 2023, Italika reached eight million customers[10] and partnered with the delivery company Rappi to provide road safety education for delivery drivers.[11] According to Infobae , Italika operates approximately 1,000 service centers as of 2022.[12] In June 2024, Italika inaugurated a new assembly plant with an annual capacity of 500,000 units, bringing total production capacity to 1.65 million motorcycles annually as of 2025.[13][14] By September 2024, the company reached nine million customers.[15]

Assembly Plant

Italika's primary assembly plant, known as Ensamblika, is situated in the Toluca 2000 Industrial Park, State of Mexico, and has been operational since 2008. The facility comprises six assembly lines, achieving an annual production exceeding 800,000 motorcycles as of 2023, with an installed capacity of approximately 1.2 million as of 2025. On average, one motorcycle is assembled every sixty seconds on each line.[16][17][18]

Italika Products

Products

Italika offers a wide range of products divided into categories such as Work, Z-Line, Sport, Dual Purpose, Scooters, ATVs, Chopper, Vort-X, Café Racer, Morbidelli, Semi-Automatic, and Electric models.[19] Additionally, Italika sells accessories and spare parts and operates a riding evaluation center in Mexico City, where customers can obtain their riding certificates.[20] The company also provides after-sales support through its service centers, with all models carrying a four-year or 40,000-kilometer warranty.[21]

Models

Italika makes models of street legal motorcycles in displacements ranging from 110 cc to 300 cc, including scooters, standards, sport, dual-purpose, and semi-automatic variants. Examples include:
  • 110–150 cc: Models like DM150 (dual-purpose), WS150 (scooter), and Vitalia (scooter)
  • 175–200 cc: Models like DM200 (dual-purpose) and Vort-X 200 (sport)
  • 250–300 cc: Models like FT250 (trail), 250Z (naked), DM250 (dual-purpose), Vort-X 250/300 (sport), and ATVs such as ATV 250 and ATV 300
Italika also makes a variety of off-road ATVs in 190–300 cc displacements.[22] Electric models are available in the Eléctricas line, with options for urban and light off-road use.[23]

Italika Racing

In partnership with Lorenzo Competición México, Italika launched Italika Racing in 2016 to promote sport motorcycling and develop emerging talent, according to Business Insider.[24] Italika Racing competitions use Italika models without modification, allowing for consistent tracking of new talent. Italika Racing has pioneered several initiatives, including the first children's electric motorcycle cup and participation in the Women's Circuit Racing World Championship, organized by Dorna Sports. This global event includes riders trained through Italika Racing, including Mexican Astrid Madrigal and Colombian Sara Varón, with Mexican Astrid Madrigal marking history as the first Mexican rider in this championship.[25][26][27]

Championships

Talento Italika Junior

This youth-focused series, certified as a Road to MotoGP program and endorsed by the Mexican Motorcycling Federation, is aimed at riders up to 17 years old. The championship includes the DM 125 Cup, Vort-X 200 Cup, and RT 250 Cup,[28][29] with its first event held in September 2017 in Querétaro.[30]

Talento Italika

Targeted at riders aged 12-21 with prior competitive experience,[31] this series, certified as a Road to MotoGP and endorsed by the Mexican Motorcycling Federation, currently features the Vort-X 300 Cup. Like the Junior Championship, it launched its inaugural season in 2017.[32]

Italika Women’s International Cup

The Italika Women's International Cup is a Latin American women’s championship certified as a Road to MotoGP event and endorsed by FIMLA.[33] Notable champions include Astrid Madrigal[34] and rider Sara Varón.[35] Other championships organized by Italika include the Ibero-American Women's Championship, featuring the Vort-X 500 model in a single-event format.[36]

Featured Riders

Prominent riders include Mexican Astrid Madrigal, a three-time champion of the Italika Women's International Cup and Latin American champion,[37] and Colombian rider Sara Varón,[38] who has competed in Spain’s ESBK Supersport 300 Championship. Both riders trained in Italika’s high-performance centers, representing Latin America in international championships. As of 2025, the team competes as Pons Italika Racing FIMLA in WorldWCR, with riders including Astrid Madrigal and Isis Carreno.[39][40]

High Performance Centers (CIAR)

Italika's High-Performance Centers (in Spanish, Centros Italika de Alto Rendimiento or CIAR) were established to promote motorcycling sports across all socioeconomic levels and develop talent from a young age.[24] Italika operates three CIAR facilities: in Sierra Esmeralda and Villa del Carbón in the State of Mexico,[41] and in Guadalajara, Jalisco[42].
