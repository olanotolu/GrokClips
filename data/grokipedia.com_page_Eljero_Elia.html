Eljero Elia

Eljero George Rinaldo Elia (born 13 February 1987) is a Dutch former professional footballer who played as a winger until retiring in late 2022. He turned professional at ADO Den Haag and then played for Twente before moving abroad to represent Hamburger SV, Juventus and Werder Bremen. In 2015, he came back to his home country to play for Feyenoord before moving to İstanbul Başakşehir in 2017. He later joined FC Utrecht in 2020 and returned to ADO Den Haag in 2021. Elia scored on his debut for Netherlands in September 2009 and was part of the Netherlands squad which reached the final at the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa.

Club career

Early career

Born in Voorburg, Elia played as a youth for local side SV Voorburg, before it merged to become Forum Sport in 1998.[3][4] He later moved to cross-city rivals TONEGIDO and played in their youth department.[5] In 1996, at age nine, Elia moved to the youth system of ADO Den Haag.[5][6] He then moved to Ajax in 2000.[4][7] Elia reflected on his time at the club, saying: "Already as a kid I loved playing for that club. Walking across the street wearing a big Ajax bag, I thought that was great. That Amsterdam bluff also suits me, I think. Challenging, fooling around, just like on the streets. Making an opponent crazy, that's the best thing there is." At under-13 level, he was sent away: "Together with Nordin Amrabat and Jeremain Lens. They thought I wasn't making a difference often enough. No, I never actually played as a winger for Ajax. Usually I was right mid, sometimes right back and sometimes central in the defence. Henny de Regt told me at the time that I was not allowed to stay. I felt so awful."[8] He then rejoined the youth system of ADO Den Haag two years later.[4][7][9] Then youth coach Carlos Roeleveld said: "I soon saw that he was special. But I also got a disappointed boy with whom I had to work. That was in the under-15. He was disappointed because he had been sent away from Ajax. He was thinking a lot."[4]

ADO Den Haag

Elia made his professional debut for ADO Den Haag on 23 January 2005 against FC Groningen at age 17, coming on as 81st-minute substitute in a 3–0 loss.[10] In his fourth appearance for ADO Den Haag, he scored his first goal in a match against AZ on 8 May, which turned out to be the winning goal, in a 2–1 win.[11] In his first season at the club, Elia played four matches and scored one goal. In his second season at ADO Den Haag, Elia soon established himself in the starting line-up, playing in midfield.[12] He scored his first goal of the 2005–06 season in a 2–2 draw against Ajax on 21 September.[13] By the end of the 2005–06 season, he had made 30 appearances and scored 2 goals in all competitions. Elia continued to establish himself in the team in the 2006–07 season, playing in midfield.[14] He scored his first goal of the season on 28 October 2006 in a 2–0 win against Heracles Almelo.[15] He scored two goals in two matches between 16 December and 23 December 2006 against Willem II and NAC Breda.[16] Along the way, Elia was dealing with injuries throughout the 2006–07 season.[17] He finished the 2006–07 season with 37 appearances and 3 goals in all competitions. However, ADO Den Haag's relegation, along with his some conflicts new coach Lex Schoenmaker and his lack of desire to want to play in the Eerste Divisie, contributed to Elia leaving the club at the end of the 2006–07 season.[18][19]

Juventus

On 31 August 2011, Juventus confirmed the signing of Elia from Hamburg on a four-year contract for a fee of €9 million.[91] He made his debut for the club, starting the match and played 45 minutes before being substituted at half time, in a 1–1 draw against Catania on 25 September 2011.[92] In a match against Bologna in the last 16 of the Coppa Italia, Elia set up a winning goal for Claudio Marchisio, who scored the second goal of the game, in a 2–1 win to help Juventus advance to the next round.[93] However, he found it hard to make an impact in the black and white shirt, due to the competitions in the midfield position.[94] Elia later made three more appearances for the club later in the 2011–12 season, as they ended up winning the Serie A title without losing a game.[95] Following this, he announced his intention to leave Juventus in order to revive his career elsewhere.[96] The following month, on 11 June 2012, Elia handed in a transfer request which was granted by Juventus.[97]

FSV Mainz 05 and 1899 Hoffenheim respectively.[115] Since the start of the 2013–14 season, he appeared in every match until missing one match due to being on the substitute bench against 1.FC Nuremberg on 8 March 2014, having previously been placed on the substitute bench in recent matches.[116] After missing one match, Elia returned to the first team against Stuttgart on 15 March 2014, coming on as a 73rd-minute substitute, in a 1–1 draw.[117] He then continued to be involved in the first team for the rest of the 2013–14 season.[118] Elia then played a contribution for the club when he set up two goals in two matches between 30 March 2014 and 5 April 2014, coming against Hannover 96 and Schalke 04 respectively.[119] Despite missing suffering injuries on two occasions throughout the 2013–14 season, Elia went on to make thirty–three appearances and scoring four times in all competitions.[120] The 2013–14 season was an improvement for the player, as his performance convinced Werder Bremen decided against selling him.[121] In the opening game of the 2014–15 season, Elia made a good start when he provided an assist for Franco Di Santo in a 2–2 draw against Hertha BSC.[122] Elia then appeared in a number of matches in the first team, playing in the left–wing position.[123] However, he was dropped from the squad for two matches after his poor performance in Werder Bremen's match, in a lost 6–0 against Bayern Munich on 18 October 2014.[124] But Elia made his return to the starting line–up against Stuttgart on 8 November 2014 and played 56 minutes before being substituted, in a 2–0 win.[125] However, his return to the first team was short–lived when manager Viktor Skrypnyk decided to drop the player from the first team matchday squad the following month.[126] Halfway through the 2014–15 season, he was sidelined on three occasions, due to injuries.[127] By the time Elia left the club in the January transfer window, he made ten appearances in all competitions. Southampton (loan)

Having hinted about leaving Werder Bremen in the January transfer window, Southampton announced on 23 December 2014, that Elia would join them on loan from 3 January 2015 for the remainder of the season, with an option to make the loan permanent at the end of the season.[132] Upon joining the club, he said: "It's a dream that I can play at Southampton. The club wanted me and the coach has played an important role in that. I have only heard good stories about Ronald Koeman and I hope he can show me the way to new success."[133] On 11 January 2015, Elia made his Premier League debut for Southampton in a 1–0 win away to Manchester United at Old Trafford; he was replaced by substitute Dušan Tadić after 63 minutes, who went on to score the winning goal.[134] Elia scored his first Premier League goals with a brace in a 2–1 victory at Newcastle United on 17 January.[135][136] Since joining the club, he was involved in a number of first team matches for the rest of the season.[137] Despite missing one match through injury, Elia went on to make seventeen appearances and scoring two times in all competitions.[138] Following this, he was keen to stay at the club permanently.[139] It came after when Werder Bremen's chief director claimed Southampton wanted to sign him permanently.[140] Elia, himself, said he was keen to join the club permanently.[141] However, Southampton opted against signing a permanent deal for the player, as the clause reportedly cost €5.5m (about £4m), which "has been seen as too expensive" for the club.[142] Despite this, Southampton was still keen to sign Elia.[143]

Following this, Elia announced his intention of leaving Feyenoord, citing to play abroad.[176] He was linked with a move to Fenerbahçe and İstanbul Başakşehir.[177] During his time at Feyenoord, Elia was among a fan favourite among the club's supporters, who chanted 'Oe-Ah-Elia'.[178]

İstanbul Başakşehir

On 13 June 2017, Elia joined Turkish side İstanbul Başakşehir on a three-year contract.[179] He made his debut for the club on 26 July 2017 in a 3–3 away draw against Club Brugge in the first leg of the third qualifying round of the UEFA Champions League, immediately scoring his first goal in the 62nd minute of the match.[180] In the return leg, Elia started in the match and helped İstanbul Başakşehir win 2–0 to advance to the next round.[181] His first appearance in the Süper Lig came on 11 August 2017 in a 1–0 home win over Bursaspor, where he scored the only goal of the match.[182] Elia scored in both legs of the UEFA Champions League's play–offs round against Sevilla, as the club were eliminated following a 4–3 loss on aggregate.[183] Since joining İstanbul Başakşehir, he quickly established himself in the first team, playing in the left–wing position.[184] However, Elia suffered a shoulder injury and had an operation that saw him out for two months.[185] It wasn't until on 18 February 2018 when he made his return from injury, coming on as a 74th-minute substitute, in a 1–0 win against Trabzonspor.[186] Elia then scored three goals in three matches between 18 March 2018 and 6 April 2018 against Beşiktaş, Alanyaspor and Yeni Malatyaspor.[187] Two weeks later on 20 April 2018, he scored his eighth goal of the season, in a 3–1 win against Kayserispor.[188] Elia followed up by assisting three times in the next three matches.[189] Having helped the club finish third place in the league, he went on to make thirty–two appearances and scoring eight times in all competitions. Ahead of the 2018–19 season, Elia was linked a move away from with İstanbul Başakşehir, with European clubs were interested in signing him, but he ended up staying at the club.[190] However, he found himself plagued with injuries and affected his playing time for the 2018–19 season.[191] Despite this, Elia scored his only goal of the season, in a 1–1 draw against Kasımpaşa on 28 January 2019.[192] Despite aiming to help İstanbul Başakşehir win the league for the first time, the club ended up finishing second place after surrendering their title chances to Galatasaray, in a 2–1 loss on 19 May 2019.[193] At the end of the 2018–19 season, he went on to make twenty–four appearances and scoring once in all competitions. Ahead of the 2019–20 season, Elia was linked with a move to Beşiktaş, but he ended up staying at İstanbul Başakşehir.[194] However, Elia suffered an injury in the 23rd minute against Fenerbahçe on 24 August 2019, as the club lost 2–0; which subsequently saw the player missed two matches.[195] But he made his return from injury, coming on as a 70th-minute substitute and setting up the fourth goal of the game, in a 5–0 win against Çaykur Rizespor on 27 September 2019.[196] Elia then scored his first goal of the season, in a 5–1 win against Kasımpaşa on 28 December 2019.[197] Following his return from injury, he continued to remain involved in the first team and this lasted until the season was disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic that pushed the season to June; the player made twenty–four appearances and scoring once so far.[198] Once the season resumed behind closed doors, Elia scored on his first appearances for İstanbul Başakşehir in three months, at the last minutes of the game, in a 2–0 win against Antalyaspor on 4 July 2020.[199] A week later on 13 July 2020, he scored his third goal of the season, in a 4–3 loss against Konyaspor.[200] In a follow–up match against Kayserispor, Elia helped the club win 1–0 to help İstanbul Başakşehir win the Süper Lig for the first time, a target he finally achieved.[201] At the end of the 2019–20 season, Elia went on to make thirty appearances and scoring three times in all competitions. It was announced on 20 March 2020 that Elia would be leaving İstanbul Başakşehir at the end of the 2019–20 season.[202][203] His contract with the club expired after the season was confirmed on 19 August 2020, making him a free agent.[204]

FC Utrecht

On 19 August 2020, Elia signed a two-year contract with Utrecht, after coming over from İstanbul Başakşehir on a free transfer.[205] He was previously linked with a move to clubs in Italy and Argentina before deciding to return to his homeland.[206] Upon joining the club, Elia said: "It is really a lie, Utrecht has already gone very far for me. I received a very nice proposal and I am very happy with it. I wanted to return to the Netherlands and they have a very nice plan, express ambition. That fits with me. Every player wants to play at such a club. You can also see that in the transfers. Utrecht is continuously improving itself."[207] Elia made his first appearance for FC Utrecht, coming on as a substitute in the 81st minute, in a 1–1 away draw in the Eredivisie against VVV-Venlo on 18 September 2020.[208] Since joining FC Utrecht, Elia found himself in and out of the starting line–up, as he maintain his fitness to earn a place.[209] However, Elia received criticism for not living up to expectation since joining the club.[210] It wasn't until on 22 December 2020 when he scored his first goal for Utrecht in the away match against Emmen, as the club won 3–2.[211] In the next league game on 27 December 2020, Elia again scored the opening goal, this time against AZ.[212] On 11 January 2021, the club announced that Elia had suffered a hamstring injury and as a result would be sidelined the following weeks.[213] Eventually, his return was delayed up to April despite returning to training months earlier.[214] It wasn't until on 11 April 2021 when he made his return to the starting line–up against Feyenoord, coming on as a 76th-minute substitute, in a 2–1 loss.[215] Following this, Elia soon had a first run ins for FC Utrecht's remaining matches of the 2020–21 season.[216] At the end of the 2020–21 season, he went on to make twenty–three appearances and scoring two times in all competitions. On 30 July 2021, ahead of the 2021–22 season, Elia's contract was terminated by mutual agreement, making him a free agent.[217]

Return to ADO Den Haag

Elia began practicing with his former club ADO Den Haag during the autumn of 2021, who were competing in the second-tier Eerste Divisie.[218] On 18 November 2021, he signed a contract with the club until the end of the season.[219] In late January 2022, he suffered a ligament tear in his ankle that required surgery and prevented him from playing for the rest of the season.[220] Elia officially announced his retirement from playing in late 2022.[221]

International career

Youth career

In March 2005, Elia was called up to the Netherlands national under-19 team, making his debut for the U19 national team, starting the whole game, in a 2–2 draw against Austria U19.[222] He scored his first goals for the Netherlands U19, in a 2–0 win against Czech Republic U19 on 14 May 2005.[223] Elia then scored his third goal for the U19 national team, in a 1–0 win against Iceland U19 on 2 September 2005.[224] A month later on 19 October 2005, he scored twice for Netherlands U19, in a 2–2 draw against Germany U19.[225] Elia went on to make ten appearances and scoring six times for the U19 national team.[226] Elia developed rapidly and at the beginning of 2006, he was selected for Netherlands U21 the first time.[227] Elia made his debut for the U21 national team, coming on as a 69th-minute substitute and scored from a penalty, in a 2–2 draw against Germany U21 on 15 August 2006.[228] He later made two more appearances for Netherlands U21 by the end of the year.[229] It wasn't until on 10 August 2007 when Elia was called up to the U21 national team and made an appearance, coming on as a 67th-minute substitute, in a 1–0 win against Macedonia U21 twelve days later.[230] He went on to make seven appearances and scoring once for Netherlands U21.[226][231] On 7 February 2007, Elia made his debut for Netherlands U20, coming on as a second half substitute, in a 3–1 loss against Denmark U20.[232] A month later on 23 March 2007, he scored his first goals for the U20 national team, in a 3–2 win against Ukraine U20.[233] Elia went on to make five appearances and scoring two times for Netherlands U20. Later in the year, he made one appearance for Netherlands B, coming against Sweden U21 on 19 November 2008, losing 3–0.[234]

Senior career

In May 2009, Elia was included in the Netherlands' senior squad for the friendly matches against Iceland and Norway by coach Bert van Marwijk.[235] However, he was dropped from the squad.[236] Once again, Elia was selected for the senior team's friendly with England.[237] However, he was dropped from the squad once again.[238] On 6 September 2009, Elia made his debut for the Netherlands against Japan in a friendly match, coming on at half-time to replace Arjen Robben, and went on to set up two goals in a 3–0 win.[239] In his second match, on 9 September 2009, Elia scored the only goal to win a match against Scotland.[240] He made his first start for the senior team, in a 0–0 draw against Australia on 10 October 2009.[241] Elia followed up by making three straight starts in the next three matches.[242] He scored his second international goal in a 6–1 friendly win over Hungary with a solo effort after coming on as a substitute.[243] In May 2010, Elia was included in the preliminary squad for the FIFA World Cup in South Africa despite facing uncertainties over his injury that he sustained while at Hamburger SV.[244] On 27 May 2010, Dutch manager Bert van Marwijk announced that Elia would be part of the 23-man final squad for the 2010 FIFA World Cup.[245] Prior to the tournament, Elia caused controversy in the Dutch media when he tweeted "cancer Moroccan", prompting a ban of use of Twitter.[246] This led Elia making an apology for his action.[247] In the first match, he came on as a substitute in the 72nd minute and set up their second goal in a 2–0 win over Denmark, with his shot hitting the post and Dirk Kuyt tapping it in.[248] Following this, Elia found his playing time, coming from the substitute throughout the tournament.[249] He later said about the role, saying: "If I come in for five or ten minutes, then I do my very best and I want to show my good side. It's nice to fill in, because the boys also help you well."[250] In the final, Elia was a 71st-minute substitute for Kuyt, as Netherlands finished runners-up to Spain.[251] Following the tournament, Elia was called up to the Netherlands squad in August.[252] He made his first start of the year for the senior national team against San Marino on 3 September 2010 and played 59 minutes before being substituted, in a 5–0 win.[253] It wasn't until on 26 January 2011 when Elia was called up to the Netherlands squad for the first time in four months.[254] He made his first appearance in five months, coming on as a second half substitute, in a 3–1 win against Austria on 9 February 2011.[255] Following this, Elia found his playing time, coming from the substitute and he helped the senior national team qualify for the UEFA Euro 2012 after beating Finland 2–0 on 6 September 2011.[256] After a year absence from the national team, Elia was called up to the Netherlands squad on 13 October 2012.[257] Three days later on 16 October 2012, he made his first appearance for the national team in over a year, coming on as a late substitute, in a 4–1 win against Romania.[258] Elia made another appearance for Netherlands in a follow–up match against Germany, coming on as a second half substitute, in a 0–0 draw.[259] After a three years absence, he was called up to the national team for the first time in three years, but did not play.[260] In May 2018, Elia was called up to the Netherlands for the first time in almost two years.[261] He made his first appearance for the senior team for the first time in almost six years, coming on as a 75th-minute substitute, in a 1–1 draw against Slovakia on 31 May 2018.[262] Elia made another appearance for Netherlands, coming on as an 82nd-minute substitute, in a 1–1 draw against Italy on 4 June 2018.[263] Elia retired from international football after these 2018 friendlies, ending his senior career with 30 appearances and 2 goals for Oranje.

Personal life

Born in the Netherlands and raised in The Hague, Elia is of Surinamese descent.[264] Elia's first name is an homage to his sister's favourite jazz singer, Al Jarreau.[265][266] As a child, he idolised Romário.[265] In an interview with Trouw , Elia said that he is a religious person and became a first-time father when his girlfriend Sanne gave birth to a daughter, Heavenly.[7][267][266] In November 2012, Elia became a second-time father when his second daughter, Seal, was born.[268] In 2019, he became a third-time father when his first son, Eljero Dream, was born.[269] In June 2015, Elia announced his marriage to his long term girlfriend, Sanne.[270] As of 2025, Elia and Sanne continue to reside in the Netherlands with their three children. In September 2008, Elia was fined €200 for assaulting a scooter rider three months earlier.[271] In addition to speaking Dutch, he speaks German and English.[272][267] On 7 March 2016, Elia was brought in for questioning and arrested for his alleged involvement in a brawl in Rotterdam city centre the week before.[273] After spending a night in jail, he was released and Feyenoord's manager Van Bronckhorst was said to be disappointed with his action and the club fined Elia as a result.[274] Although Elia maintained that he did not make contact in the brawl, he, nevertheless, was fined three months by the Rotterdam court.[275] Throughout his football career, Elia developed a reputation as a bad boy by the Dutch media.[276] Responding to the claims, he denied having such reputation, saying: "I don't know why something like this is being said. Or why I'm being labeled a problem professional. I didn't cause any trouble. I'm a quiet guy, I'm with my family the whole time and I don't do nothing – I don't go out. What else can you do? It can be hard to come up. Only what you can [do] is let your feet speak. You cannot fight against the media. The media can make you and they can break you. I can tell you from my heart, I swear on my children, the things they were saying in the newspaper was not the truth. It's not the truth. You can see it by yourself."[277] Elia described himself as an "emotional footballer", who "plays with emotion and a lot of passion", while manager Mario Been, on the other hand, called him erratic and "incredibly obsessed with the game".[278] Elia made headlines when he had a severe infection due to a poorly applied tattoo, due to feeling uncomfortable wearing the shirt, leading Hamburg's neighbours Werder Bremen have since banned their players from getting tattoos.[279] Another occurred in May 2016 when Elia had a tattoo with the name Feyenoord misspelt.[280] Along with Demy de Zeeuw and Gregory van der Wiel, he founded a brand called BALR.[281] He also opened a record label, The Culture.[269][282] Following his retirement from professional football in February 2023, Elia has pursued entrepreneurial activities, including DJing in tech-house and minimal genres as of 2025.
