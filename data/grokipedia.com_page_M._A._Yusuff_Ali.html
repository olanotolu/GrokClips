M. A. Yusuff Ali

Yusuff Ali Musaliam Veettil Abdul Kader , popularly known as M. A. Yusuff Ali (born 15 November 1955), is an Indian businessman and billionaire.[5] He is the chairman and managing director of LuLu Group International, which owns the LuLu Hypermarket chain worldwide and LuLu International Shopping Mall.[6] With annual revenue of approximately US$7.3 billion (as of 2025), LuLu Group International employs the largest number of Indian diaspora.[7][8] According to Forbes Middle East, Yusuff Ali was ranked No. 1 in Top 100 Indian business owners in the Arab World 2018.[6] As per the Forbes India's 100 Richest list published in October 2025, he was ranked 49th with a net worth of US$5.85 billion.[9] In 2006, he started his large business ventures in India, by starting a convention centre cum hotel at his native place Thrissur, Kerala namely Lulu Convention Centre.[10] In 2013, Yusuff Ali acquired 4.99% of the Thrissur-based CSB Bank[11][12] and 4.99% shares of Thrissur based Dhanlaxmi Bank.[13] Also in 2013, he increased his stake in the Aluva-based Federal Bank to 4.47%.[14] In 2014 Yusuff Ali acquired 2% stake of South Indian Bank - another Thrissur based bank.[15] In 2015, Yusuff Ali's LuLu Group purchased the Scotland Yard Building in London for conversion into a luxury hotel.[16] As of 2025, he holds approximately 10% stake in Cochin International Airport and serves on its board.[7] He has bought a 10% stake in the UK-based trading firm, East India Company, and a 40% stake in its fine foods subsidiary for around $85 million in total.[18][19][20] The Lulu Bolgatty International Convention Centre on Bolghatty Island in Kochi is one of the largest convention centres in South Asia,[21] along with Grand Hyatt hotel[22] in the same campus. Lulu group's First Mall in India was LuLu International Shopping Mall, Kochi started in 2013, then in 2019, he started shopping mall in India at Triprayar, Thrissur, the Y Mall.[23][24] In October 2021, Lulu group opened their second mall in India, Global Mall, at Rajaji Nagar, Bengaluru, Karnataka.[25] In December 2021, Lulu group opened their third mall in India at Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala.[26] In July 2022, Lulu group opened their fourth mall in India at Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh.[26] And in 2023, LuLu Group launched a new hypermarket and mall in Hyderabad, Palakkad and Coimbatore.[27][28] In 2024, the group opened malls in Kozhikode and Kottayam, with further expansions planned for Ahmedabad and Visakhapatnam by 2026.[29]

Early life

Yusuff Ali was born in Nattika, Thrissur in the erstwhile State of Travancoreâ€“Cochin on 15 November 1955. He did his schooling in Karanchira Thrissur at St. Xavier's High School.[30] He holds a diploma in Business Management and Administration. After his studies, he left India in 1973 to Abu Dhabi, where his paternal uncle, M. K. Abdullah, the chairman and the founder of the LuLu Group of Companies, was doing business. He developed the import and wholesale distribution of the group and ventured into the supermarket business by launching the Lulu Hypermarket. He started his first LuLu Hypermarket in the 1990s, a time when the UAE's retail sector witnessed a major change, with the traditional groceries and supermarkets yielding place to large neighbourhood stores and hypermarkets. While the entry of Continent (now Carrefour) in 1995 changed the face of retail business in Dubai, Yusuff Ali took charge of Abu Dhabi's retail sector by rolling out LuLu Hypermarket.[31][32]

LuLu Group International

LuLu Group International commenced its operations as a family business venture. After joining the business, Yusuff Ali diversified the business to include the import and distribution of frozen products from Europe and the United States. The products were available not only in Abu Dhabi but also in other Emirates. The business was soon expanded to include both food and non-food categories. The group also started cold storage, meat, and food processing plants, large-scale import and distribution to hotel groups, catering companies, and shipping services. By the 1980s, the group had a sizable share of the wholesale and retail food market in the UAE. The group has grown into an international group with operations spread over three continents. The group's flagship retail chain of LuLu Hypermarkets and Supermarkets is rated as one of the major players in the Middle East retail sector with over 250 stores across the GCC and other regions (as of 2025). Apart from the hypermarkets, supermarkets and department stores, the group also owns several shopping malls, namely, Khalidiyah Mall, Al Raha Mall, [Al Wahda Mall](Al Wahda Mall "Al Wahda Mall"), Mushriff Mall, Madinat Zayed Mall, Mazyad Mall, Ramli Mall, RAK Mall, Al Foah Mall, Al Khor Mall, Riyadh Avenue Mall in Saudi Arabia and Oman Avenues Mall spread across the GCC states. LuLu International Shopping Mall in Kochi, Kerala was opened on 10 March 2013. It was the group's first retail venture in India and one of the largest shopping malls in India at the time of opening. LuLu International Shopping Mall in Thiruvananthapuram was opened in December 2021. Subsequent expansions in India include the Y Mall in Thrissur (2019), Global Mall in Bengaluru (2021), LuLu Mall in Lucknow (2022), and new hypermarkets in Hyderabad, Palakkad, and Coimbatore (2023). The Group has a vast organizational structure of over 65,000 employees, representing around 40 different nationalities, out of which more than 30,000 are Indians. Retail business is the mainstay of the Group with operations in major Middle Eastern and African countries like UAE, Oman, Qatar, Kuwait, Yemen, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Egypt and Kenya, having Retail Sourcing and manufacturing bases located in the Far East, India, Africa and Guangzhou in China. Indian operations mainly include Food Processing, Export of Food & Non food products and convention center with bases in Delhi, Mumbai, Kochi, Thiruvananthapuram, Chennai, Lucknow and Thrissur. In 2013, Deloitte ranked the Group as one of the fastest-growing retailers in the world.[33] In 2025, LuLu Group inaugurated Kerala's tallest twin IT towers in Kochi's SmartCity, spanning 3.4 million sq ft and accommodating 25,000 professionals.[34]

Philanthropy

Yusuff Ali is involved in many social, charitable, and humanitarian activities in India and the Gulf states. As part of its Global CSR policy, the LuLu Group partnered with Dubai Cares to adopt schools in Gaza and Nepal, focusing on improving infrastructure, literacy training for women, and early childhood development.[35] Yusuff Ali contributed to opening a multi-faith funeral centre for the Indian community in Sharjah spanning 8.3 acres.[36] He also initiated the sale and promotion of organic products grown by people with special needs in the UAE through LuLu Hypermarkets, including vegetables from a 34-hectare farm in Abu Dhabi.[37][38] Yusuff Ali donated to relief efforts for the 2001 Gujarat earthquake, the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami, and typhoon and flood disasters worldwide.[39] In August 2018, he donated â‚¹9.5 crore for the relief and rehabilitation of victims of the Kerala floods.[39] He supports the social, economic, and religious welfare of expatriate Indians in the Persian Gulf region, including securing land for churches for the Christian community and cremation grounds for Hindus.[36] He extended help to Indians during amnesty periods in the Gulf when many lost livelihoods.[40] He also contributed to rehabilitating victims of the Kozhikode (Calicut) market fire.[36] In August 2019, Yusuff Ali pledged â‚¹50 million for flood relief in Kerala.[41] During the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, he donated AED 23.2 million (â‚¹46.5 crore) for relief efforts.[42] In 2024, he paid off a Kerala family's â‚¹8.25 lakh home loan to prevent foreclosure.[43] In 2025, he contributed AED 20 million to the UAE's Fathers' Endowment campaign and â‚¹10 crore to the Chief Minister's Distress Relief Fund for Wayanad landslide rehabilitation.[44][45]

Personal life

He is married to Shabira Yusuff Ali, with three children, and lives in Abu Dhabi, UAE.[46] His elder daughter Shabeena is married to billionaire businessman Shamsheer Vayalil.[47][48] His second daughter Shafeena is married to Adeeb Ahamed who runs Lulu Financial Holdings and Twenty14 Holdings, a hospitality investment firm.[49] Youngest daughter Shifa is married to Sharoon Shamsudheen who is into successful IT businesses.[4] In April 2021 the helicopter in which Yusuff Ali, his wife and three others including the pilots crash landed in a swamp near Kochi, Kerala. Ali and his wife were uninjured and were taken to a nearby hospital for first aid. The private helicopter owned by the businessman had suffered a technical glitch due to adverse weather.[50]
