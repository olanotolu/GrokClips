Bogota Savings Bank Center

The Bogota Savings Bank Center is a multi-purpose arena in Hackensack, New Jersey, functioning as the headquarters for Fairleigh Dickinson University's NCAA Division I athletic department and primary venue for its men's and women's basketball teams, along with volleyball, indoor track and field, and women's fencing competitions.[1] Originally dedicated on September 9, 1987, as the George and Phyllis Rothman Center, the 40,000-square-foot facility underwent a renaming on January 18, 2024, through a five-year naming rights agreement with the local Bogota Savings Bank, marking the longest and largest such corporate partnership in FDU athletics history and the only one for a basketball arena among Northeast Conference schools.[1][2] With a seating capacity of 1,852 in its Stratis Arena, the center has hosted dignitaries including former U.S. Presidents George H. W. Bush, Bill Clinton, and Barack Obama in 2009, as well as Al Gore, Leah Rabin, and Maya Angelou, underscoring its role beyond athletics in community and public events.[1] Recent enhancements include a 2022 strength and conditioning center, a 2023 men's basketball locker room renovation, and campus-wide ATM installations tied to the bank's partnership, reflecting ongoing investments in functionality and visibility.[1][2]

History

Origins and Construction

The Rothman Center, later renamed the Bogota Savings Bank Center, originated as a project to establish a comprehensive athletic and recreation facility on Fairleigh Dickinson University's Metropolitan Campus in Teaneck, New Jersey, serving as the headquarters for Division I varsity sports including basketball, volleyball, and track and field, while providing recreational amenities for students.[3] The initiative was bolstered by a $1 million donation from George Rothman, a self-made businessman, and his wife Phyllis, who aimed to support university athletics and the Bergen County community through enhanced infrastructure.[4] [3] Groundbreaking for the 40,000-square-foot structure took place on September 9, 1985, marking the start of construction to replace outdated facilities and accommodate growing athletic demands.[4] [3] The project featured a distinctive design with a tent-like roof and multi-purpose layout, including four convertible courts for basketball, tennis, and volleyball, a six-lane 200-meter indoor track, and support spaces such as locker rooms, a weight-training area, and a trainer's suite.[4] Construction concluded precisely two years after the groundbreaking, with the facility's official dedication and opening on September 9, 1987, initially named the George and Phyllis Rothman Center in honor of its primary benefactors.[4] [3] The completed arena offered seating for under 3,000 spectators in basketball configuration, expandable to 5,000 or more for larger events, and included over 2,000 parking spaces to facilitate community and university use.[4] [3]

Opening and Early Years

The George and Phyllis Rothman Center was officially dedicated on September 9, 1987, precisely two years after its groundbreaking ceremony on the same date in 1985.[3][5] Named in recognition of philanthropists George and Phyllis Rothman for their substantial contributions to Fairleigh Dickinson University (FDU), the facility immediately became the primary venue for the university's intercollegiate athletics, supplanting earlier, smaller gymnasiums on the Metropolitan Campus in Teaneck, New Jersey.[1] The center's opening marked a significant upgrade, featuring a multi-purpose arena designed to accommodate basketball, volleyball, and other sports, with an emphasis on supporting FDU's transition and growth within NCAA Division I competition in the Northeast Conference.[6] In its inaugural years, the Rothman Center hosted the FDU Knights' home games across multiple sports, fostering improved team performance and fan attendance compared to prior facilities. The men's basketball program, in particular, benefited from the new space, recording competitive seasons in the late 1980s and early 1990s, including appearances in the Northeast Conference tournament.[6] The venue's architecture, characterized by a distinctive tent-like roof supported by steel cables, provided a 5,100-seat capacity for basketball events while allowing flexibility for commencements, concerts, and community gatherings, thereby establishing it as a central hub for campus life.[7] Early operations emphasized functionality and accessibility, with the center serving as the headquarters for FDU's athletic department and enabling expanded training and administrative capabilities.[1]

Renaming and Recent Developments

On January 18, 2024, Fairleigh Dickinson University (FDU) Athletics announced a multi-year exclusive naming rights agreement with Bogota Savings Bank, rebranding the facility from the George and Phyllis Rothman Center to the Bogota Savings Bank Center.[8][5] The agreement, which provides financial support to FDU's athletic programs, followed the Knights' unexpected advancement to the second round of the 2023 NCAA Division I men's basketball tournament, enhancing the venue's visibility and marketability.[9] Originally dedicated on September 9, 1987, as the Rothman Center in honor of donors George and Phyllis Rothman, the arena had served as the primary home for FDU's basketball teams and athletic department headquarters without prior corporate naming rights.[5][1] Recent facility enhancements have focused on improving athletic infrastructure within and associated with the center. In 2022, the Michael and Denise Clarke Strength and Conditioning Center was added to support training for FDU's NCAA Division I programs.[5] This was followed in 2023 by a renovation of the men's basketball locker room, aimed at elevating player facilities amid the program's rising profile.[5] These upgrades, timed alongside the naming rights deal, reflect FDU's efforts to capitalize on recent basketball success for long-term athletic investment, though no major structural overhauls to the arena's core 5,000-seat capacity have been reported as of 2025.[8] The center continues to host Knights home games, community events such as job fairs and expos, and serves as a hub for Division I operations.[10][1]

Facilities and Features

Arena Specifications

The Bogota Savings Bank Center houses Stratis Arena, a multi-purpose venue primarily configured for basketball, with a listed seating capacity of 1,852 spectators.[1][11] The overall facility encompasses 40,000 square feet and supports various athletic activities, including volleyball, indoor track and field, and women's fencing.[1][3] Stratis Arena features four playing courts, with the two central courts surfaced in hardwood suitable for basketball and the surrounding areas covered in synthetic material for versatility in sports like tennis or volleyball.[3] It includes a six-lane, 200-meter indoor track equipped with a long jump pit, enabling track and field events alongside court-based competitions.[3] Seating arrangements incorporate chairback bleachers for enhanced spectator comfort, though the arena's compact design prioritizes functionality over large-scale crowds.[3] Additional infrastructure supports operational needs, such as eight independent locker rooms with showers, a weight-training room for student-athletes, and a trainer's suite featuring hydrotherapy facilities.[3] The arena opened on September 9, 1987, as part of the facility's construction on Fairleigh Dickinson University's Metropolitan Campus in Hackensack, New Jersey.[3]

Upgrades and Amenities

The Bogota Savings Bank Center, a 40,000-square-foot multi-purpose arena on Fairleigh Dickinson University's Metropolitan Campus in Hackensack, New Jersey, features Stratis Arena as its primary basketball and volleyball venue, with additional support for indoor track and field and women's fencing events.[1] The facility accommodates 1,852 seated spectators, providing ample viewing for university athletics and hosted events.[1] Amenities include dedicated locker rooms for various sports teams, a player lounge, film room, and the Michael and Denise Clarke Strength and Conditioning Center, which supports athlete training and performance.[1] Recent upgrades emphasize enhanced athlete resources and facility modernization. In 2022, the center added the student-athlete mental health space and renovated locker rooms for men's volleyball and track and field teams.[1] The following year saw completion of a new men's basketball locker room, player lounge, film room, and the installation of a "Champions Live Here" motivational wall.[1] As of January 2024, construction was underway on the Rothman Lobby to improve entry and gathering areas, aligning with broader athletics facility investments exceeding $2 million over the prior 12 months, though specific allocations to the arena were not detailed beyond these projects.[8] [12] Future enhancements include upgrades to the Stratis Lounge for enhanced spectator and team experiences.[1] These improvements reflect Fairleigh Dickinson University's commitment to elevating Division I athletics infrastructure without altering core capacity or arena specifications.[1]

Associated Teams and Athletics

Fairleigh Dickinson University Knights

The Bogota Savings Bank Center, located at 100 University Plaza Drive in Hackensack, New Jersey, functions as the athletic headquarters for the Fairleigh Dickinson University Knights, the NCAA Division I intercollegiate sports teams of the university's Metropolitan Campus.[1] It primarily hosts the men's and women's basketball, volleyball, indoor track and field, and women's fencing programs, with the Stratis Arena serving as the main competition space.[1] [5] The facility spans 40,000 square feet and accommodates up to 1,852 spectators for events.[1] Originally dedicated as the George and Phyllis Rothman Center on September 9, 1987, the venue has long been the central hub for the Knights' indoor athletics, supporting their competition in the Northeast Conference.[1] On January 18, 2024, Fairleigh Dickinson University announced a multi-year naming rights agreement with Bogota Savings Bank, renaming it the Bogota Savings Bank Center; the deal includes the bank serving as the presenting sponsor for men's basketball and providing scholarships and financial literacy support for student-athletes.[5] The first event under the new name occurred on January 19, 2024, featuring the women's basketball team against Stonehill College.[5] Recent upgrades have enhanced the center's role in Knights athletics, including the opening of the Michael and Denise Clarke Strength and Conditioning Center in 2022, renovated locker rooms for multiple teams between 2020 and 2022, an updated men's basketball locker room and lounge in 2023, a "Champions Live Here" motivational wall in 2023, and a dedicated student-athlete mental health space in 2022.[1] Further improvements to the Stratis Lounge are planned, underscoring the facility's ongoing commitment to supporting the Knights' competitive and developmental needs.[1] The center's four configurable courts and six-lane, 200-meter indoor track enable versatile use across sports, facilitating both practices and competitions.[13]

Basketball Programs

The Bogota Savings Bank Center's Stratis Arena serves as the primary venue for Fairleigh Dickinson University's basketball development initiatives, including camps and clinics designed for youth and prospective players. The Jack Castleberry Basketball Camps, operated in association with FDU's men's basketball program, offer skills training sessions focused on fundamental techniques, game strategy, and physical conditioning, accommodating participants from local areas such as Hackensack and broader New Jersey.[14] These camps utilize the arena's facilities during summer and off-season periods, providing access to professional-level courts and coaching from university staff.[1] The center also hosts invitational multi-team events that extend basketball programming beyond FDU's varsity schedule, such as the annual FDU Basketball Classic and other multi-team exempt tournaments (MTEs). For the 2025-26 season, these include matchups against the United States Military Academy at West Point on November 25 and East Texas A&M University on November 24, drawing regional NCAA Division I competition to foster competitive exposure and fan engagement.[15][16] Such events typically feature neutral-site games for participating teams, with FDU serving as the host, and contribute to the venue's role in regional basketball networking and talent scouting.[17] Broadcast partnerships enhance the visibility of these programs, with select youth-oriented and developmental games aired on regional networks like YES, allowing wider access to instructional content and highlights from the arena.[18] Overall, these initiatives emphasize skill-building and community involvement, leveraging the center's 1,852-seat capacity for scalable events that support grassroots basketball growth in northern New Jersey.[1]

Naming Rights and Sponsorship

Partnership with Bogota Savings Bank

On January 18, 2024, Fairleigh Dickinson University (FDU) Athletics announced a multi-year, exclusive naming rights agreement with Bogota Savings Bank, rebranding the university's Rothman Center arena as the Bogota Savings Bank Center.[8][27] The deal, described by university officials as a landmark partnership, aligns with FDU's efforts to capitalize on heightened visibility following the men's basketball team's unexpected "Cinderella run" in the 2023 NCAA Tournament, where the Knights achieved a historic upset over Purdue.[2][25] Bogota Savings Bank, a community-focused institution founded in 1891 and headquartered in nearby Bogota, New Jersey, has maintained prior ties with FDU, including hosting financial literacy seminars for student-athletes as early as March 2020.[28][25] The naming rights arrangement underscores the bank's commitment to supporting local education and athletics in Bergen County, with the 40,000-square-foot venueâ€”seating 1,852â€”continuing to serve as the primary home for FDU's Knights basketball teams and other events.[1] Specific financial terms of the agreement were not publicly disclosed, but it represents a strategic alliance enhancing both the bank's regional branding and the university's facility resources.[27]
