2023 European U23 Wrestling Championships


The 2023 European U23 Wrestling Championships was an annual age-group continental tournament organized by United World Wrestling for wrestlers aged 21 to 23, contested in men's freestyle, Greco-Roman, and women's freestyle across multiple weight classes from 57 kg to 125 kg. Held from 13 to 19 March in Bucharest, Romania, the event featured competitors from over 30 European nations and served as a key platform for emerging talents to qualify for higher-level international competitions.[1][2]
Azerbaijan dominated the overall medal standings, securing the top position with the most golds, ahead of Ukraine and Turkey, reflecting the competitive depth in freestyle and Greco-Roman disciplines where Caucasian and Black Sea region nations traditionally excel due to rigorous national training systems and talent pipelines. Notable performances included defending champions retaining titles and breakthroughs by athletes like Bulgaria's Georgi Ivanov, who claimed gold in the men's freestyle 125 kg category, highlighting the championships' role in identifying future senior-level contenders.[3][4]

Event Overview

Dates, Location, and Host

The 2023 European U23 Wrestling Championships were held from 13 to 19 March in Bucharest, Romania.[1] The event was hosted by Romania, with the competition taking place at a venue in the Romanian capital.[5] United World Wrestling, the international governing body for the sport, oversaw the organization.[1]

Background and Significance

The 2023 European U23 Wrestling Championships, the eighth edition of the event, were held from March 13 to 19 in Bucharest, Romania.[6] Organized by United World Wrestling (UWW), the tournament encompassed men's freestyle, Greco-Roman, and women's freestyle disciplines for competitors aged 20 to 23, filling the competitive void between junior (under-20) and unrestricted senior categories. This structure accommodates the ongoing physical maturation typical in wrestling, where peak performance often emerges in the mid-20s, enabling athletes to hone skills against age-appropriate rivals rather than immediately facing more experienced seniors.[7] The championships serve as a vital developmental platform, offering essential international exposure that aids in transitioning wrestlers toward senior-level success, including potential Olympic contention.[7] Empirical patterns show that medalists in U23 events frequently progress to podium finishes in continental and world senior tournaments, underscoring the event's role in talent pipelines for wrestling powerhouses. In Europe, nations with robust youth infrastructure, such as Azerbaijan, Turkey, and Georgia, consistently dominate, as evidenced by Azerbaijan's overall team victory in 2023 driven by multiple gold medals, including those from Eldaniz Bayramov.[8] This outcome highlights causal factors like specialized training regimens and national federation support in producing sustained competitive edges.[8]

Competition Formats and Weight Classes

The 2023 European U23 Wrestling Championships featured competitions in three wrestling styles: Greco-Roman (men only), men's freestyle, and women's freestyle, governed by United World Wrestling (UWW) rules. Each weight class followed the standard international championship format of a single-elimination bracket with repechage rounds to determine bronze medalists. Wrestlers defeated by the eventual finalists entered the repechage, divided into upper and lower brackets based on the round of defeat, with winners advancing to challenge semifinal losers for bronze. Brackets were drawn randomly the day prior to competition, with seeding to separate top-ranked athletes; for categories with fewer than eight entrants, preliminary pools or adjusted qualification rounds ensured fair progression. Matches consisted of two three-minute periods with a 30-second tiebreaker if needed, emphasizing takedowns, reversals, and exposures for points. Weight classes adhered to UWW's standardized categories for senior and age-group events, with official weigh-ins conducted without tolerance on competition mornings. Men's freestyle included ten categories: 57 kg, 61 kg, 65 kg, 70 kg, 74 kg, 79 kg, 86 kg, 92 kg, 97 kg, and 125 kg.[10] Men's Greco-Roman competitions covered: 55 kg, 60 kg, 63 kg, 67 kg, 72 kg, 77 kg, 82 kg, 87 kg, 97 kg, and 130 kg, focusing on upper-body techniques without leg holds. Women's freestyle featured: 50 kg, 53 kg, 55 kg, 57 kg, 59 kg, 62 kg, 65 kg, 68 kg, 72 kg, and 76 kg.

Schedule and Venue

Competition Schedule

The 2023 European U23 Wrestling Championships took place from March 13 to 19, 2023, encompassing men's Greco-Roman wrestling on the initial days followed by men's and women's freestyle wrestling events.[1] [13] The event began with weigh-ins, technical meetings, and draws on March 13, setting the stage for competitive action starting March 14.[1] Greco-Roman competitions commenced on March 14 with qualification rounds and progressed through semifinals and finals over the subsequent days, including notable sessions starting at 11:30 a.m. local time on day two.[14] Freestyle events followed, with preliminary bouts and medal matches extending into March 18 and 19, featuring multiple mats for concurrent weight class competitions.[15] All sessions adhered to United World Wrestling protocols, prioritizing athlete recovery between styles.[1]

Incidents and Controversies

Notable Incidents

In the Greco-Roman 55 kg quarter-final bout on March 13, 2023, Azerbaijani wrestler Rahim Hasanov lost to Armenian athlete Karapet Manvelyan by a score of 7:1.[18] Following the match, Hasanov refused to shake hands with Manvelyan and assaulted him by striking him in the face, an act witnessed by officials and spectators at the Polyvalent Hall in Bucharest.[19] United World Wrestling (UWW) immediately disqualified Hasanov from the competition for unsportsmanlike conduct and assault, barring him from further participation and advancing Manvelyan to the semifinals, where the Armenian wrestler ultimately secured a silver medal.[19] [20] The UWW Disciplinary Chamber later formalized the sanction in a December 2023 decision, confirming the assault occurred at the conclusion of the bout and emphasizing Hasanov's violation of the federation's code of conduct, which prohibits physical aggression outside competitive actions.[19] No injuries to Manvelyan were reported in official accounts, and the incident did not disrupt the overall tournament schedule. This event highlighted ongoing national tensions between Azerbaijan and Armenia, though UWW treated it as an individual breach rather than a broader geopolitical issue.[21] The disqualification underscored UWW's strict enforcement of behavioral standards in international competitions, with no appeals upheld from Hasanov's side.[19]

Judging and Rule Disputes

In the men's Greco-Roman 77 kg event quarterfinal on March 13, 2023, Azerbaijani wrestler Rahim Hasanov assaulted his Armenian opponent Karapet Manvelyan immediately after Manvelyan secured a 7-1 victory via a pin.[19] Hasanov refused a post-match handshake and struck Manvelyan, constituting a violation of United World Wrestling (UWW) rules prohibiting unsportsmanlike conduct and aggression.[19] [18] UWW's Disciplinary Chamber investigated the incident, confirming the assault through video evidence and witness statements, and ruled it a breach of Article 7.2 of the UWW Constitution, which mandates respect and fair play.[19] Hasanov's competition results were annulled, disqualifying him from the tournament and awarding Manvelyan progression in the bracket, ultimately resulting in a bronze medal for the Armenian wrestler.[19] [20] No appeal was lodged, and the decision stood without further escalation.[19] No significant judging disputes, such as overturned calls or challenge reviews altering outcomes, were reported across the championships' sessions in Bucharest. UWW referees applied standard protocols under the 2023 International Wrestling Rules, with video replays used for point assessments where requested, but no systemic issues or protests from delegations emerged in official records.[22] This incident marked the primary rule enforcement action, highlighting enforcement against post-match aggression amid regional tensions between Azerbaijani and Armenian competitors.[19]

Medalists

Men's Freestyle

The Men's Freestyle wrestling at the 2023 European U23 Wrestling Championships took place in Bucharest, Romania, from March 17 to 19 as part of the overall event spanning March 13-19. Competition occurred across ten weight classes: 57 kg, 61 kg, 65 kg, 70 kg, 74 kg, 79 kg, 86 kg, 92 kg, 97 kg, and 125 kg, with athletes required to be under 23 years old and adhering to United World Wrestling rules, including a best-of-three bout format in finals where applicable.[1] In the 65 kg division, Khamzat Arsamerzouev of France claimed gold after securing technical superiority (10-0) in his three preliminary bouts and advancing to the final, where opponent Bozhidar Dzhorov of Bulgaria was unable to compete due to injury.[25] At 79 kg, Georgios Kougioumtsidis of Greece defended his title from the previous year, edging Vasile Cotlear of Moldova 3-2 in a closely contested final marked by defensive wrestling and late scoring.[25] Kougioumtsidis's victory highlighted Greece's strength in the middle weights, building on his prior international successes. The heavyweight 125 kg final saw Bulgaria's Georgi Ivanov dominate Ukraine's Volodymyr Kochanov with a 10-0 technical superiority win, securing gold through aggressive takedowns and control.[4] Ivanov's performance underscored Bulgaria's prowess in super heavyweight freestyle, with podium positions also going to Efe Anil Al of Turkey for bronze.[26] Other notable results included a silver medal for Armenia's Mezhlum Mezhlumyan at 61 kg, reflecting competitive depth from Caucasian nations.[27]
