M. B. Shetty

Muddu Babu Shetty (1938 – 23 January 1982)[1] simply known as, M. B. Shetty, or Shetty was an Indian film stuntman, action choreographer and actor in Hindi cinema from the 1950s to the 1980s.[2][3] He had a towering personality with a bald head, often cast as the villain brought down by heroes half his size. He was the father of director Rohit Shetty.

Early life

He was born in 1938 in Mangalore, India. He arrived to Bombay from Udupi. His mother tongue was Tulu. He started as a waiter in Cotton Green after which he got into boxing and bodybuilding.[4]

Film career

He started off as a Fight Instructor in the 1956 movie Heer. He was cast as villain and composed stunts for numerous Hindi and Kannada cinema in the 1970s. He was an action director for numerous films, including cult films like Don (1978), The Great Gambler (1978), Trishul (1978), Deewaar (1975) and Shalimar (1978). Shetty also starred with M.G. Ramachandran (MGR), in Navarathinam (1977) Tamil movie.[5] In 1980, while supervising stunts for Bombay 405 Miles, a stuntman died in an accident, which Shetty blamed on himself, leading to depression and alcoholism that contributed to his death from liver failure in 1982.[6]

Personal life

His first wife was Vinodini, with whom he had two daughters and two sons.[7] With his second wife, Ratna, he had another son, the film director Rohit Shetty.[8] However, the children from his first marriage have publicly stated that they do not acknowledge the second marriage or Rohit Shetty as part of the family.[9][7]
