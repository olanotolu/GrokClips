Elizabeth Chambers (television personality)

Elizabeth Chambers (born August 18, 1982) is an American television personality, entrepreneur, and journalist based in San Antonio, Texas.[1][2] She founded and serves as CEO of BIRD Bakery, a retail chain specializing in baked goods using family recipes, with multiple locations in Texas that have operated for over a decade.[3][4] Chambers studied journalism at the University of Texas at Austin and began her career as a correspondent for outlets including E! News and Access Hollywood, later becoming chief correspondent for the Human Rights Foundation.[2][5] Her television work includes acting appearances in films like The Game Plan (2007) and episodes of Criminal Minds, as well as hosting the Investigation Discovery docuseries Toxic, which examines traumatic relationships drawing from her personal experiences, and serving as a judge and host on Food Network programs alongside contributions to the Today show.[6][7][8] Publicly, she was married to actor Armie Hammer from 2010 until their divorce finalized in 2023, during which time allegations of his abusive behavior emerged, prompting Chambers to address relational trauma in her media projects.[9][7]

Early life

Upbringing and education

Elizabeth Chambers was born in San Antonio, Texas, the eldest child in her family.[10][11] Following her parents' divorce when she was two years old, she moved with her mother through several locations, initially to Houston, Texas, then Denver, Colorado, and later to Half Moon Bay, California, where she secured her first modeling assignment as a teenager.[12][13] This peripatetic early childhood exposed her to diverse environments across Texas, Colorado, and California before she settled into high school in Denver.[14][15] Chambers attended high school in Denver, Colorado, amid these family transitions prompted by the divorce.[14] She subsequently enrolled at the University of Texas at Austin, pursuing a degree in broadcast journalism, a choice influenced by her desire to remain proximate to her maternal grandmother.[12][16] Upon graduating from the University of Texas at Austin, she relocated to Los Angeles to launch her career in media and entertainment.[17][18]

Career

Early modeling and media entry

Chambers began her modeling career during high school, securing runway appearances for Fendi by age 16 and features in Seventeen magazine.[19] At age 17, she relocated to Tokyo for four months, where she appeared in commercials for food products and other advertisements.[20] This early international exposure built on her initial modeling gig booked in Half Moon Bay, California, during her teenage years.[12] Following her journalism degree from the University of Texas at Austin, which she completed a semester early, Chambers entered media as a producer at Current TV, Al Gore's cable network launched in 2005.[16] Her role there involved on-the-ground reporting, including crossing the U.S.-Mexico border with undocumented immigrants to document their experiences.[17] This position marked her transition from modeling to broadcast journalism, leveraging her storytelling interests developed in college.[16] A correspondent gig at Access Hollywood soon followed, propelling her into broader entertainment media with appearances on E! News, the Today show, and competition series like Cupcake Wars.[16] Over five years in Los Angeles-based outlets, she contributed to programs including Entertainment Tonight and E! News Now, establishing her as an on-air personality before shifting toward entrepreneurial ventures.[19][21]

BIRD Bakery founding and expansion

Elizabeth Chambers founded BIRD Bakery in San Antonio, Texas, on March 31, 2012, drawing inspiration from cherished family recipes passed down from her British grandmother and mother, who had operated a local catering company in the city.[22][23] The initial location opened on Broadway, near sites tied to her family's culinary heritage, emphasizing freshly baked goods made with high-quality ingredients such as butter cakes, cookies, and savory items for breakfast and lunch.[4] The bakery expanded beyond San Antonio with a second location in Dallas's Highland Park Village, which opened in spring 2016, introducing its menu of nostalgic American treats to a broader Texas audience. A third U.S. outpost followed in Denver, Colorado, in 2021, marking entry into new markets while maintaining the brand's focus on generational recipes.[24] In 2023, BIRD Bakery achieved international expansion with its first overseas location in Grand Cayman, Cayman Islands, blending American nostalgia with local appeal through items like Chambers' grandmother's recipes adapted for the Caribbean setting.[25] By that year, the chain operated multiple sites, including additional spots in San Antonio such as Alamo Heights, reflecting steady growth amid Chambers' concurrent media career.[26][27]

Television hosting and production

Elizabeth Chambers initiated her television career in journalism after studying at the University of Texas at Austin, starting as a correspondent for Current TV.[28] She subsequently worked as chief correspondent for the Human Rights Foundation and contributed reporting to CNN, E! News Bureau, and Access Hollywood.[5][20] Chambers expanded into food-related hosting, judging competitions on Food Network shows including Chopped Junior, Chopped Sweets, and Best Baker in America.[29][30] She also delivered cooking and baking segments for the Today Show and Cooking Channel.[31] In February 2023, Investigation Discovery entered a development deal with Chambers for her to host and executive produce a series on toxic relationships.[32] This led to Toxic, a six-episode true crime program that premiered on May 5, 2025, featuring survivor interviews and analysis of relational red flags, drawing from Chambers' own marital experiences.[33][34][35]

Advocacy initiatives

Chambers has advocated for agricultural issues, drawing from her upbringing on a commercial cow-calf operation in Texas, which informed her emphasis on sustainable farming practices and rural community support.[36] Through her BIRD Bakery chain, she initiated the "Cupcakes for a Cause" program in 2012, directing proceeds from special cupcake sales toward disaster relief efforts, including aid for victims of wildfires and other crises.[37] In January 2025, she leveraged this initiative to fundraise for those affected by California wildfires, criticizing local government response as inadequate while coordinating direct donations of baked goods and resources.[38] She has supported cancer research and broader charitable causes, often integrating her culinary business to provide meals and treats to first responders during emergencies, such as extensive daily donations starting July 6, 2025, amid unspecified regional challenges.[39][40] In media projects, Chambers has focused on raising awareness of toxic relationships and intimate partner violence, producing the Investigation Discovery series Toxic in 2025, which examines patterns of abuse based on her research into divorce trauma mitigation.[7] This effort stems from personal experiences but aims at public education on relational dynamics, though critics note its reliance on anecdotal narratives over empirical data.[41] Additionally, she has served as chief correspondent for the Human Rights Foundation, contributing to reporting on global human rights violations, though specific outputs from this role remain limited in public documentation.[16]

Personal life

Marriage to Armie Hammer

Elizabeth Chambers and Armie Hammer met in 2007 through a mutual friend in Los Angeles, where Chambers was working as an anchor and correspondent for Al Gore's Current TV network.[13] At the time, Chambers was dating one of Hammer's friends, and the two did not begin a romantic relationship until after that ended.[42] Hammer organized group outings, including an "adventure day," which facilitated their initial interactions, though they dated for approximately two years before becoming engaged.[43] The couple married on May 25, 2010, in a ceremony at All Saints' Episcopal Church in Beverly Hills, California, followed by a reception at a vineyard in Malibu.[44] Hammer expressed strong commitment to marriage early in their courtship, reportedly emphasizing his desire to wed soon after they began dating seriously.[43] The wedding marked the start of a decade-long union during which they collaborated on business ventures, including Chambers' BIRD Bakery expansions, and maintained a public image of partnership through joint appearances at events like film premieres.[45] In subsequent years, the couple publicly celebrated anniversaries, with Chambers posting tributes on social media for their fifth anniversary in 2015, expressing hopes for "at least 50 more," and their tenth in 2020, highlighting 12 years together overall.[45][46] These milestones underscored a period of shared family life and professional support, though underlying tensions later emerged.[47]

Children

Elizabeth Chambers and Armie Hammer have two children together: a daughter named Harper, born on December 1, 2014, in Los Angeles, and a son named Ford Douglas Armand Hammer, born on January 15, 2017.[48][49] The couple announced Harper's birth shortly after her arrival, with Hammer sharing family photos on social media, while Ford's name was revealed publicly via Hammer's Instagram post featuring the newborn.[50] Following their 2023 divorce finalization, Chambers and Hammer maintain joint custody, prioritizing co-parenting amid relocations; Chambers has moved the children five times in five years, including to Westchester, New York, by August 2024.[51] Public appearances with both parents have occurred, such as a June 2025 reunion for Ford's milestone event, reflecting ongoing family involvement despite past controversies.[52] Chambers has emphasized protecting the children's privacy while navigating these changes, as noted in her 2023 interviews.[53]

Divorce proceedings

Chambers filed for divorce from Hammer on July 10, 2020, in Los Angeles County Superior Court, citing irreconcilable differences after nearly 10 years of marriage.[54] [55] The filing followed a joint public announcement of their separation earlier that day, in which both parties stated they had decided to dissolve their marriage amicably and prioritize co-parenting their two children.[55] Chambers sought joint legal and physical custody, spousal support, and primary physical custody of the children, while requesting the court deny Hammer's request for spousal support.[56] The proceedings extended over nearly three years, complicated by Hammer's subsequent public scandals involving allegations of sexual misconduct, which emerged in early 2021 but were not cited as grounds in the initial filing.[54] [57] On June 14, 2023, Hammer submitted his final divorce declaration, leading to a settlement agreement approved by the court on June 20, 2023.[58] [57] The agreement resolved all outstanding issues, including property division, child support, and spousal support, with the couple awarded joint legal and physical custody of their children, Harper (born 2012) and Ford (born 2017).[56] [59] Specific financial terms remained confidential, as per the settlement documents.[56]

Filmography and media appearances

Television roles and hosting

Chambers began her on-screen television work with minor acting roles in the mid-2000s, appearing as a guest in an episode of Criminal Minds in 2005 and Shark in 2006.[72] She also had roles in series such as Moonlight and 2 Broke Girls, portraying characters like Vanessa Nibotita in the latter.[73] Transitioning to journalism, Chambers served as a correspondent for networks including E! News, Access Hollywood, Current TV, CNN, and the Human Rights Foundation, reporting on various human interest and news stories.[5] Her expertise in baking led to recurring lifestyle segments on The Today Show, Food Network, and Cooking Channel, where she demonstrated recipes and promoted her Bird Bakery products.[31] As a contributor to The Today Show, she has appeared in interviews and discussions on topics including personal challenges and entrepreneurship.[3] Chambers has guest-judged baking competitions on Food Network, including Cupcake Wars and Sugar Showdown, evaluating contestants' creations based on taste, presentation, and innovation.[2] In April 2025, she debuted as host of Investigation Discovery's six-episode true-crime series Toxic: Real Stories of the Dark Side of Love, which examines abusive and manipulative relationships through survivor testimonies, with Chambers citing her own life experiences as motivation for amplifying victims' voices.[74][75]

Producing credits

Elizabeth Chambers executive produced the Investigation Discovery docuseries Toxic, which debuted on May 5, 2025, and consists of six episodes exploring the dynamics of toxic and traumatic relationships through survivor interviews and expert analysis.[76][77] Chambers, who also hosted the series, drew from her own experiences with relational abuse, including allegations surrounding her former marriage to actor Armie Hammer, to frame discussions on psychological manipulation and recovery.[7][76] The project originated from a development deal with Investigation Discovery announced in February 2023, marking Chambers' transition from on-air journalism to behind-the-scenes production in true-crime programming.[76] Episodes became available for streaming on Max following initial airings.[77] Prior professional listings occasionally describe Chambers as a producer, but no additional verified producing credits beyond Toxic have been documented in entertainment databases or industry reports.[78]
