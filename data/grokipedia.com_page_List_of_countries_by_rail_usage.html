List of countries by rail usage

This is a list of countries by rail usage. Usage of rail transport may be measured in tonne-kilometres (tkm) or passenger-kilometres (pkm) travelled for freight and passenger transport respectively. This is the number of tonnes or passengers multiplied by the average distance of their journeys in kilometres.

Rail Transport Overview

Globally, rail freight transport reached approximately 11 trillion tonne-kilometres in 2023, with China, Russia, and the United States each accounting for over 20% of the total and India around 6%. NationMaster Statista Of the estimated 4,000 billion passenger-kilometres travelled by rail worldwide in 2023, over 1,580 billion occurred in China and 1,065 billion in India, together comprising more than half of global passenger rail travel. Wikipedia Eurostat As of 2023, the average Japanese person travels about 3,280 km by rail per year (the highest in the world), compared to around 2,466 km for the average Swiss person (second highest). CEIC Data Swissinfo As of 2023, the world's railway network spans about 1.3 million kilometres, having grown since 2014.
  • Approximately 350,000 km are in Europe, mainly for passenger service.
  • Around 370,000 km are in North America, primarily for freight.
  • Over 400,000 km are now in Asia, serving both freight and passengers. Statista Wikipedia
In America and Europe, low-fare airlines and motorways continue to compete with rail for passengers. Asia has seen massive high-speed rail expansion: in 2023, it accounted for over 90% of the world's approximately 1.5 trillion high-speed rail passenger-kilometres, driven largely by China.
