Al-Asr

Al-Asr (Arabic: العصر, romanized: al-ʻaṣr, The Declining Day, Eventide, The Epoch, Time) is the 103rd chapter (sūrah) of the Qur’ān, the Muslim holy book. It contains three āyāt or verses. Surat al-‘Asr is the second shortest chapter by word count, after Al-Kawthar.[1] ۝[2] By the afternoon;
۝ verily man employeth himself in that which will prove of loss:
۝ except those who believe, and do that which is right; and who mutually recommend the truth, and mutually recommend perseverance unto each other.[1]

Surah Al-Asr (103)

Summary

  • 1-2 By time, verily mankind is in loss [3]
  • 3 Except those who believe and do righteous deeds, and enjoin one another to truth and enjoin one another to patience [3]

Meaning

1 By Al-'Asr (the time).
2 Verily! Man is in loss,
3 Except those who believe (in Islamic Monotheism) and do righteous good deeds, and recommend one another to the truth (i.e. order one another to perform all kinds of good deeds (Al-Ma'ruf) which Allah has ordained, and abstain from all kinds of sins and evil deeds (Al-Munkar) which Allah has forbidden), and recommend one another to patience (for the sufferings, harms, and injuries which one may encounter in Allah's Cause during preaching His religion of Islamic Monotheism or Jihad, etc.). Timing and contextual background of revelation

Surah Al-Asr is classified as an early Meccan surah, meaning it was revealed in Mecca during the initial phase of the Prophet Muhammad's mission, prior to the migration to Medina in 622 CE. No specific occasion of revelation (asbāb al-nuzūl) is recorded for this surah in classical sources.[8] During the early Meccan period, surahs like Al-Asr focused on affirming monotheism, warning against disbelief, and encouraging perseverance amid opposition from the Quraysh tribe. According to traditional chronologies, it follows Surah 94 (Al-Inshirah) in the order of revelation.

Theme and subject matter

This sura teaches that all human beings are in loss, except those who have iman (faith in Islam), do righteous deeds and remind others of the Haqq (truth, rights, reality) and remind others of Sabr (patience).[10] The text of the Sura can be translated as the following:
  1. وَالْعَصْرِ‌
By time (it explain that God swears By time).
  1. إِنَّ الْإِنسَانَ لَفِي خُسْرٍ‌
Indeed man is in loss,
  1. إِلَّا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالْحَقِّ وَتَوَاصَوْا بِالصَّبْرِ‌‌
except those who have faith and do righteous deeds, and enjoin one another to [follow] the truth, and enjoin one another to patience.

Importance of Surah Al-Asr

Some Islamic scholars consider this surah to encapsulate the essence of the Quranic message. For example, Imam Shafi'i stated that if only this surah had been revealed, it would have been sufficient for the guidance of humankind.
  • Ibn Kathir, a traditional exegete, holds that this sura is a warning to believers not to waste time or they could be humiliated or even ruined.
  • Imam Shafi, a jurist and traditional scholar of Islam, held that if God had only revealed this surah it would have been sufficient for the guidance of all humankind. It summarized the very essence of the Qur'anic message. Thus, Imam Shafi’i asserts that if one followed its counsel, it was enough for humankind to achieve success in life.
  • Sayyid Qutb, a modern exegete of the Qur'an, asserts that this surah outlines a complete system for human life based on an Islamic viewpoint. He says that it defines, in the clearest and most concise form, the basic concept of faith in the context of its comprehensive reality. He further says that he is gravely shocked to see the loss and ruin in which humanity finds itself. He is critical of Muslims and non-Muslims and particularly witnessing that humankind is turning away from the goodness that God has bestowed upon it. He is confident that the guidance of this surah is what made the early Muslim great as it can do for those that follow it in the long history of humanity. He says that once the early Arabs were aroused by such surahs, they quickly burst the bounds of Arabia and zealously worked toward the salvation of humanity.
  • Al-Tabarani reported a hadith that the companions of the Prophet would not part from one another without one of them reciting Surah Al-Asr to the other.
