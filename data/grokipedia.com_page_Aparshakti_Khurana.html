Aparshakti Khurana

Aparshakti Khurana (born 18 November 1987) is an Indian actor. He is the younger brother of actor Ayushmann Khurrana.[1] Khurana is known for his supporting roles in the films Dangal (2016 film) (2016), Stree (2018 film) (2018), Pati Patni Aur Woh (2019 film) (2019), and Stree 2 (2024), as well as for his starring role in the drama series Jubilee (TV series) (2023).

Early life

Khurana was born on 18 November 1987 in Chandigarh.[2] His father P. Khurana was an astrologer and an author on the subject of black magic, whereas his mother Poonam is a housewife of half-Burmese descent.[3] He completed his schooling at Hansraj Public School and graduation in law from Panjab University in Chandigarh.[2][4] He was also into sports at school[5] and captained the Haryana U-19 cricket team.[5]

Career

Khurana initially pursued a career in law, studying for five years in Chandigarh and briefly working at the Delhi High Court for three months under advocate Amarjit Singh Chandhok, where he handled case research and file management.[6] However, finding no passion in the field, he transitioned to radio, working as a radio jockey for eight years across three stations in Delhi, honing his voice modulation and entertainment skills through celebrity interviews and hosting.[7][8] He also engaged in theatre during college and later hosted television shows, which helped develop his on-screen presence. Inspired by his brother Ayushmann Khurrana's success in music and acting, Aparshakti moved to Mumbai and began auditioning. He made his acting debut in the comedy film Saat Uchakkey (2016), playing a supporting role, followed by his breakthrough in Dangal (2016 film) (2016), where he portrayed Omkar, the coach of the Phogat sisters, opposite Aamir Khan.[9][7][8] Though his role in Dangal (2016 film) went largely unnoticed initially, it marked his entry into mainstream Bollywood.[9] In 2017, he appeared in Badrinath Ki Dulhania as Varun Dhawan's friend.[10] The following year, 2018, saw him in comedic roles in Happy Phirr Bhag Jayegi and Stree (2018 film), the latter a horror-comedy hit where he played the character Vicky alongside Rajkummar Rao and Shraddha Kapoor, earning praise for his comic timing.[11][12] He also starred in the OTT film Rajma Chawal (2018).[13] Khurana's 2019 was prolific, with roles in Luka Chuppi opposite Kartik Aaryan and Kriti Sanon, Jabariya Jodi with Parineeti Chopra, and Pati Patni Aur Woh (2019 film), again with Kartik Aaryan, where his chemistry was highlighted.[14][15][16] He also featured in the short film Kanpuriye (2019).[17] In 2020, he joined the ensemble cast of Street Dancer 3D.[18] The COVID-19 pandemic shifted focus to OTT platforms. In 2021, he took his first lead role in the comedy-drama Helmet (film) opposite Pranutan Bahl, a film close to his heart for its bold themes.[19][8] He followed with Hum Do Hamare Do, playing a supporting role in the family comedy with Kriti Sanon and Rajkummar Rao.[20][21] Post-pandemic, 2022 brought Dhokha: Round D Corner, a thriller with R. Madhavan and Khushalii Kumar.[22] He reprised his Stree (2018 film) character in the horror-comedy sequel Bhediya (film) (2022).[23] In 2023, Khurana starred as Jay Khanna in the period drama web series Jubilee (TV series) on SonyLIV, portraying an ambitious screenwriter in 1940s-1950s Bombay, which received critical acclaim for his performance.[23] He continued with supporting roles in 2024 releases: Stree 2, a massive box-office success where he returned as Vicky; the heist thriller Berlin (2024 film) on ZEE5; and the sci-fi anthology CTRL (film) on Netflix.[22][24] Additionally, he hosted the business quiz show Business Baazi in 2022.[25] In music, Khurana has contributed as a playback singer and appeared in music videos, debuting as a singer-composer with "Kudiye Ni" (2019) and releasing singles like "Balle Ni Balle" (2022) and "Choti Choti Gal" (2022).[26][27][28] His discography includes tracks such as "Tere Do Naina" and "Aaya Jado Da."[29][30] As of October 2025, Khurana is set to make his Tamil debut in the sci-fi crime thriller ROOT (film) and has upcoming Hindi projects including Gunmaaster G9 and Side Heroes.[31]

Personal life

He married Aakriti Ahuja, a businesswoman and an ISB graduate (whom he met at a dance class in Chandigarh)[5] on 7 September 2014. In June 2021, the couple announced that they were expecting their first child.[32] The couple became parents to a baby girl on 27 August 2021, Arzoie A Khurana.[33]
