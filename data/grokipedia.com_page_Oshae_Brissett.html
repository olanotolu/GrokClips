Oshae Brissett

Stats at NBA.com
Stats at Basketball Reference Oshae Jahve Brissett (/oʊˈʃeɪ brɪˈsɛt/ oh-SHAY brih-SET;[1] born June 20, 1998) is a Canadian professional basketball player for Maccabi Tel Aviv of the Israeli Ligat HaAl and the EuroLeague. He played college basketball in Division I for the Syracuse Orange for two years before signing with the Toronto Raptors as an undrafted free agent in 2019 and the Indiana Pacers in 2021. Brissett joined the Boston Celtics in 2023, where he won his first NBA championship in 2024.

Early life

Brissett was born in Toronto to McKeitha McFarlane and Bernard Brissett, who had separated around the time of his birth.[2] He has an older brother, Dejon, a professional Canadian football player, and a younger sister. He is of Jamaican descent. Brissett spent the first four years of his life living in the Jane and Finch neighbourhood before moving to Mississauga.[3] After initially attending high school at St. Aloysius Gonzaga Secondary School in Mississauga, Brissett moved away from home to attend Findlay Prep in Henderson, Nevada in order to hone his skills against better competition and to improve his chances of recruitment by a major college. After Findlay Prep, Brissett returned to Canada and spent a year at the Athlete Institute in Mono, Ontario, where he excelled as team captain.[4]

College career

Brissett committed to Syracuse Orange men's basketball after receiving offers from USC, Oregon, and Memphis. He entered the Orange starting lineup immediately, providing an interior presence alongside guards Tyus Battle and Frank Howard. Brissett averaged 14.9 points and 8.8 rebounds per game in his first collegiate season.[5] Following Brissett's freshman season, there was speculation that he could test the waters in the 2018 NBA draft. However, he announced his intention to return to Syracuse for his sophomore season on April 11, 2018.[6] As a sophomore, Brissett averaged 12.4 points and 7.5 rebounds per game. After the season, he declared his eligibility for the NBA Draft and forfeited his remaining two years of collegiate eligibility.[7] However, Brissett was not drafted.

Professional career

Toronto Raptors (2019–2020)

On July 23, 2019, Brissett signed with the Toronto Raptors of the National Basketball Association.[8] On October 21, Brissett's contract was converted to a two-way contract. Under the terms of the deal, he would split time between the Raptors and their NBA G League affiliate, the Raptors 905.[9] He made his NBA debut on November 19 against the Miami Heat.[10] On January 7, 2020, Brissett played his best game of the season, scoring a then-career high 12 points and grabbing six rebounds, one assist, and one steal in a 101–99 loss against the Portland Trail Blazers.[11] In the G League, Brissett averaged 15.8 points and 6.9 rebounds per game. Following the season, the Raptors extended a qualifying offer to him, making him a restricted free agent.[12] He later signed a partially guaranteed, two-year contract extension.[13] Brissett was waived by the team at the end of training camp preceding the 2020–21 season.[14]

Fort Wayne Mad Ants (2021)

Brissett was selected 21st overall in the first 2021 NBA G League draft by the Fort Wayne Mad Ants and was later included in the single-site season roster announced on January 11, 2021.[15] He played in 12 games and averaged 18.6 points, 9.8 rebounds, and 2.3 assists in 34.6 minutes, earning a spot in the All-NBA G League Second Team.[16]

Indiana Pacers (2021–2023)

On April 1, 2021, Brissett signed a 10-day contract with the Indiana Pacers of the NBA.[17] Ten days later, he signed a second 10-day contract.[18] On April 20, 2021, Brissett made his first career start in a 109–94 loss to the San Antonio Spurs, scoring 13 points on 5-of-8 shooting from the field and 3-of-5 from three, along with six rebounds and a steal.[19] The next day, he signed a three-year deal with the team[20] and started that night's game against the Oklahoma City Thunder scoring 23 points, grabbing 12 rebounds, and recording 3 blocks.[21] On May 18, 2021, Brissett scored 23 points with five rebounds and two assists in a 144–117 play-in win over the Charlotte Hornets.[22] In the 2022–23 season, Brissett appeared in 65 games for the Pacers, averaging 6.1 points and 3.4 rebounds in 16.7 minutes per game.

Boston Celtics (2023–2024)

On July 6, 2023, Brissett signed with the Boston Celtics.[23] On October 27, 2023, he made his Celtics debut in the first game of the 2023-24 season in a 119–111 win against the Miami Heat, scoring 2 points and making 5 rebounds.[24] He went on to play 55 games during the regular season, averaging around 11.5 minutes, 2.9 rebounds, 0.8 assists, 0.3 steals, and 3.7 points per game.[25] During Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Finals against his former Indiana Pacers, Brissett played a key role in the Celtics' defense where he held the Pacers to just 1-for-3 shooting and 3 steals during his 12 minutes on the court, expanding the team's 7–0 run to 18–0 in the third quarter of the game.[26] He reached his first NBA Finals with the Celtics in 2024.[27] The Celtics defeated the Dallas Mavericks in 5 games to give Brissett his first championship. He declined a $2.5 million player option on June 23, 2024, to go to free agency.

Long Island Nets (2025)

On January 18, 2025, Brissett signed with the Long Island Nets of the NBA G League.[28]

Philadelphia 76ers (2025)

On March 14, 2025, Brissett signed a 10-day contract with the Philadelphia 76ers.[29] In 6 appearances (2 starts) with the 76ers, Brissett averaged 8.7 points, 3.7 rebounds, and 0.7 assists.

Maccabi Tel Aviv (2025–present)

On July 15, 2025, Brissett signed with Maccabi Tel Aviv of the Ligat HaAl and the EuroLeague.[30]

National team career

Youth career

Brissett represented Canada at the youth level, competing in the 2016 FIBA Americas Under-18 Championship. He averaged 16.4 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 1.0 assist per game over five games, helping Canada secure the silver medal.

Senior career

Brissett participated in preparations for the 2019 FIBA Basketball World Cup with Canada's senior men's national team but ultimately withdrew. On May 24, 2022, Brissett agreed to a three-year commitment to play with the Canadian senior men's national team.[31] Despite this, he did not appear in competitive senior international games. He was selected for the 2023 FIBA Basketball World Cup roster but was ruled out due to a knee injury. In June 2024, Brissett was invited to Canada's training camp for the 2024 Summer Olympics but was released on July 3, 2024, to focus on NBA free agency. As of October 2025, Brissett has no further recorded senior national team appearances.

Personal life

Brissett has an older brother named Dejon who was drafted second overall in the 2020 CFL draft by the Toronto Argonauts.[32] In 2024, both brothers achieved championships in their sports: Oshae with the NBA's Boston Celtics and Dejon with the CFL's Toronto Argonauts in the Grey Cup. Brissett has a daughter, Ellai Aria, born on May 28, 2020.[33]
