Hail the Sun

Hail the Sun is an American post-hardcore band from Chico, California. Formed in 2009, the band currently consists of lead vocalist and drummer Donovan Melero, guitarist Shane Gann, guitarist Aric Garcia, bass guitarist John Stirrat, and touring and studio drummer Allen Casillas. They are known for their eclectic progressive rock and math rock style, along with traditional elements of screamo and post-hardcore. They released their debut studio album, POW! Right In the Kisser! (2010), and EP, Elephantitis (2012), independently before signing to Blue Swan Records the following year. The group released their sophomore studio album, Wake , in 2014. In 2016, the band departed from the label, signing to Equal Vision Records and released their third studio album, Culture Scars on June 17, 2016, which charted at number 38 on the Billboard Top Rock Albums chart.[3] In 2017, the band released their second EP, Secret Wars , which was met with critical acclaim and was noted for the group's heavier and technical post-hardcore sound. They released their fourth studio album, Mental Knife , on September 28, 2018.[4] In 2021, the band signed with Rude Records and released their fifth studio album, New Age Filth. They returned to Equal Vision Records for their sixth studio album, Divine Inner Tension, in 2023, followed by their seventh studio album, cut. turn. fade. back., on October 24, 2025.[5][6][7] Hail the Sun has toured with Dance Gavin Dance, I the Mighty, Circa Survive, and Silverstein, among others. They have also performed at numerous music festivals such as Chain Fest at the Chain Reaction, South By So What?!, Horns Up Festival, and Swanfest.

History

2009â€“13: Formation, POW! Right In the Kisser , and Elephantitis

Originating in Chico, California, members Donovan Melero, Shane Gann, Aric Garcia, and John Stirrat formed Hail the Sun in 2009 whilst in college.[8] They wrote and recorded their debut full-length studio album, POW! Right In the Kisser! , over the course of three months, which was released on May 21, 2010.[9] The band released the EP album, Elephantitis , on July 3, 2012.[10] The creative process was heavily influenced by other post-hardcore musical groups the Fall of Troy and Hot Cross, and was dubbed "a sort of paradigm shift in the way post-hardcore can be played, combining a progressive hammer-on-pull-off heavy style of guitar playing and more free range drumming with a huge decrease in riffage, conventional time signatures and simplistic song structures resulted in a spastic, technically savvy sound which fit right in with the high energy mood and feel post-hardcore was known for."[11]

2014â€“17: Wake , Culture Scars , and Secret Wars EP

After three years of extensive touring, Hail the Sun became acquainted with Sacramento, California-based post-hardcore band Dance Gavin Dance, eventually signing to Will Swan's independent record label Blue Swan Records.[12] Hail the Sun released their sophomore studio album, Wake , on September 23, 2014, through the label, to critical acclaim.[13] Throughout 2014 and 2015, Hail the Sun toured extensively on respective tours from I the Mighty, Too Close to Touch, Dance Gavin Dance, A Lot Like Birds, the Ongoing Concept, Stolas, Polyphia, Our Last Night, Palisades, among several others. On December 3, 2015, it was revealed that the group had parted ways with Blue Swan Records and signed with record label Equal Vision Records, releasing a new demo version of the song, "Paranoia".[14] On March 25, 2016, the band released the official version of the single, "Paranoia".[15] On April 27, it was announced that Hail the Sun was set to release their third studio album, Culture Scars , on June 17, 2016.[16] The band later toured on the 2016 Vans Warped Tour.[17] Speaking about the band's third album, Culture Scars , guitarist Shane Gann said "Culture Scars is a term we came up with to refer to those scars, both physical and emotional, that come about due to certain things that society deems as normal and acceptable. We donâ€™t normally put much thought into the amount of pain that may be caused by dirty politics, found within a family with a 'problem child', or existing behind the scenes of an adult entertainment shoot. These themes are so commonplace in the world, and yet there is often so much trauma and hurt that is brought about by all these scenarios. We wanted to shed some light on the darker side of what we think of as normalcy."[18] On November 8, 2017, Hail the Sun released the single "1109" and announced their extended play (EP), Secret Wars , which was released on November 10, 2017.[19]

2018: Mental Knife

On January 2, 2018, Hail the Sun published a statement confirming that they were recording their upcoming fourth studio album with producer Beau Burchell of American post-hardcore band Saosin.[20] The band released two new songs from the album on June 20, 2018, with the entirety of Mental Knife later releasing on September 28, 2018.

2019â€“21: New Age Filth

On September 4, 2019, Hail the Sun posted an announcement of their new single and video on their Facebook page.[21] The band later announced re-entering the studio to record their next studio album New Age Filth. On April 16, 2021, their fifth studio album was released.[22]

2023â€“present: Divine Inner Tension

On January 5, 2023, Hail the Sun released a new song and music video "Mind Rider".[23] They later released their sixth studio album Divine Inner Tension on August 11, 2023.[24] This is the first release by the band where the drums were not tracked in studio by Melero.[25] On March 1, 2024 they released a new song, "Secondary Worship".

2025â€“present: Cut.Turn.Fade.Back

On June 23, 2025, Hail the Sun released a new song "The Drooling Class". Then, on August 14, 2025, they released "War Crimes", heralding the release of their seventh studio album, cut. turn. fade. back., on October 24, 2025.[26] The album was released via Equal Vision Records.

Music and influence

Donovan Melero's vocal style has been often compared to that of recording artist Anthony Green of Circa Survive and Saosin.[27] Hail the Sun has been labelled as a post-hardcore band using elements of progressive rock, math rock, screamo, and emo in its music.[28] The band's influences include At the Drive-In, Circa Survive, Coheed and Cambria, The Fall of Troy, Glassjaw, HORSE the Band, The Mars Volta, NOFX, Queen, and Weezer.[29]

Side projects

Along with fronting Hail The Sun, Donovan Melero also creates music as a solo artist, releasing his debut album Chelsea Park After Dark in 2022.[30] He was the lead vocalist of Sianvar, which has been on indefinite hiatus since 2019,[31] and is a member of Nova Charisma.[32] Donovan is also currently a booking agent for Sound Talent Group,[33] and in 2020 he founded his own record label, magazine and festival under the brand Kill Iconic.[34] In May 2025, Kill Iconic Records announced a partnership with Equal Vision Records.[35]
