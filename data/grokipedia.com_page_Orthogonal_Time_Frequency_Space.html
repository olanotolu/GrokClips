Orthogonal Time Frequency Space

Orthogonal Time Frequency Space (OTFS) is a 2D modulation technique that transforms the information carried in the delay-Doppler coordinate system. The information is transformed in a similar time-frequency domain as utilized by the traditional schemes of modulation such as TDMA, CDMA, and OFDM.[1] It was first used for fixed wireless, and is now a contending waveform for 6G technology due to its robustness in high-speed vehicular scenarios.[2]

OTFS

OTFS is a modulation scheme where each transmitted symbol experiences a near-constant channel gain even in channels at high carrier frequencies (mm-wave) or with high Doppler. The OTFS signal is localized in the delay-Doppler representation.[3][4][5] It effectively transforms the time-varying multipath channel into a 2D channel in the Delay-Doppler domain. Using this transformation, along with equalization within this domain, each symbol experiences similar channel gain throughout the transmission.[6] The modulation begins with first mapping the information symbols x[k,l]x[k, l]x[k,l] in the Delayâ€“Doppler domain to symbols X[n,m]X [n, m]X[n,m] in the time-frequency domain using the inverse symplectic Fourier transform, for creating the time-domain signal s(t)s(t)s(t) which is transmitted over a wireless channel. In some formulations, this process uses the inverse Zak transform. At the receiver end, the time-domain signal r(t)r(t)r(t) is mapped to the domain of time-frequency using the Wigner transform, which is the inverse of the Heisenberg transform, and then for symbol demodulation uses the Delayâ€“Doppler domain.[7] The technology is being considered for 6G networks.[2] In terms of transmission, the transmit signals of OTFS in either discrete time sequence or continuous time waveform are the same as that of single antenna vector OFDM (VOFDM) systems, no matter whether a channel is stationary or not. There have been several works that have shown this. One of them is in Signal Processing 214 (2024), 109254.[8]

Channel Equalization and Estimation

Low complexity equalization has been proposed based on Message Passing (MP), Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC), and Linear equalization methods.[6][9][10][11][12] The diversity of OTFS modulation has been studied.[13][14] Channel estimation pilots are transmitted in the delay Doppler domain.[15][16] Iterative Rake decision feedback equalization achieves superior or comparable performance to message passing with much lower complexity that is independent of the constellation size.[17][18][19][20] The performance of OTFS modulation in static multi-path channels has also been studied.[21] Recent advances include deep learning-based channel estimation and symbol detection schemes, as well as hybrid precoding for massive MIMO-OTFS systems.[22][23]

Practical Pulse Shaping Waveforms

It is impossible to transmit an ideal pulse shape due to the time-frequency uncertainty principle.[24] This motivated some works for practical pulse shaped OTFS systems.[25][26]

Pulsone

A pulsone (stands for pulse + tone) is the time realization of a quasi-periodic pulse in delay-Doppler and it serves as the carrier waveform of the OTFS modulation format. Of particular interest are pulsones in the crystalline regime (when the periods are greater than the spread of the channel). In this regime, the pulsone remains invariant under the operations of time delay and Doppler shift which results in non-fading and predictable channel interaction, rendering pulsones ideal for mobility and machine learning applications.[27][28] In October 2025, Cohere Technologies launched Pulsone Technology, a commercial implementation of Zak-OTFS using pulsones for enhanced performance in high-mobility and multi-satellite networks.[29]

Zak-OTFS

Zak Transform-based Orthogonal Time Frequency Space (OTFS) is a modulation technique which carries information in the Delay-Doppler domain, rather than the time-frequency domain used by traditional modulations such as OFDM. It is optimized for challenging wireless communication environments applicable to 6G use cases, such as FR3, NTN and ISAC.[30][31] Zak-OTFS adapts to different channel conditions through waveform adjustments such as spread pulsones and lattice configurations.[30] As of October 2025, Zak-OTFS has been proposed for consideration as a potential 6G waveform in 3GPP studies.[32] In October 2025, Cohere Technologies launched the Pulsone, a related waveform for OTFS commercialization targeting defense applications.[33][34][35]

Orthogonal Time Frequency Space Modulation

Application

OTFS offers several advantages in doubly-dispersive channels with high Doppler dispersion. Environments such as these are encountered in mm-wave systems, due to both larger Doppler spreads and higher phase noise.[36] Applications of OTFS waveforms for radio detection and ranging (radar) has also been proposed.[37][38] OTFS is also applied in integrated sensing and communication (ISAC) systems, using the same waveform for both radar sensing and data transmission, with advantages in high-Doppler scenarios.[39]

Patents

The idea for OTFS was first patented by Ronny Hadani and Shlomo Rakib and assigned to Cohere Technologies Inc.[40] Cohere Technologies holds over 70 patents related to OTFS, many co-invented by Hadani.
