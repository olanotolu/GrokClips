Quazi Deen Mohammad

Quazi Deen Mohammad is a Bangladeshi physician, academic and neurologist. He was the founding director of National Institute of Neuroscience, Dhaka.[2][3] He was the 37th principal of Dhaka Medical College and 19th President of Bangladesh College of Physicians and Surgeons (BCPS).

Early life and education

Quazi Deen Mohammad was born in Luxmipur, Faridpur of Bangladesh, the then East Pakistan. He passed SSC from Faridpur Zilla School and HSC from Government Rajendra College. He passed MBBS from Dhaka Medical College in 1978. He completed fellowship in Medicine from Bangladesh College of Physicians and Surgeons in 1983. He did fellowship in clinical neurophysiology from University Hospital Madison, U.S. in 1989. He achieved M.D. in Neurology from University of Dhaka in 1994.[4]

Career

Quazi Deen Mohammad joined as assistant professor of neurology at the then IPGMR, now known as Bangladesh Medical University, in 1984. He joined Dhaka Medical College as professor of neurology in 1998. He served as the principal of Dhaka Medical College from 2008 to 2014. He was the founding director of the National Institute of Neurosciences & Hospital from September 2012 to 13 February 2025, where he continues to work as professor of neurology. He served as president of the Bangladesh College of Physicians and Surgeons from March 2019 to 4 March 2023. He regularly provides treatment to his patients at S.P.R.C & Neurology Hospital.[5]

Research Activities

  • Editorial Board member, Neurology Asia (Malaysia)[6]
  • PI, Annexon 005 (ANX005) clinical trial (C1q suppressor in GBS) in Bangladesh; Phase 3 trial showed positive efficacy in reducing ventilation requirements and improving disability outcomes in GBS patients (as of 2025)[7]
  • PI, community survey Stroke, Epilepsy, Dementia
  • Senior contributor in the collaborative study between NINS and SITraN (University of Sheffield, UK) on Neurodegenerative disease particularly motor Neuron disease and Parkinson's disease.
  • Senior contributor in different collaborative study between NINS and Erasmus MC (Netherlands), CDC Atlanta (U.S.) Annexon (U.S.) and ICDDR,B

International reviewer

  • Member, Editorial Board, Neurology Asia (Malaysia)[8]

Publications

As of October 2025, Quazi Deen Mohammad has published 148 research articles in national and international journals.[9] Notable works include:
  • "Prevalence of epilepsy in Bangladesh: Results from a national household survey" (2020).[10]
  • "Prevalence and risk factors of stroke in Bangladesh: A nationwide population-based survey" (2022).[11]
  • "Establishment of National Institute of Neurosciences & Hospital, Bangladesh Perspective" (2015).[12]
