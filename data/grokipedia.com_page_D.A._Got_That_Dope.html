D.A. Got That Dope

David Lewis "D.A." Doman (born August 3, 1984), known professionally as D.A. Got That Dope (stylized as d.a. got that dope), is an American record producer from Chicago, Illinois. He began his career in the mid-2000s working with Chicago-based underground artists such as Bump J, Mikkey Halsted, and Kidz in the Hall. He handled drum programming for record producer J. R. Rotem for several years, and in 2011, he moved to Los Angeles to further pursue work in the music industry.[1] D.A. has since been credited on the singles "Privacy" by Chris Brown, "Zeze" by Kodak Black, "Taste" by Tyga, "Godzilla" by Eminem and Juice Wrld, "Demons" by Doja Cat, and "Tyrant" by BeyoncÃ©.[2][3] In 2019, Doman discovered American rapper and singer 24kGoldn, for whom he has served as A&R.[4]

Career

In 2009, D.A. Got That Dope co-produced the single "Everybody in Love" for British boy band JLS, which earned platinum certification by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI) for 600,000 units and peaked at number one on the UK Singles Chart.[5] After working with J.R. Rotem on drum programming and moving to Los Angeles in 2011, Doman established himself as a prominent producer in hip hop and trap music. In 2019, he discovered rapper 24kGoldn and has served as his A&R representative.[4] In March 2021, Doman signed a publishing deal with Warner Chappell Music, highlighting his multi-platinum successes.[5] In 2024, he co-produced "Tyrant" featuring Dolly Parton on BeyoncÃ©'s album Cowboy Carter.
