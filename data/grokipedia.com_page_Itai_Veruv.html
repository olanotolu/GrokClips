Itai Veruv


Itai Veruv is a retired Israeli major general (aluf) who commanded the Israel Defense Forces Depth Corps.[1] He rose to prominence for directing rescue and recovery operations in Gaza border communities following the Hamas attacks of October 7, 2023, where he briefed journalists on the systematic slaughter of civilians, stating, "It's not a war, it's not a battlefield, it's a massacre."[2][3] After retiring from active duty, Veruv was appointed Chief Global Engagement Officer at ValorNet, a platform focused on digital wellness and human performance optimization.[4]

Early Life and Education

Childhood and Family Background

Itai Veruv was born and raised in Kibbutz Ramat HaKovesh, a communal agricultural settlement located in the Sharon plain of central Israel, established in 1932 by members of the Hashomer Hatzair youth movement. The kibbutz's collective structure shaped his early years, fostering values of shared labor, education, and community responsibility typical of such settlements during the mid-20th century. Limited public details exist regarding his immediate family, as kibbutz upbringing often emphasized communal child-rearing over individualized parental narratives. Veruv later reflected on transitioning from kibbutz fields to military training fields, highlighting the continuity of disciplined, outdoor-oriented life from childhood into service. He enlisted in the Israel Defense Forces in 1985 at age 18 or 19, volunteering for the Paratroopers Brigade via the Nahal program for kibbutz youth.

Military Training and Initial Qualifications

Veruv enlisted in the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) in 1985 at the age of 19, volunteering for service in the elite Paratroopers Brigade. Born and raised in Kibbutz Ramat HaKovesh, he joined the Nahal Paratrooper Battalion through the youth settlement track (Bnei Mikusham), a program integrating kibbutz youth into combat service with agricultural settlement components. His initial military training followed the standard progression for paratrooper recruits, beginning with the basic fighter course (masloul locham) emphasizing physical conditioning, weapons handling, and infantry tactics. This was supplemented by airborne qualification, involving parachute jumps and specialized maneuvers unique to the brigade. Upon completion, he advanced to the infantry squad commanders course (kors mekhakim infantry), qualifying him to lead small combat teams in urban and rural operations.[5] Veruv then completed the infantry officers candidate school (kors ktzinut infantry), earning his commission as an infantry officer in the Paratroopers Brigade during 1985.[5] This rigorous program, lasting several months, covered advanced leadership, tactical planning, and decision-making under stress, preparing graduates for platoon command in high-intensity combat environments.[5] These qualifications established his foundation in airborne infantry operations, which informed his subsequent roles as a fighter and squad leader within the brigade.

Military Career

Enlistment and Early Service

Veruv enlisted in the Israel Defense Forces in 1985, volunteering for service in the Paratroopers Brigade and joining the track for children of agricultural settlements in the Nahal Paratrooper Battalion. He underwent basic infantry training as a combat soldier, followed by the infantry squad commanders course and officers training course. During his initial operational assignments in the Paratroopers Brigade, Veruv served as a platoon commander and subsequently as a company commander in the 101st Battalion.[6] These roles involved leading infantry operations, including combat patrols and training exercises typical of elite airborne units during the mid-1980s period of heightened security tensions in southern Lebanon and the West Bank.[6]

Rise Through Ranks and Key Commands

Veruv progressed through senior command roles in the Israel Defense Forces, including brigade-level leadership in the Kfir Brigade during operations in the West Bank around 2009.[7] He later served as commander of the Gaza Division with the rank of brigadier general, where in July 2015 he briefed United Nations Secretary-General Ban Ki-moon on Hamas terror tunnels during a visit to southern Israel.[8] Following these assignments, Veruv was promoted to major general and appointed head of the IDF Military Colleges, responsible for advanced training, doctrine development, and coordination of legal efforts against potential International Criminal Court investigations into IDF actions by early 2021.[9] In 2020, he assumed command of the Depth Corps, tasked with planning and executing operations deep into enemy territory, including long-range strikes, while retaining oversight of the Military Colleges.[10] Under his Depth Corps leadership, Veruv directed units focused on multi-arena warfare capabilities, as evidenced by Defense Minister Benny Gantz's January 2021 visit to the corps' long-range attack headquarters, emphasizing readiness for strikes against distant threats such as Iranian targets.[11] These roles underscored his expertise in operational depth and strategic planning within the Ground Forces structure.

Response to October 7, 2023, Hamas Attack

Major General Itai Veruv, serving as a senior IDF officer, participated in the immediate aftermath operations following the Hamas-led attack on October 7, 2023, which involved thousands of militants breaching the Gaza border and targeting southern Israeli communities, resulting in approximately 1,200 deaths and over 250 abductions.[12] On October 10, 2023, Veruv led efforts to survey and secure sites in kibbutzim such as Kfar Aza, where Hamas forces had conducted mass killings, including of civilians in their homes.[2][13] During these operations, Veruv described the scenes encountered by IDF forces as unprecedented atrocities, stating, "It's not a war, it's not a battlefield, it's a massacre," upon discovering bodies of families, including infants, killed in their residences.[12][2] He briefed journalists on the evidence of deliberate civilian targeting, noting the systematic nature of the violence that left communities devastated and requiring extensive forensic and rescue coordination.[3][14] Veruv's role emphasized documenting the scale of the assault to inform public and international awareness, amid ongoing IDF mobilization to neutralize remaining threats and recover hostages, while highlighting the failure of initial border defenses that allowed the incursion.[15] His assessments contributed to the IDF's strategic shift toward a broader counteroffensive into Gaza, underscoring the attack's characterization as a deliberate act of mass murder rather than conventional warfare.

Leadership in IDF Depth Corps and Military Colleges

Major General Itai Veruv assumed command of the IDF Military Colleges prior to 2020, overseeing the army's operational training programs and professional development for senior officers.[9] In February 2020, he was additionally appointed as commander of the Depth Corps, a unit established in 2011 to integrate and execute long-range, deep-penetration operations across ground, air, and naval forces.[10] This dual role reflects the IDF's emphasis on aligning doctrinal education with advanced operational capabilities for multi-domain warfare.[16] Under Veruv's leadership, the Depth Corps focuses on coordinating strikes and maneuvers deep in enemy territory, prioritizing offensive depth to disrupt adversary command structures and logistics.[17] He has advocated for dynamic, end-to-end operational approaches that bypass static defenses, drawing from historical lessons in conflicts with Hezbollah and Hamas.[18] The Corps conducted joint exercises, including large-scale maneuvers in Cyprus in April 2021, to enhance interoperability and demonstrate long-range strike proficiency.[19] In his capacity with the Military Colleges, Veruv has contributed to international military education initiatives, such as partnerships with the Jewish Institute for National Security of America (JINSA) for leadership training programs aimed at strengthening U.S.-Israel strategic alignment.[20] In February 2021, he was designated to lead the IDF's coordination against International Criminal Court probes into alleged war crimes, managing legal preparedness and evidentiary responses for ongoing and past operations.[9][21] These efforts underscore his role in fortifying the IDF's institutional resilience amid legal and doctrinal challenges.[11]

Post-Military Activities

Retirement from IDF

Major General Itai Veruv concluded his active-duty tenure in the Israel Defense Forces on July 16, 2023, after serving more than four years as Commander of the IDF Military Colleges and Commander of the Depth Corps. The handover ceremony took place at IDF headquarters in Tel Aviv, where he was succeeded by Major General Nimrod Aloni in both positions. Veruv's roles involved overseeing doctrinal development, officer training, and coordination of deep-strike operations against distant threats, building on his prior experience in operational commands such as Gaza Division and Ground Forces Headquarters. Less than three months later, on October 7, 2023, Veruv was recalled from retirement to lead initial IDF counter-offensive efforts following the Hamas incursion into southern Israel.[22] [23] He commanded forces securing communities like Kibbutz Kfar Aza, where he described discovering scenes of civilian massacres unprecedented in his nearly four-decade career.[22] This emergency mobilization highlighted Veruv's reserve status post-retirement, as the IDF activated experienced officers amid the surprise attack that killed over 1,200 Israelis and saw 251 taken hostage.[24] Veruv's formal retirement aligned with standard IDF practices for senior officers reaching tenure limits or age thresholds, typically around rank-specific service caps for major generals. His departure from active command did not preclude reserve obligations, enabling his rapid return to operational leadership during the crisis. No public statements from Veruv detailed personal motivations for retirement, though his successor's later criticisms of IDF accountability suggest internal reflections on pre-October 7 preparedness may have influenced senior transitions.[25]

Role in Private Sector and International Engagements

Following his retirement from the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), Itai Veruv was appointed Chief Global Engagement Officer at ValorNet, a digital platform specializing in holistic wellness, human performance optimization, and related technologies, effective June 11, 2025.[4] In this capacity, Veruv applies his over four decades of operational leadership in the IDF to drive international expansion, stakeholder partnerships, and strategic initiatives aimed at integrating military-derived resilience principles into civilian wellness applications.[4] The appointment underscores ValorNet's emphasis on recruiting experts with proven high-stakes decision-making backgrounds to address global demands for performance enhancement tools.[26] Veruv's international engagements in this role include fostering collaborations across sectors, drawing on his prior exposure to multinational military training programs, such as his graduation from the Royal College of Defence Studies in the United Kingdom during active service.[17] These efforts position him to bridge defense-sector insights with private-sector innovation, particularly in regions seeking advanced human performance solutions amid geopolitical tensions. No additional private-sector roles or formal international advisory positions have been publicly documented as of October 2025.

Public Statements and Views

On National Security and Terrorism

Following the October 7, 2023, Hamas attack on Israeli communities near the Gaza border, Major General (res.) Itai Veruv, then serving in the Israel Defense Forces (IDF), led international journalists through massacre sites including Kfar Aza and Be'eri kibbutzim, where over 1,200 Israeli civilians and soldiers were killed in total, with specific atrocities involving the deliberate targeting of non-combatants.[27][22] Veruv explicitly rejected framing the incursion as warfare, declaring, "It's not a war, it's not a battlefield. It's a massacre," emphasizing the systematic slaughter of families in their homes, including butchered children, women, and elderly residents found in burned-out structures and safe rooms.[28][27] In Be'eri, where Hamas militants killed over 100 residents, he noted that the attackers "came to kill, kill and kill," with bodies of entire families discovered inside houses, underscoring the terrorist intent over military objectives.[29][30] Veruv's on-site assessments highlighted the premeditated brutality, including decapitations and executions reported in the affected areas, which he described as unprecedented in his over 30 years of IDF service, visibly affecting even seasoned officers.[31][32] These statements positioned the event as a core terrorist operation by Hamas, aimed at civilian extermination rather than territorial conquest, aligning with forensic evidence of militants breaching fences on motorcycles and paragliders to infiltrate and target sleeping communities.[33][34] In broader national security contexts, Veruv has advocated for proactive IDF operations deep into enemy territory to neutralize threats, critiquing institutional reluctance to assume risks in such engagements as a vulnerability exploited by adversaries like Hamas.[16] This perspective, drawn from his prior commands, underscores a strategy of offensive depth action to prevent terrorist buildups and incursions, prioritizing empirical deterrence over defensive passivity in asymmetric conflicts.[16]

Engagements with Foreign Leaders

As Commandant of the IDF Military Colleges, including the Israel National Defense College, Major General Itai Veruv pursued international military education exchanges to strengthen defense cooperation. In April 2021, Veruv led a delegation from Israel's National Defence College on a visit to India from April 6 to 12, engaging with Indian defense officials to advance bilateral partnerships in military training and strategic studies.[35] In April 2023, Veruv conducted an official visit to Japan, meeting on April 17 with President Kawasaki of the National Institute for Defense Studies. The discussions emphasized the role of defense education in enhancing security collaboration between Israel and Japan, including exchanges on regional threats and institutional best practices.[36] He also visited Japan's Joint Staff College on April 24 to further these dialogues.[37] Veruv participated in multinational military exercises underscoring Israel's expanding ties with Arab states under the Abraham Accords. On October 26, 2022, he joined a joint paratrooper jump over Bahrain alongside forces from Bahrain, the United Arab Emirates, and the United States, demonstrating interoperability and commitment to regional security against shared adversaries.[38] Following his retirement from the IDF, Veruv's appointment as Chief Global Engagement Officer at ValorNet in June 2025 positioned him to extend these networks into private-sector wellness and resilience programs for military and security personnel, though specific foreign leader meetings in this capacity remain undocumented in public records.[4]
