Anuv Jain

Anuv Jain is an Indian singer songwriter and composer.[1][2] He is known for his simple chorded songs with lyrics that deal with love and heartbreak.[3][4] His music primarily contains an acoustic guitar or ukulele, unaccompanied by percussion or other instruments.[1][5][6] Considered among the most popular Indian indie artists,[7][8][9] "Baarishein" (2018), "Alag Aasmaan" (2020), "Gul" (2021), "Husn" (2023), "Jo Tum Mere Ho" (2024) and "Afsos" (2025) are some of his best known works.[10][11][12][13] Husn and Jo Tum Mere Ho charted at No.1 on Apple Music India and Spotify India charts for all genres.[14][15] Husn also ranked among the most streamed Indian songs of 2024 on the year-end-charts of all the music streaming platforms.[16][17] Anuv featured in the Forbes India 30 Under 30 List of 2023.[18][19]

Personal life

Anuv Jain was born in Ludhiana, Punjab, India, on 11 March 1995.[20] He grew up in Ludhiana alongside his twin sister, Anigha Jain, a makeup artist, and his mother, a homemaker.[21] He lost his father at the age of 17.[22] When he was 16 years old, Anuv began to write songs as a way to express his emotions.[23][24] On 14 February 2025,[25] Anuv married his long-time partner Hridi Narang, the founder of Guru Om Candles and Decor.[26][27]

Career

2018â€“2019: Breakthrough with "Baarishein"

He started writing at the age of 16 and "Meri Baaton Mein Tu" was the first song which he wrote.[28] He began uploading original work to his YouTube channel at the age of 16, with "Meri Baaton Mein Tu" as his first uploaded song in 2011, followed by a demo of "Baarishein" in 2012 at age 17. The official studio version of "Baarishein", released in 2018, was a sleeper hit. In an interview, he stated that he wrote the song when he was 17 for a girl with whom he was in love.[29] "Baarishein" became his first song to cross 100 and 200 million streams on Spotify. In an interview with Rolling Stone India, he said he takes inspiration from real life events and writes songs over a period of months.[30]

2020â€“2022: Continued acclaim with "Gul"

During the COVID-19 pandemic, he decided to pursue music as a full-time career.[1] During the lockdown, he released a number of songs among which "Alag Aasman" and "Gul" became hits.[31] He has performed live shows across the country[32][33] and at international venues including the UK, Australia, Dubai, Nepal and others.[34][35][36] Anuv performed as an opening act at Post Malone's India concert in 2022.[37] In an interview with Hindustan Times, he expressed his desire to sing for Ranbir Kapoor in his film.[38] He said in an interview that according to him, social media and DSPs are the key drivers for success in indie music genre in India.[39][40]

2023â€“present: "Husn" and further works

His 2023 release "Husn" became a smash hit and garnered more than 50 million streams on Spotify within the first month of its initial release.[41] It proceeded to cross 300 million streams across all platforms in 3 months (as of March 2024), becoming his biggest commercial hit.[42] It became his second 200 million streamed track on Spotify India (as of July 2024).[43] His 2024 release "Jo Tum Mere Ho" became his second consecutive song after "Husn" to top the music charts in India[14][15] and crossed 100 million streams on Spotify within 2 months of release (as of October 2024).[44] "Husn" became the most streamed indie song of 2024 in the year end charts of the music streaming platforms.[45] In April 2024, Anuv joined the Sounds Right organised by the United Nations.[46] An innovative global initiative to collect funds for the cause of nature conservation using nature's own sounds like ocean waves, rainstorms, wind and others, the initiative used the sound of Indian rains for 'Baarishein'.[47][48] In 2025, he released "Afsos" featuring AP Dhillon.[49] In August 2025, he released "Arz Kiya Hai" in collaboration with Lost Stories for Coke Studio Bharat.[50]

Musical influences

Jain has cited a variety of artists as influences on his music, including Ed Sheeran, A.R. Rahman, Mohit Chauhan, Linkin Park, Backstreet Boys, The Local Train, Billie Eilish, and Finneas O'Connell.[51][4]
