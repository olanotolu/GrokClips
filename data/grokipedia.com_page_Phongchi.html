Phongchi

Phongchi (フォンチー, Fonchī; born December 16, 1990) is a Japanese actress and former idol of Vietnamese descent. Often dubbed "Idoling No. 8", she was a member of the Japanese all-girl idol group Idoling!!! from 2006 to 2011. She was one of the original members. She also worked as a gravure idol, releasing several photobooks and DVDs in the late 2000s and early 2010s. Since graduating from the group, she has focused on acting, with roles in series like MIU404 (2020) and Tokyo MER (2023), as well as the 2025 film Tokyo Mer the Movie: Nankai Mission. She married a general civilian in 2022 and has one child.

Career

Phongchi was born in 1990 in Hiratsuka, Kanagawa to Vietnamese immigrant parents who came from Saigon, South Vietnam.[1] who came from Saigon, South Vietnam.[2] Phongchi participated in an audition, the SMA Teens Audition, held by Sony Music Artists in 2005, becoming the runner up.[3] She joined Idoling as an original member in 2006.[4] She began to be known as the "protégé" of Sayuri Kokusho, a seasoned actress known formerly as "Onyanko No. 8", and began appearing on numerous non-Idoling television shows in late 2007.[5] She released her first photobook titled Fon Fonchi in September 2008[6] and first DVD titled Cam On : Precious Time in October 2008.[7] Her second DVD A Letter From a Teen was shot in Okinawa[8] and released in September 2009.[9] Her second photobook Ritual Heart was shot in Bali and released in March 2010.[10] Her third DVD F : Angel's Wing was also shot in Bali[11] and released in March 2010,[12] and fourth DVD if : My Little Sister was released in November 2010.[13] In September 2010 she made her first Vietnam-related appearance at the annual Vietnam Festival event that took place at the Yoyogi Park in Shibuya, Tokyo.[14] She appeared in the event as its main host, along with several guests from Vietnam including famous Vietnamese artists such as Ho Quynh Huong and Nam Cuong.[15] Again, she worked as the host for the Vietnam Festival 2011 (September 17–18) where the guests again include Ho Quynh Huong.[16] Phongchi graduated from Idoling in December 2011 to start a career in acting, alongside "Idoling No. 7" Erika Yazawa who would later become a comedian.[17] She released her fifth DVD Kaleidoscope in January 2012.[18] It was her first DVD after leaving Idoling, where she dressed in costumes such as those inspired by Rihanna.[19]

Weight-loss obsession

Phongchi frequently discussed her weight and weight-loss plans during interviews in her Idoling!!! career (circa 2008-2011). She was a member of Idoling's Pocchari Dohmei ("Axis of Chubby"), a team consisting of the three heaviest members in the group,[20] along with Erika Yazawa and Erika Tonooka.[21] She aimed to lose weight down to 48 kg (106 lb)[22] at 163 cm (5 ft 4 in),[23] and she has stated that she considered herself overweight and was always dieting at the time. She said about it, "I can't announce my weight until I have a decent weight".[24] She has stated that she idolized the actress Risa Goto and especially the famous model Emi Suzuki, who were known for their slim figures with BMIs around 15.[23] She has revealed that she had a strong addiction to Caramel Frappuccino, a Starbucks beverage which often appears on her blog. A tall size contains about 247 kcal and she drank it twice a day. This can be one reason why she struggled with her weight, but she has stated that she could not live without it.

Personal life

Aside from her native Japanese, Phongchi is also fluent in Vietnamese. Her first DVD Cam On featured her conversations with native Vietnamese-speaking children.[25] On August 8, 2022, she announced her marriage to a non-celebrity man and pregnancy with their first child, to whom she gave birth (a daughter) on December 28, 2022.[26]
