Phone sex

Historically in the United States, such services utilized premium-rate numbers like 1-900 lines, which incurred additional charges beyond standard call rates, enabling revenue generation through extended conversation durations. Historical Development

The introduction of the telephone by Alexander Graham Bell in 1876 revolutionized personal communication, initially relying on manual switchboards operated primarily by women after 1878, when female hires replaced disruptive teenage boys for their calmer demeanors and reassuring voices. Psychological and Social Dimensions
