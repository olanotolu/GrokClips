Max Greenfield

Max Greenfield (born September 4, 1979)[1][2][3][4] is an American actor and author. He appeared in recurring roles in Veronica Mars and Ugly Betty. He co-starred as Schmidt in the Fox sitcom New Girl , for which he received nominations at the Primetime Emmy Awards, the Golden Globe Awards, and the Critics' Choice Television Awards. Since 2018, Greenfield has portrayed Dave Johnson in the CBS sitcom The Neighborhood. He voiced Roger in the Ice Age franchise, and numerous characters in the shows Bob's Burgers , Robot Chicken and BoJack Horseman. He has also written the children's books I Don't Want to Read This Book , I Don't Want to Read This Book Aloud , This Book is Not a Present and Good Night Thoughts.

Early life

Greenfield was born and raised in Dobbs Ferry, New York. He is Jewish and had a Saturday Night Live-themed Bar Mitzvah.[5] He graduated from Dobbs Ferry High School in 1998.[6] At DFHS, Greenfield played wide receiver for the football team.[7]

Career

Greenfield began pursuing an acting career in 1998 after he graduated from high school.[8] He briefly attended college at the University of Wisconsin, but dropped out after earning a 0.67 GPA his freshman year.[8] He landed guest appearances on dramas such as Boston Public, Gilmore Girls and The O.C.. Greenfield scored his first lead role on the short-lived series Modern Men as one of three bachelors who hire a life coach to help them understand women. Greenfield had more success in supporting roles on Veronica Mars (reprising the role in the film adaptation in 2014) and Greek. In 2007 he landed a recurring role on Ugly Betty. He made other guest-starring appearances on the remake of Melrose Place, the short-lived No Ordinary Family, and Castle. He created, produced, and starred in the comedy series The Gentlemen's League for the Audience Network in 2010, which followed the real-life fantasy football league he ran with actor Jerry Ferrara.[9] Greenfield had his feature film debut in the film Cross Bronx. Greenfield had a featured role in When Do We Eat?, as an Internet tycoon who, after losing his fortune, becomes a Hasidic Jew.[9] In 2011, Greenfield began playing the role of Schmidt in the sitcom New Girl. The role earned him nominations for both a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series and a Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor â€“ Series, Miniseries or Television Film.[10] In 2015, Greenfield starred in a series of McDonald's commercials promoting their Sirloin Third Pound Burgers.[11] Since 2018, Greenfield has portrayed the lead role of Dave Johnson in the CBS comedy The Neighborhood, taking over the role from Josh Lawson, who played the character in the pilot episode.[12] The series was renewed for an eighth and final season, which premiered on October 13, 2025.[13] On November 8, 2018, it was announced that Greenfield was set to reprise his role as Leo D'Amato in the 8-episode revival series of Veronica Mars.[14] In the same year, Greenfield was cast in the recurring role of the Denouement brothers on the third season of the Netflix comedy drama series A Series of Unfortunate Events.[15] Following New Girl, Greenfield appeared in films including What Men Want (2019) and Promising Young Woman (2020).[16][17] He has continued voice acting in series such as Bob's Burgers, Robot Chicken, and BoJack Horseman.[18] In 2023, he starred in the audio series The Flash: Escape the Midnight Circus.[19] Upcoming projects include the films First Time Female Director and Let's Have Kids! (announced 2022 and 2024).[20][21] In 2025, he is set to compete in season 3 of Celebrity Jeopardy!.[22]

Philanthropy

Greenfield has worked with the Young Storytellers Foundation on numerous occasions, including performing at their annual "Biggest Show" events in 2014, 2015, and 2023. He has stated that his own children have inspired him to encourage creativity and self-confidence in young people through the organization. [23] Greenfield has also supported Stand Up To Cancer and the Motion Picture & Television Fund. In 2017, he partnered with Michelob ULTRA to raise funds for GOOD+ Foundation's Fatherhood Program. [24] In 2025, Greenfield hosted the 12th Annual Wags & Walks Gala, helping raise over $1.5 million for the animal rescue organization. That August, he collaborated with Subaru Loves Learning and AdoptAClassroom.org to provide classroom supplies to teachers in high-needs schools. [25][26]

Personal life

Greenfield married Tess Sanchez, a former casting director and author, on August 9, 2008.[27] They have two children: a daughter, Lilly (born 2010), and a son, Ozzie (born 2015).[28][29] Sanchez published the essay collection We've Decided to Go in a Different Direction in 2025.[30]
