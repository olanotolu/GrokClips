Cryptids

Cryptids are animals or other beings whose present existence is disputed or unsubstantiated by science. The term "cryptid" was coined in 1983 by Canadian cryptozoologist John E. Wall.[1] Cryptozoology, the study of cryptids, is a pseudoscience claiming that such beings may exist somewhere in the wild; it has been widely critiqued by scientists.[3] The subculture is regularly criticized for reliance on anecdotal information[6] and because in the course of investigating animals that most scientists believe are unlikely to have existed, cryptozoologists do not follow the scientific method.[7] Many scientists have criticized the plausibility of cryptids due to lack of physical evidence,[8] likely misidentifications[9] and misinterpretation of stories from folklore. While biologists regularly identify new species following established scientific methodology, cryptozoologists focus on entities mentioned in the folklore record and rumor.
