New Orleans Gold

The New Orleans Gold (stylized as NOLA Gold) were an American professional rugby union team based in the New Orleans metropolitan area that competed in Major League Rugby (MLR). The team were founded in 2017.[4] Since January 2020, French rugby power ASM Clermont Auvergne has owned a minority stake in the team.[5] The team withdrew from competition at the end of the 2025 Major League Rugby season. [6]

Home field

Gold Mine on Airline

The team played at the Gold Mine on Airline, also known as the Shrine on Airline and was previously Zephyr Field, in Metairie, Louisiana, on the East Bank of the New Orleans metropolitan area.[7]

Joe Zimmerman Stadium

For the first two seasons of Major League Rugby in 2018 and 2019, the team played at Joe Zimmerman Stadium at Archbishop Shaw High School under the branding of Gold Stadium. The natural turf field with grandstand seating for up to 3,000 people is located on the West Bank at Marrero, Louisiana.[8]

Broadcasts

Home games were previously shown on Cox Sports Television. Ian McNulty and Geoffrey Ormsby were the on-air talent. In 2025, the Gulf Coast Sports & Entertainment Network became the official local broadcast network of the team, airing all 16 home and away games. National broadcasts were available on ESPN+, with delayed streams on The Rugby Network. [9] [10]

Sponsorship

For the 2022 season, NOLA Gold partnered with local Louisiana brewery Port Orleans Brewing Company to produce "GOLDen Ale", announced in December 2021. These 16-ounce cans were available for purchase in the brewery's tap room and on tap at the start of the season. It was also sold throughout the season in the team's home stadium, the Gold Mine on Airline (Shrine on Airline).[7] [11]
