John Patrick Shanley

John Patrick Shanley (born October 13, 1950) is an American playwright, theater director, and filmmaker. He won the 1988 Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay for the film Moonstruck.[2][3] His play, Doubt: A Parable, won the 2005 Pulitzer Prize for Drama and the 2005 Tony Award for Best Play; he wrote and directed the film adaptation, earning a nomination for the Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay.

Early life and education

Shanley was born into an Irish-American family in The Bronx, New York City. His mother worked as a telephone operator, and his father was a meat-packer. The neighborhood Shanley grew up in was considered very rough.[2] Shanley's academic career did not begin well, but ultimately he graduated from New York University with honors from the Steinhardt School of Culture, Education, and Human Development.[5] In his program bio for the Broadway production of Doubt: A Parable, he mentions that he was "thrown out of St. Helena's kindergarten, banned from St. Anthony's hot lunch program and expelled from Cardinal Spellman High School." He was heavily influenced by one of his first teachers, Sister Margaret McEntee, on whom he based the character of Sister James in his play, Doubt.[6] While at Cardinal Spellman High School, he saw two school productions that influenced him: The Miracle Worker and Cyrano de Bergerac. After being expelled from Cardinal Spellman, he attended Thomas More Preparatory School, a private Catholic-oriented school in Harrisville, New Hampshire, on a scholarship, where he completed his high school education.[2] After his freshman year at New York University, Shanley was put on academic probation. He then enlisted in the United States Marine Corps, serving in a stateside post during the Vietnam War.[2] Following his military service, he wrote a novel, then burned it, and returned to the university with the help of the G.I. Bill, and by supporting himself with a series of jobs: elevator operator, house painter, furniture mover, locksmith, bartender. He graduated from New York University as valedictorian in 1977,[5][8] with a degree in Educational Theatre, and is a member of the Ensemble Studio Theatre.[9]

Career

Shanley is the author of dozens of plays, which have been translated and performed around the world. He has often directed his own productions.[2] He has also written for film; his second film, Moonstruck (1987), stars Nicolas Cage and Cher, and won three Academy Awards, including one for his screenplay.[10] In 1990, Shanley directed his script of Joe Versus the Volcano. Shanley also wrote two songs for the movie: "Marooned Without You" and "The Cowboy Song."[11] He wrote the screenplay for the film Congo (1995), which was based on the Michael Crichton book.[12] His play Doubt: A Parable ran on Broadway from March 31, 2005, to July 2, 2006, and won four 2005 Tony Awards (including Best Play),[13] the Drama Desk Award (including Outstanding Play)[14] and the Pulitzer Prize for Drama.[15] Shanley wrote and directed the 2008 film version, which starred Meryl Streep, Philip Seymour Hoffman, Amy Adams and Viola Davis.[16] The screenplay was nominated for an Academy Award, and the Writers Guild of America Award for Best Adapted Screenplay.[17] Doubt: A Parable, is featured in The Fourth Wall, a book of photographs by Amy Arbus for which Shanley also wrote the foreword. In 2012, Shanley wrote the libretto for an opera version of Doubt: A Parable, which premiered at the Minnesota Opera in January 2013, with music by Douglas J. Cuomo. Until then, his experience with opera was not extensive; he had attended a few performances and had listened to recordings. As he worked on the libretto, using many lines that come directly from the play, he describes that his enthusiasm for the form grew.[18] Also in 2012, his play Storefront Church ran Off-Broadway in a production by the Atlantic Theater Company. The play concerns Bronx residents "whose lives become tangled in unexpected ways when a mortgage goes sour".[19] Storefront Church was also put up by San Francisco Playhouse in San Francisco in December 2013 where it was very well received.[20] His play, Outside Mullingar, opened on Broadway at the Samuel J. Friedman Theatre, produced by the Manhattan Theatre Club, on January 3, 2014 (in previews) and officially on January 23, 2014. Personal life

Shanley resides in New York City. He has been married and divorced twice; with his second wife, Jayne Haynes, he adopted two sons, both born in 1992.[27]

Accolades and honors

In 2004 Shanley was inducted into the Bronx Walk of Fame.[54] In 2007, Shanley was elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences. In 2009, he received the Ian McLellan Hunter Lifetime Achievement Award from the Writers Guild of America East.[55][56]
