Let's Sing and Dance

Let's Sing and Dance, formerly known as Let's Dance, is a British television programme shown on BBC One, featuring celebrities performing famous dance routines to raise money for the charities Comic Relief and Sport Relief.[2] The programme was presented by Steve Jones and Alex Jones, who replaced previous host Claudia Winkleman in 2011. In the first series, Anton du Beke was a regular judge, appearing alongside two guest panellists, but in the second series onwards, three guest judges were on the panel each week. The first series aired between 21 February and 14 March 2009 and was won by Robert Webb. The second series, in aid of Sport Relief, aired from 20 February 2010 until 13 March and was won by Rufus Hound. The third series aired between 19 February 2011 and 12 March in aid of Comic Relief, and was won by James Thornton and Charlie Baker. A fourth series aired from 18 February until 17 March 2012 in aid of Sport Relief, and was won by Rowland Rivron.[3] The fifth ran from 16 February 2013 until 9 March 2013, and was won by Antony Cotton.[4] The series' finale was the last Saturday night show to be broadcast live from BBC Television Centre. On 6 October 2016, the BBC announced that the show would return for Red Nose Day 2017 as Let's Sing and Dance for Comic Relief.[5] On 28 October 2016, it was announced that Mel Giedroyc and Sue Perkins would host the show.[6] The sixth series aired from 4 March to 25 March 2017 in aid of Comic Relief, and was won by The Chasers.[7]

Format

The show generally consisted of a series of heats followed by a final. In most series, there were three heats, each featuring six celebrity acts (solos, duos, or groups) recreating famous dance routines from films, pop videos, or performances. Viewers voted by phone for their favourite, with proceeds benefiting Comic Relief or Sport Relief. The act with the most votes advanced directly to the final, while a panel of three judges selected a second act, typically a wildcard from the remaining performers or top vote-getters, to also advance. This resulted in six acts competing in the final.[2] Series 4 (2012) deviated from this structure, featuring four heats and eight finalists.[9] The first series was announced by the BBC in January 2009 as part of the events surrounding Red Nose Day 2009 and hosted by Steve Jones and Claudia Winkleman. In 2011, Winkleman was replaced by The One Show host Alex Jones. The 2017 revival, titled Let's Sing and Dance for Comic Relief and hosted by Mel Giedroyc and Sue Perkins, modified the format to have celebrities sing and dance to iconic pop tracks, while retaining the three-heat structure leading to six finalists.[10]
