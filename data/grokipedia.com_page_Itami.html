Itami

Itami-shi is a city located in Hyōgo Prefecture, Japan. As of 1 October 2025, the city had an estimated population of 194,966 in 85,412 households and a population density of 7,800/km² (20,000/sq mi).[1] The total area of the city is 25.00 square kilometres (9.65 sq mi).

Geography

Itami is located in south-eastern Hyōgo Prefecture, with the Ina River to the east and the Muko River to the west. The city area is a flat, undulating gentle terrain throughout. JR West Japan JR Takarazuka Line (also known as the Fukuchiyama Line) and Hankyū Itami Line traverse north and south. It is roughly 10 km (6.2 mi) from Osaka and contacts Kawanishi in the north, Takarazuka in the northwest, Nishinomiya and Amagasaki in the southwest, and Ikeda and Toyonaka in the east. In Hyōgo prefecture, the population density is the second highest following Amagasaki in the south (as of 2022).

Climate

Itami has a Humid subtropical climate (Köppen Cfa) characterized by warm summers and cool winters with light snowfall. The average annual temperature in Itami is 15.0 °C. The average annual rainfall is 1475 mm with September as the wettest month. The temperatures are highest on average in August, at around 26.9 °C, and lowest in January, at around 3.7 °C.[4]

History

The area of modern Itami is part of ancient Settsu Province. The hilly area is called the Itami plateau, located between the Ina River and the Muko River, and has been continuously inhabited since the Jomon period. Stone tools, Jomon pottery and Yayoi pottery and settlement traces have been found in several areas within the city limits, and rice cultivation was done in the area from ancient times. The area also has many kofun burial mounds, including the Gogadzuka Kofun (御願塚古墳)[6] and Kashiwagi Kofun , which were built during the Kofun period. In the northern part of the city, the Itami temple ruins date from the Hakuhō period (late 7th century). The center of Itami became a wealthy castle town by the middle of Sengoku period with the construction of Arioka Castle, held by Araki Murashige who ruled under Oda Nobunaga. After the uprising and defeat of Araki, the castle was torn down. During the Edo Period, in 1661 the town was taken over by the Konoe family of court nobility, and the sake brewing industry prospered under its protection. Following the Meiji restoration, the holdings of the Konoe family were incorporated into Hyōgo Prefecture. The town of Itami was established 1 April 1889 with the creation of the modern municipalities system. Itami was raised to city status on 10 November 1940. Great portions of the city were damaged in the Great Hanshin–Awaji earthquake of 1995, but were quickly rebuilt.

Government

Itami has a mayor-council form of government with a directly elected mayor and a unicameral city council of 28 members. Itami contributes three members to the Hyōgo Prefectural Assembly. In terms of national politics, the city is in the Hyōgo 6th districts of the lower house of the Diet of Japan.

Economy

The city has a mixed economy of commerce, industry and is also a commuter town for Osaka.

Industry

The history of sake brewing in Itami dates to around 1600, during the early Edo period. In the Itami area, a method of brewing clear and colorless Japanese rice wine, now known as sake, was developed. In the Edo period, the sake brewed in Itami was popular. Sake remains a significant contributor to the local economy.

Education in Itami

Public Schools

As of May 2024, Itami has 17 public elementary schools and eight public middle schools operated by the city government, and four public high schools operated by the Hyōgo Prefectural Board of Education and one operated by the city government. In addition, the city operates one special education school for students with disabilities and the prefecture operates one special education school for students with disabilities.

Other Schools

There is a North Korean school in Itami: Itami Korean Elementary School (伊丹朝鮮初級学校).

Transportation

Airports

Most of Osaka International Airport is located in Itami (hence its common name "Itami Airport"); it is Osaka's primary domestic airport, after all international flights and some domestic flights shifted to Kansai International Airport in 1994. Despite the airport's association with Itami, the terminal complex spans Itami, the neighboring cities of Toyonaka and Ikeda, and the Itami city center is connected to the airport only by a long tunnel that passes beneath the runway and tarmac.

Culture and regular events

  • Itami Machinakanaka Bar is an eating and drinking event in the city center of Itami. It is held twice a year, in spring and autumn, and musicians can often be found performing in the streets during the event.
  • Nakumushi to Go-chō (鳴く虫と郷町) is an annual autumn event held every September in Itami's city center, celebrating insect sounds ("insect hearing") through poetry such as haiku and tanka, music concerts, study sessions, reading, workshops, and more, over the course of about 10 days.
