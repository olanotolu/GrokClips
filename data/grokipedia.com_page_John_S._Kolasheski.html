John S. Kolasheski


John S. Kolasheski is a retired United States Army lieutenant general who commanded V Corps from August 4, 2020, until his retirement in April 2024, overseeing the reactivation of the unit and the establishment of its dual headquarters in Fort Knox, Kentucky, and Poznań, Poland.[1] Commissioned as an armor officer, Kolasheski held successive commands including the 3rd Squadron, 1st Cavalry Regiment of the 3rd Infantry Division; the 2nd Brigade Combat Team, 4th Infantry Division; and the 1st Infantry Division at Fort Riley, Kansas, while also serving as deputy chief of staff at U.S. Army Forces Command.[1] His nearly 35-year career included deployments in support of Operation Enduring Freedom in Afghanistan and Operation Iraqi Freedom, earning recognition through awards such as the Distinguished Service Medal upon retirement.[1]

Early Life and Education

Family Background and Upbringing

John S. Kolasheski was born into a distinguished U.S. Army family. His father served more than 25 years in the Army, including a command of an armor battalion in Germany.[2] Due to his father's career, Kolasheski experienced an upbringing characterized by frequent relocations among Army installations worldwide, immersing him early in military culture and traditions.[2] This nomadic lifestyle across bases fostered familiarity with diverse operational environments and the demands of Army service from a young age.

Academic and Professional Training

Kolasheski earned a Bachelor of Science degree in business administration from Bucknell University in Lewisburg, Pennsylvania, graduating in 1989.[3][4] Through the university's Reserve Officers' Training Corps program, he received his commission as a second lieutenant in the U.S. Army's Armor Branch upon completion of his undergraduate studies.[4] He later pursued graduate education at the University of Central Florida, obtaining two Master of Science degrees: one in engineering management and another in modeling, simulation, and training.[3] Kolasheski's professional military education included advanced coursework at the United States Army War College, the NATO Defense College—where he studied military and strategic leadership—and the Joint Special Operations University, emphasizing joint operations planning and strategy.[5] These programs supported his progression through senior Army leadership roles, aligning with standard requirements for flag officers in operational commands.[5]

Military Career

Commissioning and Initial Service

Kolasheski received his commission as an armor officer in 1989 through the Army Reserve Officers' Training Corps program upon earning a Bachelor of Science degree in management from Bucknell University.[4][5] Following commissioning, he completed the Armor Officer Basic Course, then held at Fort Knox, Kentucky.[4] His first assignment was as a tank platoon leader, mortar platoon leader, and company executive officer in 3rd Battalion, 68th Armored Regiment, 4th Infantry Division, stationed at Fort Carson, Colorado.[6] In these roles, Kolasheski gained foundational experience in armored unit tactics, leadership, and operations within a mechanized infantry division.[6] Subsequent early assignments included command of Company D, 3rd Battalion, 8th Cavalry Regiment, 1st Cavalry Division, followed by command of the battalion's Headquarters Company; these duties encompassed participation in Operation Desert Strike, a series of U.S. cruise missile strikes against Iraqi targets, while forward-deployed to Kuwait from 1995 to 1997.[6] He later served as aide-de-camp to the commanding general of the 1st Cavalry Division at Fort Hood, Texas, providing direct staff support to senior leadership.[6] Kolasheski then took on the role of executive officer for 1st Squadron, 4th Cavalry Regiment, 1st Infantry Division, based in Schweinfurt, Germany, where he managed squadron operations and staff functions during the post-Cold War drawdown period.[6]

Major Deployments and Operational Commands

Kolasheski's major deployments began with multiple rotations in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom, where he held successive command positions in armored cavalry units. During his first notable operational command, he led the 3rd Squadron, 1st Cavalry Regiment, a component of the 3rd Infantry Division's 3rd Heavy Brigade Combat Team, deploying to Iraq in 2007 to conduct operations east of Baghdad, focusing on security stabilization and counterinsurgency efforts amid ongoing insurgent activity.[7][8] In this role, his unit reported tangible improvements in local security through targeted patrols and engagements, though challenges from improvised explosive devices and militia threats persisted.[7] Subsequently, as commander of the 2nd Brigade Combat Team, 4th Infantry Division, Kolasheski directed operations within Multi-National Division–Baghdad, overseeing brigade-level maneuvers that included urban security operations and force protection in the Iraqi capital.[9] This deployment, part of his third tour in Iraq, involved coordinating with Iraqi security forces to transition responsibilities amid the drawdown of U.S. combat brigades.[10] Kolasheski's earlier service in Iraq from 2003 to 2005 included staff roles supporting initial invasion and stabilization phases, contributing to his operational experience in armored warfare.[11] In Operation Enduring Freedom, Kolasheski served as deputy chief of staff in Kabul, Afghanistan, managing operational planning and logistics for coalition forces during a period of intensified Taliban resurgence.[8] This non-command deployment emphasized staff coordination for sustainment and mission command in a high-altitude, asymmetric threat environment. Later in his career, as commanding general of V Corps from August 2020 to April 2024, Kolasheski oversaw the corps' forward deployment of its main headquarters to Europe in March 2022, establishing operational command and control for U.S. Army forces amid heightened tensions following Russia's invasion of Ukraine.[12] Under his leadership, V Corps achieved full operational capability, integrating multinational exercises and deterrence missions across the European theater, including partnerships in Poland.[2] This marked a strategic shift to forward-postured corps operations, building on Cold War-era precedents while adapting to hybrid threats.[12]

Senior Command and Staff Positions

Kolasheski served as Deputy Chief of Staff for Operations, Plans, and Training (G-3/5/7) at U.S. Army Forces Command, Fort Bragg, North Carolina, prior to April 2018. In this staff position, he managed key aspects of operational planning, strategy, and training for the Army's primary force generation command. In April 2018, Kolasheski was assigned as Commanding General of the 1st Infantry Division and Fort Riley, Kansas, succeeding another major general. He led the division, known as the "Big Red One," through various training exercises and readiness operations until July 2020.[3] During this tenure, the division maintained its focus on armored and mechanized infantry capabilities.[3] Kolasheski was promoted to lieutenant general and assumed duties as Commanding General of V Corps on August 4, 2020, also serving concurrently as Deputy Commanding General for Europe under U.S. Army Europe and Africa.[11] He oversaw the reactivation and forward deployment of V Corps headquarters to Poland, enhancing U.S. Army presence and interoperability with NATO allies amid heightened European security concerns following Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine.[13] [14] V Corps, under his command, coordinated multinational exercises and operational support across the European theater until the change of command on April 8, 2024.[15] Kolasheski retired from active duty on April 26, 2024, after 35 years of service.[1]

Retirement and Post-Military Engagements

Military Retirement

Lt. Gen. John S. Kolasheski retired from the U.S. Army on April 26, 2024, following a retirement ceremony hosted by V Corps at its headquarters in Fort Knox, Kentucky.[1][16] The event marked the end of his tenure as commanding general of V Corps, a position he held from July 2020 until his relinquishment of command earlier that month.[1] Kolasheski's retirement concluded over 34 years of active duty service, spanning command and staff roles across multiple theaters, including deployments to Iraq and Korea.[3][17] During the ceremony, he delivered remarks reflecting on his career, emphasizing the Army's evolution and the importance of leadership in operational environments.[16][18] V Corps leadership praised his contributions to rebuilding the corps as a forward-deployable headquarters focused on European deterrence.[1]

Civilian Advisory Roles

Following his retirement from the U.S. Army on April 26, 2024, after nearly 35 years of service, Kolasheski transitioned to civilian advisory positions leveraging his expertise in national security, operational command, and international engagements.[1] He currently serves as a Senior Mentor for NATO, where he provides strategic guidance on military training, exercises, and alliance operations in Europe.[19] In July 2024, Kolasheski joined TechWerks as Executive Vice President of Global Operations, a role that incorporates advisory elements on defense-related technology integration and international contracting for U.S. government clients, drawing on his prior leadership of multinational forces.[14] On October 31, 2024, he was appointed Senior Advisor at DGA Group, an affiliate of Albright Stonebridge Group focused on global strategy and government relations, to counsel clients on defense policy, Eastern European security dynamics, and responses to geopolitical threats such as Russia's invasion of Ukraine.[19] In this capacity, he offers insights from commanding over 40,000 personnel across theaters including Iraq, Afghanistan, and Europe. Kolasheski also advises technology and innovation firms on aligning commercial solutions with military requirements and international partnerships.[19]
