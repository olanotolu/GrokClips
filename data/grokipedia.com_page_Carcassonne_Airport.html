Carcassonne Airport

Carcassonne Airport (French: Aéroport de Carcassonne , IATA: CCF , ICAO: LFMK) serves Carcassonne and southern Languedoc and is in the Aude department of the Occitanie region in France. It is 3 km (2 nautical miles) west of the city and is also known as Salvaza Airport ,[1] Carcassonne Salvaza Airport[2] or Carcassonne Airport in Pays Cathare (Aéroport de Carcassonne en Pays Cathare).[3] The airport handles commercial national and international flights as well as private, non-regular air traffic. Sources: French AIP,[2] UAF,[3] DAFIF[1][4]

History

In 1993, according to the Official Airline Guide (OAG) Carcassonne was served by Aigle Azur airlines with nonstop service to Paris Orly Airport operated with Saab 340 regional turboprop airliners. Ryanair began services to the airport in June 1998, inaugurating its route from London Stansted Airport and establishing the airport as a low-cost carrier hub. [6] During the late 1990s, the airport was being served with budget flights to and from European airports, and by 2006 had regular flight connections with Dublin, London Stansted, Liverpool, East Midlands and Charleroi. In 2011, the airport served 368,000 passengers. Atlas Atlantique Airlines operated from the airport briefly to Oran in Algeria from 2016 to 2017; however, the airline ceased operations in late November 2017. [7] As of October 2025, Ryanair is the only airline operating scheduled passenger service into the airport. [8]

Facilities

The airport sits at an elevation of 434 feet (132 m) above mean sea level. It has one paved runway designated, which measures 2,050 by 45 metres (6,726 ft × 148 ft). It also has a parallel unpaved runway with a grass surface measuring 792 by 30 metres (2,598 ft × 98 ft).[2] It can cater to aircraft operating under VFR. Inside the terminal building there are two departure gates. A small shop is land-side in the terminal. A campus of the École nationale de l'aviation civile is also at the airport.

Ground Transportation

A shuttle bus service, operated by RTCA Navette, connects the airport to the center of Carcassonne, including the medieval Cité, Square Gambetta, Carcassonne Station, and Place Davilla. It departs 45 minutes after each flight arrival and costs 6€ per person (free for children under 5), valid for the full day.[12][13] Taxis are available directly outside the terminal, providing service to the city center and surrounding areas. The fare to Carcassonne center is approximately 15-20€.[12] Several car rental companies, including Hertz, Avis, and Europcar, operate at the airport, located in the arrivals hall.[12]
