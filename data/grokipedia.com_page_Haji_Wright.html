Haji Wright

Haji Amir Wright (born March 27, 1998) is an American professional soccer player who plays as a forward or winger for EFL Championship club Coventry City and the United States national team. After making his professional debut with the New York Cosmos in 2015, he has spent most of his career in Europe, with Schalke 04 in the German Bundesliga, VVV-Venlo in the Dutch Eredivisie, SønderjyskE in the Danish Superliga and Antalyaspor in the Turkish Süper Lig. Wright made his senior debut for the United States national team in 2022 and played at the 2022 FIFA World Cup.

Youth soccer

Wright began his youth career with the LA Galaxy academy in 2012.[2] During his time there, he trained with the first team in April 2014.[3] earned the Golden Boot at the 2013 Nike International Friendlies by leading the U.S. U-17 team to a 4–1 win over Brazil with two goals.[4] and scored two goals to help win the 2014 Aegean Cup.[5] Known for his clinical finishing, aerial prowess, and impressive speed,[6] Wright was dubbed by many media outlets as one of the top prospects in the United States youth system.[7][6] His success with the under-17 national team prompted him to train with Schalke 04's academy.[8]

Club Career

New York Cosmos

In March 2015, Wright officially signed with NASL club New York Cosmos for an undisclosed fee and contract length.[9] He made his professional debut on May 27 in the third round of the U.S. Open Cup, as a 79th-minute substitute for Sebastián Guenzatti in a 3–0 home win over the Jersey Express, and then played three league games for a total of 80 minutes, starting with the game versus Indy Eleven on July 26.[10] Wright's contract expired following the season, and he did not re-sign.[11]

Schalke 04

In April 2016, Wright signed with Schalke 04.[12] In his debut with the Schalke under-19 team on April 16, Wright scored a brace against Borussia Mönchengladbach U19. Wright was promoted to the Schalke first team in May 2017.[13] He was named on the bench against FC Ingolstadt 04 on May 20, 2017.[14] In July 2018, Wright underwent a trial period with 2. Bundesliga club Union Berlin.[15] On November 24, 2018, Wright made his debut for Schalke first team when he replaced Steven Skrzybski in the 88th minute of the 5–2 win against 1. FC Nürnberg.[16] He played seven games for the team from Gelsenkirchen, all in the league, and scored a consolation goal in a 2–1 home loss to Bayer Leverkusen on December 19; the goal was assisted by compatriot Weston McKennie.[17]

VVV-Venlo

In July 2019, Wright joined VVV-Venlo on a free transfer.[18] He played 22 Eredivisie games without scoring, but scored in the first round of the KNVB Cup on October 30; coming on in the last minute, he tied the game at 2–2 at fourth-tier RKSV Groene Ster, but missed the decisive attempt in a penalty shootout defeat.[19]

SønderjyskE

In August 2020, Wright joined SønderjyskE on a free transfer.[20] He got off to a strong start in the league, with six goals in eight appearances by early November, and his performances led to him being named as the Danish Superliga Player of the Month for October 2020.[21] In July 2021, Wright wanted to move on from SønderjyskE and failed to report back for pre-season training.[22] On July 21, Turkish club Antalyaspor confirmed that they had signed Wright on loan from SønderjyskE with an option to buy.[23] However, later on the same day, SønderjyskE went out and denied that the deal had gone through because they had not yet received any payment.[24] However, on July 28, SønderjyskE confirmed that he had been loaned out to Antalyaspor for the 2021–22 season and also signed a new deal with Sønderjyske until June 2024.[25]

Antalyaspor

Wright opened his account for Antalyaspor in his third game on August 28, 2021, in which he scored a hat-trick in a 3–2 home win over Çaykur Rizespor, concluding with a penalty kick in the tenth minute of added time.[26] He scored 14 goals in 31 games in the 2021–22 Süper Lig, including eight over seven consecutive games, and nine in the last ten games. On July 19, 2022, Wright signed for Antalyaspor permanently on a three-year deal.[27] He scored 16 goals in all competitions for the club as they finished 13th in the Süper Lig.[28]

Coventry City

Wright joined EFL Championship club Coventry City on August 4, 2023, on a four-year contract for a club-record transfer fee of €9 million.[29] On August 12, 2023, he scored his first goal for the club on his home debut in a 3–0 win against Middlesbrough.[30] On March 16, 2024, Wright converted a 100th minute stoppage time goal for Coventry in the quarter-finals of the 2023–24 FA Cup against Wolverhampton Wanderers, helping the club progress beyond the round for the first time since Coventry's 1986–87 FA Cup victory.[31] In the semi-final against Manchester United at Wembley Wright scored a 95th minute equalizing penalty to complete a comeback from 3-0 down and take the game to extra time. He then provided an assist for a 121st minute goal by Victor Torp, but this was disallowed by VAR for offside and Coventry were eliminated on penalties.[32] He finished the 2023-24 season with 19 goals in all competitions, including 16 in the Championship.[33] Wright scored his first goals of the 2024-25 season against Oxford United in a 3-2 win on August 16, including a 96th minute winner after an error by Will Vaulks.[34] During a 2-2 draw against Sunderland on November 9, Wright picked up an ankle ligament injury, which kept him out of action for 4 months.[35] On March 15, Wright scored a hat-trick against Sunderland in a 3-0 victory for Coventry, these were his first goals since returning from injury, and it was also his first hat trick for the club.[36] Wright ended the 2024–25 season with 12 goals in 27 league appearances, helping Coventry finish fifth and qualify for the playoffs, although they were eliminated without promotion.34 In June 2025, Wright sustained an Achilles injury during a United States national team training camp, ruling him out of the 2025 CONCACAF Gold Cup.35 He recovered in time for the 2025–26 season and, as of October 2025, has scored 8 goals in 10 EFL Championship matches, co-leading the league scoring charts.36

International career

Wright is of Liberian and Ghanaian descent. He has represented the United States at all levels.[37] In May 2022, Wright was called into the United States senior camp by Gregg Berhalter for friendlies during the June 2022 international window.[38] Wright scored his first international goal on his senior debut for the United States on June 1, 2022, in a 3–0 friendly victory against Morocco.[39] In November 2022, Wright was named to the squad for the 2022 FIFA World Cup.[40] He scored for the United States in their 3–1 loss to the Netherlands in the round of 16.[41] On March 17, 2024, Wright was called up to the national team as an injury replacement for Josh Sargent to compete in the 2024 CONCACAF Nations League Finals.[42] On March 21, he scored an extra-time brace against Jamaica in a 3–1 win.[43] Three days later, on March 24, Wright started in the final against Mexico, helping the United States secure a 2–0 victory to win the tournament for the third consecutive time.[44] In the 2025 CONCACAF Gold Cup, Wright appeared as a substitute in the group stage matches, scoring his fifth international goal in a 5–0 win over Trinidad and Tobago on June 15, 2025.[45] He suffered an Achilles injury on June 21 and was ruled out for the remainder of the tournament.[46] The United States reached the final but lost, finishing as runners-up. Wright returned to the national team in October 2025, starting and scoring a brace—his sixth and seventh international goals—in a 2–1 friendly victory over Australia on October 14.[47]

Personal life

Haji's brother Hanif played on the under-19 team of Bonner SC in 2017.[48] Haji's cousins are also active in athletics: Joseph Putu played cornerback for the Florida Gators, his second cousin, Isaac Nana Addai, played for the Liberia national under-23 team as well as Boston City FC, and his third cousin, Joseph Addai, won Super Bowl XLI in 2007 with the Indianapolis Colts.[48][49] Wright married Jana in June 2025; they have one child.[50][51]
