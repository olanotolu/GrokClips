Max McCormick

Maxwell McCormick (born May 1, 1992) is an American professional ice hockey forward who serves as captain of the Coachella Valley Firebirds of the American Hockey League (AHL) while under contract to the Seattle Kraken of the National Hockey League (NHL), though sidelined for the 2025–26 season due to hip surgery.[1] McCormick re-signed with the Kraken on a two-year, two-way contract in June 2024.[2] McCormick was selected 171st overall in the 2011 NHL Entry Draft by the Ottawa Senators. McCormick grew up in the Green Bay, Wisconsin area where he played junior hockey for Notre Dame Academy (Green Bay, Wisconsin) before moving to the Sioux City Musketeers of the United States Hockey League. He was selected by the Ottawa Senators in the sixth round of the 2011 NHL Entry Draft. He then committed to a collegiate career attending Ohio State University for three years. On May 28, 2014, he was signed to a two-year entry-level contract with the Senators.[3] McCormick turned professional in 2014 with the Binghamton Senators of the American Hockey League (AHL). McCormick made his NHL debut on October 25, 2015 with Ottawa in a 4–1 defeat to the Arizona Coyotes.[4] On February 6, 2019, McCormick was traded to the Colorado Avalanche in exchange for J.C. Beaudin. He was immediately assigned to the team's AHL affiliate, the Colorado Eagles.[5] McCormick played out the remainder of the 2018–19 AHL season with the Eagles, adding 8 points in 25 regular season games and making two post-season appearances in the 2019 Calder Cup playoffs. McCormick then signed a one-year, two-way contract with the Carolina Hurricanes on July 25, 2019.[6] In the following 2019–20 AHL season, upon returning from injury, McCormick was assigned to the Charlotte Checkers, Carolina's AHL affiliate. McCormick collected 16 goals and 35 points in 56 games before the remainder of the season was cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic. On October 28, 2020, McCormick signed a one-year, two-way contract extension.[7] During the 2020–21 NHL season, he appeared in 12 games for the Hurricanes, recording 3 points (2 goals and 1 assist), while also playing 1 game for the Chicago Wolves of the AHL.[8] As an unrestricted free agent in the off-season, McCormick signed a one-year, two-way contract with the Seattle Kraken on September 29, 2021.[9] In the 2021–22 season, he skated in 10 NHL games for Seattle without recording a point. McCormick spent most of the year with the Kraken's AHL affiliate, the Charlotte Checkers, where he posted 29 points (13 goals and 16 assists) in 46 regular season games and added 7 points in 7 playoff games. On May 9, 2022, McCormick signed a two-year, two-way contract extension with the Kraken.[10] With the launch of the Kraken's new AHL affiliate, the Coachella Valley Firebirds, ahead of the 2022–23 AHL season, McCormick was named the team's inaugural captain on October 16, 2022.[11] He recorded 67 points (28 goals and 39 assists) in 71 regular season games and led the Firebirds with 27 points (14 goals and 13 assists) in 26 playoff games as the team advanced to the Calder Cup Finals, where they lost to the Hershey Bears in 12 games. In the 2023–24 AHL season, McCormick split time between Seattle (1 NHL game) and Coachella Valley, where he tallied 60 points (32 goals and 28 assists) in 68 regular season games and 11 points (8 goals and 3 assists) in 18 playoff games. The Firebirds were eliminated in the Western Conference Finals by the Milwaukee Admirals. McCormick re-signed with the Kraken on a two-year, two-way contract on June 30, 2024.[2] During the 2024–25 AHL season, McCormick recorded 13 points (7 goals and 6 assists) in 19 games for the Firebirds before suffering a lower-body injury. On September 30, 2025, the Firebirds announced that McCormick would undergo hip surgery and miss the entire 2025–26 AHL season.[1]
