John Ruddy

John Thomas Gordon Ruddy (born 24 October 1986) is an English professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for Newcastle United. Ruddy began his career at Cambridge United, before signing for Everton as a 19-year-old. He spent five years with Everton, but made just one appearance, while playing on loan at nine different clubs. Ruddy was signed by Paul Lambert to play for Norwich City in 2010 and, in his first season, was part of the team that finished second in the Championship, gaining promotion to the Premier League. The following year, Norwich finished twelfth in the Premier League, and Ruddy was selected by new England manager Roy Hodgson for the squad for Euro 2012. However, he was ruled out after breaking a finger during training. He made his debut in a 2â€“1 win against Italy in August 2012. He suffered a long-term thigh injury in November 2012, but returned to help Norwich to an 11th-place finish at the end of the 2012â€“13 Premier League season. After 243 appearances for Norwich, he moved to Wolverhampton Wanderers in 2017. After one season as first choice as Wolves gained promotion to the Premier League, he spent four years as backup, and left the club at the end of the 2021â€“22 season. He then joined Birmingham City, where he spent two years as first-choice goalkeeper before signing for Newcastle United in 2024, where he extended his contract in July 2025.[1]

Personal life

Ruddy was born in St Ives, Cambridgeshire,[2] where he attended St Ivo School.[3] He grew up as a fan of Manchester United.[4] Ruddy married his wife Laura on 2 June 2012. Prior to the Euro 2012 tournament, he jokingly cited breaking his finger as the reason he was able to have his wedding on that date.[5] They have two children.[6]

Club career

Cambridge United

Ruddy began his football career at Cambridge United, where his team-mates gave him the nickname "The Iceman".[7] He made his professional debut in the final match of the 2003â€“04 season at Leyton Orient's Brisbane Road stadium on 8 May 2004, keeping a clean sheet and saving a penalty.[2] Ruddy earned his place as Cambridge's first-choice keeper in the 2004â€“05 season, making 38 appearances in a season that saw the U's relegated from League Two after finishing bottom with 30 points.[8] This was despite Ruddy's efforts, which included keeping nine clean sheets and conceding just 45 goals.[9]

Everton

Ruddy had two trials at Manchester United in November 2004,[10] before moving to Everton in the summer of 2005 for a fee of around Â£250,000 that would rise depending on appearances.[11] He was called into the England Under-19 squad in 2005 after being scouted by England goalkeeping coach Ray Clemence and was touted by his manager David Moyes as a future England goalkeeper. In September 2005, Ruddy signed for Walsall on loan;[12] he made five appearances in League One before returning to Everton.[13] Loan spells with Rushden & Diamonds and Chester City followed.[14] Ruddy made his first and only appearance for Everton in February 2006 in unusual circumstances. Nine minutes into the match against Blackburn Rovers, Iain Turner was sent off for handling outside the area. Since Turner was substituting for injured first- and second-choice keepers Nigel Martyn and Richard Wright, it meant that Ruddy was the only keeper left who could play. He kept a clean sheet in a 1â€“0 victory.[15] In September 2006, Ruddy went on loan again. A one-month deal was agreed with Stockport County,[14] which was twice extended after some impressive performances.[16][17] He then agreed a one-month loan with Wrexham in February 2007,[18] but failed to establish a place in the team during his time at the Racecourse Ground and manager Brian Carey decided against extending the loan beyond the initial month.[19] Ruddy spent a week's emergency loan at League One club Bristol City in April,[20] and helped them move closer to promotion, by playing one match â€“ a 3â€“1 win over Carlisle United. He later joked that he would ask if he were eligible for a runners-up medal as City finished second and won promotion. In January 2009, he went out on loan until the end of the season to Crewe Alexandra.[21]

Motherwell (loan)

On 24 July 2009, Ruddy joined Scottish Premier League club Motherwell on a six-month loan, to vie with Michael Fraser and Sebastian Kosiorowski for a place in goal.[22] Ruddy kept clean sheets during goalless draws at home to Rangers on 12 September and away to Celtic on 17 October.[23] He also saved a Barry Robson penalty in a 3â€“2 loss to Celtic on 12 December.[24] When the original loan expired in January 2010, the Fir Park club decided that they wanted to keep Ruddy and fellow Everton loanee Lukas Jutkiewicz for longer; an initial one-month extension was agreed, followed on 27 January by confirmation that both would stay at Motherwell for the rest of the season.[25][26] Ruddy finished the Scottish Premier League season with 15 clean sheets from 34 appearances.

Norwich City

On 5 July 2010, Ruddy signed for Championship club Norwich City for an undisclosed fee, which Ruddy revealed in 2017 to have been Â£250,000.[2][27] In 2012, Paul Lambert revealed that on two earlier occasions he had considered signing Ruddy for clubs he'd previously managed: "It is funny but ... [we] ... went to watch him for Crewe at MK Dons ... but he had an absolute stinker. We also saw him when he was at Stockport and ... I decided against him at that time, but when Fraser [Forster] went and it was clear we wouldn't be able to get him back again I knew John had had a really good season at Motherwell. There was that clamour to sign Fraser, but with the finances we just couldn't do that. I wanted somebody with the same stature, a big goalkeeper, and John had that."[28] Ruddy made his debut for City against Watford in the opening fixture of the 2010â€“11 season which Norwich lost 3â€“2 at Carrow Road.[29] He kept his first clean sheet of the season in a 1â€“0 away win against Scunthorpe United on 14 August, followed by another against Swansea City a week later which included a penalty save that atoned for his error that led to it.[30][31] In Ruddy's first season for Norwich, he played 45 out of the 46 league matches and kept a clean sheet during the 1â€“0 away win against Portsmouth on 2 May 2011 that confirmed promotion to the Premier League.[30][32] Ruddy started the 2011â€“12 season as the first-choice keeper again for Norwich in the 1â€“1 draw at the DW Stadium against Wigan Athletic.[33] He followed this up during Norwich's first home Premier League match in seven years, a 1â€“1 draw against Stoke City, with a penalty stop against Jon Walters and a save denying Matthew Etherington from a direct free kick.[34] During the next match, he received his only red card of the season in the match against Chelsea after a foul on Ramires that led to a penalty.[35] However, Ruddy's fortunes were reversed during the return fixture against Chelsea in January 2012 when he kept his first clean sheet of the season during a 0â€“0 draw at Carrow Road, making several impressive stops from Fernando Torres and others.[36] Norwich finished in 12th place, and Ruddy was voted Player's Player of the Season,[5] a testament to Ruddy's performances that led to calls for his inclusion in Roy Hodgson's England Euro 2012 squad which were answered in the affirmative.[37][5] For the 2012â€“13 season Ruddy found himself first choice again ahead of Declan Rudd, Jed Steer and newcomer Mark Bunn. Once again, he saved a penalty during Norwich's first home Premier League fixture of the season, a 1â€“1 draw with Queens Park Rangers, although Bobby Zamora scored from the rebound.[38] He kept clean sheets in 1â€“0 wins against Arsenal and Manchester United, both during a run of form that saw him keep four clean sheets and concede just two goals in six matches,[39] in the last of which, against his former club, Everton, he suffered a thigh injury. He required surgery and was expected to be out for three months,[40] but was unable to return to action until 12 May 2013, in a 4â€“0 defeat of West Bromwich Albion that confirmed Norwich's Premier League status for a further season[41][42] Ruddy was ever-present in Norwich's league matches throughout the 2013â€“14 season,[43] at the end of which they were relegated to the Championship, and, despite interest from top-flight clubs,[44][45] he stayed with Norwich and was ever-present in league matches as they bounced back to the Premier League via the play-offs.[46][47] In May 2017, it was announced that Ruddy would be released when his contract expired.[48] All seven players who were being released were given the option of whether or not they chose to be available for selection in the one remaining match of the season. In the end, Ruddy was the only one who played. He was made team captain for the day (he had previously captained the side for one match in the 2013â€“14 season when SÃ©bastien Bassong and Russell Martin were both absent) and saw out his time with the club by keeping a clean sheet in a 4â€“0 win over Queens Park Rangers.[49]

Wolverhampton Wanderers

On 10 July 2017, Ruddy signed a two-year contract with Championship club Wolverhampton Wanderers.[50] His signing came days after the club's previous number one Carl Ikeme was diagnosed with acute leukaemia.[51] On 6 April 2018, Ruddy saved an injury-time penalty from Gary Madine away at Cardiff City in a 1â€“0 win, in a victory that took Wolves nine points clear of Cardiff.[52] Ruddy became the team's main goalkeeper and was part of the team that won promotion to the Premier League as champions. He was named in the division's PFA Team of the Year[53] and was awarded the EFL's Golden Glove Award for the most clean sheets with 24.[54] In August 2018 he extended his contract with Wolves, signing a deal due to last until summer 2020, despite having fallen behind new addition Rui PatrÃ­cio as the club's first-choice goalkeeper.[55] Ahead of its expiry, the club triggered their option to keep him for the 2020â€“21 season.[56] Playing against Liverpool in March 2021, PatrÃ­cio collided with his own captain, Conor Coady, and was stretchered off after receiving 15 minutes' treatment on the pitch. Ruddy came on as a concussion substitute and played the remaining few minutes of the game.[57] On 24 June 2021, Ruddy extended his contract with Wolves once again for the 2021â€“22 season.[58] In May 2022, Ruddy said that he would be leaving when his contract expired, after five years with the club.[59]

Birmingham City

Ruddy signed a one-year contract with Championship club Birmingham City on 14 July 2022. He was expected to provide competition for Neil Etheridge,[60] but went straight into the starting line-up, was ever-present up to the mid-season break, and kept seven clean sheets during that time. In November 2022, his contract was extended to run until the end of the 2023â€“24 season.[61] He remained ever-present in Championship matches until he strained a thigh during the visit to Millwall on 18 April 2023 and was replaced by Etheridge. Between them, they kept a clean sheet in a 1â€“0 win that ensured Birmingham's Championship status for a further season.[62][63] His team-mates voted him Players' Player of the Season for 2022â€“23.[64] In his second season, he made 46 appearances in all competitions as Birmingham were relegated to the third tier.[65][66] The club offered him a new contract which he chose not to accept.[67]

Newcastle United

On 1 July 2024, Ruddy signed a one-year contract with Premier League club Newcastle United.[68] He made his debut for the club in a preseason friendly against Spanish side Girona at St. James' Park on 9 August, being subbed on in the second-half for Nick Pope. On 6 June 2025, Newcastle announced that Ruddy's contract would expire at the end of June 2025, but that discussions for a new deal had been opened.[69] However he later featured on the bench in multiple pre-season friendlies, meaning he in fact signed a new contract without announcement from the club.

International career

On 16 May 2012, Ruddy received his first senior international call-up when Roy Hodgson selected him as a member of the England squad for UEFA Euro 2012.[70] However, a broken finger ruled him out of the tournament, and Jack Butland was called up to replace him.[71] Ruddy was called up on 10 August 2012 for the friendly against Italy in Bern, Switzerland, on 15 August 2012, along with Butland and Joe Hart.[72] He came on for Butland at half-time to make his senior England debut, and made some key saves as England won the match 2â€“1, keeping a clean sheet in his first England appearance after Butland conceded in the first half.[73] Ruddy was not included in Hodgson's 2014 World Cup squad, with Hart, Fraser Forster, and Ben Foster (footballer) selected instead. However, he was named on the seven-man standby list.[74]
