M.O.P. Vaishnav College for Women

M.O.P. Vaishnav College for Women (Autonomous) is an arts and science college located in Chennai, Tamil Nadu in southern India.[2] It is affiliated with the University of Madras.[3] The current principal is Dr. Archna Prasad. According to the survey on the Best Commerce Colleges All Over India by THE WEEK in association with Hansa Research (2019), M.O.P Vaishnav College is in the 36th position.[4] In the NIRF 2025 rankings, it is placed in the 101-150 band among colleges in India.[5] The college was re-accredited by NAAC in its fourth cycle with a 3.55 CGPA on a 4-point scale and an 'A++' grade (2022; valid until 2030).[6] It is located at Nungambakkam.

History

Sri Vallabhacharya Vidya Sabha collaborated with Dewan Bahadur M. O. Parthasarathy Iyengar Charities and established M.O.P. Vaishnav College for Women in 1992. The M.O.P Charities donated the land for the campus while the infrastructure and administration is handled by Sri Vallabhacharya Vidya Sabha.[7] In 2002, the college was accredited with four stars by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) and in the same year it was granted Permanent Affiliation by the University of Madras. It was granted autonomy by University Grants Commission (India) (UGC) and University of Madras in 2004.[8] The college's autonomy was extended in subsequent years, including a 10-year extension as of recent records. It underwent NAAC re-accreditation cycles, achieving an A grade in earlier assessments, and was re-accredited with an A++ grade and 3.56 CGPA on a four-point scale in 2022.[9][10]

Extra Activities

MOP Vaishnav offers various extra curricular activities for their students. The college has many clubs, workshops, seminars, cultural activities, research and journal publications.

Clubs

The college offers various club activities for each department. The main objective of the clubs is to bring out innate talents and contribute to the students' development in terms of teaching organizing, planning, evaluation, et cetera.[12]

Journals

The college publishes a series of 24 journals annually by the students with guidance from their department faculty.[13]

Sports and NCC

Apart from academics, the college also offers a platform for women who are into sports. In 2025, 15 athletes were recognized for international achievements, 69 for national, and 97 for state level.[14] Sports like Table Tennis, basketball, volleyball, chess, hockey, cricket, etc. are a part of the college. The college also provides scholarships for women who excel at state, national and international levels. [15] The college also has a National Cadet Corps division. The division has produced cadets who have represented the college in Republic Day Camp (RDC) and Thal Sainik Camp (TSC). Recent achievements include the Best ANO Award in 2023.[16] Cultural Activities M.O.P. Vaishnav College for Women conducts annual cultural extravaganzas[17] that bring out the competitive spirit of the students. The main aim is to provide endeavors that facilitate the overall development of students. The 2 main events are:
  • Abhilasha Abhilasha is a three-day talent scout held for the freshers. Besides finding latent talents in the first year students, this event serves as an ice breaker making the new students feel at home immediately. The freshers are all set to impress their seniors and the bonding is created instantaneously. Every fresher is reassured that she is in the right place. The event was held in August 2025.[18]
  • Jhankar Jhankar is a cultural extravaganza and is the most awaited event of the year for the students. Students from each department compete with the others on various platforms. It is an occasion for them to showcase their knowledge of the country's music, dance and various other art forms. It also provides them a platform to test their skills in event management. The students are excited and challenged by this opportunity. The event was held in September 2025 for postgraduate students.[19]
Apart from these activities, MOP Vaishnav is well known for its presence in the cultural events of other colleges throughout the country. VISH (Vaishnav Initiative for Social Help) is an event organized for social causes, providing students with opportunities for community engagement and enjoyment.[20]

Incubation

The college also has an Entrepreneurship Development Cell and Incubation and Innovation Centre, which provide a platform for the students to experience entrepreneurship. The cell organizes events such as entrepreneurship awareness programs, workshops, ideation sessions, and panel discussions.[21][22] Seminars The college strives to kindle the thirst for knowledge in students by conducting seminars and conferences. These seminars are designed to provide an opportunity for students to interact with the 'Best in the Business' so that they are abreast with the latest breakthroughs in their field of study. From 2020 to 2025, the college has organized numerous seminars and conferences at international, national, state, and regional levels, including the 2nd International Conference in January 2024.[23][24]
