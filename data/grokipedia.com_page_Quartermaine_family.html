Quartermaine family

The Quartermaine family is a fictional family from the ABC soap opera, General Hospital. Created by Eileen and Robert Mason Pollock and developed by Douglas Marland, the wealthy and eccentric family has been a continuous staple on General Hospital since their introduction in the 1970s. The Quartermaines were first introduced in 1977, when Stuart Damon originated the role of Alan Quartermaine. His parents and sister were next to be brought on canvas. Anna Lee originated matriarch Lila Quartermaine, with David Lewis being the first to play her husband Edward. Jane Elliot was cast as sharp-talking Tracy. The family owned business, ELQ Industries, named for the family's patriarch, Edward Louis Quartermaine, has been the center of several conflicts within the family. Notable recent events include the onscreen death of longtime matriarch Monica Quartermaine in September 2025.[1] The family is currently represented onscreen (as of October 2025) by Tracy, Ned, Olivia, Drew, Jason, Brook Lynn, Michael, Gio, Danny, Scout, Leo, Wiley and Amelia. The Quartermaines are notorious for their socialite status as well as their dysfunctional tendencies.[2] Prone to comedic fights, they annually attempt to have a traditional Thanksgiving dinner only for it to be ruined and have to order pizza.[3] At Christmas time, the family has been known for their annual Christmas reading at the Hospital's Pediatric Ward Christmas party, ever since the passing of former Chief of Staff Steve Hardy, until the party was no longer aired. In a 1992 interview, Stuart Damon described the family:
Here's a grouping of characters that, over the years, no matter what else has gone on, works. They're well-drawn, and the family is steeped in history. In soap operas, relationships are the things that work.[4]
Once an integral part of major storyline arcs,[5] the Quartermaines experienced a diminishing amount of screen time[6] during the late 2000s and 2010s, with many characters dying on-screen.[5][7][8] However, as of 2025, the family remains prominent in ongoing narratives, including ELQ estate disputes and the revelation of Gio Palmieri as a Quartermaine relative.[9] Soap opera gossip website Daytime Confidential named the family to their list of Top 10 Travesties of 2007, stating: "No. 3: The Systematic Extermination of the Quartermaine family."[10] The website speculated head writer at the time Robert Guza, Jr. was to blame, as they stated as No. 10 on their list of Top 10 Rivalries of 2007: "Guza vs. the Quartermaines and Their Fans: No battle on our list, save Viki vs. Dorian, has been fought for as long or as hard as Guza vs. the Quartermaine family and the fans who support them. The actors who portray(ed) Lila (until she died), Edward, and Alan were all fired at least once before fan outrage and campaigns brought them back in some form. The actors who portray(ed) Ned, Lois, Brook Lynn, AJ, Emily and Justus saw their characters written off or killed."[11]

ELQ

ELQ (Edward Louis Quartermaine Industries/Enterprises/International) is a multinational conglomerate headquartered in Port Charles. Owned by the wealthy Quartermaine family, it is one of the largest businesses in the region. Founded by Edward Quartermaine in 1978 as a family-owned enterprise, ownership has been distributed among Quartermaine descendants, leading to frequent internal conflicts. It is a rival company with nearby Chandler Enterprises and Buchanan Enterprises.[12] In recent years, ELQ has faced takeover attempts, including by Valentin Cassadine (2020â€“2024), who was ousted in 2024. As of 2025, Ned Quartermaine leads efforts with Michael Corinthos and Tracy Quartermaine to remove Drew Cain from involvement following merger disputes with Aurora Media. Monica Quartermaine's death in 2025 leaves her 5% shares pending distribution via will reading.[13][14]
