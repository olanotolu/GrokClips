John Simon (linebacker)

John Simon (born October 14, 1990)[1] is an American former professional football linebacker. He played college football at Ohio State, and was selected by the Baltimore Ravens in the fourth round of the 2013 NFL draft. He also played for the Houston Texans, Indianapolis Colts, New England Patriots, Tennessee Titans, and Pittsburgh Steelers.

Early life

Simon was born in Youngstown, Ohio. He played high school football for the Mooney Cardinals of Cardinal Mooney High School in Youngstown, Ohio. Ranked by Rivals.com as the No. 36 defensive tackle in the class of 2009, Simon chose Ohio State over offers from Nebraska, Notre Dame and Pittsburgh.[2] He played in the 2009 U.S. Army All-American Bowl.

College career

Simon attended [Ohio State University](Ohio State University "Ohio State University"), and played for the [Ohio State Buckeyes football](Ohio State Buckeyes football "Ohio State Buckeyes football") team from 2009 to 2012. As a junior in 2011, he received first-team All-Big Ten honors and was named a third-team All-American by the [Associated Press](Associated Press "Associated Press").[3][4] As a senior in 2012, he was named the [Big Ten Conference](Big Ten Conference "Big Ten Conference") Defensive Player of the Year and Defensive Lineman of the Year,[5] received first-team All-Big Ten honors,[6] was named a third-team All-American by the Associated Press,[7] and won the Bill Willis Trophy.[8][9] He finished his career with 154 tackles and 20.5 [quarterback sacks](Quarterback sack "Quarterback sack").

Professional career

Baltimore Ravens

Simon was selected by the Baltimore Ravens in the fourth round, with the 129th overall pick, of the 2013 NFL draft.[10] He signed a four-year, $2.545 million contract on May 21, 2013.[11] On August 30, 2014, Simon was waived by the Ravens and was signed to the practice squad the next day.[12][13]

Houston Texans

On October 7, 2014, Simon was signed by the Houston Texans off the Ravens' practice squad.[14] On October 9, 2014, Simon recorded his first NFL sack against the Indianapolis Colts.[15] On April 6, 2016, Simon signed his restricted free agent tender with the Texans.[16] In 2016, he recorded 51 tackles and 3.5 sacks.[17]

Indianapolis Colts

On March 10, 2017, the Indianapolis Colts signed Simon to a three-year contract.[18] He started nine games for the Colts in 2017 before being placed on injured reserve on December 5, 2017, with a shoulder injury.[19] On September 1, 2018, Simon was released by the Colts.[20]

Tennessee Titans

On July 25, 2021, Simon signed with the Tennessee Titans.[28] He was released on August 29, 2021.[29] He was re-signed to the practice squad on October 5.[30] He was promoted to the active roster on October 9.[31] He was released on November 6.[32] He re-signed with the Titans on November 23.[33] He was waived on December 6.[34]

Pittsburgh Steelers

On December 13, 2021, Simon signed with the Pittsburgh Steelers practice squad.[35] He signed a reserve/future contract with the Steelers on January 27, 2022.[36] He was released on May 16, 2022.[37]
