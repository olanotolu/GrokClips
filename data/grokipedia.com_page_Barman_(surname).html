Barman (surname)

Barman is a surname primarily originating in the Indian subcontinent, derived from the Sanskrit word varman, meaning "armor" or "protection," and commonly borne by members of the Rajbanshi community in West Bengal, India, and Bangladesh.[1][2] It serves as a regional variant of related surnames such as Verma or Varma, reflecting historical warrior or protective connotations in ancient Indian nomenclature.[1] Globally, the surname is most prevalent in South Asia, where it accounts for the vast majority of incidences, though it also appears in English-speaking contexts as an occupational name for a bartender or tavern keeper, stemming from the term "bar."[3][4] In demographic distributions, Barman bearers are overwhelmingly concentrated in Indo-South Asian populations, underscoring its ethnic ties to Bengali and related linguistic groups rather than widespread Western adoption.[3][5]
