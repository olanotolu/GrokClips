Dinesh Kanagaratnam

Aaryan Dinesh Kanagaratnam, also known by his initials A. D. K., ADK Sri Rascol, or simply Dinesh Kanagaratnam, is a Sri Lankan R&B and hip hop artist and music producer who has sung tracks in the Tamil language. He often collaborates with music directors in Tamil cinema as a playback singer.[1]

Early life and career

Dinesh was born in Badulla and raised in Modara, Sri Lanka. He studied information technology (IT) in Trichy, Tamil Nadu, India and started teaching IT courses in Sri Lanka. He began working with local musicians part-time to pursue his growing interest in music. In 2005, he was given a chance by Bathiya of Bathiya and Santhush, artists in Sri Lanka, and started recording rap songs until he was discovered by Vijay Antony. He collaborated with him for the song "Aathichudi"[2] for the soundtrack of the film TN 07 AL 4777 (2008)[3] which is a remake of Surangani, a song from his solo album, Tamizha[4] Back in Sri Lanka, he did a cross-culture album[5] in 2005 which includes songs in Tamil, Sinhala and English. His first collaboration with A. R. Rahman was "Magudi",[6] for the Mani Ratnam-directed film Kadal (2013). He was featured along with Anirudh Ravichander for the song "Mun Sellada"[7] in the film Manithan (2016), composed by Santhosh Narayanan. Kanagaratnam and rapper Sri Rascol formed a record label called Rap Machines[8] and have done other rap music. He continued to work in Tamil and Telugu cinema, writing and performing English language rap for soundtracks, including the title track "Maayon"[9] in Mersal (2017) composed by A. R. Rahman, the song "Gala Gala" featured with Megha from Race Gurram composed by S. Thaman and the song "Gala Gala" featured with Megha from Race Gurram composed by S. Thaman. Kanagaratnam has joined concert tours in Sri Lanka, the Middle East, India, Malaysia and Singapore. He is well known for his rap battles at every A. R. Rahman concert tours alongside Lady Kash and Blaaze.[10][11] In January 2019, Kanagaratnam performed with Bathiya and Santhush at Oba Nisa in Sri Lanka.
