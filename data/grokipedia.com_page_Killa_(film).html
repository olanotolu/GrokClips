Killa (film)

Killa (English: The Fort) is an Indian Marathi-language drama film directed by Avinash Arun. The film revolves around an 11-year-old seventh grade boy struggling to cope with the death of his father and his journey into coming of age. The film was selected for the 64th Berlin International Film Festival where it won the Crystal Bear by the Children's Jury in the Generation KPlus Selection.[2] At the 62nd National Film Awards in March 2015, the film won the Best Feature Film in Marathi award. The actor Parth Bhalerao received Special Mention for his role in this film and _Bhoothnath Returns.[3] The film was released on 26 June 2015.[4]

Killa

Plot

After the death of his father, 11-year-old Chinmay moves with his widowed mother Aruna from Pune to a small coastal village in the Konkan region due to her job transfer as a government officer. Enrolled in the seventh grade at a new school, Chinmay faces challenges adjusting to the rural environment and making friends. He forms a bond with his classmate Bandya, a reclusive boy, and together with other friends, they embark on adventures exploring the historic fort known as "Killa." The film depicts Chinmay's emotional journey through grief and coming of age, paralleled by his mother's struggles as a single parent balancing work and family pressures.[6]

Setting and filming locations

The film is set in a village on the Konkan coast of Maharashtra, not far from Ratnagiri, with mentions of Pune as the city they just moved from, and Satara as the town they are moving to at the end of the film. The fort shown in the film is Vijaydurg Fort in Sindhudurg district. The lighthouse is Jaigad Lighthouse in Ratnagiri district, and general filming took place in villages across Ratnagiri and Sindhudurg districts. The racing track shown for cycling is near Ganpatipule and the other scenes are shot in Guhagar, Vijaydurga, Palshet, and Pune.[7][8][9]

Killa (Production Section)

Production

The idea for Killa was inspired by Avinash Arun's childhood memories, including frequent family relocations due to his father's government job transfers, particularly three years spent in the coastal town of Murud, Maharashtra. The story had been developing in his mind for nearly six years by 2014. Arun, a 2011 graduate of the Film and Television Institute of India (FTII), narrated the idea to fellow FTII alumnus Tushar Paranjape, who developed it into a treatment. When Indian indie production company JAR Pictures expressed interest in producing regional films, Avinash Arun pitched his story to them in March 2013. The company decided to greenlight the project immediately, leading to six drafts of the script being completed over the next four months. Principal photography began in July 2013 and wrapped in 28 days.[10]

Release

The movie was released in Maharashtra, Karnataka, Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Goa on 26 June 2015 in 225 screens.[11] It was released in France (as La forteresse) on 7 October 2015.[12]
