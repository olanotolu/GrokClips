It Is Written

It Is Written is an internationally broadcast Seventh-day Adventist Christian television program founded in 1956 by George Vandeman.[1] Its title comes from the Gospel of Matthew: "It is written, 'Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceeds out of the mouth of God.'"[2] The programs are produced by It Is Written in Chattanooga, Tennessee.[3] It Is Written was the first religious television program to air in color, and the first to take advantage of satellite technology. The program broadcasts to millions of viewers in more than 140 countries.[4]

History

The program aired in March 1956 with founder Pastor George Vandeman as speaker. It made history as the first religious telecast to air in color.[3] Over the next half-century, It Is Written grew exponentially. Under Pastor Vandeman's direction, It Is Written entered many new evangelism arenas, including pioneering satellite technology with â€œTeleseminar '81,â€ the first live satellite uplink evangelistic presentation reaching 22 U.S. and Canadian cities.[5] His live â€œRevelation Seminarsâ€ attracted tens of thousands of eager visitors, and the telecast entered millions of homes in numerous countries. In 1991, Vandeman retired, and Pastor Mark Finley accepted the position as the ministry's new speaker/director. He also shared Pastor Vandeman's vision for evangelism, and in 1995 made history with a satellite evangelistic series called NET â€™95. NET â€™96 followed one year later, and whereas NET â€™95 had targeted North America, NET â€™96 reached the world with messages in 13 languages. It is estimated that 2,200 churches from 45 countries participated in at least one of the NETs , and more than 30,000 people were baptized at the conclusion of the meetings. The next major It Is Written satellite effort took place from 1999 through 2001 with ACTS 2000 , when the ministry covered the entire globe with multiple satellite meetings. Pastor Finley conducted a total of 10 series to a combined audience of 3 million people. By this time, the program's reach had expanded by leaps and bounds. Although hundreds of other Christian broadcasts had flooded the airwaves, It Is Written maintained its rank as one of the top 10 religious programs in North America and received more than 30 awards for excellence in programming. It Is Written entered a new era in January 2005 as Pastor Shawn Boonstra became the third speaker/director. Finley passed the mantle to Boonstra with his blessing. Pastor Boonstra came to the It Is Written headquarters from It Is Written Canada, where he had served as speaker/director for four years. As someone who joined the Adventist Church after attending an It Is Written evangelistic series, Pastor Boonstra passionately believes in the mission of the ministry. The next five years were a whirlwind of outreach via technology and public evangelism. Pastor Boonstra's Revelation Speaks Peace evangelistic series in cities like Phoenix, Portland, Los Angeles, India, and Rome energized attendees and church members alike with the hope in Christ that is revealed through the final book of the Bible. In January 2011, Pastor John Bradshaw was named new speaker/director for It Is Written.[6] In May 2011, Bradshaw conducted a short series live from Las Vegas called Babylon Rising. This effort was broadcast worldwide via satellite and the Internet. Following the success of the Babylon Rising series, Bradshaw presented a month-long series called Revelation Today live from the Cashman Center in Las Vegas in January 2012. He followed this series with meetings in Paris, Dayton, Southern Mexico, Costa Rica, Prague, New York, Charlotte and more. As of 2025, John Bradshaw continues as speaker/director, leading ongoing evangelistic efforts such as the online "Revelation Today: Great Reset" series.[7]

It Is Written Ministry

Ministry facts

It Is Written is broadcast in over 143 countries.[4] The program ranks among the top religious programs in North America.[1] In addition to broadcasting on regular network and cable stations, the telecast regularly airs on satellite networks including the Trinity Broadcasting Network (TBN), Hope Channel, and Three Angels Broadcasting Network (3ABN).[1] The ministry produces weekly programs in 12 languages.[4] Over its history, It Is Written has received more than 30 awards for excellence in programming, including 58 Telly Awards since 2011.[8]

It Is Written on the internet

It Is Written became one of the first Christian television programs to broadcast free video podcasts via iTunes in 2006.[12] On September 3, 2007, the ministry launched a daily (Mondayâ€“Friday) internet-based devotional program called A Better Way to Live. The tagline for the online program was "A daily spiritual boost for busy people," and in each program, Boonstra took one Bible verse and applied it to daily life.[5] This was followed by Bradshaw's launch of a daily one-minute "Every Word" devotional in 2011.[5] The It Is Written TV network is available on the internet, Apple TV, Roku, Amazon Fire TV, YouTube, and other platforms as of 2025.[13] Podcasts of the program continue to be available on Apple Podcasts.[14]

It Is Written on mobile

Since 2011, It Is Written has maintained a mobile presence through a dedicated app available on iOS and Android devices. The app provides access to 30-minute weekly television programs, short devotionals, ministry news, and more.[15][16] Users can also access email sign-ups and gifting opportunities, such as weekly offers for free resources, via the responsive website on mobile browsers.[17] In 2011, the initial mobile site won both a Silver W3 Award and a Silver Davey Award in the category of Best Mobile Website. It was designed and developed by Trinet Internet Solutions.[18] The app, developed by It Is Written International Television, was last updated in February 2024.[16]
