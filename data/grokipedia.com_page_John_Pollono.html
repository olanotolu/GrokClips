Early life and education

Pollono was born May 23, 1972, in New Hampshire into a working-class Italian-American family;[2] he has three sisters.[3] He was raised in Londonderry, New Hampshire and graduated from Londonderry High School.[2][4] Pollono has said that his childhood in New England has greatly influenced his work.[5][6] Pollono attended the University of New Hampshire, from which he graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in psychology in 1994.[7] After he moved to Los Angeles, he studied acting for a time at Howard Fine Studio.[5]

Career

2000â€“2010: Early career

Pollono moved around the country and worked on writing screenplays part-time, before settling in Los Angeles in 2000.[2][5] He later became interested in acting and helped to found the Jabberwocky Theatre Company in 2004. In 2008, Jabberwocky became Rogue Machine Theatre.[5] Through Jabberwocky and Rogue Machine, Pollono was able to produce the plays he had written, his first full-length play being Lost and Found which was originally produced in 2006 at the Lounge Theatre in Los Angeles.[3] His next produced play was Razorback , which was staged in 2008 at Rogue Machine. One of his one-act plays, Illuminati , won Best Play at the 2010 Network One-Act Festival in New York City and his first iteration of his play Rules of Seconds was chosen to participate in the 2011 Last Frontier Theatre Conference in Alaska.[3] During this time, Pollono also guest starred in television series including How I Met Your Mother and Grey's Anatomy , and had other small acting credits on film and stage. He also worked professionally in entertainment PR.[2][8]

2011â€“2017: Success on stage and screen

Stage

In 2011, Rogue Machine Theatre produced Pollono's black comedy play Small Engine Repair , which in large part was inspired by his upbringing outside of Manchester, New Hampshire.[9] Pollono starred as Frank, one of the play's three main characters. The play was first staged at Theatre/Theater in Los Angeles and was very successful, later transferring to the Beverly Hills Playhouse. A year-and-a-half later, the play was produced by MCC Theater in New York City and premiered at the off-Broadway Lucille Lortel Theatre.[10] This marked Pollono's debut off-Broadway as a playwright and actor. Simultaneously, Pollono's new play, Lost Girls premiered in Los Angeles at Theatre/Theater in Fall 2013.[11] Lost Girls was picked up by MCC Theater for their 2015â€“2016 season and as produced off-Broadway in Fall 2015.[12] Like Small Engine Repair , Lost Girls played at the Lucille Lortel Theatre and was directed by Jo Bonney. In 2017, Pollono joined with other Los Angeles-based playwrights to form the playwriting collective The Temblors. A reworking of his play Rules of Seconds was produced in 2017 at the Los Angeles Theatre Center.[13][14]

Screenwriting and acting

Pollono's first notable screen acting role was as Pat Dolan in the TNT series Mob City in 2013. Pollono's character appears in all six episodes of the show. Of note, Mob City stars Jon Bernthal, who Pollono had just acted opposite in Small Engine Repair at Theatre/Theater before production of the television show began. In 2014, Pollono guest starred in an episode of Intelligence. In 2016, Pollono began portraying Tyler, a co-worker of the character Randall in This Is Us. In 2014, Pollono was tapped to write the screenplay for what would become the 2017 film Stronger , which starred Jake Gyllenhaal as Boston Marathon bombing survivor Jeff Bauman.[15] The screenplay, based on Bauman's memoir Stronge r, was number two on the Black List in 2016 and the film was released in 2017 to critical acclaim.[13]

2018â€“present: Recent and ongoing work

In 2019, Pollono reprised his role as Frank in the film adaptation of Small Engine Repair , his directorial debut.[16] The film was released in September 2021. Pollono wrote the screenplay for the crime comedy Riff Raff (2024), directed by Dito Montiel and starring Jennifer Coolidge, Ed Harris, and Bill Murray.) He also had acting roles in the series Bupkis (2023) as Michael, and in the films Skincare (2024) as Gun Store Noah and Cash for Gold (2024) as Billy.[17][18]) In February 2019, it was announced that Pollono was writing a screenplay for a biopic based on the life of Hulk Hogan for Netflix, to be directed by Todd Phillips, co-written by Scott Silver, and starring Chris Hemsworth.[19] As of October 2025, the project remains in development but has faced significant delays, with Phillips stepping away as director in 2024 and Hemsworth stating it is "a while away."[20][21]

Personal life

Pollono is married to stage actress and founding member of Rogue Machine Theatre Jennifer Pollono.[22] they have two children together, including actress Sophie Pollono.[23] Pollono lives in Los Angeles.[24]
