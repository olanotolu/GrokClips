It'z Me

It'z Me (stylized as IT'z ME) is the second extended play (EP) by South Korean girl group Itzy, released by JYP Entertainment on March 9, 2020. The EP features seven tracks, including the lead single "Wannabe", with productions from Galactika, Oak Felder, Oliver Heldens, earattack, Shim Eunji, Collapsedone, Jin by Jin, Sophie, and KASS. The EP received positive reviews from critics, who praised the groups evolving sound and empowering themes of self-confidence. Upon its release, It'z Me debuted at number 1 on the Gaon Album Chart, number 5 on the US Billboard World Albums chart, and sold over 126,000 units in its first month.

Background and release

"ITZY finished filming the music video for their comeback ahead of their showcase tour in the United States. The exact timing of their comeback is undecided." â€” JYP Entertainment shared to Xportsnews.[1]
On January 28, 2020, JYP Entertainment announced that Itzy would release a new record in the spring.[2][3] On February 13, 2020, it was reported that Itzy would make a comeback on March 9, which JYP Entertainment confirmed.[4] From February 18 to 20, teaser images for the group were revealed.[5][6][7] Individual member teasers were unveiled from February 23 to 27.[8][9][10][11][12] On March 1, the track listing for the album was revealed.[13] On March 4 and 5, music video teasers for the title track "Wannabe" were released.[14][15] On March 9, Itzy released their EP It'z Me, alongside a music video for its lead single. Three physical versions of the EP were made available, titled IT'z, ME, and WANNABE.[16]

Music and composition

It'z Me is a K-pop record that blends influences of dance, hip hop, and electronic music, continuing Itzy's exploration of new areas within the K-pop scene and their discovery of their own musical identity.[17] The lead single "Wannabe" features dance-pop elements with a strong beat, characteristic of K-pop.[18] It was written and produced by Galactika. Other tracks on the EP explore a variety of genres: â€œTing Ting Tingâ€ is a rowdy-yet-fierce EDM cut with future house elements, produced with Oliver Heldens;[19] "That's a No No" is a high-energy track blending moombahton and trap;[19] "Nobody Like You" incorporates elements of intense rock;[20] "You Make Me" blends pop with hip-hop influences through bold raps;[20] "I Don't Wanna Dance" combines house music with moombahton in a party vibe;[19] and "24Hrs" is a hyperpop track described as a "quirky-pop stomper", featuring production from the late Sophieâ€”the only K-pop song she produced.[17][21]

Live performances

On March 12, 2020, Itzy made their debut performance of "Wannabe" on M Countdown.[22] From March 13 to April 6, 2020, the group performed "Wannabe" on Music Bank,[23][24] Show! Music Core,[25][26] and Inkigayo.[27][28]

Billboard's Jeff Benjamin gave a positive review, stating that "ITZY continue their string of empowering and confident singles with the most striking difference in It'z Me coming from the groupâ€™s overall sound becoming harder and featuring more electronic productions."[30] IZM's Kim Do-heon offered a mixed assessment, highlighting the powerful message in the chorus of "Wannabe", where the five members, Yeji, Lia, Ryujin, Chaeryeong, and Yuna powerfully shout out "I don't wanna be me somebody / Just wanna be me". He added that, while the song encourages individuality, the harmony in their voices evokes the idea of a collective "wannabe", reflecting a lack of distinct individuality in line with JYP's mass-appeal strategy.[29] The album received generally positive reviews from other critics. For instance, kpopreviewed praised the album's fun tracks and strong performances, rating it 8.6/10.[31] Selective Hearing noted the gritty, guitar-driven sound in tracks like "Nobody Like You", appreciating ITZY's exploration beyond pop polish.[32]

Commercial performance

"Wannabe" debuted at number 6 on the Gaon Digital Chart, marking Itzy's third top-ten hit.[33] The song also debuted at number 4 on the US Billboard World Digital Song Sales chart, becoming the group's first top-five entry since "Dalla Dalla" and their third top-ten entry overall.[34] In New Zealand, the song peaked at number 22 on the Hot Singles chart.[35] While in Japan, it debuted at number 23.[36] The group became the fifth K-pop female act to chart on the Canadian Hot 100, debuting at number 92.[37] The song topped the charts in Malaysia and Singapore, becoming their first number-one hit on both charts.[38][39] On March 21, It'z Me debuted at number 5 on the US Billboard World Albums chart.[40] According to Hanteo, the EP sold 34,269 physical copies on its first day of release, nearly four times the first-day sales of It'z Icy.[41] The EP also debuted at number 1 on the Gaon Weekly Album Chart, marking their first number-one album in South Korea.[42] It also debuted at number 27 in Poland on the OLiS chart, making it their first appearance on a European chart.[43]

Music video

The music video for the title track accumulated over 11 million views within 24 hours of its release. In April 2020, it had over 100 million views on YouTube, and as of October 2025, it has over 570 million views on the platform.[44][45]
