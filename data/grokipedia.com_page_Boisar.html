Boisar

Boisar is the largest industrial town in the Mumbai Metropolitan Region in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It was originally a part of the Thane district before becoming a part of the Palghar district. It is approximately 111 kilometers (69 miles) away from the state capital Mumbai, and 98 km (61 mi) from the union territory Daman. It is located in the district Palghar in Maharashtra. Boisar is governed by the Palghar Municipal Council (PMC). It is home to Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (MIDC). Boisar hosts India's first nuclear power stationâ€”Tarapur Atomic Power Station (TAPS).[2] It has a railway station in the Western Railway zone of Indian Railways as well as Mumbai Suburban Railway. The Mumbaiâ€“Ahmedabad high-speed rail corridor will have a stop at Boisar. Boisar is a popular weekend spot for tourists and locals due to nearby beaches, forts and lakes. The place has seen rapid development due to:
  • The existence of more than 1500 industrial units.
  • Its proximity to Mumbai & Gujarat.[3]
  • Easy access by train and road.
  • Availability of surplus power and water.
  • Its vicinity of Maharashtra's largest fishing port Satpati (20 km [12 mi]).
  • The upcoming Mumbai-Ahmedabad bullet train having a stop at Boisar.
  • The upcoming Delhiâ€“Mumbai Industrial Corridor Project (DMIC) being planned to pass via Boisar.[4]
  • The upcoming Mumbai-Vadodara expressway.
  • The expansion of sea transport project.

Demonym

A resident of Boisar is called a Boisarkar in Marathi, where the suffix "kar" indicates "resident of" or "to belong to" (commonly used informally on social media and local contexts).[5]

History

Boisar was part of the Konkan region under the Silhara dynasty (9thâ€“13th centuries), vassals of the Rashtrakuta dynasty which ruled the Deccan plateau between the 8th and 10th centuries. It was ruled by the Ahmadnagar Sultanate (1521â€“1594), the Portuguese Empire (from 1594), the Maratha Empire (1739â€“1818), the East India Company (1818â€“1857) and the British Raj (1857â€“1947). There are some forts near the city, such as Asava Fort (11 km), Tarapur Fort (11 km), Kaldurg Fort (~21 km), Shirgaon Fort (~20 km), Mahim Fort (~100 km), and Asheri Fort (25 km). Boisar was initially inhabited by the Kolis and Konkanis who were fishermen and used to live on the Arabian Sea coast. The Kolis are traditional fishermen who speak dialects of Marathi. The present Vanjari community of Boisar was part of the Indus Valley civilization and originate from Rajasthan.[6] In the 15th century they served as main grain traders, suppliers and salesmen in the Mughal army of Alauddin Khalji. They migrated to "Moradi" port (today "Murbe") to trade in Boisar because the British started the railway in 1853 which halted the grain trade. Their language is distinct from standard Marathi as it is influenced by Rajasthani and Gujarati. The Vadval community is located in Boisar and Tarapur. They are said to be the descendants of the Yadava dynasty of Devagiri who settled in the region after the dynasty's fall. They form one of the few Marathi-speaking communities which belong to Kshatriya varna but not the traditional 96-clan Maratha caste. Boisar was a town in the Bombay Presidency during the 18th and 19th centuries. It was part of Thane district after the independence of India. Palghar district has been the current district of Boisar since 1 August 2014.

Geography of Boisar

Boisar encompasses an area of 8.90 square kilometres (3.44 sq mi). It is located at 19Â° 48' 13â€³N 72Â° 45' 22â€³E[3] and has an average altitude of 14 m (46 ft)[3] at the base of the eastern slopes of the Sahyadri. Boisar is a part of the coastal country known as the Konkan with the Arabian Sea forming its western coast, and is about 7.21 kilometres (4.48 mi) from the shores. It is close to Maharashtra, Gujarat, and Daman.

Hydrography

The two main rivers flowing through the city are the Surya and the Banganga (which has Betegaon Railway Bridge). Many small creeks are found all along the western coast villages like Kolwade, Kumbhavali, and Murbe where tidal waters flood upstream. There are dams such as the Surya Dam and the Devkhop dam. Boisar has no natural lakes but it has artificial ones. The Babule Lake[7] and Navale Lake[8] near the city are known for lotus blooms in winter. The Kokner/Kokaner[9][10] hot water spring is on the banks on the Surya river; the water is a murky greenish brown due to sulphur & chemical deposits from the spring.

Pedology

Basalt[11] and medium deep grayish black soil[11] are found in the hill area and deep black soils[11] are found in the basins of Surya river. Coastal saline soil[11] occurs along the Arabian Sea. Other than basalt, minor minerals like murrum are found in the city.

Demographics

As per the 2011 census, the Boisar census town had a population of 36,151, of which 20,319 were males and 15,832 were females, with a total of 4,896 children below 6 years of age. The child sex ratio was around 919 compared to the Maharashtra state average of 894. The literacy rate was 88.49%, higher than the state average of 82.34%. The town had administration over 8,711 houses, providing basic amenities like water and sewerage, and is authorized to build roads within limits and impose property taxes. Out of the total population, 14,372 were employed (12,312 males and 2,060 females). In the census, a worker is defined as a person who performs a job, service, cultivation, or labour. Of the 14,372 workers, 89.32% were engaged in main work while 10.68% were in marginal work. Due to rapid industrialization and proximity to Mumbai, the urban area of Boisar has grown significantly since 2011. Estimates place the population of the Boisar metropolitan cluster at approximately 295,000 as of 2019.[12] Additionally, the Boisar assembly constituency had 358,362 registered voters as of October 2023, indicating further population increase.[13] Population and Literacy of Boisar census town (2011) [1]

Religion

According to the 2011 census, out of the total population of 36,151 in the Boisar census town, Hindus (83.84%) constituted the majority. Scheduled Castes (SC) made up 4.82% and Scheduled Tribes (ST) 1.76% of the population.[1] The official language is Marathi, with Hindi and English also permitted for official purposes. Marathi is the most widely spoken language, including dialects such as Vanjari, Agri, Koli, Vadvali, and Warli. Small communities of Gujaratis and Muslims speak Gujarati and Urdu, respectively. Schools mandate Marathi, Hindi, and English, with Sanskrit and Urdu as optional. Religion in Boisar census town (2011)[14]

Transportation

Railway Station

  • Boisar railway station is the main mode of transportation.
  • All local trains run between Dahanu and Churchgate;[15] Dahanu-Virar[16] has a stop at Boisar railway station.
  • It is part of the Western Railway zone of Indian Railways.[17][18]
  • There are some local trains and express trains that run through Boisar station to the Mumbai Central line and Central Railway zone of Indian Railways via the Boisar-Vasai-Bhiwandi-Panvel railway route.[19]
  • Boisar railway station has 3 platforms and multiple tracks. After a period of neglect, it has become properly maintained.
  • Boisar high-speed railway station, part of the Mumbaiâ€“Ahmedabad high-speed rail corridor, is under construction and expected to be operational by 2027. It will feature two platforms and five tracks, enhancing connectivity to major cities.[20]

Road

  • Transportation inside city is done by auto rickshaws.[21]
  • (Boisar-Palghar), (Boisar-Navapur, Nandgaon, Alewadi and Kumbhavali) and (Boisar-Tarapur) are connected via 10-seat passenger auto rickshaws and Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation buses.[22]
  • Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation provides AC[23][24] and non AC bus services to other cities in Maharashtra.
  • Boisar is connected to national highway NH 48, which connects to Maharashtra and Gujarat.
  • The under-construction Delhiâ€“Mumbai Expressway (including the Mumbai-Vadodara section) passes near Boisar via Boisar Road, with completion expected by late 2025 or 2026, reducing travel time to Vadodara and beyond.[25]

Boat Services

  • In city, the coastal area usually commutes by ferry. Routes[26] are defined by Maharashtra Maritime Board.
  • Navapur to Dandi.[26]
  • Murbe to Satpati.[26][27]
  • Murbe to Kharekuran.[26][28]
  • Ferries are available throughout the year from 6 am to 6 pm, except during monsoons. Timings of ferry can change depending on the tide.

Air

  • Boisar has no airport.
  • The nearest airport is Chhatrapati Shivaji international airport Mumbai(BOM)[29] which is 105 km (65 mi) away.

Education

Schools and Higher Secondary Schools (Junior Colleges)

There are schools and junior colleges in Boisar. Most of them are run by private individuals and trusts. The city's public school system and junior colleges are managed by the education ministry of the Government of Maharashtra. The schools and junior colleges are affiliated with the following boards:
  1. Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education.
    1. Secondary School Certificate (SSC): 10th grade.
    2. Higher School Certificate (HSC): 12th grade.
  2. Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE).
  3. Indian Certificate of Secondary Education (ICSE).
Notable schools include:
  • Atomic Energy Central School No. 2 (CBSE), located near Tarapur Atomic Power Station.[30]
  • Chinmaya Vidyalaya (CBSE).[30]
  • Sanskruti Vidyasankul (CBSE), offering nursery to higher secondary.[31]
  • U S Ostwal English Academy (State Board).[30]

Professional Programme (Internship and Apprenticeship)

  • Boisar is known for its industrial hub and most industries (especially chemical, pharmaceutical, textile and mechanical companies)[41] offer internship and apprenticeship programs to college undergraduates and postgraduates. Banks and hospitals also provide work experience for students and professionals.

Research Institutions

Boisar is near research facilities associated with the Tarapur Atomic Power Station, including nuclear-related R&D by the Nuclear Power Corporation of India Limited (NPCIL). Local institutions like St. John Institute of Pharmacy and Research in Palghar conduct pharmaceutical studies.[42][43]
