Al-Markhiya SC

Al-Markhiya Sports Club (Arabic: المرخية) is a Qatari multi-sports club, based in the Al Markhiya district of Doha.[2] Its football department plays in the Qatari Second Division. Following relegation from the Qatar Stars League after the 2023–24 season, the club finished as runners-up in the 2024–25 Qatari Second Division and currently competes in the 2025–26 season. The club was founded in 1995 as Al-Ittifaq, but was renamed to Al-Markhiya in 2004 to better represent the district where it is located.

Honours

Domestic

  • Qatari Second Division
    • Champions (6): 1995, 1996, 1997, 1999, 2017, 2022

Stadium

The Al-Markhiya Stadium, located in the Al Markhiya district of Doha, was built in 1995 and serves primarily as a multi-purpose venue for the club, including a football pitch, locker rooms, and an administrative office. It has limited spectator capacity, estimated at around 200. However, due to its insufficient capacity and facilities for competitive matches, the club frequently uses larger venues. As of the 2025–26 season in the Qatari Second Division, home games are primarily played at Grand Hamad Stadium in Doha (capacity 13,000).[3] The club was relegated to the Second Division after losing the promotion/relegation playoff to Umm Salal 2–1 on April 28, 2025.[4]

Crest

A new club crest was unveiled in a public ceremony on 31 May 2023. Designed by Qatari designer Fatima Al Nuaimi, the logo features an oryx, the national animal of Qatar, and maroon coloring, a color associated with the country's flag.[5]
