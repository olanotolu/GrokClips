2023 Euro Winners Cup

The 2023 Euro Winners Cup was the ninth edition of the annual European beach soccer club competition, featuring teams that had won their respective national leagues, and was hosted in Nazaré, Portugal, from 12 to 18 June.[1] Falfala Kfar Qassem Beach Soccer Club (BSC) from Israel emerged as champions, defeating Lenergy Pisa BS from Italy in the final to secure their maiden title in the tournament.[1][2] The event included a preliminary Euro Winners Challenge phase for additional qualifiers, followed by group stages and knockout rounds among 20 men's teams, highlighting the growing prominence of beach soccer clubs across Europe.[1] A parallel women's competition was also held, underscoring the tournament's role in promoting the sport at both elite club levels.[1]

Tournament Background

Overview and Edition Context

The Euro Winners Cup is an annual beach soccer club competition organized by Beach Soccer Worldwide (BSWW), featuring top-division teams from European national leagues and cup winners as a continental showcase for the sport's elite clubs.[2] Launched to promote high-level club beach soccer, it emphasizes fast-paced, three-period matches on sand with rules adapted for the variant, including unlimited substitutions and penalty shootouts after draws.[1] The tournament has grown in scale, drawing participants from over 20 countries and serving as a key event in the European beach soccer calendar, distinct from national team competitions like the Euro Beach Soccer Cup.[3] The 2023 men's edition, the 11th overall, was hosted in Nazaré, Portugal, from June 12 to 18, attracting a record 36 teams divided into nine preliminary groups, with progression to knockout stages determined by group winners and best runners-up.[3][1] Qualification slots were allocated based on each country's beach soccer ranking, prioritizing national champions while allowing additional entries from strong leagues.[3] This edition highlighted emerging clubs, culminating in Falfala KQ BSC of Israel defeating Italy's Lenergy Pisa BS in the final on June 18, securing the Israeli side's inaugural title in a debut final appearance for both teams.[4][2] Concurrently, the women's tournament—its eighth staging—featured parallel competition in Nazaré, underscoring the event's dual-gender format and commitment to broadening beach soccer participation across Europe.[1] The 2023 Euro Winners Cup reinforced Nazaré's status as a recurring host venue, known for its coastal infrastructure supporting large-scale beach soccer events.[1]

Dates, Venue, and Organization

The 2023 Euro Winners Cup, an annual club beach soccer competition, took place from 12 to 18 June in Nazaré, Portugal.[1] The event was hosted at the Estadio do Viveiro stadium on Praia da Nazaré beach, a venue known for its suitability for beach soccer due to the sandy terrain and coastal setting.[5] This marked a return to Nazaré, a recurring host location selected for its established infrastructure and history of hosting international beach soccer events.[6] The tournament was organized by Beach Soccer Worldwide (BSWW), the international governing body for beach soccer, which oversees qualification, format, and execution to ensure compliance with global standards.[1] BSWW coordinated logistics, including team registrations and match scheduling, in collaboration with local Portuguese authorities for venue access and free public entry to promote the sport's growth in Europe.[5] The edition featured both men's and women's competitions, with the preliminary Euro Winners Challenge serving as a qualifier running concurrently from earlier in June.[6]

Qualification and Teams

Entry Criteria and Process

National beach soccer associations nominated clubs for the 2023 Euro Winners Cup based on their performance as champions of domestic leagues or cup competitions, typically allowing up to two entries per country to represent the highest level of club achievement in the sport.[7][8] This process prioritized teams that had secured national titles in the preceding season, ensuring representation from established beach soccer nations across Europe, with France, Belgium, and Italy each fielding two squads.[9] The Euro Winners Challenge served as a key qualifying pathway, functioning as a preliminary tournament where competing clubs vied for direct spots in the main event; specifically, the two finalists advanced to the subsequent Euro Winners Cup, providing opportunities for emerging or additional teams from smaller associations to participate.[10] This dual-entry system, overseen by Beach Soccer Worldwide, balanced direct national qualification with merit-based progression to accommodate around 32 teams in the group stage while maintaining competitive integrity.[11] Nominations were finalized prior to the draw on May 18, 2023, with participating clubs required to meet BSWW's eligibility standards, including active status and compliance with international beach soccer rules.[12]

Competition Format

Overall Structure

The 2023 Men's Euro Winners Cup featured 36 participating teams divided into nine groups of four, with each group contesting a round-robin format over the initial days of the tournament.[3] This group stage determined advancement based on performance, with the top teams from each group qualifying for the subsequent knockout phase.[3] In total, 28 teams progressed from the group stage to the knockout competitions held in Nazaré, Portugal.[13] The knockout stage employed a single-elimination format, beginning with an initial round of matches that reduced the competing teams.[9] From this preliminary knockout round, 14 teams advanced to the next stage of elimination games, continuing through quarterfinals, semifinals, and culminating in a final match to crown the winner.[9] This progression ensured a streamlined path to the championship while accommodating the large field of entrants.[13] A parallel Women's Euro Winners Cup operated under a similar group-to-knockout structure, though with fewer teams, emphasizing the event's dual-category format organized by Beach Soccer Worldwide.[1] The overall design balanced inclusivity for national cup winners across Europe with rigorous elimination to identify continental club champions in beach soccer.[3]

Rules Specific to Beach Soccer Variant

The Euro Winners Cup employs the standard beach soccer rules as codified in the Laws of the Game by Beach Soccer Worldwide (BSWW), which emphasize high-scoring, continuous action on a sand surface to distinguish it from field soccer. Matches are divided into three 12-minute periods of effective playing time, with the clock running continuously except for specific stoppages such as injuries, substitutions outside designated zones, or goalkeeper possession exceeding four seconds; a three-minute break separates each period.[14][15] Each team fields five players, comprising four outfield players and one goalkeeper, with a maximum squad of 12 players permitted, though at least three players must be available to avoid forfeiture. Substitutions are unlimited and executed "flying," meaning players enter and exit through designated zones without halting play, promoting fluid rotations typical of the variant's emphasis on endurance and aerial play on sand. The playing surface measures 37 meters in length by 28 meters in width, marked with lines and featuring circular penalty areas around each goal, with goals sized at 2.2 meters high by 5.5 meters wide; barefoot play is mandatory to leverage the sand's traction and forgiveness.[14][16] Fouls are categorized as direct free kicks for infractions like tripping or charging, with no offside rule applied to encourage open play; teams accumulate fouls per period, and upon reaching five, subsequent direct free kick offenses trigger a penalty kick from the penalty spot, executed without a defensive wall and requiring the kicker to wait four seconds before approaching the ball. Goalkeepers face restrictions, including a four-second limit on possession and inability to handle rebound shots directly from teammates within their penalty area. Restarts, such as kick-ins or corner kicks, allow any player to strike the ball from any point behind the line, further accelerating the game's pace.[14][15] Ties are eliminated through a three-minute extra time period of sudden-death rules, where the first goal decides the winner; if unresolved, a penalty shootout ensues with five kicks per team, proceeding to one-on-one sudden death thereafter, ensuring definitive outcomes in knockout stages. These provisions, unaltered for the 2023 edition, prioritize spectacle and decisiveness, aligning with BSWW's framework for professional club competitions.[14][15]

Draw and Fixtures

Draw Procedure

The draw for the 36 clubs in the main group stage of the 2023 Men's Euro Winners Cup was conducted in May 2023, prior to the tournament's start on 9 June in Nazaré, Portugal. Participating clubs were divided into four pots according to their positions in the Beach Soccer Worldwide (BSWW) ranking, which evaluates club performance across international competitions. Top-seeded teams, determined by prior achievements such as the 2022 edition results, were pre-assigned to head Groups A through I: ACD O Sótão (Portugal) in Group A, SC Braga (Portugal) in Group B, Varzim SC (Portugal) in Group C, and similarly placed high-ranking clubs like Beach Royals in subsequent groups to ensure competitive balance.[3] Subsequent placements involved drawing one club from each pot into every group, adhering to the restriction that no two clubs from the same nation could be grouped together, except potentially for multiple Portuguese entries as the host nation. This seeding and restriction mechanism, rooted in BSWW protocols, aimed to distribute strength evenly and prevent domestic matchups in the initial phase. The completed groups were officially announced on 18 May 2023, following the draw event.[3] For the preliminary Euro Winners Challenge involving additional qualifiers, a separate group stage draw was executed similarly, with teams potted by BSWW rankings and national separation rules to form initial groups advancing to the main competition. This process ensured broader participation while maintaining fairness based on empirical club performance data rather than subjective allocations.[3]

Key Matchups and Schedule

The group stage draw, conducted on May 18, 2023, produced several high-profile encounters, including Juventus facing PSV Eindhoven and AS Monaco clashing with Benfica, pitting established European clubs against one another in early fixtures. These matchups were highlighted for their potential to influence group outcomes, with Juventus seeking to reclaim continental success and Benfica's attacking style anticipated to test Monaco's defense.[3] The main tournament schedule ran from June 12 to 18, 2023, at Nazaré's Praia do Norte stadium, following the preliminary qualification phase. Group stage games occurred over June 12–15, determining advancement to knockouts, while the single-elimination knockout rounds began on June 16 with the round of 32 and round of 16, proceeded to quarterfinals on June 17, and concluded with semifinals and the final on June 18.[1] On June 16, notable round-of-32 results included advancing teams like Falfala KQ BSC and Lenergy Pisa BS, setting up intensified competition among 14 progressing sides.[9] The knockout stage featured escalating stakes, with quarterfinals on June 17 eliminating contenders and semifinals determining finalists. The championship match on June 18 pitted Italy's Lenergy Pisa BS against Israel's Falfala KQ BSC, a matchup emblematic of the tournament's competitive depth, as both clubs debuted in the final—Pisa leveraging defensive resilience and Falfala countering with offensive flair. Falfala secured a 2–2 draw before prevailing 5–3 on penalties, marking the first European beach soccer club title for an Israeli team.[17][18] This final underscored the event's emphasis on endurance and precision in beach soccer's fast-paced format.[19]

Preliminary Round: Euro Winners Challenge

Group Stage Details

The Euro Winners Challenge group stage featured 16 clubs divided into four groups of four teams each, with each team playing three round-robin matches between 9 and 11 June 2023 in Nazaré, Portugal.[3] The top two finishers from each group advanced to the Challenge's knockout phase, where the four semi-finalists earned entry into the main Euro Winners Cup's round of 16 or quarter-finals, depending on seeding.[3] Groups were drawn on 18 May 2023, prioritizing national representation limits and prior continental rankings to balance competition.[3] Group A comprised AD Buarcos 2017 and Vila Flor SC (both Portugal), Miehet (Finland), and A. O. Kefallinia (Greece).[3] Key results included Vila Flor SC's 6–4 victory over AD Buarcos 2017 on 10 June and A. O. Kefallinia's 5–4 win against Miehet on the same day; on 11 June, Vila Flor SC defeated Miehet 9–0, while AD Buarcos 2017 beat A. O. Kefallinia 3–2.[20][21] Vila Flor SC topped the group with three wins, followed by AD Buarcos 2017 in second, both advancing.[21] Group C included Casa do Benfica em Viseu and São Domingos Setúbal (both Portugal), FC Genappe ASBL (Belgium), and Saint Médard en Jalles (France).[3] FC Genappe ASBL finished first with 7 points from three matches, ahead of Saint Médard en Jalles on 6 points; both progressed to the knockouts, while Casa do Benfica em Viseu earned 3 points and São Domingos Setúbal 0.[22] In Group D, Minots de Marseille (France) secured first place after a decisive late victory in their final group fixture against the second-placed team on 11 June, advancing alongside the runner-up, which included ACD O Sótão B (Portugal).[21][23] The group stage concluded with all top seeds from the preliminary round positioned to challenge for main draw qualification through subsequent knockouts.[24]

Knockout Advancement

The knockout stage of the Euro Winners Challenge commenced on 14 June 2023, following the group stage, with matches determining progression toward the final and ultimate advancement to the main Euro Winners Cup. In a notable quarter-final or defining knockout fixture, Minots de Marseille defeated fellow French side Saint Médard-en-Jalles 9–5, showcasing dominance through standout performances including four goals from Diassy and a hat-trick from Mendy.[24] This victory propelled Minots de Marseille forward in the bracket. Additional knockout contests on the same day included placement and progression matches among the lower-ranked group finishers, such as penalty shootouts resolving tied games (e.g., 3–3 draws decided via pens).[25] These ensured the semi-final lineup, with ACD O Sótão B emerging as the other finalist after navigating their path, potentially via a penalty win over AD Nazaré (3–3, pens).[25] The final took place on 15 June 2023 at Estádio do Viveiro in Nazaré, Portugal, where Minots de Marseille overcame ACD O Sótão B 9–5 to claim the Euro Winners Challenge title.[23] As victors, Minots de Marseille earned direct entry into the quarter-finals of the main Euro Winners Cup, bypassing the preliminary and group stages of the primary competition—a format designed to integrate high-performing challengers into the elite knockout phase without diluting the main draw's structure. Third and fourth places were awarded to AD Nazaré and AD Buarcos, respectively, based on subsequent placement matches.[26] This advancement mechanism highlighted the Challenge's role in expanding competitive depth while prioritizing proven knockout success for qualification.

Knockout Stage

Round of 32

The Round of 32 marked the commencement of the knockout phase in the 2023 Euro Winners Cup, featuring 16 single-elimination matches contested on 15 June 2023 at the Estádio do Viveiro in Nazaré, Portugal. The 32 participating teams were the top finishers from the main group stage (groups A through I), with pairings determined by a post-group draw that accounted for seeding based on overall rankings to avoid early clashes between top performers.[13] All fixtures were played under standard beach soccer rules, with matches consisting of three 12-minute periods and potential overtime or penalty shootouts for ties. Several results highlighted the competitive nature of the round, including dominant performances by established clubs. SC Braga/AAUM secured a resounding 14–2 victory over AS Etaples, showcasing their offensive prowess with multiple goal contributions from key players.[33] CD Marbella advanced with a 4–3 win against Rostocker Robben, edging out the German side in a closely contested encounter. BSC Vít progressed convincingly, defeating Pafos FC 7–3 in a match that underscored the Czech team's defensive solidity and counterattacking efficiency.[34] These outcomes propelled the winners into the Round of 16, while eliminated teams shifted to consolation brackets for ranking purposes. The round's structure emphasized direct elimination, with no second legs, amplifying the stakes for clubs vying for continental supremacy. Advancing sides included debutants and veterans alike, setting the stage for further upsets in subsequent knockout ties.[2]

Round of 16

The Round of 16 consisted of eight single-elimination matches played on 16 June 2023 in Nazaré, Portugal, reducing the field from 16 teams to the eight quarter-finalists.[28] Notable results included SC Braga's dominant 8–1 victory over Varzim SC.[28] Other encounters featured tight contests resolved by penalty shootouts, such as Artur Music facing ACD O Sótão, which ended 3–3 before penalties.[28] ![Flag of Portugal](./assets/Flag_of_Portugal_(official) Falfala KQ BSC advanced to the quarter-finals, where they defeated Marseille Beach Team 9–2, en route to the title.[35] Lenergy Pisa BS also progressed through this stage, ultimately reaching the final against Falfala KQ BSC after winning their subsequent knockout fixtures.[4] The outcomes highlighted the competitive nature of the knockout phase, with Portuguese, Italian, Israeli, and French clubs among the standout performers.[19]

Quarter-finals

The quarter-finals of the 2023 Euro Winners Cup were contested on 16 June 2023 at the Estádio do Viveiro in Nazaré, Portugal, featuring the eight surviving clubs from the round of 16 in single-elimination matches.[1] Falfala KQ BSC from Israel secured a decisive 9–2 victory over Minots de Marseille from France, with the Israeli side dominating possession and converting multiple opportunities in the third period.[36] In an all-Portuguese matchup, ACD O Sótão edged SC Braga 3–2, overcoming an early deficit through resilient defense and timely counterattacks.[28] Lenergy Pisa BS from Italy advanced past Atlas A.O. from Greece, maintaining their unbeaten knockout run up to that point.[37] Recreativo de Huelva from Spain progressed by defeating their quarter-final opponent, setting up intra-Spanish rivalry considerations later in the tournament though not realized in the semis.[38] The results positioned Falfala KQ BSC and Lenergy Pisa BS as semi-finalists alongside ACD O Sótão and Recreativo de Huelva, with the Israeli and Italian clubs ultimately reaching the final.[39]

Semi-finals

The semi-finals of the 2023 Euro Winners Cup were contested on 17 June 2023 in Nazaré, Portugal, determining the finalists from the quarter-final winners.[17][28] In the first match, Italy's Pisa defeated Portugal's ACD Sótão 3–2, securing advancement through a closely fought encounter that highlighted defensive resilience in the later periods.[17][28] Pisa's victory set up a final clash with the other semi-final winner, extending their unbeaten run in the knockout stages.[2] The second semi-final saw Israel's Falfala Kfar Qassem prevail 4–2 against Spain's Recreativo Huelva, with Kfar Qassem's attacking efficiency proving decisive in overcoming Huelva's early pressure.[17][28] This result marked Kfar Qassem's first appearance in the competition's final, propelled by strong contributions from key forwards.[2] The defeated teams, Sótão and Huelva, proceeded to the fifth-place matches.[28]

Third-place Match and Final

The third-place match, contested on 18 June 2023 at the main stadium in Nazaré, Portugal, pitted semi-final losers ACD O Sótão of Portugal against Recreativo Huelva of Spain. O Sótão secured bronze with a 3–2 victory, overcoming an early deficit through determined second-period play and defensive resilience in the closing stages.[40] The final, held later that day in the same venue, featured Lenergy Pisa BS of Italy versus Falfala Kfar Qassem BSC of Israel, the latter having advanced past Huelva 4–2 in the semi-finals while Pisa edged O Sótão 3–2. The match concluded 2–2 after regulation and extra time, with Falfala Kfar Qassem prevailing 5–3 in the penalty shootout to win their maiden Euro Winners Cup title; captain Amer Yatim converted the decisive kick after Pisa missed one of theirs.[4][19][41] This marked Israel's first success in the competition, highlighting the team's progression from group stage triumphs including a 5–3 quarter-final win over Naxos.[39]
