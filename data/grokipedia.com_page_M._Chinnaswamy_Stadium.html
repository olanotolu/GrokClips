M. Chinnaswamy Stadium

The M. Chinnaswamy Stadium , also known as the Karnataka State Cricket Association Stadium , is a cricket stadium in Bengaluru city of the Indian state of Karnataka.[3] The ground is owned by the Government of Karnataka and operated by the Karnataka State Cricket Association.[4] Flanked by the picturesque Cubbon Park, Queen's Road, Cubbon and uptown MG Road, this five-decade-old stadium is situated in the heart of the city of Bangalore. It has hosted Test, ODI, T20I and first-class cricket matches, as well as musical, cultural events. The stadium is the home ground of the Karnataka men's, Karnataka women's as well as franchises RCB men's and RCB women's teams.[5] It is owned by the Government of Karnataka and has been leased out to the KSCA for a period of 100 years. Formerly known as the Karnataka State Cricket Association Stadium, it was later rechristened in tribute to M. Chinnaswamy, a lawyer from Mandya and the founding member of the Mysore State Cricket Association.[6] He served the KSCA for four decades and was also president of the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) from 1977 to 1980, latter's National Cricket Academy also situated in the premises of this stadium. It is the first cricket stadium in the world to use solar panels to generate a bulk of the electricity needed to run the stadium.[7] The panels were procured through the "Go Green" initiative of the KSCA.[8][9] By January 2017 a sophisticated system of aeration and drainage was in place to avoid matches being curtailed due to a wet outfield.[10] In June 2025, a stampede outside the stadium during celebrations for Royal Challengers Bengaluru's IPL victory resulted in 11 deaths and over 50 injuries due to severe overcrowding, prompting safety concerns and the venue being deemed unfit for major events.[1] Consequently, five matches of the ICC Women's ODI World Cup 2025 were relocated to DY Patil Stadium in Navi Mumbai, and RCB may shift to a new venue for IPL 2026.[11][https://www.economictimes.com/news/new-updates/womens-cricket-world-cup-why-bengaluru-lost-world-cup-hosting-rights/articleshow/124229009.cms] The Karnataka government approved plans in October 2025 for a new 80,000-capacity multi-sport stadium on Bengaluru's outskirts to address these issues.[https://indianexpress.com/article/cities/bangalore/bengaluru-cricket-stadium-stampede-karnataka-new-stadium-10312508/]

Features

The Karnataka State Cricket Association describes the stadium as the world's first solar-powered cricket stadium. It was inspired by Germany's Freiburg football stadium, which is fully powered by solar energy. The KSCA initiated the â‚¹4.5 crore and 400 kW project in February 2015.[12] The venue has 40,000 seats.[13] Its ground is small, thus it has short boundaries; due to this, batting teams often score high totals here. It is evident by the fact that in the 2023 Indian Premier League edition, across five matches (ten innings), teams scored over 210 runs in four innings and over 170 runs in seven innings.[14] As per former Royal Challengers Bangalore bowler Harshal Patel, "It is hard to bowl at the Chinnaswamy. It is a small ground and the ball generally flies".[15]

Domestic Cricket

The arena is regular venue of domestic cricket. It is home ground of Karnataka cricket team. Since 2008, it is the home ground of IPL team Royal Challengers Bangalore. The first ever game of the IPL was held at the venue. In 1998-99 Ranji trophy final at the venue, Karnataka won against Madhya Pradesh (MP). In June 2022, 2021-22 edition final of the tournament MP won its maiden title by defeating Mumbai cricket team. 2022-23 Ranji semifinal held at the venue Saurashtra cricket team defeated Karnataka.[16][17] In the final of the 2019-20 Vijay Hazare trophy Karnataka defeated Tamil Nadu there. In October 2018, in the tournament's edition Mumbai won the final by defeating Delhi at the venue.[18][19]

Events

The Miss World 1996 beauty pageant was held at the stadium; it was the first time the event was organised in India. Irene Skliva of Greece became the winner.[20] On 4 June 2025, a crowd crush occurred outside the stadium during celebrations for Royal Challengers Bengaluru's IPL title win, resulting in 11 deaths and 56 injuries. The incident was caused by overcrowding after an announcement of free passes for a felicitation event. An investigation followed, leading to suspensions, arrests, and compensation for victims.[21]

Sustainability initiatives

The M. Chinnaswamy Stadium has adopted several sustainability initiatives to minimise its environmental impact.

Solar energy utilisation

The stadium is recognised for its utilisation of solar energy. A solar power plant was established, generating over six lakh units of electricity in the first year and reducing carbon dioxide emissions by 600 tonnes.[22] The solar panels, initially installed on the east end, plans to extend to the west end were announced in 2016 and 2022 to increase electricity generation, but as of 2024, the 400 kWp installation remains on the east side. In 2024, the solar plant saved approximately â‚¹7.2 million annually.

Water management

A comprehensive water management system is in place, which includes rainwater harvesting and a sewage treatment plant. The treatment plant recycles sewage water for turf watering, gardening, and landscaping. This initiative was developed in collaboration with the Bangalore Water Supply and Sewage Board (BWSSB).[24] For IPL 2025, the stadium began using treated wastewater from Cubbon Park for irrigation. A water purification plant, installed in 2016 with a capacity of 2.1 lakh litres daily, supports operations amid water scarcity.[26]

Subair drainage system

The 'subair drainage system' installed at the stadium in 2017 facilitates rapid water drainage, allowing the ground to dry within 15 minutes after heavy rains, thus minimising match interruptions due to wet conditions.[10] The system also provides aeration for healthier turf.

Other Eco-friendly measures

Recognition and replication

The sustainability initiatives at the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium have been noted, with some cricket associations in other states considering replicating these initiatives.[24] The eco-friendly projects, including the solar plant, were executed by the Karnataka State Cricket Association (KSCA).

Records and statistics

On 14 June 2018, after being granted 'Test Status' by the ICC in 2017, Afghanistan played their first ever test match at this venue against India.[28] The only T20I match in which double super over was played is in this stadium between India and Afghanistan on 17 January 2024 where Rohit Sharma scored his 5th century in T20I. This match is considered as the longest T20I match in world.
