Carahsoft

Carahsoft Technology Corp., founded in 2004 and headquartered in Reston, Virginia, is a privately held company serving as a master government aggregator and reseller of information technology solutions to public sector organizations, encompassing federal, state, and local governments, as well as education and healthcare markets.[1] The company distributes products, services, and training in areas including cybersecurity, multicloud, DevSecOps, artificial intelligence, and big data through partnerships with leading vendors and access to hundreds of government contract vehicles.[1][2] With over 2,000 employees, Carahsoft has expanded significantly as an intermediary in public sector IT procurement, securing $1.4 billion in federal contracts in fiscal year 2023.[1][3] Carahsoft's operations have drawn scrutiny, including a 2015 settlement requiring payment of $75.5 million with VMware to resolve allegations of violating the False Claims Act by concealing commercial pricing discounts in government sales.[4] In September 2024, the FBI raided its headquarters in connection with an investigation into a third-party entity with which Carahsoft had prior business dealings, and federal authorities have probed reseller arrangements such as a 2023 agreement with CrowdStrike intended for IRS cybersecurity tools that were never delivered to the agency.[5][6][7]

Company Overview

Founding and Headquarters

Carahsoft Technology Corp. was founded in 2004 by Craig P. Abod, who established the company to provide IT hardware, software, and services primarily to government entities.[8][9] Abod has served as president since inception, guiding the firm's focus on public sector markets including federal, state, local governments, education, and healthcare.[9][10] The company maintains its headquarters in Reston, Virginia, at 11493 Sunset Hills Road, Suite 100, Reston, Virginia 20190.[11][1] This location supports operations for over 2,000 employees dedicated to aggregating and distributing technology solutions.[1] While some records indicate incorporation under a prior name in 1999, Carahsoft's active business operations and branding commenced in 2004.[12]

Core Operations and Market Focus

Carahsoft Technology Corp. operates as a distributor and aggregator of information technology solutions, specializing in marketing and delivering software, hardware, and services to public sector clients. Founded in 2004, the company functions as a Master Government AggregatorÂ®, facilitating access to vendor technologies through established procurement contracts, including GSA Schedules and NASA SEWP vehicles. Its core activities encompass sourcing, reselling, and implementing IT products in domains such as cybersecurity, multicloud computing, DevSecOps, artificial intelligence, big data analytics, open source software, and customer experience platforms, often in partnership with resellers, systems integrators, and consultants.[1][13][14] The company's operations emphasize compliance with government acquisition regulations, enabling efficient procurement for mission-critical IT needs while providing training and support services. With over 2,000 employees based in Reston, Virginia, Carahsoft maintains a vast portfolio of vendor partnerships to aggregate solutions tailored for secure, scalable deployments in regulated environments. This model positions it as an intermediary that simplifies complex IT acquisitions, focusing on technologies that enhance operational efficiency and data management for public entities.[1][2] Carahsoft's primary market focus is the North American public sector, particularly in the United States and Canada, targeting federal agencies, the Department of Defense, state and local governments, educational institutions, and healthcare organizations. It supports IT modernization in these areas by addressing sector-specific challenges, such as secure data handling in defense and compliance-driven systems in healthcare. Business Model

Role as IT Aggregator and Reseller

Carahsoft Technology Corp. operates primarily as a master government aggregator and value-added reseller (VAR) in the public sector IT market, facilitating the distribution of software, hardware, and services from third-party vendors to federal, state, local governments, and educational institutions.[1] In this capacity, the company aggregates solutions from over 2,500 technology vendors, enabling streamlined access through its extensive portfolio of government contract vehicles, such as GSA Schedules and NASA SEWP, without developing proprietary products itself.[2] This aggregation model simplifies procurement for public sector buyers by consolidating vendor offerings under pre-negotiated terms, reducing administrative burdens and compliance risks associated with direct vendor negotiations.[17] As a reseller, Carahsoft adds value beyond mere distribution by providing sales enablement, marketing support, technical consulting, and post-sales services to both vendors and end-users, fostering an ecosystem that includes more than 10,000 channel partners such as system integrators and managed service providers (MSPs).[18] The company's reseller role emphasizes compliance with federal acquisition regulations, leveraging its status as a top-ranked GSA Schedule holder to secure awards totaling $1.4 billion in federal contracts in fiscal year 2023 and over $960 million in the first half of fiscal 2024.[3] Vendor partnerships designate Carahsoft as the exclusive or primary aggregator for public sector sales, as seen in agreements with entities like HashiCorp in 2020 and Canto in September 2025, where it handles market entry, demand generation, and fulfillment.[19][20] This dual role positions Carahsoft as an intermediary that mitigates vendor risks in government sales, such as lengthy approval processes and stringent cybersecurity requirements, while generating revenue through margins on resold products and fees for aggregation services.[13] Critics, including federal oversight bodies, have scrutinized resellers like Carahsoft for potential over-reliance on pass-through contracts, prompting expanded GSA reviews of VAR practices in 2025 to ensure value addition beyond basic resale.[21] Nonetheless, the model has driven consistent growth, with Carahsoft recognized as a Value Added Reseller of the Year by partners like Leidos for enhancing public sector access to specialized IT solutions.[22]

Procurement Vehicles and Contract Mechanisms

Carahsoft Technology Corp. serves as a master aggregator for public sector IT procurement, leveraging pre-negotiated contract vehicles to enable government agencies, educational institutions, and state/local entities to acquire software, hardware, and services efficiently without initiating lengthy competitive bidding processes. These mechanisms, such as indefinite delivery/indefinite quantity (IDIQ) contracts, multiple award schedules, and cooperative purchasing agreements, comply with Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) requirements and provide fixed pricing, volume discounts, and streamlined ordering procedures.[23] By holding these vehicles directly or as a reseller, Carahsoft reduces procurement timelines from months to days, facilitates compliance with cybersecurity mandates like FedRAMP, and aggregates offerings from over 2,000 vendors.[24] At the federal level, Carahsoft maintains the NASA Solutions for Enterprise-Wide Procurement (SEWP) V contract (NNG15SC03B), a government-wide acquisition contract (GWAC) administered by NASA that supports IT solutions including cloud services, cybersecurity tools, and enterprise software for civilian, defense, and intelligence agencies. This vehicle, effective from 2015 through at least 2025 with options for extension, allows orders up to $20 billion in ceiling value across holders and emphasizes agile procurement for emerging technologies.[25] Carahsoft also participates in the GSA Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) under IT Schedule 70, which covers information technology products and services with special item numbers (SINs) for categories like cybersecurity, cloud computing, and data analytics, enabling federal buyers to leverage competitive pricing negotiated under GSA's oversight.[23] Additionally, Carahsoft holds the Second Generation Information Technology (2GIT) Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA) (N66001-19-A) through the Department of Defense, facilitating rapid acquisition of IT hardware and software for military and defense-related needs.[24] For state and local governments (SLG) and education (EDU), Carahsoft utilizes cooperative contracts such as NASPO ValuePoint, a multi-state contracting alliance that provides master agreements for IT commodities like software licensing and hardware, accessible to over 50 participating states and localities.[26] The GSA Cooperative Purchasing Program extends federal schedules to SLG and EDU buyers for eligible IT categories, while Carahsoft maintains technology-specific and regional contracts, including those with entities like the New Jersey Schools Boards Association (NJSBA) and various city/county agreements, to address diverse jurisdictional procurement rules.[27] These vehicles collectively support fiscal year-end spending surges by offering pre-vetted terms and reducing sole-source justification needs.[26]

Vendor Partnerships and Technology Portfolio

Key Technology Categories

Carahsoft's technology portfolio centers on core categories essential for public sector IT modernization, including cybersecurity, multicloud infrastructure, DevSecOps, big data analytics, artificial intelligence (AI), and open source software. These areas enable government agencies to address mission-critical needs such as data protection, scalable computing, secure development pipelines, advanced analytics, intelligent automation, and cost-effective software deployment.[1][28] Cybersecurity forms a foundational category, encompassing tools for threat detection, zero trust architectures, and compliance with standards like Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC). Carahsoft aggregates vendor solutions to safeguard sensitive government data against evolving cyber threats, supporting federal and state entities in maintaining operational resilience.[28][1] Multicloud solutions facilitate hybrid and multi-provider cloud environments, allowing agencies to optimize workloads across platforms while ensuring FedRAMP authorization for cloud services. This category addresses interoperability challenges, enabling seamless data migration and resource allocation without vendor lock-in.[28][1] In DevSecOps, Carahsoft promotes integrated platforms that embed security into development and operations workflows, accelerating software delivery for government applications. These tools support continuous integration, automated testing, and compliance auditing, reducing deployment risks in agile environments.[28][1] Big data and analytics offerings focus on processing vast datasets for intelligence gathering and decision-making, including high-performance computing (HPC) integrations. Carahsoft's portfolio aids agencies in extracting actionable insights from structured and unstructured data sources.[1] Artificial intelligence and machine learning capabilities provide predictive modeling, automation, and natural language processing tailored for government use cases, such as fraud detection and resource optimization. These solutions leverage best-of-breed vendors to enhance efficiency without compromising ethical AI deployment.[29][1] Open source software rounds out the categories by offering flexible, community-vetted alternatives for infrastructure, databases, and applications, often customized for enterprise-scale government operations to minimize licensing costs and foster innovation.[30][1] Additional emerging categories, such as 5G connectivity and geospatial technologies, extend the portfolio to support specialized needs like mobile edge computing and location intelligence.[28]

Major Vendor Collaborations

Carahsoft Technology Corp. maintains partnerships with over 300 enterprise software vendors, serving as a primary channel for delivering IT solutions to public sector organizations through its role as a master government aggregator and reseller.[31] These collaborations enable vendors to leverage Carahsoft's established procurement contracts, such as GSA Schedules and NASA Solutions for Enterprise-Wide Procurement (SEWP), to reach federal, state, local, and educational customers without direct sales infrastructure in government markets.[32] The partnerships emphasize accelerating market entry, managing reseller networks, and providing demand-generation services tailored to compliance-heavy environments like FedRAMP-authorized cloud offerings.[32] In cybersecurity, Carahsoft's major vendor collaborations include Palo Alto Networks, CrowdStrike, CyberArk, Proofpoint, Rapid7, Sophos, and Tenable, focusing on endpoint protection, identity management, and vulnerability assessment tools optimized for government threat landscapes.[31] For instance, these partnerships facilitate the distribution of next-generation firewalls and endpoint detection platforms via Carahsoft's contracts, supporting agencies in addressing evolving cyber risks without bespoke vendor-government negotiations.[31] Cloud computing partnerships represent a cornerstone, with flagship agreements alongside Amazon Web Services (AWS), Microsoft, Google Cloud, Oracle, and Salesforce enabling scalable infrastructure, data storage, and SaaS deployments.[31] Carahsoft acts as AWS's primary public sector distributor, integrating solutions like AWS Marketplace into federal procurement streams since at least 2010, while similar arrangements with Microsoft Azure emphasize hybrid cloud migrations for defense and civilian agencies.[31] Oracle collaborations extend to database and ERP systems, and Salesforce ties support CRM customizations compliant with FISMA standards.[31] Emerging technology collaborations highlight AI and quantum advancements, including NVIDIA for GPU-accelerated computing, OpenAI and Anthropic for generative models, and specialized deals like D-Wave's January 2025 partnership for annealing quantum optimization tools aimed at public sector logistics challenges.[33][31] Cerebras joined via a September 2025 agreement to supply wafer-scale AI engines through the Department of Defense's Tradewinds marketplace.[34] Networking and data analytics vendors such as Cisco, Juniper Networks, Arista, Databricks, Cloudera, and Splunk bolster Carahsoft's portfolio for secure connectivity and big data processing.[31] Red Hat designates Carahsoft as its Master Government Aggregator, streamlining open-source modernization for IT infrastructure.[35] Intel recognized Carahsoft as its 2025 Solution Provider Marketing Partner of the Year for joint campaigns promoting edge computing in government settings.[36] These alliances prioritize vendor-neutral aggregation, though Carahsoft's selection of partners reflects a focus on scalable, compliant technologies over niche or unproven offerings.[32]

Major Contracts and Revenue Streams

U.S. Department of Defense Engagements

Carahsoft Technology Corp. facilitates the U.S. Department of Defense's (DoD) acquisition of commercial IT solutions primarily through blanket purchase agreements (BPAs) under the DoD Enterprise Software Initiative (ESI) and other vehicles, enabling DoD components to procure software, services, and maintenance without repetitive negotiations per DFARS 208.74.[37] These engagements position Carahsoft as a reseller for vendors including SAP, Appian, ServiceNow, and others, supporting cybersecurity, enterprise resource planning, and operational tools across Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, and Intelligence Community needs.[38] A foundational contract is the ESI BPA N66001-19-A-0010, awarded on March 18, 2019, with a performance period through March 17, 2026, specifically for SAP enterprise software, training, technical services, and maintenance via an Enterprise Software Agreement.[37] Carahsoft also holds subsequent ESI BPAs, such as N66001-24-A-0060, which extend access to additional vendor software for DoD-wide use.[39] Complementing these, the 2nd Generation Information Technology (2GIT) BPA, numbered N66001-19-A-0120, provides another avenue for DoD IT procurements through at least July 14, 2024, with potential extensions.[38] Notable task orders and awards under these frameworks include a multiple-award, firm-fixed-price BPA on September 10, 2024, valued at $145 million for Appian low-code automation platform licenses and support, shared among Carahsoft, Groundswell, and TD Synnex.[40] On September 30, 2025, the Department of the Air Force awarded Carahsoft a $510 million contract for ServiceNow enterprise software tools, encompassing the Government Transformation Suite for workflow automation and IT service management.[41] Earlier, on May 5, 2023, Carahsoft secured a five-year, $29 million ESI BPA for CollabNet agile software development tools and services.[42] Additionally, on January 22, 2025, Carahsoft received a $44,985,868 firm-fixed-price contract for commercial off-the-shelf software licenses and technical support, administered by a DoD contracting activity.[43] These engagements underscore Carahsoft's role in aggregating vendor portfolios to meet DoD's ESI goals of cost-effective, rapid software acquisition, though actual obligations vary by task order and fiscal year allocations as reported in federal spending databases.[24]

U.S. General Services Administration Agreements

Carahsoft Technology Corp. maintains a Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) contract with the U.S. General Services Administration (GSA), designated as 47QSWA18D008F, awarded on August 22, 2018.[44] This indefinite delivery, indefinite quantity agreement covers a broad range of information technology products, services, and solutions, including hardware, software, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and professional services under various Special Item Numbers (SINs) such as 334111 (electronic computers), 518210C (cloud computing and cloud related IT professional services), and 54151S (IT professional services).[45] The contract's current option period extends to August 21, 2028, with potential for renewal up to an ultimate completion date of August 21, 2038, enabling federal agencies to procure commercial-off-the-shelf IT offerings through Carahsoft as an aggregator and reseller.[45] [44] This MAS contract succeeded Carahsoft's prior GSA Schedule 70 agreement, GS-35F-0119Y, which was active from December 20, 2011, to December 19, 2026, and similarly focused on IT acquisitions.[46] Under the MAS framework, Carahsoft facilitates streamlined procurement for federal, state, local, and tribal governments by offering pre-negotiated pricing and terms compliant with the Federal Acquisition Regulation, reducing administrative burdens and enabling faster acquisition cycles for vendor-agnostic IT solutions.[45] The agreement supports volume discounts and is accessible via GSA Advantage, with orders directed to Carahsoft's contracts team.[45] A notable application of Carahsoft's GSA MAS involved a governmentwide enterprise license agreement for ServiceNow software, negotiated by GSA on February 23, 2018, under the IT Schedule 70 FITARA Enhancement Program.[47] This deal, executed through Carahsoft's then-active GS-35F-0119Y contract, provided tiered discounts of 4% to 10% based on purchase volume for IT service management, cybersecurity operations, and related modules, projecting millions in savings over five years with moderate adoption across on-premise, off-premise, or hybrid deployments.[47] The arrangement aimed to consolidate duplicative procurements and align with federal IT mandates, extending availability to non-federal public sector entities.[47]

Other Federal and Public Sector Contracts

Carahsoft Technology Corp. serves as a primary aggregator for IT solutions under the NASA Solutions for Enterprise-Wide Procurement (SEWP) V contracts, which facilitate government-wide acquisitions of commercial IT products and services by federal civilian agencies. The company holds Group A (contract NNG15SC03B) and Group D (contract NNG15SC27B) awards, structured as indefinite-delivery/indefinite-quantity vehicles with a base period from February 1, 2015, extended through options to October 31, 2025.[48][49] These contracts, managed by NASA but accessible to all federal entities, have supported procurements such as those by the Department of Health and Human Services, emphasizing hardware, software, and cloud-based offerings from Carahsoft's vendor partners.[50] Beyond NASA vehicles, Carahsoft maintains direct engagements with other federal civilian departments, including the Department of Homeland Security (DHS). In one instance, Carahsoft secured a blanket purchase agreement with DHS for Imation encrypted USB portable storage devices, enabling flexible data handling for agency operations.[51] Additional DHS task orders, often routed through broader schedules, include a 2021 award valued under GSA mechanisms but executed for DHS cybersecurity needs, and a 2022 indefinite-delivery contract for IT services.[52][53] These arrangements underscore Carahsoft's role in delivering specialized hardware and software to non-defense federal missions, with obligations tracked via federal spending databases reflecting ongoing utilization. In the broader public sector, Carahsoft supports state and local governments through dedicated master agreements and cooperative contracts tailored to subnational procurement rules. The company holds state-specific vehicles in jurisdictions such as Pennsylvania, Florida, Georgia, Alaska, and Mississippi, enabling streamlined access to IT categories like cybersecurity and data management.[27] These contracts often align with fiscal year cycles and include options like Texas DIR schedules, allowing local entities to leverage Carahsoft's aggregated vendor portfolio without individual negotiations.[23] Such mechanisms have expanded Carahsoft's footprint beyond federal levels, with state and local awards contributing to its overall public sector revenue by simplifying compliance with varying regional regulations.[54]

Growth and Achievements

Financial Expansion and Rankings

Carahsoft has exhibited robust revenue growth, increasing from $6.6 billion in 2019 to over $8 billion in 2020 and surpassing $16 billion by 2023.[55][56][57] This expansion aligns with a three-year growth rate of 27 percent from 2020 to 2023, driven primarily by scaled government contract awards and vendor partnerships.[57] The company's private status limits public disclosure of detailed financials, but self-reported figures and third-party validations underscore sustained scaling in public sector IT distribution.[9] This financial trajectory has earned Carahsoft repeated recognition on major rankings of private company performance. As of 2024, it appeared on the Inc. 5000 list of fastest-growing private U.S. companies for the 17th consecutive year, with prior rankings including #4,731 in 2023 (reflecting 84 percent growth from 2019 to 2022) and #3,863 in 2020.[57][58][59] In 2023, Forbes ranked it #45 among America's largest private companies, highlighting its position among top revenue generators.[60] Locally, the Washington Business Journal placed it third on its 2023 list of largest private companies in Greater Washington, based on reported revenue and employee growth to over 3,400 staff.[61] Further affirming its expansion, Carahsoft climbed to #25 on Washington Technology's 2025 Top 100 list of government contractors, up from #30 in 2024 and a 74-place rise since 2010, measured by prime contract values.[62] These rankings, derived from revenue and contract data submitted to or verified by the respective outlets, reflect operational scaling through procurement vehicle utilization rather than external funding or mergers.

Carahsoft Technology Corp. has been recognized multiple times for its rapid growth, earning a spot on the Inc. 5000 list of fastest-growing private companies in America for the 17th consecutive year in 2024, with continued inclusion as a 2025 honoree.[57][63] The company was also selected as a winner of Inc.'s third annual Power Partner Awards in 2024, highlighting its role in supporting business ecosystems.[64] In sales and channel leadership, Carahsoft was named a 2024 Premier Sales Employer by the Institute of Excellence in Sales, acknowledging its sales training and development programs.[65] Nine executives were named to CRN's 2025 Channel Chiefs list for their influence in IT channel strategies, following a similar recognition for nine in 2024.[66][67] Additionally, the National Association of State Procurement Officials (NASPO) awarded Carahsoft a 2024 Top Sales Achievement Award for its contributions to cooperative purchasing.[68] Partnership-specific recognitions include being named Public Sector Partner of the Year for 2025 by Samsara for expanding its connected operations platform in government markets.[69] Carahsoft received a supplier award from Leidos in 2025 for performance in defense and government IT supply chains.[70] It was also honored in the 2024 Splunk Regional Partner Awards for innovation and joint success in public sector deployments, and as Liferay's 2023 Partner of the Year (announced in 2024) for digital experience solutions.[71][72] Earlier, Zoom awarded Carahsoft its 2023 US and Canada Marketing Innovation Partner Award.[2]

Controversies and Legal Challenges

Historical Overcharging Allegations

In June 2015, Carahsoft Technology Corporation and VMware Inc. agreed to pay a total of $75.5 million to resolve allegations that they violated the False Claims Act by concealing commercial pricing practices and overcharging the U.S. government on sales of VMware software and services.[4] The settlement addressed claims spanning from 2007 to 2013, during which Carahsoft held a General Services Administration (GSA) Multiple Award Schedule (MAS) contract facilitating purchases by federal, state, and local government entities.[73] The core allegations centered on misrepresentations made to the GSA during and after MAS contract negotiations, where Carahsoft and VMware allegedly failed to disclose standard and non-standard discounts routinely provided to commercial customers, resulting in inflated prices charged under the government schedule.[73] Under GSA regulations, vendors are required to provide accurate and complete disclosures of commercial pricing to ensure government buyers receive prices no higher than those available in the commercial market, a provision intended to prevent overcharging through the MAS program.[4] The qui tam lawsuit initiating the case was filed in 2010 by whistleblower Dane Smith in the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Virginia (Case No. 10-CV-769).[4] Neither Carahsoft nor VMware admitted liability or wrongdoing as part of the settlement, which the Department of Justice described as resolving the claims without a determination of fault to prioritize taxpayer protection through fair pricing enforcement.[4] The resolution followed intervention by the United States after the whistleblower's initial filing, with funds directed toward compensating affected government entities for the alleged overcharges.[73] This case highlighted ongoing scrutiny of resellers like Carahsoft in ensuring compliance with pricing disclosure rules in federal procurement schedules.[4]

Recent Federal Investigations

On September 24, 2024, the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) and the Defense Criminal Investigative Service (DCIS) executed a search warrant at Carahsoft Technology Corp.'s headquarters in Reston, Virginia.[74][75] Carahsoft issued a statement indicating that Department of Justice (DOJ) representatives visited the office to investigate a company with which Carahsoft had previously conducted business, emphasizing that the probe did not target Carahsoft directly, no employees were arrested or detained, and the firm was cooperating fully.[74][75] The specific nature of the third-party entity under scrutiny remains undisclosed in public reports.[3] The raid coincided with ongoing DOJ scrutiny of Carahsoft's vendor relationships, including a civil investigation launched no later than 2022 into potential price-fixing with SAP SE.[76] This probe examines allegations that SAP and Carahsoft conspired to inflate prices on software and hardware sold to U.S. government agencies, prompting DOJ subpoenas for communications related to those sales.[76][77] SAP executives have asserted that the September 2024 raid was unrelated to their interactions with Carahsoft or the DOJ's civil investigative demands.[78] Separately, reports indicate FBI involvement in examining similar overcharging claims involving Carahsoft and other vendors like ServiceNow.[79] In February 2025, DOJ and Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) officials initiated a probe into a $32 million agreement between CrowdStrike Holdings Inc. and Carahsoft to supply cybersecurity tools for the Internal Revenue Service (IRS).[80][7] The investigation focuses on the deal's structure, revenue recognition practices, and potential involvement of CrowdStrike executives, amid concerns over compliance with federal contracting standards.[80][81] Some analyses link this inquiry to broader regulatory attention on reseller transactions following the prior year's raid, though direct causation remains unconfirmed.[82] These developments highlight intensified federal oversight of government IT resellers, potentially signaling risks to revenue models reliant on agency procurement vehicles.[3]
