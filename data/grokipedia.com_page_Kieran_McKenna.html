Kieran McKenna

Kieran McKenna (born 14 May 1986) is a professional football manager and former player who is the manager of EFL Championship club Ipswich Town. Born in England, McKenna was raised in Northern Ireland and played youth football for Enniskillen Town United and Ballinamallard United before joining Tottenham Hotspur in 2002, and had represented Northern Ireland at youth level before a hip injury curtailed his playing career. After studying sports science at Loughborough University, McKenna coached Tottenham Hotspur and Manchester United at under-18 level, and also worked as a first-team coach and assistant manager at Manchester United before being appointed as manager of Ipswich Town in 2021. Under his management, Ipswich achieved consecutive promotions to the Premier League in 2024 before being relegated at the end of the 2024â€“25 season.[1][2][3]

Early life

McKenna was born in London,[5] and was raised in the Northern Ireland county of Fermanagh.[6]

Playing career

McKenna began his footballing career as a youth team player at Northern Irish sides Enniskillen Town United and Ballinamallard United.[7][4] He joined Tottenham Hotspur as a youth player in 2002, after signing a scholarship contract with Spurs.[8] He progressed through the youth ranks to the reserve team, which he captained by 2007.[4] McKenna represented Northern Ireland at under-19 and under-21 level, earning 5 caps (0 goals) for the U19s between 2004 and 2005, and 7 caps (1 goal) for the U21s between 2005 and 2007.[7] He was made captain of Northern Ireland's under-19 side at the 2005 UEFA European Under-19 Championship by coach Mal Donaghy.[8] In 2009, at the age of 22, McKenna retired from playing due to an ongoing hip injury which prevented him from making a senior first-team appearance for Tottenham.[7]

Coaching career

Early roles and development

After retiring from playing, McKenna started his career in coaching while studying a sports science degree at Loughborough University. During his time studying, McKenna spent time as a youth coach at Tottenham Hotspur, Leicester City, Nottingham Forest, and Canadian side Vancouver Whitecaps.

Tottenham Hotspur

Upon graduating from Loughborough University, McKenna was hired as the Head of Academy Performance Analysis at Tottenham Hotspur.[9] He was offered the role of Academy Coach at Liverpool,[7] but remained at Spurs, coaching various age groups at the Tottenham Academy set-up, before being placed in charge of Tottenham Hotspur's under-18s side.[10] During his tenure at Tottenham, McKenna guided the under-18s to the semi-final of the FA Youth Cup in 2015.[7]

Manchester United

In August 2016, McKenna left his role at Tottenham to join the academy set-up at Manchester United, becoming the club's under-18s manager.[11] In his second season in charge of the under-18s, McKenna guided Manchester United to the Premier League Northern Division title.[7] Along with former United player Michael Carrick, McKenna was promoted to the first-team coaching staff and replaced Rui Faria as JosÃ© Mourinho's assistant manager, ahead of the 2018â€“19 Premier League season.[12][13] Following a poor start to the 2018â€“19 season, Mourinho left United on 18 December 2018, and was replaced the next day by former United striker Ole Gunnar SolskjÃ¦r, who was appointed as caretaker manager,[14][15] and retained both McKenna and Carrick in their coaching positions.[15] McKenna was retained in his role by interim manager Ralf Rangnick following SolskjÃ¦r's departure as United manager in November 2021.[9]

Reception

Following McKenna's arrival as Manchester United's under-18s manager, Indy Boonen, who was a teenage player for United at the time, praised McKenna by stating, "He changed everything. The way we trained was how the opponent played on the Saturday. If you played against West Brom, you trained how they are and focused on their weaknesses."[13] Jim Magilton, the Irish Football Association's elite performance director at the time, agreed with Boonen's appraisal of McKenna commenting, "He is a meticulous planner yet every session is spontaneous. Nothing is set in stone. He adjusts the session to how the players are and gets what he wants out of the session. Everything is linked and game related. Nothing is for show â€“ it has to be about the game."[7] McKenna's managerial tenure at Ipswich Town has received widespread acclaim for achieving consecutive promotions from EFL League One to the Premier League in 2023 and 2024, a feat not accomplished by the club since 1961.[36] He was named EFL Manager of the Month three times in 2023 (March, April, October) and twice in 2024 (March, April),[21][22][24][25][37][38] as well as EFL Championship Manager of the Season for 2023â€“24.[26] In May 2024, McKenna won the League Managers Association Manager of the Year award, ahead of Manchester City's Pep Guardiola and Arsenal's Mikel Arteta.[39] Despite Ipswich's relegation from the Premier League in April 2025 after one season, McKenna's overall impact has been positively received, with praise for the club's attacking style and youth development.[27] In October 2024, the University of Suffolk awarded him an honorary degree in recognition of his contributions to football.[40]

Personal life

McKenna is married and has two children.[41] Since his childhood, McKenna has been a supporter of Manchester United, with reports claiming that his love for the club influenced his decision to leave Tottenham Hotspur for the Red Devils in 2016.[7] McKenna also played Gaelic football for the minors of the Enniskillen Gaels club.[42] In 2024, McKenna was given a Honorary Doctorate by the University of Suffolk.[40]
