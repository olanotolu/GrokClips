Al Ahly (basketball)

Al Ahly is an Egyptian basketball club of Al Ahly SC, located in Cairo, that plays in the Egypt Basketball Super League. Several of its players have represented the Egypt men's national basketball team. The team is a part of the multi-sports club of the same name. The club has won eight Egyptian Super League titles, including in 2024–25, and eleven Egyptian Cup titles. Internationally, Al Ahly has won the FIBA Africa Champions Cup in 2016, the BAL in 2023, and the Arab Club Basketball Championship in 2021.

History

Ahly Basketball team was founded in 1930. It is one of the oldest basketball clubs in Egypt and in Africa.[1] The team played its first game against Al Ittihad Alexandria in front of the Egyptian King Farouk in 1937. Al Ahly won its first Egyptian national championship in the 1988–89 season. Prior to their first Super League title, Al Ahly achieved success in the Egyptian Mortabat League, winning 10 titles starting from the 2005–06 season.[2][3] In July 2021, Al Ahly signed Spanish coach Augustí Julbe as their new head coach.[4] In the following 2021–22 season, Ahly won its sixth Premier League title and thus qualified for the 2023 season of the Basketball Africa League (BAL). In the following season, Al Ahly made their debut in the BAL and had a 4–1 record in the Nile Conference, which was hosted in Cairo. In the playoffs, Al Ahly defeated REG and Stade Malien in the quarter- and semi-finals. In the final, Al Ahly defeated the Senegalese champions AS Douanes 80–65, to win their second continental title and first BAL title.[5] Al Ahly forward Nuni Omot was named the league MVP.[5] In the 2022–23 domestic season, Al Ahly won the double once again under Julbe. In the following season, Al Ahly represented Africa in the 2023 FIBA Intercontinental Cup in Singapore. On 21 September 2023, Al Ahly became the first African club to win a game in the competition's 33-year existence, following a win over the NBA G League Ignite.[6] In the 2023–24 season, Al Ahly won the Nile Conference in the 2024 BAL but were eliminated in the quarterfinals by Al Ahly Ly of Libya. They also secured their seventh Super League title. In the 2024–25 season, Al Ahly won their eighth Egyptian Super League title and the Egypt Cup. In July 2025, the club appointed Cypriot-Greek coach Linos Gavriel as head coach, replacing Augustí Julbe.[7][8]

2016 FIBA Africa Clubs Champions Cup

The 2016 FIBA Africa Clubs Champions Cup (31st edition) was an international basketball tournament held in Cairo, Egypt from 7 to 16 December 2016. The tournament, organized by FIBA Africa and hosted by Al Ahly, was contested by 10 clubs split into 2 groups of five, the top four of each group qualifying for the knock-out stage, quarter, semi-finals, and final. Al Ahly won the trophy, securing their first title in the competition. This was Al Ahly's first FIBA Africa Clubs Champions Cup title, bringing their total African club championships to three (including two African Basketball Cup Winners' Cups in 1998 and 2001), making them the most successful Egyptian basketball club in continental competitions at the time.[41][42][43][36]

Al Ahly Sports Hall

Al Ahly Sports Hall, officially known as Prince Abdullah Al-Faisal Hall, is the home arena for the club's basketball, handball, and volleyball teams.[58] It has a capacity of 2,500 spectators. As Al Ahly developed its teams in handball, basketball, and volleyball, the club recognized the need for a dedicated indoor arena. Planning and funding efforts began in 1978 but were delayed due to financial constraints. The hall finally opened on 4 February 1994. The inaugural event included a friendly basketball match between Al Ahly and Ittihad Alexandria, followed by a futsal game featuring retired Al Ahly players against those from Al-Ismaili, including notable figures such as Mahmoud El Khatib and Ali Abu Greisha.
