City-class mine countermeasures vessel

Replacement Mine Countermeasures Vessels

The Netherlands and Belgium are pursuing a joint procurement under the replacement Mine Countermeasures (rMCM) program to replace the Tripartite-class/Alkmaar-class minehunters. Each country is acquiring six new mine countermeasure (MCM) vessels, totaling 12 ships.[1] Developed by Naval Group, the vessels incorporate unmanned surface, aerial, and underwater vehicles, towed sonars, and remotely operated vehicles for mine identification and neutralization.[2] The program has faced delays, with the first vessel, Oostende, completing sea trials in July 2025 and scheduled for delivery in late October 2025.[3]

Design and development

In early 2018, the Belgian and Dutch Navies signed a Memorandum of Understanding for the joint construction and financing of the ships. A list of requirements was drawn up for the new vessels. Contenders in the race were:
  • The Franco-Belgian consortium Sea Naval Solutions, made up of French shipbuilders STX France and Socarenam, together with Belgium's EDR (and Thales),[20][21] bid for the 12 new MCM vessels. Their plan included the construction of MCM vessels and a multi-role mothership named Deviceseas. All ships would have a strong focus on autonomous systems operations.[20]
  • France's Naval Group and ECA Robotics established the Belgian subsidiary Belgium Naval & Robotics and bid for the program.[22][21]
  • Imtech Belgium and Damen Group bid for the program.[22][21]
The contract was won by Naval Group and ECA Robotics on 15 March 2019.[22][21] The mine countermeasures vessels have a length of 82 meters and displace 2,800 tonnes.[23] The new vessels take a new approach to their task: instead of clearing mines directly from the ship, the ship uses unmanned systems to do this from a distance.[24] There are a total of around 80 unmanned systems aboard the vessels.[25]

Construction

A first steel cutting ceremony for the first-in-class ship for the Belgian Navy was held on 19 July 2021, with the keel being laid on 30 November 2021. Delivery of the first ship to the Belgian Navy was initially anticipated in 2024 and to the Royal Netherlands Navy in 2025.[26] The first ship for the Royal Netherlands Navy started on 4 March 2022, with a steel cutting ceremony. The keel was laid on 14 June 2022.[27] On 8 May 2024, the Belgian Ministry of Defence announced that contractor Belgium Naval & Robotics requested a delay to the construction of the first four vessels citing unforeseen circumstances. The delay caused the first vessel, Oostende, to be delivered eight months later than initially planned, pushing her commissioning back to 2025.[28] The second vessel, Vlissingen, is to be five-to-six months delayed, with Tournai and Scheveningen also seeing delays of two and one month(s) respectively.[29] Sea trials for Oostende began in July 2024 and were completed in July 2025, with delivery scheduled for late October 2025.[3][30] Sea trials for Vlissingen began in March 2025, with delivery expected in December 2025.[31] Operational qualification of the drone toolbox began in early 2025, as planned.[3] Additional construction milestones include the keel laying for the fourth Belgian vessel, LiÃ¨ge, in January 2025 and the launch of the fourth vessel overall in November 2024.[32][33]

Equipment

Mission bay

The deployment system for the unmanned surface vessel was developed by Naval Group.[34] The mission bay is to be equipped with the "Launch and recovery system" (LARS).[35]

Drones

Surface vessels

The Exail Inspector 125 unmanned surface vessel was ordered to be a centerpiece of the system. It has a low signature (magnetic and acoustic) which would prevent a mine from being triggered. The vessel is also designed to be unsinkable.[19] Its mission is focused on communications and deployment of the drones.[4] This vessel can be deployed from the mission bay by a handling system.[35]

Operators

Future operators

Belgium (6)
Belgium gave the green-light to start the procurement on 26 January 2018 and approved a budget of 1.1 billion euros for the six Belgian MCM ships. Besides the Tripartite-class minehunters, the ships will also replace the Belgian logistical support ship Godetia. The first vessel, Oostende, is scheduled for delivery in October 2025, with the remaining vessels to follow in a staggered schedule until mid-2030.[38] [39] Netherlands (6)
Contract for the MCM vessels in collaboration with Belgium in 2019, critical design review passed in June 2020. The first vessel, Vlissingen, is scheduled for delivery by the end of 2025, with the remaining vessels to follow until mid-2030.[40] [41]

Potential operators

France
At Euronaval 2022, a partnership was signed between the Belgian, Dutch and French navies to enhance cooperation. In February 2025, Belgium, the Netherlands, and France signed a sub-license agreement granting France rights to adapt the rMCM vessel design for its SLAM-F mine warfare program, with the first order now scheduled for 2025. As a result, the French Navy intends to order six vessels based on the City-class design to fulfill the role of BDGM motherships.[42][43] [44] [45]
