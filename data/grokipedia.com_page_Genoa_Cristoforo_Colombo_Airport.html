Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport

Genoa Christopher Columbus Airport (IATA: GOA , ICAO: LIMJ) — commonly Genoa-Sestri Ponente Airport after the city district where it is located — is an international airport built on an artificial peninsula, 4 NM (7.4 km; 4.6 mi) west[1] of Genoa, Italy.

Overview

The airport began construction in 1954 and opened in 1962, at a cost of 12.8 billion lira. Building an offshore airport was not a strange or unique solution only for Genoa. Among the most conspicuous examples are other airports in Nice, Venice, Gibraltar, or [Hong Kong](Hong Kong "Hong Kong"). The current terminal building was opened in 1986. It is the most important airport in Liguria and it serves the city and Port of Genoa, as well as a considerable population in Southern Piedmont (Asti and Alessandria Provinces, southern areas of Cuneo Province). In 2018, with 1,455,626 passengers having passed through the airport,[2] Genoa was the 21st busiest Italian airport by passenger traffic. It is currently operated by Aeroporto di Genova S.P.A., which completed expansion and renovation of the passenger terminal in 2024, including the steel structure and floor slabs.[3] In 2024, the airport also added a new 2,200-square-meter warehouse as part of infrastructure improvements.[4] The airport is named after the notable Genoese navigator and explorer Christopher Columbus, who is recalled in the monument La Vela di Colombo.[5][6] The airport was the manufacturing base for Piaggio Aerospace, an Italian aircraft design and production company. In January 2022, Volotea announced to close their Genoa base after five years, leading to the cancellation of 14 routes.[7]

Ground transportation

Car

Genoa Airport can be reached by travelling in the city's inner roads and by the A10 Motorway, with the closest exit being 'Genova Aeroporto'. Taxi stands can be found just outside the airport. Fixed fares apply for groups: to Genova Brignole railway station €8 per person (minimum 3 people), to Genova Piazza Principe €7 per person (minimum 3; €6 minimum 4), as of October 2024.[12]

Train

The closest railway station to the airport is Genova Sestri Ponente-Aeroporto, served by Trenitalia regional trains to/from Piazza Principe/Brignole, Savona and Ventimiglia. The station is then connected to the airport by a bus service called Airlink, operated by AMT Genova, running every 15 minutes from 6:00 to 22:00 (journey time 5-10 minutes; bus fare €2, combined bus+train €2.20).[13]

Bus

AMT also operates Volabus, a bus service linking the airport with the Genova Piazza Principe and Genova Brignole railway stations (journey time about 30 minutes; frequency every 30-60 minutes from 5:00 to 00:20; METDaily 24-hour ticket €10).[13] Following the 2021 merger of ATP Esercizio into AMT Genova, additional regional bus services to localities in Genoa's Metropolitan City are operated by AMT, though no specific airport shuttle beyond Volabus is currently detailed in official sources.

Incidents and Accidents at Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport

Incidents and accidents

  • On 28 May 1977, a Yakovlev Yak-40EC operated by Avioligure struck the runway surface with its left wing upon touchdown at Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport, went out of control, overran the runway, and came to rest in the sea. There were no fatalities.[14]
  • On 25 February 1999, Alitalia Flight 1553, a Dornier 328-110 operated by Minerva Airlines, overshot runway 29 on landing from Cagliari-Elmas Airport due to excessive tailwind and late touchdown, crashing into the sea. Four of the 31 people on board died.[15]
  • On 24 October 1999, a Learjet 35A operated by Avioriprese Jet Executive crashed into the Golfo di Genova during an instrument approach to Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport in poor weather conditions. All three people on board were killed.[16]
