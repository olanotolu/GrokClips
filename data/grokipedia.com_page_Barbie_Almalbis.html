Barbie Almalbis

Yvette Barbra Hontiveros Almalbis-Honasan (born August 26, 1977), better known as Barbie Almalbis , is a Filipino singer, songwriter and guitarist.[1] Formerly the lead singer of the bands Hungry Young Poets and Barbie's Cradle, she pursued a solo career in 2005, releasing her self-titled compilation album, Barbie: The Singles. In 2006, she released her debut solo album, Parade. Her music is characterised by a rather quirky, but endearing vocal style, and guitar work. In 2016, she was chosen to interpret Nica del Rosario's composition "Ambon" at the 2016 edition of Himig Handog: P-Pop Love Songs.[2] In January 2025, she released her fifth studio album, Not That Girl.[3]

Greatest Day

"Greatest Day" is a song co-written and performed by Barbie Almalbis and Kitchie Nadal for Unilever's Sunsilk "Great Hair Days" campaign in 2006. A music video was produced for the Philippine TV commercial.[7]

Rockoustic Mania

Barbie Almalbis and Pupil were chosen by Juicy Fruit as their advertising models to reach out to the younger generation in their Rockoustic Mania [8] advertising events. The promotion included Juicy Fruit's Tugtog Mo! band competition, and Style mo! competition by Human and Pony footwear. The collaboration between the two artists offers a fusion of Rock and Acoustic. The AVCD features two songs, the music videos and some behind the scenes look at the artists' works. It was released on August 24, 2006. The Juicy Fruit Rockoustic Mania Final Fusion event was held on November 17, at the Tanghalang Francisco Balagtas (Folk Arts Theater), Cultural Center of the Philippines. The show featured performances from Barbie Almalbis and Pupil, guest bands Sugarfree and Up Dharma Down, and of course, from the three finalists, WTC 11, Medea, and 7th Skool. The band 7th Skool won the Tugtog Mo! Band Competition. Audio:

Tanaw Collective

Tanaw Collective is a supergroup formed in 2025 consisting of Acel Bisa, Aia de Leon, Barbie Almalbis, Hannah Romawac, Kitchie Nadal, and Lougee Basabas. They debuted with a cover of Fleetwood Mac's "Landslide" in January 2025.[9]

Panatag

"Panatag" by Hale featuring Barbie Almalbis, released November 21, 2024.[10]

Huling Sayaw | Tower Sessions

"Huling Sayaw" by Kamikazee featuring Barbie Almalbis, performed for Tower Sessions on February 27, 2025.[11]

Personal life

She is the daughter of Teresa Hontiveros Almalbis, who has served as vice mayor of Roxas City, Capiz, since 2022.[19][20] She is married to Martin Honasan with two children, Noa Stina and Liam Israel.[21]
