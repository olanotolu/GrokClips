PhonePe

PhonePe is an Indian digital payments and financial services company headquartered in Bengaluru, Karnataka, India.[5][6] It was founded in December 2015,[7][2] by Sameer Nigam, Rahul Chari and Burzin Engineer.[8] The PhonePe app, based on the Unified Payments Interface, went live in August 2016.[9] The PhonePe app is accessible in 11 Indian languages.[10] It enables users to perform various financial transactions such as sending and receiving money, recharging mobile and DTH, making utility payments, conducting in-store payments.[11][12]

History

PhonePe was founded in December 2015.[2] In April 2016, the company was acquired by Flipkart[13][14] and as part of the acquisition, the FxMart license was transferred to PhonePe and rebranded as the PhonePe wallet. PhonePe's co-founder Sameer Nigam was appointed as the CEO of the company.[15] In August 2016, the company partnered with Yes Bank to launch a UPI-based mobile payment app, based on the government-backed UPI platform.[9] In December 2022, PhonePe completed its separation from Flipkart, becoming fully independent under Walmart ownership.[16] In February 2023, PhonePe launched international UPI payments, allowing Indian users traveling abroad to pay foreign merchants with Unified Payments Interface (UPI).[17] In April 2023, PhonePe launched a new shopping app called 'Pincode', focused on hyperlocal e-commerce and built on top of the ONDC platform.[18][19] In August 2023, PhonePe launched Share.Market, foraying into stock broking and mutual fund investment via its subsidiary PhonePe Wealth Broking.[20] The platform allows users to trade in stocks and mutual funds, including intraday trading and pre-curated investment baskets.[21] In February 2024, PhonePe introduced Indus Appstore, a mobile app store.[22][23] The app store offers over 2 lakh apps and games across 45 categories.[24]

Ownership and funding

In December 2020, Flipkart and PhonePe declared a partial split, with Walmart maintaining its majority ownership in PhonePe and the two entities now functioning independently.[16] PhonePe announced the raising of $350 million from General Atlantic, a US growth equity firm, at a pre-money valuation of $12 billion.[25] Subsequently, another $100 million was raised in primary capital in February 2023 from Ribbit Capital, Tiger Global, and TVS Capital Funds, followed by $200 million in primary capital from Walmart, and another $100 million from General Atlantic at the same valuation. This brought the total raised in this funding round to $850 million.[26][27] In September 2025, PhonePe confidentially filed for an initial public offering (IPO) with the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI), aiming to raise approximately $1.3-1.5 billion at a targeted valuation of $15 billion.[28]

Legal challenges

2017 UPI guidelines dispute

On 14 January 2017, ICICI Bank blocked PhonePe transactions, citing that it did not meet the NPCI guidelines.[33][34] Initially, on 19 January 2017, NPCI instructed ICICI to allow UPI transactions via PhonePe.[35] During this period, Airtel also blocked PhonePe transactions on its platforms.[36] A day later, on 20 January 2017, NPCI renounced the previous instructions, stating that PhonePe violated the UPI norms.[37][38][39] After this, PhonePe closed its operations on Flipkart's website,[40] to align with the updated NPCI verdict. By February 2017, PhonePe resolved the issues with ICICI Bank and Airtel.[41]

Trademark disputes

PhonePe has been involved in several trademark disputes over the use of the "Pe" suffix, interpreted as a phonetic representation of "pay". In 2019, PhonePe filed a suit in the Delhi High Court against BharatPe, alleging infringement and seeking an injunction to prevent use of the "Pe" suffix. The dispute, which began with a legal notice in 2018, spanned multiple courts including the Bombay High Court. It was amicably settled in May 2024, with both parties withdrawing oppositions to each other's trademarks. [42] In May 2024, the Supreme Court of India dismissed PhonePe's appeal against DigiPe Fintech Private Limited, rejecting claims of trademark infringement. The court noted that "Pe" is a commonly used suffix in the payments sector and does not cause confusion. [43] In January 2025, the Madras High Court dismissed PhonePe's suit against BundlePe Innovations Pvt. Ltd. and LatePe, ruling that "Pe" is a generic term in digital payments (e.g., used in Google Pay, Paytm) and the prefixes "Bundle" and "Late" sufficiently distinguish the marks, with no evidence of consumer confusion. [44]

Other legal matters

In April 2025, the Karnataka High Court dismissed PhonePe's writ petition challenging a police notice under Section 91 of the CrPC to disclose transaction details in a 2022 cybercrime investigation involving online betting. The court held that user confidentiality under the Payment and Settlement Systems Act and Digital Personal Data Protection Act does not override investigative needs, emphasizing accountability in digital payments. This is PhonePe's third such penalty since 2019. [46]
