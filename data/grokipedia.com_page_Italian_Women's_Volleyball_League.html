Italian Women's Volleyball League Serie A1

The Italian Women's Volleyball League Serie A1 (Italian: Lega Pallavolo Serie A Femminile), is the highest women's volleyball league in Italy. It is organized and administered by the Italian Volleyball Federation (FIPAV). It is considered one of the oldest women's top national leagues in European volleyball, being established in 1946, and its clubs have achieved significant success in the continental European club competitions.[1][2]

History

The Italian Women's Volleyball League Serie A1 traces its origins to 1946, shortly after the establishment of the Italian Volleyball Federation (FIPAV). Initially known simply as Serie A, the league began with a small number of teams and was held in Genoa for its inaugural season. Early competitions were dominated by clubs such as Amatori Bergamo, who won the first championship in 1946â€“47, and Invicta Trieste in 1948â€“49.)[3] In the post-war years, the league grew steadily, reflecting the increasing popularity of volleyball in Italy. By the 1970s, expansion led to a restructuring: in 1977, the top division was renamed Serie A1, and a second tier, Serie A2, was introduced. This period also saw the adoption of a round-robin format supplemented by playoffs, enhancing competitiveness and spectator interest.)[4] A pivotal development occurred on June 6, 1987, when the Lega Pallavolo Serie A Femminile was founded in Modena as a consortium of Serie A1 and A2 clubs. This organization professionalized the league by managing broadcasting rights, sponsorships, and events, elevating its status. Under the Lega's stewardship, the league became a global powerhouse, particularly during the 1980s and 1990s, when Teodora Ravenna secured 11 titles between 1974 and 1992, establishing a record for dominance.[5] The 1990s and 2000s marked another golden era, with Foppapedretti Bergamo winning eight championships from 1995 to 2011. Italian clubs also achieved remarkable success in European competitions, claiming multiple CEV Champions League titles and contributing to Italy's national team's rise, including world championships in 2002 and 2018.[6][7] In the 2010s, Imoco Volley Conegliano emerged as the preeminent force, capturing eight titles from 2015â€“16 to 2024â€“25, including a seventh consecutive win in the latter season as of April 2025. The league now features 14 teams and remains one of Europe's premier women's volleyball competitions, known for high-level talent and international stars.[8][9]

Format

The league was originally played in a single round-robin format, with all clubs placed in a single group. As more clubs joined the league, the format changed to an initial stage composed of various groups with clubs advancing to a final group for the title. In 1983 a play-off format was introduced, with all clubs playing a regular season in a single group and the best teams advancing to the play-offs. Since then, the format has retained a regular season in a single group followed by playoffs, with adjustments to the number of teams over time. As of the 2024â€“25 season, 14 teams compete in a double round-robin regular season (each team plays every other team home and away), after which the top 8 teams advance to the playoffs to determine the champion.[10]
