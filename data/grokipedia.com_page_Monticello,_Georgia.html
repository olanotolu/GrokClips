Monticello, Georgia

Monticello is the largest city in and the county seat of Jasper County, Georgia, United States. The city includes historic buildings such as the Jasper County Courthouse, Monticello High School, and the Monticello Historic District. The population was 2,753 as of the 2023 U.S. Census estimate. It is 56 miles (90 km) southeast of Atlanta.

Monticello

History

Monticello was founded in 1808 as the seat of the newly formed Randolph County (originally created in 1807 and renamed Jasper County in 1812). The city was named after Monticello, the estate of Thomas Jefferson. It was incorporated as a town in 1810.

Geology and soils

The bedrock under Monticello is an intermediate-to-mafic type on which dark red clay with dark reddish brown loam topsoil is mapped as Lloyd series.

Education

Jasper County Charter System

The Jasper County Charter System holds pre-school to grade twelve, and consists of a primary school (PK-2), an elementary school (3-5), a middle school, and a high school. The district has 184 full-time equivalent teachers and 2,620 students (2023-2024).[3]
  • Jasper County Primary School (PK-2)
  • Washington Park Elementary School (3-5)
  • Jasper County Middle School (6-8)
  • Jasper County High School (9-12)

Private schools

  • Piedmont Academy - K3-12, co-ed, member of Georgia Independent School Association, established 1970

Historic school

  • Monticello High School - presently Thomas Persons Hall

Representation in media

  • The Bingo Long Traveling All-Stars & Motor Kings (1976) filmed many scenes in Monticello. The exteriors of buildings on the town square were remodeled to appear of the era of the movie.
  • Murder in Coweta County (1983), starring Andy Griffith, Johnny Cash, and June Carter Cash
  • Many scenes of My Cousin Vinny (1992) were set at the courthouse and town square, and other scenes were also shot in town.
  • The Curse of Bridge Hollow (2022), a Netflix horror comedy starring Marlon Wayans and Priah Ferguson, filmed scenes in Monticello, standing in for a Massachusetts town.[4]
