Orthez

Orthez (French pronunciation: [ɔʁtɛs]; Basque: Ortheze ; Occitan: Ortès , pronounced [urˈtɛs]) is a commune in the Pyrénées-Atlantiques department, and region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine, southwestern France.[3] It lies 40 km NW of Pau on the Southern railway to Bayonne. The town also encompasses the small village of Sainte-Suzanne , an independent commune until 1973;[4][5] residents of the town are called either Orthéziens or Sainte-Suzannais.

Geography

Orthez straddles the westward-flowing Gave de Pau, with most of the town proper having developed on the right bank. Several residential developments and an industrial park are located on the left bank, in addition to Sainte-Suzanne, an associated village entity within the town. A partially artificial lake called Lac du Grecq (commonly known as 'Lac de l'Y' i.e. 'Y Lake') 43°29′47″N 0°46′20″W, covering 7 hectares and created for flood protection, has a pleasant, scenic walking trail. Orthez station has rail connections to Tarbes, Pau, Bordeaux and Bayonne.[6][7]

History

During the 12th century, Orthez was the capital of Béarn, after Morlaàs and before Pau, which is still the prefectural administrative capital. At the end of the 12th century, Orthez passed from the possession of the viscounts of Dax to that of the viscounts of Bearn, whose chief place of residence it became in the 13th century. Froissart records the splendour of the court of Orthez under Gaston Phoebus in the latter half of the 14th century.[8] Jeanne d'Albret founded a Calvinist academy in the town in 1566, where Pierre Viret taught until his death in 1571.[9][10] An envoy sent in 1569 by Charles IX to revive the Catholic faith had to stand a siege in the battle of Orthez; the city was eventually taken by assault by the Protestant/Huguenot captain, Gabriel, count of Montgomery. In 1684 Nicholas Foucault, intendant under Louis XIV, was more successful, as the inhabitants, ostensibly at least, renounced Protestantism. It is nevertheless still a strong tradition in the town.[8] Another battle of Orthez occurred during the Napoleonic Wars on 27 February 1814, in which the British Duke of Wellington defeated Marshal Soult on the hills to the north of Orthez.

Sights

The Gave de Pau is crossed in Orthez by a 13th-century fortified bridge, known as the Pont Vieux, which has four arches of unequal sizes and is surmounted at its centre by a tower. Several old houses and the Église Saint-Pierre, with elements from the 13th, 14th, and 15th centuries, are of interest. The most notable building is the Tour Moncade (43°29′31″N 0°46′13″W), a pentagonal tower of the 13th century built around 1242 by Gaston VII de Moncade as the keep of a castle of the viscounts of Béarn, now a historical monument and museum site offering views of the town and Pyrenees. A building of the 16th century, founded in 1566 as the Académie protestante by Jeanne d'Albret, is all that remains of the old Calvinist academy; it now houses the Maison Jeanne d'Albret, a museum on Protestant history in Béarn. The town hall is a 19th-century building constructed in 1840.[13]

Economy

In the early 20th century, Orthez's industries included the spinning and weaving of hemp and flax, particularly the fabric known as toile de Béarn, flour-milling, paper and leather manufacturing, and the preparation of jambons de Bayonne and other delicacies. The area featured quarries of stone and marble, supporting a trade in leather, hams, and lime.[8] As of 2022, the economy of Orthez is predominantly service-based, employing 84.9% of the workforce, followed by industry at 7.6% and agriculture at 1.5%.[14] Agriculture remains significant, with livestock farming, viticulture (part of the Béarn AOC wine appellation since 1991), and production of Jambon de Bayonne and Ossau-Iraty cheese. Small-scale workshops continue in weaving and furniture making. Orthez is part of the Lacq-Orthez intercommunal territory, which has transitioned from the Lacq natural gas field (exploited 1957–2013, France's largest) to a hub for chemical industries and energy innovation. Key players include Arkema (specializing in lactams at its Mont site) and Air Liquide, with ongoing developments in renewable energy, hydrogen production, and circular economy projects as of 2023.[15][16] The unemployment rate stood at 9.8% in 2022, with 451 service businesses, 37 industrial establishments, and 6 agricultural ones as of 2023.[14]

Administration

Orthez has a judicial court but not an appeals court. It was the seat of a subprefecture from 1800 until 1926 (the dates of the creation and abolition of the arrondissement (district) of Orthez).

Sports

Orthez is known for basketball with the Élan Béarnais Pau-Orthez team, which is one of the most successful French basketball clubs. The team won the Korać Cup in 1984. Élan Béarnais Pau-Orthez moved to Pau in 1991. Orthez was the site for the start of Stage 16 in the 2007 Tour de France. The main sports clubs of the city are: Rugby :

  • Francis Jammes (1868–1938), poet
  • Jean-Louis Curtis (1917–1995), novelist
  • Daniel d'Auger de Subercase (1661-1732), soldier
  • Alain Ducasse (b.1956), chef.
