Elkhart Lake, Wisconsin

Elkhart Lake is a village in northwestern Sheboygan County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 941 at the 2020 census. It is within the Town of Rhine and is included in the Sheboygan, Wisconsin metropolitan area. Elkhart Lake may be best known for hosting road races on public county roads during the 1950s, later moving to a dedicated race track called Road America, a few miles south of the village.

History

The area was first inhabited by the Potawatomi people, who named the lake â€œMe-shay-way-odeh-ni-bisâ€, or Great Elk Heart Lake, because it resembles an elk's heart. [1] Early European-American settlement began in the 1850s, with German immigrants arriving primarily for farming. Few people traveled to the area until 1860, when the Sheboygan and Mississippi Railroad reached Glenbeulah, making it possible to take a stagecoach the rest of the way to Elkhart Lake. In 1872 the Milwaukee and Northern Railroad came into Elkhart Lake and brought more travelers. [2] The village was incorporated in 1894. [3]

Economy

Racing

Racing at Elkhart Lake dates back to the 1950s. The very first races were held on July 23, 1950, on a 3.35-mile road circuit on the north side of the lake. These first races were very successful for the time, drawing a crowd of over 5,000. In 1951 and 1952, races were held on a new 6.5-mile circuit that circumvented the lake, with the start/finish line in the village. The 1951 races drew an estimated crowd of 50,000 spectators and the 1952 races saw an estimated crowd of over 100,000 people. Open-road racing ended after 1952 due to safety concerns, including a fatal spectator accident during the event, prompting a state ban. Three years later, a new, specially designed track was built south of the village. Road America was built in 1955 with the first racing being held on September 10 & 11, 1955. Each year, Road America holds over 425 events. The economic impact from the track, its events, and visitors is estimated at $254 million annually for Sheboygan County (as of 2023).[6]

Arts and culture

Annual cultural events

Every July, during the WeatherTech Vintage Weekend with Brian Redman, a Concours d'Elegance featuring race cars is held in the village on July 18, followed by sports cars on July 19. This draws a large crowd as people line the streets from Road America to the shore of Elkhart Lake watching the cars drive past on their way to downtown Elkhart Lake. The cars park in the streets of Elkhart Lake, mainly on Lake Street, where curious visitors, car enthusiasts, and other racers alike walk up and down the streets looking at the cars.[7] As the concourse comes to a close, awards are announced for various categories including the best-looking car. The cars return to the track around 7:30 pm, just before the sun sets because many of the cars do not have headlights.[8] Jazz on the Vine is an annual festival held May 8â€“10. Jazz musicians from all over the United States come to the festival to perform.[9]

Tourism

There are several museums in Elkhart Lake, including the Elkhart Lake Depot Museum and Henschel's Indian Museum. The Elkhart Lake Depot Museum is a century-old railroad station that has original depot furnishings and local memorabilia.[10] Henschel's Indian Museum is located on an archaeological dig site and has a collection of Indian copper, pottery and artifacts that date back to 8,000 BC.[11][12]

Parks and recreation

Elkhart Lake features several parks and recreational areas. Within the village, Fireman's Park and Swimming Beach, located at 411 S. Lake Street, offers a seasonal sand beach open from Memorial Day to Labor Day, along with a concession stand, picnic tables, grills, sand volleyball courts, and restroom facilities. Admission is charged. June Vollrath Park at 290 N. Lincoln Street includes baseball and softball fields, a playground, volleyball and basketball courts, a horseshoe pit, picnic areas, a pavilion with restrooms, and a winter ice rink; it serves as home to local youth and high school teams. Lions Park at 319 Moraine Drive provides open space, a playground, picnic areas, and a pavilion with restrooms. Jeanette Moioffer Park at 300 Kettleview Court features a path for non-motorized vehicles, tree identification plaques, and benches for bird-watching. Memorial Park at 185 Chicago Street is a tribute to the armed forces with a brick memorial and hosts an annual Memorial Day program. Village Beach offers a sandy beach for swimming, grassy seating, and restrooms, accessible only to village and school district residents with passes. Village Square Park, at the intersection of N. Lake and Rhine Streets, includes a playground, sandbox, and shady seating areas adjacent to the public library. [13] Nearby, Sheboygan Broughton Marsh Park, a 30-acre site north of the village on County Highway J, includes 64 campsites, fishing piers, picnic and camping areas, a disc golf course, foot golf, a playground, an 80-foot observation tower, and access to snowmobile trails within the surrounding 13,000-acre Sheboygan Marsh Wildlife Area. [14]

Education

Primary and secondary schools

Elkhart Lake is served by the Elkhart Lake-Glenbeulah School District. Schools in the district include Elkhart Lake Elementary/Middle School and Elkhart Lake-Glenbeulah High School.

Public libraries

The Elkhart Lake Public Library was established in 1906 and was originally located in an old hotel. The library serves Elkhart Lake and several communities in the area.

Infrastructure

Transportation

Elkhart Lake is about one mile southeast of the northern terminus of the Kettle Moraine Scenic Drive. The Scenic Drive was part of the road racing circuit used in the 1950s. Wisconsin Highway 67 runs north/south through the city. Secondary routes include County Highway A from the northeast. County Highway J runs concurrent with the Kettle Moraine Scenic Drive through the city. They enter the city from the northwest, run north/south through the center of the city, and exit to the southwest.
