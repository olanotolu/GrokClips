Boise metropolitan area

The Boise, Idaho Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) (commonly known as the Boise Metropolitan Area or the Treasure Valley) is an area that encompasses Ada, Boise, Canyon, Gem, and Owyhee counties in southwestern Idaho, anchored by the cities of Boise and Nampa.[4] It is the main component of the wider Boise–Mountain Home–Ontario, ID–OR Combined Statistical Area, which adds Elmore and Payette counties in Idaho and Malheur County, Oregon. It is the state's largest officially designated metropolitan area and includes Idaho's three largest cities: Boise, Nampa, and Meridian. Nearly 40 percent of Idaho's total population lives in the area. As of the July 1, 2024 estimate, the Boise City, ID Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) had a population of 845,877, representing a 2.1% increase from 2023.[3] The larger Boise City–Mountain Home–Ontario, ID–OR Combined Statistical Area (CSA) had a population of approximately 902,425 as of July 1, 2023.[5] The metro area is currently the third largest in the U.S. section of the Pacific Northwest after Seattle and Portland, and is the 81st-largest metropolitan area in the United States.

Demographics

As of the 2023 American Community Survey, there were 790,640 people, 314,846 households, and approximately 208,000 families residing within the MSA.[7] The racial makeup of the MSA was 81.6% White (including Hispanic whites), 1.1% African American, 0.7% Native American, 2.4% Asian, 0.2% Pacific Islander, 6.5% from other races, and 3.4% from two or more races (approximate based on detailed categories). Hispanic or Latino of any race were 14.7% of the population.[7] The median income for a household in the MSA was $82,694, and the median income for a family was approximately $98,500 (estimated from household data). The per capita income for the MSA was approximately $39,660.[7][8]
