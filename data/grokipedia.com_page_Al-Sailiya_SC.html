Al-Sailiya SC

Al-Sailiya Sports Club (Arabic: نادي السيلية الرياضي), originally founded as Al-Qadisiya Sports Club in 1995 and renamed in 2003, is a Qatari professional football club currently competing in the Qatar Stars League. They are based in the Qatari capital Doha and play their home games at Hamad bin Khalifa Stadium.

History

Al Sailiya were founded as Al-Qadsiyah Sports Club on 10 October 1995. In 2003, the club adopted its current name, Al Sailiya. They were relegated in 2006 after finishing at the bottom of the Qatar Stars League. They won promotion back to the Qatar Stars League for the 2007–08 season, but were once again relegated to the Qatargas League in the 2010–11 season. They won promotion again for the 2011–12 season but finished last.[1] However, a decision to expand the QSL to 14 teams ensured Sailiya's place in the top flight was secure.[2] The club remained in the Qatar Stars League until the end of the 2022–23 season, when they were relegated after finishing second-from-bottom.[3] Al-Sailiya won the Qatari Second Division in the 2024–25 season to earn promotion back to the Qatar Stars League.[4]

Stadium

Built in 1995, the Al-Sailiya Stadium covers 48,000 m² and features a main arena with a capacity of 11,000 spectators, training pitches, VIP and media facilities, parking for 1,000 cars, and other ancillary buildings.[5] However, due to its facilities, the club plays its home matches at various stadiums, primarily Hamad bin Khalifa Stadium (capacity 12,000).[6] As of the 2025–26 season, following promotion from the Qatari Second Division, this arrangement continues.[7]
