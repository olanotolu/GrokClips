Kidnapping and killing of Hersh Goldberg-Polin


Hersh Goldberg-Polin (c. 2000 – c. August 2024) was an American-Israeli dual citizen abducted by Hamas militants during their October 7, 2023, attack on the Nova music festival in southern Israel, where he suffered the traumatic amputation of his left hand while defending others.[1][2]
Held captive in Gaza for nearly 11 months amid failed negotiations and intermittent proof-of-life videos released by Hamas, Goldberg-Polin was executed by his captors along with five other hostages in a tunnel complex in Rafah, with Israeli forensic analysis confirming they were shot at close range shortly before the Israel Defense Forces recovered their bodies on September 1, 2024.[3][4][5]
His case drew international attention through his parents' persistent advocacy, including addresses at global forums, highlighting Hamas's strategic use of hostages to prolong conflict and obstruct resolution efforts.[6][7]

Contextual Background

The October 7, 2023 Hamas Terrorist Attack

The October 7, 2023, Hamas-led assault on Israel commenced at approximately 6:30 a.m. local time with a massive barrage of over 3,000 rockets launched from Gaza toward southern and central Israel, overwhelming air defense systems and serving as cover for ground incursions.[8] Hamas militants, numbering in the thousands and supported by allied groups such as Palestinian Islamic Jihad, breached the Gaza-Israel border fence at over 100 points using explosives, bulldozers, motorcycles, and motorized paragliders, infiltrating military bases like Zikim and Erez, as well as civilian communities including 22 kibbutzim and towns such as Be'eri and Kfar Aza.[8] [9] These multi-front operations enabled rapid advances deep into Israeli territory, targeting both military personnel and unarmed civilians in homes, streets, and public gatherings.[10] The attack's premeditated scale and execution reflected Hamas's strategic planning over months, with training exercises documented in advance and operational blueprints recovered post-assault, prioritizing civilian massacres and abductions as core objectives rather than incidental outcomes.[9] It resulted in approximately 1,200 deaths in Israel—predominantly civilians, including women, children, and elderly individuals—and the kidnapping of 251 hostages transported into Gaza, verified through Israeli forensic identification and international corroboration.[10] [8] Hamas exploited Israeli intelligence warnings that had been dismissed or under-prioritized amid assumptions of Hamas's deterrence by economic incentives and reduced border surveillance during the Simchat Torah holiday, allowing militants to overrun outposts and perpetrate systematic atrocities like summary executions and arson.[9] Ideologically, the assault embodied Hamas's rejectionist doctrine, rooted in its 1988 charter's explicit call for jihad to obliterate Israel as an "usurper entity" and liberate Palestine through armed struggle against Jews, framing the conflict in religious terms of Islamic supremacy over infidel occupation.[11] [12] While a 2017 policy document pragmatically endorsed an interim Palestinian state along 1967 borders without recognizing Israel's right to exist—reiterating armed resistance as obligatory—the attack's ferocity underscored persistent eliminationist aims voiced by leaders like Yahya Sinwar, who pre-attack invoked historical precedents for escalating violence to dismantle the Jewish state.[13] This ideological framework, unmitigated by defensive rationales, directly facilitated the border breaches and hostage-taking operations that created opportunities for individual abductions amid the chaos.[14]

Nova Music Festival Site and Security Failures

The Nova Music Festival took place at an open-air desert site near Kibbutz Re'im in southern Israel, approximately 5 kilometers from the Gaza Strip border, attracting an estimated 3,500 attendees, mostly young civilians celebrating a multi-day electronic dance music event with stages, sound systems, and informal camping areas spread across the terrain.[15][16] The remote location's proximity to the border inherently heightened risks, as the flat desert offered limited natural barriers and facilitated rapid vehicular access from Gaza, while the dispersed crowd setup—lacking enclosed perimeters or centralized evacuation points—impeded organized flight or defense amid sudden panic.[17] Israeli military and police approvals for the event proceeded despite identified security concerns, including reports of unusual Hamas reconnaissance and border activity in preceding days, which were not met with reinforced deployments or event relocation.[18] An IDF probe determined that the military authorized the festival without informing nearby border units of the large gathering, resulting in minimal on-site security—primarily a small police contingent and no dedicated perimeter patrols—leaving the site reliant on distant response forces.[15] A Gaza Division commander inspected the venue roughly one hour before the assault and, despite available intelligence on potential threats, declined to increase troop presence or alert higher command, citing perceived low immediate risk.[19] On October 7, 2023, around 6:30 a.m., Hamas militants—numbering over 100—breached the border en masse using pickup trucks, motorcycles, and paragliders, converging on the festival after detecting it via prior surveillance or radio intercepts, then methodically pursuing fleeing attendees along access roads and into roadside bomb shelters.[20][15] Survivor testimonies describe militants firing indiscriminately at vehicles and groups, blocking escape routes with vehicles, and grenading or shooting into shelters where crowds had sought refuge, exploiting the site's open layout and attendees' disorientation from concurrent rocket barrages.[17][21] Post-event forensic analysis and eyewitness accounts confirm these tactics amplified casualties, as the absence of immediate military intervention—due to communication breakdowns and units' initial collapse under surprise—allowed unchecked rampage for hours.[22] IDF and police inquiries highlighted systemic lapses, including ignored pre-event risk assessments, insufficient joint exercises for mass gatherings near hostile borders, and over-reliance on static border fences without adaptive crowd-control protocols, which collectively exposed the venue's vulnerabilities to low-tech incursions.[23] These factors contributed to approximately 360 deaths at the site, predominantly civilians, and the abduction of dozens more, as documented in official tallies and survivor reports.[16][18]

Personal Background

Early Life and Dual Citizenship

Hersh Goldberg-Polin was born on October 3, 2000, in Berkeley, California, to American parents. His family relocated to Richmond, Virginia, during his early childhood, where he attended local institutions including Rudlin Torah Academy before the household made aliyah to Israel in 2008 when he was seven years old.[24][25][26] Settling in Jerusalem following the immigration, Goldberg-Polin completed his secondary education at Himmelfarb High School, an Orthodox Jewish institution in the city. He held dual citizenship of the United States—by birth—and Israel, obtained via his family's aliyah and subsequent residency.[27][28] Goldberg-Polin pursued interests in soccer, music festivals, and global travel during his young adulthood, often sharing aspirations for international exploration through personal accounts and activities prior to 2023.[24]

Family and Pre-Abduction Activities

Hersh Goldberg-Polin was born in Berkeley, California, in 2000 and immigrated to Israel with his family at the age of seven, settling in Jerusalem.[29] He held dual United States-Israeli citizenship, reflecting his family's bicultural ties shaped by prior residences in the US, including Richmond, Virginia, and Georgia.[30][31] His parents, Rachel Goldberg-Polin, who worked as an educator and support specialist at an English-speaking high school in Jerusalem, and Jon Polin, a serial entrepreneur in the tech sector, raised Hersh alongside his two younger sisters, Leebie and Orly, in a close-knit household.[32][33][33] The family's relocation to Israel fostered Hersh's familiarity with both American and Israeli environments, enabling periodic travel and cultural exposure that influenced his worldview and mobility between the two countries.[34] Prior to his abduction, Hersh led a low-key routine in Jerusalem, pursuing interests in soccer, music, and geography without documented engagement in political activism or high-risk endeavors.[1] He had been planning a round-the-world trip set to begin in December 2023, a venture aligned with his adventurous spirit and recent travels across Europe to attend music festivals.[1][24] Hersh's attendance at the Nova music festival on October 7, 2023, stemmed from a spontaneous decision to join his best friend Aner Shapira for what was intended as a celebratory outing near his 23rd birthday.[1] This casual choice, typical of his laid-back lifestyle involving odd pursuits and social connections in Jerusalem's youth scene, placed him at the event without prior indication of vulnerability to conflict zones.[24]

Abduction on October 7, 2023

Sequence of Events During the Attack

On October 7, 2023, during the Hamas-led assault on the Nova music festival near Kibbutz Re'im, Hersh Goldberg-Polin, aged 23, was sheltering in a roadside bomb shelter (mamad) with others when militants approached and initiated an attack by opening fire and hurling grenades into the enclosure.[35][36] One grenade detonated near him, severing his left forearm at the elbow; Goldberg-Polin improvised a tourniquet using his belt to stem the bleeding, as evidenced by subsequent video footage and family accounts corroborated by Israeli authorities.[35][37] Emerging from the shelter amid the chaos, Goldberg-Polin was seized by Hamas gunmen, who beat him and dragged his bloodied form, along with fellow festival-goers Eliya Cohen and Or Levy, before throwing them into the open bed of a white pickup truck.[38][39] Security camera and militant-recorded footage, released publicly in June 2024 by hostage families and verified by Israeli media, depicts the three men crammed together in the truck's rear, covered in blood from their injuries, while their captors filmed the scene with evident celebration as they sped toward the Gaza border.[38][40] The group was then transported across the breached Israel-Gaza border fence into the Gaza Strip, where the immediate violent seizure—contrasting Hamas's framing of such abductions as orderly "captures"—involved coordinated grenade bombardment, physical assault, and forcible bundling into vehicles, as documented in the raw footage rather than sanitized militant narratives.[38][39] This sequence underscores the militants' brutality in targeting sheltered civilians, prioritizing seizure over restraint.[35]

Immediate Injury and Initial Captivity

During the Hamas assault on the Nova music festival on October 7, 2023, Goldberg-Polin sought refuge in a roadside bomb shelter along with approximately 20 others, including his friend Aner Shapira. Militants from Hamas's Nukhba forces hurled multiple grenades into the shelter; while Shapira returned several, one detonated inside, severing Goldberg-Polin's left forearm below the elbow and causing shrapnel wounds.[35] [1] In the immediate aftermath, he fashioned a tourniquet from his belt to stanch the arterial bleeding, an action credited with preventing exsanguination and enabling short-term survival despite the absence of professional medical intervention.[35] Eyewitness accounts from survivors in the shelter confirmed the explosion's severity and Goldberg-Polin's self-applied restraint as the only barrier to fatal hemorrhage.[35] Despite his critical condition, captors dragged the bloodied and disoriented Goldberg-Polin from the shelter and loaded him into the bed of a pickup truck, as documented in bystander and militant body-camera footage recovered post-attack.[41] [42] The militants then transported him across the Gaza border, a journey spanning several kilometers under fire and chaos, prioritizing rapid extraction over stabilization of his wound.[37] This transfer, completed within hours, exposed him to further trauma including jolting over rough terrain, with no evidence of en-route medical aid from his handlers, who instead focused on evading Israeli forces.[2] Upon arrival in Gaza, Goldberg-Polin was handed over to a Hamas terror cell for initial holding, likely in a surface-level safe house before relocation to underground tunnels, per patterns reported from interrogated militants in similar abductions.[43] Captors provided no immediate surgical or antiseptic care, leaving the improvised tourniquet in place amid unsanitary conditions that heightened risks of sepsis and gangrene from the open, shrapnel-riddled stump.[44] Family-commissioned forensic reviews of later-released footage and intelligence indicated that the lack of prompt debridement or antibiotics in the first days exacerbated tissue necrosis, underscoring the captors' indifference to preserving hostage viability beyond transport.[35]

Period of Captivity

Evidence of Survival and Hamas Propaganda Efforts

On April 24, 2024, Hamas released a video constituting the primary visual proof of Hersh Goldberg-Polin's survival after 200 days in captivity, depicting him seated against a white wall with a shaved head and his left forearm amputated above the elbow, appearing gaunt from evident malnutrition.[45] In the footage, Goldberg-Polin recited a prepared statement accusing the Israeli government of abandoning hostages and prioritizing military operations over their release, a message his family described as coerced and painful evidence of his deteriorated state.[46][47] The release followed Qatari pressure on Hamas to provide proof-of-life documentation, yet U.S. and Israeli assessments framed it as deliberate propaganda designed for psychological warfare, aiming to demoralize the Israeli public and pressure negotiations by exploiting hostage imagery.[48][49] Hamas's method involved scripting content under duress, with captives ordered to deliver specific accusations, a tactic corroborated by forensic reviews of prior videos showing hostages reciting lines verbatim without deviation, indicative of threats rather than voluntary expression.[50] Further signs of Goldberg-Polin's survival emerged sporadically through unverified reports from released hostages referencing his presence in tunnels, though lacking independent confirmation beyond the April footage until a late August 2024 Hamas video captured his final coerced message shortly before execution, reinforcing the pattern of timed releases for leverage.[47] Counter-terrorism analyses reject such statements as genuine pleas, emphasizing empirical context of captivity under armed terrorist oversight, where free articulation is impossible and content serves Hamas's strategic goals of sowing division and stalling rescue efforts.[50] This approach aligns with Hamas's historical use of hostages in media operations, including execution threats to amplify bargaining power and domestic discord in Israel.[45]

Conditions Imposed by Captors and Survival Strategies

Hersh Goldberg-Polin and the five other hostages whose bodies were recovered from a Rafah-area tunnel endured confinement in a subterranean network characterized by prolonged darkness, severe restrictions on movement, and minimal sustenance. Autopsy examinations conducted by Israel's Abu Kabir Forensic Institute revealed evidence of long-term physical neglect across all six bodies, including significant malnutrition and severe weight loss consistent with diets limited primarily to energy bars and scarce water over months of captivity.[43][51][52] The captives were subjected to restraints, with at least one body showing signs of having been tied up, and periodic physical abuse, as corroborated by accounts from hostages initially abducted alongside Goldberg-Polin, such as Or Levy and Eliya Cohen, who described the initial transfer into Gaza as involving shocking deprivation and violence. Medical neglect was acute for Goldberg-Polin, whose left hand had been severed by grenade shrapnel during his abduction on October 7, 2023; no prosthetic or adequate wound care was provided, exacerbating chronic pain and infection risks, as indicated by the overall forensic findings of untreated deterioration. Hamas's placement of the group in tunnels proximate to high-value military assets, including areas associated with leader Yahya Sinwar, positioned them as de facto human shields, deterring Israeli operations per IDF intelligence assessments and complicating any potential rescue.[53][51][43] Amid these impositions, Goldberg-Polin demonstrated notable resilience, drawing on psychological strategies to sustain himself and fellow captives. Released hostage Or Levy, who was abducted with Goldberg-Polin from the same shelter, credited a mantra shared by Goldberg-Polin—"The last of the human freedoms is to choose one's attitude"—adapted from Viktor Frankl's writings, with helping him endure isolation and despair during their shared early captivity. Peer testimonies from survivors highlight Goldberg-Polin's efforts to maintain group morale through storytelling and humor, fostering a sense of solidarity despite the captors' efforts to isolate and demoralize them.[54][55]

Execution and Body Recovery

Timing and Method of Killing by Hamas

The bodies of Hersh Goldberg-Polin, Eden Yerushalmi, Carmel Gat, Alexander Lobanov, Almog Meir Jan, and Ori Danino were recovered by IDF forces on August 31, 2024, from an underground tunnel complex in Rafah, southern Gaza Strip.[56] Pathological examinations conducted shortly thereafter established that the six hostages had been killed by Hamas captors 48 to 72 hours prior, placing the time of death between August 29 and 30, 2024.[57][58] Autopsies confirmed the method of killing as execution-style gunshots fired at close range, with multiple entry wounds consistent with deliberate Hamas action rather than combat-related injuries.[57] The IDF's subsequent investigation, including ballistic analysis, attributed the deaths directly to Hamas militants who fled the site after the act, leaving no evidence of Israeli forces' involvement in the fatalities.[58] This execution aligned with Hamas operational patterns under pressure from the IDF's Rafah offensive, where advancing troops triggered panic among guards, leading to orders for the hostages' murder to thwart potential rescue.[59][58] Forensic details further revealed chronic conditions among the victims, including severe malnutrition, significant weight loss from prolonged neglect, and untreated injuries from their October 7, 2023, abduction—such as Goldberg-Polin's severed arm—but the immediate cause of death was unequivocally the fresh ballistic trauma inflicted in the tunnel.[52] No contradictory evidence emerged suggesting external factors beyond Hamas culpability.[57]

IDF Operation and Forensic Details

The Israel Defense Forces (IDF) conducted a targeted overnight operation on August 31–September 1, 2024, in the Rafah area of southern Gaza, where troops from the 162nd Division located and entered a Hamas-operated tunnel shaft beneath a building.[58] The raid, informed by intelligence gathered during prior efforts to dismantle Hamas's al-Tel Sultan Battalion in the region, enabled the intact recovery of the bodies of six hostages, including Hersh Goldberg-Polin, without any reported IDF casualties.[58][56] Following extraction, the bodies were transported under military chain-of-custody protocols to Israel's Abu Kabir Forensic Institute for identification and examination. DNA analysis confirmed the identities as Goldberg-Polin, Carmel Gat, Eden Yerushalmi, Alexander Lobanov, Almog Sarusi, and Ori Danino, all abducted alive on October 7, 2023.[57][60] Forensic autopsies revealed multiple gunshot wounds to each body, inflicted at close range, with wound characteristics and post-mortem indicators such as rigor mortis freshness establishing that the deaths occurred 48 to 72 hours prior to recovery—between August 29 and 30, 2024.[57][61] The timing aligned with IDF advances closing in on the site, supporting determinations that Hamas guards executed the hostages to thwart potential rescue, as detailed in the IDF's subsequent investigation.[58][57]

Immediate Aftermath

Family Notification and Burial

The parents of Hersh Goldberg-Polin, Rachel and Jon Goldberg-Polin, received official notification from the Israel Defense Forces of their son's death on September 1, 2024, following the recovery of his body the previous day in a Gaza tunnel.[62] The family issued a public statement confirming the news amid intense media attention, expressing devastation and thanking supporters for their efforts during the 11-month ordeal.[63] Hersh Goldberg-Polin was buried on September 2, 2024, at Har Hamenuchot cemetery in Jerusalem's Givat Shaul neighborhood, in accordance with traditional Jewish rites including rapid interment and communal mourning.[64] The funeral drew thousands of attendees, including family members and select Israeli officials, who gathered to pay respects in a procession through Jerusalem streets.[65] In statements following the notification and burial, Rachel and Jon Goldberg-Polin attributed sole responsibility for their son's murder to Hamas, describing it explicitly as a killing committed by his captors during captivity.[66] They rejected any narratives implying shared culpability, emphasizing Hamas's deliberate execution as the causal factor in his death.[67] Following the burial, the Goldberg-Polin family shifted their advocacy focus toward measures to prevent future abductions, continuing public campaigns to highlight the risks of hostage-taking by militant groups while honoring Hersh's memory through calls for enhanced security protocols.[68]

Legal Actions Against Perpetrators

In September 2024, the U.S. Department of Justice unsealed criminal charges against senior Hamas leaders, including Yahya Sinwar, Mohammed Deif, and Ismail Haniyeh, for conspiring to provide material support to terrorism, conspiracy to murder Americans, and other offenses related to the October 7, 2023, attacks that killed and kidnapped U.S. citizens, with Hersh Goldberg-Polin's murder explicitly cited as part of the ongoing terrorism investigation.[69][70] Goldberg-Polin's U.S. citizenship invoked federal jurisdiction under statutes like 18 U.S.C. § 2332b, enabling prosecution of extraterritorial acts of terrorism against Americans, though practical enforcement remains constrained by Hamas's operational base in Gaza.[71] Israeli authorities have pursued indictments against Hamas operatives involved in the October 7 abductions through military tribunals, relying on intelligence, surveillance footage, and recovered evidence to identify participants in specific kidnappings, including those at the Nova festival site where Goldberg-Polin was seized. However, direct indictments naming his individual abductors have not been publicly detailed, with efforts focused on broader Hamas command structures amid ongoing conflict operations. Civil remedies under Israeli law, such as claims via the state's compensation fund for terror victims, provide families pathways for restitution, drawing on precedents from prior Hamas cases where courts awarded damages based on verified perpetrator involvement. Internationally, the International Criminal Court (ICC) Prosecutor applied for arrest warrants in May 2024 against Hamas leaders for war crimes and crimes against humanity, including the taking of civilian hostages during the October 7 assault, encompassing cases like Goldberg-Polin's abduction and execution.[72] These efforts face inherent limitations, as Hamas's non-recognition of the ICC and lack of custody cooperation in Gaza territories hinder arrests and trials, rendering symbolic designations more feasible than enforcement. U.S. and Israeli precedents, such as civil suits under the Anti-Terrorism Act against Hamas funders, underscore alternative accountability for victims' families, prioritizing financial penalties over criminal extradition.[73]

Broader Responses and Controversies

Domestic Israeli Reactions and Policy Debates

Following the recovery of Hersh Goldberg-Polin's body on August 31, 2024, from a tunnel in Rafah alongside those of five other hostages executed by Hamas, mass protests swept major Israeli cities, including Tel Aviv, where tens of thousands demanded that Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu prioritize a hostage-release deal over continued military operations, accusing the government of abandoning captives through inaction.[74][75] Counterprotests and statements from right-leaning figures emphasized hardline elimination of Hamas's capabilities, arguing that negotiations had repeatedly failed due to the group's bad-faith rejections, and that body recoveries validated the necessity of sustained pressure to deter future abductions.[76][77] Netanyahu addressed the nation on September 1, 2024, seeking forgiveness for the hostages' deaths while defending the government's dual-track approach of military action and diplomacy, stating that Hamas had refused genuine negotiations and that operations in areas like Rafah enabled the retrievals, thereby refuting left-leaning critiques of deliberate abandonment as empirically contradicted by Hamas's documented executions of captives slated for potential release.[78][79] He vowed intensified consequences for Hamas leadership, aligning with security establishment assessments that partial deals incentivize further killings without addressing the organization's existential threat.[76] Hostage families exhibited divisions, with Goldberg-Polin's parents, Rachel and Jon, publicly urging deals as the sole path to returns despite acknowledging Hamas's twice-derailing of their son's prospective releases, while others highlighted empirical evidence of Hamas rejecting viable proposals—such as those in 2024 involving phased exchanges—and executing hostages preemptively to manipulate negotiations.[80][81] This split underscored causal realities: military degradation of Hamas infrastructure had rescued over 100 hostages via operations, yet pure negotiation risks emboldening captors who view killings as leverage, per analyses from families and experts.[77] In Knesset deliberations, the episode fueled calls for intelligence reforms to avert repeats, including proposals to centralize oversight under the Prime Minister's Office separate from IDF and Shin Bet structures, aiming to improve real-time tracking and prevent operational gaps exposed on October 7, 2023, though coalition resistance framed such changes as politicized distractions from Hamas accountability.[82] Broader debates rejected rushed state inquiries into October 7 failures, prioritizing wartime focus on deterrence over retrospective blame, with bereaved and hostage kin accusing delays in probes of shielding systemic lapses.[83]

International Media Coverage and Political Statements

U.S. political attention to the case was heightened by Goldberg-Polin's dual American-Israeli citizenship, with President Joe Biden issuing a statement on August 31, 2024, describing himself as "devastated and outraged" over the killing and affirming that "Hamas leaders will pay the price" for the murder.[84] The Goldberg-Polin family had met with Biden at the White House on multiple occasions, including advocacy efforts highlighted during the Democratic National Convention in August 2024, where his parents pleaded for hostage releases amid stalled negotiations.[2] Vice President Kamala Harris echoed this in a separate statement, recommitting to freeing American hostages without equivocation.[85] In contrast, coverage in European media and statements from UN officials often framed the execution within the broader Israel-Hamas conflict, frequently incorporating Gaza casualty statistics and emphasizing negotiation "stagnation" without isolating Hamas's role in premeditated killings or its rejection of viable deals.[86] UN Secretary-General António Guterres issued a general call for hostage releases on September 4, 2024, but drew criticism for omitting explicit condemnation of Hamas, referring only to "hostages" without naming the perpetrators responsible for the murders.[87] This pattern aligns with observed tendencies in international outlets to downplay terrorist agency, as evidenced by passive phrasing in reports—such as CNN's initial description of Goldberg-Polin's death as simply "has died" rather than murdered by captors—potentially reflecting institutional biases that equivocate on Hamas's culpability to maintain perceived balance.[88][89] Media anomalies included limited scrutiny of Hamas's coerced propaganda videos featuring Goldberg-Polin, where hostages were forced to deliver scripted messages under duress, a tactic documented by freed captives and Israeli authorities but often underemphasized in international reporting to avoid complicating narratives of mutual negotiation failures.[50] [90] Coverage frequently highlighted stalled talks as a symmetric impasse, attributing delays to Israeli intransigence while sidelining Hamas's demands for full military withdrawal and prisoner releases that exceeded prior agreements, thereby obscuring the group's strategic use of hostages as leverage.[91] European parliamentary mentions, such as by MEP Assita Kanko in September 2024, condemned the killing but remained outliers amid broader reticence from heads of state.[92] Such framing contributed to underreporting of the execution's premeditation, with forensic evidence of close-range gunshot wounds to the head indicating deliberate Hamas action shortly before IDF recovery on August 31, 2024, rather than incidental wartime deaths.[93]

Hostage Negotiation Failures and Criticisms of Hamas Tactics

Negotiations for the release of hostages held by Hamas, including Hersh Goldberg-Polin, were primarily mediated by Qatar and Egypt, with U.S. involvement, but repeatedly failed due to Hamas's demands for a full Israeli military withdrawal from Gaza and the release of thousands of Palestinian prisoners, including those convicted of terrorism.[94][95] These conditions were viewed by Israeli officials as exceeding acceptable security risks, as a complete withdrawal would enable Hamas to regroup and rearm without eliminating its military capabilities, while prisoner exchanges risked releasing individuals likely to resume terrorist activities.[96] A partial ceasefire deal in November 2023, brokered under similar mediation, resulted in the release of approximately 50 hostages—primarily women and children—but Hamas retained adult male civilians like Goldberg-Polin and soldiers as leverage for subsequent phases, categorizing them as "stubborn" captives to extract further concessions.[97] Subsequent talks in 2024, including rounds in Doha, collapsed amid Hamas's phased approach, which tied incremental hostage releases to progressive Israeli withdrawals and demanded guarantees of no resumption of military operations, effectively aiming to end the conflict on terms preserving Hamas's governance in Gaza.[98][99] This strategy prolonged captivity, as evidenced by repeated breakdowns, such as in August and December 2024, where Hamas rejected proposals without full capitulation to its terms.[100] Critics, including international legal analyses, have condemned Hamas's tactics as bad-faith maneuvers constituting war crimes, particularly the use of civilian hostages as bargaining chips to extend the conflict and secure political survival rather than humanitarian release.[101] Hamas's execution of Goldberg-Polin and others in August 2024, shortly before an IDF rescue operation, was interpreted as a deliberate act to eliminate leverage and intensify pressure on Israel, aligning with patterns of hostage-taking prohibited under international humanitarian law.[102] While some Israeli advocates for immediate deals argued that any release saves lives regardless of long-term risks, this perspective was countered by empirical data on prisoner exchanges, such as the 2011 Gilad Shalit deal, where numerous released Palestinians, including Hamas leaders, reengaged in terrorism, with Israeli assessments indicating high recidivism rates among security prisoners—often exceeding 50% involvement in attacks post-release—thus justifying reluctance to concede to demands that could perpetuate cycles of violence.[103]
