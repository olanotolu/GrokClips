Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall

Kiernan Frank Dewsbury-Hall (born 6 September 1998) is an English professional footballer who plays as a central midfielder for Premier League club Everton. Born in England, Dewsbury-Hall is eligible to play for the Republic of Ireland national team through family heritage. Dewsbury-Hall started his career with Leicester City, joining their youth system in 2006. He signed his first professional contract in 2017, and made his senior debut in the FA Cup in 2019. He went on loan to Blackpool in 2020 and to Luton Town for the 2020â€“21 season. After impressing for Luton, he broke into the Leicester first-team the following season. The team was relegated in the 2022â€“23 season, but Dewsbury-Hall played an integral role in returning the club to the Premier League by winning the Championship, and won Leicester Player of the Season. He signed for Chelsea in the summer of 2024, and transferred to Everton the following summer.

Early and personal life

Born in England, Dewsbury-Hall is of Irish descent.[4] He grew up in Shepshed, Leicestershire, where he attended Newcroft Primary Academy and Shepshed High School.[5][6] Growing up, he idolised Paul Scholes and was also interested in snooker, crediting this to his father.[7]

Career

Leicester City

Early career

Dewsbury-Hall was born in Nottingham, Nottinghamshire, and grew up in Shepshed, Leicestershire.[8][9] He joined his first club, Shepshed Dynamo Warriors of the Leicester Mutual League, at age six,[6] where he played at the under-8 and under-9 levels.[10][11] He joined the Leicester City Academy in 2006 aged eight from Shepshed Dynamo Warriors.[12][13] After impressing for the under-18, he was promoted to the under-23 in March 2017. He signed his first professional contract with the club in June, a two-year deal.[14] In 2019, he was named as the Development Squad Player of the Year.[9]

Style of play

Dewsbury-Hall is known for his box-to-box style of play, covering large areas of the pitch.[58] He has strong passing skills, good ball control, and contributes both defensively with effective tackling and offensively by creating opportunities.[59] His high stamina allows him to press opponents and recover the ball consistently.[60] He is versatile, capable of playing as a No. 8, No. 10, or on the left, and excels in progressive passing and dribbling to support patient build-up play.[61]

International career

Although Dewsbury-Hall was born in England, he is eligible to play for the Republic of Ireland national team through family heritage.[62] As of October 2025, the Football Association of Ireland (FAI) has been urged to approach him amid interest from the national team manager in selecting him, given his lack of senior caps for England.[63]
