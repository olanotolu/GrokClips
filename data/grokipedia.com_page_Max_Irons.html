Max Irons

Maximilian Paul Diarmuid Irons (born 17 October 1985) is an English and Irish actor.[1] He is known for his roles in films and television productions such as Red Riding Hood (2011), The White Queen (2013 miniseries), The Host (2013), Woman in Gold (2014), The Riot Club (2014), Bitter Harvest (2017), The Wife (2018), Flowers in the Attic: The Origin (2022 miniseries)[2], and Miss Austen (2025 miniseries)[3]. He also starred in the spy thriller series Condor (2018–2020).

Early life

Maximilian Paul Diarmuid Irons was born in the London Borough of Camden on 17 October 1985,[5][6] the son of Irish actress Sinéad Cusack and English actor Jeremy Irons. He has an older brother, Samuel Irons, who is a photographer.[7] Their mother's family was deeply involved with theatre: they are the grandsons of actors Cyril Cusack and Maureen Cusack.[8] Through his mother, Irons is a half-brother of politician Richard Boyd Barrett; his aunts are the actresses Niamh Cusack, Sorcha Cusack, and Catherine Cusack, and his uncle is theatre producer Pádraig Cusack. Irons attended the Dragon School in Oxford,[9] and Bryanston School in Dorset,[10] before finishing at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama, where he graduated in 2008.[11] He was dyslexic and struggled in school, as the teaching methods did not suit his learning style. His father discouraged him from going into an acting career.[12] While first starting off in acting, Irons worked as a barman.[13]

Career

Acting

In 2011, Irons played Henry in Catherine Hardwicke's Red Riding Hood.[7] He was chosen to play Jared Howe in the 2013 film adaptation of The Host , based on Stephenie Meyer's science fiction novel of the same name.[14] In the 2013 television series The White Queen , Irons took the leading role of Edward IV of England.[15] The series, based on Philippa Gregory's best-selling historical novel series The Cousins' War , was broadcast weekly on BBC One, ending on 18 August 2013.[16] Irons appeared as Fritz Altmann in Woman in Gold (2015). He also appeared in The Riot Club (2014), the film adaptation of the play Posh.[17] In 2016, he starred in the ITV miniseries Tutankhamun as archeologist Howard Carter.[18] In 2017, he starred as Yuri in Bitter Harvest and as Charles Hayward in Crooked House , based on a 1949 novel by Agatha Christie of the same name. Terence Stamp, Glenn Close, Gillian Anderson, and Stefanie Martini also are among the large cast in the film.[19] In April 2017, producers announced Irons had been cast to play Joe Turner, the role created by Robert Redford in Three Days of the Condor (1975), in a television series based on the movie, which aired from 2018 to 2020.[20] In 2018, he appeared as Al in the miniseries The Little Drummer Girl and as Bill in the film Terminal. He also starred as David Castleman in The Wife (2018). In 2022, he starred as Malcolm Foxworth in the Lifetime miniseries Flowers in the Attic: The Origin , a prequel adaptation.[2] As of 2025, he starred as Henry Hobday in the BBC/PBS miniseries Miss Austen.[21]

Modelling

Irons has modelled for companies including Burberry and Mango.[22] As of 2012, he was on a modelling contract for Macy's I.N.C. collection for Fall/Winter 2012. In 2015, he was named one of GQ 's 50 best-dressed British men.[24]

Personal life

In 2013, Irons began dating Tatler fashion director Sophie Pera.[25] They were married in Oxfordshire on 30 November 2019.[26] The couple had a daughter in 2023.[27]
