List of cricket grounds in England and Wales

This is a list of cricket grounds in England and Wales, listed in alphabetical order and based on each traditional English and Welsh county. The venues in this list have all staged first-class (from 1772), limited overs (from 1963), or Twenty20 (from 2003) matches. Venues used only for junior or minor matches are excluded. Some of the venues are dated to the 17th and 18th centuries and many are now defunct (marked by †). The article first lists international venues, followed by domestic grounds organized by county.

Domestic grounds in Wales

Glamorgan

Key domestic grounds used by Glamorgan include:
  • St Helen's, Swansea (first-class since 1924; List A and T20)
  • Cardiff Arms Park, Cardiff (first-class 1921–1930)
  • Ynysangharad Park, Pontypridd (first-class 1931–1932; List A)[153]
  • Eugene Cross Park, Ebbw Vale (List A 1988)[154]

Other Welsh counties

Key grounds in other Welsh counties used for minor counties or higher include:
  • Pen-y-Pound, Abergavenny (Monmouthshire; Wales Minor Counties home since 1990s)[155]
  • The Oval, Caerphilly (Glamorgan outground; List A 1969)[156]
  • No first-class or List A grounds identified in Breconshire, Caernarvonshire, Cardiganshire, Carmarthenshire, Denbighshire, Flintshire, or Pembrokeshire beyond minor counties usage covered in the referenced lists.
