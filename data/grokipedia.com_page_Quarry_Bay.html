Quarry Bay

Quarry Bay is an area beneath Mount Parker in the Eastern District of Hong Kong Island, in Hong Kong. Quarry Bay is bordered by Sai Wan Ho to the east, Mount Parker to the south, North Point to the west, and Victoria Harbour to the north. Quarry Bay is a residential and business district in Hong Kong. One Island East, the seventh tallest building in Hong Kong, is located in Quarry Bay. Some government departments such as the Accounting and Financial Reporting Council have relocated their offices from Central and Wan Chai to Quarry Bay over the years, and some multinational companies have their offices located in the district, such as Ernst & Young, BNP Paribas, LVMH and Boston Consulting Group, etc. Cityplaza and Kornhill Plaza, being one of the largest shopping malls on Hong Kong Island is at Quarry Bay. With dense population as well as developed economic and commercial activities in the district, Quarry Bay has a considerable traffic demand. It is one of the transportation hubs in Hong Kong. There are two MTR stations in the district, being Quarry Bay Station and Taikoo Station. Quarry Bay Station is the intersection of the Island line and Tseung Kwan O line. Other transportation means in the district include buses, minibuses, and trams.

Taikoo

The eastern part of Quarry Bay, namely Quarry Point , was largely owned by Swire and therefore many places and facilities are named after the company's Chinese name, Taikoo. The river originally flowed into the bay, however it was shut off from the sea with the construction of the Tai Koo Reservoir to supply fresh water to the Taikoo Dockyard, the Taikoo Sugar factory at Tong Chong Street (ç³–å» è¡—), and later the Swire Coca-Cola factory at Greig Road (åŸºåˆ©è·¯) and Yau Man Street (ä½‘æ°‘è¡—). Lai Chi

The western end of Quarry Bay was historically part of North Point; during the 1930s its beaches became one of the most popular places for holding swimming galas in Hong Kong. From this basis an upmarket entertainment complex, the Ritz Nightclub (éº—æ± å¤œç¸½æœƒ; lai6 chi4 ye6 jung2 wui2), was built in the area in 1947. The nightclub was demolished a few years later to make way for the construction of apartment buildings during the latter half of the 1950s. Nonetheless, for years afterwards, the western part of Quarry Bay continued to be known informally as Lai Chi (éº—æ± ), made more so by the name being homophone to Cantonese for "late as usual" (ä¾‹é²) â€“ a reference to King's Road, until 1984 the only thoroughfare in the area and thus infamous for traffic congestion. To this day, some buildings in the western part of Quarry Bay are named as "North Point something building", although they are across the modern-day limit of North Point at Man Hong Street / Healthy Street West.

Nan Fung Sun Chuen

Nan Fung Sun Chuen (å—è±æ–°é‚¨), built in 1978, is a private apartment estate. Consisting of 2,827 apartment units in 12 buildings distributed along Greig Road and Greig Crescent with a car park at the centre of the development, it was developed by Nan Fung Development.[23] Because of the relatively large size of the development, it serves as the benchmark for premises developed in the late 1970s in the property market. The tower blocks range in height from 28 to 33 floors. Blocks 1 to 5 are at 32 to 40 (even numbers) Greig Road while blocks 6 to 12 are at 27 to 15 Greig Crescent.

Taikoo Shing

Taikoo Shing is a private residential development in Quarry Bay. Consisting of 61 mansions distributed along Taikoo Wan Road & Taikoo Shing Road, it was developed by Swire.

Tourism

Fireboat Alexander Grantham Exhibition Gallery

The Alexander Grantham was a fireboat of Hong Kong's Fire Services Department, serving from 1959 to 2002 as the city's last steam-powered fireboat. The fireboat was named after former Governor Sir Alexander Grantham. The boat has since retired from service and been replaced by other vessels. On 10 March 2006, the 500-tonne fireboat was successfully hoistedâ€”the first project of its kind in Asiaâ€”into its new permanent home in the Central Concourse of Quarry Bay Park, Hong Kong, where it has been converted into the Fireboat Alexander Grantham Exhibition Gallery managed by the Hong Kong Museum of History. Opened to the public as a museum in 2007 with free admission, the 1,200 sq m gallery houses the vessel and multimedia exhibits on its history and firefighting in Hong Kong.

"Monster Building" complex

The "Monster Building" is a residential complex of five interconnected buildings constructed in the late 1960s as part of the Parker Estate. The complex consists of Fook Cheong Building, Montane Mansion, Oceanic Mansion, Yick Cheong Building, and Yick Fat Building, forming a dense E-shaped structure on King's Road. Known for its monolithic appearance, it has become a popular tourist spot and filming location, including in Transformers: Age of Extinction (2014), though visitors are advised to respect residents and avoid private areas.

Woodside Biodiversity Education Centre

Woodside Biodiversity Education Centre, also known as the "Red House," is situated at 50 Mount Parker Road, Quarry Bay. Built in 1922 as a senior staff quarter for the Taikoo Sugar Refinery and restored in 2015 by the Agriculture, Fisheries and Conservation Department (AFCD), the 3,386 sq m centre comprises three themed exhibition galleries introducing Hong Kong's precious natural resources and biodiversity, along with a nature garden, resources centre, and activity room for seminars, workshops, and guided tours. The centre aims to foster public awareness, knowledge, and understanding of the inherent value of Hong Kong's biodiversity assets and to marshal public support and action for nature conservation.[24]

Government

Quarry Bay hosts several government and statutory bodies. The head office of the independent statutory Securities and Futures Commission (SFC) is located at 54/F, One Island East, 18 Westlands Road.[25] The Accounting and Financial Reporting Council (AFRC), another independent statutory body, has its office at 10/F, Two Taikoo Place, 979 King's Road.[26] The Environmental Protection Department (EPD) operates a regional office at Chinachem Exchange Square, 1 Hoi Wan Street.[27] Additionally, the Hong Kong and Islands Division of the Drainage Services Department is based at 23/F, 1063 King's Road.[28] The Countryside Conservation Office and parts of the Environment and Ecology Bureau are also located at Chinachem Exchange Square.[29]

Hiking

Quarry Bay is home to multiple hiking trails, including the Quarry Bay Tree Walk, the Mount Parker Road Green Trail and the Hong Pak Country Trail, which connects further to nearby trails.

Facilities

The Woodside Biodiversity Education Centre (also known as the Red House) is a biodiversity centre with exhibition galleries showcasing Hong Kong's biodiversity and displays of educational resources on local wildlife. The centre is situated on Mount Parker Road, with benches available nearby for picnics.[24] Wartime remains including the Wartime Stoves and Anti-Air-Raid Caves are preserved throughout Mount Parker Road Green Trail and the Hong Pak Country Trail. Hikes throughout Quarry Bay are often packed with resting benches, washrooms, pavilions, maps as well as trash bins.

Mountains

Mount Butler, Mount Parker and Siu Ma Shan are mountains available for hiking. These mountains are linked towards more hiking options such as the Jardine's Lookout or approach for Tai Tam Reservoirs.

Nearby areas

Stanley can be reached from Tai Tam Reservoirs via Violet Hill and The Twins. Shek O Country Park is also reachable from Tai Tam Reservoirs, which offer more hiking options. Tai Tam Reservoirs is situated near the Wong Nai Chung Reservoir Park.
