D-segment

The D-segment is the 4th category of the European segments for passenger cars, and is described as "large cars".[1][2] It is equivalent to the Euro NCAP "large family car" size class,[3] and the present-day definition of the mid-size car category used in North America.[4][5] Compact executive cars are part of the D-segment size category. D-segment sales represented about 7% of the market in the 2010s.[6][7] By 2024, the segment's market share in the EU had declined to about 5.5%.[8]

Characteristics

Most D-segment cars are sedans/saloons or wagons/estates but hatchbacks, and coupes have been common. Pricing and specification of D-segment cars can vary greatly, from basic low-cost transport to more luxurious and expensive models. As of 2021 the typical D-segment category size ranges from about 4.6 to 4.8 m (15.1 to 15.7 ft).

Recent years (2022â€“2024)

The D-segment continued to decline as a share of the EU car market, falling to approximately 4.5% in 2023 and 4.2% in 2024, amid rising SUV popularity. Total registrations for the upper medium segment were around 470,000 units in 2023 and 445,000 in 2024. Top models included the BMW 3 Series (leading with ~110,000 units in 2023) and Tesla Model 3 (~120,000 units in 2023), with increasing adoption of electrified variants.[21][22]

Market Share in Europe

2019

After years of decline, the midsized car segment is actually up 1% in 2019 to 1.05 million sales, maintaining a 6.7% share of the overall car market.[6]

2020

The midsized car segment is down 25% in 2020 to just under 790,000 sales, as its share of the European car market drops to 6.6%, slightly down from 6.7% last year. And luxury brands have now officially taken over control of this class, improving their share to 62.3% from 60.5% last year and claiming four of the top-5 positions. The top-3 players all gain share, outperforming not only the class but also the overall market.[7]

2023

The D-segment market share declined to 6% in 2023, amid rising SUV dominance (54% overall share) and electrification pressures, according to data up to October 2023.[23]
