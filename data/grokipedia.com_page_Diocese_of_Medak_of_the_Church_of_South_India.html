Diocese of Medak of the Church of South India

The Diocese of Medak is one of the prominent dioceses in the Church of South India, a united Protestant church with its headquarters in Medak comprising nearly 250 presbyters ministering to Telugu, Lambadi, Tamil,[2] Kannada, Malayalam,[5] Hindi and Urdu, English[2] and other linguistic groups numbering nearly one-third of a million (as of 2016)[1] spread over 105[1] pastorates and administered through 3 District Church Councils[1] (DCC), namely, the Town DCC, the Medak DCC and the Godavari DCC geographically located in the erstwhile civil districts of Adilabad, Nizamabad, Medak, Rangareddy, Hyderabad and Mahabubnagar in Telangana.

History

Christianity in India

According to tradition, Saint Bartholomew, one of the Twelve Apostles, landed in Maharashtra and began his mission in Kalyan.[6] He was followed by Saint Thomas,[7] who, per the predominant Indian Christian tradition, landed on the Malabar Coast in Kerala in 52 A.D., where he won many converts and established seven churches before traveling to the Coromandel Coast in Tamil Nadu, where he was martyred in 72 A.D. Some accounts, such as the Acts of Thomas, place Thomas's earlier activities in northern India under King Gondophares in regions like Bharuch and Taxila.[8][9] After centuries of inactivity, missions were revived with the arrival of Saint Francis Xavier in 1542, followed by Robert de Nobili in 1605, who arrived in Goa on the western coast. Christianity in Telangana

Catholic missionaries first set foot in the erstwhile Hyderabad State in 1535[2] during the sixteenth century, followed by the American Methodist missions in 1873,[2] the Baptist missions in 1875,[2] the Wesleyan Methodist missions in 1878,[14][2] and later the Mennonite Brethren missions in 1899.[15]

The beginnings of the church

Once the Wesleyan Methodist Missionary Society set foot in the erstwhile Hyderabad State in 1878,[2] the missionaries led by Henry Little, William Burgess, and the Indian Evangelist Benjamin Wesley[2] pioneered the spread of the Gospel and helped establish churches in areas northward of Hyderabad City, winning new converts to Christianity. Meanwhile, efforts were made by visionary pastors to form the Church of South India, for which missionary societies came forward for negotiations, including[16] Anglicans, Baptists, Congregationalists, Lutherans, and Wesleyan Methodists. However, the talks toward Church Union did not go well with the laymen from the Baptists and the Lutherans despite efforts by their clergy[17] led by Professor Muthyala Theophilus, CBCNC, and William Powlas Peery, AELC.[18] Meanwhile, in 1923,[19] the Wesleyan Methodist Missionary Society participated in the negotiations toward Church union and formally agreed to join the Church of South India in 1947, with Frank Whittaker becoming the first Bishop of the Diocese of Medak.

Ministerial formation

The Ministerial Secretary of the Diocese of Medak acts as the Vocation Promoter and for those discerning their avocation towards priesthood, the Diocese of Medak through its Ministerial Secretary examines candidates to determine their preparedness for the priestly vocation at varying points of time. First, candidates are admitted to the Diocese of Medak and attached with a Priest in congregations and after a year or two they are sent for ministerial formation to a seminary affiliated to the Senate of Serampore College (University). Presently, the Diocese of Medak divides candidates between the Andhra Christian Theological College, Secunderabad and to the United Theological College, Bangalore. It was in Medak that the first seminary was established prior to 1947.[22] Incidentally, this College moved over to Dornakal and became the Andhra Union Theological College which later in 1964[23] joined hands with the Anglicans, Baptists, Congregationalists, Lutherans, Methodists and Wesleyan Methodists to form the Andhra Christian Theological College,[23] originally located in Rajahmundry and moved to Secunderabad in 1973.[24] The Diocese is also represented in the United Theological College, Bangalore which was established in 1910.[22] The Diocese of Medak through its scholarly Clergy who have been involved in ministerial formation both at the Andhra Christian Theological College, Secunderabad and at the United Theological College, Bangalore.   • A note on Assistant Bishop - in - Medak (Bishop Bunyan Joseph)
During the 1950s, the Diocese accommodated Bishop Bunyan Joseph of the erstwhile Anantapur-Kurnool Diocese which had since been integrated into Rayalaseema Diocese. He was Assistant[21] Bishop - in - Medak and worked together with Frank Whittaker, Eber Priestley, B. G. Prasada Rao[21] and others. Bunyan Joseph also served as Presbyter - in - Charge at the CSI-Church of St. John the Baptist, Secunderabad during 1956-57[32] and 1960-61[32] until he finally retired in October 1963.[33]
