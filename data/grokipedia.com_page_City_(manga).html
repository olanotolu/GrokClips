City (manga)

City (stylized in all caps) is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Keiichi Arawi. It began serialization in September 2016 in Kodansha's seinen manga magazine Morning. It has been published in 14 tankōbon volumes. In North America, the series is licensed by Kodansha USA. An anime television series adaptation titled City the Animation , produced by Kyoto Animation, aired from July 7 to September 29, 2025. It is the studio's first non-sequel anime in six years, and the studio's second anime adapted from a manga series by Arawi after the 2011 adaptation of Nichijou.

City

Plot

The story follows the Mont Blanc Trio, consisting of university sophomores Midori Nagumo and Wako Izumi and freshman Ayumu Niikura at Mont Blanc University, as they navigate their lives alongside a populace filled with not-quite-ordinary people in the city aptly called City.[6]

Characters

Mont Blanc Trio

  • Midori Nagumo (南雲 美鳥, Nagumo Midori)
    Voiced by: Mikako Komatsu[7] (Japanese); Erica Lindbeck[8] (English)
    A 20-year-old university sophomore at Mont Blanc University. She works as a waitress at the Makabe's Western Bistro, and she is a resident of the Mokumesei Manor.
  • Niikura (にーくら, Niikura)
    Voiced by: Aki Toyosaki[7] (Japanese); Cat Protano[8] (English)
    An 18-year-old freshman at the same university as Nagumo and Wako, who dreams of becoming a photographer. She is also a resident of the Mokumesei Manor.
  • Wako Izumi (泉 わこ, Izumi Wako)
    Voiced by: Yui Ishikawa[7] (Japanese); Brenna Larsen[8] (English)
    Similar to Nagumo, she is a university sophomore at the same university as Nagumo and Niikura and is also a resident of the Mokumesei Manor. However, she has an airhead personality and likes to stalk Nagumo.

Makabe family

  • Tsurubishi Makabe (真壁 鶴菱, Makabe Tsurubishi)
    Voiced by: Yoshihisa Kawahara[9] (Japanese); Brent Mukai[8] (English)
    The owner and head chef of their family restaurant that specializes in Western-style cuisine. He is married to his wife, who is an archaeologist, and father to their children, Tatewaku and Matsuri.
  • Tatewaku Makabe (真壁 立涌, Makabe Tatewaku)
    Voiced by: Miyu Irino[9] (Japanese); Kevin Andrew Rivera[8] (English)
    A second-year high school student and a substitute player on his school's soccer team. He is the older brother to Matsuri. He has a crush on Riko.
  • Matsuri Makabe (真壁 まつり, Makabe Matsuri)
    Voiced by: Ayaka Nanase[9] (Japanese); Sarah Miller-Crews[8] (English)
    A middle school student who spends her free time with Ecchan, her best friend. She is the younger sister to Tatewaku.
  • Obaba (御婆)
    Voiced by: Kazutomi Yamamoto[10] (Japanese); Kaitlyn Robrock (English)
    Tsurubishi's mother-in-law and Tatewaku and Matsuri's grandmother. She is the owner of Mokumesei Manor, an apartment complex where the Mont Blanc Trio resides.

Media

Manga

The series is written and illustrated by Keiichi Arawi and began serialization in Kodansha's Morning magazine on September 29, 2016.[19][20] The series' initial serialization concluded on February 4, 2021.[21][22] Kodansha published the first run in 13 volumes.[23] An online autograph contest was held to celebrate the final volume's release.[24] The series resumed in Morning on December 12, 2024, three years after its conclusion.[25] As of July 2025, 14 volumes have been released.[26] At Anime Expo 2017, Vertical announced they licensed the series for English publication.[27]

Anime

An anime television series adaptation, titled City the Animation , was announced on September 21, 2024. It is produced by Kyoto Animation as the studio's first non-sequel anime since the arson attack in 2019 and directed by Taichi Ishidate, with character designs and chief animation direction handled by Tamami Tokuyama, color designs by Kana Miyata, art direction by Shiori Yamazaki, 3D direction by Tatsunori Kase, sound direction by Yōta Tsuruoka, and music by the Piranhans.[7] The 13-episode series aired from July 7 to September 29, 2025, on Tokyo MX and other networks. The opening theme is "Hello", performed by Furui Riho, while the ending theme is "Lucky", performed by TOMOO.[9][11] Amazon Prime Video streamed the series worldwide.[53]

Reception

At San Diego Comic-Con 2019, Rob McMonigal picked the series as the best continuing series for grown-ups.[56] The early volumes of the manga were praised by most critics. Sean Gaffney from A Case Suitable for Treatment gave the series praise, saying it "made him smile".[57] Ian Wolf from Anime UK News stated that he liked the series, stating that fans of Arawi's style in Nichijou will also like this series.[1] Ross Locksley from UK Anime Network felt similarly about the manga, rating it a seven and saying the rating is a nine for fans of Nichijou and a five for anyone else.[59] However, Katherine Dacey from The Manga Critic was more critical, calling the series' jokes "tired slapstick [humor]" and the characters "strenuously unpleasant".[60] A 2025 retrospective review of Volume 1 by Anime News Network awarded it an overall grade of A-, praising its funny and heartwarming content as well as the cartoonish yet detailed artwork.[6]

Anime

The 2025 anime adaptation, City the Animation, has received positive reception for its vibrant animation, comedic timing, and faithful adaptation of Arawi's style. On IMDb, it holds a 7.8/10 rating from over 450 users as of October 2025, with reviewers describing it as "pure fun" and "chaotic" yet effective in its humor.[61] Critics have highlighted Kyoto Animation's effort, calling it a "feel good" series that blends anarchic comedy with rich storytelling about friendship and community.[62][63] User reviews on MyAnimeList praise it as a "perfect, heartwarming, laugh out loud" comedy and a worthy successor to Nichijou.[64] Some noted its quirky character designs and stylistic choices, such as ultra-flat backgrounds emphasizing movement.[65]
