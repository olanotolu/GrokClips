Kidsgrove

Kidsgrove is an historic market town in the Borough of Newcastle-under-Lyme in Staffordshire, England, with a population of 23,464 (2021 census).[1] The town is situated adjacent to the Staffordshire/Cheshire border. It forms part of the Potteries Urban Area (also known as the Stoke-on-Trent built-up area). The town lies within the Kidsgrove & Ravenscliffe ward.[2]

History

From the 18th century, Kidsgrove grew around coal mining, although the pits have now closed, with the last colliery closing in December 1993. Clough Hall Mansion in the town is now demolished (demolished in 1927). The engineer James Brindley cut the first Harecastle Tunnel on the Trent and Mersey Canal near the town; Thomas Telford cut the second. Kidsgrove also marks the southern extremity of the Macclesfield Canal. There is a legend regarding a headless ghost that is said to haunt the Harecastle Tunnel. The ghost is said to be that of a young woman who was murdered inside the tunnel. She is referred to as the "Kidsgrove Boggart". R.J. Mitchell, the designer of the Spitfire fighter aircraft, was born in Butt Lane, Kidsgrove. Kidsgrove was made an urban district in 1904 with the abolition of the Wolstanton Rural District, including the parishes of Kidsgrove and Newchapel. Talke, previously part of the Audley Urban District, was added in 1932. Kidsgrove railway station was opened by the North Staffordshire Railway on 9 October 1848 as Harecastle , later becoming Kidsgrove Central. There were two other stations on the closed loop line namely Kidsgrove Liverpool Road, opened 15 November 1875 and Market Street Halt, opened 1 July 1909. In 1975, kidnapping victim Lesley Whittle was kept in Bathpool Park, south of Kidsgrove, in an air ventilation shaft from a disused coal mine. She was kept in the shaft for several weeks, with a noose around her neck, and was hanged. Donald Neilson, the killer known as the "Black Panther," was later convicted of murdering her after repeated delays in getting the ransom. The town used to house the English Electric site on West Avenue, Nelson Industrial Estate. Over the years it went through various name changes: GEC, CEGELEC, Alstom, Converteam before finally General Electric. GE closed the Kidsgrove site in 2016[3] and the last remaining original building has since been demolished, leaving only the recently erected warehouse which is now under use of another company.

Transport

Several First Potteries bus routes serve Kidsgrove, with routes 7/7A (Hanley to Kidsgrove) and 36/36A (Meir to Kidsgrove) terminating there as of October 2025. Other routes including 38, 39, and 103 also pass through the town. [4] The railway connects Kidsgrove railway station with Crewe, Manchester, and Stoke-on-Trent. [5] The Trent and Mersey Canal runs through the town. [6] Kidsgrove is also home to a portion of the A50 road and is very close to the A34 and the A500.

Kidsgrove Sports

Sport

Kidsgrove Athletic F.C. play in the Northern Premier League Division One West.[7] Kidsgrove has the Linley & Kidsgrove R.U.F.C. rugby club and the Kidsgrove Cricket Club cricket club.

Media

Since the town is close to the Cheshire-Staffordshire border, some households may receive signals from multiple transmitters, but local news and television programmes are primarily provided by BBC West Midlands and ITV Central, with signals received from the Sutton Coldfield transmitting station.[8] Local radio stations are BBC Radio Stoke, Hits Radio Staffordshire & Cheshire, Greatest Hits Radio Staffordshire & Cheshire, 6 Towns Radio and HitMix Radio, a community-based radio station.[9] The Sentinel is the town's local newspaper.[10]

Kidsgrove Scouts

Kidsgrove is home to the 1st Kidsgrove Scout Group in the Potteries North district, which traces its origins to around 1910 (with formal leadership established in 1914). The affiliated Kidsgrove Scouts Drum & Bugle Corps has undertaken regular tours to the DCA World Championships in the US. As of 2025, the corps continues to compete in Drum Corps United Kingdom (DCUK) events and international competitions.

Local governance

Kidsgrove is situated in North West Staffordshire on the Cheshire border and is part of the Borough of Newcastle-under-Lyme. The Wards of Kidsgrove & Ravenscliffe, Butt Lane & Talke and Newchapel & Mow Cop are in the Stoke-on-Trent North Parliamentary Constituency represented by David Williams. Following boundary changes that took effect for the 2024 general election, parts of Mow Cop are now in the Congleton constituency, represented by Sarah Russell, while the rest remains in Stoke-on-Trent North.[11][12] Kidsgrove has a Town Council of twenty members, separated into four Wards, Talke and Butt Lane, Kidsgrove Central & Ravenscliffe, Hardings Wood and Newchapel & Mow Cop. Kidsgrove Town Council was created in 1974 when Kidsgrove Urban District Council was abolished when the area was absorbed by Newcastle Under Lyme Borough Council under the Local Government Act 1972. Kidsgrove has a mayor who is elected by the Town Council every 12 months. This position is mainly a civic role, and the Mayor acts as an ambassador for Kidsgrove. Kidsgrove Town Council is based at Kidsgrove Town Hall. [13]

Rotary Kidsgrove

Founded on 15 January 1969 and presented with its charter on 2 July the same year, the Rotary Club of Kidsgrove (RCK) has been active within the community whether fundraising, volunteering or helping out local projects for over 50 years. The club is also part of District 1210 within Rotary International in Britain and Ireland. Originally meeting in the Masonic Hall and Institute in the town, the group now meets at The Bleeding Wolf in Scholar Green.[14] During November RCK hold a bonfire night and fireworks at Clough Hall Park and during December they can be seen on the streets of Kidsgrove with their Santa float, collecting for local good causes. The club continues to organize these events annually, including the bonfire and fireworks display scheduled for 1 November 2025.[15] In April 2012 RCK were also announced as the Rotary International in Britain and Ireland winners of the Club Online Presence Award. This was the first time the award has been presented and recognises the achievements the group have made through their website and a variety of social media avenues in the year.
