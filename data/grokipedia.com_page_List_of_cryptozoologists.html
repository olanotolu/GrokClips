List of cryptozoologists

This is an alphabetical list of notable cryptozoologists and people associated with the field of cryptozoology, including prominent skeptics and hoaxers.

A

  • Michael M. Ames (1933–2006), Canadian anthropologist and co-editor of Manlike Monsters on Trial: Early Records and Modern Evidence [1]

B

  • Cliff Barackman, investigator on Finding Bigfoot[2]
  • Henry H. Bauer (b. 1931), American chemist, professor, and Loch Ness Monster researcher[3]
  • Jon-Erik Beckjord (1939–2008), American cryptozoologist and ufologist
  • John Bindernagel (1941–2018), Canadian wildlife biologist and Bigfoot researcher; author of North America's Great Ape: the Sasquatch[4][5][6]
  • Tom Biscardi (b. 1948), Bigfoot researcher involved in multiple hoaxes. Well known as a hoaxer himself.[7]
  • John Blashford-Snell (b. 1936), British explorer and Life President of the Centre for Fortean Zoology[1][8]
  • Joshua Blu Buhs, Bigfoot skeptic and author of Bigfoot: The Life and Times of a Legend[9][6][10]
  • Neville Bonney, Australian botanist and Tantanoola Tiger researcher[1]
  • Maurice Burton (1898–1992), author of The Elusive Monster: An Analysis of the Evidence from Loch Ness and Loch Ness Monster skeptic[3][11]
  • R. G. Burton, Brigadier General in the British Army and author of several texts on unidentified dogs, wolves, and other canines[1]
  • Peter C. Byrne (1925–2023), explorer, media personality, Bigfoot researcher.[1][12][13]

C

  • Robert Todd Carroll (1945–2016), author of The Skeptic's Dictionary and Bigfoot skeptic[14]
  • David Hatcher Childress (b. 1957), American pseudoarchaeologist and cryptozoologist[15]
  • Jerome Clark (b. 1946), American ufologist and author of over a dozen books on paranormal phenomena including Cryptozoology A to Z[16]
  • John Colarusso, Canadian linguist and author of Ethnographic Information on a Wild Man of the Caucasus[1]
  • Loren Coleman (b. 1947), author of several books on cryptozoology and notable cryptozoologists[17][18][19]
  • John Robert Colombo (b. 1936), Canadian writer and author of Mysteries of Ontario[20]
  • John Conway, Australian palaeoartist and co-author of Cryptozoologicon[21]
  • Peter Costello (author) (b. 1940), British author and cryptozoologist known for In Search of Lake Monsters)
  • William R. Corliss (1926–2011), anomalist[22] and author of many books pertaining to unexplained phenomena in the natural world[23]
  • Paul Cropper, Australian cryptozoologist and author of Out of the Shadows: Mystery Animals of Australia[1][24][25]

D

  • David J. Daegling, American anthropologist who has performed research on Bigfoot video evidence[5]
  • René Dahinden (1930–2001), Swiss-Canadian Bigfoot researcher[6][26]
  • Vine Deloria Jr. (1933–2005), Dakota activist and proponent of fossil giants[15][27]
  • Tim Dinsdale (1924–1987), Loch Ness Monster researcher[3]
  • Rick Dyer, American businessman and Bigfoot hoaxer[7][28]

E

  • Richard Ellis (1938–2024), marine life artist and author of Monsters of the Sea and The Search for the Giant Squid.[29][30]

F

  • Tim Fasano (1956–2019), American taxi driver, blogger, and Bigfoot researcher[31]
  • James "Bobo" Fay, host of Finding Bigfoot [32]
  • Paul Freeman (1943–2003), Bigfoot researcher[33]
  • Richard Freeman (b. 1970), British cryptozoologist and Centre for Fortean Zoology researcher [34]

G

  • Clive Gamble (b. 1951), British anthropologist; wild men skeptic and co-author of In Search of the Neanderthals[35]
  • Josh Gates (b. 1977), host of paranormal and cryptozoology-focused television shows including Destination Truth[36]
  • Albert Samuel Gatschet (1832–1907), Swiss-American anthropologist and lake monster researcher[1]
  • Ken Gerhard (b. 1967), Centre for Fortean Zoology researcher and author of several books including Big Bird!: Modern Sightings of Flying Monsters[37]
  • Bob Gimlin (b. 1931), American horse trainer who controversially claims to have filmed a living Bigfoot in 1967[38][39][4][6]
  • Rupert Gould (1890–1948), Loch Ness Monster researcher[3]
  • John Willison Green (1927–2016), Canadian journalist and Bigfoot researcher[4][5][6][18]
  • J. Richard Greenwell (1942–2005), secretary of the International Society of Cryptozoology[5][18][40]

List of Cryptozoologists (H–I Section)

H–I

  • Gathorne Gathorne-Hardy, 5th Earl of Cranbrook (born 1933), British naturalist and Beruang Rambai researcher[1]
  • Bernard Heuvelmans (1916–2001), Belgian-French cryptozoologist and author of several books on the topic including On the Track of Unknown Animals[1][6][9][41]
  • William Charles Osman Hill (1901–1975), British primatologist and cryptozoologist[12]
  • Frederick William Holiday (1921–1979), English journalist and Loch Ness Monster researcher[1]
  • Ranae Holland, host of Finding Bigfoot[42]
  • Trader Horn (1861–1931), English ivory trader and explorer; proponent of the Amali[1]

J–K

  • Barnaby Jones, cryptozoologist, author, founder of Cryptids Anomalies and the Paranormal Society, host of Monsters on the Edge podcast[43]
  • John Keel (1930–2009), American ufologist and Mothman researcher; author of The Mothman Prophecies
  • Aleksandr Kondratov (1937–1993), Russian scientist and proponent of living dinosaurs; author of Динозавра ищите в глубинах (English: Dinosaurs in the Depths)[44]
  • C. M. Kosemen (b. 1984), Turkish artist and co-author of Cryptozoologicon[21]
  • Grover Krantz (1931–2002), American physical anthropologist and Bigfoot researcher[1][5][6][45]
  • Laura Krantz, relative of Grover Krantz and host of Wild Thing[46]

L

  • Richard S. Lambert (1894–1981), English-Canadian writer; author of Exploring the Supernatural: The Weird in Canadian Folklore[20]
  • Rula Lenska (b. 1947), Polish-English actor and conservation activist; co-author of Mammoth Hunt: In Search of the Giant Elephants of Nepal[10]
  • Willy Ley (1906–1969), German-American science writer and author of several texts on cryptozoology, including Exotic Zoology[1]
  • Daniel Loxton (b. 1975), Canadian writer and cryptozoology skeptic; co-author of Abominable Science!: Origins of the Yeti, Nessie, and Other Famous Cryptids[9]

M

  • Roy Mackal (1925–2013), University of Chicago professor known for interest in Loch Ness Monster and Mokele mbembe[1]
  • Vladimir Markotic (1920–1994), Croatian-American anthropologist and cryptozoologist[1]
  • Adrienne Mayor (born 1946), author of Fossil Legends of the First Americans and cryptozoology skeptic[1][47]
  • Jim McClarin, American Bigfoot researcher[4]
  • Jeffrey Meldrum (born 1958), Idaho State University anatomy and anthropology professor; Bigfoot researcher[4][5][6][48][45]
  • Reinhold Messner (born 1944), Italian mountaineer; Yeti skeptic and author of My Quest for the Yeti[1]
  • Marc Wolfgang Miller, American explorer and cryptozoologist[1]
  • Matthew Moneymaker, founder of the Bigfoot Field Researchers Organization and host of Finding Bigfoot[5][14][31]

N

  • Darren Naish (b. 1975), British palaeontologist and cryptid skeptic; author of Hunting Monsters: Cryptozoology and the Reality Behind the Myths and Cryptozoologicon[1][9]
  • John R. Napier (1917–1987), primatologist and Bigfoot researcher[1]
  • Joe Nickell (1944–2025), American paranormal skeptic and co-author of Lake Monster Mysteries: Investigating the World's Most Elusive Creatures[9][49]
  • Rory Nugent (b. 1952), American explorer and Mokele-mbembe researcher[1]

Anthonie Cornelis Oudemans

O

Anthonie Cornelis Oudemans (1858–1943), Dutch zoologist and sea serpent researcher[1]

P–Q

  • Roger Patterson (1933–1972), Bigfoot researcher who controversially claimed to have filmed Bigfoot in 1967[38][39][6]
  • Boris Porshnev (1905–1972), Soviet historian and cryptozoologist[1][50][51][52]
  • Donald Prothero (b. 1954), American palaeontologist and cryptozoology skeptic; co-author of Abominable Science!: Origins of the Yeti, Nessie, and Other Famous Cryptids[9]
  • Robert Michael Pyle (b. 1947), American lepidopterist and Bigfoot researcher; author of Where Bigfoot Walks: Crossing the Dark Divide[1][6]

R

  • Benjamin Radford (b. 1970), American writer and cryptozoology skeptic; author of Bigfoot at 50: Evaluating the Evidence[1][9][45]
  • Brian Regal, American science historian and cryptozoology skeptic.[6] author of Searching for Sasquatch: Crackpots, Eggheads and Cryptozoology
  • Bob Rickard, British writer and former editor of the Fortean Times[1][53][54]
  • Byambyn Rinchen (1905–1977), Mongolian scholar and proponent of Almas research[1][50]
  • Robert H. Rines (1922–2009), Loch Ness Monster researcher[55]

S

  • Ivan T. Sanderson (1911–1973), paranormal writer and cryptozoologist[1][53][15]
  • Esteban Sarmiento, primatologist who has researched Bigfoot evidence[45]
  • Peter Scott (1909–1989), co-founder of the Loch Ness Phenomena Investigation Bureau[55]
  • Daniel O. Schmitt, American anthropologist who has performed research on Bigfoot video evidence[5]
  • Frank Searle (1921-2005) spent 14 years looking daily for the Loch Ness Monster[56]
  • Eduard Seler (1849–1922), German anthropologist and cryptozoology skeptic[1]
  • Myra Shackley (b. 1949), British archaeologist and author on wildmen and Bigfoot; author of Wildmen: Yeti, Sasquatch, and the Neanderthal Enigma
  • Tony "Doc" Shiels (1938–2024), British magician and cryptozoologist[53]
  • Karl Shuker (b. 1959), British cryptozoologist and author[1][25]
  • Paul Sieveking (b. 1949), British writer and former editor of the Fortean Times[1][54]
  • Tom Slick (1916–1962), American adventurer and cryptozoologist[18]
  • Roderick Sprague (1933–2012), American anthropologist and Bigfoot researcher; author of The Scientist Looks at Sasquatch[1]
  • Chris Stringer (b. 1947), British anthropologist; wild men skeptic and co-author of In Search of the Neanderthals[35]
  • Daris Swindler (1925–2007), American anthropologist and Bigfoot skeptic[45]

T

  • Odette Tchernine (1897–1992), British author and cryptozoologist[1]
  • Édouard Louis Trouessart (1842–1927), French zoologist and early proponent of a cryptid in Lake Chad[1]
  • Marcello Truzzi (1935–2003), skeptic[22] and founder of several research groups including the Committee for the Scientific Investigation of Claims of the Paranormal (CSICOP)
  • Jamsrangiin Tseveen (1880–1942), Buryat scientist and Almas researcher[50][57]
  • Frank Turk (1911–1996), British phantom cat researcher[1]

W

  • M. A. Wetherell (1883–1939), leader of a 1933 expedition to find the Loch Ness Monster and hoaxer behind the "surgeon's photograph"[55]
  • Ezekiel Stone Wiggins (1839–1910), Canadian psychic and cryptozoologist[20]
  • Paul Willis, Australian science communicator and Yowie skeptic[6]
  • Nicholas Witchell (b. 1953), English journalist and Loch Ness Monster researcher[55]

Y–Z

  • Zhou Guoxing – Chinese paleoanthropologist known for researching the yeren (Chinese wildman).[58]
