How to Get Away with Murder season 5

The fifth season of the ABC American television drama series How to Get Away with Murder was ordered on May 11, 2018, by ABC. It began airing on September 27, 2018, with 15 episodes like the previous seasons and concluded on February 28, 2019. This was made in a deal with Viola Davis that the series would be a limited series with only 15 or 16 episodes per season.

How to Get Away with Murder (season 5)

Plot

The fifth season is set three years after Annalise Keating's (Viola Davis) successful class action lawsuit before the Supreme Court. Returning to Middleton University, Annalise teaches a seminar on civil actions and is recruited by the Governor of Pennsylvania for a high-profile position, while facing political opposition. She takes on a pro bono case involving a death row inmate whose conviction is challenged by new evidence. The "Keating Four"â€”Michaela Pratt (Aja Naomi King), Connor Walsh (Jack Falahee), Asher Millstone (Matt McGorry), and Laurel Castillo (Karla Souza)â€”have graduated and are advancing in their careers: Michaela as a law clerk, Connor working in tech with his fiancÃ© Oliver Hampton (Conrad Ricamora), Asher as an assistant district attorney, and Laurel grappling with family secrets surrounding her infant son. A new group of students joins Annalise's class, including Gabriel Maddox (Rome Flynn), whose arrival stirs tensions. The season explores Nate LaHey's (Billy Brown) relentless investigation into the murder of his father, implicating members of Annalise's circle and drawing in the FBI under Agent Nora Pollock (Marcia Gay Harden). Bonnie Winterbottom (Liza Weil) confronts her past traumas, and Frank Delfino (Charlie Weber) navigates loyalty conflicts. Personal milestones, such as Connor and Oliver's wedding plans, intersect with escalating threats from the powerful Castillo family and revelations about past crimes.[1][2]

How to Get Away with Murder (Season 5)

Production

Development

How to Get Away with Murder was renewed for a fifth season on May 11, 2018, by ABC. In March 2018, series creator and showrunner Peter Nowalk told Deadline Hollywood his plans for the fifth season, confirming that the wedding between Jack Falahee and Conrad Ricamora's characters would take place, as well as a flashback episode further exploring the marriage between Viola Davis and Tom Verica's characters Annalise and Sam Keating, respectively. Additionally, he stated that the season's flashforwards were expected to be "twisty, and more fun", and tonally different. The first table read occurred on July 13, 2018.

Casting

In June 2018, Rome Flynn was promoted to the series' main cast after appearing in a guest capacity in the fourth-season finale. In July 2018, Amirah Vann was promoted to the series' main cast after recurring in the fourth season. Later that month, Timothy Hutton joined the main cast.

Filming

Filming for the season started on July 19, 2018.

Reception

Critical response

On review aggregator Rotten Tomatoes, the fifth season has a rating of 86%, based on 7 reviews.[2] Writing for The A.V. Club, Kayla Kumari Upadhyaya criticized the "messy" storytelling of the season and the series as a whole.
