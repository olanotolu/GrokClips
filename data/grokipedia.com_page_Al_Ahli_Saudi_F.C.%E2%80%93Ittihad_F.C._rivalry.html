Jeddah Derby

The Jeddah Derby also known as the Red Sea Derby (Arabic: ديربي جدة, romanized: derbi jeddah), is the football matches contested between Al-Ahli and Al-Ittihad, both from Jeddah, Saudi Arabia. The rivalry started in 1938 and is considered to be one of the strongest and most viewed matches in Asia and the Arab world. The rivalry was temporarily halted during the 2022–23 season when Al-Ahli were relegated to the Saudi First Division League for the first time in their history, but Al-Ahli won promotion back to the Saudi Pro League for the 2023–24 season, resuming top-flight derbies.[3]

History

The first-ever match between these two teams was in 1951. The two teams played a friendly match in Al-Saban Stadium in Jeddah which ended 0–0. In 1958, both teams met each other in the final of the Crown Prince Cup in what would be the first final between these two teams. Over 12,000 fans attended the match at the Al-Saban Stadium in Jeddah. Al-Ittihad defeated Al-Ahli 3–2 to lift their first Crown Prince Cup trophy. The first league match between the two sides was during the first season of the Saudi League, the 1976–77 season. Al-Ahli won the match 2–0 thanks to goals from Saad Al-Harbi and Idris Adam. Throughout the late 20th and early 21st centuries, the Jeddah Derby became a fixture of Saudi football, with numerous intense encounters in league and cup competitions. Notable moments include Al-Ittihad's largest victory of 8–2 in the 1966 Regional League, and Al-Ahli's response with significant wins in subsequent decades. The rivalry intensified with multiple cup finals and league clashes, contributing to its status as one of Asia's most viewed matches. The derby faced a major interruption when Al-Ahli were relegated from the Saudi Pro League at the end of the 2021–22 season, marking their first-ever drop to the First Division. This halted top-flight meetings until Al-Ahli secured promotion by winning the 2022–23 Saudi First Division League. The rivalry resumed in the 2023–24 Saudi Pro League season, with the teams contesting matches thereafter, including a 2–2 draw on 5 April 2025.
