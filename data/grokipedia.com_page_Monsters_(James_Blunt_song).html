Monsters (James Blunt song)

"Monsters" is a song by English singer-songwriter James Blunt. It was written by Blunt, Amy Wadge and Jimmy Hogarth for Blunt's sixth studio album, Once Upon a Mind (2019).[1] It was released as the fourth single from the album on 18 October 2019.[2] Blunt chose to donate all of the profit made from the song to Help for Heroes and British Legion charities.[3]

Background

James Blunt's father Charles was diagnosed with stage four chronic kidney disease.[4] Blunt wrote "Monsters" to "express his feelings about his father and his illness, almost as if it's a touching farewell to his father".[5] In an interview with Good Morning Britain, Blunt said:[4]
Really that has been an amazing moment. Because when you realise your father's mortality it's a great opportunity to say the things I'd like to say to him. So I have written a song called Monsters for him.
In the same interview he made a plea for type O kidney donors to step forward.[4] In January 2020 MSN reported Blunt's father had been scheduled for a transplant.[6] He underwent the transplant successfully later that year and, as of 2025, is reported to be doing well.[7]

Music video

The music video for "Monsters" was released on 15 January 2020. [8] It was filmed in Henley, Oxfordshire, United Kingdom, and directed by Vaughan Arnell. [9] The music video features James Blunt and his father Charles Blount.[5] The video focuses on a close up of James Blunt's face as he sings the first two verses and choruses of the song, and then switches to a wider angle showing himself and his father sitting at a table while he sings the final part.[5] Throughout the video, Blunt is visibly emotional and tearful.[10] Australian television presenter Lisa Wilkinson said she was "left bawling" after watching the music video of the song.[11] Monika Barton of Newshub wrote about the music video, "Go on, have a little watch and see if you have a heart, or merely just a sharp, pointy piece of flint where it should be".[12]

Reception

Minnie Wright of the Daily Express wrote, "Heartbreaking perspective illuminates the place from which Blunt has penned the deeply personal Once Upon A Mind with the arrival of Monster".[13] Lauren Murphy of Entertainment.ie wrote, "Before you watch this video, be warned that it's pretty tough going if you're feeling in any way emotionally fragile, or have lost a parent".[14] Rudi Kinsella of Joe wrote, "This is one of the most emotional music videos we've seen in a very long time".[15]

2023 revival

In 2023, the song gained renewed attention through Iam Tongi's performance on the finale of American Idol season 21, where Tongi, the season's winner, duetted with Blunt.[16][17] The performance was described as a "full-circle moment" and emotionally moving, bringing judges Katy Perry and Jelly Roll to tears.[18][17] The duet version was later released as a single, debuting on the Billboard charts.[19]

Monsters (song) in popular culture

American Idol Season 21 winner Iam Tongi auditioned with this song, which went viral and became the show's most viewed audition video on its YouTube page. Tongi dedicated the song to his dad, who had passed away from kidney disease a few months prior to the audition.[22][18] Blunt did not know that Tongi would audition with "Monsters" until he learned that the song briefly topped the U.S. iTunes chart after the audition aired. He described Tongi's audition as "deeply moving... you can't help but be touched. I was like everyone else when I watched him do it, I became a fan."[23] Blunt and Tongi performed a duet of the song on Idol 's season finale, with the pair trading lyrics until the latter broke down during the middle of the song. Tongi eventually regained his composure and finished, earning a hug from Blunt at the end. Blunt said of the performance,
"Breathe a last word/You can feel my hand on your own... That was the bit I sang on American Idol with Iam Tongi and he broke down in tears, thinking about his own father. You suddenly realize the desperation of that moment, because, for him, his father had died and [was] really living that."[16][17][23]
The song appeared on Billboard 's Digital Songs chart at No. 4, from 10,000 downloads.[19] Blunt and Tongi released a studio version of their duet on August 25, 2023. Tongi wrote on social media, "From covering it on TikTok 3 years ago to singing it in my audition on @americanidol, to performing it at the Idol finale with @jamesblunt , â€œMonstersâ€ means so much to me."[18] On May 1, 2024, Tongi performed a duet of "Monsters" with fellow contestant Oliver Steele at Steele's father Toby's funeral. Steele posted the performance on his Instagram account, drawing condolences and praise from Idol fans and alumni. He wrote, "I'll be honest, I wasn't sure I was gonna be able to stand on stage and sing. Having @wtongi there to stand beside me helped me stay strong for my father. I love you brother. Thank you for being there for me, and for being there for my dad."[24]
