John Parker Wilson

John Parker Wilson (born October 17, 1985) is an American former professional football player who was a quarterback in the National Football League (NFL). Wilson played college football at the University of Alabama. He went undrafted during the 2009 NFL draft before signing with the Atlanta Falcons. After his playing career, Wilson worked in finance, scouting, and broadcasting, serving as a color commentator for Alabama football from 2018 to 2023 and currently as an analyst for AL.com.[1][2]

College career

Freshman season

After helping lead Hoover High School to high school state championships in 2002 and 2003, Wilson accepted a scholarship offer to play for the Alabama Crimson Tide.[3][4] At the time, he also had scholarship offers from several Southeastern Conference schools, including Alabama's rival, Tennessee.[4] He saw limited playing time during his freshman year, backing up full-time starter Brodie Croyle. His first career touchdown came against South Carolina, a 36-yard pass to wide receiver Keith Brown, in Alabama's 37â€“14 rout of the Gamecocks.[5] Wilson led a late touchdown drive against Auburn when Alabama was behind 28â€“10 and he added a two-point conversion pass to make the final score 28â€“18.[6] He would finish the season with only 98 yards and two touchdowns.[7]

Sophomore season

In 2006, Wilson started all 13 games for the Crimson Tide. He finished the season with a passing efficiency of 128.92, completing 216â€“ofâ€“379 passes for 2,707 yards, 17 touchdowns, and ten interceptions. He threw for over 200 yards in the first seven games of the season, setting a new school record, and finished with a school record nine 200-yard games.[8][9]
  • His best game was at Arkansas (completing 80.0% of his passes for 243 yards, three touchdowns, and no interceptions)[10]
  • He had strong performances at LSU (completing 62.9% of his passes, throwing for 291 yards, two touchdowns, and 1 interception), and against Ole Miss (completing 57.1% of his passes, throwing for 206 yards, two touchdowns and no interceptions)[11][12]
  • He had a solid performance in a loss against rival Auburn (completing 54.5% of his passes for 252 yards, two touchdowns, and one interception). Wilson also fumbled twice on sacks against Auburn and both fumbles were recovered by Auburn which set up touchdowns for Auburn. He was intercepted by David Irons late in the fourth quarter to seal the win for Auburn.[13]
  • His worst games were against Mississippi State (completing only 48.7% of his passes and throwing two interceptions), at Florida (completing 52.5% of his passes, but throwing three interceptions), and at Tennessee (completing only 44.8% of his passes).[14][15][16]

Atlanta Falcons

Wilson was signed by the Atlanta Falcons after going undrafted in the 2009 NFL draft.[30] He did not play in any regular season games. Wilson played most of the game during the 2010 preseason game against the New England Patriots. He assisted in scoring one touchdown during that game.[31] He was released during final cuts on September 3, 2011, and re-signed to the Falcons' practice squad the following day.[32][33] On December 2, 2011, it was reported that the Vikings tried to sign Wilson off of the Falcons practice squad. Instead, Wilson chose to sign to the Falcons' 53 man roster.[34] On August 26, 2012, he was waived by the Falcons in order for the team to maintain a league mandatory 75-man roster during the preseason.[35]

Jacksonville Jaguars

Wilson was signed to the Jacksonville Jaguars practice squad on September 1, 2012.[36] He was promoted to the active roster on October 27 and released on October 29.[37][38] He was signed back to the practice squad on October 31. Wilson was signed to the active roster after the conclusion of the 2012 season. He was released by the team on February 27, 2013.[39]

Pittsburgh Steelers

One day after being released by the Jacksonville Jaguars, the Pittsburgh Steelers claimed Wilson off waivers.[40] The Steelers were the only NFL franchise to make a claim for Wilson.[41] Wilson was later released by the Steelers on August 25, 2013.[42] After his time with the Steelers, Wilson did not sign with another NFL team.

Personal life

After his football career, Wilson became a financial adviser. As of 2025, he works for Morgan Stanley, where he was recognized on Forbes' Best-In-State Wealth Advisors list.[43][44][45] In 2016, Wilson rejoined Alabama to assist in practice with the scout team, mimicking the mannerisms and play of LSU quarterback Danny Etling to prepare the team.[46] On July 26, 2018, Wilson agreed to become the color commentator for Alabama football radio broadcasts, a role he held until stepping away after the 2022 season.[47][48] As of 2025, Wilson serves as an Alabama football analyst for AL.com.[1] Wilson is married and lives with his wife in Birmingham.[44]
