Phuket (city)

Phuket (/puːˈkɛt/ poo- KET; Thai: เทศบาลนครภูเก็ต or ภูเก็ต, pronounced [pʰūː.kèt]) is a city in the southeast of Phuket Island, Thailand, and the capital of Phuket province. Designated a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy in 2023, it is renowned for its Sino-Portuguese architecture and vibrant food scene. As of 2019, the city municipality had a population of 79,308[2] and an urban population of 252,515 in the entire district of Amphoe Mueang. When considering the broader metropolitan area, the population is approximately 455,000 (2024 est.)[3]. Phuket is 862 km (535.6 mi) south of Bangkok.[4]

History

Phuket has a history dating back centuries, with significant development as a port on the west coast of the Malay Peninsula where Chinese immigrants arrived in large numbers during the 19th-century tin mining boom. Phuket Old Town is a quarter studded with heritage buildings along core streets including Thalang, Phang Nga, Dibuk, Krabi, Yaowarat, Ratsada, Phuket, and Soi Romanee. These older buildings show Phuket town's former prosperity. They were constructed when tin mining was an important industry on the island. Their architectural style is called "Sino-Portuguese", characteristic of which is a single or two-storey building with a narrow front compensated for by considerable depth. The tiles, doors, perforated windows, and other details are all influenced by Chinese and European styles combined.[5] "Phuket Old Town" is a 2.7 km2 area. In 2019, the Fine Arts Department and the Phuket provincial authorities announced plans to prepare a proposal to the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) for Phuket Old Town to be listed as a World Heritage Site, but as of 2025, it remains unsubmitted.[6] In 2004, the town was elevated to city status (thesaban nakhon , Thai: เทศบาลนคร).

Culture

Phuket's culture is a vibrant blend of Thai, Chinese (particularly Peranakan), and Malay influences, shaped by its history as a trading and tin-mining hub. The city features a diverse array of religious sites, including Buddhist temples, Chinese shrines, mosques, and smaller Hindu shrines, reflecting its multicultural population. The predominant religion is Theravada Buddhism.[7] Buddhist temples in the city, such as Wat Chalong, are major attractions for national and international tourists.[8] Along streets and within temple complexes, Hindu shrines depicting statues of Ganesha and Brahma can also be seen.[9] Phuket Old Town is renowned for its Sino-Portuguese architecture, characterized by colorful shophouses with ornate facades, intricate stucco work, and a fusion of Chinese and European design elements developed during the 19th-century tin mining boom.[5][6] The city annually hosts the Phuket Vegetarian Festival, a nine-day Chinese observance of the Nine Emperor Gods Festival typically held in October. The event features dramatic processions, fire-walking rituals, and widespread vegetarian cuisine, drawing participants and visitors to shrines like Jui Tui Shrine.[10]

Climate

Phuket has a tropical savanna climate (Köppen Aw), characterized by high temperatures year-round, a pronounced wet season from May to October, and a drier period from November to April. Recent years have seen intensified heatwaves, with the all-time high temperature of 39.4 °C recorded in April 2024.[11]

As of 2024, the city municipality population was reported as 71,284, suggesting a recent decline, though this figure lacks a specific source in the article. The broader Mueang Phuket district had an urban population of 252,515, while the province reached 424,009.[[17] Phuket city has a diverse population, including people of Thai, Chinese (particularly Hokkien descent), and Malay Muslim backgrounds, reflecting its historical trading port status (covered in the History section).

Transportation in Phuket

Transportation

Locals and tourists alike mainly use songthaews and local buses to travel around. Electric buses, which replaced the former pink songthaews in June 2025, serve routes around Phuket Old Town, while blue songthaews connect Phuket Old Town with the various beach resorts around the island.[18][19] Phuket International Airport is approximately 36 kilometres (22 mi) northwest of Phuket Old Town. As of 2025, there is an hourly scheduled bus service between the airport and Phuket Old Town.[20]
