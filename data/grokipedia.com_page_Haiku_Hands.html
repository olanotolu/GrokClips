Haiku Hands

Haiku Hands are an alternative dance electronic music trio from Sydney and Melbourne, Australia. The group consists of Claire Nakazawa, Mie Nakazawa, and Beatrice Lewis.[1] The Nakazawa sisters are from Sydney and Lewis is from Melbourne.[2]

Touring

The group toured nationally with the Groovin' the Moo and Listen Out music festivals. They have also played festivals including Splendour in the Grass, St Jerome's Laneway Festival, Wonderland Scarehouse Tour, WOMADelaide, Pitch Festival and Party In The Paddock. Haiku Hands have supported Flight Facilities and Bloc Party on their Australian tours.[3][4][5] In 2019, they toured with Sofi Tukker as support on the R.I.P. Shame World Tour.[6] Haiku Hands have continued touring nationally and internationally in subsequent years. In 2022, they performed at WOMADelaide. They appeared at the Governors Ball Music Festival in New York in June 2023. In early 2024, the group completed a headline tour of the UK and Australia, followed by a performance at the Sweet Relief! festival in Brisbane in September 2024.[7][8]

Members

Haiku Hands is an Australian electronic music trio consisting of sisters Claire Nakazawa and Mie Nakazawa from Sydney, and Beatrice Lewis from Melbourne.[2] Beatrice Lewis serves as the primary producer, while Claire and Mie Nakazawa are the MCs and vocalists.[3] In earlier years, Mataya Young performed with the group for select shows, particularly in Australia.[9]
