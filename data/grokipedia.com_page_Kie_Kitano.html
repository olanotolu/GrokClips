Kie Kitano

Kie Kitano (北乃 きい, Kitano Kii; born March 15, 1991)[1] is a Japanese actress and singer. She began her career as a gravure idol, winning the Miss Magazine 2005 Grand Prix.

Career

Kitano was born in Kanagawa Prefecture, and is a former member of the celebrity girls futsal team Miss Magazine. In 2005, Kitano won a Miss Magazine Award,[1] becoming the youngest (age 14) to receive that award. In 2007, Kitano was chosen to star for the first time in the drama Life. To properly portray her character, Kitano saved up and bought every volume of the manga on which the drama is based. With Happy Dining Table, she won the 31st Japan Academy Prize in the Newcomer of the Year category.[2] In February 2008, she also won Best New Talent at the 29th Yokohama Film Festival for the same film.[3] After acting in Life and Kofuku no Shokutaku, she appeared in the promotional video for the single released on October 10, 2007 by LITTLE. Between December 2007 and January 2008, she served as the third cheering manager for Japan's High School Soccer Tournament, following Maki Horikita and Yui Aragaki. She was nominated for Best Supporting Actress at the Milano Film Festival in 2014 for her role as a music producer's assistant in The Tenor Lirico Spinto.[4] Kitano has continued her acting career into the 2020s, appearing in the Netflix series Burn the House Down (2023) and the film Top Sword (2024), among other projects.[5]
