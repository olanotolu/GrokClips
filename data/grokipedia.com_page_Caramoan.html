Caramoan

Caramoan, officially the Municipality of Caramoan (Central Bikol: Banwaan kan Caramoan; Tagalog: Bayan ng Caramoan), is a municipality in the province of Camarines Sur, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 51,728 people.[7]

History of Caramoan

According to local tradition, the name Caramoan has been used since 1619, when Spanish Franciscan missionary friar Francisco de la Cruz y Oropesa is said to have founded a settlement in Baluarte on the Caramoan Peninsula. This settlement was reportedly transferred to the administration of the Holy Bishopric in 1696. Due to frequent Moro raids, the town was relocated to its present site around 1687.[8] Prior to Spanish arrival, the area was known as Guta de Leche, possibly derived from the milkdrop stalagmites found among the rocks of Guta Port. Upon the arrival of the Spaniards, the place came to be called "Carahan," referring to the sea turtles abundant along the peninsula's shores at the time. During the Philippine-American War and World War II, Caramoan experienced occupation and local resistance efforts, though specific details remain limited in records.

Geography

Caramoan is bounded on the north by the town of Garchitorena (formerly part of Caramoan) and the Philippine Sea of the Pacific Ocean; on the north-east by the island province of Catanduanes; on the south by Lagonoy Gulf; on the east by the Maqueda Channel and on the west by the municipality of Presentacion. The municipality covers approximately 276.00 square kilometres (106.56 sq mi) with approximately 71 kilometres (44 mi) of irregular coastline surrounded by the vast ocean, bay, seas and swamps. It is approximately 500 kilometres (310 mi) from Metro Manila; 118 kilometres (73 mi) from the municipality of Pili, where the seat of the provincial government and the Naga Domestic Airport are located; and 134 kilometres (83 mi) east from Naga City, the heart of Bicol. The municipality is located at the tip of the Caramoan Peninsula, a rugged place of land extending into the waters of the Maqueda Channel on the north and east and Lagonoy Gulf on the south. It has been dubbed as the Emerging Paradise of the Pacific due to its white and pink sand beaches known internationally.

Economy

The economy of Caramoan is primarily based on agriculture and fishing, with principal products including rice, coconuts, abaca, and various marine resources from its coastal location. Tourism has emerged as a significant sector, boosting local income through visits to its islands and beaches. Tourism

Tourist destinations include the Caramoan National Park, the Centro, and Gota Beach, where activities include diving, swimming, snorkeling and spelunking.

Survivor series

Caramoan has been the site for several editions of the competitive reality television show Survivor. The area has hosted the U.S. version in two consecutive seasons (Survivor: Philippines and Survivor: Caramoan), the Serbian version for two consecutive seasons and the Israeli version for three consecutive seasons (Survivor: The Philippines, Survivor: Fans vs. Survivors, Survivor: Camarines), as well as two additional seasons in 2018 (Survivor: VIP, aired 2019) and 2019. Bulgarian Survivor also filmed its fourth season there in May to July 2009. Robinson 2010, the 12th season of the Swedish version of the franchise, was filmed in Caramoan from May to June 2010, followed by the 25th season in 2023 (aired 2024) and the 27th season in 2024 (aired 2025). Finnish versions were filmed there in 2018 with all-celebrity cast, and in 2019 half-famous and half-regular people cast. The 8th season of Koh-Lanta, the French edition of Survivor, also was shot there.
