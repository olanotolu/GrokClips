Boity Thulo

Boitumelo Thulo (born April 28, 1990), popularly known as Boity , is a South African television personality, rapper, actress, businesswoman and model. In 2019, Thulo was named as one of Forbes Africa's 30 Under 30 for her contribution in the music and entertainment sector.[2] She came to the spotlight after the release of her debut single "Wuz Dat?" in 2018 featuring Nasty C. In 2020, she signed a record deal with Def Jam Africa and released her EP 4436.[3] The EP was nominated for Best Hip Hop Album at the 27th South African Music Awards. In 2025, Thulo shifted focus towards interior design while hinting at new music releases and won the Best Fashion Influencer award at the South African Fashion Awards.[4][5]

Early life

Thulo was born in Potchefstroom, North West, where she was raised by her maternal grandparents.[6] She attended Potchefstroom Primary School and Potchefstroom Girls' High School. She is the only daughter of Modiehi Thulo, her mother.[7] Thulo studied psychology and criminology at Monash South Africa, but later dropped out because her mother was unable to pay for her varsity fees.[8] She then signed to a casting agency which resulted in her appearing on a Wimpy commercial around 2010.[9]

Career

Film and television

Thulo's television career began when she presented the YOTV educational show "Crib Notes" in 2011. She then was a co-host on the SABC 1 The Media Career Guide Show, which she hosted alongside Thomani Mahlaba.[10][11] She also served host duties in several television shows, including SkyRoom Live, Ridiculousness Africa, Club 808, Zoned, Change Down, and Big Brother Africa.[12] In 2012, Thulo starred on the drama series Rockville, which was her first starring role. She appeared for all 4 seasons playing "Mpho Bogatsu".[13] Thulo's film debut was in 2014 when she starred on a comedy short-film Dear Betty, where she played "Betty".[14] She also was featured in the film Mrs Right Guy (2016) where she played "Marie" which was not a huge role.[15] In 2023, Thulo competed as a contestant on season 1 of The Masked Singer South Africa.[16] In October 2021, Boity received a nomination for African Social Star at 47th People's Choice Awards.[17]

Music

Thulo made her rapping debut on the Migos Culture Tour in South Africa on 21 October 2017 at TicketPro Dome.[18] As Migos were delayed by a few hours, Thulo joined Nasty C as one of the opening acts until the trio arrived.[19] On 30 August 2018, Thulo released her debut single "Wuz Dat?", featuring Nasty C.[20] The song became the first ever single by a female rapper in South Africa to be certified platinum and this made it the best selling single of all time by a female rapper in that country.[21] It also won the category of "Best Collaboration" at the 2018 South African Hip Hop Awards, thus making her the first lead female rapper to achieve this.[22][23][24] On 8 February 2019, she released her second single titled "Bakae".[25] In October 2020, Boity headlined Rose Fest along with Ami Faku, Simmy and Busiswa.[26] On 4 December 2020, she released the EP 4436, centering mainly on Boity, and containing features from Riky Rick, and a variety of other artists.[27] The album was nominated at 27th South African Music Awards as Best Hip Hop album. In 2020, it was announced that Boity had closed a recording deal with Def Jam Africa. She was the first to mention it on Twitter.[28] On August 19, 2021, she teased her amapiano single "#The Yano Way" on Instagram Live, featuring Daliwonga and Felo Le Tee; the track has not been officially released as of 2025.[29] In November 2024, Thulo released the collaborative album The Extra Mile with rapper Roiii.[30]

Other ventures

In 2016, she collaborated with Sissy Boy for a clothing capsule collection aimed at "being sexy and celebrating curves".[31] Thulo continued her venture with Sissy Boy as they released a new collection in July 2018.[32] In 2018, she partnered with Impulse SA to create limited edition fragrances.[33] She also graced the cover of Cosmopolitan SA for the September issue.[34]

Business

In 2018, Thulo launched her own fragrance line, Pink Sapphire Eau de Parfum, in collaboration with Halo Heritage.[35] The product was discontinued in 2024 after sales declined.[36] In October 2020, she launched the Boity Haircare Collection with Halo Heritage, a range of products for women with kinky and curly hair.[37] The line includes shampoo, conditioners, scalp treatment, repair serum, and edge control gel. In July 2022, she introduced Be You by Boity, an expanded haircare brand focused on natural hair care.[38] In March 2021, Boity announced the launch of BT Signature, a range of alcoholic and non-alcoholic sparkling fruit beverages.[39]

Personal life

Relationships

Thulo previously dated rapper Cassper Nyovest, which was seen as an on-again, off-again relationship.[40] The pair got engaged but called off their engagement and ended their relationship in December 2015.[41] Nyovest married Pulane Mojaki in 2024.[42] Thulo dated actor Anton Jeftha from 2022 until early 2023.[43] In January 2025, she congratulated Jeftha on his engagement to Tegan Goldman.[44] In October 2021, Thulo was hospitalized after an alleged assault by Bujy Bikwa during a brawl at a hotel in Midrand.[45] She pressed assault charges against him, but dropped them in June 2023, after which the two reconciled.[46]

Honours

Forbes Africa 30 Under 30 creatives

In July 2019, Thulo was named by Forbes magazine amongst the 30 under 30 creatives category list for her contribution to the music and entertainment sector.[2] The list featured other young African creatives like Nigerian musician Burna Boy, South African actress, TV and radio personality Thando Thabethe, Ghanaian contemporary artist Joseph Awuah-Darko, also known as Okuntakinte and Kenyan film director, producer and screenwriter Kevin Njue.[2]
