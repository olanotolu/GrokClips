Robbie Chosen

Stats at Pro Football Reference Robbie Chosen (born Robby Anderson; May 9, 1993), previously known as Robbie Anderson and briefly Chosen Anderson, is an American professional football wide receiver for the Washington Commanders of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football for the Temple Owls and was signed by the New York Jets as an undrafted free agent in 2016. He has also played in the NFL for the Carolina Panthers, Arizona Cardinals, Miami Dolphins, and San Francisco 49ers.

Early life

Born in Fair Lawn, New Jersey, Anderson moved to South Florida as a child.[1] Anderson attended and played high school football at South Plantation High School in Plantation, Florida, where he also excelled in track and field.[1][2]

College career

Anderson committed to Temple University to play college football for the Temple Owls over offers from other schools including Illinois, Indiana, and Marshall.[3] After redshirting his first year in Philadelphia, Anderson saw time on special teams during his redshirt-freshman season and practiced primarily as a defensive back. Following Matt Rhule's hiring as Temple's head coach, Anderson switched to wide receiver in 2013 and caught 44 passes for 791 receiving yards and nine touchdowns.[4] In the 2013 season, Anderson set a Temple school-record for receiving yards in a game with 239 against SMU.[5][6] Anderson missed the 2014 season, however, due to academic eligibility issues.[7] Anderson spent 2014 taking classes at a community college in Florida. Anderson returned to Temple for his senior season in 2015 season and had 70 catches for 939 yards.[8] Anderson's reception and yardage totals were the second most ever by a Temple player in school history. Anderson was selected to the Eastern College Athletic Conference All-Conference team and Third Team All-Conference team by Phil Steele in recognition of his senior season. Following his senior season, Anderson was selected to play in the 2016 Eastâ€“West Shrine Game.[9]

New York Jets

2016 season

Despite running a 4.36-second 40-yard dash at Temple's pro day, Anderson went undrafted in the 2016 NFL draft. On May 1, 2016, the New York Jets signed Anderson to a three-year, $1.63 million contract that included a signing bonus of $10,000 as an undrafted free agent.[11] Anderson entered training camp competing against Jalin Marshall, Chandler Worthy, Jeremy Ross, and Charone Peake to be the Jets' fifth wide receiver.[12] He went on to lead all players in the 2016 pre-season in receiving yards with 264, and also had 13 catches and three touchdowns, which helped him earn a spot on the Jets' final 53-man roster and become their fifth wide receiver entering the regular season, behind Brandon Marshall, Eric Decker, Quincy Enunwa, and Jalin Marshall.[13] He made his professional regular season debut in the Jets' season-opening 23â€“22 loss to the Cincinnati Bengals.[14] On September 25, 2016, Anderson made his first career reception on a 26-yard pass from Ryan Fitzpatrick and finished the 24â€“3 loss to the Kansas City Chiefs with a total of two receptions for 34 receiving yards.[15] The next game, Anderson was named the Jets' starting slot receiver against the Seattle Seahawks and finished the 27â€“17 loss with two catches for 12 receiving yards.[16] Following Eric Decker's season-ending injury on October 12, 2016, Anderson became Jets' starting slot receiver.[17] On December 5, 2016, he made four receptions for 61 yards and caught his first career touchdown on a 40-yard pass from Bryce Petty. The New York Jets went on to lose to the Indianapolis Colts 41â€“10.[18] The following week, Anderson had a season-high six catches and 99 receiving yards in a 23â€“17 victory over the San Francisco 49ers.[19] In a Week 15 matchup against the Miami Dolphins, he led the Jets with 80 receiving yards, made four receptions, and caught a 40-yard touchdown pass from Petty as the Jets lost by a score of 34â€“13.[20] He finished his rookie season with 42 receptions, 587 receiving yards, and two touchdowns in 16 games and eight starts.[21]

Carolina Panthers

2020 season

On April 1, 2020, the Panthers signed Anderson to a two-year, $20 million contract that included $12 million guaranteed and a signing bonus of $8 million.[42] The signing reunited Anderson with former Temple head coach and recently hired Carolina Panthers' head coach Matt Rhule and former Temple teammates Keith Kirkwood, P. 2021 season

During Week 1, against the Jets, Anderson had a 57-yard touchdown on his only reception of the game as the Panthers won 19â€“14.[50] Anderson finished the 2021 season with 53 receptions for 519 receiving yards and five receiving touchdowns.[51]

2022 season

Anderson began the season with 102 yards and a touchdown in a Week 1 matchup against the Cleveland Browns[52] In Week 6 against the Los Angeles Rams, Anderson was kicked out of the game by interim head coach Steve Wilks after Anderson got into an argument with wide receiver coach Joe Dailey.[53]

Arizona Cardinals

On October 17, 2022, the day after the game against the Rams, Anderson was traded to the Arizona Cardinals for a 2024 sixth round draft pick and a 2025 seventh round draft pick.[54] While in Arizona, Anderson played in ten games and had seven receptions for 76 yards and no touchdowns.[2] On March 8, 2023, he was released by the Cardinals.[55]

Miami Dolphins (first stint)

On April 17, 2023, Anderson signed with the Miami Dolphins.[56] He was released on August 29, 2023 and re-signed to the practice squad.[57][58] On September 24, 2023, while the Dolphins led the Broncos 56â€“13, Chosen, who had been a practice squad activation that day, caught a 68-yard pass from Mike White that he ran in for a touchdown.[59] He was signed to the active roster on September 30.[60] He was released on November 16, and re-signed to the practice squad.[61] He was signed back to the active roster on November 21.[62] He finished the 2023 season with four receptions for 126 yards and a touchdown, which came in Week 3 against the Broncos in a 70â€“20 win.[63]

San Francisco 49ers (first stint)

On August 12, 2024, Chosen signed with the San Francisco 49ers.[64] On August 27, 2024, he was released by the San Francisco 49ers during the announcement of the 53-man roster for the 2024 season.[65]

Miami Dolphins (second stint)

On August 29, 2024, Chosen signed with the Miami Dolphins practice squad.[66] He was released by the Dolphins on September 17 after appearing in two regular season games,[67] finishing with one reception for five yards.[68]

San Francisco 49ers (second stint)

On August 3, 2025, Chosen signed with the San Francisco 49ers.[69] He was released on August 26, 2025, and did not appear in any regular season games.[70]

Washington Commanders

On October 1, 2025, Chosen signed with the practice squad of the Washington Commanders.[71] He was elevated to the active roster for Week 7 on October 18, 2025, and made his debut on October 20 in a 44â€“22 loss to the Dallas Cowboys, recording 4 receptions for 36 yards.[72][73] Chosen reverted to the practice squad on October 21, 2025.[74]
