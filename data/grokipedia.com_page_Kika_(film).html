Kika

Eligio "Kika" de la Garza II (September 22, 1927 – March 13, 2017) was an American lawyer, Korean War veteran, and Democratic politician who served 16 consecutive terms as the U.S. Representative for Texas's 15th congressional district from 1965 to 1997.[1][2] Born in Mercedes, Texas, to a family of Mexican descent, de la Garza graduated from the University of Texas at Austin with a law degree in 1952 and practiced law in Mission, Texas, before entering politics in the Texas House of Representatives from 1952 to 1964.[1][3] His congressional tenure focused on agriculture, border trade, and Hispanic community interests, culminating in his role as chairman of the House Committee on Agriculture from 1995 to 1997, where he advanced policies supporting rural economies and international farm agreements.[1] De la Garza's legacy endures through the E. "Kika" de la Garza Fellowship Program, administered by the U.S. Department of Agriculture to promote collaboration between Hispanic-serving institutions and federal agricultural initiatives, reflecting his commitment to education and minority advancement in policy spheres.[4][5]

Politics and Public Service

Eligio "Kika" de la Garza II (September 22, 1927 – March 13, 2017) served as a Democratic U.S. Representative for Texas's 15th congressional district from January 3, 1965, to January 3, 1997, securing 16 consecutive terms in elections where he consistently received over 60% of the vote in his border-region district.[1] Prior to his federal service, de la Garza represented Hidalgo County in the Texas House of Representatives starting in 1953, focusing on local issues in the Rio Grande Valley.[6] A Korean War veteran who enlisted in the U.S. Army in 1950 and served until 1952, de la Garza emphasized agricultural policy during his congressional tenure, authoring legislation that supported citrus growers and subtropical farming in South Texas amid economic pressures from imports and weather variability.[7] [8] De la Garza chaired the House Committee on Agriculture from 1991 to 1995, advancing bills such as the 1990 crop insurance reforms that expanded federal protections for producers facing drought and market fluctuations, which data from the U.S. Department of Agriculture showed reduced farm bankruptcies in affected regions by 15% in the following decade.[1] He also addressed border infrastructure and trade, co-sponsoring measures to fund port-of-entry expansions at facilities like the one later named the Kika de la Garza Processing and Administrative Building in 2000, reflecting his advocacy for efficient customs operations handling over 1.5 million vehicles annually by the late 1990s.[9] His bipartisan approach, evidenced by collaborations with Republican colleagues on farm bills, contributed to the passage of the 1996 Federal Agriculture Improvement and Reform Act, which phased out certain price supports while increasing conservation funding by $2.8 billion over seven years.[10] As a co-founder of the Congressional Hispanic Caucus in 1976, de la Garza prioritized representation for Hispanic communities, pushing for bilingual education funding and immigration reforms tied to agricultural labor needs, though his positions often balanced enforcement with economic pragmatism in a district where cross-border commerce exceeded $10 billion annually by 1996.[11] He retired in 1996 citing health concerns after accumulating over 18,000 legislative cosponsorships, leaving a record of committee leadership that included service on the influential Ways and Means Subcommittee on Trade.[1] No other prominent figures bearing the name Kika have held significant elected political offices or public service roles at the national or state level, based on congressional records and biographical directories.[7]

Entertainment and Performing Arts

Kika Edgar, born Sandra Ericka Edgar Garza on January 9, 1985, in Tampico, Tamaulipas, Mexico, is a Mexican actress and singer whose career originated in theater and musical productions.[12] She trained in acting, singing, and ballet at Televisa's Centro de Educación Artística, debuting in the musical Clap!... El lugar de tus sueños in 2003.[13] Edgar transitioned to telenovelas, earning recognition for roles in Amor real (2003), Contra viento y marea (2005), and La Tempestad (2013).[14] Her stage work includes leading roles in Mamma Mia! (with over 200 performances by 2023) and Cabaret, which she first performed 19 years prior to reprising in 2025.[15] [16] As a recording artist, she issued the album Broadway in 2011, covering musical theater standards.[17] Kika Markham, born Erika S. L. Markham in 1940 in Macclesfield, Cheshire, England, is an English actress with a career spanning film, television, and stage.[18] She debuted in film as a nurse in Bunny Lake Is Missing (1965) and later appeared in Outland (1981) alongside Sean Connery as his wife.[19] [20] Additional credits include Killing Me Softly (2002) and Franklyn (2008).[21] Markham's television roles feature guest appearances in Agatha Christie's Poirot (1989–2013 series), Minder (1979–1994), Cracker (1993–1996), and multiple episodes of Doctors (2000–present).[20] She is the daughter of actor David Markham and was married to actor Corin Redgrave until his death in 2010, with whom she had two children.[19]

Sports

Francisca Nazareth, commonly known as Kika Nazareth, born November 17, 2002, is a Portuguese professional footballer who plays as a forward for FC Barcelona Femení in Spain's Liga F and represents the Portugal women's national team.[22][23] She transferred to FC Barcelona on July 4, 2024, from Benfica for a record €500,000 fee, marking the highest transfer in Portuguese women's football history.[24] Nazareth began her professional career with futsal before transitioning to football, accumulating 128 official matches and 82 goals across clubs prior to Barcelona, contributing to 12 titles including four league championships.[25] In her debut 2024–25 season with Barcelona, she recorded 6 goals in 29 appearances across all competitions before an injury interruption.[26] In the UEFA Women's Champions League, she has scored 1 goal in 2 matches as of October 2025, including key contributions in group stage fixtures.[27] Internationally, Nazareth has been a regular for Portugal since her senior debut, aiding qualification efforts for major tournaments with her pace and finishing; she earned the Campeonato Nacional Feminino Player of the Year and Golden Boot in her standout pre-Barcelona season.[24][28] Her statistical profile highlights efficiency, with career totals exceeding 80 senior goals.[28]

Visual Arts and Influencers

Kika Karadi (born 1975) is a Hungarian-American visual artist based in New York, recognized for her large-scale abstract paintings that respond to modernist aesthetics through monochromatic compositions and layered applications of ink and paint.[29] Her works often employ stencils, masking tape, and oil-based black ink on glass or canvas, creating time-based, geometric abstractions exhibited at institutions such as the Chinati Foundation in Marfa, Texas.[30] Karadi's paintings have been shown at galleries including Bortolami in New York, where her pieces feature in solo and group exhibitions emphasizing abstract formalism.[31] Kika Kim, whose real name is Kristina Kim, is a content creator and vlogger of Korean-Kazakh descent based in Los Angeles, producing lifestyle and travel videos on YouTube.[32] Her channel documents personal experiences such as her relocation to Los Angeles in early 2024, including apartment tours and daily routines in the city, alongside trips to South Korea featuring cultural events like New Year's celebrations in Seoul.[33] Kim's content emphasizes cozy, aspirational living, with videos on topics like first-class flights from Seoul to Los Angeles and family vacations in Busan, amassing views through relatable travel narratives.[34] Francisca Cerqueira Gomes, professionally known as Kika Gomes or @kikagomes, is a Portuguese model and social media influencer with over 1 million Instagram followers, focusing on fashion and personal branding.[35] She has participated in high-profile events such as L'Oréal Paris' Le Défilé during Paris Fashion Week in September 2025, marking her as the first Portuguese model to walk the runway for the brand, which promotes themes of women's empowerment and beauty diversity.[36] Gomes' Instagram posts highlight styled outfits, collaborations with fashion entities, and lifestyle content, including features in Vogue Portugal's "What's In My Bag" series in September 2024.[37]

Arts and Media

Films

Kika (1993) is a Spanish black comedy film written and directed by Pedro Almodóvar.[38] Released on October 29, 1993, it stars Verónica Forqué in the lead role as a cosmetologist, alongside Peter Coyote as an American novelist, Victoria Abril as a television host, Alex Casanovas, and Rossy de Palma.[38] The narrative critiques media sensationalism, voyeurism, and the commodification of violence and sex in popular culture.[39][40] Forqué received the Goya Award for Best Actress for her performance, marking a key recognition in Almodóvar's ensemble-driven style.[41] The film holds a 59% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes based on 17 reviews, with critics noting its chaotic blend of farce and social commentary.[42] Kika (2025) is a French-Belgian drama marking the narrative feature debut of director Alexe Poukine, who previously earned awards for her short film Palma (2020).[43][44] Running 110 minutes and primarily in French, the film world premiered in competition at the Cannes Film Festival's Critics' Week on May 16, 2025, and screened subsequently at festivals including the Tokyo International Film Festival.[43][45][44] It centers on a social worker confronting sudden bereavement and economic precarity while pregnant, emphasizing themes of emotional endurance amid adversity.[46] Early reception highlights its tragicomic tone and empathetic portrayal of grief, with audiences and reviewers praising Poukine's raw depiction of resilience without sentimentality.[43][47] The film achieved a 3.5 average rating on Letterboxd from over 850 user logs as of late 2025.[48]

Television and Broadcasting

KiKA, a German free-to-air public-service television channel dedicated to children's programming, launched on January 1, 1997, initially under the name Der Kinderkanal.[49] It operates as a joint venture between the ARD broadcasting consortium and ZDF, both public entities funded primarily through household fees rather than advertising, with headquarters in Erfurt.[50] The channel airs content daily from approximately 5:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m., providing a continuous schedule of ad-free broadcasts to ensure accessibility for families without commercial interruptions.[50] KiKA's content strategy prioritizes educational value intertwined with entertainment, featuring a mix of animated series, live-action adventures, and factual documentaries designed to foster cognitive and social development.[51] The channel targets children aged 3 to 13, encompassing preschoolers (with simpler, visually engaging formats), primary school-aged viewers (incorporating basic literacy and numeracy themes), and pre-teens (offering more complex narratives and real-world explorations).[52] This demographic focus reflects a deliberate curation to align with varying developmental stages, drawing from in-house productions and licensed international content adapted for German audiences. Among its flagship programs, Die Sendung mit der Maus stands out as a cornerstone of KiKA's educational lineup, originating in 1971 but prominently featured on the channel since its inception; it delivers episodic "Lach- und Sachgeschichten" (fun and factual stories) explaining phenomena like manufacturing processes or natural events through animations starring the Mouse, Duck, and Elephant characters.[53] Other aggregated series unique to KiKA's branding include Sandmännchen (a bedtime storytelling format with dream sequences) and Tigerenten Club (interactive variety show encouraging viewer participation), which consolidate popular German children's content previously scattered across ARD and ZDF affiliates into a dedicated stream.[54] These selections underscore KiKA's role in aggregating and preserving culturally significant programming while introducing original segments tailored to its broadcast window.

Digital Media and Applications

The Kika Keyboard is a free Android application developed by Kika Tech, a company specializing in AI-enhanced input methods, launched to provide users with customizable typing experiences through emojis, themes, GIFs, and stickers.[55][56] The app integrates features such as swipe input, voice typing introduced in 2018, cloud-based dictionaries, and adjustable keyboard sizes to facilitate faster and more expressive communication across messaging platforms.[57] Kika Tech's development emphasizes artificial intelligence for predictive text and personalization, enabling users to apply fonts, widgets, and dynamic backgrounds while supporting integration with apps like WhatsApp and social media.[58][59] The keyboard has been recognized by Google as Top Developer in 2015 and Best of 2016, reflecting its early adoption in enhancing mobile interactions.[58][60] In terms of user metrics, Kika Keyboard has reached over 500 million devices worldwide, with approximately 280 million total users and 80 million monthly active users as of recent reports.[58][60] It holds the position of the top keyboard app in the United States on the Google Play Store, prioritizing functionality for emoji-rich and themed inputs without requiring subscriptions.[55]

Organizations and Brands

Retail and Commerce

Kika operated as a chain of furniture and home goods retailers, primarily headquartered in Austria, with a business model centered on large-format stores offering a wide assortment of affordable, ready-to-assemble and assembled furniture, appliances, and decor items targeting middle-market consumers. The retailer emphasized value pricing through economies of scale, in-house brands, and supplier partnerships, stocking over 30,000 products across categories like living room sets, bedrooms, kitchens, and textiles to facilitate one-stop shopping for home furnishing needs.[61][62] Established in 1973 by Austrian entrepreneur Herbert Koch as a furniture production and sales entity, Kika expanded rapidly in the late 20th century by opening hypermarket-style outlets that combined showroom displays with warehouse storage, mirroring strategies of competitors like IKEA but with a focus on European-sourced designs. By the early 2000s, following mergers such as with the older Leiner chain (founded 1910), the Kika/Leiner group had grown to approximately 70 stores across Austria, Hungary, Romania, the Czech Republic, and Slovakia, serving millions of annual customers through physical locations and emerging e-commerce channels.[62][63][64] The company's operational scope extended to Central and Eastern Europe, where it adapted to local markets by offering region-specific product lines while maintaining a core emphasis on affordability amid rising competition from discounters and online platforms. However, persistent financial pressures, including ownership changes under groups like Steinhoff and subsequent insolvencies, led to contractions starting in 2019, with asset sales to rivals such as XXXLutz and eventual full closure of remaining outlets by January 2025, marking the end of over a century of combined Kika-Leiner operations and resulting in 1,350 job losses in Austria alone.[65][66][67]

Non-Profit Foundations

Stichting Kinderen Kankervrij (KiKa), meaning Children Cancer Free Foundation, is a Dutch non-profit organization dedicated exclusively to funding research on all forms of childhood cancer.[68] Founded in 2002, KiKa channels public donations into innovative treatments aimed at reducing treatment-related pain, enhancing cure rates, and improving quality of life for affected children.[69] As the sole Dutch charity supporting comprehensive research across childhood cancer types, it prioritizes grants for projects that address unmet needs in diagnostics, therapies, and supportive care.[70] KiKa's mission centers on achieving a 100% cure rate for childhood cancer, with approximately 600 new cases diagnosed annually in the Netherlands.[68] The foundation serves as the primary financial backer of the Princess Máxima Center, Europe's largest specialized facility for pediatric oncology research and treatment.[68] It avoids general health advocacy, focusing instead on targeted investments in scientific projects, such as those exploring chemotherapy modifications for bone cancer or novel therapies for brain stem tumors.[71][72] Since inception, KiKa has disbursed over €258 million in grants, supporting 235 active research initiatives, each typically lasting four years and costing between €400,000 and €800,000.[73] These efforts have contributed to raising the five-year survival rate for Dutch children with cancer from 72% to 81%, through advancements in less invasive treatments and better post-diagnosis outcomes.[70] In 2023 alone, KiKa allocated nearly €3 million to five new projects targeting germ cell tumors, liver cancer, and brain stem malignancies.[72] The foundation's model emphasizes measurable progress, with funded studies yielding data on reduced side effects and higher remission rates in specific subtypes.[74]

Technology Products

Kika Tech's primary technology product is the Kika Keyboard, a mobile software application incorporating artificial intelligence for enhanced text input and communication. Launched in the mid-2010s, the app utilizes AI-driven features such as predictive text generation, intelligent autocorrect, and contextual suggestions for emojis, GIFs, and stickers to improve typing efficiency and expressiveness across messaging platforms.[58][75] The software supports customizable layouts, including one-handed modes and split-screen adaptations, with over 3,000 emojis and stickers integrated for user personalization.[58] In addition to consumer-facing apps, Kika Tech provides software development kits (SDKs) designed for embedding secure, personalized virtual keyboards into third-party digital applications and services. These SDKs facilitate AI-powered communication enhancements, focusing on natural language processing and user-specific adaptations without compromising data security.[75] The company's AI engine extends to voice input technology and predictive modeling, enabling seamless integration into smart devices for broader self-expression tools.[76] Kika Tech's ecosystem, powered by these technologies, has achieved over 400 million installations globally as of 2019, emphasizing mobile-first innovations in input methods rather than hardware development.[77] No hardware products, such as dedicated devices or peripherals, have been launched under the Kika brand; efforts remain centered on software optimizations for existing mobile ecosystems.[78]
