Max Crocombe

Maxime Teremoana Crocombe (born 12 August 1993) is a New Zealand professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for EFL Championship club Millwall. He also represents the New Zealand national team.[1] Crocombe moved to England at an early age and began his professional career with Oxford United who had signed him from non-League side Buckingham Town. He went on to play on loan with Banbury United, Nuneaton Town, Barnet and Southport before signing permanently with Carlisle United. In 2017 he moved to Salford City where he was part of the City side that won back to back promotions to the Football League. In 2019, he moved to play in the Australian A-League with spells at Brisbane Roar and Melbourne Victory. Returning to England in 2021, he played for Grimsby Town until 2023, Burton Albion until 2025, before joining Millwall.

Early life

Crocombe was born in Auckland, New Zealand, before moving to England at a young age. He had spells with Milton Keynes Dons and Luton Town[1] before joining Buckingham Town.[3] Crocombe attended Oakgrove School in [Milton Keynes](Milton Keynes "Milton Keynes") along with fellow footballer and former Ghanaian international Jeffrey Schlupp.[4]

Club career

Buckingham Town

In the summer of 2009, Crocombe signed for Buckingham Town at the age of 16. He made 33 senior first team appearances before being spotted by scouts of Oxford United.[3] Despite being a goalkeeper he scored one goal in his time at Buckingham Town after coming on as an outfield substitute in a 3â€“1 league defeat.[5]

Oxford United

Crocombe joined Oxford United as a scholar under youth-team coach Chris Allen and signed his first professional contract in April 2012.[6] He almost made his debut shortly afterwards after injuries to Ryan Clarke and Wayne Brown, and the termination of Connor Ripley's loan,[7] however Oxford eventually signed Arsenal's Emiliano MartÃ­nez on loan[8] and Crocombe had to wait for his debut. Whilst at the club, he has been coached by Alan Hodgkinson and Wayne Brown.[9] Crocombe's Football League debut came in a 1â€“1 draw with Burton Albion on 29 January 2013.[10] He kept his first clean sheet for the club in his second league appearance, a 1â€“0 away win over Dagenham & Redbridge in April 2013, which ended a five-match winless run.[11] He remained in goal for the rest of the league season, keeping three consecutive clean sheets: the 1â€“0 win over Dagenham & Redbridge, followed by 3â€“0 wins over Accrington Stanley and Rochdale.[12] By the 2013â€“14 season he had established himself as deputy to Oxford's long-standing number 1, Ryan Clarke. Crocombe appeared twice in the 2014â€“15 season, both times in the Football League Cup. In his second appearance he held Premier League side West Bromwich Albion to one goal in 120 minutes and saved their first two spot kicks in the penalty shoot-out.[13] At the end of the season, which included a productive loan spell at Nuneaton Town, the club triggered a 2-year option in his contract to extend his stay until the end of 2017.[14]

Loan to Nuneaton Town

In February 2015, Crocombe signed on loan at Conference Premier side Nuneaton Town until the end of the season.[15] After impressing with a clean sheet on his debut, he was named in the Non-League Paper 's Team of the Day.[17] He did not concede a goal in any of his first 6 games and was named man of the match twice in his short spell for the club.

Loan to Barnet

Crocombe was loaned to Barnet on 18 September 2015, with the Bees without a senior keeper due to an injury and a suspension.[18]

Loan to Southport

In October 2015 Crocombe was recalled from Barnet and sent on a three-month loan to Southport of the National League.[19] After a series of impressive performances in his first 3 months for the club he was named Southport's Player of the Month for both November 2015 and January 2016.

Carlisle United

Crocombe left Oxford by mutual consent and signed a one-year deal with Carlisle United on 4 August 2016.[20]

Salford City

In May 2017 he joined Salford City.[21] In October 2017 Crocombe was sent off during a match against Bradford Park Avenue for urinating at the side of a stand[22] A spectator made a formal complaint and the incident was later reported to the police. Crocombe subsequently posted an apology on Twitter and explained "I was in a very uncomfortable position and made an error of judgement that spoiled a great win".[23]

Brisbane Roar

In July 2019, Crocombe joined Australian A-League side Brisbane Roar to challenge Jamie Young for the starting goalkeeper position.[24] In October 2020, Crocombe left Brisbane Roar.[25]

Melbourne Victory

After leaving Brisbane Roar, Crocombe joined Melbourne Victory in October 2020.[26] Crocombe began the season as the Victory's starting goalkeeper, playing in the first 10 matches of the season, before being replaced as the starting goalkeeper by Matt Acton. He was released on 11 June 2021.[27]

Grimsby Town

On 30 July 2021, Crocombe returned to England, penning a one-year deal with Grimsby Town.[28] Initially being signed as a back-up keeper, Crocombe became Grimsby's first choice keeper in December at the expense of club veteran James McKeown.[29] On 23 May 2022, with Grimsby trailing 1â€“0 to Notts County in the National League play-off eliminator, in the final minute of added on time Crocombe came up for a free kick and with the ball falling to him it bounced off his knees as he prepared to shoot before dropping to Gavan Holohan who scored the equaliser, with Crocombe claiming the assist. Grimsby went on to win the game 2â€“1 in extra time.[30] Crocombe played in the 2022 National League play-off final as Grimsby beat Solihull Moors 2â€“1 at the London Stadium to return to the Football League.[31] Crocombe was ever-present in goal for Grimsby's 2022â€“23 League Two season and cup campaigns, playing a total of 5,340 minutes, the fourth highest in world football that season.[32] He was part of the Grimsby team that reached the quarter-finals of the 2022â€“23 FA Cup by beating League One clubs Plymouth Argyle, Cambridge United and Burton Albion in rounds one to three, Championship club Luton Town in a fourth round replay, and Premier League side Southampton in the fifth round.[33] In doing so they became the first team to beat five teams from higher divisions in the history of the FA Cup.[34] At the end of the 2022â€“23 season, Crocombe turned down a new contract and confirmed he would be leaving Grimsby.[35]

Burton Albion

On 30 June 2023, Crocombe signed for League One club Burton Albion on a two-year deal.[36] Following the conclusion of the 2023â€“24 season, he was named Men's Player of the Season.[37] On 13 May 2025, the club said it had offered the player a new contract.[38] However, Crocombe opted to depart the club at the end of his contract.[39] He made 92 appearances during his two year spell with Burton.[39]

Millwall

On 24 June 2025, Crocombe signed for Championship club Millwall on a free transfer.[39] As of October 2025, he has been named in the matchday squad twice but is yet to make a competitive appearance.[40]

International career

Youth

On 22 January 2013, Crocombe was called up to the provisional New Zealand under-20s squad for the 2013 OFC U-20 Championship in Fiji, the regional qualifying tournament for the 2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup.[41] He was confirmed in the final squad of 20 on 20 February 2013 and made his debut in the 1â€“0 win over Vanuatu.[42][43] Crocombe kept clean sheets in both his appearances and was voted the best goalkeeper of the tournament, earning him the Golden Glove Award.[44] He was confirmed in the New Zealand under-20 squad for the 2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup in Turkey[45] and was described as a key player for the team.[46] Crocombe played in two of New Zealand's games as the team exited the competition at the group stage. In June 2015, Crocombe was included in the New Zealand under-23s squad for the Pacific Games, which was used as a qualifying tournament for the 2016 Summer Olympics.[47] Crocombe kept 3 clean sheets in New Zealand's 3 group stage games before the team was disqualified from the tournament for fielding an ineligible player.[48]

Senior

In March 2015, Crocombe was called up to the full New Zealand national team squad for a friendly against South Korea.[49] A year later, he was included in the senior squad for the 2016 OFC Nations Cup.[50] He made his full international debut for the All Whites in a friendly against Canada in March 2018.[51] In 2019 Max was targeted as a top prospect for the Cook Islands, after returning to Oceania with Brisbane Roar.[52] On 6 June 2023, after a spell away from the team, Crocombe was called up to the New Zealand squad for friendlies against Sweden and Qatar.[53] Crocombe started the game against Qatar which was abandoned at half time when the New Zealand players refused to play the second half after Michael Boxall was alleged to have been racially abused by one of the Qatari players, New Zealand had been leading 1â€“0.[54] Crocombe won the Golden Glove award at the 2024 OFC Men's Nations Cup having conceding no goals in the tournament.[55] He started in goal for the majority of New Zealand's successful qualification campaign for the 2026 FIFA World Cup.[56] Following qualification for the 2026 FIFA World Cup, Crocombe started in New Zealand's 1â€“3 friendly defeat to Australia on 9 September 2025.[57] He was included in the squad for October friendlies against Poland (0â€“1 loss on 9 October) and Norway (1â€“1 draw on 14 October) but remained an unused substitute in both matches, with Alex Paulsen starting in goal.[58][59][60][61]
