Osborne Clarke

Osborne Clarke is an international legal practice headquartered in London, England, UK with offices in the United Kingdom, Germany, Italy, Belgium, Spain, Sweden, France, the Netherlands, China (via association), India (via BTG Legal), Singapore, the United States (including New York, San Francisco, and Miami), and Poland. As of 2025, the firm has over 340 partners and approximately 2,500 employees spread across its 26 offices around the world.[4] The firm was involved in the development of the railways in Southern England through its historic relationship with Isambard Kingdom Brunel and the Great Western Railway.[5]

History

Osborne Clarke was founded in Bristol, England, in 1748 by Jeremiah Osborne. In 1987 Osborne Clarke opened its first London office. In the following years, the firm opened offices in Reading (1998), Silicon Valley (2000), Cologne (2001), and Munich (2005). The law firm played a significant part in the development of the railways in the UK when it was appointed to represent the Great Western Railway Company, working closely with Isambard Kingdom Brunel on the establishment of the Great Western Main Line route from London Paddington to Bristol and the West Country.[6] The nature of the relationship between Osborne Clarke and Brunel is best highlighted by one notable occasion when Jeremiah Osborne the Third (grandson to the founder) is said to have personally rowed Brunel down the River Avon to survey the bank for the route of the Great Western Railway.[7] The partnership's recent history has been characterised by two strands of development. Firstly, they have undertaken significant international expansion, particularly into the United States and Germany.[8] On 9 July 2012 the firm announced that two European firms, SLA Studio Legale Associato in Italy and Spanish firm Osborne Clarke Spain, had become part of Osborne Clarke.[9] Secondly the firm has seen considerable expansion in the media and technology sectors, in which they are considered a tier 1 firm.[10] Notable clients in this sector include Yahoo!, Facebook,[11] Dell, PwC,[12] Google,[13] PokÃ©mon Company[14] and Tripadvisor.[15] In 2015 Osborne Clarke was named The Lawyer's firm of the year.[16] In 2019 the firm was awarded the Best Corporate Culture Award at the Managing Partners Forum (MAP) Awards.[17]

Structure

After expanding significantly through the dot-com bubble of the late 1990s and early 2000s, the firm underwent a comprehensive restructure. Osborne Clarke operates as a Swiss Verein, a decentralized association of independent legal practices that collaborate internationally.[3] It retains a comprehensive range of departments across sectors such as technology, media, and intellectual property. As of the Legal 500 2026 guide, the firm holds Tier 1 rankings in 29 UK practice areas, including media and entertainment in London, and Tier 2 in intellectual property: trade marks, copyright, and design (encompassing brand management).[35]
