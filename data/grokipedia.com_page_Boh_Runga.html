Boh Runga

Boh Runga is a New Zealand singer-songwriter, guitarist, recording artist, and jewellery designer. She rose to prominence as the lead vocalist, guitarist, and primary songwriter for the pop-rock band Stellar*, which achieved multi-platinum sales in New Zealand and garnered seven Tui New Zealand Music Awards, including Best Female Vocalist, Best Songwriting, Single of the Year, and Best Album.[1] Runga, the older sister of singer Bic Runga, co-founded Stellar* in the late 1990s and contributed to its blend of punchy pop-rock and electronica elements that dominated local charts with top-10 singles.[2] Beyond music, she launched Boh Runga Jewellery in 2007 with the Birdland collection, developing a brand focused on sterling silver and gold pieces infused with personal narratives, cultural motifs, and New Zealand-inspired symbolism, now available worldwide through boutiques and online sales.[3] In addition to solo recordings and collaborations with artists such as Brooke Fraser and Che Fu, Runga owns and operates Old North Studios in Auckland, where she produces and engineers recordings drawing on over three decades of industry experience.[1]

Musical career

Early involvement and band formation

Boh Runga began her musical involvement in Christchurch during her teenage years, performing in local bands from the age of 14. She secured a position in a resident covers band at the Papanui Tavern, where she met drummer Andrew Maclaren in the mid-1980s.[14] Maclaren initially recruited Runga as a potential vocalist for his band Amez Amez, but after recognizing her songwriting talent, they collaborated on original material despite early reservations about her abilities.[18] Facing constrained opportunities in Christchurch's music scene, Runga and Maclaren relocated to Auckland in 1992 to advance their career. There, they expanded the lineup by recruiting bassist Kurt Shanks and guitarist Chris van de Geer, solidifying the core quartet that would become Stellar* around 1994â€“1995.[18] [4] Runga served as lead vocalist and guitarist, with Maclaren on drums, Shanks providing bass and backing vocals, and van de Geer on guitar and vocals.[18] The nascent band focused on independent releases, including early singles that preceded their major-label breakthrough. In 1996, Stellar* issued their debut single "Happy Gun" via Papa Pacific Records, marking an initial foray into recording and distribution that built momentum ahead of signing with Sony Music.[18] This period established the group's pop-rock sound, influenced by Runga's Christchurch roots and the collaborative dynamics formed with her new Auckland bandmates.[18]

Stellar* era

Stellar* formed in Auckland in 1994 when Boh Runga and drummer Andrew Maclaren, who had relocated from Christchurch two years earlier, recruited guitarist Chris van de Geer and bassist Kurt Shanks to complete the lineup.[19] Runga served as lead vocalist and guitarist, contributing songwriting that blended pop melodies with rock energy.[20] The band initially released independent singles, including "Ride" recorded with temporary guitarist Joel Haines, before securing a deal with Sony Music.[18] Their debut album, Mix, arrived on 2 August 1999 and propelled Stellar* to prominence in New Zealand, dominating radio airplay with tracks like "All Comes Down" and "Let's Groove".[21] The record earned multiple Tui Awards, including for Highest Selling NZ Album and Best Female Vocalist, reflecting its commercial dominance with sales exceeding platinum certification.[22] Mix showcased Runga's charismatic stage presence and the band's tight instrumentation, establishing them as a key act in the late-1990s Kiwi pop-rock scene.[20] The follow-up, Magic Line, released in 2001, continued their momentum with hits such as "Concrete Jungle" and secured additional Tui Awards, including for Best Group and Best Video.[22] Despite lineup stability and touring success, internal creative tensions emerged by the mid-2000s, leading to a hiatus.[23] Stellar* effectively disbanded around 2010 after sporadic activity, with Runga shifting focus to solo endeavors while crediting the era for honing her performance skills.[24] The band's output remains a benchmark for accessible, radio-friendly New Zealand rock, with Mix retrospectively praised for its unpretentious hooks amid a landscape of emerging indie alternatives.[18]

Solo recordings and performances

Boh Runga's debut solo album, Right Here, was released on May 4, 2009, marking her transition from band frontwoman to independent artist.[25] The record features pop rock and singer-songwriter elements, with production handled by Marshall Altman in his Hollywood studio over a six-month period.[26] Tracks such as "Starfish Sleeping," released as a lead single in December 2008, and "Evelyn," her second single with an accompanying music video, highlighted her songwriting focused on personal introspection and relationships.[27] [28] No subsequent solo albums have been released, though a special bonus edition of Right Here appeared in 2010 with additional tracks.[29] Live performances supporting Right Here included promotional appearances and acoustic sets, aligning with Runga's established stage presence from over 30 years in music.[1] She has expressed a continued affinity for solo gigs, describing them as a preferred outlet amid band reunions.[15] Occasional solo outings, such as covers like "Would Ya Give Your Heart" shared online in 2010, underscore her versatility in intimate venues.[30] These efforts, while not yielding extensive touring data, complemented her pivot to jewelry design without fully supplanting Stellar*-era collaborations.[21]

Recent activities and reunions

In 2023, Boh Runga joined the lineup for ATOMIC!, an all-female concert series honoring pioneering women in rock, performing alongside artists such as Julia Deans of Fur Patrol and Dianne Swann of When the Cats Away.[31][32] This event marked her contribution to a collaborative showcase emphasizing female-led rock performances in New Zealand.[32] Stellar* reunited for a 25th anniversary celebration of their debut album Mix with a sold-out concert at Q Theatre in Auckland on March 28, 2025, featuring original members Boh Runga, Andrew MacLaren, Kurt Shanks, and Chris van de Geer.[33] The performance highlighted the band's early 2000s hits and their seven TÅ«Ä« Award wins from the album's release era.[33][34] The band continued reunion activities with a performance at Spark Arena in Auckland on August 2, 2025, as part of The Biggest Pub Gig In The World event.[35] In October 2025, Stellar* announced a summer tour alongside Th' Dudes and The Mockers, featuring Runga on lead vocals and select original lineup members, scheduled for New Zealand venues in early 2026.[36] Runga also participated in Atomic 2.0, an extension of the ATOMIC series, with performances listed through 2025-2026, reinforcing her involvement in female-centric rock ensembles.[37] These activities reflect a pattern of periodic Stellar* reunions focused on anniversary milestones and festival appearances since the band's 2010 hiatus.[33][36]

Business and creative ventures

Jewellery design origins

Boh Runga exhibited an early interest in jewellery design, creating pieces by hand during her childhood, a practice later recalled by her mother as foundational to her later professional pursuits.[38] Her formal entry into jewellery design occurred in 2007, following an introduction to the New Zealand Mint that prompted a commission for her inaugural collection.[38][3] This marked a transition from her established music career with the band Stellar* in the 1990s, leveraging her creative skills in sketching and conceptualizing wearable art.[38] The debut collection, titled Birdland and released in 2007, drew inspiration from New Zealand's native birds, emphasizing motifs of feathers, wings, and flight to evoke themes of freedom and connection to the natural environment.[38][3] Runga personally hand-drew the initial concepts, which were produced in sterling silver and gold, partnering with artisan Toyah Attwell for manufacturing and branding to ensure quality and narrative-driven presentation, including signature story cards accompanying each piece.[3] This origin emphasized jewellery as meaningful, gift-oriented items fostering a sense of belonging, rooted in Runga's observations of New Zealand's flora, fauna, and cultural myths.[3]

Boh Runga Jewellery development and impact

Boh Runga Jewellery originated in 2007 when Boh Runga, transitioning from her music career, received an opportunity to design a collection for The New Zealand Mint, a bullion company.[3] This initial project, named Birdland, marked the debut of her jewellery line, focusing on sterling silver and gold pieces inspired by New Zealand motifs such as native birds and feathers.[39] Runga's design process emphasized thematic storytelling, beginning with collection names and narratives to ensure pieces conveyed personal meaning and longevity, rather than fleeting trends.[40] The brand evolved into Boh Runga Design Ltd., with Runga serving as creative director, expanding production to include 9ct yellow and rose gold alongside sterling silver.[41] Collections like Feather Kisses emerged as top sellers, featuring delicate, nature-inspired elements that appealed to gift buyers seeking sentimental value.[42] By the 2010s, the line was distributed through New Zealand retailers such as Walker & Hall and Silvermoon, alongside an online presence that facilitated direct sales and limited-edition releases.[43] Recent developments include a website relaunch in late 2024 to enhance e-commerce and digital marketing, supported by agency partnerships for growth.[44] Commercially, the business has sustained Runga's post-Stellar* career, reportedly generating around $3 million in annual revenue as of 2025, reflecting steady demand for her accessible luxury pieces priced from $150 to $600.[45] Its impact lies in positioning Runga as a multifaceted creative, bridging music and artisanal design, with products marketed explicitly for giftingâ€”such as lockets and pendants symbolizing connectionâ€”which has cultivated a loyal domestic market in New Zealand.[39] While not disrupting global jewellery trends, the brand's emphasis on ethical, locally evoked designs has contributed to niche recognition in Pacific fashion circles, evidenced by in-store appearances and seasonal promotions.[46] No major international expansion data is available, underscoring its regional footprint.[47]

Discography and achievements

Stellar* contributions

Boh Runga fronted Stellar* as lead vocalist and guitarist, delivering the band's energetic pop-rock sound characterized by her distinctive, flame-haired stage presence and versatile guitar riffs that complemented the group's blend of guitars and electronic elements.[48] Her vocal performances anchored key singles like "Violent," which topped charts and secured the 2000 New Zealand Music Award for Single of the Year, and "Undone," contributing to the band's rapid rise with five radio hits within 18 months of their 1999 debut.[49] [48] As a primary songwriter, Runga co-authored multiple tracks across Stellar*'s discography, including "Every Girl" and other cuts on the platinum-certified debut album Mix (1999), which sold over 75,000 copies and earned the band their first Best Album award.[48] [50] On the follow-up Magic Line (2001), she co-wrote songs such as "Taken" and "All It Takes," helping the album achieve commercial success and further NZ Music Awards recognition.[48] For the third album, Something Like Strangers (2006), Runga contributed to compositions like "For a While," marking the band's evolution toward more introspective material before their initial disbandment.[48] Runga's individual accolades underscored her pivotal role, including the 1999 New Zealand Music Award for Most Promising Female Vocalist and the 2000 Best Female Vocalist award, amid Stellar*'s tally of eight total wins that cemented their status as Sony Music New Zealand's highest-selling local act.[48] [49] Her leadership drove the band's national breakthrough, with Mix alone propelling Stellar* to mainstream prominence through high-energy live shows and radio dominance.[48]

Solo works

Boh Runga's debut solo album, Right Here, was released on May 4, 2009, by CBS Records.[25] The album was produced by Marshall Altman and recorded in his Hollywood studio, featuring 13 tracks blending pop rock and singer-songwriter styles.[51] Prior to the full release, the lead single "Starfish Sleeping" was issued in December 2008.[52] A second single, "Evelyn", followed in 2009.[28] In 2022, Runga released the single "Rock Steady", a cover of Aretha Franklin's 1971 song co-produced with P-Money.[53] The track, released on November 15, 2022, marked her return to solo music after over a decade, emphasizing her vocal delivery and appreciation for the original's groove.[16] No further solo albums have been released as of 2025.[2]

Awards and commercial success

Stellar*, with Boh Runga as lead vocalist and guitarist, achieved significant commercial success in New Zealand, becoming Sony Music NZ's highest-selling Kiwi band of their era. Their 1999 debut album Mix reached number one on the NZ album chart, sold 75,000 copies, and earned 5Ã— platinum certification.[54][55] All three Stellar* albums charted in the top 10 of the NZ album charts, with Mix and its 2001 follow-up Magic Line combining for 53 weeks in the top 40.[49] The band secured eight Tui Awards (New Zealand Music Awards) across Mix and Magic Line. For Mix alone, Stellar* won seven TuIs, including Album of the Year, Single of the Year for "Every Little Thing", Best Group, Best Songwriting, and Best Female Vocalist, the latter awarded to Runga.[34][56] At the 2000 New Zealand Music Awards, Runga personally received the Best Female Vocalist Tui, presented alongside her sister Bic Runga's International Achievement Award.[57] Runga's solo music career has yielded limited documented awards or sales figures comparable to Stellar*, with focus shifting post-2010 to performances and reunions rather than chart dominance. No major commercial certifications or Tui wins are recorded for her independent releases, though she has maintained visibility through live appearances and collaborations.[49]
