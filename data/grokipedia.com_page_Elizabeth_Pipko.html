Elizabeth Pipko

Elizabeth Pipko (born June 26, 1995) is an American political operative, author, media personality, and former model recognized for her tenure as national spokesperson for the Republican National Committee and her establishment of The Exodus Movement to promote conservative values among Jewish Americans.[1][2] Born in New York City to Soviet émigré parents, Pipko, a first-generation American of Jewish heritage, initially competed as a figure skater and modeled for agencies like Wilhelmina, appearing in publications such as Maxim and Harper's Bazaar.[2][1] She transitioned to politics by joining Donald Trump's 2016 presidential campaign, later criticizing institutional antisemitism and pro-Palestinian campus activism, particularly at institutions like Harvard, where she pursued studies through the Extension School.[1][3] Pipko has authored works including Finding My Place: From the Streets of Kyiv to the American Dream, chronicling her family's Soviet-era experiences, and launched the Lest People Forget initiative for digital Holocaust education.[4][1] Her advocacy emphasizes strong U.S.-Israel ties and Republican outreach to Jewish voters disillusioned with Democratic policies.[3]

Early Life and Education

Family Heritage and Upbringing

Elizabeth Pipko was born on June 26, 1995, in New York City to parents who had immigrated from the former Soviet Union, making her a first-generation American.[2] Her father, an attorney, escaped the Soviet Union in 1981 after enduring persecution, arriving in the United States with limited resources and building a new life as an immigrant.[5] Her mother, a touring concert pianist, and maternal grandfather, the Soviet-born artist Marc Klionsky—known for portraits of notable figures including presidents and royalty—represented a family legacy of Jewish cultural resilience amid Soviet-era oppression.[6] [7] The family's Russian-Jewish heritage, marked by risks taken to flee anti-Semitic policies and economic hardship, instilled in Pipko an early awareness of freedoms absent in their homeland of origin.[8] Raised in New York, Pipko attended the Rabbi Schneier Park East Day School, a Jewish institution that fostered her commitment to religious observance and cultural identity.[8] Her parents arrived in America with approximately $90, emphasizing through their experiences the value of opportunity and self-reliance, in contrast to the suppression of Jewish practice and personal ambitions they faced under Soviet rule, where dissent could result in physical punishment.[9] This upbringing highlighted the sacrifices of her grandparents and parents, who prioritized escape from persecution to enable open Jewish expression for their descendants, shaping Pipko's perspective on heritage as a foundation for gratitude toward American liberties.[10]

Academic Background

Elizabeth Pipko earned a Bachelor of Liberal Arts (ALB) from Harvard University's Extension School.[11][12] She later obtained a Master of Science (MS) from the University of Pennsylvania, pursuing the latter after her involvement in the 2016 presidential campaign.[13][14] These degrees reflect her commitment to higher education amid a career spanning modeling and political advocacy.[15]

Modeling Career

Entry into the Industry

Pipko was discovered on the street in New York City by a photographer at age 17, who took test shots of her and forwarded them to Wilhelmina Models, a prominent agency.[10] [16] She signed with Wilhelmina Models that year, marking her formal entry into the industry while still residing in her hometown.[17] [11] Her prior experience as a competitive figure skater contributed to her physical discipline and poise, facilitating the transition from athletics to modeling.[12] Her debut professional assignment was an editorial shoot for Fitness magazine, which she later described as a defining early milestone that affirmed her potential in the field.[16] This opportunity arose soon after signing with the agency, highlighting the rapid validation of her look and marketability in commercial and editorial contexts.[18] Wilhelmina's representation enabled initial placements in high-profile outlets, though Pipko has noted the industry's competitive demands required resilience developed from her skating background.[19]

Professional Experiences and Challenges

Pipko began her modeling career at age 17, signing with the Wilhelmina agency in New York City after being discovered unexpectedly.[18][20] Her early experiences in the industry emphasized lessons in self-value and resilience amid its competitive demands.[18] She appeared in high-profile publications such as Maxim, Esquire, and Sports Illustrated, establishing a presence in fashion and swimwear modeling.[20][18] The modeling world presented inherent challenges, particularly for Pipko as a young entrant, contributing to her struggles with anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem during her teenage years.[21] She has described the industry as "equally brutal" and cutthroat compared to politics, with sharp-elbowed competition requiring constant navigation of professional relationships.[20] A pivotal challenge emerged in 2019 following her public endorsement of Donald Trump on Fox & Friends, which triggered backlash from fashion industry figures; she subsequently lost contact with agents, photographers, and fellow models, effectively sacrificing her career trajectory to align with her political beliefs.[18][20] Despite this, Pipko later re-entered modeling to a limited extent, viewing the earlier setbacks as a formative pivot rather than a permanent barrier.[20]

Political Awakening and Involvement

Initial Political Engagement

Elizabeth Pipko's initial foray into politics occurred during the 2016 U.S. presidential election when, at age 21, she walked into a Donald Trump campaign volunteer center at Trump Tower in Manhattan and began as a part-time volunteer.[22] She advanced quickly to a full-time role as nationwide coordinator for volunteer operations on the Trump campaign.[22][13] Pipko maintained secrecy about her involvement throughout the campaign, fearing backlash in the modeling industry, which she described as overwhelmingly supportive of Trump's opponents.[23] This discretion stemmed from her concurrent career as a swimsuit and lingerie model, where public conservative views could limit opportunities.[24] Her engagement reflected an early commitment to Trump's platform, though no prior organized political activity is documented.[22] Following Trump's victory on November 8, 2016, Pipko returned to modeling while retaining ties to Republican circles, including meeting her future husband, Darren Centinello, during the campaign.[22][23] This period laid the groundwork for her subsequent political roles, marking a transition from private support to public advocacy years later.[13]

Support for Donald Trump and Campaigns

Elizabeth Pipko expressed early support for Donald Trump during his 2016 presidential campaign, concealing her political views from peers in the modeling industry to avoid professional repercussions. In a January 2019 appearance on Fox & Friends, the then-23-year-old model disclosed that she had maintained her Trump endorsement as a closely guarded secret amid widespread opposition within fashion circles.[25] Her involvement deepened through direct participation in Trump's campaigns, where she transitioned from modeling to political advocacy. Pipko credited the campaign environment with providing purpose, stating in a July 2025 Instagram post that she discovered her calling "in the most unlikely of places, a presidential campaign."[26] As a former spokesperson for the Trump campaign, she engaged in outreach efforts emphasizing unity and policy priorities.[27] Pipko actively campaigned for Trump in 2024, delivering speeches at rallies to mobilize supporters. On November 4, 2024, she spoke alongside Republican National Committee co-chair Lara Trump at a Trump event in Reading, Pennsylvania, the day before the election, highlighting themes of resilience and victory.[28] [29] In the same period, she accompanied Trump to significant sites, including a visit to the Ohel of Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson on October 9, 2024, marking the one-year anniversary of the October 7 attacks, underscoring her role in faith-based and pro-Israel mobilization.[30] Post-election, Pipko continued voicing strong endorsement of Trump's leadership. In a December 2024 Fox News Radio interview, she affirmed her trust in Trump's intent "to bring people together," reflecting on his unifying potential amid partisan divides.[31] By March 2025, she described Trump as having "completely changed my life" and "the perfect person to be president," attributing personal and ideological transformation to his influence.[15] Her advocacy often intertwined Trump's record on Israel and antisemitism with broader campaign narratives, as in a September 2024 Jerusalem Post discussion where she promoted his "peace through strength" doctrine as a bulwark against regional threats.[32]

Republican National Committee Tenure

Appointment as National Spokesperson

In May 2024, the Republican National Committee (RNC) appointed Elizabeth Pipko as its national spokesperson, a role focused on communicating the party's positions to media and the public.[33] The appointment came amid a leadership transition following the 2024 Republican primaries, with Michael Whatley as chair and Lara Trump as co-chair, emphasizing outreach to key demographics including Jewish voters and pro-Israel supporters.[3] Pipko's prior experience included coordinating nationwide volunteer operations for Donald Trump's 2016 presidential campaign and subsequent advocacy work, which positioned her as a rising voice in Republican communications.[13] The announcement gained attention on May 6, 2024, when Pipko appeared on WABC radio to discuss her new responsibilities, highlighting the RNC's strategy to counter Democratic messaging on issues like foreign policy and domestic security.[34] At 28 years old, Pipko's youth, New York background, and vocal support for Trump were cited as assets for appealing to younger conservatives and urban audiences, though some media outlets framed her modeling career alongside her political ascent.[35] RNC leadership selected her for her ability to articulate policies on U.S.-Israel relations and antisemitism, areas where she had previously engaged through events and writings, amid rising concerns over campus protests and international conflicts.[33]

Key Roles and Achievements in GOP Outreach

Pipko assumed the role of national spokesperson for the Republican National Committee (RNC) on May 13, 2024, tasked with communicating the party's positions and expanding its appeal to diverse voter groups, particularly through public advocacy on foreign policy and domestic issues affecting minority communities.[3][33] In this capacity, she has prioritized outreach to Jewish Americans, leveraging her background to underscore the GOP's pro-Israel stance and contrast it with perceived Democratic shortcomings on antisemitism, thereby aiming to realign traditionally Democratic-leaning Jewish voters.[36][13] A key achievement in her tenure includes amplifying the Republican platform's emphasis on "peace through strength" in Middle East policy, including promotion of the Abraham Accords brokered under former President Donald Trump, which she credits with advancing U.S.-Israel relations and regional stability without concessions to adversaries.[32] During the July 2024 Republican National Convention in Milwaukee, Pipko publicly affirmed the party's environment as safer and more supportive for Jewish participants than Democratic gatherings, citing reduced tolerance for antisemitic rhetoric and stronger commitments to Israel's security.[36] Her efforts have extended to media engagements, such as interviews highlighting GOP achievements in countering antisemitism and fostering Jewish outreach, building on her prior co-founding of the Jexodus initiative in 2018–2019, which sought to mobilize Jewish voters toward Republican support amid rising concerns over Democratic policies.[37] These activities have positioned her as a bridge between the RNC and Jewish communities, with her appointment itself noted for enhancing the party's visibility and credibility among Soviet Jewish émigré descendants and pro-Israel advocates.[13][33]

Advocacy and Public Commentary

Jewish Community Outreach and Pro-Israel Advocacy

Elizabeth Pipko has focused her Republican National Committee (RNC) tenure on expanding outreach to Jewish voters, particularly emphasizing the party's commitment to combating antisemitism and bolstering U.S.-Israel relations following the October 7, 2023, Hamas attacks on Israel.[32] Appointed as RNC national spokesperson in May 2024, she has coordinated efforts to highlight former President Donald Trump's "peace through strength" approach, including speeches at synagogues on Shabbat and appearances at the Republican Jewish Coalition's annual leadership summit in Las Vegas in September 2024.[13] [32] In December 2024, she was additionally named National Committeewoman for New York, further positioning her to strengthen GOP ties with the Jewish community.[33] To address rising antisemitism, Pipko launched "Lest People Forget" in 2023, described as the world's first digital Holocaust museum utilizing decentralized technology to educate and preserve historical memory against denialism.[3] She has criticized failures in Holocaust education and linked increased antisemitic incidents to policies under the Biden-Harris administration, advocating for expanded Title VI protections as implemented during Trump's presidency.[3] In February 2025, Pipko co-launched Chabad Friends with actor Michael Rapaport, an initiative aimed at uniting and strengthening the Jewish community through kosher dining experiences, accessible Torah study, and targeted community-building programs.[38] Pipko's pro-Israel advocacy underscores Israel's strategic importance to U.S. national security, promoting Trump's achievements such as relocating the U.S. embassy to Jerusalem in 2018 and brokering the Abraham Accords in 2020 as models for regional stability.[3] She has opposed appeasement of adversaries like Iran and critiqued Democratic policies for weakening alliances, arguing that a second Trump term would prioritize Israel's defense against threats.[32] Post-October 7, she partnered with Esteem Couture on a clothing line, directing 10% of proceeds to support Israeli women impacted by the attacks.[13] As a self-identified Zionist and granddaughter of Soviet Jewish immigrants, Pipko frames her efforts as continuing generational fights against hatred while appealing to Jewish Americans on bipartisan security concerns rather than partisan divides.[33]

Efforts Against Antisemitism and Democratic Criticisms

In 2019, Pipko founded the Exodus Movement, also known as Jexodus, to encourage Jewish Americans to abandon the Democratic Party, which she accused of fostering antisemitism through inadequate responses to controversial statements by figures like Representative Ilhan Omar.[6][39] The initiative highlighted Democratic failures to unequivocally condemn antisemitic tropes, such as Omar's remarks on AIPAC's influence, positioning the movement as a call for Jewish voters to realign with the Republican Party's stronger pro-Israel stance.[6][40] As national spokesperson for the Republican National Committee starting in 2021, Pipko amplified criticisms of Democratic handling of rising antisemitism, particularly on college campuses, attributing it to unchecked liberal biases and reluctance to address anti-Israel activism.[41] She argued that Democrats' responses to incidents of Jewish hatred often prioritized political correctness over decisive action, contrasting this with Republican commitments to combating such threats.[42] In public statements, Pipko described the Democratic Party as indifferent to Jewish concerns, stating, "There is anti-Semitism in the Democratic Party. They don't care about Israel or the Jewish people," in reference to party tolerance for anti-Zionist rhetoric equated with antisemitism.[43][39] Pipko's advocacy extended to educational efforts against historical amnesia fueling modern antisemitism, including her project "Lest People Forget," aimed at informing younger generations about the Holocaust to counter resurgence.[44] She has repeatedly condemned Democratic leadership for downplaying campus antisemitism—evident in protests following October 7, 2023—as a symptom of broader institutional left-wing tolerance, urging recognition of these patterns over partisan denial.[45][46] Through speeches and media appearances, such as on Fox Business and podcasts, she framed Democratic policies as enabling environments where antisemitic indifference thrives, while emphasizing Republican alternatives focused on security and heritage preservation.[41][47]

Authorship and Media Career

Published Books

Elizabeth Pipko has published three books, consisting of two self-published poetry collections from her teenage years and a memoir reflecting on her family's immigrant experience and American identity.[48][49] Her debut, Sweet Sixteen, a poetry collection exploring adolescent joys and challenges, was self-published through Archway Publishing on April 19, 2013 (ISBN 978-1-4808-0073-1).[50] The work draws from personal reflections on youth, with themes of poignancy and growth, as noted in its promotional description.[51] This was followed by About You, another self-published poetry volume through Archway Publishing in 2018 (ISBN 978-1-4808-6310-8), featuring concise, lyric-like verses intended to console and inspire readers amid personal trials.[52][48] In 2020, Pipko released Finding My Place: Making My Parents' American Dream Come True, published by Post Hill Press (ISBN 978-1-64293-559-2), a memoir detailing her upbringing as the daughter of Soviet Jewish immigrants, her embrace of religious Judaism, and the exceptional opportunities America provided her family.[49][53] The book emphasizes themes of gratitude for American values and critiques of alternative societal models, aligning with her later public advocacy.[49]

Media Appearances and Ongoing Influence

Elizabeth Pipko has made numerous media appearances, primarily on conservative outlets, where she comments on Republican politics, pro-Israel advocacy, and criticisms of Democratic policies. On September 23, 2024, she appeared on Fox Business to contrast former President Donald Trump's media engagement with Vice President Kamala Harris's approach, emphasizing Trump's direct communication style.[54] She featured on Dr. Phil on November 5, 2024, discussing her background as an RNC spokesperson, author, and political operative.[55] In 2025, Pipko continued frequent Fox News segments, including a June 26 appearance on Fox News @ Night critiquing Democratic strategies in New York City mayoral races, and an August 11 Sky News Australia interview addressing Nancy Pelosi's alleged insider trading evasion.[56][57] Her commentary often focuses on Middle East policy and domestic security. On October 9, 2025, Pipko joined Fox Across America to argue that a Trump presidency would have prevented the October 7, 2023, Hamas attacks and critiqued stalled peace efforts under the Biden administration.[58] She reiterated Trump's "peace-through-strength" approach on Fox on October 13, 2025, highlighting deal-making successes in the region.[59] Additional 2025 spots included Fox Business on September 8 discussing pro-family policies' potential to boost marriages and birth rates, and April 23 addressing Democratic misalignment with American values.[60][41] Pipko has also engaged in podcasts, such as the Success Story episode on July 21, 2025, sharing career advice, and earlier ILTV interviews on youth education regarding Israel.[61][44] Post-RNC tenure, Pipko maintains influence through commentary and advocacy. By May 2025, she joined Greece's Antenna Group as an exclusive political commentator, extending her reach internationally while retaining U.S. focus.[62] In September 2025, The Jerusalem Post recognized her as a "ViZionary" for promoting Jewish remembrance and Republican outreach amid rising antisemitism.[46] With over 212,000 Instagram followers as of late 2024, she uses social media for direct engagement, posting on October 23, 2025, about her father's Soviet escape to underscore immigration and opportunity themes.[63] Her ongoing roles amplify GOP-Jewish community ties, including speeches like her September 5, 2024, remarks at the Republican Jewish Coalition summit.[64] This sustained presence positions her as a bridge between fashion, media, and conservative activism.

Personal Life and Beliefs

Faith, Identity, and Family

Elizabeth Pipko identifies strongly as a Jew, shaped by her family's history of fleeing religious persecution in the Soviet Union, where her parents and grandparents endured beatings and risks for their faith under communist rule.[18] [65] As the daughter of Jewish immigrants from the former Soviet Union and granddaughter of the renowned Jewish artist Marc Klionsky, Pipko has emphasized that her heritage compels her to embrace her identity openly and proudly, viewing it as a privilege earned through her ancestors' sacrifices.[8] [66] [15] Raised in New York City, Pipko attended the Park East Day School under Rabbi Arthur Schneier, an institution rooted in Orthodox Jewish education, which reinforced her religious observance.[23] Her steadfast Jewish faith informs her public advocacy, including efforts against antisemitism and support for Israel, as she has described it as a guiding force in her political and personal life.[67] [13] In December 2018, Pipko married Darren Centinello, a fellow Trump campaign operative whom she met during the 2016 election cycle, in a traditional Jewish ceremony at Mar-a-Lago resort in Palm Beach, Florida.[8] [6] [23] The wedding was officiated by the same rabbi who had married her parents 28 years earlier, underscoring the continuity of her family's religious traditions.[8] The couple has no publicly reported children as of 2025.[13]

Core Political and Philosophical Views

Elizabeth Pipko identifies primarily as a supporter of Donald Trump and the Republican Party, viewing Trump as an outsider who embodies common-sense leadership rather than traditional politics. She has described Trump as "the perfect person to be president" due to his ability to deliver tangible results, such as moving the U.S. embassy to Jerusalem and brokering the Abraham Accords, and credits him with personally transforming her life during a period of personal struggle.[15] Pipko emphasizes Trump's resilience and pro-Israel stance as aligning with her belief in "peace through strength," positioning him as a defender of both American and Israeli interests against common adversaries.[32] Her endorsement of Trump stems from his perceived honesty and rejection of elite political norms, which she contrasts with alternatives like Hillary Clinton or Joe Biden, stating she would "die on that hill" in his defense.[15] Pipko's political outlook is deeply shaped by her family's history of fleeing Soviet communism, instilling a profound appreciation for American freedoms, individual opportunity, and the rule of law as safeguards against authoritarianism. She views being American as "an incredible gift that nobody should take for granted," a privilege earned through her parents' sacrifices, and advocates for policies preserving economic freedom and religious liberty to honor such immigrant legacies.[18] While wary of rigid labels like "conservative" due to their potential to foster division, she aligns with GOP principles on limited government intervention, strong U.S.-Israel relations, and opposition to socialist policies, critiquing the Democratic Party for enabling antisemitism and abandoning Jewish voters.[18] [67] This perspective drives her efforts, such as founding the Exodus Movement in 2019, to encourage Jewish Americans to reconsider monolithic Democratic loyalty in favor of parties prioritizing security and heritage.[67] Philosophically, Pipko prioritizes authenticity, self-reliance, and faith-guided action, asserting that her Jewish identity comes "first" and informs every decision, including opposition to abortion based on Torah principles while favoring state-level resolutions.[18] [15] She promotes civility in political discourse, urging mutual respect for differing beliefs on topics like religion and policy, provided they do not harm others, and stresses freedom of thought as essential to the American experiment.[67] Pipko also supports pro-family policies, seeing them as vital for societal renewal, and critiques "woke" cultural trends and media bias for eroding national resilience and open debate.[60] [15] Her worldview underscores judging individuals by actions over affiliations, rooted in a commitment to religious liberty and the moral imperative to combat threats like antisemitism through principled advocacy.[18]

Controversies and Receptions

Backlash from Fashion and Liberal Circles

Elizabeth Pipko concealed her support for Donald Trump and her role as a staffer on his 2016 presidential campaign from modeling industry colleagues, citing fears that disclosure would terminate her career in a field dominated by liberal viewpoints.[68][69] In January 2019, she publicly revealed her affiliations during an appearance on Fox & Friends, stating that the fashion world's political homogeneity made open conservatism untenable for professional advancement.[70] This disclosure prompted immediate professional fallout, including lost modeling opportunities, as she later explained that her beliefs directly conflicted with industry expectations.[18] Pipko has described the backlash as a form of informal cancellation, where peers distanced themselves or withheld work due to her Trump endorsement, contrasting sharply with the sector's tolerance for progressive activism.[23] She emphasized that while modeling agencies and colleagues rarely confronted her explicitly, the subtle exclusion—such as unreturned calls and declined bookings—signaled rejection of her views, ultimately leading her to pivot fully to political advocacy by mid-2019.[18] This experience underscored broader tensions in entertainment-adjacent industries, where conservative expressions often invite ostracism absent equivalent scrutiny for left-leaning positions.[69] Liberal political circles have similarly critiqued Pipko's advocacy, particularly her founding of the Exodus Movement in 2019 to encourage Jewish voters to abandon the Democratic Party over perceived tolerance of antisemitism within its ranks.[6] Democratic responses framed her efforts as partisan exaggeration, with party spokespeople dismissing claims of systemic bias as unfounded attacks on figures like Ilhan Omar, though without direct personal targeting of Pipko beyond general rebuttals.[6] Her subsequent roles, including as an RNC spokesperson, amplified such friction, as liberal media outlets portrayed her transition from modeling to Trump-aligned commentary as opportunistic, while overlooking analogous career shifts by progressive influencers.[24]

Defenses, Achievements, and Broader Impact

Elizabeth Pipko's appointment as national spokesperson for the Republican National Committee in May 2024 marked a significant achievement, positioning her as a key communicator for the party's outreach to Jewish voters and pro-Israel advocates.[33] In this role, she emphasized Republican commitments to combating antisemitism and supporting Israel, particularly highlighting Donald Trump's "peace through strength" strategy during the 2024 campaign.[32] Her prior work as founder of the Exodus Movement (originally Jexodus), launched in 2019, aimed to persuade Jewish Democrats to realign with Republicans amid perceived Democratic tolerance for antisemitism and anti-Zionism.[23] [39] In response to criticisms from liberal and fashion industry circles accusing her of betraying progressive norms by embracing conservatism, Pipko has defended her positions by arguing that political labels fail to capture nuanced views on issues like Israel and antisemitism.[69] She has described facing cancellation attempts from both political extremes but maintained that her advocacy stems from personal heritage as the granddaughter of Soviet Jewish immigrants, prioritizing empirical threats like rising campus antisemitism over ideological conformity.[23] Supporters, including within the Republican Jewish Coalition, have praised her resilience, viewing her as a "beacon" for integrating Jewish identity with GOP priorities despite industry backlash.[13] Pipko's broader impact includes galvanizing Jewish voter shifts toward Republicans, with her Exodus Movement contributing to reported increases in Jewish support for Trump post-October 7, 2023, attacks on Israel.[71] She co-founded the "Jewish Voices for Trump" coalition in August 2024, amplifying pro-Israel messaging to counter Democratic policies.[72] Additionally, her 2023 launch of a technology-driven global Holocaust remembrance platform sought to educate amid surging antisemitism, while collaborations like a clothing line donating proceeds to Israeli victims underscored her fusion of media influence with advocacy.[73] These efforts have positioned her as an influential figure bridging modeling, authorship, and politics to promote unapologetic Zionism and conservative values within American Jewish communities.[3]
