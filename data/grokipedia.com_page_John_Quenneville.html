John Quenneville

John Quenneville (born April 16, 1996) is a Canadian former professional ice hockey centre. Quenneville played in the National Hockey League (NHL) for the New Jersey Devils and Chicago Blackhawks, as well as in European leagues, most recently with Tappara in the Liiga during the 2024–25 season before retiring.[1] Quenneville was selected by the New Jersey Devils in the first round (30th overall) of the 2014 NHL entry draft.

Playing career

Junior

Quenneville was selected by the Brandon Wheat Kings in the 3rd round (54th overall) of the 2011 WHL Bantam Draft.[2] He played with the Brandon Wheat Kings from the 2012–13 WHL season to 2015–16 WHL season.[3] While playing with the Wheat Kings, Quenneville was rated as a top prospect who was viewed as a possible first round selection heading into the 2014 NHL entry draft.[4][5] He was eventually drafted 30th overall by the New Jersey Devils in the 2014 NHL Entry Draft.

Professional

On July 13, 2015, Quenneville signed a three-year entry-level contract with the Devils,[6] and after concluding his WHL career that season he subsequently joined the Albany Devils for the 2016–17 season. He made his NHL debut on December 1, 2016, against the Chicago Blackhawks.[7] On January 5, 2017, Quenneville was named to the 2017 AHL All-Star Classic, along with Joseph Blandisi.[8] He was recalled to the NHL on March 15, 2017,[9] and scored his first NHL goal in a 3–2 overtime win over the New York Rangers on March 21, 2017.[10] On June 22, 2019, Quenneville was traded to the Chicago Blackhawks in exchange for John Hayden.[11] On June 28, the Blackhawks signed Quenneville to a two-year contract extension.[12] He spent the 2019–20 and 2020–21 seasons with the Blackhawks' AHL affiliate, the Rockford IceHogs, recording 6 points in 28 combined games.[13] On July 21, 2021, Quenneville was selected from the Blackhawks at the 2021 NHL expansion draft by the Seattle Kraken.[14] As a pending unrestricted free agent, Quenneville was not signed by the Kraken, and on August 10, 2021, he agreed to his first contract abroad in accepting a tryout arrangement with Swiss club, ZSC Lions of the National League (NL).[15] Through making an initial positive impression, Quenneville converted his tryout into a one-year deal for the remainder of the 2021–22 season on September 5, 2021.[16] He concluded his first European season with 20 goals and 17 assists for 37 points in 46 regular season games.[13] Remaining in Europe, Quenneville as a free agent secured a contract with Swedish top flight club, Leksands IF of the Swedish Hockey League (SHL), on July 15, 2022.[17] In the following 2022–23 season, Quenneville was limited by injury and registered 5 goals and 7 assists for 12 points through 19 regular season games with Leksands before being released to return to North America.[13] On February 2, 2023, Quenneville returned to the AHL by signing for the remainder of the season with the Belleville Senators, the primary affiliate of the Ottawa Senators, where he recorded 4 goals and 11 assists for 15 points in 25 games.[18][13] On December 12, 2023, Quenneville signed a one-year contract with HC Lugano of the Swiss National League.[19] During the 2023–24 season, he posted 2 goals and 8 assists for 10 points in 20 regular season games and 0 points in 2 playoff games with Lugano. He also represented Team Canada at the Spengler Cup, tallying 2 goals and 4 assists for 6 points in 4 games.[13] On November 9, 2024, Quenneville signed a season-long contract to play with Tappara in the Finnish Liiga.[20] In the 2024–25 season, he recorded 7 goals and 7 assists for 14 points in 24 games with Tappara before retiring from professional hockey on September 27, 2025.[13][1]

International play

As a 16-year-old, Quenneville was chosen to compete with the Canada Pacific squad at the 2013 World U-17 Hockey Challenge.[21] He helped Team Canada capture the bronze medal at the 2014 IIHF World U18 Championships.[22] Quenneville made his senior international debut with Team Canada at the 2023 Spengler Cup, where he recorded two goals and four assists in four games as Canada advanced to the semifinals.[23]

Personal life

Quenneville's older brother Peter was drafted in the seventh round by the Columbus Blue Jackets in the 2013 NHL entry draft,[24] and his younger brother David Quenneville was drafted 200th overall in the 2016 NHL entry draft.[25] Former NHL player and head coach Joel Quenneville is his second cousin.[26][27] Former NHL defenseman Johnny Boychuk is his uncle by marriage.[28]
