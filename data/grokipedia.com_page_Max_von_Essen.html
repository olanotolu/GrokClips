Max von Essen

Max von Essen (born January 11, 1974) is an American Tony Award nominated stage and screen actor, and vocalist. His Broadway credits include roles in An American in Paris, Anastasia, Evita, and Chicago. He also starred as Marvin in the United States national tour of Falsettos and as Keith in the television series Dexter: Resurrection (2025)[1].

Life and career

Raised on Long Island, von Essen is a graduate of South Side High School in Rockville Centre, New York. He attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, and after graduation toured with Liza Minnelli.[2] He is a member of the Von Essen family, who are part of the German nobility and Swedish nobility. A son of Rita and Thomas Von Essen, who was the New York City Fire Commissioner during the 9/11 terrorist attacks on the World Trade Center, he is the youngest of four children. He is openly gay.[3] He toured Europe in West Side Story and was a cast member of the U.S. national tour of Chicago. He made his Broadway debut in Jesus Christ Superstar in 2000 as Disciple and Jesus of Nazareth understudy.[4] He appeared in Les MisÃ©rables on Broadway as the replacement for Fauchelevent and other roles,[5] and starred in Dance of the Vampires alongside Michael Crawford in 2002.[6] He played the role of student revolutionary leader Enjolras (replacement) in the Les MisÃ©rables Broadway revival in 2006 at the Broadhurst Theater.[7] In 2006, he was a soloist at the biggest Andrew Lloyd Webber musical gala to date, held in Tallinn, Estonia.[8] He performed in the national tour of Xanadu as "Sonny" in 2008[9] and in the Roundabout Theater Company Off-Broadway production of Maury Yeston's Death Takes a Holiday at the Laura Pels Theatre in 2011.[10] In 2015, von Essen played the role of Parisian aristocrat Henri in the Broadway production of An American in Paris, for which he received a Tony Award for Best Featured Actor in a Musical nomination.[11] Following this, he originated the role of Gleb in Anastasia on Broadway (2017â€“2019), starred as Marvin in the U.S. national tour of Falsettos (2019), and returned to Broadway as Billy Flynn in Chicago (2023â€“present, as of October 2025).[12]
