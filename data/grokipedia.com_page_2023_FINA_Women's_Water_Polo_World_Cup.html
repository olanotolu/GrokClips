2023 World Aquatics Women's Water Polo World Cup

The 2023 World Aquatics Women's Water Polo World Cup was the 18th edition of the tournament. It ran from 11 April to 25 June 2023.[1] The Super final took place between 23 and 25 June 2023 in Long Beach, California, United States.[2] From this year on, the tournament was replacing the FINA Water Polo World League.[1] The United States won their fourth straight and fifth overall title, after defeating the Netherlands in the final.[3]

Format

There were two divisions. In Division 1, the top eight teams from the World Championships competed. The eight teams played in two qualification rounds in April at different venues: Round 1 in Rotterdam, Netherlands (11â€“13 April), and Round 2 in Athens, Greece (19â€“21 April). In Round 1, they were divided into two groups of four for round-robin play. In Round 2, the top two from each Round 1 group played a round-robin (points carried over) for seeding, while the bottom two from each played another round-robin; the top four from the upper group and the top two from the lower group advanced to the Super Final. Teams outside the top eight participated in Division 2 via an intercontinental qualification tournament in Berlin, Germany (2â€“4 May), with eight teams divided into two groups for round-robin play, followed by classification and crossover games; the top two teams qualified for the Super Final, held between 23 and 25 June 2023.[4][5][6] At the end of the tournament, the last-ranked team was relegated to Division 2 and the winner of Division 2 moved to Division 1 for the following year. A win gave a team three points, a win after penalties two, a loss after penalties one and a loss after regular time zero points.[7]

The draw was held on 1 December 2022.[8] The first tournament was played from 11 to 13 April in Rotterdam, Netherlands and the second tournament between 19 and 21 April 2023 in Athens, Greece.[6] The top four teams from the first tournament qualified for the super final but participated in the second tournament as well, where the last two spots were played out.[2]
