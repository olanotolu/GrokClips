Itajaí

Itajaí (Portuguese pronunciation: [itaʒaˈi]) is a municipality in the state of Santa Catarina (state), Brazil. It is located on the northern central coast of Santa Catarina (state) and is part of the Vale do Itajaí, on the right bank of the Itajaí-Açu river mouth. It lies at the mouth of the Itajaí River, at 2 m (7 ft) above sea level. The city has the largest gross domestic product in the State of Santa Catarina (state). [1]

History

Prior to European arrival, the region was inhabited by indigenous groups such as the Xokleng. The city was founded on June 15, 1860, but the colonization of Itajaí had started in 1658 when the Paulista João Dias D'Arzão arrived in the region. In 1750, Portuguese colonists coming from Madeira and the Azores made this region their home. By 1823 it became a prominent region for Portuguese settlers and, at the end of the 19th century, received a great number of German immigrants. Itajaí and most of Santa Catarina State area is prone to torrential storms specially during the Spring season. Floods are frequent and even tornadoes can form. In late November 2008, 128 people perished in the Santa Catarina state floods due to heavy rains in southern Brazil. Large parts of the city were underwater and the port was seriously damaged. Many lost their homes and other possessions and had no ready shelters to turn to, for the rain combined with river waters invaded every section of the city and most of Central and Coastal Santa Catarina State. President Lula da Silva and other officials flew over the area to evaluate the damage and offer help. The state was declared a disaster area by the State and Federal governments.

Religion

According to the 2022 IBGE census, the predominant religions in the municipality are Roman Catholic (52.13% of the population with 10 years or older) and Evangelical (30.98%). Other groups include those without religion (8.68%), Spiritist, Umbanda, Candomblé, Buddhism, and other religions (collectively about 8.21%), but with less representation. https://itajaipedia.com.br/artigos/religiao-censo-do-ibge-2022/

Geography

Itajaí occupies an area of 289.215 km² (2022), with approximately 20% urban and 80% rural or conservation areas. It is located on the north coast of Santa Catarina (state), specifically at the mouth of the Itajaí-Açu River. It borders the cities of Balneário Camboriú, Camboriú, Brusque, Santa Catarina, Gaspar, Santa Catarina, Ilhota and Navegantes. It is the headquarters of the largest fishing port in Brazil and one of the largest universities in the state, Univali (Universidade do Vale do Itajaí). Itajaí is located in the Lower Valley of the Itajaí River, on the coast and the Atlantic Ocean. It is situated at the mouth of the Itajaí-Açu River, allowing the existence of the port. The city is basically flat, due to being at sea level. The highest point is Morro da Cruz, approximately 180 meters above sea level. It is part of the active Região Metropolitana da Foz do Rio Itajaí, together with neighboring municipalities. The BR-101 is the main link to the city, and highways SC-100, SC-412 (formerly BR-486), SC-470 and BR-470 link Itajaí to the rest of the state.

Climate

Itajaí has a humid subtropical climate (Köppen climate classification: Cfa) with year-round precipitation, though higher in summer than winter. Historical extremes include a high of 40 °C (January 1985) and a low of -1 °C (July 1975). Recent records (2010–present) show highs up to 39.0 °C and lows to 1.3 °C.

Economy

International trade

As of 2022, the municipality of Itajaí conducted 1.51% of Brazil's total export trade with $5.049 billion (USD) worth of goods. This figure places Itajaí as the 6th highest exporter in Brazil.[2] A significant portion consists of animal products, with the Port of Itajaí serving as Brazil's top hub for chicken exports as of 2025 and handling 34.41% of national pork exports in 2023.[3][4]

Port of Itajaí

The Port of Itajaí is the main port of Santa Catarina, and the second largest in Brazil in terms of the movement of containers. It serves as the main port for exports in the region, and almost all production of the state of Santa Catarina moves through it at some point.

Tourism

Itajaí has many beaches, among them are the Molhes, Atalaia, Jeremias, Cabeçudas, Morcego, Brava (Angry) Beach and Solidão. One well-known tourist attraction is the Parrot's Beak, a 6-meter-tall rock in the shape of a parrot. The city also has extensive rural areas and natural scenery, with a rich heritage of the Portuguese people and Germans immigrants. Itajaí has a pier for passenger vessels, serving the coast of Santa Catarina. The Marejada, a Portugal festival of fishing and seafood, is the principal festival of the city, showcasing attractions relative to the ocean and the Azores. Itajaí is also the hub of Clube Náutico Marcílio Dias, a sport association for both association football (soccer) and competitive rowing (sport). In 2013 it has been chosen as the arrival point of the Transat Jacques Vabre and it was a stopover for the Volvo Ocean Race in 2012, 2015 and 2018, as well as for The Ocean Race in 2023, with future stopovers planned for 2027 and 2031.[5] The city is served by Ministro Victor Konder International Airport located in the adjoining municipality of Navegantes. The Marejada has been held annually each October in Itajaí since 1987.[6] The 2024 edition, its 35th, attracted over 120,000 visitors.[7]
