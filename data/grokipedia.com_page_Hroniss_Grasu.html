Hroniss Grasu

(/həˈroʊnɪs ˈɡrɑːsuː/ hə-ROH-niss GRAH-soo; born August 12, 1991) is an American professional football center. He played college football for the University of Oregon, was drafted by the Chicago Bears in the third round of the 2015 NFL draft, and is married to WNBA player Sabrina Ionescu.[1] Stats at Pro Football Reference

Early life

A native of Los Angeles, California, Grasu attended Crespi Carmelite High School, where he was an All-State offensive lineman. Regarded as a three-star recruit by Rivals.com, Grasu was ranked as the No. 12 center prospect in his class.[2]

College career

Grasu redshirted his freshman year in 2010 before taking over as the starting center for the Oregon Ducks as a redshirt freshman in 2011, remaining the starter through his senior season in 2014. He earned second-team All-Freshman honors from Phil Steele in 2011,1 as well as first-team All-Pac-12 honors in 2012,2 2013, and 2014.3 He was named a first-team All-American in 2013 and 2014.4 He was also a finalist for the Rimington Trophy in 2013 and 2014.[3]

Chicago Bears

Grasu was selected by the Chicago Bears in the third round (71st pick overall) of the 2015 NFL draft.[6] The move reunited him with college teammate Kyle Long. On August 30, 2016, Grasu was placed on injured reserve.[7] On September 2, 2018, Grasu was released by the Bears.[8]

Baltimore Ravens (first stint)

On September 24, 2018, Grasu was signed by the Baltimore Ravens.[9] He played in three games before being released on November 24, 2018.[10]

Miami Dolphins

On December 12, 2018, Grasu was signed by the Miami Dolphins, but was released nine days later.[11][12]

Tennessee Titans

On February 7, 2019, Grasu was signed by the Tennessee Titans, reuniting with college teammate Marcus Mariota.[13] He was released on August 31, 2019.[14] He was re-signed on September 10, 2019.[15] He was released again on October 8, 2019.[16] He was signed once again on October 31, 2019.[17] He was released on December 3, 2019.

Baltimore Ravens (second stint)

On December 4, 2019, Grasu was claimed off waivers by the Ravens.[18]

San Francisco 49ers

On August 20, 2020, Grasu signed with the San Francisco 49ers.[19] He was released on September 5, 2020, and signed to the practice squad the next day.[20][21] After being elevated to the active roster for the team's first two games, Grasu was promoted to the active roster on September 26, 2020.[22] He was placed on the team's reserve/COVID-19 list by the team on November 19, 2020,[23] and activated on November 25.[24] He was placed back on the COVID-19 list on December 28, 2020,[25] and activated on January 14, 2021.[26]

Las Vegas Raiders

On October 25, 2021, Grasu was signed to the Las Vegas Raiders practice squad.[27] After the Raiders were eliminated in the 2021 Wild Card round of the playoffs, he signed a reserve/future contract on January 17, 2022.[28] On August 30, 2022, Grasu was waived by the Raiders and signed to the practice squad the next day.[29][30] He was promoted to the active roster on December 10.[31] On August 29, 2023, Grasu was released by the Raiders and re-signed to the practice squad.[32] He was signed to the active roster on December 21.[33] Following the 2023 season, Grasu became an unrestricted free agent and has not signed with an NFL team as of October 2025.[34][35]

Personal life

Grasu is of Romanian descent.[36] His parents, Ștefan and Mariana Grasu, emigrated from Romania to Los Angeles in 1982 and opened Greco's New York Pizzeria on Hollywood Boulevard.[37][38] His older brother, Nico, was a placekicker for Washington State (2008–10). Grasu was roommates with Bryan Bennett, Oregon's former backup quarterback and a former teammate of Grasu at Crespi.[39] Grasu married New York Liberty guard Sabrina Ionescu in March 2024.[1][40]
