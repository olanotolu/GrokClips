New York-New York Hotel and Casino

The New York-New York Hotel and Casino is a casino resort located at 3790 South Las Vegas Boulevard on the Las Vegas Strip in Paradise, Nevada, featuring a New York City theme with full-scale replicas of landmarks including the Statue of Liberty, Brooklyn Bridge, and Manhattan skyline facades.[1][2] Opened on January 3, 1997, after construction began in March 1995 at a cost of $460 million as a joint venture between MGM Grand Inc. and Primadonna Resorts, the property was developed to evoke the energy of Manhattan through its architecture, signage, and streetscape simulations.[3][4][2] Owned and operated by MGM Resorts International, it offers approximately 2,000 guest rooms and suites, a 65,000-square-foot casino with slots and table games, multiple dining options, and entertainment venues including the Big Apple Coaster, a roller coaster reaching speeds over 67 mph with a 180-degree heartline twist and 203-foot drop.[2][5][6] The resort's defining characteristics include its immersive urban theme that integrates gaming, hospitality, and thrill rides, contributing to its role as a staple attraction on the southern Strip adjacent to the Tropicana Avenue intersection.[1][2]

History

Development and Construction

The New York-New York Hotel and Casino was developed through a joint venture between MGM Grand Inc. and Primadonna Resorts Inc., formed in 1994 to capitalize on the Las Vegas Strip's expansion.[7] MGM contributed an 18-acre plot at the corner of Tropicana Avenue and the Las Vegas Strip, while Primadonna provided $40 million in equity financing, with the project supported by additional debt and equity totaling $460 million.[7] The venture aimed to replicate a compressed version of 1940s New York City architecture to attract East Coast visitors amid a boom in themed mega-resorts.[8] Construction began in March 1995 and involved erecting 12 New York-style skyscrapers at one-third scale, housing 2,024 guest rooms across structures modeled after landmarks including the Empire State Building, Chrysler Building, and Municipal Building.[9] The tallest tower, a 47-story replica of the Empire State Building, reached 529 feet in height.[9] Architectural design was led by Gaskin-Bezanski & Associates, with structural engineering by IMB Structural Engineers; Schuff Steel fabricated and erected 15,000 tons of structural steel for the complex.[9] Key construction elements included a replica Brooklyn Bridge connecting the property's casino floor, themed after Greenwich Village and Times Square, and the integration of the Manhattan Express roller coaster weaving through the skyline replicas.[8] The project encompassed 84,000 square feet of gaming space and family-oriented attractions like a Coney Island-style entertainment area, completed without reported major delays or innovations beyond the thematic scaling and steel-intensive fabrication.[9][8]

Opening and Initial Operations

The New York-New York Hotel and Casino opened to the public on January 3, 1997, following a $460 million construction project undertaken as a joint venture between MGM Grand Inc. and Primadonna Resorts.[10] The property debuted with 2,024 guest rooms, including 104 suites, and an 84,000-square-foot casino floor offering slots, table games, and other gaming options.[11] Initial operations emphasized the resort's New York City-themed attractions, such as a Coney Island-style roller coaster known as the Manhattan Express, a 150-foot-tall replica of the Statue of Liberty, and a pedestrian Brooklyn Bridge entrance connecting to the Las Vegas Strip.[10] Operators projected strong initial visitation, estimating 18 million guests for the property's first full year, capitalizing on its location at 3790 South Las Vegas Boulevard and proximity to the newly constructed Tropicana Avenue bridge over the monorail tracks.[10] The resort's 300,000-square-foot footprint incorporated themed districts evoking Times Square, Greenwich Village, and Central Park, with dining and entertainment venues launching alongside the hotel and casino to draw families and tourists seeking a condensed Manhattan experience.[10] Early programming included live performances and arcade attractions integrated into the Coney Island boardwalk area, supporting round-the-clock operations typical of Strip properties.[12] MGM Grand Inc. handled much of the promotional rollout, highlighting the resort's scale models of Manhattan landmarks like the Empire State Building and Chrysler Building at one-third size.[10]

Expansions and Renovations

In the years following its 1997 opening, the New York-New York experienced limited physical expansions, with initial plans for a 350-room addition announced shortly after launch but resulting in no net increase beyond the original 2,024 rooms.[13] A significant exterior renovation occurred between 2013 and 2014 as part of MGM Resorts' broader development of The Park, an open-air dining and entertainment district linking New York-New York to the adjacent Monte Carlo (later rebranded Park MGM), which included facade updates to enhance pedestrian connectivity and visual appeal along the Las Vegas Strip.[14] This work also involved the removal of thematic elements such as a fountain modeled after a New York City Fire Department fireboat, streamlining the property's landscape. The most extensive recent overhaul was a $63 million guest room remodel initiated in August 2022, encompassing all 2,024 standard rooms, premier rooms, Spa Suites, Marquis Suites, and Players Suites, with modernized designs featuring updated furnishings, technology, and city-inspired aesthetics while preserving the New York theme.[15][16] Completion of the project was targeted for summer 2023, introducing refreshed accommodations to compete with newer Strip properties.[17] As of August 2025, additional renovations were underway, impacting operational areas like cashier services and contributing to extended wait times for guests.[18] These updates reflect ongoing efforts by MGM Resorts to maintain competitiveness amid evolving visitor preferences and infrastructure demands on the Las Vegas Strip.

Notable Incidents

On July 6, 2007, Steven Zegrean, a 51-year-old unemployed painter estranged from his family, entered the casino floor and fired 16 shots from a semiautomatic handgun, wounding five individuals with non-life-threatening injuries including graze wounds and a broken leg.[19] Zegrean, who had been staying at the hotel, surrendered to police after the incident; he was convicted of five counts of attempted murder and sentenced in October 2009 to a prison term of 26 to 90 years, with parole eligibility after 26 years.[20] In mid-December 2020, the Big Apple Coaster experienced a derailment on its lift hill during closed testing of newly introduced Premier Rides train cars, with the middle car of the three-car train coming off the track and causing damage to the ride structure, wheels, and bogies.[21] No injuries occurred as the incident happened without passengers, leading to an indefinite closure for repairs and inspections that delayed the ride's reopening until March 2021.[21] The coaster, known for its rough operation, has been associated with rider injury claims including whiplash and neck strain, though specific fatality data remains absent from public records.[22] On June 24, 2023, a pickup truck ignited in the hotel's parking garage, damaging three additional vehicles but resulting in no reported injuries or broader structural impact to the property.[23]

Ownership and Operations

MGM Resorts Acquisition and Management

The New York-New York Hotel and Casino opened on January 3, 1997, as a joint venture between MGM Grand, Inc. and Primadonna Resorts, Inc., with each holding a 50% ownership interest.[24] On March 1, 1999, MGM Grand completed its acquisition of Primadonna Resorts through an all-stock transaction valued at approximately $276 million, assuming about $325 million in debt and gaining full control of the property along with Primadonna's Primm Valley resorts.[25] [26] In May 2000, MGM Grand merged with Mirage Resorts, Inc., in a $6.4 billion dealâ€”the largest gaming industry transaction at the timeâ€”forming MGM MIRAGE, which consolidated ownership and operations of New York-New York under the new entity.[24] The company acquired Mandalay Resort Group in 2005 for $7.9 billion, further expanding its portfolio, and rebranded as MGM Resorts International in 2010 to reflect its broader hospitality focus.[27] [28] Under MGM Resorts International's ownership evolution, the real estate assets of New York-New York were contributed to MGM Growth Properties in 2016 and subsequently acquired by VICI Properties in a $17.2 billion transaction completed in April 2022, establishing a long-term lease structure where MGM pays rent to VICI while retaining operational control.[29] [30] MGM Resorts manages New York-New York as part of its Las Vegas Strip operations, integrating it into a cluster with adjacent properties like MGM Grand and Excalibur for shared resources and guest experiences. As of September 2025, the resort falls under the oversight of Mike Neubecker, president and chief operating officer for MGM Grand, Excalibur, New York-New York, and the Signature at MGM Grand, who reports to MGM's corporate leadership in Las Vegas.[31] Daily operations emphasize gaming, hospitality, and themed entertainment, with MGM leveraging centralized systems for revenue management, marketing, and compliance with Nevada Gaming Control Board regulations.[1]

Economic Role in Las Vegas

The New York-New York Hotel and Casino serves as a substantial employer in Las Vegas, with a workforce ranging from 1,001 to 5,000 individuals across roles in gaming, hospitality, maintenance, and entertainment operations.[32] This employment supports local households through wages and benefits, contributing to the stability of the regional labor market amid fluctuations in tourism demand. As an operator under MGM Resorts International, the property integrates into the broader Strip ecosystem, where MGM's Las Vegas resorts collectively reported net revenues of $2.1 billion in the second quarter of 2025, reflecting a 4% year-over-year decline attributed to softened mid-market visitation.[33] The casino's gaming floor, themed attractions such as the Big Apple Coaster, and New York-inspired amenities drive visitor expenditures on slots, table games, lodging, and dining, funneling economic activity into Clark County. While individual gross gaming revenue figures for the property are not publicly disaggregated in Nevada Gaming Control Board reports, it operates within the Las Vegas Strip segment, which generated over $5.3 billion in gross gaming revenue in fiscal year 2024 across 26 major licensees.[34] These activities yield taxable gaming win, with Nevada imposing a 6.75% rate on gross revenue from slots and a graduated scale up to 6.75% for tables, funding state education, infrastructure, and public services.[35] Overall, New York-New York bolsters Las Vegas's tourism-dependent economy, which relies on gaming and hospitality for approximately 330,000 jobs and $59.6 billion in annual impact as of 2023, with the Strip's themed resorts like this one enhancing draw for domestic and international visitors despite periodic downturns in international travel.[36] Its role exemplifies how individual properties sustain multiplier effects, including indirect jobs in supply chains and secondary spending, though economic contributions vary with broader market conditions like reduced group travel post-pandemic.[37]

Architecture and Design

Exterior Replicas and Scale

The exterior design of the New York-New York Hotel and Casino features scaled replicas of prominent New York City landmarks, creating a condensed representation of the city's skyline and streetscape along the Las Vegas Strip. These elements include a 150-foot (46 m) tall replica of the Statue of Liberty, positioned at the corner of Las Vegas Boulevard and Tropicana Avenue, constructed at approximately half the scale of the original monument's total height from pedestal base to torch.[38][39] The replica captures the statue's form without the full pedestal height of the original 305-foot (93 m) structure, emphasizing visual impact within the constrained space of the resort property.[40] Adjacent to the Statue of Liberty replica stands a 300-foot-long (91 m) pedestrian replica of the Brooklyn Bridge, spanning a lagoon and facilitating guest access to the property's entrance. This bridge model reduces the original's 1,595-foot (486 m) main span to about one-fifth scale, prioritizing thematic authenticity over proportional fidelity to accommodate the site's layout and integrate with surrounding water features.[41][42] The hotel's twin towers replicate New York skyscrapers at roughly one-third scale, with the taller structure mimicking the Empire State Building at 529 feet (161 m) in height and 47 stories, compared to the original's 1,250 feet (381 m) to roof and 102 stories. This scaling allows the facades of twelve stylized skyscrapers to form a mock Manhattan skyline along the property's lower levels, evoking the density of Midtown while fitting the 84-acre (34 ha) development footprint. Additional replicas, such as the Soldiers and Sailors Monument, enhance the base-level streetscape, blending architectural homage with functional casino access.[43][9][44]

Interior Layout and Theming

The interior of the New York-New York Hotel and Casino replicates elements of New York City neighborhoods, particularly evoking the West Village through cobblestone pathways, brownstone facades, and storefronts designed to mimic urban streetscapes.[45] The layout centers on an 84,000-square-foot casino floor, surrounded by themed corridors that simulate city blocks, incorporating faux landmarks such as a replica Christopher Street Station and a Fulton Fish Market-inspired food stall.[1] [45] A prominent Times Square Panasonic sign adorns Bleecker Street within the complex, enhancing the immersive urban atmosphere.[45] Public areas feature integrated New York-inspired elements, including a bar styled after the Union Square subway station adjacent to the casino's gaming tables for craps, roulette, and slots.[45] The entrance lobby draws from 30 Rockefeller Center's design, leading to escalators and pathways connecting to dining venues like Gallagher's Steakhouse with dark wood interiors reminiscent of its Manhattan counterpart.[1] [45] Additional theming includes a Times Square-inspired bar with dueling pianos and a Brooklyn Bridge setting for live music, blending entertainment with the overall cityscape motif.[1] Hotel towers, such as the New Yorker Tower, incorporate Art Deco elevators and room numbering styled like subway lines, maintaining the thematic consistency into guest accommodations remodeled in a contemporary Soho loft aesthetic with bold colors and raw materials.[1] [45] Unique fixtures, like a fountain modeled after a New York City Fire Department fireboat, underscore the resort's campy, over-the-top homage to the city's landmarks and infrastructure.[45] This design approach creates a compact, navigable interior that prioritizes experiential immersion over expansive scale.[46]

Critical Reception of Design

Upon opening in January 1997, the New York-New York Hotel and Casino's design, featuring a compressed replica of the Manhattan skyline with structures like a 47-story Empire State Building facsimile and a 35-story Chrysler Building imitation at roughly one-third real scale, drew architectural commentary highlighting its theatrical spectacle over authenticity. Paul Goldberger of The New York Times deemed it "one of the remarkable moments in recent American architecture" for encapsulating Las Vegas's entertainment ethos through a "whole city, turned in a swoop into a theme park," yet criticized its superficiality as "theme-park entertainment masquerading as architecture" that packages New York City's image "as tightly and cleanly as an apple in plastic shrink-wrap."[47] The design aligns with postmodern Las Vegas precedents analyzed in Robert Venturi and Denise Scott Brown's Learning from Las Vegas, prioritizing symbolic form as a "duck"â€”where the building itself advertises its themeâ€”over a "decorated shed" with applied ornament, presenting a miniaturized skyline as overt signage for the casino's New York motif.[48] In recognition of its thematic execution, the property earned the Outstanding Achievement Award in Hotel & Casino Design from the Themed Entertainment Association in 1998, affirming its impact within entertainment architecture despite broader debates on the genre's divergence from conventional urban design principles.[49]

Facilities and Attractions

Casino Gaming Floor

The casino gaming floor at New York-New York Hotel and Casino occupies 84,000 square feet and provides a range of slot machines and table games themed to evoke the energetic atmosphere of New York City.[5] It features over 900 slot machines in various denominations, including popular titles such as Wheel of Fortune, Megabucks progressives, Blazing 7â€™s, Monopoly, and Lightning Link series with Hold & Spin jackpot mechanics.[50] These slots incorporate progressive jackpots, with recent payouts exceeding $8.2 million in September jackpots, including a $572,660 Wheel of Fortune win.[50] Table games number 53, encompassing blackjack, Free Bet Blackjack, craps (including crapless craps with enhanced payouts on points 2, 3, 11, and 12), roulette, baccarat, 3-Card Poker, Ultimate Texas Holdâ€™em, Let it Ride, I Luv Suits, Crazy 4 Poker, and Face Up Pai Gow Poker.[51] Betting limits range from $10 to $5,000 per hand, accommodating a broad spectrum of players.[51] The crapless craps variant is positioned in the main dice pit adjacent to the Center Bar for convenient access.[51] Additional facilities include a BetMGM Sportsbook for wagering on sports events.[5] The floor lacks a dedicated player-versus-player poker room, relying instead on house-banked poker derivatives within the table games pit.[51] Casino hosts assist with player services, including marker applications for credit play.[5]

Hotel Rooms and Suites

The New York-New York Hotel and Casino houses 2,024 guest rooms and suites across 45 floors, designed to evoke New York City apartments with contemporary loft aesthetics.[52] A $63 million renovation project, initiated in August 2022, updated 1,830 rooms and 155 suites, incorporating modern features such as pillow-top mattresses, granite bathrooms, smart TVs with streaming capabilities, reliable Wi-Fi, and digital room keys via the MGM app, with completion by summer 2023.[53][17][54] Standard rooms emphasize bold NYC styling in sizes starting at 350 square feet, including the Soho King (1 king bed, maximum 3 guests) and Soho Two Queen (2 queen beds, maximum 4 guests), available with or without Las Vegas Strip views; larger Soho Premier variants offer 450 square feet with premium comforts.[55] Wellness-focused Stay Well rooms, also 350 square feet, integrate air purification, vitamin C-infused showers, and motion-sensor lighting in king or two-queen configurations.[55] Suites provide elevated space and amenities, such as the Spa Suite (500 square feet, 1 king bed, maximum 3 guests) blending NYC flair with loft comforts, and the Marquis Suite or Stay Well Marquis Suite (700 square feet, 1 king bed, maximum 3 guests) featuring spacious seating and wellness tech.[55] The Players Suite spans 800 square feet with 2 queen beds (maximum 4 guests), while one-bedroom options like the Luxury Suite or Penthouse Suite measure 1,100 square feet (1 king bed, maximum 3 guests) for VIP-style living; the largest, the Two Bedroom Penthouse Suite, covers 2,600 square feet with 2 king beds (maximum 6 guests).[55] All accommodations prioritize functionality, with elements like movable work surfaces and executive desks to accommodate both leisure and business travelers post-renovation.[17]

Dining Options and Nightlife

The New York-New York Hotel and Casino features a range of dining establishments emphasizing New York-inspired cuisine, from casual food halls to upscale steakhouses.[56] The NYNY Food Hall provides quick-service options including New York-style pizza from New York Pizzeria, chicken wings from Wing Zone, and sandwiches from Capriotti's.[57] Village Street Eateries offers additional casual fare such as pizza from Sirrico's, deli sandwiches from Greenberg's Deli, and fish and chips from Fulton's Fish Frye.[58] Fine dining includes Il Fornaio, an Italian restaurant specializing in wood-fired pizzas, pastas, and regional dishes prepared with imported ingredients.[56] Gallagher's Steakhouse serves dry-aged steaks, seafood, and classic sides in a traditional setting modeled after its New York counterpart.[59] Nine Fine Irishmen provides pub grub like shepherd's pie and fish and chips alongside imported beers in an authentic Irish pub atmosphere.[56] Other venues encompass America, offering regional American dishes with a 90-by-20-foot map of the United States; Gonzalez y Gonzalez for Mexican cuisine; Chin Chin for Asian fare; and Tom's Watch Bar for sports viewing with bar food.[60][59][61] Nightlife centers on themed bars and lounges with live entertainment rather than large-scale nightclubs. Coyote Ugly Saloon hosts bartenders dancing on the bar, live DJs, and drink specials in a high-energy environment inspired by the film.[62] The Bar at Times Square evokes New York City's vibrancy with cocktails and pub atmosphere.[63] Additional options include the Center Bar for casino-floor drinks and buyout events accommodating up to 200 guests; Midtown Bar and The Chocolate Bar for casual libations; and the Lobby Bar with gaming specials.[64][65] Nine Fine Irishmen doubles as a nightlife spot with live music performances.[56]

Big Apple Coaster and Rides

The Big Apple Coaster is a steel hypercoaster at the New York-New York Hotel and Casino on the Las Vegas Strip, featuring a track that weaves through the property's New York City-themed skyline and offers views of the surrounding Strip.[6] Opened on January 3, 1997, coinciding with the hotel's debut, the ride was originally named Manhattan Express and cost $25 million to construct.[66] Manufactured by the Japanese firm TOGO, it stands as the sole operating TOGO roller coaster in North America.[67] The coaster includes a 203-foot lift hill, a 144-foot drop, reaches speeds up to 67 mph, incorporates two inversionsâ€”a 180-degree heartline roll and a dive loopâ€”and has a ride duration of 2 minutes and 40 seconds.[68] It was the first roller coaster worldwide to feature the heartline twist and dive maneuver combination.[6] Riders must meet a minimum height of 54 inches without footwear, with no specified age restriction, and the attraction operates daily from 11:00 a.m. to midnight, subject to weather and maintenance.[6] Single-day ride tickets cost $25, with re-ride options at $10 and discounted rates for locals, military, and first responders; premium passes include priority access and merchandise.[6] The coaster accommodates weddings on its platform and attracts over 1.4 million riders annually.[6] In 2004, magnetic brakes were installed to improve stopping precision.[66] On December 2020, during testing of new Premier Rides train cars on the lift hill, the middle car derailed, damaging the structure but resulting in no injuries; testing halted pending repairs.[21] The ride's TOGO trains have been noted for roughness attributable to the manufacturer's wheel configuration, though this has not led to closures beyond routine maintenance.[69] Adjacent to the coaster entrance, the Big Apple Arcade provides family-oriented entertainment with modern video games alongside classics such as Skee-Ball, air hockey, and NBA Fever.[6] No other powered rides beyond the coaster are featured in this attraction area, emphasizing arcade gaming as the complementary experience.[6]

Entertainment and Shows

Resident Performances

Zumanity, a Cirque du Soleil production billed as an adults-only (18+) exploration of sensuality through acrobatics, aerial feats, and cabaret elements, debuted at the New York-New York Hotel and Casino on September 17, 2003, and ran for over 7,700 performances until its final show on March 14, 2020.[70][71] The show, which featured topless performers and themes of human desire, was staged in a 850-seat theater within the resort and was marketed as a departure from Cirque's family-oriented spectacles, drawing mixed reviews for its provocative content amid Las Vegas's evolving entertainment landscape.[72] Cirque du Soleil announced its permanent closure on November 16, 2020, citing the impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic and the company's bankruptcy proceedings, though it had already been shuttered since March.[72][71] In the same theater, Mad Apple by Cirque du Soleil premiered on May 26, 2022, as a high-energy variety show evoking New York City's nightlife with acrobatics, tap dance, comedy sketches, live music, and aerial acts performed by a cast of 21 artists.[73][74] The production, which runs Tuesday through Saturday at 7:00 p.m. and 9:30 p.m., incorporates elements like trampoline routines, contortion, and audience interaction, seating up to 850 guests and emphasizing urban grit over sensuality.[75] As of 2025, Mad Apple remains the resort's flagship resident show, with tickets starting around $32 and performances continuing without interruption post-premiere.[76] Earlier, comedian Rita Rudner held a residency in the Cabaret Theatre starting April 2001, delivering stand-up routines focused on observational humor about marriage and everyday life, which lasted until approximately 2006.[77] This engagement positioned her as a draw for the newly opened property, though it predated the Cirque dominance and drew smaller crowds compared to later acrobatic spectacles. No other long-term music or comedy residencies have been prominently featured at the venue, with entertainment shifting toward Cirque's theatrical formats.[76]

Special Events and Programming

The Brooklyn Bridge promenade at New York-New York functions as an open-air venue for recurring live music programming, offering free performances by various bands across genres such as rock, 1980s hits, and cover sets, primarily on weekends from evening hours.[78][79] These events create an energetic atmosphere with Strip views, drawing crowds for casual listening amid the property's New York-themed exterior.[78] Specific lineups have included acts like Stereo A Band delivering high-energy sets on Saturdays.[80] Adjacent to this area, the Nine Fine Irishmen pub hosts traditional Irish music sessions by its house band every Friday through Sunday from 4:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m., emphasizing folk tunes and Celtic instrumentation as a staple of the venue's programming.[81] The Bar at Times Square provides interactive dueling pianos entertainment, where performers take audience song requests and encourage sing-alongs in a high-volume, participatory format suited to nightlife crowds.[63][1] Special events occasionally augment this schedule, with adjustments to pricing, hours, and access during holidays or private bookings, though specific one-off concerts or festivals are less frequently documented compared to resident productions.[63] The property's event spaces support conferences and gatherings, but public programming prioritizes accessible, no-cover music series over large-scale ticketed spectacles.[82] Historical concert records indicate sporadic bookings of touring acts in the theater, separate from ongoing programming.[83]

Controversies and Legal Matters

Design-Related Lawsuits

In 1997, shortly after the New York-New York Hotel and Casino opened on January 3, the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) filed a trademark infringement lawsuit against the property's owners, Primadonna Resorts Inc. and MGM Grand Inc., in U.S. District Court in New York.[84] The suit centered on the resort's one-third-scale replica of the NYSE's neoclassical facade in its interior "financial district," which initially bore the inscription "NEW YORK NEW YORK STOCK EXCHANGE" and incorporated stylized elements resembling the NYSE's registered trademark of its columned building front.[85] The NYSE alleged violations of the Lanham Act, including trademark infringement, unfair competition, and dilution, claiming the design caused public confusion, diluted its mark, and ridiculed its dignified image by associating it with gambling.[86] The complaint sought demolition of the facade, removal of all NYSE references, disgorgement of profits, and punitive damages.[84] The district court granted summary judgment in favor of New York-New York in 1999, ruling there was no likelihood of consumer confusion given the resort's obvious parody context, Las Vegas theme-park setting, and geographic separation from the actual NYSE.[87] The U.S. Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit affirmed the dismissal in April 2002, holding that the modified replica and signageâ€”such as the renamed "New York-New York $lot Exchange" slot clubâ€”did not suggest affiliation or endorsement, and any nominative fair use was protected absent bad faith intent to confuse.[85] The court emphasized that the design's humorous, non-commercial mimicry in a entertainment venue distinguished it from deceptive uses, rejecting dilution claims due to lack of evidence of tarnishment.[85] Separately, in December 1998, architectural firm Domingo Cambeiro Professional Corp. filed its third lawsuit against casino designer Mark Advent and Tri-M Holdings Inc., alleging misappropriation of trade secrets and breach of a 1993 confidentiality agreement in the development of New York-New York's New York City-themed design.[88] Cambeiro claimed Advent improperly shared and modified its conceptual plansâ€”originally commissioned by Americon Companies for a replica-laden New York mall including the Empire State Building and Statue of Libertyâ€”for submission to Primadonna Resorts without authorization, influencing the resort's skyline replicas and overall aesthetic.[88] Advent denied the allegations, describing the suits as baseless harassment following prior dismissals, including a failed copyright claim; no final resolution or award against the resort's operators was reported, as the action targeted the designer rather than the property directly.[88]

Security Incidents Including 2007 Shooting

On July 6, 2007, Steven Zegrean, a 51-year-old unemployed painter estranged from his family, fired multiple rounds from a .223-caliber rifle positioned on a second-floor balcony overlooking the New York-New York casino floor, wounding four gamblers below.[19][89] The victims, ranging in age from 23 to 55, sustained non-life-threatening injuries including gunshot wounds to the legs and arms; Zegrean was promptly tackled and subdued by casino security guards after firing approximately 20 rounds.[90][91] Authorities described Zegrean as "distressed" with no apparent motive beyond personal turmoil, and he faced 52 charges including four counts of attempted murder.[19] In July 2009, a jury convicted him on 51 counts, leading to a sentence of 26 to 90 years in prison; Zegrean died of a heart attack while incarcerated.[20][91][92] The incident highlighted vulnerabilities in casino surveillance and response protocols, as Zegrean had entered the property armed without prior detection, though rapid intervention by guards prevented further casualties.[89] No fatalities occurred, but the event prompted temporary evacuations and reinforced Las Vegas Strip security measures, including enhanced patrols in elevated areas.[19] Subsequent security breaches at the property have included multiple armed robberies at the cashier cage. On January 10, 2018, a suspect robbed the cage at gunpoint, fleeing with an undisclosed amount before arrest.[93] In March 2018, another felon, previously convicted of violent crimes, held up the same location with a handgun, demanding cash under threat of violence.[94] A June 2020 robbery involved a perpetrator simulating a firearm with his hand concealed in a pocket, coercing a cashier to surrender over $4,700.[95] These incidents underscore persistent risks in high-traffic financial areas, with perpetrators exploiting brief lapses in oversight despite on-site security presence.[96] In a 2011 civil case, Humphries v. New York-New York Hotel & Casino, plaintiff Michael Humphries alleged negligent security after being assaulted by another patron on the casino floor, citing prior unreported violent episodes at the property as evidence of inadequate staffing and response times.[97] The Nevada Supreme Court in 2017 ruled that evidence of previous assaults could support claims of foreseeable risks but affirmed dismissal on proximate cause grounds, noting the guard's quick arrival despite delays in broader intervention.[98] Such cases reflect ongoing debates over casino liability for patron violence, though criminal incidents remain isolated relative to visitor volume.[97]

Other Disputes and Public Backlash

In 2001, the U.S. Department of Justice initiated an investigation into the New York-New York Hotel and Casino for violations of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), prompting a comprehensive settlement agreement that required the property to remediate barriers in areas including parking facilities, entrances, elevators, guest rooms, swimming pools, and public restrooms.[99] The agreement mandated structural alterations, such as installing accessible routes and adaptive features in over 2,000 guest rooms, along with staff training on assisting disabled patrons and policies to prevent discrimination, reflecting empirical evidence of widespread inaccessibility at the time of the DOJ's compliance review.[100] The hotel has encountered multiple disputes with the National Labor Relations Board (NLRB) over union access and employee rights, stemming from tensions with the Culinary Workers Union Local 226 and affiliates. In a 2001 case, the NLRB ruled that the hotel unlawfully restricted union handbilling on its premises, enforcing access rights for off-duty employees of contractors operating restaurants within the facility.[101] A 2011 NLRB decision further held that denying off-duty contractor employees entry for protected union solicitation violated the National Labor Relations Act, as the hotel's property rules did not sufficiently justify the exclusion given the employees' prior work presence and lack of disruption.[102] These rulings arose from causal disputes over balancing property control with federal labor protections, with the hotel petitioning courts to challenge NLRB jurisdiction but ultimately facing enforcement of access obligations.[103] More recently, in May 2025, a federal district court determined that New York-New York violated the NLRA by prohibiting pro-union handbilling activities on a public sidewalk adjacent to the property, preserving organizers' rights despite the hotel's security concerns.[104] Such incidents highlight recurring friction in Las Vegas casino operations, where union campaigns have targeted MGM Resorts properties, including allegations of anti-union tactics leading to NLRB complaints, though outcomes depend on evidence of interference with concerted activities.[105] The property has also been implicated in civil lawsuits under the Trafficking Victims Protection Reauthorization Act (TVPRA), where plaintiffs allege that hotel staff overlooked signs of sex trafficking, such as repeated room bookings under suspicious circumstances and failure to report to authorities.[106] For example, one 2023 filing claimed exploitation occurred at New York-New York among other Las Vegas venues, asserting negligence in monitoring high-risk indicators like cash payments and frequent male visitors, part of a broader wave of over 100 similar suits against Nevada hotels since 2018 amendments enabling third-party liability claims.[106] These cases, often filed by victims represented by firms specializing in TVPRA litigation, have prompted defenses centered on lack of actual knowledge and compliance with reporting protocols, with settlements in some MGM-related matters but no admissions of systemic failure.[106] Public scrutiny has been limited, focusing more on industry-wide vulnerabilities than property-specific backlash, though the suits underscore causal risks from high-volume transient occupancy in casino environments.
