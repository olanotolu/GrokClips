Dino Prižmić

Dino Prižmić (born 5 August 2005) is a Croatian professional tennis player. Prižmić has a career-high ATP singles ranking of world No. 115, achieved on 22 September 2025. He is currently the No. 3 player from Croatia. In 2025, he won back-to-back ATP Challenger titles in Zagreb and Bratislava. Prižmić won the boys' singles title at the 2023 French Open.

Career

Juniors

Prižmić reached two finals in the European Junior Championships, the first in 2019 in the under 14s and then in 2022 in the under 18s, but lost both of them. During his career on the ITF Junior Circuit, Prižmić achieved a career-high ranking of No. 8 on 2 January 2023. His best junior result was winning the boys' singles title at the 2023 French Open, where he defeated Juan Carlos Prado Ángelo in the final.

2022: ATP debut

In April 2022, Prižmić reached his first ITF final at a clay-court tournament in Dubrovnik, Croatia, where he lost to Gergely Madarász in straight sets.[1] Prižmić made his ATP debut in the singles draw of the 2022 Croatia Open Umag as a wildcard where he lost to Bernabé Zapata Miralles in the first round by retirement.

2023: First ATP win and Challenger title, Davis Cup, top 155 debuts

With his fifth ITF title in Poreč, Croatia, Prižmić became the best under-18 player in the world at No. 381 on 20 March 2023. In April, Prižmić then qualified for the Banja Luka Open in Bosnia and Herzegovina, but fell to Richard Gasquet in the first round. A few months later, he received a wildcard into the Croatia Open and defeated Duje Ajduković in the first round to record his first ATP victory. He then defeated Zsombor Piros to reach his maiden ATP quarterfinal, losing to eventual champion Alexei Popyrin. In August, Prižmić competed at the Banja Luka Challenger and won his maiden Challenger title, defeating Kimmer Coppejans in the final.[2] Shortly after he was appointed by coach Vedran Martić to the Croatia Davis Cup team, alongside Borna Ćorić, Borna Gojo, Ivan Dodig and Mate Pavić. He made his Davis Cup debut in September, losing to Mackenzie McDonald in the group stage against the United States.[3] In October, Prižmić qualified for the main draw of the Stockholm Open where he defeated sixth seed and world No. 30 Jiří Lehečka in the first round, his first-ever win over a Top 50 player. He reached a career-high singles ranking of No. 155 on 23 October 2023.[4]

2024-2025: Grand Slam and Top 150 debuts

At the Australian Open, Prižmić defeated Mariano Navone, Duje Ajduković and Aziz Dougaz to qualify for his first Grand Slam. In his Grand Slam main-draw debut, Prižmić faced defending champion and world No. 1 Novak Djokovic, winning 16 games and saving 6 match points before losing in four sets in a four-hour match; it was the longest first-round match in Djokovic's Grand Slam career. Prižmić attempted to qualify for the 2024 French Open but lost in the first qualifying round to Stefano Napolitano.[5] At Wimbledon, he reached the second qualifying round, defeating Clément Tabur before losing to Hugo Gaston.[6] He received a special main draw entry at the 2024 BMW Open in Munich under the NextGen programme for players under 20 ranked in the top 250 but later withdrew. In 2025, Prižmić won back-to-back Challenger titles at the 2025 Zagreb Open, defeating Luca Van Assche in the final, and the 2025 Bratislava Open, defeating Valentin Royer in the final.[7] These victories propelled him back into the top 175 at No. 175 on 16 June 2025, a rise of 77 spots. He became just the second Croatian, after Mario Ančić, to win three Challenger titles as a teenager (including his 2023 Banja Luka win). Following these successes, Prižmić reached the finals of the Milan, San Marino, and Grodzisk Mazowiecki Challengers in June and July, losing to Marco Cecchinato, Lukas Klein, and Kamil Majchrzak respectively.[8] He made his ATP main draw return at the Umag Open in July, reaching the quarterfinals with wins over Juan Manuel Cerúndolo and Hamad Medjedovic before losing to Luciano Darderi.[9] At the US Open, Prižmić qualified for the main draw by defeating Alexis Galarneau, Murphy Cassone, and Jason Kubler, then lost in the first round to Andrey Rublev.[10] In September, he represented Croatia in the Davis Cup, winning against Jozef Kovalík in January qualifiers but losing to Corentin Moutet in the September qualifiers against France. Later that month, he reached the round of 16 at the Chengdu Open, defeating Terence Atmane before falling to Lorenzo Musetti.[11] Prižmić entered the top 150 in July 2025 following his San Marino final and peaked at a career-high No. 124 on 18 August 2025.[4] In October, he retired in the first round of Stockholm qualifying due to injury.[8]
