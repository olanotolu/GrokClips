M. J. Walker

Stats at NBA.com
Stats at Basketball Reference James Michael "M. J." Walker Jr. (born March 28, 1998) is an American professional basketball player for the College Park Skyhawks of the NBA G League. He played college basketball for Florida State Seminoles.[1]

High school career

Walker attended Jonesboro High School in Jonesboro, Georgia. He initially played football but decided to focus solely on basketball after his sophomore year. As a freshman in 2013–14, Walker helped lead Jonesboro to a 30–3 record and their first GHSA Class AAAA state championship. As a sophomore in 2014–15, Walker averaged 17.3 points, 4.5 rebounds, 3.0 assists, and 3.0 steals leading Jonesboro to a 32–1 record and the 2015 Georgia Class 4A state championship. As a junior in 2015–16, Walker averaged 22.3 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 3.6 assists leading Jonesboro to a 28–5 record, regional title, and the 2016 Class 4A state championship game, where they finished as runners-up. As a senior in 2016–17, Walker averaged 27.8 points, 6.5 rebounds, and 2.4 assists leading Jonesboro to a 23–6 record.

College career

As a freshman in 2017–18, Walker averaged 7.0 points, 1.7 rebounds, and 1.1 assists per game. Florida State finished 23–12 overall and reached the Elite Eight of the NCAA Tournament.[3] As a sophomore, Walker averaged 7.5 points and 2.2 rebounds per game. Florida State compiled a 29–8 record and reached the Sweet Sixteen of the NCAA Tournament.[4] As a junior in 2019–20, Walker averaged 10.6 points, 1.7 rebounds, and 1.5 assists per game. Florida State finished 26–5 overall, winning the ACC regular season championship before the season was suspended due to the COVID-19 pandemic. At the conclusion of his junior season, Walker was named All-ACC Honorable Mention.[5] As a senior, he averaged 12.2 points, 2.5 rebounds, and 2.5 assists per game, earning Second Team All-ACC honors. Following the season, he declared for the 2021 NBA draft, forgoing the additional season of eligibility granted by the NCAA due to the COVID-19 pandemic.[6]

Professional career

Westchester Knicks (2021)

After going undrafted in the 2021 NBA draft, Walker signed with the New York Knicks on August 20, 2021,[7] but was waived on October 16.[8] In October 2021, he joined the Westchester Knicks as an affiliate player.[9] Walker averaged 10.7 points, 2.6 rebounds and 2.3 assists per game.

Phoenix Suns (2021–2022)

He signed a 10-day COVID-19 hardship exemption contract with the Phoenix Suns on December 30, 2021.[10] He appeared briefly in two games for the Suns that season.

Return to Westchester (2022–2023)

On January 9, 2022, Walker was reacquired by the Westchester Knicks. In 14 games during the 2022-23 season, Walker averaged 9.3 points, 3.4 rebounds, and 2.1 assists per game. On September 22, 2022, Walker re-signed with the New York Knicks on a training camp deal but was waived a day later.[11] On October 24, 2022, Walker was named to the training camp roster for the Knicks.[12]

Vancouver Bandits (2023)

On June 6, 2023, Walker signed with the Vancouver Bandits of the Canadian Elite Basketball League.[13] However, he was released on July 8 after averaging 11.2 points through 9 games for the club.[14]

Brampton Honey Badgers (2023)

On July 29, 2023, Walker signed with the Brampton Honey Badgers ahead of the postseason.[15]

Saint-Quentin (2023–2024)

On August 3, 2023, Walker signed with Saint-Quentin of the LNB Pro A.[16]

Greensboro Swarm (2024)

On January 19, 2024, Walker joined the Greensboro Swarm.[17]

Canterbury Rams (2024)

On March 12, 2024, Walker signed with the Canterbury Rams of the New Zealand National Basketball League (NZNBL) for the 2024 season.[18] With the Rams, he won an NZNBL championship.

Greensboro Swarm (2024–2025)

On October 27, 2024, Walker rejoined the Greensboro Swarm,[19] but was waived on November 13, after one game.[20] However, he was re-signed six days later.[21] In the 2024-25 G League season, he appeared in 45 games for Greensboro, averaging 8.8 points, 2.0 rebounds, and 1.5 assists per game.[22] In October 2025, Walker signed an Exhibit 10 contract with the Atlanta Hawks but was waived on October 14, 2025.[23] As of October 2025, he is a free agent. Walker played for the United States national under-18 basketball team at the 2016 FIBA Americas Under-18 Championship in Valdivia, Chile.[24] In four games, he averaged 2.0 points, 1.8 rebounds, and 1.0 assist per game, helping his team win the gold medal.[24][25]

Personal Life of Walker

Personal life

Walker is the son of James and Jackie Walker, and has an older sister, Amoni, who played for Miles College (2015–2019). His father, James, played college basketball at Norfolk State University and his mother, Jackie, played women's college basketball for Hampton University.[27]
