Hahm Eun-jung

In this Korean name, the family name is Hahm. Hahm Eun-jung (Korean: 함은정; born December 12, 1988),[4][5] known professionally as Eunjung and also Elsie ,[5] is a South Korean singer, actress, model, host, entertainer, and lyricist. She began her career at the age of seven as an entertainer in 1995, when she won the Little Miss Korea competition and debuted as a child actress in the same year in a television drama A New Generation of Adults (1995). Since then, she has taken on several roles in movies, television series and also being cast in various commercial films. After three years of training, she debuted as a member of South Korean girl group T-ara in July 2009 (and later its subgroup T-ara N4). She has won the Child Actor Award for her role in "Drama of The Year" multi-winner Land at the 2005 SBS Drama Awards. She was also nominated and won awards for the romantic comedy _Coffee House_ (2010), the musical drama Dream High (2011), reality show We Got Married (2011–12), family drama Be My Dream Family (2021), and daily soap opera Love Twist (2021–22), as well as recent dramas such as Su Ji and U Ri (2024) and Queen's House (2025), earning the Excellence Award at the 2024 KBS Drama Awards.[6][7][8][9] She debuted as a solo artist by the name Elsie with her EP, I'm Good , on May 7, 2015, which went on to win her the Korean Artist award at the 4th V Chart Awards.[10] Hahm's career extends beyond music and cinema—she has forayed into modeling, hosting, and TV.[11][12] She has hosted numerous events, including the Dream Concert, Grand Bell Awards, and Jecheon International Music & Film Festival. In 2012, she hosted an episode of Saturday Night Live Korea along with S.E.S.'s Bada and Horan, which drew a rating of 1.444%.[13]

Early life and education

Hahm was born in Sanggye-dong, Nowon District, Seoul, South Korea.[2] Her mother, a graduate of Ewha Womans University, was a former piano teacher who quit teaching to manage Hahm's career when she began working as a child entertainer in 1995.[14] While attending seventh grade, Hahm started Taekwondo lessons, achieved a 3rd dan black belt,[15] and won three different competitions. Hahm graduated high school in 2007 and later that year enrolled in Dongguk University where she majored in performance.[16] She is an only child. Hahm's late mother was a piano instructor who later became her agent, while her father owns a small raw fish restaurant in Gangwon Province.

Career

1995–2008: Career beginnings

In 1995, when Hahm was seven years old, she participated in the "Little Miss Korea" pageant and won.[17] That same year, she appeared in the youth drama A New Generation of Adults, which aired on KBS.[17] Afterwards, she had several minor roles in movies and dramas, along with being cast in several commercial films. In 2007, Hahm made appearances in a number of music videos. Namely, eighteen-year-old Hahm is featured in SG Wannabe's music video "Gasiri" from Story In New York. Later that year, she would also be seen in F.T. Island's music videos: "Thunder"; "Only One Person", and "A Man's First Love Follow Him To The Grave". She also appeared in the 2008 horror film Death Bell, where she plays a student who was murdered by her teacher.[18] Eunjung self-taught herself Pansori, a traditional Korean singing style, by practicing with old tapes to qualify for her first major role in Land.

2009–2012: Rising popularity

Hahm originally trained to be an actress, and later debuted as a member of T-ara in July 2009. She was the first leader of the group, until September 2010.[19][20] Hahm featured in Davichi's music video "Time, Please Stop" which was released on May 6, 2010.[21] Her first major acting role since joining T-ara was in SBS drama Coffee House, which aired from May 17 to July 17, 2010.[22][23][24][25][26] That same year, she was cast in the horror film White: Melody of Death.[27][28] Hahm's popularity began to rise in 2011 when she starred in the teen drama Dream High.[8] The drama was a success and brought high domestic viewership ratings. It also gained popularity in other countries and won several international awards.[29] From 2011 to 2012, she joined MBC's variety program We Got Married, pairing up with actor Lee Jang-woo.[30] She won the Best Newcomer Award at the MBC Entertainment Awards.[31] Hahm was then cast in historical dramas The King of Legend (2010–2011) and Insu, The Queen Mother (2011–2012).[32][33] In June 2012, Hahm was confirmed to star in the drama Five Fingers. However, on August 22, it was revealed that she had been dropped from the drama. The Korean Actors Labor Union demanded that Hahm be issued an apology by the producer and crew. In February 2013, Yein E&M issued a formal apology for firing Hahm without notice.[34] In October 2013, MBK Entertainment revealed that Eun-jung participated in the arrangement of T-ara's upcoming track "I Know The Feeling" from their newest EP Again. Deoksang Park, the composer of the song proposed the collaboration.[35]

2014–2017: Return to acting and solo debut

Hahm made her return to acting in 2014, with SBS' weekend period drama Endless Love.#Cast) In 2015, it was announced that Hahm was offered the lead role in the Thai romantic comedy Micro Love, making her the first female idol to star in a Thai film.[36] In May, Hahm made her solo debut under the stage name Elsie. She released her first mini album I'm Good, with the lead single of the same name featuring K.Will. The Chinese version of her music video peaked at number two on YinYueTai's music video charts. On October 14, Hahm released the song Goodbye, as the soundtrack of T-ara's web-drama Sweet Temptation. The song was also included in her repackaged mini album, Goodbye, released on October 30. In 2016, Hahm was cast in Flower of Evil, a thriller film directed by Cho Sung-kyu, released in 2017.[37] In 2017, Hahm was cast in MBC's daily drama, Sisters-in-Law, which premiered in April that year.[38]

2018–present: New agency and solo career

In January 2018, Hahm confirmed her departure from MBK after her contract expired. In February, Hahm signed an exclusive management contract with UFO Production, making her the company's first-ever artist. UFO Production, a subsidiary of technology company Pobis TNC, is more known for producing the MBC TV weekend drama Money Flower.[39] She left UFO Production in 2020 and signed with Cabin 74. On May 24, it was confirmed that Hahm was cast in KBS's romantic comedy-drama Lovely Horribly, starring Park Si-hoo and Song Ji-hyo.[40] On February 26, 2019, it was announced that Hahm would be the narrator for the Korea-Vietnam co-production documentary named The Muse Story, which will be broadcast on KBS World.[41] On October 14, 2020, her label announced that Hahm will star in an upcoming film called "I will sing" as a singer.[42] Hahm was cast in the leading role of the TV drama Be My Dream Family, which aired on KBS1 every weekday from March 29, 2021,to October 1. The drama was well-received for its heartwarming portrayal of family dynamics.[43] On July 15, Hahm left Cabin 74 after her contract expired.[44] On September 27, Hahm signed a new exclusive contract with Management Koo.[45] In 2021, along with Supernova's Gonil, Eun-jung took the lead role in short original drama Cell Division which was released on DramaX. The drama was also sold in Japan to multiple broadcasters—this includes streaming services like Amazon's Prime Video,[46] Yahoo Japan's GYAO,[47] music.Jp[48] and Fuji TV's FOD.[49] It premiered on KNTV Japan on May 26.[50] On February 15, 2023, Hahm was confirmed to be part of the main cast of JTBC's variety program Differential Class for its new season which premieres on March 5. Differential Class is an informative variety show that first aired in 2017, achieving a peak rating of 5%. The show has also garnered over 500,000 followers and more than 200 million views on YouTube.[51] Later in the year, Eun-jung was cast in Kunio Shimizu's "Dressing Room" portraying the character "D". The play was well-received and its run was extended until May 28, 2023, for encore performances. According to FN News, the play received an Interpark rating of 9.8 and a high rate of repeat attendance, earning the nickname "acting power show" for its cast of professional actors.[52] On February 19, 2024, Hahm signed an exclusive contract with Mask Studio.[53] In September 2024, Eun-jung was appointed as a judge for the 4th Geumcheon Fashion Film Festival which will be held in Geumcheon District from September 6 to September 9.[54] Hahm starred in the revenge drama The Queen's House on KBS2, which aired from April 28 to September 19, 2025, and received positive reviews for her lead performance as Kang Jae-in.[55] In October 2025, Hahm was confirmed for dual lead roles in MBC's upcoming daily drama The First Man.[56]

Public image and impact

Acting

Hahm Eun-jung, who debuted as a child actress and model, has significantly diversified her career over the years. She has frequently been recognized as one of the best idol-actors throughout her three-decade acting career.[57] In 2011, NewsWire wrote that she "has proven herself to be the best acting idol of her time", while Cine21 hailed her the next-generation "horror queen" due to her standout roles in horror movies White: Melody of Death and Death Bell , —a genre rarely explored by idols.[57] That same year, a Sports Seoul survey ranked her as the third-best acting idol, as voted by 100 idols.[58] Her reputation as a skilled actress was further cemented in 2012 when she secured third place (tied) in a critics' survey conducted by Nate.[59] Alongside her T-ara groupmates Jiyeon and Hyomin, they are collectively referred to as "T-ara's 3 acting idols".[60] In September 2024, Eun-jung was appointed as a judge for the 4th Geumcheon Fashion Film Festival, held in Geumcheon District from September 6 to September 9.[54] Eun-jung has starred in numerous acclaimed projects, including Land (2004),[61][62] Coffee House (2010),[63] Dream High (2011),[64][65] Queen Insu (2011),[66] and more recently Love Twist (2021–2022),[67] and Suji& Uri (2024),[68] which have achieved strong ratings and received widespread praise both commercially and critically. Eun-jung's performances have earned her several prestigious nominations, including multiple nods for Excellence Awards at the MBC Drama Awards[69] and KBS Drama Awards,[70] as well as a nomination for the Top Excellence Award at the APAN Star Awards.[71] These are among the highest honors in South Korea's acting industry. In 2024, her role in Suji & Uri earned her the Excellence Award at the KBS Drama Awards.[9]

Cultural impact

Eun-jung rose to prominence not only through her acting but also through her widespread popularity on Cyworld pages, an early social media platform, where T-ara members frequently ranked in the top visitor positions with hundreds of thousands of weekly views.[72] Hahm's successful musical and acting career secured her numerous brand endorsements across South Korea.[11][12][73] In addition to her commercial success, Eun-jung served as an ambassador for various organizations in South Korea and abroad.[74][75][76] Her international representation includes attending the Korea-Brunei 30th Year Relations Ceremony in 2014.[77] In 2022, Hahm was awarded the Korea-China Culture Exchange Award Grand Prize at the World Creators Awards.[78] She maintains a channel on Bilibili, one of the major Chinese streaming platforms, which reached 100,000 subscribers in 2021. In October 2025, Hahm announced her marriage to film director Kim Byung-woo, scheduled for November 30, 2025, which received widespread congratulations from fans and media.[79]

Other ventures

Endorsements and promotions

Hahm has modeled for several brands since before her debut as a singer. Her first-ever advertisement was a print ad for Korea's No.1 mineral water, Jeju Samdasoo, in 1998.[73] In February 2011, she was selected as an exclusive model for the clothing brand SPRIS, alongside actor Kim Soo-hyun. Known as one of the most popular fashion brands among young people, SPRIS previously featured notable figures such as Lee Joon-gi, Kim Beom, and 2PM. The campaign included multiple TV commercials and a 3-minute music-drama video featuring a newly recorded song by Hahm and Kim, titled "Crazy4s". However, the track was never officially released.[11] She also endorsed tonimori alongside Song Joong-ki in 2012 and KDB Daewoo Securities in 2012, as well as Look Optical with 2PM.[80] In 2015, Hahm became the face of Burger King in Korea, following fellow T-ara members Hyomin and Jiyeon, who had endorsed the brand the previous year. Her promotional posters were displayed nationwide.[80] The following year, she was chosen as a model for Coca-Cola alongside Yubin and Park Yoon. From 2015 to 2017, she actively participated in various promotional events for the brand, including its 130th-anniversary exhibition.[12][81]

Ambassadorships

Hahm has been appointed as an ambassador for numerous institutions and events across various countries since her debut in 1995. In 2011, she was appointed as the public relations and goodwill ambassador for the National Police Agency, receiving an ambassador award from Police Commissioner Cho Hyun-oh at the National Warfare Guard Festival on March 8.[74] That same year, she became an ambassador for ETN's audition show Global Super Idol in Thailand, where she also served as a mentor and judge.[82] In 2014, she was selected as a PR ambassador for the Korea-Brunei cosmetic joint venture.[77] In 2016, she was appointed as a public relations ambassador for Dongguk University, receiving her appointment certification from the university's president on October 17.[76] In 2017, as the public relations ambassador for the 9th Gangneung Coffee Festival, she attended the opening ceremony and received the talent ambassador plaque from the mayor of Gangneung.[83] She has also supported cultural initiatives, acting as an ambassador for the Chunsa Film Festival in 2020.[80] In 2025, she served as MC for the Korean Hanbok Model Selection Contest.[84]

Smile (book)

In 2016, Eunjung published her first pocket-book, Smile , inspired by her experiences while filming the reality show What Should I Do? in Thailand. In the book, she shares her thoughts and emotions from her time traveling and working in the country. The title reflects her impression of the Thai people's constant warmth and smiles.[85] The book was a limited release and published in three languages—English, Korean, and Thai. Beyond writing, Hahm was also involved in the creative process, designing the cover and accompanying merchandise.[86]

Philanthropy and activism

Eunjung is an advocate for pet protection. In July 2016, Eunjung participated in the 'Protect Us' campaign. Hosted by premium pet magazine Life & Dogue and Thank You Studio, It aimed to revise the animal protection act in South Korea and prevent animal abuse.[87] A year later, she participated in G9's abandoned animal campaign.[88] The singer donated several fashion items through TV Report. All donations collected were donated to the animal protection civic group 'KARA' and used for abandoned animal rescue.[89] In 2016, Eunjung traveled to Thailand to volunteer to distribute food at Sanam Luang.[90] In 2018, to celebrate her 30th birthday, Hahm held a charity event in Vietnam during which she met up with her fans, visited a nursing home and provided meals to 300 homeless people. She also gave gifts to 150 of her fans including bottles, photos, key chains and slogans that she made herself. She explained that her Vietnamese fans celebrated her last birthday which warmed her heart and inspired her to organize the event.[91] In August 2024, Hahm participated in "Daehan is Alive" campaign to celebrate the National Liberation Day of Korea. As a holder of a level 2 certificate in Korean history, she released a multi-lingual video introducing independence activist and poet Lee Yuk-sa in collaboration with Professor Seo Kyung-deok on August 14.[92]

Personal life

In 2010, Eunjung revealed that she holds a 3rd-degree black belt in Taekwondo.[15] She has showcased her martial arts skills on various live television programs.[15] Her expertise in Taekwondo proved beneficial in her acting career, particularly in the historical drama King Geunchogo (2010–2011), which required action sequences such as swordsmanship.[71] In addition to Taekwondo, Eunjung trained in ballet as a child.[93] Eunjung's mother, Lee Eun-joo, who had graduated from Ewha Womans University with a degree in music and quit her career to support Eunjung's, passed away on November 25, 2024. The funeral was held at Asan Medical Center in Seoul on November 29, 2024.[86] On October 16, 2025, Eunjung announced her marriage to film director Kim Byung-woo, known for The Divine Fury (2019) and Omniscient Reader: The Prophecy (upcoming). The private ceremony is scheduled for November 30, 2025, in Seoul. She cited her mother's recent passing and turning 40 as factors influencing her decision to prioritize family.[74]
