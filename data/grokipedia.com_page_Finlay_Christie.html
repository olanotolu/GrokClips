Finlay Christie

Finlay Turner Christie

Finlay Turner Christie (born 19 September 1995) is a Scottish-born New Zealand rugby union player who plays as a halfback for Tasman in the Bunnings NPC, where he captains the team, and the Blues in Super Rugby. Born in Scotland, he represents New Zealand at international level after qualifying on residency grounds.

Early life

Christie was born in Peebles in Scotland, where he grew up before his family moved to Aberdeen. They emigrated to New Zealand when he was aged 7, settling in the city of Pukekohe. He represented New Zealand in gymnastics and excelled in rugby during his schooldays. Christie attended Saint Kentigern College where he played for the college's top side. He represented Counties Manukau at under 19 level before heading south to Christchurch to study a Bachelor of Commerce degree at the University of Canterbury. In 2016 he was awarded the Hawkins Medal as Canterbury's top club rugby player for his performances for the University of Canterbury.[1]

Club career

Provincial

Christie's performances at club level saw him sign with Mitre 10 Cup side Tasman for the 2016 Mitre 10 Cup season. Christie made his debut in Round 1 against Waikato at Lansdowne Park in Blenheim scoring a try. He was part of the Tasman side that won the Mitre 10 Cup for the first time in 2019. In Round 9 of the 2020 Mitre 10 Cup Christie played his 50th game for the Mako against Canterbury at Lansdowne Park in Blenheim. The Mako went on to win their second premiership title in a row with Christie playing every minute of the 12â€“13 victory over Auckland in the final at Eden Park. Tasman won their first Ranfurly Shield in September 2024 by defeating Wellington 28â€“15, with Christie contributing significantly through turnovers and defense in subsequent defenses, including a 31â€“17 win over Auckland in October 2024. They lost the shield to Taranaki 29â€“42 later that month.[2][3][4] In July 2025, Christie was named captain for his 10th season with Tasman in the Bunnings NPC.[5]

Super Rugby

Following his impressive first season at provincial level, he signed with Super Rugby side the Chiefs ahead of the 2017 Super Rugby season. At the end of the 2017 season Christie signed with the Hurricanes for the 2018 Super Rugby season. In 2018 Christie made 6 appearances for the Hurricanes scoring a try on debut against the Sunwolves in Wellington. He played a further 8 matches for the Hurricanes in the 2019 Super Rugby season before moving to the Blues for the 2020 Super Rugby season. He was out injured before the season was cancelled, but then played in the Super Rugby Aotearoa competition making his Blues debut against his old team the Hurricanes at Eden Park.[6] Christie was named in the South Island squad for the North vs South rugby union match in 2020 coming off the bench in a 38â€“35 win for the South. Christie had an outstanding 2021 Super Rugby season particularly during the Super Rugby Trans-Tasman competition where he started all of the Blues 6 games as they won the competition with a 23â€“15 win over the Highlanders in the final. He had a very good 2022 Super Rugby Pacific season as the Blues made it to the final where they lost 7â€“21 to the Crusaders. In the 2023 Super Rugby Pacific season, the Blues topped the regular season standings but were eliminated in the quarter-finals by the New South Wales Waratahs 17â€“41 at Eden Park.[7] He missed most of the 2024 Super Rugby Pacific season through injury, but came back to play a starring role in the Grand Final, where he set up Caleb Clarke for one of his hat trick of tries, as the Blues went on to win their 4th title, beating neighbours, the Chiefs, by 41-10, and ending a 21 year title drought. As defending champions, the Blues reached the semi-finals of the 2025 Super Rugby Pacific season but lost 14â€“25 to the Crusaders in Christchurch, with Christie performing strongly at halfback.[8][9]

International career

After the 2021 Super Rugby season, Christie was named in the New Zealand squad to play Tonga and Fiji in the July Steinlager Series.[10] He made his debut against Tonga at Mount Smart Stadium, coming off the bench in a 102â€“0 win, becoming All Black number 1196.[11][12] He played a further four test matches in 2021, against Fiji, South Africa, and Australia. Christie was selected for the All Blacks' 2022 end-of-year tour, where he made five appearances, including starts against England and Wales.[13] In 2023, Christie was included in the All Blacks squad for the Rugby World Cup in France, where he featured in five matches as New Zealand reached the final, earning a silver medal. He accumulated six caps that year.[14] Christie continued with the All Blacks in 2024, playing three tests, including against England. He also represented the All Blacks XV twice that year, scoring 5 points.[15] In 2025, Christie was recalled for the Rugby Championship, starting in matches against Argentina in August and September. As of September 2025, he has 27 caps for the All Blacks, with 5 points (1 try).[15]
