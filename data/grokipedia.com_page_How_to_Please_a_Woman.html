Production

Development

RenÃ©e Webster conceived How to Please a Woman after encountering a real Australian enterprise offering sexual services for women, managed by self-identified housewives, which prompted her to investigate the varied motivations of female clients pursuing paid intimate encounters. This real-world observation informed the film's exploration of midlife women's unmet needs for domestic and sexual fulfillment, amid stagnant personal relationships and societal expectations around aging and desire.[10][11] Script development commenced under Screen Australia's Gender Matters program in 2016, initially titled Her Predilection, as part of efforts to foster gender-balanced narratives in Australian screen content during a period of industry initiatives promoting female perspectives from 2016 onward. Webster shaped the premise around a protagonist launching an all-male cleaning service that inadvertently incorporates sexual elements, deliberately challenging conventional romantic comedy structures by prioritizing women's initiative in intimacy and exposing male emotional exposure in service roles. Further refinement occurred through Screenwest's West Coast Visions program, awarded in 2019 to Webster alongside producers Tania Chambers and Judi Levine, emphasizing visionary female-driven projects in Western Australia.[10][12][13] The screenplay secured $750,000 in production funding from Screenwest's West Coast Visions initiative, supplemented by Screen Australia investment and private backers, enabling pre-production advancement by early 2021 under Feisty Dame Productions and Such Much Films. Webster's vision integrated causal insights from gender relations, portraying how economic redundancy and relational inertia propel women toward unconventional solutions, while ensuring the tone blended humor with underlying pathos to reflect authentic interpersonal vulnerabilities.[10][14] Pre-production encountered setbacks from the COVID-19 pandemic, which disrupted timelines and necessitated script adjustments in 2020 to maintain equilibrium between comedic escalation and dramatic realism, averting overly sentimental or farcical deviations from the core premise of transactional dynamics evolving into genuine connection. These revisions preserved the narrative's grounding in empirical patterns of female dissatisfaction and male adaptability, aligning with Webster's intent for relatable character arcs over idealized tropes. Principal photography commenced in Fremantle in 2021 following these hurdles.[10]

Casting

Sally Phillips was cast in the lead role of Gina in early 2021, selected by director RenÃ©e Webster for her fearlessness in tackling emotionally charged and comedic material.[15] Phillips, known for her sharp comedic timing in Veep and Bridget Jones's Diary, brought a layer of relatable British wit to the Australian production, aligning with the film's themes of midlife female empowerment and vulnerability.[16] Her deep connection to the script allowed for authentic contributions during preparation, enhancing the balance of humor and pathos in the provocative narrative.[15] Erik Thomson was chosen for the supporting role of the husband, drawing on Webster's prior collaboration with him in Aftertaste, where his natural preparation and performance depth proved effective for nuanced male characters.[15] The ensemble of male actors portraying the cleaners, including Alexander England, was selected to embody a mix of physical presence and emotional range, supporting the film's reversal of traditional gender objectification without reported hesitancy in the industry.[16] Female supporting roles featured Caroline Brazier as Sandra, alongside Hayley McElhinney and Tasma Walton, chosen to depict realistic midlife friendships across diverse backgrounds, emphasizing age-appropriate authenticity in representing women's relational dynamics.[16] This casting approach reinforced the film's focus on grounded female solidarity, challenging stereotypes through credible, relatable portrayals rather than idealized tropes.[15]

Filming

Principal photography for How to Please a Woman commenced in April 2021 in Western Australia, primarily utilizing locations in Perth and Fremantle to capture authentic domestic and coastal settings reflective of everyday Australian suburban life.[17][18] Fremantle's port city backdrop and Leighton Beach featured prominently for exterior sequences, enhancing the film's grounded portrayal of character interactions.[14][19] Director RenÃ©e Webster emphasized naturalistic depictions of bodies and intimacy, prioritizing non-exploitative representations of diverse sexualities and older physiques to underscore themes of vulnerability without sensationalism.[20] This approach extended to on-set practices, where an intimacy coordinator ensured safe and consensual handling of scenes involving sexual content, aligning with industry standards for ethical production.[21] Filming occurred amid the COVID-19 pandemic, with the production adhering to Western Australia's health guidelines, including restrictions that influenced scheduling in Fremantle during May 2021.[22] Post-production, including editing by Merlin Eden, was completed in Perth, resulting in a 107-minute runtime that interwove comedic and dramatic elements for tonal balance.[23][24][25] Eden's work focused on pacing to sustain emotional depth alongside humor, facilitating the film's readiness for its December 2021 premiere.[26]

Plot

Synopsis

Gina, a 50-year-old woman trapped in a passionless marriage, receives an unexpected birthday gift from her friends: a male sex worker intended to assist with her personal satisfaction.[27] Rather than rejecting the gesture, this encounter inspires her to launch an all-male cleaning service tailored for female clients in suburban Australia.[28][23] As the business expands, Gina hires a team of male cleaners and navigates escalating client expectations, which push the operation toward offering more intimate services.[27] This evolution brings interpersonal tensions among the staff, challenges in managing demands, and Gina's own explorations of desire and autonomy amid her faltering personal relationships.[28] The narrative traces the company's growth alongside Gina's evolving self-awareness, culminating in reckonings that blend entrepreneurial success with private transformations against the backdrop of everyday domestic life.[23][27]

Release

Premiere

The world premiere of How to Please a Woman occurred at the Perth Festival's Lotterywest Film Festival, screening from December 27, 2021, to January 2, 2022, at the University of Western Australia's Somerville Auditorium.[14][29] This debut event showcased the film's blend of comedy and drama centered on a middle-aged woman's exploration of sexuality, drawing attention to its Western Australian production roots.[30] Following the festival premiere, the film received a nationwide theatrical rollout in Australia on May 19, 2022, distributed by Madman Entertainment.[11] Promotional efforts positioned the movie as a humorous examination of female autonomy in relationships and intimacy, targeting audiences interested in stories of personal reinvention amid life transitions.[16] Internationally, it debuted on Sky Cinema in the United Kingdom on July 8, 2022, marking an early streaming entry for European viewers prior to wider theatrical expansions.[31] Marketing materials, including trailers, underscored the comedic elements to highlight relational dynamics and self-discovery, framing the narrative as an accessible entry into discussions of adult female experiences.[32]

Distribution and box office

The film was distributed theatrically in Australia by Madman Entertainment, opening on May 19, 2022, across 292 screens.[33] It grossed approximately AUD 2.4 million domestically, reflecting a modest performance for an independent comedy-drama amid post-pandemic market recovery and competition from major releases.[33] [34] Internationally, Beta Cinema handled sales to territories including the U.S., U.K., New Zealand, Czech Republic, and Hungary, with limited theatrical runs yielding smaller returns such as NZD 316,901 in New Zealand over 105 days on 75 screens, USD 8,145 in the Czech Republic, and USD 32,111 in Hungary.[35] [36] The film's niche appeal and indie budget constrained broader commercial success, prioritizing targeted markets over wide releases.[37] By late 2022, it became available for streaming on platforms including Hulu in the U.S., contributing to ongoing viewership without reported specific VOD metrics.[38] The overall box office trajectory underscored challenges for Australian independents in 2022, where total local earnings for such titles often remained under AUD 3 million despite cultural resonance.[34]

Reception

Critical response

Critics gave How to Please a Woman mixed reviews, praising its comedic take on female empowerment and Sally Phillips' lead performance while faulting its uneven execution and superficial treatment of themes. The film holds a 71% approval rating on Rotten Tomatoes, based on 35 reviews, with a consensus highlighting its charm and aspirational qualities.[27] On Metacritic, it scores 54 out of 100 from 8 critics, indicating mixed or average reception.[39] Positive assessments often lauded the film's humor and its bold exploration of female desire. The Guardian's Leslie Felperin commended Phillips for her "charming" portrayal, noting how she uses her talent "to make a case for women learning to manage and take charge of their own pleasure."[40] Similarly, RogerEbert.com's Sheila O'Malley, awarding 2.5 out of 4 stars, praised the "extremely witty" script and its focus on women voicing their needs, stating, "Itâ€™s about learning to acknowledge what you want, being able to say it out loud, and then being able to show someone."[25] These elements were seen as refreshing in a 2022 landscape of sex-positive comedies, with outlets like Film Threat appreciating its aspirational edge.[41] Criticisms centered on formulaic plotting, pacing issues, and an uneven tone that diluted the film's messaging. The New York Times's Glenn Kenny described it as an "unsteady comedy" that skips substantive engagement with sexuality, relying on "ellipses that skip from foreplay to pillow talk" in sex scenes, potentially undermining its high-concept premise of a cleaning-and-pleasure service.[42] Reviews from 2022, such as in Screenhub, pointed to a "superficial focus" evident in underdeveloped legal and relational conflicts, while others noted the narrative running "out of plot" before resolution, leading to contrived coincidences.[43][41] Some critiques highlighted a perceived imbalance in gender portrayals, with male characters bordering on caricatures in service of empowerment tropes, though this was not universally emphasized.[44]

Audience and cultural reception

The film earned an audience rating of 6.3/10 on IMDb from 2,230 user votes, indicating moderate approval among general viewers.[28] Many positive responses highlighted its humorous take on female-led entrepreneurship and sexual self-discovery, with viewers appreciating the relatable depiction of midlife reinvention for women.[45] Some feedback noted enjoyment of the lighthearted reversal of traditional service roles, though a subset of reviews critiqued the premise for uneven pacing or contrived humor.[45] In Australia, where it premiered in May 2022, the movie achieved solid box office performance for an independent comedy, grossing about A$2.3 million domestically and contributing to broader international earnings of $1.82 million.[46] Its availability on streaming platforms like Hulu and Sky Cinema from 2022 onward facilitated wider access, sustaining interest through 2023 via on-demand viewership.[47] Public discourse centered on its sex-positive framing of female pleasure, prompting online conversations about autonomy and intimacy norms in everyday relationships, particularly among women sharing personal anecdotes in reviews and social media.[40][48] While lacking major awards or viral trends, the film's grassroots appeal sparked niche debates on challenging relational expectations, with some audiences praising its unapologetic focus on women's desires amid limited mainstream controversy.[49]
