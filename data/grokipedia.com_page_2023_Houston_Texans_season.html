2023 Houston Texans season

The 2023 season (2023 NFL season) was the Houston Texans (Houston Texans)' 22nd season in the National Football League (National Football League) (NFL) and the first under head coach (List of Houston Texans head coaches) DeMeco Ryans (DeMeco Ryans). While the team went into the season with low expectations as a rebuilding period and started 0–2, they not only improved on their 3–13–1 record from last year with a Week 9 win over the Tampa Bay Buccaneers (2023 Tampa Bay Buccaneers season),[2] but the Texans qualified for the playoffs for the first time since 2019 (2019 Houston Texans season) with a win over the Indianapolis Colts (2023 Indianapolis Colts season) in Week 18, ending with a 10–7 record. After the Jacksonville Jaguars (2023 Jacksonville Jaguars season) lost to the Tennessee Titans (2023 Tennessee Titans season) the day after, the Texans also clinched the AFC South for the first time since 2019. The Texans became the fifth team in NFL history to make the playoffs with both a rookie quarterback and a rookie head coach,[3] as well as making history as the first team in NFL history to win a division entirely with a rookie head coach and rookie quarterback. In the Wild Card round of the playoffs (2023–24 NFL playoffs), the Texans blew out the Cleveland Browns (2023 Cleveland Browns season) with a 45–14 win.[4] The team's season would officially come to an end when they would lose to the one-seed Baltimore Ravens (2023 Baltimore Ravens season) 10–34 in the Divisional round, preventing them from making their first AFC Championship appearance in franchise history. In the NFL draft (2023 NFL draft), the Texans selected quarterback C. J. Stroud (C. J. Stroud) from Ohio State (2022 Ohio State Buckeyes football team) second overall (List of second overall NFL draft picks). Stroud would break several NFL and franchise rookie records on his way to being named NFL Offensive Rookie of the Year (NFL Offensive Rookie of the Year), becoming the first player in franchise history to win the award.[5] After trading with the Arizona Cardinals (2023 Arizona Cardinals season), the Texans would select Alabama (2022 Alabama Crimson Tide football team) defensive end Will Anderson Jr. (Will Anderson Jr.) third overall. Anderson was named the NFL Defensive Rookie of the Year (NFL Defensive Rookie of the Year), marking the fourth time in NFL history that teammates won the offensive and defensive rookie of the year awards.[6] The Houston Texans drew an average home attendance of 71,193 in 9 home games in the 2023 NFL season.

2023 Houston Texans Offseason

On January 8, the Texans fired Lovie Smith after just one rebuilding season as the head coach with a record of 3–13–1.[7] On January 31, the Texans hired San Francisco 49ers defensive coordinator and former player DeMeco Ryans as their new head coach.[8] During the offseason, the Texans made several roster moves, including trading wide receiver Brandin Cooks to the Dallas Cowboys (2023 Dallas Cowboys season) for a fifth-round pick and a 2024 sixth-rounder,[9] and acquiring guard Shaq Mason from the Tampa Bay Buccaneers (2023 Tampa Bay Buccaneers season) along with a seventh-round pick.[10]
