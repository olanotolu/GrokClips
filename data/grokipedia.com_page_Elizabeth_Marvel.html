Elizabeth Marvel

Elizabeth Marvel (born November 27, 1969)[1] is an American actress.[2][3][4] Her more prominent roles include Det. Nancy Parras on The District, Solicitor General Heather Dunbar on House of Cards, and President Elizabeth Keane on Homeland. Film roles include Burn After Reading; Synecdoche, New York; True Grit; Lincoln (alongside husband Bill Camp); The Meyerowitz Stories; The Color Purple (2023); and Treasury Secretary Joanna Worth in G20 (2025). She also had a recurring role in season 2 of the FX series Fargo; the Netflix miniseries Unbelievable; as Sister Francesca in the HBO Max series Mrs. Davis (2023); and Linda in the HBO limited series Love & Death (2023). She played "The Major" in the series Manifest and Diane in the Apple TV+ series Presumed Innocent (2024).[5]

Early life and education

Marvel was born in Los Angeles, California.[5] She was raised in Shillington, Pennsylvania.[2] She trained at the Interlochen Arts Academy, and then the Juilliard School (BFA).[1]

Career

Since the early 1990s she has appeared in off-Broadway plays.[6] Marvel appeared in many stage productions throughout her career. Her first professional role was as Isabella in Measure for Measure at Canada's Stratford Festival in 1992.[7][8] She has won Obie Awards for her work in ThÃ©rÃ¨se Raquin and Misalliance (1998),[9] A Streetcar Named Desire (2000)[10] and Hedda Gabler (2005).[11] She returned to the role she originated Off-Broadway of Brooke Wyeth in Other Desert Cities, which was played by Rachel Griffiths in its Broadway premiere. In 2009, for her performance in Fifty Words, she was nominated for a Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Actress in a Play.[12] Marvel first gained widespread attention on television, with her four seasons playing the regular role of Nancy Parras in the CBS series The District (2000â€“04). She played a variety of guest and recurring roles on Lights Out, Law& Order: Special Victims Unit, Nurse Jackie, Person of Interest, 30 Rock, The Good Wife, and The Newsroom. She had a recurring role as Constance Sharp in season 2 of the FX series Fargo (2015) and as Grace in the Netflix miniseries Unbelievable (2019). She also portrayed The Major in the NBC series Manifest (2018â€“2023). In film, Marvel has appeared in Burn After Reading (2008), directed by the Coen brothers, Synecdoche, New York (2008), and in True Grit (2010),[13] as the adult Mattie Ross. She appeared in The Bourne Legacy (2012), Lincoln (2012), and Hyde Park on Hudson (2012). Later films include The Meyerowitz Stories (2017), The Color Purple (2023), and she is set to star in the upcoming horror film Devoted (2025).[14] In 2009 she played Louisa May Alcott in scenes from the writer's life in the documentary profile "Louisa May Alcott: The Woman Behind 'Little Women'" that aired on the PBS series American Masters.[15] In 2013, she was cast as lead in the CW family drama pilot Blink, opposite John Benjamin Hickey.[16] She was noted for portraying Heather Dunbar in Netflix's political drama House of Cards. In 2016, she was cast as President-elect Elizabeth Keane for the sixth and seventh seasons of Showtime drama series Homeland.[17] Marvel's recent television work includes roles in Love & Death (2023), Mrs. Davis (2023), and Presumed Innocent (2024).[5]

Personal life

Marvel married actor Bill Camp on September 4, 2004. The couple have one child, a son born in 2007.[18] In October 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, Marvel revealed on Live with Kelly and Ryan that she and her family were living on a farm in Vermont.[19] The family later returned to New York.[20]
