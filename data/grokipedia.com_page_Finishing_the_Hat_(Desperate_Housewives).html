Finishing the Hat (Desperate Housewives)

Finishing the Hat[1] is the second part of the two-hour series finale of the ABC television series Desperate Housewives. It is the twenty-third and final episode of the show's eighth season and the 180th episode overall, which was broadcast on May 13, 2012.[2] Although the season was promoted as "Kiss Them Goodbye", the series finale was promoted as "The Final Kiss Goodbye".

Plot

Katherine Mayfair makes a surprise return to Wisteria Lane, having amassed a fortune as the owner of a frozen food conglomerate in France, and offers Lynette a job as the head of her United States expansion.[5] Tom encourages her to take the position.[5] Meanwhile, Bree stands trial for the murder of Gabrielle's stepfather, Alejandro Perez. On her deathbed from cancer, Karen McCluskey confesses to the crime, exonerating Bree, and dies peacefully at home with Roy by her side.[5][6] Bree is tasked with obtaining a rare 45 rpm record of "Finishing the Hat" and a turntable to play it for the dying Mrs. McCluskey. However, when Bree visits on the day of Renee's wedding, she discovers that Trip Weston has already procured them, impressing her with his thoughtfulness.[5] Susan reveals to her friends that she is moving to be closer to Julie, who is pursuing a Ph.D. While helping Julie through the final weeks of her pregnancy, Susan attempts to set her up on a date with her obstetrician.[5] On Renee's wedding day to Ben Faulkner, Julie, Gabrielle, and Susan assist her. In the limousine en route to the ceremony, Julie's water breaks, soaking and ruining Renee's expensive gown. In a panic, Renee screams as Susan jumps into the driver's seat to rush Julie to the hospital. Gabrielle and Renee try to steal a $6,000 replacement gown from Gabrielle's store but fail when Susan drives off with their getaway car. Renee runs to the venue, arriving disheveled but is calmed by Ben, and the wedding proceeds successfully.[5][7] Trip crashes the wedding and finally convinces Bree of his genuine care for her. They kiss and reconcile.[5] At the hospital, Julie gives birth to a healthy baby girl with Susan's support.[5] During a final poker game before Susan's departure, the women vow it will not be their last, but as Mary Alice narrates in voice-over, it proves to be.[5] In flash-forwards, Lynette accepts the job and moves to New York with Tom, where she becomes a successful CEO; they settle into a penthouse overlooking Central Park and enjoy their lives together with six grandchildren.[5][6] Gabrielle gets promoted but later, with Carlos's support, starts a personal shopping website called "Gabrielle's Closet," which evolves into her own show on the Home Shopping Network (HSN); they relocate to a mansion in California.[5] Bree marries Trip and moves to Louisville, Kentucky, where she enters politics and becomes a member of the state legislature.[5][6] In one of the final scenes, Susan drives her family around Wisteria Lane one last time before they leave, watched unseen by the ghosts of characters who died on the lane, starting with the most recentâ€”Susan's husband Mike Delfinoâ€”and proceeding in roughly reverse chronological order, ending with Mary Alice Young, whose suicide catalyzed the series.[5] The episode closes with life continuing on the lane: Jennifer, the buyer of Susan's house, hides a mysterious jewelry box in a locked cabinet as Mary Alice comments that mysteries and secrets will always persist on Wisteria Lane.[5][6]

Ratings

The finale was watched by 11.12 million American viewers, earning a 3.2/8 rating/share with adults 18â€“49.[8] It was the most watched program of the night, tied season high ratings with the season 8 premiere "Secrets That I Never Want to Know", and was the most watched episode of the show since the season 7 episode "Searching", watched by 11 million viewers. The finale was also up from the previous season's finale "Come on Over for Dinner", which was watched by 10.25 million viewers and received a 3.1 rating in the 18â€“49 category.[9] The finale was also up from the previous episode "The People Will Hear", which was watched by 9.22 million viewers and received a 2.7/7 rating.[10] The finale was competing against Survivor: One World Reunion on CBS, which was watched by 7.72 million viewers and held a 2.3/6 rating, and Celebrity Apprentice on NBC, which averaged 5.48 million viewers and held a 1.8/5 rating in the 18â€“49 demographic. ABC reported that the episode gained an additional 2.1 million viewers (rising to a total of 13.2 million viewers) and 0.9 rating in the 18â€“49 demographic (rising to a total of a 4.1 rating), in the week following the original broadcast due to DVR recordings.[11] In Canada, the finale was watched by 1.60 million viewers, placing seventeenth for the week.[12]

Critical reception

The episode received critical acclaim from critics. Reviewers for The Washington Post called the episode "a tidy, affectionate send-off."[5] Sabrina Ford of The Province called it a "happy ending. If it were [filmed in Wisteria Lane], we could count on a happy ending."[13] Christina Tran of TV Fanatic gave the episode a generally positive review, saying "While this final season has had its fair share of ups and downs, I thought that Marc Cherry and company gave us a very satisfying ending. I wasnâ€™t left needing more, but instead, only realizing how much I would truly miss Desperate Housewives. "[6] Alberto E. Rodriguez of the Toronto Star called the finale "a fitting way to end the series that was always seen through the eyes of a dead neighbor."[7]

Accolades

This episode was submitted for consideration for Kathryn Joosten due to her nomination for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series at the 64th Primetime Emmy Awards. For her performance in this episode (and the previous episode, "Give Me The Blame"), Brenda Strong was nominated for her second consecutive Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Voice-Over Performance as Mary Alice Young.
