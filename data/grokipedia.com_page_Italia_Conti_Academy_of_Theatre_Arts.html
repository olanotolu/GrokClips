Italia Conti Academy of Theatre Arts

The Italia Conti Academy of Theatre Arts , also known simply as Italia Conti ,[1] is a drama school based in Woking, England. It was founded by the English actress Italia Conti in 1911.[2] Italia Conti offers courses in acting, musical theatre, and dance, catering mostly for 16+ and 18+ students through its higher education programmes, and for younger learners (3–18 years) through its Associate Schools and pre-vocational courses.[3] Italia Conti is a member of the Federation of Drama Schools formerly Drama UK,[4] and accredited by the Council for Dance, Drama and Musical Theatre (CDMT).[5] Italia Conti was a member of National Council for Drama Training (NCDT) before the organisation dissolved in 2012. Originally based in London, the institution has occupied various premises throughout its history. In August 2022, Italia Conti consolidated its operations into a new facility in Woking, Surrey, which includes specialised studios for dance, acting, and musical theatre, as well as recording and television production spaces.[6]

History

The Italia Conti Academy of Theatre Arts was founded in 1911 by the English actress Italia Emily Stella Conti in London, initially operating from a basement in Great Portland Street. It emerged from Conti's success in training child performers for the West End play Where the Rainbow Ends, becoming one of Britain's oldest drama schools. Early students included notable figures such as Noël Coward and Gertrude Lawrence.[2] During World War II, the school's premises were bombed, destroying early records. Following the war, the academy relocated to Clapham, where it continued to offer full-time courses in acting, dance, and theatre arts at secondary, further, and higher education levels.[2][7] In 1981, the academy expanded to Goswell Road in Islington, where it began running Junior and Musical Theatre courses. The BA (Hons) Acting and CertHE Introduction to Acting courses continued to be delivered from the Avondale site in Clapham.[2][8] In August 2022, Italia Conti consolidated all operations into a new state-of-the-art facility in Woking, Surrey.[9]

Courses

Main Provision

The following courses taught at the academy are validated by the University of East London[10], with the academy being a member of the Federation of Drama Schools and courses accredited by the Council for Dance, Drama and Musical Theatre (CDMT)[5].

Acting

  • BA (Hons) Acting, a three-year full-time professional actor training for students aged 18+.[10]
  • CertHE Introduction to Acting, a one-year full-time foundation course for students aged 18+.[10]

Musical Theatre

  • BA (Hons) Musical Theatre, a three-year full-time professional training for students aged 18+.[10]
  • CertHE Musical Theatre Performance, a one-year full-time foundation course for students aged 18+.[10]
  • FdA Musical Theatre, a two-year full-time vocational foundation degree for students aged 16+, validated by the University of Chichester.[11]
  • Level 6 Diploma in Professional Musical Theatre, a three-year full-time vocational course for students aged 16+.[12]

Dance

  • CertHE Dance, a one-year full-time foundation course for students aged 18+.[13]
  • FdA Dance, a two-year full-time vocational foundation degree for students aged 16+, validated by the University of Chichester.[14]
  • Level 6 Diploma in Professional Dance, a three-year full-time vocational course for students aged 16+.[15]
  • BA (Hons) Professional Arts Practice (Top-up), allowing progression from a Level 5 or Level 6 non-degree qualification to a BA (Hons), available across disciplines.[10]

Other

  • BA (Hons) Acting (Musical Theatre), a three-year full-time course integrating acting and musical theatre for students aged 18+.[10]

Theatre Arts School (for Juniors)

The Theatre Arts School, also known as the ‘Juniors’ , was a co-educational independent school for pupils aged from 10 to 16 years old. The Theatre Arts School was first located in the Avondale building in Clapham, London and then at Goswell House, Barbican, London. The school was accredited by the Independent Schools Council and monitored by the Independent Schools Inspectorate. Pupils would follow an academic curriculum to ensure they graduated with qualifications whilst also studying a range of performance skills. In May 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic, it was announced that the Theatre Arts school would close in the summer of 2021 after 109 years.[16]

Buildings and facilities

Woking, Surrey (New Building)

In August 2022, Italia Conti moved to a new facility in Henry Plaza, Woking, Surrey.[9] This facility brings together the School of Acting , which was previously delivered from the Avondale Building in Clapham North, London, and the School of Musical Theatre & Dance that was split between Barbican, London, and Guildford, Surrey sites.[9]

Previous locations

  • Great Portland Street, London, 1911–1930.[17]
  • Lambs Conduit Street, London, 1931–1941.[17]
  • Bournemouth, 1941–1945 (temporary during World War II).[17]
  • 12 Archer Street, London, 1945–1972.[17]
  • Avondale (72 Landor Road), Clapham, 1960–2022.[17]
  • Italia Conti House, ("the academy"), in Goswell Road, Islington, London, 1981–2022.[2]
  • The Italia Conti Arts Centre in Guildford, which housed Musical Theatre and Performing Arts with Dance Teacher Training Courses.[2]

School for Stars

A CBBC series entitled School for Stars followed pupils at the Italia Conti Academy of Theatre Arts, showcasing the school's activities and featuring events from the students' day-to-day lives.[19] The program consisted of three series, each with 10 episodes. Each series followed a set of students during the school term. The series mainly featured the Theatre Arts School (Juniors) and occasionally featured students from the various 16+ courses. School for Stars first aired in 2011, and a third series began on 2 September 2013. The show was narrated by Reggie Yates.[20]
