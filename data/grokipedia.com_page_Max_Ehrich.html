Max Ehrich

Max Lewis Ehrich (born June 24, 1991)[1] is an American actor, singer, and dancer. He is known for his roles as Fenmore Baldwin on the CBS daytime soap opera The Young and the Restless[2] (2012–2016), Hunter May on the CBS series Under the Dome[3] (2014–2015), and as a principal dancer in the Disney film High School Musical 3: Senior Year[4] (2008). In October 2025, Ehrich was arrested in Florida on charges of domestic battery against a family member, which his mother has publicly disputed as fabricated.[5][6]

Life and career

Ehrich was raised in Marlboro Township, New Jersey,[4] in a Jewish family with mother Rhonda Ehrich[7] and father Bruce Ehrich.[8] He made his film debut starring in One Easy Job (2004), then had a supporting role as principal dancer in High School Musical 3: Senior Year. Breaking into television, Ehrich played Randy in two episodes of Ugly Betty.[9] He then played 16-year-old jock Jesse Moretti in the 2009 Lifetime TV-movie The Pregnancy Pact , starring Thora Birch, Camryn Manheim, and Nancy Travis.[10][11] He filmed two pilots for CBS in 2010, "Quinn-tuplets" and "Team Spitz",[12] and later appeared as a recurring guest star in the Country Music Television sitcom Working Class , starring Melissa Peterman.[3] He played Adam in iStart A Fan War , a TV-movie for the Nickelodeon show iCarly. In 2012, Ehrich joined the American CBS Daytime soap opera The Young and the Restless as Fenmore Baldwin, portraying the character until 2015.[2] The following year he received a Daytime Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Younger Actor in a Drama Series for his portrayal.[13][14][15] Ehrich chose a scene where Fenmore tells Jamie that Summer is the one cyberbullying him.[16][17] From 2014 through 2015, he appeared in a recurring role throughout CBS' Under the Dome , as Hunter May. He additionally has appeared on series including Law & Order: Special Victims Unit. He had a recurring role on the Nickelodeon show 100 Things to Do Before High School , and appeared as Quinn in six episodes of Embeds.[9] In 2019, Ehrich appeared in the Netflix film Walk. Ride. Rodeo.[9] Since then, he has continued acting in projects such as the Lifetime series American Princess (2019), the digital soap opera The Bay (2021–present, as of 2025), and films including Southern Gospel (2023) and A Cowboy Christmas Romance (2023), while also releasing music singles like "LAX" (2025).[1]

Personal life

Ehrich was born to Bruce and Rhonda Ehrich in Marlboro, New Jersey.[7][8] He has three siblings: Matthew, Lindsay, and Jessica.[8] Ehrich is Jewish; one of his grandfathers attended Weequahic High School in Newark, New Jersey.[7][8] Ehrich dated actress Veronica Dunne from 2014 to 2017.[1] He and singer-songwriter Demi Lovato began dating in March 2020 and became engaged in July 2020.[19] Two months later, the couple called off their engagement.[20][21] He briefly dated singer Sonika Vaid later in 2020.[1] By November 2020, he was reported to be dating singer Mariah Angeliq.[22] The couple soon ended their relationship. As of 2025, no further long-term relationships have been publicly reported.[1] On October 8, 2025, Ehrich was arrested in St. Lucie County, Florida, on charges of domestic battery against a family or household member, specifically alleging he attacked his mother, who is over 65 years old, during a livestream involving nitrous oxide.[23][24] His mother later denied the allegations, stating it was a misunderstanding and defending her son.[25] Ehrich entered rehab for nitrous oxide addiction following the incident.[26]
