Oscar da Silva

Oscar Leon da Silva (born 21 September 1998) is a German-Brazilian professional basketball player for Bayern Munich of the German Basketball Bundesliga (BBL) and the EuroLeague. He played college basketball for the Stanford Cardinal.

Early life and career

Da Silva was a standout for Ludwigsgymnasium in Munich and was also a member of the MTSV Schwabing in club play, where he averaged 21.0 points, 8.3 rebounds, 2.0 assists, 2.0 steals and 1.7 blocks per game in the 2016–17 season. He was a two-time NBBL All-Star.[1][2] Da Silva also attended the Internationale Basketball Akademie München (IBAM).[3] He signed with Stanford on October 25, 2016, choosing the Cardinal over offers from California and several Ivy League institutions.[4]

College career

After arriving at Stanford, da Silva injured an ankle in practice and missed a month.[5] He averaged 6.2 points and 4.7 rebounds per game as a freshman.[6] As a sophomore, he averaged 9.5 points and 6.0 rebounds per game.[7] Da Silva scored a career high 27 points and had 15 rebounds and three assists on February 1, 2020, in a 70–60 upset of Oregon. As a result, he was named Pac-12 player of the week on February 3.[8] On February 8, da Silva suffered a mild concussion and lacerated head that required stitches after a collision with Evan Battey of Colorado.[9] At the conclusion of the regular season, da Silva was named first-team All-Pac-12.[10] As a junior, da Silva averaged 15.7 points and 6.4 rebounds per game.[11] He averaged 18.5 points and 6.7 rebounds per game as a senior. Da Silva was named to the first-team All-Pac-12,[12] the Pac-12 All-Defensive Team, and the conference Scholar-Athlete of the Year.[13]

Professional career

On March 24, 2021, da Silva announced he had signed on to play for MHP Riesen Ludwigsburg of Germany's Basketball Bundesliga for the remainder of the 2020–21 season. Following that, he began training in preparation for the 2021 NBA draft.[14] After going undrafted in the 2021 NBA draft, da Silva joined the Oklahoma City Thunder for the 2021 NBA Summer League.[15] On September 30, he signed with Alba Berlin until 2024.[16] He won the German championship and the German cup title in 2022. On July 11, 2022, he signed a three-year deal with FC Barcelona.[17] On June 23, 2024, da Silva parted ways with the Catalan powerhouse.[18] On July 4, 2024, da Silva returned to the Bundesliga for Bayern Munich on a three-year deal.[3] With Bayern, he won the 2024–25 German League championship.[19] In March 2025, da Silva had surgery on his right knee after suffering a medial ligament injury during the EuroLeague game against Paris Basketball and remained sidelined for several weeks, but he recovered and returned to the court later in the season.[20]

National team career

In 2016, da Silva played for Germany at the Albert Schweitzer Tournament, an international U18 tournament in Mannheim, Germany. He averaged seven points, 5.4 rebounds and 1.7 blocks per game and won the gold medal.[21] Later in the year, da Silva averaged 9.5 points and six rebounds per game for Germany at the 2016 FIBA U18 European Championship in Samsun, Turkey, leading his team to a fourth-place finish.[21] He helped Germany to fifth place at the 2017 FIBA Under-19 World Cup in Cairo, averaging 10.3 points and four rebounds per game.[22] Da Silva made his senior debut for the German national team on August 5, 2023, in a friendly game against Sweden, where he scored 9 points.[23] In February 2024, da Silva played in the FIBA EuroBasket 2025 qualifiers, including a debut official FIBA game against Montenegro where he recorded 19 points and 7 rebounds.[24] Da Silva was selected to Germany's roster for the 2024 Paris Olympics. Although he saw limited playing time, Germany won the bronze medal.[25] In 2025, da Silva participated in the FIBA EuroBasket 2025 qualifiers and the tournament itself, where Germany won the gold medal. Over 9 games in EuroBasket 2025, he averaged 3.2 points and 1.1 rebounds per game.[26]

Personal life

Da Silva has a Brazilian father and German mother. His father was a professional boxer before immigrating to Germany in the 1990s and owns a Brazilian restaurant in Munich.[5] Da Silva has a younger brother, Tristan da Silva, who plays basketball for the Orlando Magic of the National Basketball Association as of October 2025.[28] At Stanford University, he was a biology major and has carried out stem cell research.[2] He is fluent in six languages: English, French, German, Latin, Portuguese, and Spanish.[29] In 2025, da Silva began working with the Young Refugee Center in Munich, leading basketball sessions for unaccompanied minors who have arrived in Germany.[30]
