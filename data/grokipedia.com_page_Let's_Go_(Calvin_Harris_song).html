Let's Go (Calvin Harris song)

Calvin Harris singles chronology Ne-Yo singles chronology Music video
"Let's Go" on YouTube "Let's Go " is a song by Scottish DJ Calvin Harris featuring American singer Ne-Yo. The track was released in the United Kingdom on 22 April 2012 as the third single from Harris' third studio album, 18 Months (2012).[1] On 29 April 2012, precisely a week after its release, "Let's Go" debuted at number two on the UK Singles Chart for the week ending dated 5 May 2012— marking Harris' third consecutive number two hit (held off by Carly Rae Jepsen's song "Call Me Maybe").[2] It also appeared on the deluxe version of Ne-Yo's fifth studio album, R.E.D. The song received a nomination for Best Dance Recording at the 55th Grammy Awards held in February 2013. The song was certified gold by the RIAA in the United States on June 29, 2020.[3] The song was used for a Pepsi and Pepsi Max advert that features Lionel Messi, Didier Drogba, Fernando Torres, Wojciech Szczęsny, Frank Lampard, Sergio Agüero and Jack Wilshere.[4] It was also used as the opening track to the London New Year’s Eve fireworks display for 2019–20. The Los Angeles Dodgers use this song when they hit a home run.

Critical reception

Upon its release, "Let's Go" received generally positive reviews from music critics. Robbie Daw of Idolator described it as a "high-octane club banger" that captures the "zeitgeist of the moment" with its motivational lyrics and energetic production.[5] In a mixed review, PopCrush noted that while the track is "bouncy" and easy to dance to, it "doesn't quite reach the heights" of Harris's previous hit "We Found Love" due to its similar structure and reliance on the beat over lyrics. Ne-Yo's vocals were praised for marking a successful transition to dance music.[6] That Grape Juice called the song club-ready, with Ne-Yo's vocals complementing the production, but criticized it for blending into the "flooded" market of generic dance tracks at the time.[7]

Music video

The music video, directed by Vincent Haycock, was released on 14 May 2012 on Vevo.[8] It features scenes from Tokyo, Berlin, Rio de Janeiro, and Los Angeles, following four couples immersed in local dance cultures. Ne-Yo appears in the Los Angeles segments, boxing in a gym and singing from the back seat of a car. Calvin Harris is seen DJing at a club party.[9]

Live performances

Ne-Yo performed "Let's Go" during the player introductions at the 2013 NBA All-Star Game, as part of a medley including "Let Me Love You (Until You Learn to Love Yourself)" and "Forever Now" https://thisisrnb.com/2013/02/ne-yo-performs-medley-at-nba-all-star-game/. He also performed the song at the 1st Indonesian Choice Awards on May 18, 2014 https://www.wowkeren.com/berita/tampil/00051374.html.
