2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election

The 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election was held on 18 March 2023 to elect the governor of Kaduna State, Nigeria, succeeding two-term incumbent Nasir El-Rufai of the All Progressives Congress (APC). Uba Sani, the APC nominee and a former senator aligned with El-Rufai, narrowly defeated Isa Ashiru, the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) candidate and a businessman, with 730,002 votes to Ashiru's 719,196, while third-place finisher Jonathan Asake of the Labour Party received 58,283 votes.[1][2][3] The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) declared Sani the winner on 20 March, meeting constitutional requirements by securing over 25% of votes in more than two-thirds of the state's 23 local government areas.[1][2] The contest was marked by intense competition, reflecting Kaduna's history of ethno-religious divisions and political rivalries, with campaigns focusing on security, economic development, and infrastructure amid ongoing banditry and farmer-herder conflicts in the region.[4] Sani's victory extended APC dominance in the state, where the party had won the prior two gubernatorial races decisively, but the slim marginâ€”under 11,000 votesâ€”signaled eroding support possibly linked to voter fatigue or localized grievances.[1][2] The PDP rejected results from several local governments, alleging discrepancies between manual tallies and uploads to INEC's electronic IReV portal, alongside claims of vote suppression and irregularities, prompting Ashiru to file a petition at the Kaduna State Governorship Election Petition Tribunal.[1][5] The tribunal dismissed the case in September 2023 for lack of evidence, a ruling upheld by the Court of Appeal in November 2023 and the Supreme Court in January 2024, affirming Sani's election on procedural and evidentiary grounds.[6][7] While broader Nigerian election observers noted instances of political violence nationwide, including in Kaduna, no specific widespread disruptions invalidated the outcome per judicial review.[8]

Electoral Framework

Electoral System and Procedures

The 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election was conducted under the framework of the Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria 1999 (as amended) and the Electoral Act 2022, with the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) serving as the primary electoral authority responsible for its organization, supervision, and declaration of results.[9][10] The election utilized a direct system of universal adult suffrage, whereby eligible voters elected the governor through an open secret ballot at designated polling units.[11][9] To be declared the winner, a candidate required the highest number of valid votes cast statewide and at least one-quarter of the votes in no fewer than two-thirds of Kaduna State's 23 local government areas; failure to meet these criteria triggered a supplementary election between the top two candidates.[9][10] Voting occurred on March 18, 2023 (postponed from March 11 due to logistical challenges), from 8:30 a.m. to 2:30 p.m., with all voters in queue by closing time permitted to cast ballots; priority access was given to persons with disabilities, pregnant women, nursing mothers, and the elderly.[11][10] Voter accreditation preceded voting and mandated presentation of a Permanent Voter's Card (PVC), verification against the voter register, and biometric authentication via INEC's Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS), which employed fingerprint or facial recognition to prevent multiple voting; indelible ink marked accredited fingers, and failed verifications barred participation.[11][10] Accredited voters then received ballots to mark secretly in screened cubiclesâ€”selecting one gubernatorial candidate per party ballotâ€”before folding and depositing them into transparent ballot boxes; photography of marked ballots was prohibited.[11][9] Post-voting, polling officials conducted manual counting and sorting of ballots in the open, announcing results by party at the polling unit level, with party agents and observers present to witness; forms (Form EC8A) recorded figures for manual collation upward through ward/registration area centers, local government area collation centers, and finally the state collation center in Kaduna, where the chief returning officer declared the winner.[11][10] BVAS facilitated electronic transmission of polling unit-level results to INEC's Results Viewing Portal (IReV) for public access, enhancing transparency, though discrepancies were resolved via original forms if uploads failed.[11][9] Supplementary polls, if required, followed similar procedures but only in affected polling units where the vote margin was less than the number of disenfranchised voters.[10]

Voter Eligibility and Registration

Voter eligibility for the 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election adhered to criteria established by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) under Nigerian electoral law. Eligible individuals were required to be Nigerian citizens aged 18 years or older on the date of registration, residing in Nigeria, and free from any legal incapacity to vote, such as felony convictions or adjudged unsoundness of mind.[12] [11] Registration was tied to the applicant's area of residence, employment, or origin within Kaduna State, ensuring voters participated at polling units in their designated local government areas for state-level contests like the gubernatorial election.[12] The registration process utilized INEC's Continuous Voter Registration (CVR) framework, which allowed new registrantsâ€”primarily those reaching voting age since the prior exercise or previously unregistered eligible citizensâ€”to enroll via biometric verification.[12] For the 2023 elections, CVR commenced in early 2021 and concluded nationwide on June 30, 2022, after which no new registrations were accepted ahead of the March 18, 2023, gubernatorial poll in Kaduna.[13] Applicants could initiate enrollment through online pre-registration on INEC's portal, submitting personal details and a photograph to schedule an appointment, followed by an in-person visit to a designated center for fingerprint and facial biometric capture, issuance of a Temporary Voter Card, and eventual collection of the Permanent Voter's Card (PVC).[12] Direct walk-in registration at centers was also available without prior online steps, requiring proof of identity, age, and nationality if challenged.[12] By the close of CVR, Kaduna State ranked among the top states for registered voters, contributing to Nigeria's total of over 93 million on the final roll.[14] To participate in the election, voters needed to present their PVC at assigned polling units, where accreditation occurred via INEC's Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) using fingerprints or facial recognition to confirm presence on the voter register and prevent multiples.[11] Failure to possess a PVC barred voting, emphasizing INEC's focus on verifiable identity to mitigate fraud.[11]

Political and Historical Context

State Governance Prior to Election

Nasir Ahmad El-Rufai of the All Progressives Congress (APC) served as governor of Kaduna State from May 2015 to May 2023, succeeding the People's Democratic Party (PDP) administration amid promises of fiscal prudence, infrastructure revival, and security enhancement.[15] His tenure emphasized public sector reforms, including consistent budget publications for transparency and the establishment of a dedicated Ministry of Internal Security and Home Affairs to address rising threats from banditry and communal clashes.[16] Internally generated revenue (IGR) surged from approximately N4.5 billion in 2015 to N60 billion by 2023, driven by tax administration improvements, land reforms, and digitized revenue collection, which funded capital projects without excessive debt accumulation.[17] In education, El-Rufai prioritized human capital development by declaring free and compulsory basic education up to junior secondary level, renovating thousands of classrooms, and constructing new facilities with added water and sanitation provisions.[18] A landmark 2017 competency test for over 40,000 primary school teachers revealed widespread deficiencies, leading to retraining, dismissals of underperformers, and recruitment drives to elevate teaching standards, though the policy sparked protests from affected unions.[19] Health reforms focused on facility upgrades and affordable access, aligning with broader goals in the state's development plan to integrate education and healthcare for equitable service delivery.[20] Security remained a persistent challenge, with banditry, kidnappings, and farmer-herder conflicts escalating, particularly in rural areas; the state recorded over 1,225 insecurity incidents in the two years preceding the 2023 election, underscoring gaps in federal-state coordination despite local vigilante support and El-Rufai's threats to import foreign mercenaries.[21] Critics, including former senator Shehu Sani, accused the administration of inadequate backing for security forces and indirect concessions to bandits, while El-Rufai countered that federal policies, such as ransom payments, exacerbated the crisis.[22] These dynamics, coupled with urban demolitions for urban renewal and occasional religious tensions, polarized public opinion, setting a backdrop of reformist zeal amid unresolved violence that influenced voter priorities ahead of the March 2023 polls.[23]

Key Political Dynamics and Influences

The 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election was characterized by intense rivalry between the All Progressives Congress (APC) and the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), framed as a two-horse race between APC candidate Uba Sani and PDP candidate Isa Ashiru, amid 17 total contenders including spoilers from the New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP) and Labour Party (LP).[4][24] The absence of an incumbent seeking re-election, due to term limits for outgoing Governor Nasir El-Rufai, shifted focus to policy legacies and calls for change, with analysts anticipating a voter-driven contest evaluating El-Rufai's eight-year tenure of reforms including tax hikes and security measures.[4][25] A pivotal influence was El-Rufai's strategic endorsement of Uba Sani, his long-time aide and successor, aimed at perpetuating his governance model; this included granting Sani unprecedented access to state cabinet meetings for policy continuity.[25][4] In contrast, Ashiru drew strength from PDP's earlier successes in Kaduna's presidential and legislative polls, leveraging grassroots networks and dissatisfaction with El-Rufai's policies to mount an "onslaught" against APC continuity.[25][4] Ethno-religious identities profoundly shaped voter mobilization, with Kaduna's north-south divideâ€”predominantly Muslim Hausa-Fulani in the north versus diverse Christian groups in the southâ€”amplifying tensions; traditional power-sharing norms for balanced Muslim-Christian tickets were upended since 2019, fueling perceptions of marginalization in southern Kaduna, which historically leaned PDP but saw LP's Jonathan Asake siphon votes there.[4][24] Religion directly influenced outcomes, as leaders exploited affiliations to sway voters, evident in areas like Giwa Local Government where surveys of 150 respondents confirmed religious mobilization overrode other factors, mirroring national trends from the Muslim-Muslim presidential ticket.[26][24] Elements of ethnic revenge against perceived northern dominance and El-Rufai's style further intensified identity-based voting, though both leading candidates were northern Muslims.[24]

Candidate Selection Processes

All Progressives Congress Primaries

The All Progressives Congress (APC) held its gubernatorial primary election for Kaduna State on May 26, 2022, utilizing an indirect voting system in which accredited party delegates selected the candidate.[27][28] Incumbent Governor Nasir El-Rufai, a key APC figure in the state, endorsed Senator Uba Sani, representing Kaduna Central, as his preferred successor prior to the vote, influencing delegate alignments amid competition from other aspirants.[29] Uba Sani emerged victorious with 1,149 votes out of the total cast, as announced by Anachuna Izu, chairman of the APC's electoral committee for the primary.[27][30] Among the contenders was Alhaji Sani Mohammed Sha'aban, a prominent businessman and APC stalwart, who alleged procedural irregularities including manipulation of delegate lists and exclusion of legitimate participants during the voting process.[31] Sha'aban's challenge led to a lawsuit filed at the Federal High Court in Kaduna, seeking nullification of the results and Uba Sani's candidacy on grounds of non-compliance with APC guidelines and the Electoral Act.[32] On November 4, 2022, Justice Mohammed Lawal-Garba dismissed the suit, ruling that Sha'aban lacked sufficient evidence of irregularities and affirming Uba Sani's nomination as valid under party rules.[33][34] This judicial endorsement cleared the path for Uba Sani's advancement as the APC's official candidate for the March 18, 2023, general election, despite internal dissent that highlighted tensions over delegate accreditation and zoning preferences within Kaduna's diverse ethnic and religious demographics.[27]

People's Democratic Party Primaries

The People's Democratic Party conducted its gubernatorial primary election for Kaduna State on May 25, 2022, as part of the nationwide primaries to select candidates for the 2023 general elections.[35] The indirect primary involved delegates from the party's wards and local government areas voting at a designated venue in Kaduna.[36] Isa Mohammed Ashiru, a former member of the House of Representatives from Kudan, emerged as the PDP's nominee, defeating five other aspirants in a contest marked by competition among experienced politicians.[37][38] The defeated candidates included former Senator Shehu Sani, ex-Governor Mukhtar Ramalan Yero, former National Emergency Management Agency head Sani Sidi, and two others whose identities were less prominently reported in contemporaneous accounts.[36][38] Ashiru's victory positioned him as the party's standard-bearer against the incumbent All Progressives Congress administration.[37] No official vote tallies were publicly detailed by the party or independent observers in immediate reports, though Ashiru was formally declared the winner by PDP officials shortly after the voting concluded.[36] The process proceeded without widely reported disruptions or legal challenges at the time, reflecting the PDP's adherence to its internal delegate-based selection guidelines amid broader national primaries.[35] Ashiru, holding the traditional title of Sarkin Bai of Kudan, leveraged his legislative experience and local ties in securing delegate support.[37]

Other Parties' Nominations

The Labour Party (LP) conducted internal primaries to select its gubernatorial candidate, with Jonathan Asake emerging victorious on August 11, 2022.[39] Asake, a former member of the House of Representatives and ex-president of the Southern Kaduna Peoples Union, was affirmed despite reported internal disputes over the nomination process, including attempts to replace the candidate multiple times prior to finalization.[40] The New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP) nominated Senator Suleiman Othman Hunkuyi as its candidate through a party primary on June 6, 2022.[41] Hunkuyi, a former senator representing Kaduna North in the 8th National Assembly, secured the ticket amid pledges to reverse certain policies of the incumbent administration if elected.[42] He later unveiled his running mate in September 2022.[43] Smaller parties, including the Social Democratic Party, African Democratic Congress, and others, generally nominated candidates via consensus selections or abbreviated internal conventions compliant with the Independent National Electoral Commission's June 2022 deadlines for primary elections.[44] These processes lacked the scale and public contention of major-party primaries, resulting in INEC clearing 14 additional candidates beyond those of the APC, PDP, LP, and NNPP for a total of 17 on October 5, 2022.[45][46]

Campaign Dynamics

Major Campaign Issues

Security emerged as a paramount concern in the 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election campaigns, driven by persistent banditry, kidnappings, and ethno-religious violence that had disrupted rural communities and economic activities across the state. Uba Sani, the APC candidate, outlined plans in his SUSTAIN manifesto to invest in security technologies, establish a Kaduna State Security Trust Fund, and bolster community vigilance groups to enhance local responses to threats.[47] Isa Ashiru, the PDP candidate, similarly prioritized security in his agenda, promising coordinated efforts to restore peace and protect citizens from ongoing insurgent activities that had led to thousands of displacements and fatalities in prior years.[48] Economic revitalization, particularly through agriculture and trade, featured prominently, reflecting Kaduna's agrarian base and high poverty rates exceeding 50% in rural areas. Sani pledged to expand agribusiness via special agro-processing zones, irrigation schemes, and incentives for mixed farming and ranching to boost food security and employment for the state's youth-heavy population.[47] Ashiru focused on agriculture and rural development as core pillars, advocating for investments to address farmer-herder conflicts and improve market access amid national inflation rates hovering around 20% during the campaign period.[48] Both candidates addressed infrastructure deficits, with Sani committing to a state-wide master plan including power expansion (targeting 84MW thermal and 100MW solar plants) and urban transit systems, while critiquing uneven development under prior administrations that favored urban centers like Kaduna city over peripheral regions.[4] Human capital development, encompassing education and health, underscored campaigns amid Kaduna's low literacy rates (around 54% statewide) and overburdened facilities. Sani promised 12 years of free basic education and upgrades to 255 primary health centers with expanded insurance coverage to combat out-of-school children numbers exceeding 700,000.[47] Ashiru highlighted education, health, youth empowerment, and housing as priorities, aiming to reverse policy grievances such as mass demolitions that displaced traders and exacerbated urban poverty.[48][4] These issues reflected broader voter demands for accountable governance, with candidates engaging civil society forums to advocate issue-based discourse over ethnic or religious divisions.[49]

Key Events and Debates

On January 18, 2023, candidates from the major parties participated in a public debate organized by the BBC Hausa Service at Kaduna State University Auditorium, focusing on security challenges, healthcare improvements, and poverty alleviation.[50] Uba Sani of the APC pledged to build on incumbent Governor Nasir El-Rufai's initiatives, highlighting an increase in state-employed doctors from fewer than 40 to over 400, and emphasized agricultural development, skills training, and support for women and vulnerable groups to boost school enrollment and economic opportunities.[50] Isa Ashiru of the PDP outlined plans for integrating skill acquisition programs into schools and constructing three diagnostic centers across senatorial districts, while prioritizing the employment of local doctors and nurses.[50] Suleiman Hunkuyi of the NNPP criticized El-Rufai's policies and committed to reversing them to address systemic issues.[50] Earlier, on September 26, 2022, a citizen-led agenda-setting dialogue hosted by the Partnership for Issues-Based Campaign in Nigeria (PICaN) in Kaduna allowed residents to question leading candidates on pressing concerns including security, education, and infrastructure.[51] Hunkuyi promised enhanced security through better-trained and equipped personnel for rural and urban areas, alongside education reforms such as infrastructure upgrades, policy reviews on teacher dismissals and school fees, and reversal of discontinued feeding programs.[51] Uba Sani attended in person but centered responses on policy continuity under APC governance, while Ashiru participated remotely via Zoom without detailed positions recorded on these topics.[51] Campaign activities were overshadowed by ongoing banditry and terrorism, which disrupted rallies and mobilization in affected areas, contributing to resident skepticism about participation amid fears of violence.[52] On January 21, 2023, Uba Sani held an event in Southern Kaduna emphasizing peace and unity to mitigate ethnic and religious tensions influencing voter sentiment.[53] Additionally, an APC women's mega rally on January 25, 2023, featured Sani's commitments to gender equity in governance.[54] Near the election, on March 3, 2023, Ashiru challenged rivals to a debate on Islamic principles in governance, though no confirmation of its occurrence exists in reports.[55]

Voter Mobilization and Strategies

The All Progressives Congress (APC) candidate, Uba Sani, established a 47-member campaign committee in April 2022 to coordinate statewide mobilization efforts, including outreach to party structures and zonal leaders.[56] In September 2022, Sani convened meetings with zonal party executives across Kaduna's three senatorial districts to refine electoral strategies, emphasizing grassroots engagement and voter turnout in key local government areas.[57] These efforts built on the influence of outgoing Governor Nasir El-Rufai, whose political machinery supported Sani's succession bid amid PDP challenges, focusing on promises of continued infrastructure development to consolidate support in urban and northern strongholds.[25] The People's Democratic Party (PDP) candidate, Isa Ashiru, prioritized agenda-based outreach, unveiling a five-point plan in January 2023 targeting security, economic revival, education, health, and infrastructure to appeal to disaffected voters.[58] Ashiru's team welcomed defectors from APC and New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP) in December 2022, particularly in Sabon Gari Local Government Area, to bolster local networks and expand voter base through expanded patronage.[59] Endorsements from groups like the Kaduna Traders Association in February 2023 aided mobilization among commercial communities, while Ashiru publicly urged massive voter participation and vote protection on March 13, 2023, ahead of the March 18 poll.[60][61] Religious affiliations significantly shaped mobilization in religiously divided areas, such as Giwa Local Government Area, where candidates and supporters leveraged ethnic-religious ties to rally voters, mirroring national patterns from the February presidential contest where Muslim-Muslim tickets influenced turnout.[26] Both major parties traded accusations of incitement during campaigns, with APC criticizing PDP for ethnic appeals and PDP countering on governance failures, amid broader efforts to counter low national turnout through door-to-door canvassing and rally turnout, though empirical data indicate persistent apathy contributed to subdued participation.[62][8]

Pre-Election Conduct

Electoral Preparations and Timetable

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) released the official timetable and schedule of activities for the 2023 general elections, including gubernatorial contests in states such as Kaduna, on February 26, 2022, in compliance with Section 28(1) of the Electoral Act 2022, which mandates publication not later than 360 days before polling day.[63][64] This national framework applied uniformly to Kaduna State, with no state-specific deviations noted. Key pre-nomination activities included the notice of election issued on February 28, 2022, and the conduct of political party primaries commencing from March 1, 2022, for gubernatorial candidates, though INEC later specified a window from April 4 to June 9, 2022, following an extension to accommodate party processes.[63][64] Voter-related preparations emphasized continuous voter registration (CVR), which ran from June 28, 2021, to July 31, 2022, resulting in 4,335,208 registered voters in Kaduna State after deduplication, with 479,231 new registrations during the period.[64] Permanent Voter Cards (PVCs) collection reached 4,164,473 in Kaduna, achieving a 96% rate, facilitated by nationwide display centers from November 19 to 25, 2022.[64] The voters' register was published on January 12, 2023, followed by notice of poll on January 30, 2023.[63] INEC also trained over 52,000 ad-hoc staff in Kaduna, including 1,033 supervisory presiding officers, 50,216 presiding and assistant presiding officers, and 930 collation/returning officers, using cascade methods and the INECPRES platform, with emphasis on Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) operations.[64] A nationwide mock accreditation exercise occurred on February 4, 2023, across select polling units to test BVAS and procedures.[64] Nomination processes required submission of forms by August 18, 2022, with final lists of candidates published on October 4, 2022.[63] Campaigns for gubernatorial elections commenced on October 12, 2022, and were scheduled to end on March 9, 2023.[63][65] Logistics preparations involved deploying 3,672 vehicles in Kaduna via memoranda of understanding with transport unions, alongside non-sensitive materials distributed two months prior and sensitive materials secured at Central Bank vaults for last-minute delivery.[64] Registration Area Centers (RACs) in Kaduna achieved 100% activation by March 17, 2023.[64] The gubernatorial election, originally set for March 11, 2023, was postponed to March 18, 2023, by INEC to address logistical challenges, BVAS-related court orders from the presidential phase, and material shortages, extending the campaign endpoint to March 17, 2023.[64][66]

Reported Incidents of Violence and Irregularities

The National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) reported political killings in Kaduna State in early February 2023, condemning the incidents as violations of rights to peaceful assembly, association, and political participation amid rising political intolerance ahead of the general elections.[67] These killings were part of a broader pattern in the North West geo-political zone, though specific victim identities and perpetrators were not detailed in official statements. Additionally, the NHRC highlighted incessant arson attacks on Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) offices in Kaduna a few weeks prior to the February 25 presidential and National Assembly elections, which disrupted logistical preparations for subsequent polls including the March 18 gubernatorial election.[67] In Lere Local Government Area, gunmen attacked the residence of a Labour Party (LP) candidate for the federal constituency on or around February 1-14, 2023, resulting in two deaths; this incident was classified as election-related violence targeting political actors.[68] Separately, on March 14, 2023, militants assaulted communities in Mubushi and Langsong villages within Zangon Kataf Local Government Area in Southern Kaduna, killing 10 Christians and wounding 14 others; local sources attributed the attack to efforts to displace Christian voters in areas potentially unfavorable to the All Progressives Congress (APC), thereby suppressing turnout ahead of the gubernatorial vote four days later.[69] Reports of pre-election irregularities in Kaduna were limited compared to violence, with no widespread documentation of issues such as voter registration fraud or distribution of fake accreditation materials specific to the gubernatorial race; however, the arson on INEC facilities raised concerns over compromised voter verification processes and material security.[67] Overall, these events contributed to heightened insecurity in a state historically prone to ethno-religious tensions, though observers noted that violence did not reach the scale of post-2011 election clashes.[8]

General Election Execution

Voting Day Operations

The 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election voting was conducted on March 18, 2023, across 8,012 polling units established for 4,335,208 registered voters.[64] The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) deployed the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) for voter verification using biometric fingerprints or facial recognition, enabling simultaneous accreditation and voting where feasible, with accreditation typically completed in 20-30 seconds per voter.[64] BVAS units facilitated result uploads to the INEC Result Viewing Portal (IReV), achieving a 94.85% transmission rate in the state.[64] Polling officially commenced at 8:30 AM and concluded at 2:30 PM, with extensions permitted for voters queued by closing time; early activations of Registration Area Centres (RACs) began around 7:30 AM to support timely material distribution.[64] Logistics encompassed 6,791 vehicles for transporting personnel, BVAS machines, and sensitive materials, alongside 100% RAC activation prior to voting day.[64] Priority queues were provided for vulnerable groups, including the elderly, disabled, and pregnant women, to streamline access.[64] Special arrangements accommodated 125,411 internally displaced persons (IDPs) through dedicated voting at 245 polling units.[64] While BVAS performed optimally in most units, isolated technical glitches and network issues necessitated on-site reconfiguration by INEC technical teams.[64] No widespread disruptions to voting operations were documented specifically on March 18 in Kaduna, though pre-existing security concerns contributed to inadequate protection in some areas, prompting supplementary polls in four affected constituencies due to over-voting and localized irregularities.[64][8]

Official Results and Declarations

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) declared Uba Sani, the All Progressives Congress (APC) candidate, as the winner of the 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election on March 20, 2023, at the state collation center in Kaduna.[1][70] The Returning Officer, Professor Lawal Suleiman Bilbis, Vice-Chancellor of Usmanu Danfodiyo University, Sokoto, announced the results after collating votes from all 23 local government areas, confirming Sani met the constitutional requirements of securing the highest votes and at least 25% in two-thirds of the state's local governments.[1][70] Sani received 730,002 votes, surpassing Isa Ashiru of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), who garnered 719,196 votes, by a margin of 10,806 votes.[1][70] Other major contenders included Jonathan Asake of the Labour Party (LP) with 58,283 votes and Suleiman Hunkuyi of the New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP) with 21,405 votes.[1][70]

Breakdown by Districts and Areas

The election results demonstrated clear regional divides across Kaduna State's 23 local government areas (LGAs), with Uba Sani of the APC securing majorities in most northern and central LGAs, while Isa Ashiru of the PDP performed strongly in several southern LGAs characterized by ethnic and religious diversity.[71] Following the collation of results from 22 LGAs, the APC had amassed 379,373 votes compared to the PDP's 367,560, highlighting the APC's edge in populous urban and Hausa-Fulani dominated districts like Kaduna North, Kaduna South, Igabi, and Zaria.[71] In southern LGAs, the PDP and Labour Party (LP) candidates drew significant support from Christian-majority communities. For instance, in Kaura LGA, Isa Ashiru (PDP) received 15,108 votes, ahead of the LP candidate's 12,950 and Uba Sani's 7,748, from 37,254 accredited voters out of 101,279 registered.[72] Similar patterns emerged in areas like Jema'a and Zangon Kataf, where PDP leveraged local grievances over security and governance to outperform the APC.[71] The remaining LGAs, including contested Lere where PDP alleged discrepancies between manual collation and INEC's IReV portal uploads, tipped the statewide balance to Sani with 610,881 votes (54.4%) against Ashiru's 511,185 (45.5%).[71][1]

Controversies and Allegations

Claims of Electoral Malpractice

The Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) and its gubernatorial candidate, Isa Ashiru, filed a petition challenging the election of All Progressives Congress (APC) candidate Uba Sani, alleging widespread electoral irregularities and fraud that undermined the March 18, 2023, poll.[74] The petitioners asserted that Ashiru had secured the popular mandate but that results were manipulated through over-voting, ballot stuffing, and non-compliance with the Electoral Act in multiple polling units.[6] They supported these claims with testimony from 24 witnesses and documentary evidence, contending that such malpractices invalidated the declared outcome favoring Sani.[74] Specific allegations centered on discrepancies in 24 polling units across seven wards in four local government areas, affecting approximately 16,300 registered voters.[74] The PDP argued that these incidents included inflated voter turnout exceeding accredited numbers and unauthorized alterations to result sheets, which they described as systematic rigging orchestrated to favor the APC.[6] Ashiru's camp further deposed that the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) failed to adhere to electoral protocols, such as proper collation procedures and voter verification, rendering the process neither free nor fair.[74] The Governorship Election Petition Tribunal, in a 2-1 split decision on September 28, 2023, found partial merit in the claims of irregularities in the specified units, declaring the election inconclusive and ordering a supplementary poll within 90 days.[74] However, the Court of Appeal, in its November 24, 2023, judgment, dismissed the PDP's appeal as lacking sufficient evidence, ruling that the alleged over-voting and manipulations did not substantially affect the overall result and that the election complied with the Electoral Act.[6] The Supreme Court upheld this on January 19, 2024, affirming Sani's victory and rejecting the malpractice assertions for want of credible proof.[75]

Violence and Security Issues

Security challenges in Kaduna State during the lead-up to the March 18, 2023, gubernatorial election were exacerbated by ongoing banditry in the northwest region and ethno-religious tensions, particularly in southern Kaduna, where attacks aimed to displace Christian communities and suppress voter turnout in areas unfavorable to the ruling All Progressives Congress (APC).[69] The National Human Rights Commission condemned political killings and the repeated arson attacks on Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) facilities in Kaduna, noting these acts as violations of rights to assembly, association, and participation in the electoral process.[67] A notable pre-election incident occurred on March 14, 2023, when gunmen attacked villages in Mubushi and Langsong in southern Kaduna, killing 10 Christians and wounding 14 others; observers linked the assault to efforts to intimidate voters in strongholds of opposition candidates, including the Labour Party's Christian nominee and the Peoples Democratic Party's deputy governor candidate.[69] Earlier, in November 2022, gunmen murdered the Labour Party's women's leader in Kaura Local Government Area, highlighting targeted violence against opposition figures.[8] Militant groups, including Boko Haram affiliates, issued threats of post-election attacks on Christian villages in Kaduna, contributing to the displacement of over 20,000 residents and further straining security arrangements.[76] Despite these threats, no widespread violence was reported on voting day itself in Kaduna, though the pervasive insecurity from banditry limited campaign activities and voter mobilization in rural areas.[8] The election's postponement from March 11 to March 18 due to logistical issues in some states amplified fears of exploitation by non-state actors.[76]

Responses from Stakeholders

The Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) and its gubernatorial candidate, Isa Ashiru, rejected the election results announced by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) on March 20, 2023, alleging widespread electoral malpractice, including discrepancies between results uploaded on INEC's IReV portal and those collated at local government levels, particularly in Lere Local Government Area.[71] Ashiru insisted that the PDP had won the election based on polling unit results and called for its cancellation, describing the process as rigged in favor of the All Progressives Congress (APC).[77] The party urged supporters to remain calm pending further action but filed a petition at the election tribunal challenging Uba Sani's victory.[78] INEC defended the declaration of Uba Sani as winner with 379,702 votes to Ashiru's 358,309, stating that collation followed electoral guidelines despite technical glitches with result transmission via the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) and IReV in some areas.[79] The commission acknowledged isolated incidents of violence and logistical delays but maintained that these did not substantially affect the overall outcome, attributing discrepancies to human errors during manual collation rather than systemic fraud.[79] The APC, through spokespersons and supporters, dismissed PDP allegations as baseless attempts to undermine a credible process, emphasizing that Sani's victory reflected voter preference amid improved security compared to prior elections.[80] Party leaders highlighted the narrow margin as evidence of a competitive but fair contest, urging acceptance of results to avoid escalating tensions.[80] International and domestic observers, including the European Union Election Observation Mission, noted that the Kaduna election occurred in a tense environment with reports of voter intimidation and delays but assessed it as largely peaceful relative to the presidential poll, though undermined by inconsistent BVAS usage and inadequate transparency in result aggregation.[66] Civil society groups echoed concerns over violence in southern Kaduna but refrained from outright invalidation, calling for thorough investigation of specific malpractice claims.[66]

Post-Election Litigation

Petitions Filed

The Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) and its candidate, Isa Ashiru, filed a petition challenging the declaration of Uba Sani of the All Progressives Congress (APC) as the winner of the 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election. The petition, lodged at the Kaduna State Governorship Election Petition Tribunal, named as respondents Uba Sani, the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC), and the APC.[81][82] It was submitted within the 21-day statutory period following INEC's announcement of results on March 20, 2023.[83] The petitioners alleged substantial non-compliance with the Electoral Act 2022, including widespread electoral malpractices such as deduction of votes credited to the PDP, stuffing of ballot boxes, mutilation and destruction of sensitive electoral materials, and over-voting in multiple polling units across several local government areas. They further claimed that these irregularities affected the outcome in at least seven polling units in four local government areas, rendering the election inconclusive in those areas, and contended that Ashiru had scored the majority of lawful votes. The petition sought orders to either nullify Sani's victory and declare Ashiru the winner or order a rerun in the disputed units.[84][74][85] No other significant petitions were filed against the election results by third parties or other candidates, with the PDP's challenge representing the principal post-election litigation at the tribunal level.[86]

Tribunal Proceedings and Rulings

The Governorship Election Petition Tribunal sitting in Kaduna, chaired by Justice Victor Oviawe, heard the petition filed by Isa Ashiru of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) and the party itself against Uba Sani of the All Progressives Congress (APC), the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC), and others.[74] The petitioners alleged substantial non-compliance with the Electoral Act 2022, including over-voting, ballot stuffing, voter suppression, and manipulation of results in favor of the APC candidate.[87] Proceedings began shortly after the 21-day window for filing petitions closed in early April 2023, with pre-hearing sessions addressing motions on competence, including a preliminary objection by respondents that the PDP's pre-hearing notice was filed out of time.[87] During substantive hearings, the petitioners called 24 witnesses and tendered documentary evidence, such as alleged conflicting INEC result sheets and forms, to substantiate claims of irregularities across multiple local government areas.[74] Respondents, including INEC and APC, countered with their own witnesses and arguments, asserting that any discrepancies were minor, did not vitiate the election, and that petitioners failed to meet the burden of proof under sections 135 and 136 of the Electoral Act, requiring demonstration of substantial impact on the overall result.[6] The tribunal admitted evidence on disputed polling unit forms but rejected broader claims of systemic fraud lacking corroboration.[87] On September 28, 2023, the three-member panel delivered its judgment virtually, resulting in a split 2-1 decision on key issues. The majority dismissed the petition as lacking merit, holding that the PDP failed to prove allegations of non-compliance or criminal conduct substantially affected the election outcome, thereby upholding Sani's victory in the overall tally.[87][6] However, the panel unanimously or by majority nullified results in 24 specific polling units across seven wards in four local government areasâ€”due to established over-voting and failure to use BVAS accreditation dataâ€”ordering INEC to conduct supplementary elections there within 90 days for approximately 16,300 registered voters.[74] This partial invalidation did not alter the declared winner, given the election's statewide scope and vote margin exceeding 200,000.[81] The ruling sparked conflicting interpretations: Sani and APC hailed it as a full affirmation of the electorate's mandate, emphasizing dismissal of the core petition, while Ashiru and PDP described it as a nullification of Sani's election, focusing on the supplementary order and vowing further appeals.[87] The tribunal's emphasis on evidentiary thresholds underscored that isolated irregularities, without proof of widespread subversion, do not warrant overturning an entire gubernatorial poll.[6]

Appellate Outcomes

The Court of Appeal, sitting in Abuja, affirmed Uba Sani's victory as governor on November 24, 2023, in a unanimous judgment delivered by Justice Obietonbara Daniel-Kalio, with Justices James Gambo Abundaga and Mohamed Baba Idris concurring.[88][89] The panel dismissed the five-ground appeal filed by Isa Ashiru of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), which alleged electoral malpractices including over-voting, result manipulation, and ballot stuffing, ruling that these claims were unmeritorious and unsupported by evidence.[88] It further held that the March 18, 2023, election substantially complied with the Electoral Act and reversed the tribunal's order for supplementary elections in 22 polling units across four local government areas, deeming the tribunal's assessment of evidence unreliable on that point.[89][88] The Supreme Court of Nigeria dismissed the PDP and Isa Ashiru's subsequent appeal on January 19, 2024, in a lead judgment by Justice Tijjani Abubakar, describing it as frivolous, vexatious, and devoid of merit.[83] The apex court affirmed the Court of Appeal's ruling, citing the appellants' failure to comply with pre-hearing notice requirements under the Electoral Act and their inability to substantiate allegations of malpractices, over-voting, result manipulation, and ballot stuffing.[83] This decision conclusively upheld Uba Sani of the All Progressives Congress (APC) as the duly elected governor, marking the final resolution of the litigation challenging the election outcome.[83]

Analysis and Implications

Electoral Integrity Assessment

The 2023 Kaduna State gubernatorial election occurred against a backdrop of national electoral challenges, including insecurity and operational lapses, yet judicial oversight affirmed the process's substantive validity. The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) declared Uba Sani of the All Progressives Congress (APC) the winner with 905,777 votes, against Isa Ashiru's 718,851 for the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), on March 18, 2023. PDP allegations of widespread rigging, including vote manipulation and non-compliance with electoral laws, prompted a petition to the Kaduna State Governorship Election Petition Tribunal. The tribunal dismissed the claims in September 2023, finding insufficient evidence of irregularities such as over-voting or falsified results, with witnesses presented by petitioners inadvertently corroborating the official tallies.[90] Appellate courts reinforced this assessment. The Court of Appeal in November 2023 upheld Sani's victory, ruling that PDP evidence failed to substantiate accusations of ballot stuffing, vote deduction, or mutilation of materials, emphasizing the lack of credible proof beyond speculation. The Supreme Court in January 2024 dismissed PDP's further appeal, noting procedural deficiencies in the initial petition, including time-bar issues, while not disturbing the lower courts' merits-based rejection. These rulings indicate that, despite partisan claims, no empirical dataâ€”such as mismatched BVAS records or forensic ballot analysisâ€”warranted nullification, distinguishing the Kaduna case from more contested national polls. Observer reports highlighted both advancements and flaws impacting integrity. The European Union Election Observation Mission (EU EOM) noted improved Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) functionality and result transmission (62-97% upload rate by March 19), enhancing transparency over the February presidential election, but documented organized violence in 21 states, including Kaduna-specific incidents of candidate attacks and intimidation that suppressed voter participation. Low turnout, mirroring Nigeria's 28.6% national average for governorship races, stemmed partly from banditry and fear in Kaduna's insecure areas, potentially skewing outcomes toward parties with stronger security alignments. While vote-buying and thuggery persisted regionally, EU EOM found no systemic collation failures in Kaduna sufficient to alter results.[91] Causal factors underscore partial integrity: Pre-existing insecurity, rather than deliberate fraud, likely drove apathy and uneven access, benefiting APC's incumbency advantages without proven collusion. Absent court-validated malfeasance and with technological safeguards mitigating some risks, the election's outcome aligned with verifiable voter preferences in accessible areas, though broader suppression eroded full democratic equivalence. Credible international monitoring, less prone to local partisan capture than domestic media narratives, supports this tempered validation over unsubstantiated rejection.[87]

Political Realignment Effects

The 2023 gubernatorial election victory of Uba Sani of the All Progressives Congress (APC) catalyzed a wave of defections from the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) to the APC, including prominent figures such as former Governor Mukhtar Ramalan Yero, Senators Shehu Sani, Suleiman Othman Hunkuyi, and Danjuma La'ah, as well as Representative Amos Gwamna Magaji and three members of the Kaduna State House of Assembly.[92] These shifts, occurring primarily in 2024 and 2025, reflected dissatisfaction with PDP's post-election performance and attraction to Sani's administration's focus on security and development, thereby bolstering APC's organizational strength despite retaining PDP control over three senatorial districts and eight of eleven National Assembly seats from the 2023 polls.[93] Subsequent by-elections in August 2025 demonstrated voter realignment, particularly in southern Kaduna strongholds traditionally aligned with PDP due to ethnic and religious dynamics. In the Chikun-Kajuru Federal Constituencyâ€”a PDP bastion since 2015â€”the APC's Felix Bagudu secured victory with 34,580 votes against PDP's 11,491, a margin of 23,089 votes, while APC candidates also dominated state assembly races in Zaria Kewaye and Basawa.[94][95] This marked a departure from 2023 patterns where PDP prevailed in urban southern local government areas like Chikun, signaling a pivot toward APC driven by governance outcomes including reduced out-of-school children numbers and infrastructure improvements.[93] These developments weakened opposition coalitions, such as one associated with former Governor Nasir El-Rufai, and fostered cross-ethnic support for APC by addressing southern marginalization through appointments like General Christopher Musa's as Chief of Defence Staff.[95][93] The realignment has positioned APC for potential dominance in the 2027 elections, with Sani's integration of defectors aimed at unifying the party amid internal rifts, though PDP anticipates reversals based on public sentiment over federal policies.[92]
