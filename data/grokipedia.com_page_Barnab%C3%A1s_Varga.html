Barnabás Varga

Barnabás Varga (born 25 October 1994) is a Hungarian professional footballer who plays as a forward for Nemzeti Bajnokság I club Ferencváros and the Hungary national team.

Club career

With 26 goals for Paks, Varga was the top scorer of the 2022–23 Nemzeti Bajnokság I season. On 1 June 2023, Varga moved to then defending champions, Ferencváros. On 27 July 2023, Varga scored his first European goal against Shamrock Rovers in the 2023–24 UEFA Europa Conference League. On 6 August 2023, Varga scored his first goal in the 2023–24 Nemzeti Bajnokság I season against Fehérvár FC at the Sóstói Stadion. On 27 August, he scored a hat-trick in a 6–1 victory over former club Paks in the fifth game day of the league season. He followed this up with another hat-trick in the club's next league match, a 6–2 win over Zalaegerszeg on 3 September.[1] On 22 October 2023, Varga was injured in a Nemzeti Bajnokság I match against Diósgyőri VTK. Ferencváros manager, Dejan Stanković, said that Varga paid for his matches in the national team. Therefore, he had to miss the UEFA Europa League match against K.R.C. Genk. In an interview with M4 Sport, Gábor Kubatov, the president of Ferencváros, said that he is happy that he had been convinced to purchase the top scorer of the 2022–23 Nemzeti Bajnokság I season. However, Kubatov was hesitating during the summer of 2023 to sign or not to sign Varga. In January 2024, Varga started training with the team after his injury. On 6 February 2024, Varga scored four goals in a 5–1 victory over arch-rivals MTK Budapest FC. After the match, Dejan Stanković, Ferencváros manager, said that Varga is a fascinating football player. In an interview, published on Nemzeti Sport, Varga said that scoring four goals was really beneficial for his soul after his long injury. On 20 April 2024, the Ferencváros–Kisvárda tie ended with a goalless draw at the Groupama Aréna on the 29th match day of the 2023–24 Nemzeti Bajnokság I season which meant that Ferencváros won their 35th championship. On 15 May 2024, Ferencváros were defeated by Paks 2–0 in the 2024 Magyar Kupa Final at the Puskás Aréna. On 3 October 2024, he scored against Tottenham Hotspur in the 2024–25 UEFA Europa League league phase match in Budapest. The match ended with a 2-1 defeat for Ferencváros. On 7 November 2024, he scored twice in a 2024–25 UEFA Europa League league phase match against FC Dynamo Kyiv. After the match, he was included in the 4th match day of the Europa League. He scored twice again in the next round of the league phase against the Swedish Malmö FF, bringing his goal tally to five goals in the first five games of the EL season. In the third qualifying round of the 2025–26 UEFA Champions League, Ferencváros drew 0–0 away to PFC Ludogorets Razgrad on 6 August 2025 before winning the second leg 3–0 at home on 12 August, with Varga scoring both of his goals in the 38th and 79th minutes.[2][3] Ferencváros advanced to the play-off round, where they faced Qarabağ FK. Varga scored the lone goal in a 1–3 home defeat on 19 August. In the return leg on 27 August, he added another from the penalty spot in a 2–2 draw, but Ferencváros were eliminated 5–3 on aggregate and dropped into the 2025–26 UEFA Europa League league phase.[4][5] In the Europa League league phase, Varga scored the winner with a header in a 1–0 victory at K.R.C. Genk on 3 October 2025.[6] On 23 October, he opened the scoring in a 3–2 win at Red Bull Salzburg.[7]

International career

On 27 March 2023, Varga made his debut as a substitute for the Hungary national team in a UEFA Euro 2024 qualifying match against Bulgaria, which Hungary won 3–0.[8] On 20 June 2023, in his second cap, he scored his first international goal in a 2–0 home win over Lithuania.[9] Varga added two more goals in qualifying, scoring the opener in 1–2 away and 2–1 home victories against Serbia in September and October 2023, respectively.[10][11] He netted again on 17 October 2023, heading Hungary's second goal in a 2–2 draw away to Lithuania.[12] These four goals helped Hungary finish second in Group G and qualify for UEFA Euro 2024. On 14 May 2024, Varga was named in Hungary's squad for UEFA Euro 2024.[13] On 8 June 2024, in a pre-tournament friendly, he scored twice as Hungary beat Israel 3–0 at the Nagyerdei Stadion.[14] Prior to the tournament, The Guardian included Varga among six "late bloomers" who could shine at the championship, alongside Robert Andrich, Jasir Asani, Abdülkerim Bardakcı, Álex Grimaldo, and Maximilian Mittelstädt.[15] At Euro 2024, Varga featured in all three group stage matches. He scored Hungary's only goal in a 1–3 loss to Switzerland on 15 June in Cologne.[16] Hungary drew 0–0 with Germany on 19 June, before Varga's tournament ended early in the 1–0 defeat to Scotland on 23 June, where he suffered a serious head injury after colliding with goalkeeper Angus Gunn during a free kick, resulting in unconsciousness and a fencing response. He was stretchered off after a 10-minute stoppage and later confirmed to be in stable condition.[17] Hungary finished third in Group A and were eliminated. Following recovery from facial fractures and surgery, Varga returned to international duty in the 2024/25 UEFA Nations League, where Hungary competed in League A Group 3 against Germany, the Netherlands, and Bosnia and Herzegovina. He made six appearances without scoring as Hungary placed third.[18] In the relegation play-offs in March 2025, Varga featured in both legs against Turkey, but Hungary lost and were relegated to League B.[19] On 10 June 2025, Varga scored in a 2–1 friendly defeat to Azerbaijan.[20] In the 2026 FIFA World Cup qualifiers, Varga opened the scoring in a 2–2 draw away to the Republic of Ireland on 6 September 2025.[21] Three days later, on 9 September, he netted twice in a 2–3 home loss to Portugal, including a header for the opener and an equalizer in the 84th minute.[22] On 14 October 2025, he played the full match in a qualifier against Portugal.[19]

Personal life

In an article published on Nemzeti Sport, Gábor Thury described Varga as a late bloomer. Varga was about to give up his career in his early 20s, when he received an offer from SV Mattersburg. Varga resides in a small village, Szentpéterfa, located close to the Austrian border. In an interview published on Nemzeti Sport, he said that when he retires he wants to spend his life there.
