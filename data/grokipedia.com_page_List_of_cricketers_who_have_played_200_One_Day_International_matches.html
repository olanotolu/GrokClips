List of cricketers who have played 200 One Day International matches

Allan Border of Australia was the first player to play 200 ODIs. Sachin Tendulkar of India has played the most matches (463). One Day International (ODI) cricket is played between international cricket teams who are Full Members of the International Cricket Council (ICC) as well as select Associate members with ODI status. Unlike Test matches, ODIs consist of one innings per team, having a limit in the number of overs, currently 50 overs per innings – although in the past this has been 55 or 60 overs.[1] ODI matches are a subset of List A cricket and so records and statistics are recorded both specifically for ODIs and within List A. The earliest match recognised as an ODI was played between England and Australia in January 1971;[2] since when there have been over 4,900 ODIs played involving more than 25 teams. The frequency of matches has steadily increased, partly because of the increase in the number of ODI-playing countries, and partly as the cricket boards of those nations seek to maximise their revenue with the increased popularity of cricket, a process that dates from the time of the Packer Revolution.[3] In ODIs playing 200 matches is considered to be a significant achievement. Allan Border of Australia was the first cricketer to have reached this landmark. Sachin Tendulkar of India has played in most ODIs, having represented India in 463 matches. As of October 2025, 86 cricketers have reached this landmark, including 16 from India, the most among all ODI-playing nations.[4]
