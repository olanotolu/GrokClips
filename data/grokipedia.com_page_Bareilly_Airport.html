Bareilly Airport

Bareilly Airport (IATA: BEK , ICAO: VIBY) is a domestic airport serving Bareilly, Uttar Pradesh, India at Indian Air Force's Trishul Air Force Base in Izzatnagar, activated for civilian operations under the UDAN regional connectivity scheme, located approximately 6 km (3.7 mi) north of the city centre.[2]

Bareilly Airport History

History

The Airports Authority of India approved construction of a passenger terminal at the Trishul Air Force Base.[3] The district administration bought 10 hectares (25 acres) of land from local farmers for the project.[4] The AAI began the tender process to award construction contracts for the airport in September 2017, and expected the civil enclave to be ready by May 2018, pending the approval from the Government of Uttar Pradesh.[5] However, the IAF requested changes in the layout of the taxiway connecting the terminal to the runway.[6] After the Ministry of Defence approved the taxiway, passenger service was hoped to begin by February 2019.[7] In September 2020, the Airports Authority of India announced that Bareilly Airport would begin operations under the Regional Connectivity Scheme - UDAN (RCS-UDAN).[2] The civil enclave was completed at a cost of â‚¹65 crore (US$7.7 million).[1] The airport was inaugurated on 8 March 2021 with the first commercial flight from Delhi operated by Alliance Air.[1]

Terminals

Terminal 1

The airport's first passenger terminal was inaugurated by the state civil aviation minister, Nand Gopal Nandi, and Union Minister, Santosh Gangwar, on 10 March 2019, at the air force base. The terminal building covers an area of 2,500 square meters, and can handle 150 passengers (75 arrival, 75 departure) during peak hours. Its new apron, measuring 95Ã—100 metres, provides parking space for two ATR-72 type aircraft at a time, as per IMG norms, and has a parking facility for 168 cars.[2][9]

Terminal 2

A new terminal building, next to the first terminal towards north, was inaugurated on 8 March 2021, as part of the airport expansion project. It is spread over 3,020 square metres and has a capacity to accommodate over 400 passengers (200 arrival and 200 departure) during peak hours. It has two baggage conveyor belts and six check-in counters. The dimension of Link Taxiway is 835 m x 18 m. Trishul Air Force Base, Bareilly

Trishul Air Force Base, Bareilly, one of the Indian Air Force's largest bases, is part of the Central Air Command (India). The base hosts No. 8 Squadron equipped with Sukhoi Su-30MKI fighters and No. 111 Helicopter Unit with HAL Dhruv helicopters.[11] It previously operated No. 102 Squadron's MiG-25 (Foxbat) reconnaissance aircraft, capable of flying up to 80,000 feet (24,000 m), until their retirement in 2006.[12] The base features an underground hangar.
