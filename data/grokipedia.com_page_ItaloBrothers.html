ItaloBrothers

ItaloBrothers is a German dance project from Nordhorn, Germany. The project originated in 2005 with vocalist Mathias Metten, producer Kristian Sandberg, and Zacharias Adrian. As of 2022, the core members are Mathias Metten, Kristian Sandberg, Hanno Lohse (formerly Cansis), and Dietmar Pollmann, with Zacharias Adrian as a past member. In January 2025, the team expanded to include Jasper Iske (from the DJ duo FrenchSisters) and Daniel Terwolbeck (a Hamburg-based content producer). They signed to Virgin Records in 2020. They are most successful in Scandinavia and Central Europe, but also have toured in Eastern Europe.[1] Their releases have appeared on multiple labels, including Zooland Records (their primary early label), Kontor Records, Ultra Music, and Sony Music Entertainment. Their most successful singles to date include: "Stamp on the Ground", "My Life Is a Party", "This Is Nightlife" and "Summer Air". Their latest single as of August 2025 is "Moonlight Shadow 2025".[2]

Music style

The general musical style of ItaloBrothers in their early years is comparable to Italian dance artists like Gigi D'Agostino and has influences of Italo dance, hands up and jumpstyle. They were also compared to Scooter.[3] In contrast to their initial Italo dance-focused sound, singles like "Cryin' In The Rain", "My Life Is a Party" and "This Is Nightlife" incorporated more commercial European dance music elements, including house and electro. This style can be compared to artists like R.I.O., 2010s releases of Manian and Cascada, and Mike Candys.[4][5][6] Over time, ItaloBrothers evolved their sound. From 2016 to 2021, they incorporated tropical house and deep house elements. This was followed by slap house influences in 2020â€“2022. Since 2022, their music has shifted toward hardstyle, as seen in recent releases like "Hardcore" (2024) and "Rave Eternally" (2025).[7][8]

Musical career

2005â€“08: Musical beginnings

The band was founded in November 2005, the three band members having already made music together since 2003.[9] Their first official release was "The Moon", an italo-dance track released in July 2006 on an EP by their label Zooland called Zooland Italo EP.[10] In 2007, they gained growing popularity with the release of "Moonlight Shadow", a cover version of the 1980 release of the same name by Mike Oldfield and a cooperation with German dance group Tune Up! called "Colours of the Rainbow". In 2006 and 2007, they also released various remixes of songs by other musicians for which they re-recorded the vocals and published them as "Italobrothers New Voc Rmx". Sometime in 2008, they released a cover of "[(I Just) Died In Your Arms]((I Just)_Died_In_Your_Arms "(I Just) Died In Your Arms")" by Cutting Crew and "Learning to Fly" by Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers, but those two songs were never released onto streaming services or CDs. Their next two releases, "Counting Down the Days" and "Where Are You Now?", established a fanbase throughout Europe.

2009â€“2010: Commercial success in Scandinavia

A new single, "So Small", which is a cover of the Carrie Underwood song of the same name, was published in November 2009. It is the only single that was directly available on CD. Another release, the digital Love is on Fire EP , was released in March 2010. The song "Stamp on the Ground", which was originally released in September 2009, was selected as official song for the Norwegian Russefeiring event 2010. The song was therefore re-released in Scandinavia and became a huge success, reaching the Top 20 in Norway[11] and Denmark.[12] The song also gained a chart position in Sweden[13] and in the dance charts of Switzerland[14] and Austria.[15] The song was certified 2Ã— Platinum in Denmark (2022).[16] Following the success of "Stamp on the Ground", an album called Stamp! was released in December 2010 which contains all to this point released singles and five new tracks. One of this tracks with the title "Upside Down" was said to be a copy of the Russian singer Reda's track "Ja budu s Taboj". Her label DaTa Music took proceedings against the further release of the track, but the end of this lawsuit is unknown.[17] A follow-up single called "Radio Hardcore" was released in December 2010 and was able to recreate the success of "Stamp on the Ground" in Denmark.[12]

2011â€“12: Change of music style and commercial success in Central Europe

In May 2011, a track named "Cryin' in the Rain" was published, that differs very much from the common music style as it is a ballad with House elements. However, there was also released an "IB HandsUp! Remix" featuring the common style. This release was seen as an experiment. For their next release, "Boom", they worked together with the rapper Carlprit. This track is again in their old style, but features dubstep parts while Carlprit is rapping. For the second time ItaloBrothers have produced the official Russ anthem, this time called "Pandora 2012"[18] which was released on 13 April 2012.

2012â€“2014: "My Life Is a Party" and singles

On 27 July 2012 the band released a single called "My Life Is a Party" which is a cover of the track "Dragostea din tei" by O-Zone from 2004. The radio edit of the song is still in typical ItaloBrothers style but the video features a remix by German dance band R.I.O., and was released on Kontor Records thus gaining a huge amount on YouTube views. The song entered the Top 20 in Austria,[19] and managed to reach the first chart positions for the band in their home country, Germany,[20] and in Switzerland, too.[21] The song also charted in France[22] and Belgium.[23] Another track named "This Is Nightlife", which contains elements of the song "Ecuador" by Sash! from 1997, was uploaded to YouTube on 13 March 2013[24] and was released as single two days later. The band changed their music style similar to the style of the video remix of their last single because it was much more successful than their old style. The song was able to recreate the success of the last single in Germany[20] and Austria[19] and additionally entered the Top 30 in Switzerland.[21] The band announced that they again produced a song for a Norwegian Russefeiring event, this time called "Luminous Intensity"[25] which was officially released on 19 April 2013. Italobrothers released the single "Up 'N Away" on 28 March 2014. On 28 November 2014, then they released another single "P.O.D." (People of DreamHack). Also, the single "Let's Go" Feat. P. Moody was released in December 2014.

2015-2017: "One Heart", "Summer Air" and singles

In January 2015 the single "One Heart" was released, ItaloBrothers worked with Floorfilla as well as rapper P. Moody for the track. The song embodies their first placement in the French single charts. After the song was released, Zacharias Adrian would leave ItaloBrothers and Hanno Lohse (aka. Cody Island) would soon join. April 2015 saw the release of "Springfield" with the ItaloBrothers working alongside Norwegian DJ Martin Tungevaag to produce the song. The track reached major success in Norway. In May 2015 the single "Welcome To The Dancefloor" was released. The single is a cover of "Rhythm Is a Dancer" by German eurodance band Snap! from the years 1992. They returned to their hands-up style with the song "Sleep When We're Dead", which was released in August 2015. In November 2015 a collaboration track with Danish singer Amalie SÃ¸lberg was published, called "Kings & Queens". With their track "Generation Party", released in July 2016 they released a song in a similar style as "Springfield". Later in summer 2016 the ItaloBrothers released the single "Summer Air", which became their biggest hit single yet. After a re-release in early 2017 the tropical-house/future-bass track reached high success in several Scandinavian countries as well as in Central Europe. In Norway they jumped on number 2.
