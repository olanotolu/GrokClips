List of countries by net international investment position per capita

The net international investment position (NIIP) per capita is the difference between a country's external financial assets and liabilities, divided by its population.[1] This list ranks countries by this metric, indicating net creditor or debtor status on a per-person basis.

List of creditor nations

This is a list of the top creditor nations of the world sorted by their net international investment positions (NIIPs) per capita. A creditor nation is a sovereign state that has a positive NIIP.[2] The table uses data primarily from 2015 (with some up to 2018), sourced from websites approved by the International Monetary Fund,[3] and includes Macau and Hong Kong because of their special economic statuses. Note: This data is outdated as of 2025; recent figures from sources like CEIC and national banks show significantly higher NIIP values and shifted rankings for many countries (e.g., Norway's NIIP per capita now exceeds $300,000). For current data, refer to IMF BOP/IIP statistics.[4] Population figures may list citizens only or total population, therefore ranking and figures may vary. The 6 Gulf Cooperation Council countries are widely considered to be creditor nations (and perhaps some of the largest ones), but because of Islamic sensitivities about credit and debt, they seldom report their external assets and liabilities.[5][6]

List of debtor nations

This is a list of debtor nations by net international investment position per capita. This is a list of debtor nations of the world sorted by their net international investment positions (NIIPs) per capita. A debtor nation is a sovereign state that has a negative NIIP, i.e. a country that has net external liabilities , NOT net external assets.[2] The table uses data from respective national government statistical agencies, Eurostat, or IMF. Though many do, a large portion of nations do not report data to the IMF. Disclaimer: Note that parallel reports from government or international agencies may report vastly different data. This list is incomplete and focuses on selected major economies; values are approximate and subject to revisions.
