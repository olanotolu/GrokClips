M-Sport

M-Sport is a motorsport engineering company headquartered at Dovenby Hall near Cockermouth, United Kingdom. It is primarily known for entering the FIA World Rally Championship (WRC) since 1997 in partnership with Ford, manufacturing race and rally cars, and providing parts and motorsport services to customers. The company has an automotive evaluation facility at its headquarters, and a second manufacturing facility in Kraków, Poland.[1][2][3] As of the 2025 season, the M-Sport Ford World Rally Team contests the World Rally Championship and the WRC2 support championship, with drivers including Grégoire Munster and Josh McErlean. M-Sport also organises, operates and acts as promoter of the Junior WRC championship on behalf of the FIA, with the 2025 season concluding on October 19, 2025. They also provide the official TOCA engine option used in BTCC since 2022.[4][5][6]

History

M-Sport began in 1979 when rally driver Malcolm Wilson formed Malcolm Wilson Motorsport, primarily to facilitate his own rallying career and offering motorsport contracting services to customers such as rally car preparation.[7] Reflecting the career of Malcolm Wilson as a driver, the company grew steadily through the 1980s.[8] Wilson and his wife Elaine formed a second company in 1987, M-Sport Limited (known as M. Sport Limited until 2019), trading and manufacturing rally car parts and components.[1] Following the withdrawal of Ford from motorsport entries in the mid-1990s, M-Sport were awarded the contract to operate and promote the Ford World Rally Team in the WRC from 1997 with Wilson as Team Principal. With his driving career effectively over, M-Sport assumed all trade of Malcolm Wilson Motorsport,[9] legally from 1 January 1998.[1] The business expanded over time to become a successful manufacturer of rally cars, sold to competitors worldwide.[10] In 2010, M-Sport established a subsidiary in Poland (M-Sport Poland Sp. z o.o.), which handles manufacturing of two-wheel-drive rally cars and supports global operations.[11] By 2021, they offered rally cars in all 5 tiers of the Rally Pyramid. Besides rally and the Ford partnership, the company also ran the official Bentley Motorsport outfit competing in the GT World Challenge Europe Endurance Cup between 2014 and 2019. In 2018, the company became technical partner to Jaguar, assisting in the build of the I-Pace eTrophy racecar used in the championship of the same name.[2] In 2020, M-Sport was selected to supply the new TOCA engine for the British Touring Car Championship (BTCC), beginning in 2022.[12] In 2022, M-Sport released the new Ford Puma Rally1 with hybrid powertrain for Rally1 category. Hybrid system will recover energy during braking and can be deployed via electric motor together with the internal combustion engine.[13][14]

Motorsport

World Rally Championship

Ford World Rally Team (1997–2012)

At the end of 1996, Ford Motorsport selected the company to spearhead the Ford team's entry for the 1997 World Rally Championship season with the Ford Escort WRC, a hastily built World Rally Car equivalent of the Group A car. Victories in Greece and Indonesia saw faith in the small team rewarded, and they went on to finish runner-up in the manufacturers' championship. By the middle of 1998, Ford chose M-Sport to design and build the new Ford Focus WRC. The new car appeared in 1999 with a new driver, Colin McRae, and immediately sprang to the attention of the world press by recording fastest stage times on its first event, the Monte Carlo Rally. However, the two cars entered were excluded from the event due to the use of an illegal modified water pump. By the third event — the Safari Rally in Kenya — the team had scored its first WRC victory. A month later, an on-form Colin McRae made it two in a row at the Rally of Portugal. With a move to new premises at Dovenby Hall, M-Sport expanded rapidly, employing over 170 people. In 2006, the Wilson-led Ford team took the manufacturers' World Rally championship title;[15] it was the first time Ford had achieved this feat in 25 years of competing. Since then, M-Sport Ford has secured three FIA World Rally Manufacturers' Championships (2006, 2007, 2017), over 70 victories, and more than 280 podiums as of 2025.[16] For the 2008 season, the company employed the driving talents of Mikko Hirvonen and Jari-Matti Latvala in their challenge for the World Rally Championship. The 2009 season saw Mikko Hirvonen and Citroën's Sébastien Loeb fight a closely fought battle throughout the year which ended with Loeb the victor by just one point.

M-Sport World Rally Team (2010–present)

Beside the Ford entry, the company also ran two cars on behalf of the M-Sport Ford World Rally Team.[15] Matthew Wilson and Henning Solberg were the two drivers competing in 2010. On occasions, a third car was run with a "guest" driver.

WRC Academy and Junior WRC

M-Sport organizes, operates, and acts as promoter of the Junior WRC championship on behalf of the FIA, providing Ford Fiesta Rally3 cars and support to drivers aged 18-26. The company also supported the WRC Academy program, which ran from 2013 to 2021 to nurture young talent.[19]

Other series and championships

M-Sport launched the one-make Fiesta Sporting Trophy rally series in 2006, which evolved into the Fiesta Sporting Trophy International (FSTi) and later the ERC Fiesta Rally3 Trophy, which it continues to operate as of 2025.[15][20][21][22] From 2014 to 2020, M-Sport prepared Bentley Continental GT3 cars for the Blancpain Endurance Series (now GT World Challenge Europe) and British GT Championship. M-Sport supplied Ford Fiesta Rallycross Supercars to Global Rallycross Championship teams Hoonigan Racing Division from 2013, and Chip Ganassi Racing and Bryan Herta Autosport from 2015. M-Sport, alongside Ford Performance and Hoonigan Racing Division, developed the Ford Focus RS RX, which competed in the 2016 FIA World Rallycross Championship. M-Sport has provided the official engine option for TOCA used in the BTCC since the 2022 season.[12][23]

Ford Fiesta S2000

At the beginning of 2009 Ford with M-Sport started work on a S2000 rally version of the new Ford Fiesta. The new car, designed by Christian Loriaux, was launched on 18 November 2009 at M-Sport's headquarters. The car made its public debut as a course car at the IRC Rally Scotland driven by Matthew Wilson. Ford did not run a works entry in the 2010 IRC but M-Sport did its own programme of events with Ford's support both on the technical and financial side.[24] M-Sport announced on 8 January 2010 that it was entering the IRC which means that Ford will be a registered manufacturer in the series.[25] Works Ford WRC driver Mikko Hirvonen gave the car its first competitive outing on the Monte Carlo Rally leading the event from start to finish.[26] A number of private entries were made in the 2010 Super 2000 World Rally Championship.
