Anweshaa

Anwesshaa (born Anwesha Dattagupta; 15 December 1993[1][2][3]) is an Indian singer and composer in Hindi, Bengali, Kannada, Tamil, Marathi and other Indian languages. She first appeared in the reality show Amul STAR Voice of India, Chhote Ustaad at the age of 14. As of 2025, she continues to release music and perform internationally, including a US tour.[4]

Biography

Anwesha attended Lycée School in Kolkata. She is the only child of Mita and Kushal Dattagupta. She was born in Virar, Maharashtra. When she was four, the family shifted base to their hometown in Kolkata. She went to school there. Later on, she completed her graduation from Amity University, Kolkata.[5][6][7]

Music career

Anwesshaa appeared on national television for the first time in the year 2007. Thereafter, she has been a part of various other shows. She started her career as a playback singer with the song "Ek Je Achhe Raja" in the Bengali film Khela (2008). Anwesshaa's first breakthrough in Bollywood was in Golmaal Returns for Pritam Chakraborty at the age of 14. She has recorded songs for many films including I Am 24, Raanjhanaa, Revolver Rani, Dangerous Ishq, Panchlait, I Am Banni, Kaanchi, Prem Ratan Dhan Payo, Expose, Gurudakshina, Do Lafzon Ki Kahani, Ye Kaisa Tigdam, Bansuri, I Love Desi, Love You Soniyo, Dhoop Chaav, Malhar and others. Apart from Hindi and Bengali, she has also made her debut with other Indian languages, including Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Gujarati, Marathi, Nepali, Rajasthani, Bhojpuri. Her playbacks are for over 75 films and TV serials across all languages to date. She has received honours like the Filmfare Award East for Best Playback Singer (Female).[1] In 2023, she composed music and background score for Marathi film Pahije Jatiche, making her debut in Marathi cinema.[2] In 2025, she released the album Rabindrasangeet For New Generation and the single "Moner Kone Uthlo Hawa" from Rakhbo Moner Majhe.[8][9]

Amul STAR Voice of India Chhote Ustaad

In 2007–08, she participated in the juniors version of the popular musical reality show Amul STAR Voice of India named Chhote Ustaad. Aishwarya Majmudar was winner and Anwesha became runner up in competition.[37]

After Chhote Ustaad

  • Anwesha recorded her first playback song for the Bengali film Khela, directed by Rituparno Ghosh, a Bengali film director, on 6 May 2008.[5]
  • Anwesha got her first break as a commercial playback singer in the film Golmaal Returns (Music Director – Pritam) where she sings for Amrita Arora/Kareena Kapoor.[38]
  • She sang the title song for the TV show Waar Parriwar by Gajendra Singh alongside Shaan.
  • Anwesha recorded a song with Planman Motion Pictures in the 2012 romantic comedy I M 24 with music director Jatin Pandit.[39]
  • Anwesha also participated in NABC-2009 held at San Jose, California where she delivered soulful numbers with Shaan

Awards and nominations

Anwesshaa has received numerous awards for her playback singing and performances. Below is a partial list:

Filmfare Awards East

  • 2013: Best Female Playback Singer – "Saradin Aar Sara Raat" from Rupkatha Noy[1]

Mirchi Music Awards

  • 2014: Best Female Vocalist (Upcoming) – "Nee Enagee" from Hottegagi Genu Battegagi (South)[43]
  • 2015: Female Vocalist of the Year – "Ekla Cholo Re" from Bojhena Shey Bohbena Returns (Bengali)[44]
  • 2024: Female Vocalist of the Year – "Sharodiya Sur" (non-film, Bengali)[45]

Other awards

  • 2009: Most Promising Young Talent – Big Music Award by 92.7 Big FM[5]
  • 2010: Best Contestant – Star Parivaar Awards (Music Ka Maha Muqabla)[46]
  • 2010: Best Female Playback Singer – Tele Samman Award (title track of Binni)[46]
  • Singer of the Series – Chhote Ustaad (2008)[5]
  • 2024: Best Folk/Regional Song – "Sukh Dukh" (Indian Music Awards)[47]
  • 2024: Best Versatile Singer of the Year – Bengal Icon Award[48]

Nominations

  • 2024: Top 5 in Best Gujarati Song – "Sukh Dukh" (Indian Music Awards)[49]
