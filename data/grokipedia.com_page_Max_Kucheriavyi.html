Max Kucheriavyi

Maksym Kucheryavyi (born 9 May 2002) is a Ukrainian professional footballer who plays as a central midfielder. A native of Kyiv, he progressed through local youth academies before being scouted to Scotland, where he signed a professional contract with St Johnstone in 2021, appearing in the Scottish Premiership and lower-tier loans such as to Brechin City and Kelty Hearts.[1][2][3] Kucheryavyi's tenure at St Johnstone, which extended via a contract renewal until summer 2025, featured limited first-team starts amid the club's struggles, culminating in his release as a free agent on 1 July 2025.[1][3] Internationally, he has earned caps for Ukraine's under-21 side, including a goal two minutes into his debut in 2022, and contributed to the under-23 team's Olympic qualifying efforts in 2024.[4][5] Amid the 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine, Kucheryavyi, who continued training with St Johnstone while supporting national causes, collaborated on fundraising initiatives to aid affected civilians in his homeland.[6] His career trajectory reflects resilience in adapting to professional football abroad during geopolitical turmoil, though standout senior achievements remain modest as of late 2025.[1]

Early life

Upbringing in Ukraine

Maksym Kucheriavyi was born on 9 May 2002 in Kyiv, Ukraine, to Ukrainian parents residing in the capital.[7][8] His family maintained strong ties to the city, where he spent his formative years amid a local environment rich in youth sports activities following Ukraine's 1991 independence, which fostered grassroots football programs across urban centers like Kyiv.[9][10] Kucheriavyi's early interest in football manifested through participation in neighborhood and school-based activities, leading to his enrollment at Kyiv's Youth Sports School No. 15 (DYuSSh-15), a local academy dedicated to basic technical and tactical training for young players.[11][4] At this institution, he underwent foundational development, including drills on ball control, passing, and positional play, in line with standard Ukrainian youth curricula emphasizing physical conditioning and team fundamentals before the age of 16.[12] This period laid the groundwork for his skills without formal professional scouting, reflecting the accessible nature of district-level football in post-Soviet Ukraine, where thousands of children annually joined similar academies supported by municipal funding.[5]

Youth career

Development and scouting

Kucheriavyi honed his skills at DYuSSh-15 Kyiv, a prominent youth sports school in the Ukrainian capital, where he joined at a young age and quickly distinguished himself despite his smaller stature among peers.[11][4] He contributed to successes in local competitions such as the Kyiv Cup and represented Ukrainian youth sides in international tournaments in Spain and Slovakia, earning recognition as the top Ukrainian U16 midfielder in 2018 for his performances.[12] In 2018, at the age of 16, Kucheriavyi was identified during a Kyiv youth tournament by Craig Levein, then manager of Scottish club Heart of Midlothian, who was in the city for the UEFA Champions League final and scouted local talent.[5] Levein extended an invitation for Kucheriavyi to train with Hearts in Edinburgh, marking his initial exposure to European professional environments.[5][12] From 2018 to 2020, Kucheriavyi shuttled between Kyiv and Scotland, participating in training sessions with Hearts while complying with FIFA's restrictions on international moves for minors, which limited his time abroad to short periods.[5][12] Although no contract materialized with Hearts following Levein's departure from the club in 2020, these trials facilitated his talent identification and paved the way for professional opportunities in Scotland.[5]

Personal life

Background and residence

Maksym Serhiyovych Kucheriavyi, whose patronymic indicates a father named Serhiy, was born on 9 May 2002 in Kyiv, Ukraine.[1] He developed through Kyiv's Youth Sports School No. 15 before relocating to Scotland at the end of 2020, aged 18, to join St Johnstone on a professional contract.[4] Kucheriavyi has resided primarily in the Perthshire region of Scotland since his move, tied to the location of St Johnstone's McDiarmid Park stadium.[1] This relocation occurred amid growing opportunities in Scottish football, though his Ukrainian roots remained prominent, with family members staying in Ukraine.[4] The 2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine heightened challenges for Kucheriavyi, who reported sleepless nights and direct communication with relatives facing disruptions in Kyiv and surrounding areas, yet he continued adapting to UK residency through his club commitments.[37][38] Following his release from St Johnstone on 1 July 2025 as a free agent, details of his ongoing residence in Scotland or elsewhere remain unconfirmed.[7]
