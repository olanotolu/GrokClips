2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election

The 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election was held on 18 March 2023 to elect the governor of Nigeria's Enugu State for a four-year term commencing 29 May 2023.[1] Peter Ndubuisi Mbah, the candidate of the incumbent Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) and former chief of staff to outgoing governor Ifeanyi Ugwuanyi, was declared the winner by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) with 160,895 votes, narrowly defeating Chijioke Edeoga of the Labour Party (LP) who received 157,552 votes; other candidates, including Uche Nnaji of the All Progressives Congress (APC) with 14,575 votes and Frank Nweke Jr. of the All Progressives Grand Alliance (APGA) with 17,983 votes, trailed significantly.[1][2] The election, marked by low voter turnout consistent with broader patterns in southern Nigeria's off-cycle polls, saw collation delays in local government areas like Nkanu East and Nsukka due to reported irregularities, including adjustments to vote tallies after verification with Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) data.[1][3] Post-election, Edeoga and Nnaji challenged Mbah's victory in court alleging fraud and non-compliance, but the Enugu State Governorship Election Petition Tribunal, Court of Appeal, and Supreme Court successively dismissed the petitions for lack of sufficient evidence, affirming Mbah's election.[4][5] The contest reflected PDP's entrenched dominance in Enugu amid a national Labour Party surge, with Mbah's win preserving the party's hold despite the tight margin that underscored competitive shifts in voter preferences.[1]

Electoral Framework

Voting System and Eligibility

The 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election employed a first-past-the-post voting system, under which the candidate receiving the plurality of valid votes cast statewide was declared the winner.[6] This plurality-based approach, stipulated in Nigeria's Electoral Act 2022, required no minimum vote threshold or runoff, with results determined by manual collation at polling units, wards, local government areas, and the state level following accreditation and voting.[7] The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) oversaw the process, mandating the use of the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) for biometric verification of voters via fingerprint or facial recognition prior to ballot issuance, a measure introduced under the Electoral Act to curb multiple voting and enhance integrity.[8] Voting occurred on March 18, 2023, after a nationwide postponement from March 11 due to logistical challenges with BVAS deployment and non-sensitive materials distribution.[9] Eligibility for candidacy as governor required candidates to be Nigerian citizens by birth, at least 35 years old, members of a registered political party sponsored by that party, and educated to at least Senior Secondary Certificate Examination (SSCE) level or equivalent.[10] Additional INEC requirements included submitting Form EC9 with evidence of party nomination, educational credentials, and tax clearance, with no convictions for offenses carrying imprisonment exceeding one year without a fine option in the preceding 10 years.[11] For voters, participation was restricted to Nigerian citizens aged 18 or older, resident in Enugu State, mentally sound, and possessing a valid Permanent Voter's Card (PVC) issued by INEC following continuous voter registration exercises concluded by June 2022.[12] INEC's regulations prohibited voting without BVAS accreditation matching the PVC, with polling limited to designated units between 8:30 a.m. and 4:00 p.m., extendable only for disruptions.[13]

Role of INEC and Regulations

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) served as the primary body responsible for organizing, supervising, and declaring results in the 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election, conducted on 18 March 2023 following a postponement from 11 March due to nationwide logistical deficiencies in non-sensitive materials distribution and personnel deployment.[9][7] INEC's mandate, derived from the Electoral Act 2022, encompassed voter registration verification, compilation of the state's voters' register (encompassing approximately 2.1 million eligible voters in Enugu), accreditation via the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) to prevent multiple voting, and manual collation of results from 17 local government areas upward to the state level.[14][15] INEC enforced regulations prohibiting electoral malpractices such as vote-buying, intimidation, and over-voting, with BVAS configured to capture biometric fingerprints or facial images for accreditation, cross-checked against the Permanent Voter Card (PVC) and voters' register.[16][7] Polling units operated from 8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m., extending if queues persisted, with results transmitted electronically via the INEC Result Viewing Portal (IReV) for public scrutiny post-uploading from polling units, though implementation faced technical glitches in some states including Enugu.[15][8] Collation followed a hierarchical process: ward to local government, then state headquarters, culminating in the declaration by the Enugu State Returning Officer, Professor Charles Ani of the University of Nigeria, Nsukka.[16] Under Section 28 of the Electoral Act 2022, INEC retained authority to postpone elections in cases of security threats or administrative failures, as exercised nationally for the 2023 off-cycle gubernatorial polls.[14] The commission also regulated party compliance, including nomination deadlines (set for 12 June 2022) and candidate substitution up to 6 March 2023, while deploying over 5,000 ad hoc staff and security personnel in Enugu to enforce secrecy of the ballot and access restrictions at polling sites.[17][7] Post-election, INEC's report highlighted a voter turnout of approximately 22% in Enugu, attributing low participation to apathy and logistics, with provisions for petitions to election tribunals within 21 days of declaration.[8][14]

Political Background

Historical Context and Incumbency

Enugu State, established on August 27, 1991, from the eastern portion of Anambra State, has experienced continuous governance by the People's Democratic Party (PDP) in the democratic era since 1999.[18] The state's first civilian governor under the Fourth Republic, Chimaroke Nnamani of the PDP, secured victory in the January 9, 1999, election, defeating the Alliance for Democracy candidate with a substantial margin reflective of PDP's early consolidation of power in the Southeast.[19] This pattern persisted through subsequent cycles: Sullivan Chime (PDP) won in 2007 and was re-elected in 2011, followed by Ifeanyi Ugwuanyi (PDP), who triumphed in 2015 with 753,837 votes against the All Progressives Congress (APC) candidate's 19,209, and again in 2019 with 750,772 votes per official Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) tallies, underscoring PDP's electoral hegemony amid weak opposition structures and regional loyalties.[20][21] Ugwuanyi's incumbency from May 29, 2015, to May 29, 2023, emphasized relative peace and incremental fiscal reforms, elevating the state's Internally Generated Revenue (IGR) from N18 billion in 2015 to higher figures by 2023, though its national ranking remained modest.[21] His administration initiated security measures like the Enugu State Forest Guards to curb forest-based crimes and invested in infrastructure, including rehabilitating facilities such as the Colliery Hospital into the Enugu State University of Medical Sciences Teaching Hospital and establishing the Enugu State University of Education.[22][23] Judicial enhancements featured modernized courtrooms and model customary courts, while public expenditure reforms aimed at efficiency.[24] Critics, however, noted limited transformative change in core metrics like IGR per capita or unemployment reduction over eight years, attributing stagnation to reliance on federal allocations and PDP's internal dynamics rather than bold innovation.[21] As a term-limited incumbent, Ugwuanyi's PDP retained structural advantages in the 2023 race, endorsing Peter Mbah as successor to leverage incumbency benefits like party machinery and voter familiarity, amid opposition fragmentation that historically favored PDP in Enugu's one-party-like landscape.[19] This continuity reflected causal factors such as ethnic solidarity in the Igbo-dominated Southeast and APC's internal divisions, which routed it in prior local contests, enabling PDP to maintain control without robust challengers eroding its base.[18][19]

Dominant Issues and Party Landscape

The political landscape in Enugu State ahead of the 2023 gubernatorial election was characterized by the longstanding dominance of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP), which had controlled the governorship since the return to democracy in 1999. This hegemony was sustained through the "Ebeano" political dynasty, a patronage network originating under former governor Chimaroke Nnamani and perpetuated by successors Sullivan Chime and Ifeanyi Ugwuanyi, fostering a near one-party system via clientelistic ties and resource allocation that influenced voter loyalty and party primaries.[25][26][27] The Labour Party (LP) emerged as the primary challenger, capitalizing on the regional momentum from Peter Obi's presidential campaign, which amplified anti-establishment sentiments in the Southeast and elevated LP's visibility beyond its traditional marginal role. The All Progressives Congress (APC), despite fielding Uche Nnaji as its candidate with promises centered on infrastructure and technology, operated on the periphery, hampered by PDP's entrenched networks and limited federal appeal in the state.[27][28] Dominant issues revolved around tackling pervasive insecurity, including sporadic attacks by "unknown gunmen" linked to separatist agitations and farmer-herder clashes, with PDP candidate Peter Mbah advocating technology-enabled security measures to restore stability. Economic revitalization was paramount, as candidates addressed high poverty ratesâ€”exacerbated by prior administrations' failures, according to LP's Chijioke Edeogaâ€”and pledged job creation, agricultural modernization, and GDP expansion, with Mbah targeting growth from $4.5 billion to $30 billion through private-sector-led initiatives in ICT and manufacturing. Infrastructure deficits, such as poor rural roads, inadequate water supply, and transport gaps, featured prominently, alongside commitments to education, healthcare, and youth empowerment to foster inclusive development.[29][30][31]

Primaries and Nominations

PDP Nomination Process

The People's Democratic Party (PDP) conducted its nomination process for the 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election through an indirect primary system, involving votes from party delegates selected at ward and local government congresses.[32] The process adhered to the PDP's national guidelines, which required aspirants to undergo screening by the party's national screening committee before proceeding to the primary election scheduled for May 25, 2022.[33] Prior to the primary, tensions arose over the delegates' list, leading to a May 6, 2022, interim injunction by an Enugu State High Court restraining the PDP from altering it, amid claims of irregularities in earlier congresses.[34] Incumbent Governor Ifeanyi Ugwuanyi played a pivotal role by endorsing Peter Mbah, a former state commissioner for budget and planning, as his preferred successor. On May 22, 2022, fifteen out of sixteen PDP gubernatorial aspirants from the Enugu East senatorial district signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) agreeing to support Ugwuanyi's choice, effectively withdrawing from the contest and paving the way for a consensus candidacy.[35] This political alignment, rooted in the PDP's zoning formula favoring Enugu East, reduced competition and minimized potential violence often associated with primaries.[33] On May 25, 2022, the primary election took place at the party secretariat in Enugu, where Peter Mbah secured the nomination with 611 votes from the delegates, while the remaining aspirants received zero votes due to their prior withdrawals.[32] Mbah's victory was declared unopposed in practice, reflecting the pre-primary consensus, and he was subsequently presented with the PDP flag as the party's candidate for the March 18, 2023, general election.[33] No significant legal challenges overturned the outcome, though the process highlighted the influence of incumbency and internal party dynamics in Nigerian state-level nominations.[34]

Labour Party Nomination Process

The Labour Party conducted its gubernatorial primary for Enugu State on August 4, 2022, selecting Chijioke Edeoga as its candidate.[36] Edeoga, who had previously contested the PDP's primary but lost, emerged unopposed after being the only aspirant to purchase and submit the party's expression of interest and nomination forms by the deadline.[36] [37] During the primary election, held in Enugu, Edeoga secured unanimous support from the 81 accredited delegates, reflecting the absence of competing candidates and consensus within the party's state structure at that stage.[37] This outcome aligned with the Labour Party's national guidelines under INEC regulations, which permitted primaries to proceed via direct or indirect methods but emphasized delegate-based selection for state-level contests.[38] The process faced immediate legal scrutiny from rival claimants alleging procedural flaws, including inadequate notice and non-compliance with party constitution.[39] In December 2022, a Federal High Court in Abuja nullified the primary, prompting an appeal.[39] The Court of Appeal subsequently reinstated Edeoga, ruling in his favor on grounds that the challengers lacked standing and the primary adhered to substantive requirements.[40] This decision was upheld by the Supreme Court on February 27, 2023, confirming Edeoga's candidacy ahead of INEC's final candidate list submission deadline.[41]

APC Nomination Process

The All Progressives Congress (APC) held its indirect primary election for the Enugu State gubernatorial candidacy on May 26, 2022, at the party's South-east Zonal Office in Enugu.[42][43] Uche Nnaji, a businessman originating from Akpugo in Nkanu West Local Government Area within Enugu East Senatorial District, presented himself as the sole aspirant after others either did not participate or withdrew prior to the vote.[44][42] With no competing candidates, Nnaji was acclaimed the nominee through a confirmatory ballot involving party delegates.[43][42] A total of 1,300 delegates were accredited to participate, with 1,100 votes cast: 1,070 valid votes in favor of Nnaji and 30 declared void.[42] The process proceeded without reported disputes or legal challenges at the time, aligning with the APC's national schedule for state primaries under the Electoral Act 2022 guidelines.[43][44]

Other Parties' Nominations

The All Progressives Grand Alliance (APGA) held its gubernatorial primary for Enugu State in June 2022, where former Minister of Information Frank Nweke Jr. emerged as the consensus candidate after securing the necessary votes in a process supervised by the party's electoral committee.[45] Nweke, who had previously served as a federal minister and spokesperson for the presidency, selected Dr. Princess Edith Ugwuanyi, a medical practitioner, as his running mate to balance the ticket with representation from Enugu North Senatorial District.[46] The Young Progressive Party (YPP) nominated Ugochukwu Edeh, a businessman and philanthropist, as its gubernatorial candidate through its internal selection process ahead of the election.[47] Edeh, born in 1987 and hailing from Enugu State, campaigned on promises of youth employment and industrial development, including building factories to generate 34,000 jobs.[48] Other minor parties also fielded candidates as cleared by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC). The Action Alliance (AA) nominated Ude Olisaemeka, while the African Democratic Congress (ADC) selected Nnaji Zimuzochukwu Michael.[49] These nominations followed standard party primaries or consensus mechanisms required under Nigerian electoral law, though specific details on contests or delegates for these smaller parties remain limited in public records. Additional parties, including the Social Democratic Party (SDP) and New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP), participated but garnered minimal attention and votes in the election.[49]

Campaign and Platforms

Major Candidates' Strategies

Peter Mbah, the PDP candidate, centered his campaign on an ambitious economic transformation agenda, promising to expand Enugu State's GDP from $4.4 billion to $30 billion within eight years through investments in infrastructure, agriculture, and technology sectors.[50] His strategy emphasized detailed policy blueprints outlined in his manifesto, including the construction of 260 smart schools, universal water supply within 100 days of inauguration, and youth empowerment programs to foster entrepreneurship and skills development.[51] Mbah leveraged the PDP's entrenched patron-client networks and the "Ebeano" political dynasty associated with outgoing Governor Ifeanyi Ugwuanyi to consolidate support in rural areas and among traditional stakeholders, while conducting extensive town hall meetings and media engagements to highlight verifiable past achievements as a banker and former commissioner.[52] Chijioke Edeoga, representing the Labour Party, adopted a grassroots mobilization approach, capitalizing on the national "Obidient" movement led by Peter Obi to appeal to youth and urban voters disillusioned with PDP dominance.[53] His platform focused on restoring integrity in governance, improving security, and promoting inclusive development, though he publicly downplayed the need for a formal manifesto, asserting that his legislative experience as a House of Representatives member provided sufficient guidance for effective leadership.[54] Edeoga's tactics included door-to-door canvassing and alliances with civil society groups to challenge alleged PDP overreach, positioning his candidacy as a revolutionary break from entrenched patronage politics.[55] Ugochukwu Nwoye of the APC pursued a platform advocating for alignment with federal policies under the incoming Tinubu administration, emphasizing anti-corruption measures, infrastructural renewal, and economic diversification, but his efforts were undermined by severe intra-party factionalism that fractured mobilization and resource allocation.[56] The APC's campaign in Enugu suffered from leadership disputes, limiting Nwoye's visibility and resulting in subdued rallies and minimal voter outreach compared to PDP and LP efforts.[57]

Key Debates and Events

The campaign period for the 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election included several public engagements, such as town halls and rallies, amid a competitive landscape dominated by the PDP's Peter Mbah and the Labour Party's Chijioke Edeoga. On January 31, 2023, the National Human Rights Commission convened a town hall meeting for gubernatorial candidates across 18 states, including Enugu, to discuss human rights commitments in their platforms, emphasizing electoral integrity and citizen protections.[58] Religious organizations also facilitated interactions, with the Diocese of Nsukka hosting town hall meetings with candidates starting November 25, 2022, to address community concerns on governance and security.[59] A notable televised debate occurred on February 1, 2023, organized by BBC Igbo, where Mbah and Edeoga, among others, outlined their visions, focusing on economic revitalization, infrastructure deficits, and tackling insecurity from unknown gunmen and communal clashes.[60] Mbah emphasized ambitious targets, including universal water access and GDP growth from $4.4 billion to $30 billion by 2030, positioning his campaign on private-sector experience and continuity with the PDP's "Ebeano" network.[31] Edeoga, leveraging national Labour Party momentum from Peter Obi's presidential bid, critiqued incumbency patronage and promised anti-corruption reforms and youth empowerment, appealing to voters disillusioned with PDP dominance.[27] Rallies marked key mobilization efforts, particularly for Mbah. On January 21, 2023, an Enugu East senatorial district rally saw cross-party stakeholders, including APC and PDP figures, endorse Mbah, highlighting zoning consensus favoring Enugu East after the prior governor's Enugu North tenure.[61] A larger PDP event on February 14, 2023, featured Mbah alongside presidential candidate Atiku Abubakar, where he reiterated pledges for poverty eradication and industrial hubs to address unemployment rates exceeding 30%.[62] Edeoga's Labour Party events drew crowds in rural areas like Isi-Uzo, emphasizing grassroots appeals against elite capture, though specific large-scale rallies received less documented coverage compared to PDP gatherings.[55] Persistent debates centered on senatorial zoning and power rotation, a constitutional norm in Enugu to ensure equity among Enugu North, East, and West districts, which both major candidates from Enugu East invoked to legitimize their bids while accusing rivals of subverting it through primaries.[63] Campaign discourse also highlighted infrastructure gaps, such as chronic water shortages and erosion, with Mbah proposing measurable targets like 260 liters of water per capita daily, contrasting Edeoga's focus on equitable resource distribution over technocratic promises.[64] No major inter-party violence disrupted events, though underlying tensions from patronage networks influenced voter mobilization.[27]

Election Administration

Timetable and Preparations

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) issued the official timetable and schedule of activities for the 2023 general elections, including gubernatorial contests, on 28 September 2022.[68] Key milestones encompassed the conduct of party primaries starting from 1 March 2022, submission of nomination forms online from 1 to 15 July 2022, publication of candidates' personal particulars on 22 July 2022, and finalization of the list of nominated candidates by 4 October 2022.[68] Campaigns officially commenced on 12 October 2022 and were required to end at midnight on 9 March 2023, with the election date set for 11 March 2023 but postponed to 18 March 2023 owing to delays in delivering non-sensitive materials such as ballot papers and result sheets across multiple states.[68][9] In Enugu State, political parties adhered to the federal timeline for primaries, with major contests occurring in late May 2022. The Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) held its gubernatorial primary on 25 May 2022, selecting Peter Mbah as its candidate after he secured a significant delegate vote margin.[33] The All Progressives Congress (APC) and other parties, including the Labour Party, conducted their primaries concurrently around the same date, amid debates over zoning and candidate eligibility.[69] INEC's preparations emphasized logistical readiness and technological safeguards, including the continuous collection of Permanent Voter Cards (PVCs) through to election day and the publication of the certified national voters' register on 15 February 2023 following verification exercises.[70] The commission trained over 1.6 million ad hoc staff nationwide and deployed Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) devices to all polling units for biometric verification, aiming to curb multiple voting.[8] In Enugu, regional stakeholder consultations, such as the South-East meeting on 13 January 2023, addressed potential disruptions by coordinating with security agencies on polling unit access and material distribution.[71] Despite these efforts, challenges like PVC collection rates below 50% in some areas persisted, contributing to lower projected turnout.[8]

Security and Logistical Challenges

The 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election on March 18 encountered significant logistical hurdles, primarily stemming from delays in the deployment of election materials and personnel. Only 5.11% of the state's 4,145 polling units opened by the scheduled 8:30 a.m. start time, with 90.59% commencing between 8:31 a.m. and 10:00 a.m., attributed to late arrivals of non-sensitive materials, insufficient vehicles from transporters amid fuel scarcity, and difficult terrain in rural areas.[8] These issues were compounded by drivers' hesitancy due to security threats and late configuration of Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) devices, which INEC linked to pre-election risks in the Southeast region.[8] Security challenges exacerbated these delays and disrupted voting in multiple local government areas (LGAs). Thugs attacked polling units in Udenu, Nkanu West, Nsukka, and Igbo-Eze South LGAs, using gunshots to scatter voters, carting away ballot materials, and snatching BVAS devices in at least 10 affected units, including six in Udenu alone.[8][72] These incidents disenfranchised over 10,000 voters across the affected areas, with specific disruptions reported in units like Umugwu Village Square (1,152 registered voters) in Udenu and CPS Ovoko (up to 2,378 voters) in Igbo-Eze South.[72] Clashes between party agents further heightened tensions, contributing to an environment of intimidation noted by observers.[73] INEC responded by canceling voting in disrupted units and suspending result collation in Nsukka and Nkanu East LGAs pending a review of violence reports, though the commission declined Labour Party requests for mandatory runoffs under the Electoral Act 2022, citing inconclusive outcomes in only select cases resolved via supplementary polls on April 15.[74][72] Despite inadequate security personnel at some sites, INEC described the overall process as largely peaceful, with no widespread fatalities but persistent risks from prior attacks on facilities, including a 2021 arson at its Enugu headquarters.[8]

Reported Irregularities

Allegations of Fraud and Rigging

The Labour Party (LP) candidate Chijioke Edeoga rejected the March 18, 2023, Enugu State gubernatorial election results announced by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) on March 20, alleging systematic rigging and electoral malfeasance that favoured the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) candidate Peter Mbah.[75] Edeoga claimed the poll was marred by over-voting and other irregularities, particularly in Nkanu East Local Government Area, where he asserted LP agents were denied access to collation centres and results were manipulated.[76] The LP further accused INEC of failing to upload results from polling units to the Bimodal Voter Accreditation System (BVAS) portal in real-time, enabling post hoc alterations, and described the outcome as a "theft of the people's mandate."[75] These claims echoed pre-election warnings from opposition parties, including the All Progressives Grand Alliance (APGA), which on March 3 alleged PDP plans to compromise BVAS and disrupt voting through violence or technical sabotage.[77] In response, the Enugu PDP campaign council denied any rigging, attributing Mbah's victoryâ€”160,895 votes to Edeoga's 82,232â€”to genuine voter preference across ethnic and senatorial zones, and dismissed over-voting allegations as fabrications already refuted by INEC's verification processes.[76] The PDP countered that its supporters faced militarization and suppression in strongholds like Nsukka Senatorial District, where LP allegedly benefited from undue interference.[76] APGA candidate Frank Nweke Jr. similarly rejected the results, aligning with LP's fraud narrative but without specifying additional evidence beyond general manipulation claims.[76]

Violence and Voter Suppression Incidents

Reports of violence during the March 18, 2023, Enugu State gubernatorial election primarily involved attacks on polling units and materials, disrupting voting in several local government areas (LGAs) and leading to the disenfranchisement of over 12,000 registered voters across at least 15 affected polling units. Incidents were concentrated in Udenu, Nsukka, Nkanu West, and Igbo-Etiti LGAs, where unknown gunmen and thugs targeted electoral officials and infrastructure, stealing ballot materials and halting proceedings. The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) acknowledged violence against its staff, suspending further collation of results in Enugu State on March 21, 2023, pending security reviews.[78][72] In Udenu LGA, gunmen attacked polling units including Umugwu Village Square, Amaukwu 1, Okpu Village Square, Nshama Village Hall, Uzoagu Village Hall, and Agudele Village Hall around 2:45 p.m., seizing materials and detaining officials until approximately 1:34 a.m. the following day, as documented in reports from presiding officers Wilfred Abigail Ifeyinwa and Ugwu Daniel Onyebuchi submitted to INEC. This affected 5,864 voters in these units alone. Similarly, in Nsukka LGA, thugs burned electoral materials at Lejja Polling Unit 001 after partial voting, impacting around 600 voters, while disruptions in Amadim Village Square, Obuiyi 2 Idi, and Itako Idi prevented voting for 1,144 others.[72] Voter suppression extended to Nkanu West LGA, where polling at Primary School Ogbeke and Amakpu Village Hall was aborted due to thug incursions, disenfranchising 1,395 voters, and Igbo-Etiti LGA's CPS Iheaka 1, CPS Ovoko (Ajuona Ward 001), and CPS Ovoko (Ajuona Ward 004), affecting 5,326 individuals amid material theft and intimidation. INEC canceled elections in these units citing security threats but did not conduct supplementary polls as mandated by Section 52 of the Electoral Act 2022, a decision contested by the Labour Party (LP) candidate Chijioke Edeoga in a March 24, 2023, letter to INEC's chairman and later in tribunal petitions alleging the disruptions favored the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP). Voter testimonies, such as from Uchechukwu Eze in Nsukka, described armed men chasing away queues and destroying BVAS machines, contributing to an environment of fear that observers like the International Institute for Democracy and Electoral Assistance noted in Enugu alongside other states.[72][79] These events occurred against a backdrop of pre-election tensions, including the February 23, 2023, killing of a senatorial candidate in Enugu, though not directly tied to gubernatorial polling. No fatalities were reported specifically during the gubernatorial vote in Enugu, unlike national tallies of 28 deaths across 2023 polls, but the localized disruptions narrowed PDP candidate Peter Mbah's declared 3,345-vote margin over Edeoga, prompting claims that the affected votersâ€”predominantly in LP strongholdsâ€”could have altered outcomes if accommodated. Tribunal proceedings dismissed broader non-compliance allegations, including violence, for lack of sufficient proof of substantial impact, though INEC's failure to rerun highlighted logistical and security lapses.[80][81]

Official Results

Statewide Tally

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) declared Peter Ndubuisi Mbah of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) the winner of the 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election on 22 March 2023, following collation of results from the state's 17 local government areas. Mbah received 160,895 votes, narrowly defeating Chijioke Edeoga of the Labour Party (LP), who polled 157,552 votes, a margin of 3,343 votes.[1][2][82] Mbah met the constitutional requirement of securing the highest number of votes and at least 25% of votes in two-thirds (11) of the local government areas.[83] Other major candidates trailed significantly: Frank Nweke Jr. of the All Progressives Grand Alliance (APGA) obtained 17,983 votes, while Uche Nnaji of the All Progressives Congress (APC) garnered 14,575 votes.[83] The total valid votes cast were approximately 361,000, reflecting a turnout of about 18% from the 1,995,389 registered voters, amid reports of disruptions in collation, particularly in Nsukka and Nkanu East local government areas, which delayed the final announcement.[84][2]

Breakdown by Senatorial Districts

In the Enugu North Senatorial District, comprising Igbo-Etiti, Igbo-Eze North, Igbo-Eze South, Nsukka, Udenu, and Uzo Uwani local government areas, Chijioke Edeoga of the Labour Party secured victories in all six LGAs, thereby winning the district. The Enugu East Senatorial District, which includes Enugu East, Enugu North, Enugu South, Isi-Uzo, Nkanu East, and Nkanu West LGAs, saw Peter Mbah of the Peoples Democratic Party prevail overall, despite Edeoga's win in Enugu North LGA; Mbah captured the remaining five LGAs in the district. In the Enugu West Senatorial District, encompassing Anambra East, Aninri, Awgu, Ezeagu, Oji River, and Udi LGAs, Mbah dominated with wins in five LGAs, while Edeoga took Oji River, ensuring Mbah's district victory. Mbah's margins in Enugu East and West offset Edeoga's strong showing in Enugu North, leading to Mbah's statewide win with 160,895 votes to Edeoga's 148,923.

Breakdown by Local Government Areas

![2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election results by LGA][center] The 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election results were collated from the state's 17 local government areas (LGAs), with polling unit results aggregated at ward and LGA levels before state-level declaration by INEC.[84] Peter Mbah of the PDP secured victories in key LGAs in the Enugu East and Enugu West senatorial districts, including urban centers like Enugu North, Enugu South, and Nkanu areas, contributing to his statewide margin.[1] Chijioke Edeoga of the LP prevailed in several LGAs within the Nsukka senatorial district, reflecting stronger support in northern parts of the state.[85] Provisional results after collation from eight LGAs showed a near tie, with each major candidate winning four LGAs, underscoring the competitive nature before final tallies favored PDP.[85] In Udi LGA, PDP received 13,633 votes while LP garnered 10,109, APC 1,648, and APGA 1,734.[86] LP raised concerns over results in Nkanu East LGA, alleging overvoting with 33,975 total votes cast exceeding accredited voters, though INEC upheld the figures in collation.[87] The geographic distribution highlighted ethnic and zonal dynamics, with PDP dominance in southern and central LGAs contrasting LP gains in Nsukka-zone areas like Nsukka, Udenu, and Igbo-Eze North.[88] Official INEC forms from polling units (Form EC8A) formed the basis for LGA tallies, uploaded partially to the IReV portal for transparency, though full LGA aggregates were primarily handled at collation centers.[89] Despite these, opposition claims of discrepancies in PDP-stronghold LGAs persisted, later addressed in tribunal proceedings.[90]

Post-Election Litigation

Tribunal Proceedings

Chijioke Edeoga, the Labour Party candidate, filed a petition challenging Peter Mbah's declaration as the winner of the March 18, 2023, Enugu State gubernatorial election, naming Mbah, the Peoples Democratic Party, and the Independent National Electoral Commission as respondents.[91] The petition, lodged within the 21-day statutory period following the election, alleged substantial non-compliance with the Electoral Act 2022, including over-voting in multiple local government areas, and questioned Mbah's eligibility on grounds of certificate forgery related to his National Youth Service Corps exemption certificate.[92] [93] The Enugu State Governorship Election Petition Tribunal, chaired by Justice Kudirat Murayo Akano, commenced pre-hearing sessions in May 2023, streamlining issues to focus on the core allegations of electoral malpractices and candidate disqualification.[94] Edeoga presented evidence through 30 witnesses, including claims of discrepancies in voter registers and ballot forms indicating over-voting exceeding 25% of accredited voters in affected polling units, while intending to call up to 36 witnesses on the forgery issue but ultimately closing his case in July 2023.[95] Respondents countered with documentary evidence from INEC affirming compliance and Mbah's valid qualifications, arguing that the petitioner failed to meet the burden of proof under Section 135 of the Electoral Act.[91] Parties adopted final written addresses on August 16, 2023, after which the tribunal reserved judgment.[96] On September 21, 2023, the three-member panel delivered a unanimous ruling dismissing the petition for lacking credible evidence to substantiate the claims of over-voting or forgery, holding that isolated irregularities did not vitiate the overall election outcome and that Mbah met all constitutional requirements for office.[97] [98] Edeoga's counsel rejected the verdict, alleging procedural flaws, though the tribunal's decision emphasized the petitioner's inability to demonstrate widespread non-compliance sufficient to nullify Mbah's 160,182-vote margin.[99] [93]

Appellate Court Rulings

The Court of Appeal, sitting in Lagos, delivered its judgment on November 10, 2023, unanimously affirming the election of Peter Mbah of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) as governor of Enugu State and dismissing the appeal filed by Chijioke Edeoga of the Labour Party (LP).[100][101] The three-member panel, led by Justice Jummai Sankey, held that Edeoga failed to prove non-compliance with the Electoral Act 2022, lack of valid votes, or that Mbah was not qualified to contest, resolving all issues against the appellant for lacking merit and upholding the Enugu State Governorship Election Petition Tribunal's earlier dismissal of the petition.[102] Edeoga had challenged Mbah's March 18, 2023, victory on grounds including alleged over-voting, certificate forgery, and electoral malpractices, but the appellate court found no evidence warranting nullification of the results declared by the Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC).[103][104] Subsequently, the Supreme Court, in a unanimous decision on December 22, 2023, dismissed Edeoga's final appeal and affirmed the concurrent findings of the tribunal and Court of Appeal, declaring Mbah's election valid.[4][105] A five-member panel led by Justice Inyang Okoro ruled that Edeoga did not substantiate claims of electoral fraud against INEC or Mbah, emphasizing that allegations of irregularities required concrete proof beyond mere assertions, and thus upheld Mbah's mandate as governor.[106][107]

Outcomes and Analysis

Voter Turnout Statistics

The 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election recorded a voter turnout of 18 percent, among the lowest in the country and tied with Abia State.[3] This figure reflects total votes cast numbering 353,463 out of approximately 2,112,793 registered voters statewide.[3][108] Of the registered voters, 1,995,389 (94.4 percent) had collected their permanent voter cards prior to the election.[108] Turnout varied significantly across Enugu's 17 local government areas, with urban centers like Enugu North and Enugu South generally exhibiting higher participation rates compared to rural districts such as Uzo-Uwani and Isi-Uzo, though specific LGA-level accredited voter figures were not aggregated in official statewide summaries.[3] The low overall participation aligned with broader trends in southern Nigerian states during the March 18, 2023, polls, where voter apathy, logistical challenges, and security concerns contributed to subdued engagement, in contrast to higher turnouts exceeding 40 percent in select northern states like Kano and Jigawa.[3] INEC deployed biometric voter accreditation systems (BVAS) across 4,145 polling units, facilitating the recording of accredited voters, though exact statewide accreditation totals beyond the votes cast were not publicly detailed in collation reports.[8]

Long-Term Political Implications

The 2023 Enugu State gubernatorial election reinforced the dominance of patron-client networks within the People's Democratic Party (PDP), particularly the Ebeano political dynasty originating from former Governor Sullivan Chime, which facilitated Peter Mbah's victory through established patronage structures and voter mobilization tactics.[27] This continuity marginalized emerging opposition from the Labour Party (LP), whose candidate Chijioke Edeoga garnered significant support amid national LP momentum but failed to disrupt PDP's localized control, highlighting the resilience of personality-driven alliances over ideological shifts in Enugu's electoral dynamics.[52][109] Mbah's subsequent defection from the PDP to the All Progressives Congress (APC) on October 14, 2025, represents a pivotal long-term realignment, driven by the PDP's diminished national influence following its 2023 presidential loss and the strategic imperative to align with the federal ruling party for resource allocation and political inclusion.[110][111] This move has elicited endorsements from local stakeholders, including town union leaders, positioning Enugu for enhanced federal partnerships in infrastructure and economic initiatives, such as improved access to budgetary allocations under President Bola Tinubu's administration.[112][113] The defection undermines the PDP's traditional stronghold in the Southeast, potentially fragmenting its base ahead of the 2027 elections and encouraging similar pragmatic defections among Igbo politicians seeking mainstream national engagement rather than regional isolationism.[111][114] Advocacy groups have already mobilized cross-party support for Mbah's re-election bid, citing his administration's developmental recordâ€”including infrastructure stridesâ€”as justification for sustained leadership under the APC banner.[115] This evolution signals a broader causal shift from PDP-centric loyalty to outcome-oriented federal alignment, which could elevate Enugu's role in national politics while exposing vulnerabilities in opposition structures reliant on ethnic mobilization.[116]
