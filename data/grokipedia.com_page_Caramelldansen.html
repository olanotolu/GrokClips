Caramelldansen

Caramelldansen (Swedish for 'The Caramell Dance') is the first track from Swedish music group Caramell's second and final album Supergott released on 16 November 2001. It became an Internet meme in the mid-2000s after a sped-up version of the song was attached to a video loop from the Japanese visual novel Popotan . The meme originated on the Japanese video-sharing site Nico Nico Douga in 2007, where users paired the sped-up song with the looping animation, leading to its global spread via YouTube. This version of the song was officially released in 2008 in Sweden as part of Supergott (Speedy Mixes) and in Japan as "U-u-uma uma" (ã‚¦ãƒƒãƒ¼ã‚¦ãƒƒãƒ¼ã‚¦ãƒžã‚¦ãƒž(ã‚œâˆ€ã‚œ)), which charted on Oricon and won the 23rd Japan Gold Disc Award for Single of the Year in the Western Music category. A virtual group called Caramella Girls was launched to promote the song, renditions in other languages, and other cover songs and original songs.

Internet phenomenon

The meme started as a fifteen frame Flash animation loop showing Mai and Mii, characters of the Japanese visual novel Popotan, doing a hip swing dance with their hands over their heads to imitate rabbit ears, and the chorus of a sped up version of the song.

Background

Popotan first appeared as a Japanese PC game on 12 December 2002. After the anime was aired from 17 July to 2 October 2003, short GIF animations clips were created from the opening of the anime and posted on the internet. The clips were matched with various songs, with titles ranging from "Popotan dance" to "Sexy bunny dance".[1] In early 2006, a sped-up version of the song was posted by a DJ named Speedycake to 4chan's /b/radio board. According to an interview with Ruakuu, Speedycake said the speed-up came from a mixing mistake while transitioning the "Caramelldansen" song to a faster BPM, and it ended up being "squeaky and high pitched", but that people were requesting for it anyway. Later that year, its chorus part was combined with the animation loop and posted to 4chan by a "Sven from Sweden".[1] As the video and song clip gained popularity, it became a meme. Artists and fans started to copy the animation and include other characters performing the dance. Its boom began at the end of 2007 in Japan (known as the "Uma uma Boom") where an explosion of different Caramelldansen iterations appeared in the Japanese video-sharing site Nico Nico Douga.[2] The meme soon after spread to YouTube and became a global phenomenon. Lore SjÃ¶berg wrote in a Wired magazine article about how Flickr users "look down from Flickr Hills into YouTube Chasm and see wailing, gnashing of teeth, and endless versions of "Caramelldansen" and they are sore afraid."[3]

Popularity

While the group Caramell had disbanded in 2002, the group's music started to spread widely across the Internet thanks to the popularity of this Internet meme. I like it. It's very funny."[4] The meme is not limited to the small Flash animation loops. 3D animation shorts have been released performing the dance, and live action videos made by fans. Japanese music distributor Exit Tunes gained the rights from the original Caramell producers, Remixed Records, to distribute the sped-up version of the original song in Asia, releasing first an album in April 2008 called Uma Uma Dekiru Trance wo Tsukutte Mita which included "Caramelldansen" (named "U-u-uma uma" (Speedycake Remix)) and other popular meme songs at the time. Toromi, the voice actor who voiced Mii in Popotan , also covered the song on a single Toroâ˜†Uma.[10] There was also an official single "U-u-uma uma" release[11] Uma Uma Dekiru Trance reached number 20 on the Oricon charts and stayed on for 16 weeks;[12] and the "U-u-uma uma" single reached number 16 and stayed 14 weeks.[13] In March 2009, it was awarded Single of the Year (International) at Recording Industry Association of Japan's 23rd Japan Gold Disc Awards.[14][15] Remixed Records released the sped-up version of the original Supergott album on Apple's iTunes Store; the album was called Supergott Speedy Mixes. In Japan, this was titled U-u-uma-uma SPEED with the song titles completely rewritten with emoticons. Speed reached number 48 on Oricon and stayed 5 weeks.[16] Remixed Records also released a set of Caramelldansen Speedy Mixes. On 17 September 2008, they released an English version of the song called "Caramelldancing".[17][18] A German version of the song, "Caramelltanzen", was released on 15 April 2009.[19]

Caramella Girls is a virtual band created by Remixed Records in 2008 to promote the "Caramelldansen" song. They first appeared in the Japanese release "U-u-uma uma" single on 21 May 2008 as two anime character counterparts for the two female vocalists Malin SundstrÃ¶m and Katia LÃ¶fgren, removing the rest of the band members.[11] They were then redesigned to be a girl group of three virtual girl characters â€“ Mindy, Nadine, and Vera. At some live events, they would appear in masks and costumes.[30][31] Remixed Records has further rebranded all related releases on music platforms from Caramell to "Caramella Girls".[32][33][34] On 18 March 2011, Caramella Girls released the song "Boogie Bam Dance", available in English, Spanish, and German.[35] In October 2012, the band released the Caramelldancing Remixes EP, which features remixes of the English version of the Caramelldansen song by Crazy 1, No Trixx, and DJ Triplestar.[36] Caramella Girls has since released other songs and videos. Some of these were written by Kim Andre Arnesen and Kristian LagerstrÃ¶m; and some are covers of other bubblegum pop tunes.[37] In 2020, they released a digital compilation album (without "Caramelldansen") called Sweet Decade.[37] The group has remained active, releasing new singles and EPs through 2025, including "Halloween" and "Tutti Frutti Lover" in 2023, "Melody", "Mamma Mia", and the Caramelldansen Rock EP in 2024, and "Mr PopIt" and "Bailando" in 2025.[38]

Use in other media

In July 2009, the Taiwanese gaming company Gamania launched an advertising campaign featuring the "Caramelldansen" song and dance for the Japanese version of its online game Lucent Heart.[39] "Caramelldansen" has been used in Japanese arcade games, including as a licensed song in the 2013 edition of Dance Dance Revolution.[40] A rhythm game based on the dance, Caramella Girls, was released by Remixed Records Games on iOS on 18 November 2009.[41] Entertainment group LoadingReadyRun performs the dance for their Desert Bus for Hope charity fundraisers most years, sometimes multiple times during a single run.[42] In August 2010, the characters in the American Disney Channel program Phineas and Ferb perform the dance in the episode "Summer Belongs to You" when they stop by Tokyo.[43][44] The dance has also appeared as a purchasable "Cat Ear Dance" emote in the video game Destiny 2, added in 2021.[45] In the World of Warcraft "Mists of Pandaria" expansion pack, the choreography for the female Pandaren dance emote is derived from this meme.[46] In 2025, Caramella Girls released remastered versions of "Caramelldansen" in four languages, contributing to its revival on social media platforms like TikTok.[47]
