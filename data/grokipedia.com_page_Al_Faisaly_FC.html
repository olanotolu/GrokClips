Al Faisaly FC

Al-Faisaly KSA FC (Arabic: نادي الفيصلي) is a Saudi Arabian football club based in Harmah.[2] the club currently competes in the Saudi First Division League, the second tier of Saudi football.[3][4]

History

The club is named after King Faisal (r. 1964–75), who was crown prince at the time of the club's founding.[5] Al Faisaly finished as the runner-up in the 2005–06 first division season, thus becoming the first club from the Sudair region to achieve promotion to the top flight but went straight back down after just one season. Al Faisaly achieved promotion for the second time to the top flight by winning the 2009–10 first division title, and competed in the Saudi Professional League until they were relegated in the 2021–22 season after twelve consecutive seasons in the top flight. Following relegation, Al-Faisaly finished 5th in the 2022–23 Saudi First Division League. They improved to 4th place in 2023–24 but lost in the promotion playoffs to Al-Qadsiah. In the 2024–25 season, the club ended 14th.[6] During the 2017–18 season, Al Faisaly managed to reach the 2018 King Cup Finalfor the first time in their history, but fell short to Al Ittihad, losing 3–1 in extra time.[7] Al Faisaly reached their second King Cup Final in four years, but this time they managed to win 3–2 against Al Taawon in the 2020–21 King Cup edition with Júlio Tavares scoring a hat-trick to secure their first ever top flight title, as well as their first AFC Champions League qualification. Al Faisaly also qualified to the 2021 Saudi Super Cup after winning their first ever King Cup title on 27 May 2021.[8] Al-Faisaly was defeated in the super cup by Al-Hilal 4–3 on penalties after a 2–2 draw.

AFC Champions League debut

The club's debut campaign in the 2021–22 AFC Champions League (group stage held in 2022) was notable, as Al Faisaly pulled off a surprising 2–1 upset win over Qatari giants and two-time AFC Champions League winners Al Sadd. Al Faisaly topped Group E with 2 wins, 3 draws, and 1 loss, advancing to the round of 16 against Iranian side Foolad. However, the club lost 1–0 on aggregate (0–0 away, 0–1 home) and was eliminated.
