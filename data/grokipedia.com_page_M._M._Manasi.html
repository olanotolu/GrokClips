M. M. Manasi

M. M. Manasi is an Indian playback singer trained in Hindustani vocals[1] and also a dubbing artist. She has sung more than 170 songs in Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Hindi, and English. Her hits in Tamil include "Stylish Thamizachi" in Aarambam, "Kattikeda" in Kaaki Sattai, "Semma Mass" in Massu Engira Masilamani, "Robo Romeo" in Tamizhuku En Ondrai Azhuthavum, "Sottavaala" in Puli, "Aatakari Maman Ponnu" in Ilayaraja's 1000th film Tharai Thappattai[1] and "Ranjithame" in Varisu. She won the SIIMA Award for Best Female Playback Singer (Telugu) for "Rangamma Mangamma" from the 2018 Tollywood period drama film Rangasthalam.

Early life

Manasi was born to Tamil-speaking parents in Mumbai on 8 January 1993, where she was raised before moving to Chennai with her family to pursue music. At the age of 2, she started learning music. Her first public performances were bhajans sung during Navratri and Ganpati poojas. She completed her studies at the School of Management at D.G. Vaishnav College. She has a sister, M. M. Monisha, who is also a playback singer.

Career

Manasi was introduced by Ilaiyaraja to the Kannada music industry for the movie Drishya and by Vidhyasagar in Malayalam for Oru Indian Pranayakadha. She made an entry into Bollywood with "Daddy Mummy" in Bhaag Johnny for music director Devi Sri Prasad. This song has garnered over 364 million views on YouTube as of October 2025.[2] Apart from singing, Manasi has also done voice acting for leading heroines in the Tamil film industry. These include Baahubali , Thozha , Dharmadurai , Devi for Tamannaah Bhatia, Maari for Kajal Agarwal, Anjaan for Samantha, Kamalinee Mukherjee in Iraivi , Meaghamann for Hansika Motwani, Darling and Velainu Vandhutta Vellaikaaran for Nikki Galrani, Tamizhuku En Ondrai Azhuthavum for Bindhu Madhavi, Idharkkuthane Aasai Pattai Balakumara for Swathi, Thirumanam Enum Nikkah for Nazriya, and Oh My Kadavule for Ritika Singh.[3] She provided the voice for Ritika Singh in Oh My Kadavule. In recent years, Manasi dubbed for Aishwarya Rai Bachchan in the Kannada versions of Ponniyin Selvan: I (2022) and Ponniyin Selvan: II (2023). She also sang "Semmannu Thaane" for the 2025 film Aghathiyaa.[4] Manasi has sung in Telugu and has many charting songs to her credit, like "Bhelpuri" in Aagadu , "Notanki" in Power , "Private Party" in Sarrainodu , "Gunde Aagi Pothande" in Shivam , "Meenakshi" in Masala , "Om Sarvani" in Legend , "Mara O Mara" in Tadakha , "Lucky Lucky Rai" in Balupu , and "Rangamma Mangamma" in Rangasthalam.
