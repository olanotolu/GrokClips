Aoba Fujino

Aoba Fujino (藤野 あおば, Fujino Aoba ; IPA: [ɸɯʑino aꜜoba]; born 27 January 2004) is a Japanese professional footballer who plays as a forward for Women's Super League club Manchester City and the Japan national team.

Fujino's Club Career

Club career

Fujino started her youth career at Minamiosawa FC before joining Nippon TV Menina, the youth academy of Tokyo Verdy Beleza. Unable to progress through the youth ranks, she left the team and instead went on to Jumonji High School. She rejoined Tokyo Verdy Beleza upon her graduation from high school.[1][2] Fujino made her WE League debut on 16 October 2021.[3] On 2 August 2024, she signed a three-year deal with Women's Super League side Manchester City.[4] She scored her first goal for the club during a 3-2 UEFA Women's Champions League win against St. Pölten on 16 October 2024.[5]

International career

Fujino made her senior international debut on 9 October 2022, starting in a 2–0 friendly victory over New Zealand.[6] In July 2022, Fujino was selected for the Japan U-20 national team for the 2022 U-20 Women's World Cup where Japan were the runners-up.[7] She scored her first senior international goal on 14 July 2023, in a 5–0 friendly win against Panama.[8] On 13 June 2023, she was included in Japan's 23-player squad for the 2023 FIFA Women's World Cup.[9] On 26 July 2023, she scored her first World Cup goal against Costa Rica in the 2nd round of the 2023 FIFA Women's World Cup Group C, becoming the youngest Japanese goal scorer (19 years and 180 days) in the World Cup for both men and women.[10][11] On 14 June 2024, Fujino was included in the Japan squad for the 2024 Summer Olympics, where she scored a stunning free-kick goal in a 2–1 group stage loss to Spain. Japan reached the quarter-finals, where they lost 1–0 to the United States in extra time.[12][13][14] Fujino was part of the Japan squad that won the 2025 SheBelieves Cup, playing in all matches including the final.[15] As of October 2025, she continues to feature for Japan in international friendlies, such as the match against Italy on 24 October 2025.[16]
