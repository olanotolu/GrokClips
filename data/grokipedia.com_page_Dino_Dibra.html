Dino Dibra

Dino Dibra (18 April 1975 – 14 October 2000), also known as The Sunshine Boy, was an Australian suspected murderer and a victim of the Melbourne gangland killings.

Imprisonments

Dibra was born to Albanian parents.[1] Between the ages of 15 and 18, Dibra was charged with a number of petty offences, including possession of drugs (marijuana). At the age of 19, he was charged with threatening to kill, threatening to inflict serious injury and unlawful drug possession. He was jailed and fined for these offences. In 1996, Dibra was imprisoned again, this time for 18 months for serious reckless driving offences.[2] In 1997, Dibra was jailed again for unlicensed and careless driving and failing to answer bail.[2]

Drug dealer

Police believe that Dibra was involved in selling and distributing cocaine, ecstasy and amphetamines. He and an old friend Rocco Arico were regular faces at the Dome nightclub in Prahran, Melbourne, as well as a Crown Casino nightclub.[2] Dibra was associated with several Melbourne underworld figures, including Mark Moran, Jason Moran, Andrew Veniamin, Carl Williams and Nik Radev at different times.[2] Dibra also ran hydroponic cannabis crops with Andrew Veniamin in Sunshine; they were arrested in March 1999, when a .45 pistol and .22 sawn-off rifle were found, but resumed the operation by May 1999.[2] In 1998, two bouncers were shot outside the Dome Nightclub in Prahran on December 6.[2] Dibra was one of the suspects in the shooting, along with four others, and it is believed that the shooting followed a dispute relating to drug supply.[2]

Kidnapping

On 2 August 1999, Dibra and friend Rocco Arico were among several men charged over the assault and kidnapping of the brother of an associate of Arico. At the time, police had bugged Dibra's house and car, seeking information in relation to the Dome bouncer shooting. The police subsequently raided Dibra's Taylors Lakes house, where the victim was still locked inside the boot.[2] Dibra and Arico were charged with a variety of offences over the incident.

Implication in underworld murders

On 23 November 1998 Charles Hegyalji, known as "Mad Charlie", was killed at his Caulfield home. Dibra was implicated in the murder and it is believed that it was drug-debt-related. Hegyalji's close friend, Mark Brandon "Chopper" Read, in the documentary film Fat Belly: Chopper Unchopped, revealed that he believes Dibra was the perpetrator. No charges were filed against Dibra in relation to this killing. Whilst it had been believed that underworld figure "Mad" Richard Mladenich was also shot dead by Dino Dibra in a room in the seedy St Kilda Esquire Hotel on 16 May 2000, Rocco Arico was also a suspect. On 30 October 2019 Victoria Police arrested Thomas “Little Thommy” Ivanovic in relation to Mladenich’s murder, though he was not charged with it and was instead charged with insurance fraud; the case remains unsolved as of 2025.[4]

Murder

Dibra was shot dead outside his Krambruk Street home in Sunshine West, Victoria on 14 October 2000 at approximately 9:15 pm. Police suspect that Dibra's murder was payback.[5] The Purana Taskforce stated publicly that Dibra was murdered by Andrew Veniamin, though they never charged Veniamin over Dibra's murder.[6] Veniamin and Paul Kallipolitis are suspected of having shot Dibra. In 2017, the Victorian coroner named Veniamin and Kallipolitis as suspects following a summary inquest and closed the case, stating that police did not expect to obtain further evidence.[7]

Underbelly

The character of Dino Dibra is portrayed in the Nine Network's television series Underbelly by Daniel Amalm. He first appeared in episode four (Cocksure) and in his opening scene, Dibra was shown shooting a character named Bruno Berlozzi, who later died, outside a nightclub.
