Maxi Kleber

Stats at NBA.com
Stats at Basketball-Reference.com Maximilian Kleber (born 29 January 1992) is a German professional basketball player for the Los Angeles Lakers of the National Basketball Association (NBA). At 2.08 m (6 ft 10 in), he plays the power forward position.

Early life

Kleber was introduced to basketball at the age of eight by his older brother, with whom he practiced.[1] He played in the youth ranks of TG Veitshöchheim, SC Heuchelhof and TG Würzburg.[2]

Professional career

Würzburg (2011–2014)

Kleber made his professional debut in Germany's top-flight Basketball Bundesliga during the 2011–12 season, representing the s.Oliver Baskets, a team based in his hometown Würzburg. In his first year, he was a role player on the team with an average of 7 minutes a game. In his second season, he grew to become an important player on the team, that averaged 9.9 points and 6.7 rebounds per game.[3] In 2012, he declared for the NBA draft before removing his name from the list.[4] He became auto-eligible for the NBA draft in 2014, but was not picked by any team.[5]

Obradoiro (2014–2015)

In the 2014 off-season, he signed a two-year deal with Spanish club Obradoiro CAB.[6] In the 2014–15 ACB season, he was named MVP of round 25 after scoring 36 points against Fuenlabrada.[7] Kleber made 33 appearances in the league that year, averaging 11.5 points and 6.5 rebounds per game.[8]

Bayern Munich (2015–2017)

On 1 July 2015, Kleber returned to Germany and signed a two-year deal with Bayern Munich.[9] In 2015–16, he played 24 Bundesliga games for Bayern, including 17 starts, averaging 8.0 points and 4.9 boards per game. He appeared in 37 games (37 starts) during the 2016–17 Bundesliga season, supplying averages of 9.0 points, 5.3 rebounds and 1.8 assists per game.

Dallas Mavericks (2017–2025)

On 13 July 2017, Kleber signed with the Dallas Mavericks.[10] He made his NBA debut on 21 October 2017 against the Houston Rockets.[11] Kleber moved from end of bench piece to key rotation player in his first few years, increasing his points, rebounds, assists and blocks per game numbers in each of his first three seasons. He was re-signed by the Mavericks on 10 July 2019.[12] On 21 February 2020, Kleber scored a career-high 26 points against the Orlando Magic in a 122–106 victory. In the 2019–20 season, Kleber posted a career high 9.1 points per game. He led the league in games played with 74 games and finished 19th in the league in blocks per game.[13] Kleber played in his first playoffs that year against the second seed Los Angeles Clippers. Kleber was tasked with guarding Kawhi Leonard for most of the series. Kleber struggled shooting the ball in the series, averaging 6.7 points per game and making just 5 of his 26 three-point attempts across the six-game series.[14] Kleber began the 2020–21 season strong, averaging 7.4 points per game and shooting 47.2% from three-point range in his first 9 games. Kleber would go on to miss the next 10 games however, as he recovered from COVID-19.[15] Kleber would return to action 2 February 2021 against the Phoenix Suns. He logged 17 minutes of game play, grabbing 6 rebounds and scoring 2 points. Kleber and the Mavericks would go on to lose to the Los Angeles Clippers in the first round of the playoffs in 7 games. On 18 April 2022, during game 2 of the first round of the playoffs, Kleber scored 25 points and hit eight 3-pointers in a 110–104 win over the Utah Jazz.[16] In the 2022 off-season, he signed a four-year contract extension with the Mavericks.[17] On 17 March 2023, Kleber made a buzzer-beating, game-winning three-pointer in a 111–110 win over the Los Angeles Lakers.[18] Kleber helped Dallas reach the 2024 NBA Finals where the Mavericks lost to the Boston Celtics in five games.[19] In the 2024–25 season, injuries limited Kleber to 34 games with the Mavericks, where he averaged 4.1 points and 3.2 rebounds in 18.7 minutes per game.[13]

Los Angeles Lakers (2025–present)

On 2 February 2025, Kleber was traded, alongside Luka Dončić and Markieff Morris, to the Los Angeles Lakers in exchange for Max Christie, Anthony Davis and a 2029 first-round pick. The Lakers also traded Jalen Hood-Schifino and a 2025 second round pick to the Utah Jazz, who also acquired a 2025 second-round pick from the Mavericks.[21] Following the trade, Kleber suffered a foot injury, causing him to miss the remainder of the regular season.[22] On April 30, 2025, during the first round of the playoffs, Kleber made his debut for the Lakers. He scored two points in a 103–96 loss against the Minnesota Timberwolves, eliminating the Lakers from the playoffs.[23] On October 21, 2025, the Lakers announced that Kleber had suffered a left oblique strain and would be re-evaluated in two weeks, missing at least the start of the 2025–26 season.[24]

National team career

Kleber represented the senior German national team in several international competitions.[25] In 2014, he played during the EuroBasket 2015 qualification rounds. He also participated in the 2019 FIBA World Cup European qualifiers and the tournament itself, averaging 11.2 points, 3.0 rebounds, and 1.8 blocks per game over five games.[26] Kleber sat out the 2020 Summer Olympics (held in 2021), as he recovered from an Achilles injury.[27] He did not play in subsequent major tournaments, including the 2022 EuroBasket and 2023 FIBA World Cup. In November 2024, Kleber announced he would not return to the national team.[28]
