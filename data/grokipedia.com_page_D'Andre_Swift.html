D'Andre Swift

D'Andre Tiyon Swift (born January 14, 1999) is an American professional football running back for the Chicago Bears of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football for the Georgia Bulldogs and was selected by the Detroit Lions with the third pick of the second round of the 2020 NFL draft. In 2023, he was traded to the Philadelphia Eagles, where he earned his first Pro Bowl selection before signing with the Bears in 2024. Career NFL statistics as of Week 7, 2025 season Stats at Pro Football Reference

Early life

Swift was born to Darren and Ayanna Swift in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.[1] He began displaying his running abilities in youth football, starting at age 7 with the Enon Eagles, a church-league team, where he scored six touchdowns in his debut game.[2] Swift attended St. Joseph's Preparatory School in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.[3][2] As a senior, he played high school football and rushed for 1,564 yards on 149 carries with 25 touchdowns.[4] He was rated as a four-star recruit.[5] He committed to the University of Georgia to play college football.[5][6]

College career

2017: Freshman year

As a freshman at Georgia in 2017, Swift was the third running back behind future NFL starters Nick Chubb and Sony Michel.[1][4] On 81 carries, he rushed for 618 yards with three touchdowns.[7] He earned All-Freshman SEC honors.[8]

2018: Sophomore year

In his sophomore year, Swift split carries fairly evenly with junior Elijah Holyfield, though he was used more often as a receiver.[9] He had three consecutive games with over 100 yards rushing in games against Florida, Kentucky, and Auburn that season.[10] In the SEC Championship against Alabama, he had 16 carries for 75 rushing yards and a rushing touchdown to go along with six receptions for 63 receiving yards and a receiving touchdown in the 35â€“28 loss.[11] He was named second-team All-SEC.[12]

2019: Junior year

As a junior, Swift recorded five games with over 100 rushing yards and had a stretch of four consecutive games with a rushing touchdown. He totaled 196 carries for 1,218 rushing yards and seven rushing touchdowns to go along with 24 receptions for 216 receiving yards and one receiving touchdown in 14 games.[13] He earned first-team All-SEC honors.[14]

Detroit Lions

2020

Swift was selected by the Detroit Lions with the 35th overall pick in the second round of the 2020 NFL draft.[16] On July 5, 2020, the Lions signed Swift to a four-year contract,[17] worth $8.5 million.[18] In his rookie season, Swift shared the backfield with Adrian Peterson and Kerryon Johnson.[19] In Swift's professional debut on September 13, 2020, he recorded his first career rushing touchdown, but later dropped what would have been a game-winning touchdown pass in the final seconds against the Chicago Bears.[20] On October 18, in Week 6, Swift rushed for 116 yards and two touchdowns against the Jacksonville Jaguars, becoming the first Lions rookie running back to rush for 100-plus yards and score two touchdowns in a game since Barry Sanders in 1989.[21] In Week 10, against the Washington Football Team, he had 149 scrimmage yards and a receiving touchdown in the 30â€“27 victory.[22] Overall, Swift finished the 2020 season with 114 carries for 521 rushing yards and eight rushing touchdowns to go along with 46 receptions for 357 receiving yards and two receiving touchdowns in 13 games.[23]

2021

Swift was joined by Jamaal Williams in the Lions' backfield for the 2021 season.[24] Over the season's first eight games, Swift totaled over 100 yards from scrimmage in four of them.[25] In Weeks 10 and 11, Swift recorded 130 and 136 rushing yards against the Pittsburgh Steelers and Cleveland Browns.[26][27] In the 2021 season, Swift appeared in 13 games. He finished with 151 carries for 617 rushing yards and four rushing touchdowns to go along with 62 receptions for 452 receiving yards and two receiving touchdowns.[25]

2022

Swift started the 2022 season with 15 carries for 144 rushing yards and one rushing touchdown in a 38â€“35 loss to the Philadelphia Eagles.[28] In Week 17 against the Bears, he had 117 scrimmage yards, one rushing touchdown, and one receiving touchdown in the 41â€“10 victory.[29] Overall, in the 2022 season, Swift finished with 99 carries for 542 rushing yards and five rushing touchdowns to go along with 48 receptions for 389 receiving yards and three receiving touchdowns.[30]

Philadelphia Eagles

On April 29, 2023, the Eagles traded a 2025 fourth-round pick for Swift and a swap of 2023 seventh-round picks during the 2023 NFL draft.[31] In Week 2, Swift ran for a career-high 175 yards and a touchdown in a 34â€“28 win over the Minnesota Vikings, earning NFC Offensive Player of the Week.[32][33] The following week, in a 25â€“11 win over Tampa Bay, Swift racked up another 130 rushing yards on 16 carries.[34] In Week 17, Swift recorded 61 rushing yards, giving him 1,000 rushing yards in a season for the first time in his career. On January 3, 2024, Swift was selected to the 2024 Pro Bowl Games, the first Pro Bowl of his career.[35]

Chicago Bears

2024

On March 13, 2024, Swift signed a three-year, $24 million contract with the Chicago Bears.[36][37] After averaging an inefficient 1.8 yards per carry through his first three games, Swift rebounded in Week 4 against the Rams, totaling 16 carries for 93 yards and a touchdown while catching all seven targets for 72 yards as the Bears won 24â€“18.[38] He finished the 2024 season with 233 carries for 869 rushing yards and six rushing touchdowns, along with 42 receptions for 386 receiving yards.[39]

2025

In 2025, Swift appeared in six games for the Bears, rushing 89 times for 419 yards and three touchdowns while recording 16 receptions for 166 yards and one touchdown, as of October 2025.[40]
