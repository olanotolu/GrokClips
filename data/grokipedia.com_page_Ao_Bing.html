Ao Bing

In this Chinese name, the family name is Ao. Investiture of the Gods character Ao Bing (Chinese: 敖丙; pinyin: Áo Bǐng) is a character in classical Chinese mythology and literature, such as the Investiture of the Gods(Fengshen Yanyi). He is a dragon prince and the third son of Ao Guang, the Dragon King of the East Sea. In Fengshen Yanyi, Ao Bing has no detailed personality; he simply follows orders from his father and is killed by Nezha during a confrontation over sea creature harassment. Following his death, which escalates conflict with the Dragon Kings, Ao Bing is deified as the Huagai Xingjun, lord of the Huagai constellation—a Celestial Canopy (also known as the Star of Loneliness and intellectual protection)—by Jiang Ziya at the novel's conclusion.[Investiture of the Gods Chapter 12 pages 141 - 143] In various adaptations of the Fengshen Yanyi narrative, the Dragon Kings' family, including Ao Bing, are typically portrayed as antagonists or oppressors. He is often depicted as arrogant, spoiled, and cruel, abusing his power as a dragon prince, serving as a major antagonist in Nezha's story. In modern portrayals, such as the 2019 film Ne Zha and its 2025 sequel, Ao Bing is reimagined as a compassionate friend or soul-mate[1][2][3] to Nezha, even when he occasionally opposes him, representing a significant departure from his Fengshen Yanyi depictions while incorporating mischievous traits like sneaking out to play.

Origins of the Third Son of the Eastern Sea Dragon King

Origins

The third son of the Eastern Sea Dragon King was first mentioned in the Complete Tale of Avalokiteśvara and the Southern Seas (Nánhǎi Guānyīn Quánzhuàn), a mid-16th century Ming dynasty novel. In one of the chapters focused on his eight-year-old sister Longnü attaining enlightenment, he had turned himself into a fish, sneaking out of the sea-palace to swim but accidentally captured by a fisherman and sold in the market, later he was saved by Guanyin(Avalokiteśvara) and his disciple Sudhana (Shancai Tongzi).[4][5] Further ancient mention of him before this is still unknown. In the earliest mention of the Dragon King Sāgara(Ao Guang), only the daughter is mentioned in the Buddhist texts of the Lotus Sutra-Devadatta.[6] The legend of "The Eight Immortals Cross the Sea", from Ming dynasty folklore, mentions the two older brothers of the third son as MoJie(Ao Jia) and LongDu(Ao Yi) who were killed by the immortals.[7][8] In a variation of the Legends of the White Snake from the 1992 TV series New Legend of Madame White Snake, the Green Snake character, Xiao Qing, is revealed to be the lost daughter of Ao Guang, making her the third son's possible half-sister.[9] The Dragon Kings did not have the family surname "Ao" until centuries later in the work of classical arts. The name "Ao Bing" for the third son of the Eastern sea Dragon King first appeared in Fengshen Yanyi (late 16th century).

In Fengshen Yanyi

In Chapter 12 of the novel, Ao Bing is the third son of Ao Guang, the Dragon King of the East Sea. He lives in the Crystal Palace with his elder brothers, Ao Jia and Ao Yi. As members of the dragon clan, they are associated with controlling rain and weather. Nezha's bathing in the Nine Curve River causes tremors that disturb the Crystal Palace. Ao Guang sends the yaksha Li Gen to investigate, but Nezha kills him with his Universal Ring. Ao Bing then sets out, with his father's consent, to confront Nezha over the incident. He rides a tumultuous pegasus accompanied by troops.[10][11] Ao Bing angrily confronts Nezha, leading to combat. Ao Bing wields a jade lance, while Nezha employs his Universal Ring and Sky Buddling Damask. Nezha synchronizes these weapons to strike Ao Bing dead, causing him to revert to his true form as a silver dragon.[12][13] After the Shang dynasty's defeat, Jiang Ziya confers god titles from the Fengshen bang. Ao Bing is appointed as the Huagai Xing (華蓋星) Star Lord, based on Chinese constellations. He and Li Gen are among pre-existing divine beings who receive new titles in the registry, with Li Gen appointed as the God of the Great Misfortune Star (Dahuo Xingjun).

Folklores

According to folklore, when Nezha caused havoc in the East Sea, he used the Universe Ring (Qiankun Quan) to kill the blue-white dragon, Ao Bing. Later, the immortal Taiyi Zhenren revived the Ao Bing using tendon-relaxing vines and white pine bark. After coming back to life, the Ao Bing retreated underground, built another palace, and continued cultivating until he attained enlightenment and ascended to the heavens. This miraculous cave was left behind, and people named the White Dragon’s cultivation palace "White Dragon Palace." White Dragon Palace is now a tourist destination that combines mountains, water, and caves into a scenic attraction. It is located in Hanzeng Town, 18 kilometers west of Jiangyou. Its most distinctive features are its primitive, natural, and ancient charm. Stretching 1.2 kilometers in length, the cave is divided into both water and dry sections.[14]

Worship

Ao Bing is occasionally enshrined in temples dedicated to the Dragon Kings of the Four Seas. For example, the Sihai Longwang Temple in Batu Pahat, Malaysia, established in 1983, features a 12-foot-tall statue of Ao Bing.[15]

In popular culture

Film

  • Ao Bing appears as a major character in the animated films Ne Zha (2019) and Ne Zha 2 (2025), voiced by Han Mo in Chinese and Aleks Le in English. The films, loosely based on Investiture of the Gods , depict Ao Bing as friendlier than in prior depictions, and he becomes friends with the title character. Both films broke numerous box-office records upon their initial releases[16], with Ne Zha 2 in particular becoming the highest-grossing film of all time in China (over ¥7.2 billion as of February 2025), the highest-grossing animated movie of all time (over $2.15 billion worldwide), and the fifth highest-grossing movie of all time (approximately $2.15 billion worldwide as of October 2025)[17].

Video games

  • In the 2022 mobile game Dislyte, the character "Long Mian" is based on Ao Bing as an "avatar" Esper blessed with his powers, specializing in control abilities like Freeze and speed reduction.
  • Ao Bing appears as a character in the 2003 action video game Mystic Heroes, adapted from Investiture of the Gods.
