Dimitri Petratos

Dimitrios Petratos (born 10 November 1992) is an Australian professional association football player who plays as a forward for Indian Super League club Mohun Bagan Super Giant. He holds dual Australian and Greek citizenship. Petratos has played for four different A-League clubs. The majority of Petratos's career has been in the A-League. However, he has played in Malaysia, South Korea, Saudi Arabia, before moving to India, where he won the ISL Cup in 2023 with Mohun Bagan Super Giant.[1]

In June 2013, Petratos was signed by the Roar on a one-year deal, along with former Melbourne Victory defensive midfielder Diogo Ferreira.[13][14][15] On 26 December 2013, Petratos scored his first career hattrick against his former club Sydney FC. He managed to feature in all of Brisbane's 29 games, finishing with a goal tally of 7 and an equally impressive 5 assists.

Ulsan Hyundai

On 24 January 2017, Petratos was signed, along with his brother Kosta by Newcastle Jets on a 3-year contract, starting in the 2017â€“18 season.[16] However, less than two weeks later, he accepted an offer to join Korean club Ulsan Hyundai on a 3-year deal for a sum of $300,000, which would be split evenly between Newcastle and Brisbane.[17]

Newcastle Jets

On 13 June 2017, following his release from Ulsan Hyundai, Petratos signed a two-year deal with Newcastle Jets.[18]

Al-Wehda

On 10 September 2020, Petratos signed a two-year contract with Saudi Professional League club Al-Wehda. He scored 9 goals in 28 league appearances over the next two seasons.[19]

Western Sydney Wanderers (loan)

On 8 August 2021, Petratos joined Western Sydney Wanderers on a season-long loan from Al-Wehda. He contributed 3 goals in 23 A-League appearances during the 2021â€“22 season.[20]

Mohun Bagan

On 18 July 2022, Petratos joined Indian Super League club Mohun Bagan. On 10 October he made his debut against Chennaiyin FC, during which he provided the assist for Manvir Singh's opening goal. He quickly proved his worth to the team by scoring a hat-trick against Kerala Blasters in only his second game. As the season progressed, he emerged as a key player for his team, leading them to finish third in the points table and eventually guiding them to the Indian Super League playoffs final. In the final against Bengaluru FC, Petratos demonstrated his skill and composure by scoring both the goals for his team, which included two penalties, as the game ended in a 2â€“2 draw after extra time. He also scored his penalty in the resulting shoot-out, which ultimately led his team to win the ISL trophy. Petratos finished the season with an impressive twelve goals and seven assists in 23 matches, cementing his status as a valuable member of the team. International career

Youth career

Petratos represented Australia at youth levels. He made one appearance for the under-17 team in 2008, scoring one goal. Between 2009 and 2011, he earned nine caps for the under-20 side, netting two goals, and was part of the team that finished as runners-up at the 2010 AFC U-20 Asian Cup in China. In 2011, he played twice for the under-23 team without scoring.

Senior career

After rumours throughout the 2017â€“18 A-League season of an international call-up, Petratos was selected for Australia's senior squad for friendlies ahead of the 2018 FIFA World Cup. He made his debut starting on the left wing against Norway on 23 March 2018, playing 74 minutes before being substituted for Robbie Kruse in a 1â€“4 loss. Four days later, on 27 March, he was an unused substitute in the 0â€“0 draw with Colombia. Petratos came on as a substitute for 17 minutes in a 4â€“0 win against the Czech Republic on 1 June 2018. He was named in Australia's 23-man squad for the 2018 World Cup in Russia but did not feature in any matches during the tournament.[24] His third and final senior cap came on 7 June 2019, when he substituted in for six minutes during a 0â€“1 friendly defeat to South Korea.

Personal life

Petratos is of Greek ancestry, and comes from a footballing family. His father Angelo played as a defender for Sydney Olympic FC,[25] his younger brother Kosta was his teammate at the Jets, his younger sister Panayiota previously played for the Newcastle W-League team in 2021, his younger brother Maki played for the Newcastle Jets and his youngest sister Anastasia played for Sydney Olympic FC, including in the Women's NPL1 in 2022.[26][27][28][29]
