D-Frag!

D-Frag! (Japanese: ディーふらぐ!, Hepburn: Dīfuragu!), also known as D-Fragments, is a comedic manga series by Tomoya Haruno that began serialization in Media Factory's Monthly Comic Alive in July 2008. A 12-episode anime television series adaptation by Brain's Base aired from January 6 to March 24, 2014.[2]

Plot

The story focuses on a semi-connected series of sketches as the wannabe delinquent Kenji Kazama is forced into joining his school's struggling "Game Creation Club" (Game Development Club in the Seven Seas Entertainment translation), led by the diminutive but surprisingly strong president Roka Shibasaki. The club's members, including Roka's friend Sagiri Amatsuka and Kenji's own gang members Ataru Oshino and Kenzan Takahashi, exhibit various eccentricities and are assigned parodied elemental affinities based on their personalities, which drive Kenji crazy. As he attempts to distance himself from the club, he increasingly encounters not only his fellow club members but also other eccentric students from Fujou Academy who test his sanity in different ways.[3]

Characters

Most main characters' names are references to railway stations in Tokyo, particularly on the Keiō Line. As of the manga's latest volume (Volume 19, October 2024), the first names of some characters, such as Takao and Funabori, remain unrevealed.[4]

Main characters

  • Kenji Kazama (風間 堅次, Kazama Kenji)
    Voiced by: Katsuyuki Konishi (Japanese); Austin Tindle[5] (English)
    The leader of the "Kazama Party" and one of the head delinquents in school, he was forcibly recruited into the club after attempting to help them put out a fire. He is capable of fighting but refrains from hitting girls, leading to challenges against the other club members. He later employs trickery and deception in competitions. The club members refer to him as the "Wind" elemental, based on his name. He consistently serves as the tsukkomi (comedic straight man) amid the surrounding antics.
  • Roka Shibasaki (柴崎 芦花, Shibasaki Roka)
    Voiced by: Kana Hanazawa (Japanese); Bryn Apprill[5] (English)
    The President of the Game Creation Club (Provisional). She has blonde hair. Her element is "Fire", though she later claims her true element is "Darkness". Her primary combat method involves placing a bag over opponents' heads to blind them, aligning with the darkness theme. She carries spare bags for repeated use. Despite her small stature, she is regarded as the "Strongest Darkness" and the strongest student in school. She calls Kenji her "important person" and displays jealousy toward other girls approaching him. In recent manga volumes, her family dynamics with Tsutsuji are further explored.[6]
  • Chitose Karasuyama (烏山 千歳, Karasuyama Chitose)
    Voiced by: Chiwa Saitō (Japanese); Whitney Rodgers[7] (English)
    A second-year and the President of the student council. Her authority is so great that even teachers fear her. Her element is "Earth", with combat methods including punching, throwing dirt, or digging traps. She has a habit of burying victims up to their necks.
  • Sakura Mizukami (水上 桜, Mizukami Sakura)
    Voiced by: Mikako Takahashi (Japanese); Megan Shipman[8] (English)
    A first-year and a pink-haired tomboy. Her element is "Water", and she is often seen with a water bottle. Her combat involves pouring water on her hands or forcing it down opponents' throats. She refers to Kenji and Noe as her siblings, though they deny the relation. Later manga arcs highlight her evolving sibling-like bonds within the group.[9]
  • Minami Ōsawa (大沢 南, Ōsawa Minami ; alt. Minami Ohsawa)
    Voiced by: Ami Koshimizu (Japanese); Caitlin Glass[8] (English)
    The faculty advisor of the club. Her element is "Lightning" due to carrying a taser. She frequently sleeps, even during her own classes, and exhibits poor memory, such as forgetting a past student's name who lent her the tracksuit she wears constantly.
  • Takao (高尾)
    Voiced by: Shizuka Itō (Japanese); Tia Ballard[7] (English)
    President of the "real" Game Creation Club, with no known first name. She is friends with Roka despite their past rift leading to the formation of the provisional club. She harbors a crush on Kazama. Following a loss to the Game Creation Club (Provisional), she frequents their activities more than her own club's. Recent chapters depict her deepening involvement in inter-club dynamics.[10]

Secondary characters

Real Game Creation Club

  • Tsutsumi Inada (稲田 堤, Inada Tsutsumi)
    Voiced by: Megumi Toyoguchi (Japanese); Morgan Garrett[5] (English)
    A glasses-wearing girl with a perverted streak who often dresses Oka in girly outfits. She serves as Chitose's counterpart in the "real" Game Creation Club.
  • Oka Sakuragaoka (岡桜ヶ丘, Sakuragaoka Oka)
    Voiced by: Ryōko Shiraishi (Japanese); Megan Vander Pluym[5] (English)
    A boy with a very feminine appearance. Due to a misunderstanding at their first meeting, Kenji refers to him as a girl, which he has not corrected.
  • Yamada (山田)
    Voiced by: Takahiro Fujiwara (Japanese); Chris Rager (English)
    A big, muscular club member. Though he appears brutish, he is actually a timid pacifist.

Kazama Gang

  • Ataru Kawahara (河原 中, Kawahara Ataru)
    Voiced by: Jun Fukuyama (Japanese); J. Michael Tatum[5] (English)
    Student council Vice-President with a masochistic streak who both admires and fears Chitose.
  • Yokoshima (横縞)
    Voiced by: Hiroki Gotō (Japanese); Josh Grelle[5] (English)
    A short but fat student and member of the Kazama Gang. Despite his delinquent image, he is highly intelligent, placing second in the school placement test.
  • Hiroshi Nagayama (長山ひろし, Nagayama Hiroshi)
    Voiced by: Kenichirou Matsuda (Japanese); Ian Sinclair[5] (English)
    A tall member of the Kazama Gang.

Former student council

  • Tama Sakai (境 多摩, Sakai Tama)
    Voiced by: Kana Ueda (Japanese); Lindsay Seidel[5] (English)
    A third-year student and former Student Council President before Chitose. She holds a grudge against Chitose from childhood and plots to disband the Game Creation Club (Provisional). She excels in combat using her twin-tails hairstyle, renowned as the strongest at Fujou High—capable of repelling Roka—and known as "The Legendary" at other schools. After losing to Kenji in a competition between the ex-student council and the Game Creation Club (Provisional), she develops an interest in him and participates in club activities despite not being a member. In later manga volumes, her rivalry with Chitose intensifies through school events.[11]
  • Naganuma (長沼)
    Voiced by: Akira Ishida (Japanese); Clifford Chapin[5] (English)
    Former student council Vice President and an otaku.
  • Azuma Matsubara (松原 東, Matsubara Azuma)
    Voiced by: Yuuko Sanpei (Japanese); Monica Rial[5] (English)
    Former student council treasurer, courteous but capable in combat.
  • Shinsen (神泉)
    Voiced by: Eri Sendai (Japanese); Leah Clark[5] (English)
    Former student council secretary. She suffers from an unstable stomach and frequently vomits, earning the nickname "Barfie" from friends and enemies alike. She works various part-time jobs and often encounters club activities.

Others

  • Hachi Shiō (子王 八, Shiō Hachi)
    Voiced by: Kōki Miyata (Japanese); Micah Solusod[12] (English)
    The club's other male member, with an unrequited crush on Roka. He uses the space between a bookshelf and the wall as his "seat". Other members refer to him as a "Light" elemental due to sparkles surrounding his appearance. Roka treats him harshly, while others ignore him. He later joins the Kazama Gang.
  • Funabori (船堀)
    Voiced by: Aki Toyosaki (Japanese); Felecia Angelle[13] (English)
    Kenji's classmate, skilled at housework and often handling class chores. She dislikes attention and blushes at praise. She has a crush on Kenji. Her first name remains unrevealed as of Volume 19 (2024).[14]
  • Noe Kazama (風間 之江, Kazama Noe)
    Voiced by: Emiri Katō (Japanese); Jad Saxton[5] (English)
    Kenji's little sister, sharing his spiky hair and role as the comedic straight man in chaotic situations. Kenji often comforts her amid the insanity. The club calls her an "ice" elemental for her love of ice cream and refers to her as the "little sister" element.
  • RaGaiGar (ラガイガー)
    Voiced by: Nobuyuki Hiyama (Japanese); Scott Freeman[5] (English)
    A man dressed in a tokusatsu outfit portraying a superhero.
  • Shawn KoneKone (ショーン・コネコネ)
    Voiced by: Jōji Nakata (Japanese); Bill Jenkins[5] (English)
    Kenji's English Literature teacher. He rescued the Kazama Gang (and RaGaiGar) from a fire in their childhood, inspiring Kenji to help others. A parody of Sean Connery with Indiana Jones elements, he wields the "Dandy" element.
  • Tsutsuji Shibasaki (柴崎 つつじ, Shibasaki Tsutsuji)
    Roka's younger (though taller) sister, attending a lower-ranked high school after failing Fujou High's entrance exams. She prepares elaborate lunchboxes for Roka and aims to "protect" her from Kenji. Recent chapters expand on her protective role and interactions with the club.[15]
  • Kudanshita (九段下)
    A third-year student at Fujou High with a perpetually scary smiling expression. Despite this, she is dependable and offers guidance to Kenji occasionally. Nicknamed "Kusshi" (クッシー). In later manga arcs, she provides counsel during school crises.[16]

Media

Manga

Written and illustrated by Tomoya Haruno, the manga began serialization in Media Factory's Monthly Comic Alive in July 2008. The chapters are being collected and released in the tankōbon format by Media Factory. As of October 2025, twenty volumes have been released. Seven Seas Entertainment has licensed the manga.[17]

Anime

An anime television series adaptation by Brain's Base aired from January 6 to March 24, 2014 on TV Tokyo. The series is directed by Seiki Sugawara, Makoto Uezu is in charge of series composition, while Nijine composed the music.[2] Funimation licensed the series in North America.[40] Following the merger with Crunchyroll, it is streamed on Crunchyroll.[41]
