Bodø (town)

Bodø (pronounced [ˈbûːdøː] ⓘ, Lule Sami: Bådåddjo) is a town in Bodø Municipality in Nordland county, Norway. The town is the administrative centre of both the Bodø Municipality and Nordland county. It is located on the Bodø peninsula between the Vestfjorden and the Saltfjorden. Bodø is located just north of the Arctic Circle. It is the largest urban area and town in both Nordland county and Northern Norway.[1] The 15.03-square-kilometre (3,710-acre) town has a population (2024) of 43,322 and a population density of 2,882 inhabitants per square kilometre (7,460/sq mi).[1] Bodø was a European Capital of Culture in 2024, along with Bad Ischl and Tartu. One of Norway's most successful football clubs, FK Bodø/Glimt, is based in the town.

History

The village of Bodø was granted town status as a kjøpstad in 1816 and soon after, in 1818, it was known for the Bodø affair, smuggling of contraband cargo by British merchants that later were compensated by Norway. The town of Bodø was established as a municipality on 1 January 1838 (see formannskapsdistrikt law). On 1 January 1938, a part of the neighboring Bodin Municipality (population: 559) was transferred into the town of Bodø. During World War II, on 27 May 1940, Bodø was heavily bombed by the German Luftwaffe as part of the Norwegian Campaign, destroying 420 of the town's 760 buildings, including key infrastructure like the radio station and an improvised airstrip; the town was almost completely leveled and subsequently rebuilt in a modern style after the war.[2][3] On 1 January 1959, another part of Bodin Municipality (population: 1,303) was transferred into the town of Bodø, expanding its size considerably. During the 1960s, there were many municipal mergers across Norway due to the work of the Schei Committee. On 1 January 1968, the town of Bodø (population: 14,252) was merged with Bodin Municipality (population: 13,323) and this created the much larger Bodø Municipality.

Toponymy

The town is named after the old Bodøgård farm (Old Norse: Boðvin), since the town was built on its ground. The first element might be boði which means "sunken rock" or "skerry" and the last element is vin which means "meadow" or "pasture". The last element may have been misunderstood as øy which means "island" (and written with the Danish language form ø).

Climate

Bodø features an oceanic climate (Cfb) in the 1991-2020 base period. Bodø is one of the northernmost cities in the world and among those inside the Arctic Circle with a temperate four-season climate. The weather in Bodø depends on weather pattern; long lasting weather patterns with Atlantic lows bringing rain and overcast can occur in all seasons, but so can sunny weather with highs over Northern Scandinavia/Western Russia. Located on a peninsula in the Norwegian Sea, Bodø has potential for strong winds both from the west and east. The "midnight sun" is above the horizon from approximately 3 June to 11 July (about 38 days), and the period with continuous daylight lasts a bit longer. The all-time low −18.5 °C (−1.3 °F) was recorded in February 1966, which was the coldest month on record with a mean of −8.9 °C (16.0 °F). The all-time high 30.7 °C (87.3 °F) was set in July 2019, while July 2014 was the warmest month with a 24-hr mean 17.3 °C (63.1 °F) and average daily high 21.6 °C (70.9 °F). The warmest night recorded was June 29, 1972 with overnight low 21.7 °C (71.1 °F). The average date for the first overnight freeze (below 0 °C (32.0 °F)) in autumn is October 12 (1981-2010 average). The driest month on record was January 2014 with no precipitation at all, while the wettest was September 2009 with 293 mm. Recent decades have seen warming, and there has been no overnight air frost in June since 1981. As of 2025, no new temperature extremes have been recorded, but warming trends continue, with recent years showing milder winters. With its location on the Arctic Circle the city features one of the largest latitudinal temperature anomalies on Earth. Institutions

The main campus of Nord University is located 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) outside the city centre. Approximately 11,300 undergraduate and graduate students study at the university.[4] Bodø hosts one of the campuses of the Norwegian Police University College, located outside Oslo. The Norwegian Civil Aviation Authority is situated in Bodø, as is the Joint Rescue Coordination Centre of Northern Norway. The Norwegian Armed Forces headquarters for North Norway is located at Reitan, east of the city. The main hospital is Nordlandssykehuset HF, which has local, regional, and national areas of responsibility. SB Nordlandsbuss has its headquarters in Bodø, as does Bodø Energi. City Nord, a major shopping centre in Nordland, is located in the town of Bodø.

Transportation

Rail transport

Bodø Station serves as the northern terminus of the Nordland Line that runs south to Trondheim, where it connects to the rest of the Norwegian rail network. It isn't the northernmost station though, with Tverrlandet Station being the northernmost station of the Nordland Line and Narvik Station, terminal of the Ofoten Line, being the northernmost station in the whole of Norway. The Ofoten Line, however, doesn't have a connection to the rest of Norway's railway network, thus making Bodø also the northern terminus of the central Norwegian railway system. As of October 2025, services south of Mo i Rana are disrupted due to landslides, with bus replacements operating; north of Mo i Rana, including to Fauske and Rognan, trains run normally, though frequencies vary. Full rail service to Trondheim is expected to resume partially from November 2025.[5]

Bus traffic

Bodø is served by several bus lines to various destinations. Inside Bodø, there is a bus network with 4 lines, serving as public transport in Bodø, and 4 long-distance bus lines connecting to different parts of the country. Notable is line 100, which connects to Narvik, where it is possible to change onto trains of the Ofoten line towards Sweden at Narvik Station.

Ferries

Bodø is an important ferry port, connecting to several other parts of Norway. It is serviced by the Ferry Bodø –Røst – Værøya – Moskenes by operator Torghatten Nord. Nordland Fylkeskommune operates the Hurtigbåt services from the city center terminal. Hurtigbåt services go to Sandnessjøen, Svolvær, Væran, and Gildeskål Additionally, Bodø is a stop on the renowned Hurtigruten (Kystruten Bergen-Kirkenes) Norwegian coastal ferry line and sees daily services towards Bergen and Kirkenes by operator Hurtigruten AS or Havila Kystruten, who jointly run the Kystruten service between Bergen and Kirkenes.

Air Travel

Bodø Airport lies just south of the city centre and was opened in 1952. The airport is run by Avinor. In 2024, it served 1,862,081 passengers[6] and is the site of Bodø Air Traffic Control Center. It is served regularly by SAS (Scandinavian Airlines), Norwegian Air Shuttle and Widerøe. The airline Widerøe has its head office in Bodø.

Culture

Bodø's local newspaper is the Avisa Nordland. The Norwegian Aviation Museum and Nordland Museum are located in Bodø. Nordland Museum features a Sami exhibit, a Viking treasure exhibit, and an exhibition about Bodø's history from 1816 to the present. The Bodø Cathedral was built in 1956, representing post-war architecture, whereas the Bodin Church just outside the city centre dates from the 13th century, representing a typical medieval stone church. Other churches in the town include Hunstad Church and Rønvik Church. The cultural centre "Stormen" (the tempest) was opened in 2014. It contains a library, a concert hall and theater. The building is designed by Daniel Rosbottom and David Howarth. Bodø is host to the cultural festivals Nordland Musikkfestuke and Parkenfestivalen every summer, as well as the free and volunteer based Bodø Hardcore Festival in early winter. The Northern Norwegian Art Museum reopened in a new facility in Bodø in February 2025, showcasing a collection of historical and contemporary art from Northern Norway, including Sami works.[7] Fram Kino was the first cinema in Norway. It was started in the year 1908.

Sports

Bodø's main professional team is the football club Bodø/Glimt, playing in Eliteserien, the top division of football in Norway. The club has achieved significant success in recent years, winning the Eliteserien in 2020, 2021, 2023, and 2024, and qualifying for the 2025–26 UEFA Champions League, where it hosted historic matches at Aspmyra Stadion, including the first-ever Champions League game north of the Arctic Circle in September 2025.[8] In addition to Bodø/Glimt, Bodø has had several teams at national top level, including Grand Bodø (women's football), Junkeren (women's handball) and Bodø HK (men's handball). The most well-known sporting arena in Bodø is Aspmyra Stadion, which in addition to being the home of Bodø/Glimt has hosted one international match. Also, the multi-purpose indoor Bodø Spektrum, contains full-size football and handball courts, as well as several swimming and bathing facilities. The town is also home to Bodø Barbarians, a prominent rugby league team active as of 2025.[9]
