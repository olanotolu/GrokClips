Hager Group

Hager SE is a manufacturer of electrical installations based in Blieskastel, Germany.[2] The company has been family-owned and run since its foundation in 1955 by the Hager family.[3] Hager Group produces solutions for electrical installations in residential, commercial, and industrial buildings. Due to acquisitions of specialized companies worldwide since the 1990s, Hager SE offers products ranging from energy distribution, cable management to building automation and security systems under its various brands, including Hager. Hager Group owns the brands Hager, Advizeo, Berker, Eficia, Elcom, E3/DC, Bocchiotti/Iboco, Pmflex, Daitem, and Diagral.[4]

History

In 1955, Hager oHG, elektrotechnische Fabrik was founded by brothers Oswald and Hermann Hager, together with their father Peter Hager in Ensheim in the Saarland region of Germany.[5][6] Since 1945, Saarland had been under the economic control of France and had no access to the German market. However, Hager wanted to gain a foothold in both markets. In 1959, the Hager brothers founded their first foreign subsidiary, Hager Electro S. A., in Obernai, Alsace, in north-eastern France.[7] In 1966, Hager began systematic training of its electricians, whose expertise has created a culture of customer loyalty, something that continues to this day. Hager's modular rotary fuse carrier was patented in Germany in 1968 and in France in 1970. At the same time, the first mass-produced distribution board, the Hager-Rapid-System, was launched on the French market. In 1973, Hager achieved sales of 43 million Deutsche Marks in Germany and in 1974 the company reached a turnover of 22 million francs in France. In 1976, Hager launched the mini Gamma enclosure,[8] in 1982 the company started producing residual-current circuit breakers (RCCBs) in Germany.[9] A new production facility with a high-bay warehouse was opened in Blieskastel. Hager Group began to market itself as a complete service provider for electrical installations in buildings in the 1980s, setting up sales companies in Europe (Switzerland and Great Britain).[10] In the mid-1990s, Hager set up distribution channels in the United Arab Emirates (Dubai), Singapore, Malaysia, Hong Kong, China, Australia and New Zealand. In 2007, Hager Group became a European Company: Hager SE.[11] In 2018, Hager Group was a leading provider of electrical installation systems worldwide.[12] In August 2019, the group was ranked number 128 in the top 500 family-owned businesses in Germany according to the magazine Die Deutsche Wirtschaft.[13] By 2022/23, the group's revenue had reached â‚¬3 billion.[14] In 2025, Hager Group invested â‚¬18 million in new production facilities at its Blieskastel headquarters.[15]

Acquisitions

In 1992, the group acquired Lumetal, a manufacturer of distribution boards from Porcia, Italy.[21] Hager Group acquired the German company Tehalit in 1996, a manufacturer of cable management systems and cable ducts.[22] In 1998, the group acquired the French electronic timer manufacturer Flash, whose registered office was in Saverne.[23] Prior to this, Hager Group manufactured only mechanical timers. The same year, the company also acquired British manufacturer Ashley & Rock from Ulverston, whose products were manufactured according to British Standards.[24] In 2002, the Polish company Polo, whose registered office was in Tychy, was integrated into the company.[24] In 2004, Hager Group acquired Swiss company Weber AG, a switchgear manufacturer.[24] The same year, it acquired French manufacturer Atral, which also produced security systems under the Hager brand and manufactured products for the brands Diagral, Daitem, and Logisty until the divestiture of Atraltech in 2023.[24][25][26] In 2006, Hager entered the Brazilian market when it acquired 100 % of the shares in the company Eletromar.[24] On 1 January 2009, Hager acquired Electraplan Solutions GmbH.[27] In 2010, Hager acquired Berker, a German manufacturer of switches, whose registered offices were in SchalksmÃ¼hle and Ottfingen.[28] In 2012, Hager acquired the German family firm Elcom, a producer of intercoms.[29] In 2015, Hager Group acquired Bocchiotti, an Italian family-owned company specializing in cable management systems.[30] In 2017, it acquired E3/DC GmbH, a German developer of inverters and energy storage systems.[31] In 2023, Hager Group acquired Pmflex, an Italian plastics manufacturer for cable protection, and Eficia, a French provider of energy management software.[32][33] In 2024, it acquired advizeo, a French firm offering energy efficiency services for buildings.[34] In 2025, advizeo (a Hager subsidiary) acquired Comgy, a German software company specializing in energy data collection and visualization.[35]

Brands and Products

Hager brand offers solutions and services for electrical installations in residential, commercial and industrial buildings. Berker manufactures switches and switch systems as part of Hager Group. Bocchiotti/Iboco, the Italian market leader in cable management and room distribution systems, is also part of Hager Group whilst Elcom produces intercom systems for residential and office buildings. There are four different areas of application for Hager Group's products and services:
  • Energy distribution and metering systems, including energy management and VDI concepts for electrical installation
  • Cable management systems for power and data distribution
  • Switch ranges and building control
  • Security systems
Since 2018, Hager Group has been working on electromobility with Audi AG. The aim of the collaboration is to connect the Audi e-tron model with Hager Group's Home Energy Management System (HEMS).[36]

  • E3/DC, provider of solar energy storage, backup power, and e-mobility solutions
