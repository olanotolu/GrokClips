Anything Goes is the second studio album by American country music duo Florida Georgia Line. It was released on October 14, 2014, by Republic Nashville.[3] The release of the album was announced August 15 from Central Park in New York City on Good Morning America.[4][5] The album was produced by Joey Moi.[6] Its first single, "Dirt", became Florida Georgia Line's fourth number one hit when it peaked at number one on the Billboard Hot Country Songs chart.[7] The duo, which disbanded in 2025, embarked on the Anything Goes Tour to further promote the album.[8]

Critical reception

Anything Goes has received mixed reviews from critics. On aggregate, the album holds a Metacritic score of 61 out of 100, based on four reviews, indicating "generally favorable reviews."[[9]] Writing for AllMusic, Stephen Thomas Erlewine criticized the anonymity of the duo and their music, claiming that "they're playing to the largest possible audience, so nobody should be surprised that Anything Goes is so broad it avoids such messiness as personality." He awarded the album 3 stars out of 5.[1] Tom Roland awarded the album 4 out of 5 stars in his review for Billboard, saying that "it shores up the duo's country flanks, and demonstrates that FGL intends to aggressively protect its progressive place in the genre, one that the act essentially designed on its own."[2] Jon Caramanica of The New York Times described the album as "a literal breeze, with songs that land like feathers," noting that it "doesn't pack quite the raw shock of the debut" but praised its lighter tone.[[10]]

Commercial performance

The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 with first-week sales of 197,000 copies.[11] The album was certified Gold by the RIAA on December 1, 2014, and Platinum on March 29, 2016, for combined sales and streams of a million units.[12] As of June 2015, the album has sold 858,000 copies in the US.[13] It also reached number one on the Top Country Albums chart. In Canada, the album debuted at number two on the Canadian Albums Chart, selling 16,000 copies.[14]
