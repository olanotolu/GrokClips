John Roberts (journalist)


John Roberts (born November 15, 1956) is a Canadian-American broadcast journalist known for his extensive coverage of White House politics and national affairs across major U.S. networks.[1][2] Roberts began his career in Canadian media, working as an anchor and correspondent for City TV in Toronto and co-anchoring CTV's morning news program.[2] He later joined CBS News, where he spent over 14 years as chief White House correspondent, chief medical correspondent, and weekend evening news anchor, reporting on events such as the Atlanta Olympic bombing, the death of Princess Diana, and the TWA Flight 800 crash.[2] In 2006, he moved to CNN, co-anchoring American Morning until 2010.[2] Since joining Fox News Channel in 2011, Roberts has served as a senior national correspondent and was appointed chief White House correspondent in 2017, providing on-the-ground reporting during the Donald Trump presidency.[2][3] Currently, he co-anchors America Reports weekdays from 1-3 PM ET, delivering analysis on current events.[2] His career accolades include an Emmy Award, a New York Press Club award, three national Emmy Awards for specific reporting, and a National Headline Award for coverage of the 2006 Israeli-Lebanese border crisis.[2] Roberts has occasionally drawn attention for pressing administration officials on key issues, such as during a 2020 exchange with White House Press Secretary Kayleigh McEnany where he urged her to cease deflection amid questions on extremism.[4] In August 2025, he was hospitalized with severe malaria contracted abroad but has since recovered and returned to broadcasting.[5] Personally, Roberts is married to ABC News anchor Kyra Phillips, with whom he has fraternal twins born in 2011.[1][6]

Early Life

Upbringing and Education

John Roberts was born John Robertson on November 15, 1956, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada.[7] [1] He grew up in the Greater Toronto Area, attending Erindale Secondary School in Mississauga before transferring to Lorne Park Secondary School.[8] Roberts pursued higher education at the University of Toronto at Mississauga, completing his studies in 1978.[2] [8] During his time there, he volunteered at the university's radio station, an experience that steered him toward broadcasting rather than his initial plan of attending medical school.[9] This early involvement in campus media marked the beginning of his professional interest in journalism.[7]

Professional Career

Initial Roles in Canadian Media

Roberts began his broadcasting career in Canadian radio during the 1970s, serving as a music announcer at stations including CFOS-Radio in Owen Sound, Ontario, CHYM-Radio, and CJBK-Radio in London, Ontario, where he worked as a broadcaster by 1977.[7] His early radio roles involved market reports and music programming, establishing foundational experience in on-air delivery.[10] Transitioning to television, Roberts joined CITY-TV in Toronto as an anchor and correspondent, co-hosting music-focused programs such as The NewMusic alongside Jeanne Beker and Toronto Rocks.[11] Under the on-air name J.D. Roberts, he also worked at MuchMusic, hosting the Pepsi Power Hour and contributing to the network's entertainment coverage during the 1980s.[12] These roles emphasized music journalism and youth-oriented content, reflecting CITY-TV's innovative, street-level style at the time.[8] In 1990, after a brief stint in the United States, Roberts returned to Canada to co-anchor CTV's national morning program Canada AM until 1992.[2] This position involved daily news segments, interviews, and lifestyle reporting, broadening his exposure to mainstream television audiences.[13] His Canadian media tenure laid the groundwork for international opportunities, culminating in his relocation to U.S. networks.[14]

Moves to U.S. Broadcast Networks

In 1989, after several years anchoring and reporting for Canadian outlets including CITY-TV and MuchMusic, Roberts relocated to Miami, Florida, to work as an anchor and correspondent for WCIX-TV, a CBS-affiliated station.[15] He remained there until 1990, gaining initial U.S. television experience amid the city's pronounced racial and social divides, which he later described as eye-opening compared to Canada.[10] Roberts briefly returned to Canada in 1990 to co-anchor the CTV morning program Canada A.M. until 1992.[16] That year, he rejoined the CBS fold at the network level as co-anchor of CBS Morning News, based in New York, marking his transition to national U.S. broadcast journalism.[17][18] This move followed his growing reputation in Canadian media and embedded him in CBS's morning programming, where he also substituted on CBS This Morning.[19] Roberts spent over 14 years at CBS News, advancing through roles such as New York-based evening news correspondent and chief medical correspondent before becoming chief White House correspondent in 1999.[2] In February 2006, seeking broader reporting opportunities, he departed CBS for CNN as a senior national correspondent in Washington, D.C., co-anchoring American Morning from 2007 to 2010.[19][20] These shifts reflected his pursuit of high-profile assignments amid competitive network dynamics, though CNN's cable focus differed from CBS's traditional broadcast model.[21]

Tenure at CBS and CNN

Roberts joined CBS News in 1992, where he worked for approximately 14 years until 2006.[2][22] During his tenure, he served as chief White House correspondent from 1999 to 2005, reporting on significant events such as the impeachment of President Bill Clinton, the disputed 2000 presidential election, and the September 11, 2001, terrorist attacks.[7] He also acted as chief medical correspondent and anchored the weekend edition of CBS Evening News.[2][1] In February 2006, Roberts transitioned to CNN as a senior national correspondent, based initially in Washington, D.C.[22][16] He covered national political developments and anchored segments like This Week in Washington. In April 2007, he relocated to New York to co-anchor CNN's morning program American Morning alongside Kiran Chetry, a role he held until December 2010 amid a show revamp.[2][16][23] Roberts' departure from CNN was announced on January 3, 2011, as he accepted a position at Fox News Channel.[24][25] His time at both networks highlighted his focus on White House and national political reporting, with CBS emphasizing on-scene coverage of administration actions and CNN featuring studio anchoring of daily news digests.[21]

Position at Fox News


John Roberts joined Fox News Channel on January 3, 2011, as a senior national correspondent based in the Atlanta bureau.[13][26] Prior to this, he had departed CNN after five years there.[27] In this initial role, Roberts contributed to coverage of national stories and presidential elections, having reported on every such contest since 1996.[28] On December 12, 2016, Fox News appointed Roberts as its chief White House correspondent, with him assuming the position on Inauguration Day, January 20, 2017, and relocating to Washington, D.C.[3][29] This promotion followed his work on the Republican presidential primary and general election campaigns.[29] As chief White House correspondent, Roberts covered the administrations of Presidents Donald Trump and Joe Biden, providing on-the-ground reporting from the White House.[2] In addition to his correspondent duties, Roberts currently co-anchors America Reports weekdays from 1 to 3 p.m. ET on Fox News Channel.[2] This dual role underscores his extensive experience in political journalism, spanning over three decades.[2]

Reporting and Contributions

Major Assignments and Coverage


Roberts served as an embedded reporter with the 2nd Light Armored Reconnaissance Battalion of the U.S. Marines during the initial invasion of Iraq in March 2003, providing live reports from southern Iraq as forces advanced toward Baghdad while with CBS News.[2] He continued war coverage at CNN, anchoring This Week at War focused on operations in Iraq and Afghanistan, and reported from combat zones during the 2006 U.S. mid-term elections.[16] Additionally, he covered the 2006 Israel-Hezbollah conflict, for which he received an Edward R. Murrow Award.[16] In domestic disaster reporting, Roberts anchored CBS Evening News coverage of Hurricane Katrina in 2005, spending 10 consecutive days on the ground documenting the devastation in New Orleans and its aftermath.[2] [30] He also reported on the death of Pope John Paul II that year.[2] Later, at Fox News, he covered Hurricane Irene in 2011 and the U.S. Ebola outbreak.[2] As Chief White House Correspondent for CBS in the late 1990s and early 2000s, and later for Fox News starting in 2017, Roberts covered multiple presidential administrations, including those of Bill Clinton, George W. Bush, and Donald Trump.[2] At Fox, he attended briefings and reported on key events such as the 2018 U.S.-Russia summit in Helsinki, the 2019 U.S.-North Korea summit in Hanoi, NATO summits, and Trump's visits to the United Kingdom.[2] He provided extensive coverage of Trump's 2021 second impeachment trial and the January 6, 2021, Capitol events.[2] Roberts also reported on the 2016 Republican presidential primary, interviewing candidates including Trump, Carly Fiorina, and Marco Rubio, and covered national conventions and debates in 2012 and 2016.[2] More recently, he covered the Israel-Hamas war from Jerusalem and the 2025 inauguration of President Trump and Vice President Vance.[2] Roberts has anchored major programs across networks, including weekend editions of CBS Evening News, CBS Morning News, and co-anchoring CNN's American Morning from 2007 to 2010.[16] [2] His international assignments include the NATO bombing of Yugoslavia, the July 2005 London bombings, and earthquakes in Turkey.[16]

Awards and Recognitions

Roberts received three national Emmy Awards during his tenure at CBS News: one for coverage of the 1996 Atlanta Centennial Olympic Park bombing, another for the 1997 death of Diana, Princess of Wales, and a third for reporting on the mid-air explosion of TWA Flight 800 off Long Island, New York, in July 1996.[15] While anchoring evening newscasts at WCBS-TV in New York from 1994 to 1995, he earned an additional Emmy Award and a New York Press Club award for his reporting.[16] In 2009, during his time at CNN, Roberts was inducted into the Canadian Broadcast Hall of Fame in recognition of his contributions to broadcasting.[10]

Controversies and Criticisms

Engagements with Political Figures

Roberts engaged in a contentious exchange with White House Press Secretary Kayleigh McEnany on October 1, 2020, during a briefing following President Donald Trump's debate comments urging the Proud Boys to "stand back and stand by." Roberts repeatedly sought a "definitive and unambiguous" condemnation of white supremacist groups from McEnany, who instead emphasized Trump's prior disavowals and shifted focus to left-wing extremism; Roberts expressed visible frustration on air, later describing her response as evasive.[31][4] This interaction drew praise from some media outlets for pressing the administration but criticism from Trump supporters who viewed it as overly adversarial toward a Republican-led White House. In May 2020, amid Trump's public attacks on Fox News as "fake" and overly critical, Roberts conducted a private one-on-one discussion with the president immediately after Air Force One landed in Michigan, separate from the press pool. The off-record conversation fueled speculation among fellow reporters about potential efforts to mend relations or influence coverage, highlighting tensions between the network and the administration despite Fox's generally favorable stance toward Trump.[32] The Biden campaign targeted Roberts with two memos on June 4 and 5, 2024, accusing him of misleading viewers on "America's Newsroom" about President Joe Biden's international diplomacy, including claims that Biden had limited interactions with world leaders compared to predecessors; the campaign labeled these assertions false and demanded corrections, portraying Roberts as part of a pattern of Fox News distortion favoring Trump.[33] Earlier, in an unspecified 2024 incident, the campaign criticized Roberts for attributing Biden's insulin price caps to Trump, prompting calls for Fox to retract the segment.[34] Roberts drew scrutiny from conservatives in October 2025 for on-air mockery of Trump's prior disavowals of Project 2025, a conservative policy blueprint, after the president embraced elements of it; Roberts highlighted the inconsistency, eliciting backlash from Trump allies who accused him of undermining the former president.[35] These episodes underscore Roberts' role in straight-news reporting, where probing questions to officials from both parties have alternately provoked accusations of insufficient loyalty from partisans on the left and right.

Accusations of Bias and Responses

Roberts has faced accusations primarily from conservative commentators and viewers that his reporting displays insufficient allegiance to former President Donald Trump, particularly during the latter's White House tenure. In July 2018, after Trump labeled CNN reporter Jim Acosta "fake news" and a "terrible person" during a press event in London, Roberts publicly stated that the criticism of Acosta and NBC was "unfair," prompting backlash from some Fox News audience members who viewed it as disloyalty to the president rather than defense of journalistic standards.[36][37] A similar pattern emerged in October 2020, when Roberts pressed White House Press Secretary Kayleigh McEnany for a "definitive and unambiguous" condemnation of white supremacist groups following Trump's debate comments, expressing frustration at deflections by stating, "I'm tired of this. I'm really tired of this." Conservative critics, including online commentators, accused him of anti-Trump bias for the pointed questioning, interpreting it as aligning with mainstream media narratives.[31][4] In response, Roberts dismissed the detractors, remarking on air, "For all of you on Twitter who are hammering me for asking that question, I don't care," emphasizing his commitment to eliciting clear answers regardless of political pressure.[38] Roberts has also encountered direct scrutiny from Trump, who in September 2019 summoned him to the Oval Office post-briefing to dispute Roberts' on-air fact-check of Trump's claim that the Alabama-LSU football game would be played in Tuscaloosa despite weather forecasts, with Trump insisting the reporter accept his version uncritically.[39] In interviews, Roberts has countered perceptions of favoritism or bias by arguing that rigorous questioning serves the public interest and that assumptions of Fox News leniency toward Trump overlook the network's internal diversity of views.[37] No prominent accusations of left-leaning bias have surfaced from progressive sources, with some outlets noting his confrontational style toward administration officials as evidence of professional independence.[31]

Personal Life

Family and Relationships

John Roberts was previously married to his high school sweetheart, with the union ending in divorce in 2008.[40] Roberts has been married to Kyra Phillips, an investigative correspondent for ABC News, since their engagement in 2010.[40][41] The couple welcomed fraternal twinsâ€”a daughter, Sage Ann Roberts, and a son, Kellan Clay Robertsâ€”on March 15, 2011, with Sage born first.[6][40] The family resides in the Atlanta area.[41]

Health Incidents

In August 2025, John Roberts was hospitalized for a severe case of malaria, marking the first such diagnosis his physician had encountered.[42][43] He attributed the infection to a July trip to Indonesia, experiencing intense symptoms including uncontrolled shivering during a live broadcast and pain extending from his head to his toes.[44][45] Roberts shared updates via X, indicating recovery progress and an expected return to air on September 2, 2025.[46] In February 2022, Roberts underwent a procedure to implant a pacemaker after hospitalization for failing heart electrical circuitry, stemming from a blocked stent identified during emergency tests including MRIs and CAT scans.[47][48] He had previously received three stents years earlier, linked to his history of smoking.[49] Roberts returned to hosting America Reports shortly after the implantation.[47] In October 2022, Roberts missed several days of broadcasting to undergo surgery addressing post-diverticulitis complications.[50] Roberts has also reported heart attack-like symptoms in 2017 during a White House press conference, amid ongoing cardiovascular concerns managed through lifestyle changes post-stent procedures.[49]
