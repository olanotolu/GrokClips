Dina Lohan

Donata Melina Nicolette "Dina" Lohan (/ˈdiːnə ˈloʊ.ən/ DEE-nə LOH-ən; née Sullivan;[2] born September 15, 1962)[3] is an American television personality who rose to fame as the mother and manager of actress Lindsay Lohan. She is also the mother of Michael Jr., Aliana and Dakota Lohan. As of 2025, she continues to make public appearances and engage on social media.[web:2][web:9]

Early life

Lohan was born and raised in New York City, to John L. and Ann Sullivan.[3] She has two brothers, Matt and Paul. She is of Italian (from her mother) and Irish descent, and was raised Catholic.[4][5][6]

Career

Lohan is a former singer and dancer.[7] She has worked as a talent manager, primarily for her children including Lindsay and Ali Lohan.[8] Lohan and her younger daughter Ali starred in the reality show Living Lohan, which debuted on May 26, 2008 on the cable television network E!. Lohan is also one of the executive producers of the series. In 2019, Dina was a cast member on season 2 of Celebrity Big Brother.[8]

Personal life

In 1985, she married Michael Lohan. The couple briefly separated in 1989, and later reunited.[9][10] Dina filed for divorce in 2005,[11] and the couple reached a divorce settlement in August 2007,[12] which was finalized within 60 days. She was granted custody of their two youngest children, Ali and Cody.[12] He had worked as a Wall Street trader and businessman who inherited his father's pasta business and has been in trouble with the law on several occasions.[9][13] The Lohans have four children: Lindsay (b. 1986), Michael, Jr. (b. 1987), Aliana (b. 1993), and Dakota "Cody" Lohan (b. 1996). Dina is a grandmother to three grandchildren: Luai Lohan Shammas (b. 2023), from her daughter Lindsay; and Isabelle (b. 2021) and Summer (b. 2024), from her son Michael Jr.[14] Lohan was arrested for speeding and driving under the influence in Nassau County, [Long Island](Long Island "Long Island") on September 12, 2013,[15] and agreed to a plea deal which included admitting guilt. She was sentenced to 100 hours community service, had her drivers license revoked for one year, and was fined $3,000.[16] Lohan was arrested for driving under the influence and leaving the scene of an accident in Nassau County on January 11, 2020. CNN reported Lohan also faced "four other charges: operating an unregistered vehicle, operating an uninspected vehicle, driving without a license, and aggravated unlicensed operation of a vehicle in the 3rd degree, the online court records state."[17] On December 3, 2021, she pleaded guilty to leaving the scene of an accident without reporting and to felony DWI and was sentenced to 18 days in jail, loss of her license for 18 months, and five years’ probation during which she must participate in a DWI program and have an ignition interlock on her vehicle to make sure she is not drinking before driving.[18]
