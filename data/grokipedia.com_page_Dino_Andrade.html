Dino Andrade

Dino S. Andrade (born September 16, 1963)[1] is an American voice actor in video games and animated projects. He manages dual careers as both a voice actor and the creator and former owner and operator of the geek dating and community website SoulGeek.com (2007–2024).

Early life

Andrade was born to Mexican parents. His father was serving in the United States Air Force at the time of his birth, and the family lived on an Air Force base during his early childhood before returning to California. Andrade has one sister, Sabrina, born six years later to the day, on September 16, 1969. He says that his love of all things science fiction, horror and fantasy is lifelong and in his blood. He has been quoted saying, "I’m one of those guys that while in acting class, along with everyone else studying, I was studying Batman comics," thus attributing his love of animation and graphic storytelling to making his career in voice acting the natural choice for him.[2]

Career

Andrade started as an actor on stage and doing comedy improvisation, having been trained at the Groundlings. His official start in voice over work happened when he drove a friend and fellow actor to an audition to do some background looping on the 1985 feature film Girls Just Want to Have Fun, starring the then unknowns Sarah Jessica Parker and Helen Hunt. He was asked if he would like to audition as well and was hired for the film. From this, he was recommended to the director Steve Miner who was doing post-production on the horror/comedy film House, starring William Katt, George Wendt and Richard Moll. Though not credited in the final release, Andrade was hired to do the creature sounds of the demons that kidnap a child that Katt's character has to rescue.[2] He continued in voice acting and acting in general until the early 1990s when he left acting to pursue independent film making. Around 2002, Andrade returned to acting with a focus on voice acting training and has been in voice acting ever since.[3] In addition to voice acting, Andrade created, owns, and operates the geek dating and community website SoulGeek.com.[3]

Personal life

Andrade was married to the voice actress Mary Kay Bergman from 1990 until her death by suicide on November 11, 1999.[4] Andrade has publicly stated that she suffered in secret with mental health issues until her death.[5] Andrade has become active in raising mental health awareness. He had planned to launch the Mary Kay Bergman Project, a mental health outreach program for artists and entertainers, but ultimately decided not to proceed, as it was deemed unnecessary given existing resources and for emotional reasons. He encourages support for established suicide prevention and mental health organizations.[6] He has since remarried[5] and has one child with his spouse. Andrade has an interest in science fiction, horror, fantasy and animation. He was inspired to create SoulGeek.com, a geek dating and community website. The site was launched in July 2007.
