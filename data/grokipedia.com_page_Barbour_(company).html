Barbour (company)

J. Barbour & Sons, Limited, trading as Barbour, is an English luxury and lifestyle brand founded by John Barbour in 1894 that designs, manufactures and markets waxed cotton outerwear, ready-to-wear clothing, footwear and accessories under the Barbour and Barbour International brands. It remains family-owned and operated. Founded in South Shields, England, as an importer of oil cloth, J. Barbour and Sons Ltd became known for its waxed cotton jackets, a common element of British country clothing; some refer to any waxed cotton jacket, regardless of brand, as a "Barbour jacket".[2][3] Barbour holds a royal warrant for the supply of â€˜waterproof and protective clothingâ€™ from King Charles III (originally granted 1987, renewed 2024).[4][5]

History

John Barbour, a native of Galloway, Scotland, founded J. Barbour and Sons Ltd in South Shields, England, in 1894 as an importer of oil-cloth.[4][2] The surrounding community included seamen and sailors, so Barbour produced clothing suitable for the harsh weather conditions they faced in the North Sea. During WWII, Barbour created weatherproof outdoor clothing for the military, including the submarine service.[6][7] John's grandson Duncan, a keen motorcyclist, introduced waxed cotton motorcycling suits in 1936 during his tenure at the company.[7] The iconic wax cotton International motorcycle jacket was worn by many riders in the International Six Day Trials circuit, with Steve McQueen being one of the well known riders to have worn it.[6] The headquarters for J. Barbour and Sons Ltd at the time of its foundation was Market Place, South Shields and the company remains in Simonside, South Shields today having occupied its current site since 1981. Since July 2016, Scottish actor Sam Heughan has been Barbour's first Global Brand Ambassador.[8]

Leadership

Since the incorporation of J. Barbour & Sons as a limited company in 1912, the company has always been led by a member of the Barbour family. The company is currently led by the family's fourth generation, Dame Margaret Barbour, who took over in 1973 after the death of her mother-in-law, Nancy Barbour.8 The family's fifth generation is represented by Helen Barbour, who has been the Vice Chair since 1997 and is next in line for the chairmanship.[9]

Products

In addition to its waxed and quilted jackets, J. Barbour & Sons Ltd is famous for sweaters, moleskin clothing, corduroy clothing, and tattersall shirts. Barbour entered the "waterproof-breathable" market with its own type of waterproof liners, Cordura external fabric, and polar fleece sweaters. Some Barbour products contain outdoor-orientated features such as snug-fitting collars, "storm cuffs", waterproof pockets with drainage holes, and wide "game" pockets. In recent years, the company modernized its collections to appeal to a younger clientele while maintaining credibility with the traditional countryside customer. Many of the more fashionable ranges are influenced by the "Barbour International" motorcycling ranges. Taking inspiration from the company's 1936 waxed cotton motorcycling suit and the 1951 "Original Green" motorcycling jacket, modern collections are fashionable interpretations of the companies heritage in bikes. While the traditional waxed cotton jackets are still manufactured in Simonside, South Shields, some of the other products are manufactured outside the United Kingdom.[4] Despite traditional connotations with rural life, Barbour jackets saw increased popularity as an urban fashion item in the 2010s decade.[10][2] In the 2010s and early 2020s Barbour collaborated with several urban fashion brands including Wood Wood[11], Ally Capellino[12] and White Mountaineering.[13] The Barbour Beaufort jacket is a waterproof waxed cotton coat with bellow pockets. Despite being designed in 1983 for country sports, over four decades later its popularity continues, but it is now as likely to be seen in a Soho bar or at a festival, as it is on a country estate.[14][15] More recent collaborations, such as with ALEXACHUNG (2019), GANNI (2022), and Gucci (2023), have further expanded the brand's appeal to contemporary audiences.[15]

Services

Wear-and-tear on Barbour garments can be addressed by Barbour's repair service, which patches and reinforces jackets. Barbour repairs and rewaxes over 60,000 jackets annually at their factory in Simonside, South Shields and at other facilities.[16] As part of the Wax for Life program launched in 2019, customers can return waxed jackets for professional repair and rewaxing or use at-home care kits. The program also includes the Re-Loved initiative, where old waxed jackets are upcycled, repaired, rewaxed, and resold, or customers receive a voucher for a new purchase.[17][18] In October 2024, Barbour introduced the Quilt for Life service, allowing cleaning and repairs for quilted jackets at their South Shields facility.[19] Ahead of current sustainability trends, Barbour has been re-waxing clothing for over 100 years, increasing the garments' longevity.[16] Aside from offering merchandise within Barbour retail stores and via the Barbour website, Barbour wholesales all of its collections to many upscale shops worldwide.

In popular culture

Barbour's waxed jackets and coats have appeared in 2012's Skyfall, in which James Bond, played by Daniel Craig, wears a modified Barbour jacket.[20] Barbour jackets have also featured in television series such as The Crown and Succession.[2] Actor Sam Heughan, known for his role in Outlander, has served as Barbour's global brand ambassador since 2016, appearing in campaigns and capsule collections inspired by his Scottish heritage.[21] Barbour has a long-standing line of clothing inspired by actor and avid motorcycle racer Steve McQueen, who famously wore Barbour during his motorcycle races, such as the 1964 International Six Days Trial.[22][2]
