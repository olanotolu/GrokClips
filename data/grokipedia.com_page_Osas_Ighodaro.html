Osas Ighodaro

Osariemen Martha Elizabeth Ighodaro (born 26 October 1990), better known as Osas Ighodaro , is a Nigerian-American actress, producer, television host and philanthropist. She won the 2010 Miss Black USA Pageant and founded the Joyful Joy Foundation , a fundraising organization for malaria.[1] Ighodaro emerged as the highest-grossing Nollywood actress of 2018[2] and was awarded Best Actress in a Drama consecutively at the 2022 and 2023 AMVCA for her roles in Rattlesnake: The Ahanna Story[3] and Man of God , [4] respectively. In 2025, she starred in and produced 3 Cold Dishes and hosted the AMVCA.

Early life and background

Ighodaro was born on 26 October 1990 in Bronx, New York, United States, and raised in the Bronx, to Nigerian parents of Edo origin. She obtained her degree in Broadcast Journalism with dual minor degrees in Entrepreneurship and Theater from Pennsylvania State University. She further obtained her MA in Fine Arts from the Actors Studio Drama School at Pace University. She is a member of Alpha Kappa Alpha sorority and the founder of Imuetinyan Productions.

Ighodaro's Career

Career

Ighodaro began her acting career in New York after graduating college. She appeared in small roles on U.S. television series such as Law & Order and Meet the Browns, along with performing in theatre plays and indie films, including a minor role in the feature film Cadillac Records (2008). In 2012, she moved to Nigeria where she played the role of Adanna in the television series Tinsel. Since then, she has starred in Nollywood productions such as Gbomo Gbomo Express (2015) and The Smart Money Woman (2020). Following her performances in Rattlesnake: The Ahanna Story (2021) and Man of God (2022), she was awarded Best Actress in a Drama at the 2022 and 2023 Africa Magic Viewers' Choice Awards, respectively. Ighodaro co-hosted The Headies 2022, The Headies 2023, the 2014 Africa Magic Viewers' Choice Awards, and the family show Maltina Dance All (2013â€“2015). In 2025, she hosted the Africa Magic Viewers' Choice Awards and continued starring in films such as When Love Strikes (2024).

Personal life

Ighodaro married Gbenro Ajibade in 2015.[5] They had a daughter in 2016.[5] The couple separated in late 2018[5] and divorced in 2019.[6] They co-parent their child amicably.[7]
