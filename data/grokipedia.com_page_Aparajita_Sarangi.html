Aparajita Sarangi


Aparajita Sarangi (born 8 October 1969) is an Indian politician and former civil servant who serves as the Member of Parliament for the Bhubaneswar constituency in Odisha, representing the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP).[1][2]
Elected in 2019 and re-elected in 2024 with 512,519 votes, she has been active in parliamentary debates and international forums, including as an executive member of the Inter-Parliamentary Union (IPU).[3][4][5]
A 1994-batch Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer of the Odisha cadre, Sarangi applied for voluntary retirement on 15 September 2018 and formally quit the service in November before joining the BJP on 27 November 2018 to contest elections.[6][7][8]
Born in Muzaffarpur, Bihar, to Ajit Mishra, an English professor, and Kusum Mishra, she earned a B.A. in English Honours from S.M. College, Bhagalpur University.[9][1]
As a national spokesperson for the BJP, she is noted for her outspoken positions on national security and governance issues.[4][10]

Early life and education

Upbringing and family background

Aparajita Sarangi was born on 8 October 1969 in Muzaffarpur district, Bihar, to Ajit Mishra and Kusum Mishra.[1][9] Her father, Ajit Mishra, served as a professor of English, reflecting an academic family environment that emphasized education.[2][11] Limited public details exist regarding her specific childhood experiences or early schooling in Bihar, though her formative years occurred in this region prior to pursuing higher education.[9] Sarangi hails from a Brahmin family, with both parents contributing to a scholarly household.[12] She is married to Santosh Kumar Sarangi, and the couple has two children: a son named Shikhar Sarangi and a daughter named Archita Sarangi.[1][13]

Academic achievements and civil services preparation

Sarangi completed her matriculation from Mount Carmel School in Bhagalpur, Bihar.[12] She subsequently pursued higher education, graduating with a Bachelor of Arts degree (honors) in English from S. M. College, under Bhagalpur University in Bihar.[1][9] After completing her undergraduate studies, Sarangi focused on preparing for the Union Public Service Commission's Civil Services Examination (CSE). She cleared the CSE in 1994 on her attempt, securing selection into the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) in the Odisha cadre as a 1994-batch officer.[9][11] This achievement occurred when she was approximately 24 years old, marking her entry into public administration without prior attempts documented in available records.[9] No specific details on her preparation strategy, such as coaching institutes or study duration, are publicly detailed in official or verified sources.

Civil service career

Entry into IAS and initial postings

Aparajita Sarangi was selected to the Indian Administrative Service in 1994 following her success in the Civil Services Examination conducted by the Union Public Service Commission, marking her first attempt at the exam, and was allotted to the Odisha cadre.[11][9] Her early career focused on sub-divisional administration, where she served as Sub-Collector in multiple subdivisions, including her first posting at Hindol in 1997.[14] In these roles, she handled responsibilities such as revenue administration, law and order maintenance, and developmental oversight at the grassroots level, typical for probationary officers transitioning from training.[9] She also acted as Additional District Magistrate during this period, gaining experience in district-level coordination.[14]

Major administrative roles and accomplishments

Sarangi served as Sub-Collector in three subdivisions and Additional District Magistrate from 1996 to 1998.[1] [12] From 1998 to 2006, she held the position of District Magistrate and Collector in four Odisha districts: Khordha, Nuapada, Koraput, and Bargarh.[1] [12] In these roles, she focused on local governance, revenue administration, and development initiatives in tribal and underdeveloped areas, earning recognition for administrative efficiency.[11] As Municipal Commissioner of Bhubaneswar from 2006 to 2009, Sarangi oversaw urban development projects that modernized the city's infrastructure, including the organization of street vendor zones and aesthetic enhancements such as Saura tribal art murals on public walls.[11] [12] These efforts contributed to improved urban planning and cultural integration in the state capital.[11] Subsequently, from 2009 to 2013, she was Secretary in the Odisha state government's departments of School Education, Panchayati Raj, and Textiles, where she organized social and cultural programs targeting youth and women empowerment.[12] [11] In 2012, she received the Shakti Samman award for her contributions to public service.[12] [11] On central deputation from 2013 to 2018, Sarangi served as Joint Secretary in the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India, where she implemented the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) with emphasis on transparency and accountability mechanisms.[11]

Challenges and criticisms during tenure

During her tenure as Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation Commissioner from 2006 to 2009, Sarangi implemented urban reforms including the establishment of vending zones to regulate street commerce, which earned praise for improving city infrastructure but drew resistance from informal vendors affected by relocations.[15] These measures, aimed at enhancing public order and hygiene, faced pushback from local traders who viewed them as disruptive to livelihoods, though no formal charges of misconduct arose.[16] Sarangi later described broader challenges in the male-dominated IAS cadre, attributing difficulties to "male insecurities" and egos that hindered collaboration and advancement for female officers. In a June 2022 interview, she explained that such dynamics made it hard to "handle men" in bureaucratic settings, contributing to frustrations over 24 years of service despite her reputation for diligence.[17] No substantiated allegations of corruption or maladministration were leveled against her during postings, contrasting with occasional political critiques post-resignation.[11]

Resignation from service

Aparajita Sarangi, a 1994-batch Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer of the Odisha cadre, applied for voluntary retirement from service (VRS) in 2018 after approximately 24 years in the bureaucracy.[11][16] Her decision came amid reports of internal bureaucratic challenges, including instances where professional dynamics influenced her career trajectory, though she publicly emphasized a shift toward direct public service through electoral politics.[18] The Odisha state government approved Sarangi's VRS request on November 14, 2018, following a period of deliberation that included a notice indicating potential deemed approval if not explicitly addressed.[19][20][21] At the time, she had over 11 years of service remaining until mandatory retirement, positioning her resignation as a deliberate exit to pursue opportunities outside administrative roles.[22] The approval facilitated her immediate transition, with Sarangi formally joining the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) on November 27, 2018, explicitly to contest the 2019 Lok Sabha elections from Bhubaneswar.[23][21] No formal objections from central or state authorities were reported, distinguishing her case from other IAS resignations that faced prolonged scrutiny under the Narendra Modi administration.[24] This process underscored her established administrative record, including prior central deputations, as a factor in the relatively swift clearance.[25]

Entry into politics

Joining the Bharatiya Janata Party

Aparajita Sarangi, a 1994-batch Odisha-cadre Indian Administrative Service officer, took voluntary retirement from service in September 2018 before formally joining the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) on November 27, 2018, at the party's national headquarters in New Delhi.[8][26] The event was presided over by BJP national president Amit Shah, with Union Minister Dharmendra Pradhan and other senior leaders present, marking her transition from bureaucracy to active politics.[27][15] Sarangi cited her motivation as expanding public service beyond the constraints of administrative postings, stating she aimed "to work for people on a very large scale."[26] BJP strategists viewed her induction as a strategic move to bolster the party's outreach to women voters in Odisha, where the ruling Biju Janata Dal held a perceived advantage in this demographic, and positioned her as a likely candidate for the Bhubaneswar Lok Sabha seat in the upcoming 2019 general elections.[21][23] Her entry aligned with a broader trend of senior civil servants resigning to join politics ahead of polls, amid discussions on the ethics of such transitions under civil service rules requiring a cooling-off period.[28]

2019 Lok Sabha campaign and victory

Aparajita Sarangi, a former Indian Administrative Service officer who had served as commissioner of the Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation, joined the Bharatiya Janata Party on November 27, 2018, resigning from service to pursue politics.[8] The BJP fielded her as its candidate for the Bhubaneswar Lok Sabha constituency in the 2019 general elections, pitting her bureaucratic expertise against the Biju Janata Dal's (BJD) incumbent influence in the urban seat, which the regional party had held since 1998.[29] Her campaign leveraged her 24 years of administrative experience, focusing on development agendas aligned with Prime Minister Narendra Modi's national vision, including infrastructure improvements and criticism of the BJD government's handling of poverty and resource mismanagement in Odisha despite the state's mineral wealth.[30] Sarangi's primary opponent was Arup Patnaik, a retired Indian Police Service officer and former Mumbai Police Commissioner, nominated by the BJD to retain the seat previously won by Prasanna Kumar Patasani.[22] The contest highlighted an IAS-versus-IPS dynamic, with Sarangi emphasizing local governance reforms from her municipal tenure and the need for central intervention to address urban challenges like traffic and sanitation in Bhubaneswar.[31] Polling occurred on April 23, 2019, as part of the third phase of the national elections, with voter turnout in the constituency reflecting strong urban participation.[32] Results were declared on May 23, 2019, with Sarangi securing victory by 23,839 votes, polling 486,991 votes (48.4% of the valid votes) against Patnaik's 463,152 (46.1%).[33] This win marked the BJP's breakthrough in the BJD-dominated Bhubaneswar seat, contributing to the party's eight seats in Odisha amid a national mandate for the National Democratic Alliance.[34]

Parliamentary career

Key legislative activities and contributions

Sarangi has been an active participant in Lok Sabha proceedings, with attendance rates of 85% in the 17th Lok Sabha (2019-2024) and 89% in the 18th Lok Sabha (2024 onwards), surpassing national averages in both terms.[35][36] She raised 212 questions in her first term and 109 in the initial period of her second term through August 2025, focusing on topics including artificial intelligence applications, capacity building for women, urban housing affordability, and e-mobility schemes.[35][36] These questions often highlighted constituency-specific concerns in Bhubaneswar, such as infrastructure and governance efficiency, while also addressing national issues like health and family welfare. In debates, Sarangi contributed 36 interventions during the 17th Lok Sabha on matters including mining irregularities in Odisha on December 14, 2022, and the Wild Life (Protection) Amendment Bill on August 2, 2022.[35] Her eight debates in the 18th Lok Sabha included support for modernizing immigration laws under the Immigration and Foreigners Bill, 2025, emphasizing consolidated government powers to address infiltration and border security.[37] She did not introduce any private members' bills in either term, aligning with the low national average for such initiatives.[35][36] Sarangi's committee service underscores her legislative influence, particularly in oversight and policy formulation. Appointed to the Standing Committee on Housing and Urban Affairs in September 2019, she leveraged her prior role as Bhubaneswar Municipal Commissioner to contribute to urban development deliberations, including questions on gender equality in urban planning and affordable housing.[1][36] Other roles encompass the Committee on Ethics (since October 2019), Public Accounts Committee (August 2024), Committee on External Affairs (September 2024), and Joint Committee on the Waqf (Amendment) Bill, 2024 (August 2024), where she participated in scrutinizing financial accountability and policy reforms.[1] A prominent contribution involved advocating for women's legislative representation; in September 2023, she spoke in support of the Constitution (One Hundred and Twenty-Eighth Amendment) Bill, 2023, which proposed reserving one-third of seats for women in Parliament and state assemblies, emphasizing equal participation in national development.[38] Her interventions often drew on empirical governance experience to critique inefficiencies and propose practical enhancements, such as in health welfare and urban infrastructure committees.[1]

International engagements and national security advocacy

Sarangi has participated in India's diplomatic outreach efforts on counter-terrorism, notably as part of a multi-party delegation in May-June 2025 under Operation Sindoor, aimed at conveying New Delhi's zero-tolerance policy following the Pahalgam terrorist attack.[39] [40] The delegation, led by JD(U) MP Sanjay Kumar Jha, visited Japan on May 22, South Korea on May 24, Singapore on May 27, Indonesia on May 28, and Malaysia on May 31, where Sarangi engaged with parliamentarians, think tanks, and academia to emphasize India's preference for peace while asserting its resolve to protect citizens against cross-border terrorism.[41] [42] During these interactions, she highlighted international support for India's position, including endorsements from Malaysian officials, and reiterated that combating terrorism enjoys broad global consensus.[43] [44] In September 2025, as a member of the Parliamentary Standing Committee on External Affairs, Sarangi met with U.S. Congresspersons to discuss bilateral issues, including national security priorities.[36] She also led bilateral discussions in Geneva, Switzerland, in October 2025, at the request of Indian parliamentary colleagues, focusing on inter-parliamentary cooperation.[45] During the Inter-Parliamentary Union (IPU) executive committee meeting in October 2025, she criticized Pakistan's role in regional instability, framing India's responses as acts of self-defense and urging that national security override partisan divides.[46] [47] Sarangi has advocated for robust national security measures in parliamentary proceedings, including support for the Immigration and Foreigners Bill, 2025, which she described as essential to bar entry to individuals posing threats, stating that India is not a "dharamshala" for unchecked immigration.[37] [48] In the context of the Ethics Committee probe into the "cash-for-query" case, she raised concerns over systemic vulnerabilities that could compromise national security through unauthorized access to parliamentary resources.[49] Her positions consistently align with a firm stance on counter-terrorism, asserting India's readiness to act decisively to safeguard its people while promoting diplomacy.[50] [51]

Infrastructure and constituency development initiatives

As the Member of Parliament for Bhubaneswar since 2019, Aparajita Sarangi has prioritized infrastructure enhancements and constituency development through targeted use of her Member of Parliament Local Area Development (MPLAD) funds and advocacy with state and central authorities. Her initiatives emphasize urban mobility, public amenities, and rural-urban connectivity in the Bhubaneswar parliamentary constituency, which spans eight blocks and includes the capital city. Sarangi has released periodic report cards detailing these efforts, such as her February 2024 summary of 50 achievements over five years, highlighting sanctioned projects worth hundreds of crores.[52][53] Key transportation infrastructure projects include the facilitation of 10 flyovers and four foot-over bridges at a total cost of â‚¹508 crore, addressing traffic congestion in Bhubaneswar. The Hansapal flyover, a long-delayed project, saw renewed momentum under her advocacy, with Sarangi publicly addressing implementation hurdles in January 2025. Additionally, she championed the Bhubaneswar Bypass project, which received environmental clearance in August 2025, aimed at easing urban traffic by diverting heavy vehicles. A landmark achievement was the approval of the 111-km six-lane access-controlled Capital Region Ring Road in August 2025, connecting Bhubaneswar's peripheral areas and benefiting millions through improved logistics and reduced city congestion.[53][54][55][56] In rural and community development, Sarangi allocated MPLAD funds for modern libraries doubling as coaching institutes in each of the eight blocks, with â‚¹53 lakh budgeted per facility to include digital infrastructure and reading centers, announced in June 2025. She also inaugurated â‚¹20 crore worth of projects in Khordha block in January 2025, comprising a new auditorium and a daily market at TLC field to boost local commerce. Urban amenities efforts include a proposed community park in Nayapalli, land acquisition for the Vishwakarma community, and early initiatives like installing 1,000 solar street lights starting in 2020. Sarangi's September 2025 discussions with Odisha Chief Minister Mohan Charan Majhi advanced these, including Notified Area Council declarations for better governance. These projects reflect a convergence model to maximize fund utilization, though implementation timelines have occasionally faced delays due to land and environmental clearances.[57][58][59][60][61]

2024 re-election and ongoing term

In the 2024 Indian general election, Aparajita Sarangi secured re-election from the Bhubaneswar Lok Sabha constituency on a Bharatiya Janata Party ticket, polling 512,519 votes.[3] Polling occurred on May 25, 2024, with results declared on June 5, 2024.[62] She defeated the Biju Janata Dal candidate by a margin of 35,152 votes, improving her 2019 victory margin of 41,365 despite a more competitive field including a Congress contender.[3] During her ongoing term in the 18th Lok Sabha, Sarangi has actively participated in parliamentary proceedings, raising 109 questions on topics such as the SASCI tourism scheme in February 2025 and urban planning committees in December 2024.[36] She has advocated for uninterrupted implementation of development projects under the new BJP-led Odisha state government, praising Chief Minister Mohan Charan Majhi's administration in April 2025 for its performance in transforming the state capital.[63] Sarangi met Majhi in September 2025 at Lok Seva Bhavan to discuss constituency-specific infrastructure and development initiatives, reflecting her continued focus on local advancement.[64] Her engagements underscore alignment with the central and state BJP governments' priorities on economic growth and urban infrastructure in Bhubaneswar.[36]

Controversies

COVID-19 protocol violation allegations

In June 2020, Aparajita Sarangi was fined â‚¹300 by Bhubaneswar police for failing to wear a mask and violating social distancing norms during a political meeting attended by her and approximately 20 BJP workers.[65][66] Sarangi acknowledged the lapse and paid the penalty, with images of the gathering circulating on social media prompting the enforcement action.[67] On October 9, 2020, Sarangi faced further accusations during her birthday celebration, where she was photographed without a mask amid a crowd of women supporters exhibiting no social distancing, prompting claims of habitual non-compliance with COVID-19 protocols.[68][69] The following day, the Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation (BMC) sealed her constituency office for 15 days, citing violations including mass gatherings, absence of masks, and lack of sanitization, with the site scheduled for disinfection during the closure.[70][71] Odisha's Revenue and Disaster Management Minister, D.S. Mishra, escalated the matter by writing to the Union Ministry of Home Affairs, alleging repeated breaches by Sarangi despite prior advisories, warnings, and at least three prior FIRs against her for similar infractions since July 2020.[72][73] Sarangi denied flouting guidelines in the birthday incident, claiming adherence to protocols, while countering with evidence of violations by Biju Janata Dal (BJD) leaders to highlight perceived selective enforcement amid the state's BJD-led government.[73][74] The BJD criticized her actions as setting a poor precedent for public compliance.[75]

Political criticisms and responses

In May 2024, during the Lok Sabha election campaign, Odisha Chief Minister Naveen Patnaik, leader of the Biju Janata Dal (BJD), publicly questioned Aparajita Sarangi's contributions to Bhubaneswar's development over her tenure as MP, accusing her of stalling key infrastructure projects and failing to secure central funds effectively.[76][77] BJD leaders, including V.K. Pandian, echoed these claims, portraying Sarangi as obstructive to state-led initiatives in the capital.[77] Sarangi responded by challenging Patnaik to a direct debate on her parliamentary record, citing specific achievements such as securing over â‚¹1,000 crore in central funding for Bhubaneswar projects, including smart city enhancements and the Bhubaneswar Bypass, and attributing the attacks to BJD's frustration after 24 years of governance failures in Odisha.[78][77] The BJD further alleged in April 2024 that Sarangi violated Election Commission guidelines by involving children in her campaign rallies and meetings for political gain, urging action against her.[79] No formal Election Commission ruling was reported, and Sarangi's campaign continued without interruption. In July 2025, amid protests over a Balasore student's self-immolation, BJD spokespersons accused Sarangi of engaging in petty politics by shifting blame to the opposition's past governance rather than focusing on victim justice.[80] Sarangi countered that BJD and Congress, despite decades in power, had neglected women's safety infrastructure, calling their bandh calls opportunistic distractions from systemic failures under their rule.[81]

Political positions

Critiques of regional governance failures

Sarangi has repeatedly attributed Odisha's persistent poverty rates to systemic governance shortcomings under the Biju Janata Dal (BJD) administration, arguing that despite over two decades in power, the state lags in poverty alleviation metrics. In April 2019, she highlighted that Odisha's high poverty incidence reflects "failed governance," pointing to inadequate utilization of central funds and policy inertia as root causes.[30] She has criticized the state government's handling of temple endowments and heritage sites, alleging widespread encroachments and mismanagement of religious lands. In May 2022, Sarangi noted that of the 44,221 acres under the Endowments Department, only 8-10% remained in temple possession due to alleged illegal allocations and poor oversight, including disputes over land for the Haribaldev Jew temple.[82] Similar concerns were raised in March 2021 regarding demolitions around heritage structures near temples, which she described as reflective of haphazard urban planning under Chief Minister Naveen Patnaik.[83] On corruption and administrative capture, Sarangi accused a close aide to the Chief Minister, V.K. Pandian, of effectively hijacking state governance in June 2023, claiming decisions were funneled through this bureaucrat rather than elected processes. She linked this to scandals like the Dera Sacha Sauda land allocation, labeling the BJD's "transparent governance" narrative a "monumental failure" marked by nepotism and opacity as of October 2023.[84][85] Sarangi has pointed to Odisha's underwhelming rankings in national performance indices as evidence of policy lapses, particularly in the NITI Aayog's Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) India Index for 2020-21, where the state scored below national averages in goals related to poverty, health, and infrastructure. In June 2021, she lambasted the government for failing to improve from prior years, attributing stagnation to ad hoc decision-making across sectors.[86][87] She reiterated this critique in a November 2021 interview, decrying the "ad hoc approach in everything" from disaster response to economic planning.[88] Environmental and resource governance has also drawn her ire, with allegations of unchecked illegal sand mining under BJD watch, which she raised in parliamentary and public forums as enabling mafia control and revenue losses, exacerbating ecological degradation in riverine areas.[89] These positions underscore her broader advocacy for accountability, often contrasting state-level inertia with central interventions.

Stances on national issues and ideological alignment

Sarangi aligns ideologically with the Bharatiya Janata Party's emphasis on nationalism, developmental priorities, and governance accountability, viewing the party as inherently focused on public welfare and economic progress rather than divisive politics.[90] As a former bureaucrat turned politician, she prioritizes anti-corruption measures and efficient administration, criticizing systemic failures in prior regimes that perpetuated poverty and unemployment through governance lapses.[30][11] On national security, she advocates uncompromising defense of India's sovereignty and a zero-tolerance approach to terrorism, participating in global parliamentary engagements to project this position. During the Inter-Parliamentary Union Assembly in Geneva on October 20, 2025, Sarangi directly countered Pakistan's invocation of Jammu and Kashmir, declaring the region "will always remain an integral part of India" and highlighting Pakistan's pattern of deflecting from its internal failures.[91][10] In 2025 delegations, including Operation Sindoor outreach across multiple nations, she emphasized India's post-Pahalgam attack resolve, noting the demolition of nine terrorist infrastructures and a shift in diplomatic tenor toward proactive deterrence.[92][93] Economically, Sarangi promotes the government's vision of self-reliant growth, frequently citing India's ascent to the world's fourth-largest economyâ€”surpassing Japanâ€”with forecasts to overtake Germany as third-largest within three years, backed by $690 billion in foreign exchange reserves as of May 2025.[44][94][95] She endorses inclusive policies under Prime Minister Narendra Modi, describing his December 2024 parliamentary address on development pledges and corruption eradication as "enlightening," while raising concerns over skill gaps, such as only 42.6% employability among over 1 crore annual graduates.[96][97] In social domains like women's safety, she contends opposition parties forfeit credibility on the issue due to inaction in past incidents, contrasting this with BJP administrations' prompt responses, including Chief Minister Mohan Charan Majhi's interventions and President Droupadi Murmu's hospital visits as of July 2025.[98] Sarangi upholds party discipline by disavowing deviations from BJP values, such as a June 2025 assault by a local affiliate on a municipal official, deeming it "condemnable" and incompatible with the ideology of ethical conduct.[99][100]
