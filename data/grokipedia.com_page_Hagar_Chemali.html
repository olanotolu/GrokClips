Hagar Chemali

Hagar Hajjar Chemali is an American foreign policy analyst, political satirist, and media personality specializing in national security, sanctions, and counter-terrorist financing.[1][2] She is the creator and host of the award-winning YouTube series Oh My World!, which delivers weekly satirical breakdowns of international news and geopolitics.[1][3] Chemali spent twelve years in U.S. government service, including as Director for Syria and Lebanon at the National Security Council from 2010 to 2012, Spokesperson for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence at the Department of the Treasury, and Director of Communications and Spokesperson for the U.S. Mission to the United Nations from 2015 to 2016.[1][3] Born to Lebanese immigrant parents and raised in Greenwich, Connecticut, Chemali holds a Master of International Affairs from Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs, with concentrations in international security policy, the Middle East, counter-terrorism, and conflict resolution, as well as a Bachelor of Arts in political science from Barnard College.[1][3] In her Treasury roles, she advised on Asia and Middle East policy in the Office of Terrorist Financing and Financial Crimes, beginning as a special advisor in 2006, and managed public affairs on sanctions and illicit finance.[1] Currently, she serves as CEO of Greenwich Media Strategies, a communications consulting firm; adjunct associate professor at Columbia SIPA; and nonresident senior fellow at the Atlantic Council's GeoEconomics Center.[1][3] She frequently comments on networks including MSNBC, CNN, BBC, and Bloomberg, and is a term member of the Council on Foreign Relations.[3] Fluent in English, French, Arabic, and Italian, Chemali resides in Connecticut as a wife and mother of three.[1]

Early Life and Education

Upbringing and Family Background

Hagar Chemali was born in Greenwich Hospital and raised in Greenwich, Connecticut.[4] [1] Her parents are Lebanese immigrants from Beirut who fled the Lebanese Civil War.[5] [1] Her mother is Jewish and her father is Christian, reflecting a mixed interfaith background common among some Lebanese families.[6] This heritage, combined with stories of persecution and displacement shared by her family, shaped her early awareness of geopolitical instability and cultural pluralism.[5]

Academic and Early Professional Influences

Chemali earned a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from Barnard College, completing her undergraduate studies between 1998 and 2002.[7] This degree laid the groundwork for her interest in international relations and policy.[3] She pursued advanced studies at Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs, obtaining a Master of International Affairs in 2004 with concentrations in international security policy and the Middle East.[2] Her coursework emphasized counter-terrorism, conflict resolution, and regional dynamics, providing analytical frameworks that directly informed her subsequent focus on financial tools for national security.[1] These academic emphases, drawn from rigorous training in policy analysis and geopolitical strategy, oriented her toward roles addressing transnational threats rather than purely theoretical pursuits; for instance, an early attempt to enter PhD programs in related fields resulted in rejections, redirecting her toward practical policy application and building professional resilience.[8] Transitioning from academia, Chemali's early professional roles at the U.S. Department of the Treasury involved counter-terrorist financing and financial crimes, where she served as a senior policy advisor on Asia and as a Middle East policy advisor in the Office of Terrorist Financing and Financial Crimes.[9] These positions, beginning shortly after her master's graduation, applied her SIPA-honed expertise in sanctions and security policy to real-world efforts disrupting illicit networks, marking the start of her 12-year government tenure.[10] The integration of academic insights into operational policy work, without named mentors dominating public accounts, underscored a self-directed path influenced by empirical policy challenges in the post-9/11 era.[8]

Government Service

U.S. Treasury Department Roles

Chemali served in the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Terrorism and Financial Intelligence (TFI), which is responsible for safeguarding the financial system against illicit use and combating threats such as terrorist financing and weapons proliferation. She held policy advisory roles as a Senior Policy Advisor on Asia and as a Middle East Policy Advisor within the subordinate Office of Terrorist Financing and Financial Crimes (TFFC), where her work involved developing strategies to disrupt financial networks supporting terrorism and sanctions evasion in those regions.[1][10] From January 2014 to June 2015, Chemali acted as Spokesperson for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence, managing all media relations and public affairs for the Under Secretary for Terrorism and Financial Crimes.[11] In this capacity, she handled communications on key TFI initiatives, including sanctions designations, enforcement actions against illicit finance, and policy responses to global financial threats.[2][1] Her tenure coincided with heightened U.S. efforts to counter financing of groups like ISIS and enforce sanctions on entities in Asia and the Middle East, as evidenced by contemporaneous Treasury announcements involving TFI-led actions.[12]

U.S. Mission to the United Nations

From 2015 to 2016, Hagar Chemali served as Director of Communications and Spokesperson for the U.S. Mission to the United Nations during the Obama administration.[1][2] In this capacity, she directed all communications and public diplomacy efforts for the Mission, managing its global press strategy under Ambassador Samantha Power.[10][10] Chemali acted as the public face of the U.S. delegation to the United Nations, handling public affairs related to U.S. foreign policy positions on international issues addressed by the body.[10] Her responsibilities included shaping messaging on multilateral diplomacy, responding to global media inquiries, and coordinating press engagements amid high-profile UN debates on topics such as counterterrorism, sanctions enforcement, and regional conflicts.[2] This role followed her prior service as Spokesperson for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence at the U.S. Department of the Treasury, leveraging her expertise in national security communications.[1] During her tenure, the U.S. Mission navigated complex international dynamics, including efforts to advance Obama-era priorities like the Iran nuclear deal implementation and responses to Syrian civil war developments, though specific Chemali-led initiatives are not publicly detailed in available records.[3] She departed the position in 2016 to enter the private sector, concluding a 12-year span in U.S. government national security roles.[10]

National Security Council Positions

Hagar Chemali served as Director for Syria and Lebanon at the White House National Security Council from 2010 to 2012.[2] [10] In this capacity, she advised senior White House officials on U.S. policy toward those countries and coordinated interagency efforts to implement related initiatives.[2] [9] Her responsibilities encompassed managing responses to evolving regional dynamics, including the December 2010 onset of the Arab Spring protests across the Middle East and the March 2011 Syrian uprising that precipitated the Syrian civil war.[1] [13] Chemali's work involved aligning executive branch actions on diplomatic, humanitarian, and security measures amid escalating instability in Syria and Lebanon, though specific policy outcomes during her tenure reflected broader Obama administration priorities such as targeted sanctions and multilateral diplomacy rather than direct military intervention.[2] [14] No public records indicate she held additional NSC positions.[3]

Private Sector and Media Career

Founding Greenwich Media Strategies

Hagar Chemali established Greenwich Media Strategies, LLC in Greenwich, Connecticut, after concluding her role as director of communications and spokesperson for the U.S. Mission to the United Nations in 2016.[1] The firm operates as a strategic communications consultancy, offering services in public affairs, geopolitical analysis, and media strategy, drawing on Chemali's prior experience in national security roles across the U.S. Treasury Department and National Security Council.[2][15] As founder and chief executive officer, Chemali advises clients on navigating complex foreign policy issues and enhancing communications in high-stakes environments, with a focus on areas such as counterterrorism, sanctions, and international diplomacy.[16] The company's inception marked Chemali's transition from government serviceâ€”spanning over a decadeâ€”to private-sector expertise, enabling independent analysis and consulting unbound by official constraints.[5] Greenwich Media Strategies has since positioned itself as a niche provider for entities seeking informed perspectives on global risks and policy communications.[17]

Creation and Hosting of Oh My World!

Hagar Chemali created Oh My World! in May 2020 as an independent media project to deliver global news and geopolitical analysis in an accessible, engaging format targeted at younger audiences and those new to international affairs.[18] The show's dual missions emphasize simplifying complex world storiesâ€”such as conflicts, human rights issues, and diplomatic developmentsâ€”while incorporating political satire to highlight tyrants, expose abuses, and showcase positive global initiatives, thereby fostering awareness of how these events impact everyday life, particularly in the United States.[19] Chemali, drawing on her background in national security communications, positioned the series as a counter to traditional media's perceived dryness or bias, aiming for unfiltered, high-energy delivery through humor, graphics, sound effects, and occasional impersonations.[11] As host, writer, and producer, Chemali has maintained a weekly release schedule on YouTube since the inaugural episode on May 8, 2020, with episodes typically lasting around 10 minutes to provide concise summaries of the week's major events.[20][21] The format includes fast-paced news rundowns explaining causal links and implications, supplemented by segments like "Our Geek List" profiling innovators and change-makers, as well as spin-off animated videos under "Geek Out with Oh My World!" that break down foundational concepts in international relations, such as theories and institutions.[19] This structure has extended beyond video to live campus events, social media interactions including Instagram polls and Q&As, and collaborations with educational institutions, where teachers have integrated episodes into curricula for middle and high school students.[18] The series operates under Chemali's firm, Greenwich Media Strategies, which she founded in 2016, integrating Oh My World! into her broader consulting on communications and geopolitics.[15] By 2021, it had garnered recognition as an award-winning web series, with growing subscriber bases, media appearance requests (e.g., MSNBC, BBC), and institutional adoption, though its satirical edge has occasionally drawn scrutiny for unapologetic critiques of authoritarian regimes and policy failures across ideological lines.[18][2] Chemali's hands-on role ensures consistency in tone, prioritizing empirical event coverage over narrative spin, as evidenced by episode archives focusing on verifiable developments like Ukraine conflicts or Middle East tensions without deference to prevailing institutional biases.[21]

Other Media Appearances and Consulting

Chemali serves as a frequent commentator on national security and U.S. foreign policy, appearing regularly on networks including MSNBC, CNN, BBC, and Bloomberg.[1] Her guest spots often focus on Middle East conflicts, such as Iran's regional influence and U.S. strikes on Iran-backed militias, as discussed on MSNBC's Katie Phang in February 2024.[22] On CNN News Central in August 2025, she analyzed Lebanon's efforts to disarm Hezbollah and Israel's strikes near hospitals.[23] [24] Other appearances include MSNBC's Morning Joe in June 2025 addressing distinctions between anti-Netanyahu and anti-Israel sentiments, and CNN NewsNight with Abby Phillip in October 2025 covering broader foreign policy developments.[25] [26] She has also contributed to Bloomberg discussions on Israel-Hamas cease-fire dynamics in June 2024 and CNBC commentary on Israel's military objectives post-October 7, 2023, predicting tactical successes but long-term challenges.[27] [28] In addition to media commentary, Chemali founded and leads Greenwich Media Strategies, LLC, offering strategic communications consulting, public affairs, media engagement, and geopolitical analysis primarily in national security, counter-illicit finance, and human rights domains.[2] [29] The firm has registered under the Foreign Agents Registration Act for representing foreign clients, including the Iraq Private Banks League in 2018.[30] Her consulting draws on prior government experience to advise on policy communications and crisis response.[16]

Political Views and Public Commentary

Foreign Policy Expertise and Middle East Perspectives

Chemali's foreign policy expertise in the Middle East derives primarily from her tenure as Director for Syria and Lebanon at the White House National Security Council from 2010 to 2012, where she advised on and coordinated U.S. policy toward those countries, including responses to the Syrian civil war and Hezbollah's activities in Lebanon.[1] Earlier, as a Policy Advisor on the Middle East in the U.S. Treasury Department's Office of Terrorist Financing and Financial Crimes, she contributed to sanctions targeting Iranian networks, terrorist financing, and illicit finance supporting regional instability.[1] Her academic focus at Columbia University's School of International and Public Affairs on international security policy and the Middle East, combined with Arabic language proficiency, further informed her analysis of counter-terrorism and conflict dynamics in the region.[1] In commentary on Iran, Chemali has emphasized the efficacy of stringent U.S. sanctions as a non-military tool to curb Tehran's regional aggression and nuclear ambitions. She argued in a 2019 analysis that sanctions imposed during the Trump administration successfully inflicted financial strain on Iran's economy, reducing funding for proxies like Hezbollahâ€”which faced revenue shortfalls leading to operational cutsâ€”and Shia militias in Iraq, while limiting Iran's low-enriched uranium production as reported by the IAEA.[31] These measures, she contended, undermined Iran's support for terrorism and prevented escalation to war by achieving policy objectives through economic pressure rather than direct conflict.[31] More recently, following Iran's 2024 missile attacks on Israel, Chemali warned that further Iranian escalation would provoke a "very fierce response" not only from Israel but also from the United States, highlighting Iran's awareness of the risks involved in broader confrontation.[32] Regarding Lebanon and Hezbollah, Chemali has advocated for a peace agreement between Lebanon and Israel as a strategic means to isolate and weaken the Iran-backed group, describing it as a "major blow" that would sever Hezbollah's territorial leverage along the border.[33] In assessments of the 2024 cross-border conflict, she expressed that Israel should not fear escalation with Hezbollah, underscoring the group's vulnerabilities amid Lebanese public disillusionment and economic pressures.[34] Her analysis often frames Hezbollah's influence as propped up by Iranian funding, which sanctions have historically constrained, limiting its military sustainment in Syria and beyond.[31] On Syria, Chemali's perspectives highlight persistent challenges to normalization with Israel due to entrenched Iranian and Russian alliances under Bashar al-Assad, noting in late 2024 that Assad's regime continues to rely on these patrons to counter rebel advances, perpetuating proxy entanglements.[35] Drawing from her NSC experience, she has critiqued the regime's destabilizing role in the broader Middle East, advocating U.S. policies that prioritize countering Iranian expansionism through targeted financial measures over indefinite military engagement.[1]

Views on U.S. Domestic Politics

Chemali has consistently opposed Donald Trump's presidential campaigns, framing electoral abstention or protest votes as tantamount to supporting him. In an August 17, 2016, opinion piece published in the Stamford Advocate, she warned that failing to vote in the general election would equate to electing Trump, whom she described as potentially leading to "the loudest mistake in the history of American presidents," citing widespread Republican skepticism toward him during Connecticut primaries.[36] This stance persisted into subsequent cycles. On February 25, 2024, during coverage of the Michigan Democratic presidential primary, Chemali characterized the "uncommitted" ballot optionâ€”used by over 13% of voters as a protest against President Joe Biden's Israel-Hamas war policyâ€”as effectively functioning as "a vote for Trump," arguing it risked undermining Democratic unity without advancing policy goals.[37] Chemali has also addressed antisemitism within U.S. political discourse, particularly during the 2024 campaign. In an August 7, 2024, Bloomberg interview, she criticized a Democratic messaging strategy highlighting Republican vice-presidential nominee J.D. Vance's past associations as an example of antisemitism, stating it "undermines our own American values" and calling on Vice President Kamala Harris to "set the record straight" to avoid conflating legitimate criticism with prejudice.[38][39] Although Chemali served in senior roles across the Obama administration's National Security Council, Treasury Department, and U.S. Mission to the United Nationsâ€”positions typically held by Democratic appointeesâ€”Connecticut voter records list her as having no formal political party affiliation.[40] Her commentary on domestic politics remains limited compared to her foreign policy focus, often intersecting with electoral strategy rather than standalone policy debates on issues like healthcare or the economy.

Criticisms, Controversies, and Reception

In 2017, Hagar Chemali's firm, Greenwich Media Strategies, was reported to have been paid six-figure sums by the United Arab Emirates to shape United Nations discussions on the Saudi-led intervention in Yemen, including efforts to discredit non-governmental organization reports alleging war crimes and humanitarian crises.[41] This lobbying work, conducted after Chemali's departure from Obama administration roles, prompted criticism from outlets like The Intercept for prioritizing state interests over evidence-based accountability in the conflict, which had resulted in thousands of civilian deaths and widespread famine risks by mid-2017 according to UN estimates.[41] The arrangement was legal under U.S. foreign agent registration rules, but it highlighted tensions between private advocacy and public scrutiny of Gulf state actions. Chemali's public commentary on Middle East issues, including strong support for Israel amid conflicts with Hezbollah and Hamas, has elicited polarized responses, with some Arab media portraying her as advancing pro-Zionist positions.[42] For instance, in August 2025, Raseef22 described her social media activity as promoting narratives aligned with Israeli perspectives while endorsing U.S. political figures like Donald Trump, framing this as controversial within Lebanese-American communities critical of such stances.[42] No formal investigations or legal challenges have arisen from these views or her firm's activities. Reception of Chemali's media ventures has been largely positive among audiences focused on accessible foreign policy analysis. Her YouTube series Oh My World!, launched to deliver weekly global news summaries in under 10 minutes, earned a Webby Award nomination for its innovative format blending education and entertainment.[43] Appearances on outlets like Bloomberg and CNN have positioned her as a credible voice on national security, with commentators noting her ability to distill complex events without partisan overreach.[38][44] Overall, her work has garnered acclaim for demystifying geopolitics, though it attracts skepticism from progressive circles wary of her Obama-era background combined with later hawkish leanings on Iran and Yemen.

Personal Life

Family and Residence

Hagar Chemali is married to Julien Chemali.[45] The couple wed on May 24, 2011, in a ceremony announced in local media.[45] They reside in Greenwich, Connecticut, where Chemali founded her media and consulting firm.[4][36] The family lives in the mid-country area of town.[4] Chemali and her husband have three children: two sons, Alexander and Benjamin, and a daughter.[46][47] In 2020, Alexander was approximately 6.5 years old and Benjamin 4 years old.[46]

Advocacy and Community Involvement

Chemali has served on the board of directors of the Greenwich United Way since February 2020, contributing to the organization's efforts in community philanthropy and local support services in Greenwich, Connecticut.[46][48] As an alumna of Greenwich Academy, she holds a trustee position at the institution, supporting its educational mission for girls in the region.[49] In her Greenwich community, Chemali engages with her children's schools and the Bruce Museum, including membership in the museum's Bruce Contemporaries young professionals group, which fosters cultural and educational initiatives.[4][50][51] Chemali advocates for formal recognition of the Armenian Genocide, authoring a 2018 CNN opinion piece calling on President Trump to affirm it as a matter of historical truth and U.S. foreign policy consistency, and delivering speeches at Greenwich events, such as a 2025 flag-raising ceremony addressing its implications for preventing future atrocities.[52][53] She also serves as a director of the Serve America Movement, a nonpartisan group promoting centrist policies and independent candidates, which she co-founded and has supported through endorsements like that of Connecticut gubernatorial candidate Bob Stefanowski in 2018.[54][55]
