Barkatullah University

Barkatullah Vishwavidyalaya, commonly known as Barkatullah University, is a public state university in Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, India, established in 1970 under the name Bhopal University and renamed in 1988 to honor Professor Barkatullah Bhopali, a Bhopal-born freedom fighter and scholar who advocated for Indian independence.[1] The university functions primarily as an affiliating institution, granting degrees to affiliated non-technical colleges in and around Bhopal while maintaining its own teaching departments for advanced studies.[2] Its 400-acre campus, situated along the Hoshangabad highway approximately 3 kilometers from Habibganj Railway Station, houses facilities supporting a range of undergraduate, postgraduate, diploma, and doctoral programs across 12 faculties, including arts, sciences, commerce, management, law, engineering, medicine, and education.[1][3] The university has expanded access to higher education through initiatives like the Institute of Open and Distance Education, established in 1974, which caters to remote learners.[2] In recent assessments, it received an 'A' grade accreditation from the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) in 2025, marking an upgrade from its prior 'B' grade and reflecting improvements in academic infrastructure and governance, though student critiques have questioned the evaluation's rigor.[3][4] Barkatullah University also secured recognition in the Merit, Excellence, and Impact (MEI) World Ranking 2025 for contributions in academics, research output, and social initiatives, including sustainable development projects.[5] Notable departments include the Chakravarti Rajgopalachari Institute of Management, founded in 1979 for postgraduate business education, and a central library holding over 75,000 books, thousands of theses, and specialized collections.[1][2] Despite these developments, the university has faced scrutiny over administrative lapses, such as a 2016 investigation uncovering approximately 20 fraudulent PhD degrees issued under certain supervisors, highlighting persistent challenges in research oversight.[6] More recently, in 2024, campus unrest erupted during an event commemorating Bhagat Singh's birth anniversary, underscoring tensions around historical commemorations and event management.[7] These incidents contrast with the institution's role in regional education but point to needs for stronger procedural integrity amid its growth as a hub for over 100 affiliated colleges.[1]

History

Establishment and Early Years

Barkatullah University was established in 1970 as Bhopal University, a public institution in Bhopal, the capital of Madhya Pradesh, India, to address regional demands for higher education.[1] The founding aligned with post-independence efforts to expand access to university-level studies in central India, initially serving as a teaching-cum-affiliating body under state legislation.[1] Its territorial jurisdiction encompassed eight districts, facilitating affiliations with local colleges while developing on-campus postgraduate programs.[1] The university's initial campus spanned approximately 400 acres along the Hoshangabad highway, positioned about 3 kilometers from Habibganj Railway Station for accessibility.[1] Core infrastructure featured an Administrative Block, Library Block, and dedicated buildings for emerging departments, laying the groundwork for academic operations amid limited resources typical of nascent state universities.[1] Early emphasis was placed on foundational faculties such as Arts, Science, Life Science, and Social Science to support multidisciplinary teaching and research.[1] Key early initiatives included the creation of the Institute of Open and Distance Education in 1974â€“75, approved by the University Grants Commission to extend educational reach beyond traditional enrollment.[8] By 1981, the University Press was operationalized with funding from the state government and UGC, enabling in-house publication of academic materials and enhancing institutional self-sufficiency.[1] These steps marked incremental growth, prioritizing infrastructure and outreach before broader expansions in the late 1980s.[1]

Renaming and Expansion

In 1988, Bhopal University was renamed Barkatullah Vishwavidyalaya to commemorate Professor Barkatullah Bhopali, an Indian revolutionary and independence activist born in Bhopal in 1854, who advocated for India's freedom from British rule through diplomatic efforts in countries like Japan, Germany, and Afghanistan.[1] The change reflected regional pride in his legacy as a native scholar and anti-colonial figure, distinct from national icons like Gandhi, emphasizing local contributions to the independence movement.[1] Post-renaming, the university expanded its operational scope, extending jurisdiction over eight districts in Madhya Pradeshâ€”Bhopal, Sehore, Vidisha, Raisen, Hoshangabad, Harda, Betul, and Rajgarhâ€”to encompass a broader regional educational network.[1] This growth included establishing extension centers in Betul and Bhivara for decentralized access to higher education and affiliating colleges across these areas, incorporating diverse institutions such as one engineering college, two education colleges, and three medical colleges.[1] The expansion also involved developing specialized teaching departments in fields like arts, sciences, life sciences, and social sciences, alongside infrastructure enhancements on its 400-acre campus along the Hoshangabad highway, supporting increased enrollment and program diversity without compromising its focus on non-technical higher education.[1]

Key Milestones Post-2000

In 2002â€“2003, Barkatullah University established a Bioinformatics Centre under its Department of Biotechnology, funded by the Government of India's Department of Biotechnology, to support computational facilities, training, and research in bioinformatics.[1] The university received a 'B' grade accreditation from the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) in 2015, reflecting its academic and administrative standards at that time.[1] In August 2025, Barkatullah University achieved its first 'A' grade accreditation from NAAC, marking an improvement in institutional quality, research output, and infrastructure as evaluated by the council's peer team.[4][3] On September 3, 2025, Madhya Pradesh Chief Minister Mohan Yadav announced â‚¹100 crore in development projects under the Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (PM-USHA) scheme, including new hostels, an IT hub, an agricultural institute, and enhanced student facilities to bolster campus infrastructure and employability programs.[9]

Campus and Infrastructure

Location and Layout

Barkatullah University is situated in Bhopal, the capital city of Madhya Pradesh, India, along Hoshangabad Road (also referred to as Hoshangabad Highway), approximately 3 kilometers from Rani Kamlapati Railway Station.[10][1] The precise address is Hoshangabad Road, Bhopal 462026, placing it in a relatively accessible suburban area conducive to academic activities while being integrated into the city's green belt.[11][12] The campus spans approximately 400 acres, providing expansive grounds that include academic buildings, administrative facilities, and green spaces such as gardens, lawns, and an herbal garden, which contribute to reduced noise pollution and a serene environment.[10][13][12] Key structures encompass the Administrative Block, Library Block, Life Sciences Block, Law Block, Humanities Block, Social Science Block, Physics Block, Applied Geology Building, Institute of Open and Distance Education Block, and C.R. Institute of Management.[14][15] This layout supports a centralized yet specialized distribution of departments, with surrounding vegetation enhancing the ecological footprint of the site.[12]

Facilities and Resources

Barkatullah University's campus spans approximately 400 acres along the Hoshangabad highway, approximately 3 km from Habibganj Railway Station, encompassing various specialized blocks including the Administrative Block, Library Block, Life Science Block, Microbiology Building, Law Block, Humanities Block, Social Science Block, Physics Block, and Applied Geology Building.[1] These structures support academic and research activities across disciplines. The university maintains central facilities such as a health center, computer center, and science instrumentation center to aid operations.[1] The Central Library houses nearly 75,000 books, 5,703 theses, 3,000 research magazines, and 60 Sanskrit manuscripts, supplemented by 15 daily newspapers, 12 weeklies, and various journals, with dedicated reference and thesis sections.[1] It provides access to e-resources and supports scholarly work through its collections. Governance and Administration

Organizational Structure

Barkatullah University operates under a governance framework established by the Madhya Pradesh Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, 1973, which defines its key officers and authorities, including the Chancellor, Vice-Chancellor, Executive Council, and Academic Council.[17] The Chancellor, the Governor of Madhya Pradesh, holds the highest ceremonial and oversight position, with powers to appoint the Vice-Chancellor, preside over convocations, annul proceedings, and resolve disputes on authority constitutions.[17] The Vice-Chancellor serves as the chief executive and academic officer, chairing both the Executive Council and Academic Council, managing day-to-day operations, ensuring statutory compliance, and exercising emergency powers subject to later ratification.[17] As of 2025, Prof. Suresh Kumar Jain holds this position.[18] The Registrar acts as the administrative head under the Vice-Chancellor, handling records, minutes, and procedural duties; currently, Shri Anil Sharma occupies this role.[18] A Rector position exists but remains vacant.[18] The Executive Council functions as the primary executive body, responsible for financial management, property administration, appointing teaching staff, admitting affiliated colleges, and enforcing discipline, with a quorum of four members.[17] It comprises the Vice-Chancellor, Rector (if appointed), four Deans of Faculties (currently Prof. Ansuja Tiwari, Dr. Seema Raja, Dr. Sadhana Danoriya, and Prof. Anita Dhurve), two University professors (e.g., Prof. Tahera Abbasi), government nominees (e.g., Dr. Mathura Prasad from Higher Education and Shri Neeraj Marawi from Finance), six Chancellor-nominated members (including representatives from Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes/Other Backward Classes), and the Registrar as ex-officio secretary.[18][17] Positions for three Court-elected members and four affiliated college principals remain vacant.[18] The Academic Council oversees academic policies, including curriculum, examinations, teacher qualifications, and research recommendations, proposing ordinances and advising on scholarly matters.[17] Its composition includes the Vice-Chancellor, Rector, Deans, heads of departments and boards, elected representatives from teaching staff and affiliated colleges, and nominees from higher education authorities, with a quorum of ten members.[17] Additional bodies, such as the Finance Committee, support specialized functions like budgeting, though detailed current memberships are not publicly enumerated beyond the core authorities.[18] This structure emphasizes centralized executive control balanced by academic input, typical of state universities in Madhya Pradesh.[17]

Leadership and Administrative Challenges

Barkatullah University has encountered recurrent leadership issues, particularly concerning vice-chancellors accused of irregularities and prolonged absences. In February 2016, the Madhya Pradesh government considered removing Vice-Chancellor M.D. Tiwari under section 52 of the university act due to allegations of improper appointments and financial mismanagement.[19] Similarly, in 2017, Vice-Chancellor Binod Kumar faced employee protests over his extended tenure beyond a one-year contract, prompting assurances of internal probes into related misbehavior claims.[20] Administrative challenges have persisted, including scrutiny over unauthorized hiring practices. As of July 2024, investigations targeted 162 employees at the university for alleged illegal appointments without state government approval, exacerbating financial and governance strains.[21] The Madhya Pradesh High Court upheld the dismissal of project officer Narendra Tripathi in the same period, citing procedural violations.[22] These issues reflect broader systemic problems, such as staff shortages, inadequate lab facilities, and delays in degree conferral, which have fueled student dissatisfaction since at least 2016.[23] Under current Vice-Chancellor S.K. Jain, appointed around 2022, leadership visibility has drawn criticism. In May 2025, students affiliated with NSUI protested his extended absence from campus by displaying "missing person" posters, highlighting perceived neglect of administrative duties amid ongoing enrollment declines.[24] Additional incidents, such as a 2022 gherao of the VC residence over alleged police misconduct in hostels and a 2024 controversy involving a warden forcing Hindu female students to apologize for attending a religious event, underscore lapses in oversight and conflict resolution.[25][26] These challenges have contributed to declining student enrollment, with the university reporting poor attendance figures as of April 2023, prompting administrative reviews but limited resolution.[27] Persistent governance gaps, including inadequate facilities and unresolved protests, indicate underlying causal factors such as political interference in appointments and weak accountability mechanisms, as evidenced by repeated judicial interventions and government threats of removal.[28]

Distance Education Initiatives

The Institute of Open and Distance Education (IODE) at Barkatullah University, initially established as the Department of Correspondence Courses in 1975 with approval from the University Grants Commission, marked an early institutional effort to expand access to higher education through non-traditional modes in Madhya Pradesh.[40][41] This initiative aimed to offer flexible learning options for working professionals, rural students, and others unable to pursue conventional on-campus programs, emphasizing affordability and equity in educational delivery.[42] As one of the pioneer entities in distance education in India, IODE transitioned to its current form to align with evolving regulatory frameworks, including recognition by the Distance Education Bureau (DEB) under the UGC for specific offerings.[43] IODE provides a range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and diploma programs designed for self-paced study, including Bachelor of Arts (B.A.), Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com), Master of Arts (M.A.) in various specializations, Bachelor of Library and Information Science (B.Lib.I.Sc.), Master of Library and Information Science (M.Lib.I.Sc.), and postgraduate diplomas.[44] These courses incorporate printed study materials, periodic contact classes, and examinations conducted by the university, with admissions typically processed annually through merit-based selection or entrance where applicable.[40] The programs maintain equivalence to regular degrees, subject to DEB approvals, enabling graduates to pursue further studies or employment opportunities recognized under UGC norms.[43] Key operational features include study centers across Madhya Pradesh for learner support and the use of online platforms for result announcements and administrative services, reflecting adaptations to digital tools post-2010s.[45] While specific enrollment figures are not publicly detailed in recent reports, IODE's model has historically supported thousands of learners annually, contributing to the university's role in democratizing education amid India's growing demand for flexible higher learning options.[42] The institute operates under ongoing UGC-DEB scrutiny to ensure compliance with quality standards, such as curriculum relevance and assessment integrity.[46]

Affiliated Institutions

Affiliated Colleges

Barkatullah University maintains affiliations with colleges spanning eight districts in Madhya Pradesh: Betul, Bhopal, Harda, Narmadapuram, Raisen, Rajgarh, Sehore, and Vidisha.[47] These institutions encompass government-run, private, and autonomous entities, which deliver undergraduate and select postgraduate curricula in disciplines including arts, sciences, commerce, education, and management, with the university handling syllabus prescription, examinations, and degree conferral.[48] [49] Autonomous colleges, granted operational flexibility in internal administration while adhering to university standards, include the Bhopal School of Social Sciences in Bhopal district, which offers postgraduate programs such as M.A. in English Literature and Sociology.[49] Government colleges, funded by the state, feature prominently in urban centers; examples comprise Government MLB Girls PG College in Bhopal, supporting women's education across multiple streams, and Government Motilal Vigyan Mahavidyalaya in Bhopal, focused on science and vocational training with annual affiliation fees reflecting program scale, such as 425,000 rupees for select departments as of recent records.[50] Private colleges form the majority of affiliates, often specializing in professional courses like B.Ed. and M.B.A., with affiliations renewed periodically based on infrastructure and faculty compliance; district-wise distributions include multiple entries in Bhopal for management faculties and rural areas like Sehore for education programs.[51] The university's oversight ensures alignment with its faculties, though private institutions must deposit renewal feesâ€”full amounts for urban colleges and adjusted for rural onesâ€”to maintain status, as stipulated in affiliation guidelines.[51] This structure supports regional access to higher education, with examinations centralized under university control for uniformity.[52]

Oversight and Integration

Barkatullah University maintains oversight of its affiliated colleges primarily through an affiliation process governed by Statute No. 27 (Revised), which mandates the formation of an inspection committee to evaluate proposed or existing institutions before granting or renewing affiliation.[53] The committee conducts on-site visits to inspect infrastructure, facilities, and academic resources, while interviewing principals, teachers, and management to ensure compliance with university standards and state regulations, including prior No Objection Certificates (NOCs) from the Madhya Pradesh government for new colleges.[53] This process aims to verify adherence to norms set by the University Grants Commission (UGC) and the state university act, though provisional affiliations have occasionally been issued without full inspections, as documented in cases from 2023.[54] Integration of affiliated colleges into the university system occurs via centralized academic control, where institutions must follow syllabi prescribed by Barkatullah University for undergraduate and postgraduate programs across faculties such as arts, science, commerce, and education.[49] The university conducts examinations for these colleges, handles result processing, and enforces curriculum uniformity to align with its degree offerings, covering approximately 223 affiliated institutions (64 government and 159 private) as of recent records. Administrative integration includes annual affiliation renewals via online portals requiring submission of prior affiliation letters, affidavits, and compliance documents, facilitating real-time oversight of operational standards.[55] Ongoing supervision mechanisms under the Madhya Pradesh Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, 1973, empower the university to direct periodic inspections of affiliated colleges to monitor teaching quality, faculty qualifications, and resource availability.[17] In practice, this has involved issuing notices for detected irregularities, such as faculty anomalies in 20 private colleges in June 2025, prompting corrective actions.[56] However, implementation challenges persist, including the university's failure to operationalize an academic flying squad mandated 17 years prior for routine monitoring, leading to documented lapses in supervision, particularly for private institutions in specialized programs like physical education.[57][58] These gaps have been highlighted in probes revealing inadequate tracking of affiliations and compliance, underscoring tensions between regulatory intent and execution.[59]

Research and Achievements

Research Centers and Outputs

The Biotechnology and Bioinformatics Centre at Barkatullah University functions as a Sub Distributed Information Centre (Sub-DIC) under the Biotechnology Information System Network (BTISnet) of the Department of Biotechnology, Government of India, established in 2002-03. Its core objectives encompass delivering computational infrastructure and foundational training in bioinformatics to support students, research scholars, and faculty engaged in biological sciences and allied disciplines.[60][61] The affiliated Department of Biotechnology facilitates practical research activities, including hands-on workshops on topics such as bioinformatics tools and structural bioinformatics analysis, aimed at enhancing technical skills for molecular biology and computational applications.[62] Ongoing research projects are hosted at the centre, focusing on biotechnology applications, though specific project details remain documented through governmental portals without public enumeration of outcomes.[63] The university's Incubation Centre supports innovation-driven research by providing access to equipped laboratories, instrumentation facilities, and workshops tailored for natural resource utilization and technology development, alongside campus-wide resources like Wi-Fi and libraries to foster entrepreneurial and applied research initiatives.[64] Research outputs include scholarly publications from university-affiliated researchers, with departmental contributions in fields like biosciences and genetics; for instance, the Department of Biosciences, established in 1975, integrates research in biological disciplines yielding peer-reviewed works on topics such as bioremediation and protein engineering.[32] The institution has conducted events like the First Barkatullah University Research Seminar on March 18-19, 2019, to disseminate findings and promote interdisciplinary collaboration.[3] Doctoral research is emphasized through PhD admissions and recognized centres, with historical records indicating over 1,500 theses completed between 2000 and 2009 across disciplines.[65]

Notable Accomplishments

The Pharmacy Department of Barkatullah University secured a patent for an Ayurvedic formulation designed for rapid hair removal while simultaneously addressing blackheads, moisturizing, and cleansing the skin, providing a safer alternative to chemical-based methods that often cause irritation, rashes, or pigmentation issues. Developed by research scholar Steffi Thomas under the supervision of Professor Ragini Gothwal (department head) and Dr. Ashwani Mishra, the innovation was highlighted by Vice-Chancellor Professor S.K. Jain as a significant advancement in pharmaceutical research.[66] In May 2025, the university achieved recognition in the Merit, Excellence, and Impact (MEI) World Ranking, acknowledging its progress in research outputs, academic quality, and societal contributions since its establishment in 1970.[5] This milestone builds on institutional efforts to foster innovation, including the adoption of a dedicated Research Innovation Policy emphasizing patent filings and knowledge translation. Accreditations, Rankings, and Evaluations

NAAC Assessments

Barkatullah University received an 'A' grade accreditation from the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) in its third-cycle re-assessment, with a Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) of 3.09 out of 4.00.[67] This outcome was determined at NAAC's 257th Standing Committee meeting on August 1, 2025, and publicly announced in early August, marking the university's first attainment of an A grade.[4][68] Prior to this, the university held a 'B' grade from NAAC's 2015 assessment, reflecting lower performance across criteria such as curricular aspects, teaching-learning processes, and research output.[1] The 2025 upgrade followed an on-site inspection concluded in July 2025, during which the university aimed to demonstrate enhancements in infrastructure, governance, and academic quality to secure improved funding and recognition under schemes like PM-USHA, which allocated Rs 100 crore post-accreditation.[69][4] Despite the official endorsement, the A grade has elicited criticism from students, who argue it overlooks persistent issues including dilapidated infrastructure, irregular examinations, and administrative inefficiencies on campus.[70] These concerns, voiced through student representatives and covered in regional media, highlight potential discrepancies between NAAC's evaluationâ€”based on self-reported data, peer review visits, and quantitative metricsâ€”and ground-level realities, though NAAC maintains its process emphasizes verifiable evidence and institutional self-improvement.[71] The accreditation validity period aligns with NAAC's standard five-year cycle for A-graded institutions, subject to ongoing compliance.[3]

Other Rankings and Recognitions

Barkatullah University has participated in India's National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF) evaluations, submitting institutional data for the 2025 cycle, though it did not achieve a ranking within the top 200 universities band.[72][73] In independent global assessments, the university ranked 157th among Indian institutions and 3979th worldwide according to EduRank's 2025 metrics, which evaluate research output, non-academic prominence, and alumni influence across 25 topics.[74] It also appeared in the SCImago Institutions Rankings for 2025, placing 87th in India and 2811th globally, based on research, innovation, and societal impact indicators.[75] The university earned inclusion in the MEI World Ranking 2025, recognizing its contributions in academics, research output, and social impact, as reported in May 2025.[5] As a state public university established under the Madhya Pradesh government, it holds UGC recognition, enabling it to confer degrees and access central funding schemes.[76]

Controversies and Criticisms

Student Protests and Campus Issues

In November 2024, female students at Barkatullah University's hostel protested against warden Ayesha Rais, accusing her of restricting their religious freedoms by prohibiting visits to the campus temple, imposing strict time limits on returns (leading to harassment for being 10 minutes late after a permitted Sunderkand recitation), and forcing Hindu students to write apology letters for participating in the event.[77][26] Around 200 members of the Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad (ABVP) joined the demonstration on November 11, playing Ram Dhun on campus and demanding the warden's removal for alleged bias and threats of expulsion.[78] The university administration had not publicly responded to the allegations by late November, amid claims that the restrictions violated students' rights to religious practice.[79] In September 2024, a university directive sparked outrage among hostel residents by conditioning participation in Durga Puja celebrations on full payment of fees by October 6, classifying non-payers as unauthorized intruders during the festival.[80] Students, many from economically weaker backgrounds facing delays in fee payments, viewed the order as coercive and infringing on religious observance, prompting plans for a collective protest outside the administration building with support from student unions.[80] Hostel fees totaled approximately â‚¹28,500 annually plus â‚¹21,600 for meals, and officials defended the measure as a disciplinary tool to ensure only paying residents accessed facilities, though the vice-chancellor and registrar declined comment.[80] On May 23, 2025, the National Students' Union of India (NSUI) staged a symbolic protest by pasting "missing person" posters of Vice-Chancellor S.K. Jain across campus, highlighting his absence since early May, which had stalled operations including exam evaluations, portal functionality, administrative payments, and affiliations for private colleges.[24] NSUI activists offered a â‚¹1,100 reward for information on Jain's whereabouts and threatened a police missing person report if he did not return within three days, citing the acting VC's lack of full authority as exacerbating delays.[24] No response came from Jain despite outreach attempts. Campus issues fueling protests include persistent infrastructure deficiencies, such as outdated laboratories lacking equipment and disrepair in facilities, alongside low attendance rates, which students argued were overlooked in the university's NAAC 'A' grade accreditation in August 2025.[70] These grievances have periodically led to demonstrations, often organized by student political groups like NSUI and ABVP, reflecting broader administrative lapses in hostel management and oversight.[70]

Examination and Administrative Irregularities

Barkatullah University has faced multiple allegations of examination malpractices, including paper leaks and irregularities in evaluation processes. In 2006, a suspected fake mark-sheet scam prompted the formation of a committee comprising senior officials to investigate discrepancies in student records.[81] A notable incident occurred around 2014 in the Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) examinations, where employees and students allegedly colluded to remove question papers and answer sheets from secure custody, leading to a chancellor-appointed committee to probe the irregularities.[82][83] Further examination issues emerged in 2018 when a video surfaced showing first-year students inspecting answer sheets intended for final-year Bachelor of Arts evaluations, highlighting suspected massive irregularities in the marking process conducted by the university's exam coordinator.[84][85] This prompted a government-ordered probe into the handling of BA exam papers.[85] Paper leaks continued in subsequent years; in April 2022, the B.Com second-year exam was canceled after a confidential envelope was reportedly opened prematurely, allowing the paper to circulate.[86] By May 2022, over 1,100 cases of unfair means, such as cheating during exams, had been registered across ongoing university tests.[87] In July 2023, four students were booked for leaking a BBA paper on financial markets and services.[88] Administrative irregularities have compounded these issues, particularly in recruitment and financial oversight. In 2014, an alleged â‚¹300 crore scam involving university funds led to a revived probe by the economic offences wing.[83] By 2016, the Madhya Pradesh government considered removing the vice-chancellor amid charges of irregularities, including unauthorized appointments of 11 assistant professors and a director in 2014.[19] In December 2006, the university's executive committee canceled at least four recent departmental appointments following complaints.[89] More recently, a 2023 whistleblower complaint exposed a recruitment scam, identifying 163 employees hired without government approval; by July 2024, scrutiny intensified on 162 such staff members.[90][21] These incidents reflect persistent challenges in governance and procedural integrity at the institution.

Disputes Over Accreditations

In August 2025, Barkatullah University received an 'A' grade from the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC), a designation contested by students who cited ongoing infrastructural deficiencies, low attendance attributed to disengaging curricula, and administrative shortcomings as evidence of an unduly favorable evaluation.[70] Critics among the student body alleged faculty mismanagement, including biased grading and manipulation of academic records, which they claimed were overlooked during the assessment process.[70] Complaints were formally submitted to higher education authorities, arguing that such systemic issues rendered the accreditation's integrity questionable.[70] The preceding NAAC inspection in July 2025 intensified disputes, with students accusing the university of staging improvements to facilities, including the temporary operation of a canteen exclusively for the evaluators' visit.[91] Reports indicated that protesting students were prevented from engaging directly with the NAAC peer team, raising concerns over selective presentation of campus conditions.[69] Historical precedents include a 2016 incident where the university's NAAC reportâ€”issued after it failed to elevate its prior gradingâ€”disappeared from records, highlighting administrative negligence in accreditation oversight.[92] Additionally, in 2013, the institution encountered difficulties affiliating colleges lacking NAAC certification for Bachelor of Physical Education programs, resulting in unauthorized student admissions and regulatory non-compliance.[93] These episodes reflect recurrent challenges in maintaining transparent and substantive accreditation standards.

Notable Associates

Alumni

Shivraj Singh Chouhan, who obtained an M.A. in Philosophy as a gold medalist, later served as Chief Minister of Madhya Pradesh from 2005 to 2023 and currently holds the position of Union Minister of Agriculture and Farmers' Welfare.[94] Hassan Sheikh Mohamud, recipient of a Master of Arts in technical education completed in 1988, was elected President of Somalia in 2012 and re-elected in 2022.[95] Rahat Indori, who earned an M.A. in Urdu literature in 1975, became a renowned Urdu poet, lyricist, and professor known for his ghazals and contributions to Bollywood films such as Mission Kashmir.[96] Pamposh Bhat, holder of an M.Sc. in Chemistry, has worked as a climate change expert and senior advisor on environmental policy in South Asia.[97] Surendra Singh Baghel, a B.Com. graduate, has represented the Kukshi constituency as a Member of the Madhya Pradesh Legislative Assembly since 2018.[98]

Faculty and Administrators

The administration of Barkatullah University is led by Vice-Chancellor Prof. Suresh Kumar Jain, who assumed office on October 4, 2022.[99] [18] The Registrar is Dr. I. K. Mansoori, responsible for overseeing academic and administrative operations.[100] Faculty at the university span disciplines including sciences, life sciences, and management, with department heads and deans managing academic programs.[35] Prominent among them is Prof. N. K. Gaur in the Department of Physics, who has authored 295 publications accumulating over 2,200 citations as of recent academic records.[101] Prof. Ashwani Wanganeo serves as Dean of the Faculty of Life Sciences and Head of Department, with over 37 years of service focused on biological sciences research and teaching.[102] Other key faculty include Prof. Vinoy Shrivastava, Head in reproductive endocrinology and neuroendocrinology, leading research on toxicological impacts in reproductive health.[103] Prof. Pavan Mishra holds a professorship in management studies, with roles extending to group direction for MBA programs since September 2015.[104] These administrators and faculty contribute to the university's research output, though institutional challenges such as leadership absences have drawn student scrutiny, as evidenced by protests against Vice-Chancellor Jain's reported prolonged off-campus tenure in May 2025.[24]
