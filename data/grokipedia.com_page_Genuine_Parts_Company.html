Genuine Parts Company

Genuine Parts Company (GPC) is an American automotive and industrial parts distributor based in Atlanta, Georgia.[1] Established by brothers Carlyle and Malcolm Fraser in 1928, the company has approximately 63,000 employees (as of December 31, 2024).[2] In addition to the United States, GPC has operated in Australasia, Belgium, Canada, France, Germany, Mexico, the Netherlands, Poland, and the United Kingdom. GPC's subsidiaries include industrial parts distributor Motion as well as NAPA Auto Parts, which primarily sells parts in North America.[3]

Company overview

Founded in 1928 [4], GPC is headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia and consists of multiple subsidiaries that distribute automotive replacement parts and industrial replacement parts, including electrical/electronic materials. The company has paid a cash dividend to shareholders every year since going public in 1948. The company moved into its new headquarters building in the Wildwood area of Cobb County, Georgia in June 2016. S. P. Richards, the office products distributor, was sold on June 30, 2020, to H.I.G. Capital. [5] [6]

Company History

Genuine Parts Company (GPC) was founded by brothers Carlyle and Malcolm Fraser in Atlanta in 1928.[7] GPC's headquarters were located at Atlanta's Circle 75, in Cobb County, Georgia, starting in 1979. Approximately 400 employees worked in the 115,000-square-foot space. In 2014, GPC announced plans to relocate its headquarters to Wildwood Office Park, next to an existing operational support facility.[8] The company completed the relocation to its new headquarters in Wildwood in June 2016.[9] William P. Stengel II is GPC's president and chief executive officer (CEO). He joined the company as GPC's first chief transformation officer in 2019,[10][11] became president in 2021,[12] and became chief operating officer in 2023.[13] Stengel succeeded Paul Donahue, who became the CEO of GPC in 2016. Donahue succeeded Tom Gallagher,[14] who initially continued as chairperson,[15] until Donahue was elected by the board of directors to that role in 2019.[16][17][18] Donahue transitioned to executive chairman in 2024.[19][20] GPC ranked number 262 on the magazine's list of America's Largest Public Companies in 2018. The company also ranked number 678 on Forbes' World Best Employers list in 2020, and number 731 on the Forbes Global 2000 list in 2024.[21]

Subsidiaries

GPC has two primary business segments: Motion, which focuses on industrial products in North America and Australasia; the automotive brand NAPA Auto Parts, which operates in North America, Europe, and Australasia; and other key subsidiaries including Repco (Australasia automotive aftermarket), UAP (Canada), and Alliance Automotive Group (Europe).[28] According to Industrial Distribution, Motion started as a distributor of bearings and industrial supplies, and expanded to offer products related to automation, conveyance, hydraulics, fluid power, and robotics.[29] GPC acquired Motion in 1976. GPC's electrical products division, EIS Inc., merged with Motion in 2018,[30] before being sold in October 2019.[28] Based in Birmingham, Alabama, Motion has approximately 9,000 employees and 170,000 customers as of 2023.[29] NAPA Auto Parts was established in 1925.[31] Some NAPA Auto Parts stores are owned and operated by GPC, but most are independently owned and operated.[32] There were approximately 6,000 NAPA Auto Parts stores in 2020.[31] GPC acquired UAP Inc. of Canada in 1998 and the Australian car parts supplier Exego Group (which includes Repco) in 2013.[33][34] GPC and its four business units acquired nineteen companies in 2016.[35] In 2017, GPC acquired a 35 percent stake in industrial distributor Inenco Group,[36] completing the full acquisition in 2019, as well as Alliance Automotive Group.[37][38] GPC acquired Kaman Distribution Group (KDG), a provider of industrial solutions such as automation, conveyance, and fluid power, in 2022.[29] In February 2024, Motion acquired Bender Hydraulic and Hose Service, two fluid power distributors.[39] In April 2024, GPC acquired Motor Parts & Equipment Corporation (MPEC), the largest independent owner of NAPA stores in the U.S., adding 181 locations.[27]

Former subsidiaries

In 1975, GPC acquired S.P. Richards, which was described by Industrial Distribution as "a distributor of general office products, technology products and accessories, office furniture, JanSan and safety supplies".[28] In 2018, GPC announced plans to spin off S.P. Richards and merge it with Essendant to form a combined entity under the Essendant name, but the agreement was terminated later that year without completion.[41] GPC sold S.P. Richards through a series of transactions in 2020, starting with the Canada division in January. The U.S. operations were sold to an investor group and the Supply Source Enterprise business, which included The Safety Zone and Impact Products operations, were sold to an affiliate of H.I.G. Capital. GPC continued with Motion and NAPA Auto Parts.[28]
