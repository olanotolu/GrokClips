List of cricket grounds in Nepal

This is a list of cricket grounds in Nepal at various levels, including major international venues, regional grounds, and proposed stadiums. Nepal has 4 international cricket venues i.e. two at Mulpani, one at Kirtipur and one at Pokhara.

Dang International Cricket Stadium

Dang International Cricket Stadium (Nepali: दाङ अन्तर्राष्ट्रिय क्रीकेट मैदान), also known as Ghorahi Int'l Cricket Ground is proposed Int'l Cricket Stadium. It is located in Dang Valley, Ghorahi, Dang.[38][39] This stadium will be one of the biggest cricket stadium in Nepal. The capacity of the stadium will have over 30,000 spectators. It is the home ground of Lumbini Lions in Nepal Premier League. Additionally, this stadium is also the home ground of Lumbini Province cricket team and Lumbini Province women's cricket team. Ghorahi Cricket Ground has been hosting under-16 and under-19 provincial-level tournaments. It has also hosted the Dang Cricket League twice and the Sagarmatha T20 League one time. The Ghorahi Cricket Stadium in Ghorahi Sub-Metropolitan City does not have as much infrastructure as the stadium in Lamahi, but Ghorahi has a good ground capable of hosting big national tournaments.[40] Note: The following grounds previously listed here have progressed beyond the proposal stage as of October 2025:
  • Kajini International Cricket Ground (Suryodaya): Under construction, planned capacity 1,000, estimated completion 2025.
  • Sainamaina Cricket Ground (Lord Buddha International Cricket Stadium): Under construction, planned capacity 25,000.[41]
  • 1907 Arena Cricket Stadium (Arjunchaupari): Under construction since July 2025, capacity 10,000, projected opening 2026.
  • Marylebone Biratnagar Jwala Cricket Ground (Biratnagar): Operational since prior to 2025, hosting Nepal T10; capacity ~15,000. Home of Biratnagar Kings in Nepal Premier League.[42]
  • Sanfebagar Cricket Ground (Sanfebagar): No recent developments; original 2013 proposal appears inactive.
These may warrant relocation to other sections (e.g., under construction or regional grounds) in the full article.
