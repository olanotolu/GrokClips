Dr. Elizabeth Olivet

Dr. Elizabeth Olivet is a fictional character on the NBC police procedural and legal drama television series Law & Order and its spin-offs, portrayed by actress Carolyn McCormick. Olivet is a clinical and forensic psychiatrist who frequently consults for the New York City Police Department (NYPD) and the Manhattan District Attorney's office, providing expert testimony on criminal psychology, mental competency, and trauma. She debuted in the second season premiere episode "Confession" (1991), where she counsels Detective Mike Logan following the murder of his partner, Sergeant Max Greevey.[1][2] McCormick appeared as Olivet in 87 episodes of the original Law & Order from 1991 to 2009, making her one of the franchise's most recurring guest characters. Olivet also guest-starred in six episodes of Law & Order: Special Victims Unit from 1999 to 2018, as well as in Law & Order: Trial by Jury and Law & Order: Criminal Intent. She is notable as one of only five characters to appear across all four New York City-based series in the franchise.[2][3]
