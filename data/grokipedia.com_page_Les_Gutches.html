Leslie Lyle Gutches (born February 21, 1973) is an American former wrestler and coach. His accomplishments include becoming a World Champion in freestyle wrestling at the 1997 World Wrestling Championships,[1] the Dan Hodge Trophy as the nation's best college wrestler in 1996, becoming the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) champion in the 177 lb. weight division in both 1995 and 1996, earning All-American status in 1994, 1995 & 1996, becoming a member of the 1996 United States Olympics freestyle wrestling team and winning numerous other tournaments and honors.

Youth

Gutches grew up in a wrestling family in Southern Oregon, with his father Les Gutches Sr., an Oregon high school wrestling coach, having wrestled competitively.[2] He began wrestling at the age of seven and has the unique distinction of winning national championships in all of the wrestling age groups, including three Oregon state titles (1989â€“1991) while a student at South Medford High School.[3][4]

College

Considered the university's best wrestler in modern history with a 134â€“10 record,[5] Gutches had a tremendous collegiate wrestling career as a four-year letterman[5] of the Oregon State Beavers wrestling team, culminating in winning the Dan Hodge Trophy as the best college wrestler in the United States in 1996.[6] He placed 5th at the NCAA championships in 1994 at 177 lb, and went on to win the title in both 1995 (33â€“0) and 1996 (36â€“0), both undefeated seasons.[7][8] This earned him All-American status each of the three years, which is awarded to the top 8 athletes in each weight class every year.[9] He graduated from Oregon State University with a Bachelor's degree in Physical Anthropology and a minor in German Language in 1996. In 2006 he graduated with a Master's degree in Business Administration and was a member of the Beta Gamma Sigma Honor Society. He was also a three-time NCAA First-Team Academic All-American as an undergraduate.[10]

International

Gutches achieved his greatest accomplishment in 1997, when he won the gold medal in freestyle wrestling at the 1997 World Wrestling Championships, defeating Ukrainian Eldar Assanov in the final in Krasnoyarsk, Russia.[1] Gutches was again a World medalist in 1999, when he won the bronze medal in the 85 kg event in Ankara. Gutches won gold medals at the Pan American Wrestling Championships in the 82 kg weight class in 1993 (Guatemala City) and bronze in 1994 (Mexico City).[9] Other gold medal finishes at international competitions included the 1999 FILA Wrestling World Cup (85 kg), the 1999 Pan American Games (85 kg), and the 1998 Goodwill Games (85 kg).[9] Gutches was a member of the U.S. freestyle wrestling team at the 1996 Summer Olympics, where he placed 7th in the 82 kg event.

Coaching

Gutches was a full-time assistant coach at Oregon State from 1996 to 1999, a volunteer coach in 2001, and again a full-time assistant coach in 2002â€“2006, coaching five wrestlers to All-American honors.[11] He was also an assistant coach for the United States National Team at the 2002 Freestyle World Cup, his team winning the title that year.[12]

Personal

In January 2010, it was announced that Gutches had been named the Director of Program Development for USA Wrestling.[13] He was promoted to Associate Executive Director for Programs and Strategy in October 2012.[10] He was later promoted to Chief Operating Officer in 2022.[14] Gutches left USA Wrestling sometime after 2022. Following his time at USA Wrestling, Gutches joined The Chamber of Medford & Jackson County in Medford, Oregon.[15] Previously, he worked as a commercial loan officer at Citizens Bank for four years, and lived in the Corvallis, Oregon region with his wife, the former Jennifer Busen, who played basketball at Oregon State. They lived in Colorado Springs, Colorado, with their daughter Alexis and son Logan. As of 2025, Gutches resides in Medford, Oregon. Gutches is one of the three founders of G3 Sports Performance Center in Corvallis, Oregon.[16]
