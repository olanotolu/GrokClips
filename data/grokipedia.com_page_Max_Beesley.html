Max Beesley

Maxton Gig Beesley Jr. (born 16 April 1971) is an English actor and musician. His television and film credits include The History of Tom Jones, a Foundling (1997), The Match (1999), Hotel (2001), Kill Me Later (2001), The Last Minute (2001), Bodies (2004–2006), The Last Enemy (2008), Survivors (2008–2010), Mad Dogs (2011–2013), Suits (2013), Empire (2015–2016), Ordinary Lies (2015), Jamestown (2017–2019), The Outsider (2020), The Midwich Cuckoos (2022), Operation Fortune (2023), Hijack (2023), and Suspect: The Shooting of Jean Charles de Menezes (2025). In 2024, he appeared as boxing promoter Henry Collins in Guy Ritchie's The Gentlemen (2024).[1]

Early life

Beesley was born on 16 April 1971 in Burnage, Manchester.[2][3] His father, Maxton Beesley Sr, is a professional jazz drummer and actor,[3] and his mother was a jazz singer who performed under the stage name Chris Marlowe.[4] He has a step-brother, Jason Milligan, who is also an actor.[5] His middle name was inspired by the American actor Gig Young.[4] Beesley has studied at the Chetham's School of Music,[6] and at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama.[7] Beesley is an advocate for method acting,[4] being tutored by Sheila Gray, owner of the independent acting studio in Manhattan.[8]

Career

Acting

Beesley came to prominence with his first major acting role in BBC's The History of Tom Jones, a Foundling ,[4] a 1997 television mini-series based on the book of the same name. In 1999, he played the lead role of Wullie Smith, in the Scottish comedy film The Match , working with Isla Blair, Samantha Fox and Richard E Grant.[4] In 2001, he played a bank robber in the film Kill Me Later , alongside Selma Blair.[4] The same year, he starred as Antonio in Mike Figgis' movie Hotel.[4] In 2003, he starred in the film Red Roses and Petrol alongside Malcolm McDowell.[2] In 2004, he starred in the ethics challenging BBC Three dark medical drama Bodies ,[3] which ran until 2006, and won best drama at the Royal Television Society awards.[4] In 2006, he starred as Charlie, the hotel Deputy Manager in the BBC One series Hotel Babylon.[4] In June 2007, Beesley starred in the ITV series Talk to Me.[2] In 2008, he starred in the BBC One thriller The Last Enemy ,[2] with Benedict Cumberbatch and Robert Carlyle. In October 2008, he was one of the lead characters in BBC One's remake of the 1970s drama Survivors ,[2] as remorseless former criminal Tom Price. The series consisted of six episodes, with a second series of six episodes transmitted in January 2010. He starred in the ITV drama The Reckoning in April 2011.[2] Beesley gained more exposure with American audiences with a recurring role on Suits ,[2] in 2013 and from October 2015 with another recurring role, this time in the US spy drama Homeland ,[2] opposite Claire Danes, as ex-British special forces bodyguard Mike Brown. From 2017 to 2019 he starred in Jamestown as Henry Sharrow, set in 1619 and depicting life in the early English colony of Virginia.[2] He was cast as Seale Bolton in The Outsider on HBO in June 2019, which aired from January 2020.[2] In 2021, he starred as Ben Harris in Operation Fortune: Ruse de Guerre , opposite Jason Statham.[2] 2021 also saw him return to television, starring in The Midwich Cuckoos opposite Keeley Hawes for Sky TV.[2] In 2022, Beesley joined the cast of Hijack for Apple TV opposite Idris Elba and Neil Maskell.[2] In 2024, he starred as boxing promoter Henry Collins in Guy Ritchie's The Gentlemen for Netflix, alongside Theo James, Kaya Scodelario, and Ray Winstone.[9][10] In 2025, Beesley starred in the Disney+ miniseries Suspect: The Shooting of Jean Charles de Menezes .[1] Also in 2025, he joined the cast of the untitled Jack Ryan film for Amazon MGM Studios, directed by Andrew Bernstein and starring John Krasinski.[11]

Voice and advertising

Beesley is also a voice over artist and has worked for multiple TV advertisements and documentaries including ITV, Energizer's lithium batteries, and PC World.[12][13] He also provided the voiceover for Manchester United's 2008–2009 season review DVD.[14] He appeared in a TV campaign for the UK recruitment company Jobsite, and was also featured on their homepage.

Music

Beesley is also a session musician, and solo artist, having had a successful career before becoming an actor. He was a chorister at Manchester Cathedral and studied at Chetham's School of Music.[15] He also studied percussion and classical piano at the Guildhall School of Music & Drama with soul singer Omar and toured with the Brand New Heavies on keyboards and percussion. He has also toured or recorded with George Benson, Earth, Wind & Fire, Stevie Wonder, The JB's and Zero 7. He is also a vibraphone player.[16] Beesley has performed in concert as a percussionist and pianist for Robbie Williams, Take That, Jamiroquai, and the Paul Weller Movement.[15] He played percussion and piano at Williams' Knebworth gigs in summer 2003 and at Hyde Park for Live 8 London. Williams' first concert of his Close Encounters Tour in Perth on 30 November 2006 saw Beesley as percussionist for the second half of the show. He also played drums during the performance of "Rudebox" at Williams' second Brisbane concert and during a BBC Electric Proms show. In 1989, at the age of 18, his first ever boss was Paul Weller.[7] In 1993, Beesley was a member of the jazz band Incognito, but in 1994, after an eight-month tour with them, decided to end his music career and start acting.[16] On 28 October 2006, Beesley appeared as a special guest pianist for James Brown during his performance at the Roundhouse in London as part of the BBC's Electric Proms festival.[17] In 2020, he recorded his first solo album titled Groove Spectrum at Capitol Studios in Los Angeles with Grammy-winning producer/mixer Al Schmitt mixing the album. It features Paul Weller, Robbie Williams, Fred Durst, Lisa Stansfield, jazz pianist Christian Sands, Tommy Blaize, Antonique Smith, John Turrell, Omar, and Francci Richards. A multi-instrumentalist, Beesley wrote, played, and scored the album. He recorded his first solo instrumental album Zeus in January 2023 in Los Angeles with luminaries Steve Gadd, Walt Fowler, Christian Sands, Dean Parks, Luis Conte, and producing collaborator Jerry Meehan on bass. The album was released on 29 September 2023 through Légère Records and features the vibraphone, one of the instruments Beesley is mainly associated with. He wrote, arranged, mixed, and produced the album under his "Max Beesley's HIGH VIBES" banner, and has continued to promote the record with live performances, including shows at Ronnie Scott's in London in November 2025. Critics cite the record as being heavily influenced by the music of Herbie Hancock, The Brecker Brothers, and vibraphonist Roy Ayers. In 2023, Beesley announced a new solo album for release in 2025, featuring full orchestrated songs.[18]
