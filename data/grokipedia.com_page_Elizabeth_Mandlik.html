Elizabeth Mandlik

Last updated on: October 20, 2025. Elizabeth Hana Mandlik (born 19 May 2001) is an American tennis player. She is the daughter of Grand Slam champion Hana Mandlíková. Mandlik has career-high rankings by the WTA of 97 in singles and 187 in doubles.[1] She has won nine singles titles and three doubles titles on the ITF Circuit, most recently the W100 at Edmond in October 2025.[2]

Career

2019: WTA Tour debut

Mandlik made her WTA Tour main-draw debut at the 2019 Luxembourg Open in the doubles tournament, partnering with Katie Volynets.

2022: Tour match win & top 125

Ranked No. 240, Mandlik qualified for the main draw and earned her first WTA Tour tournament win at the Silicon Valley Classic, defeating Alison Riske-Amritraj in straight sets.[3] In the second round, she took world No. 4 and second seed, Paula Badosa, to three sets, losing only in a final set tiebreaker.[4][5] As a result, she moved 60 positions into the top 200 in the rankings, at world No. 181. Mandlik won the US Open Wildcard Challenge to enter the women's singles tournament where she made her major main-draw debut, 33 years after her mother last played in the US Open. Mandlíková became the first US Open women's singles champion in the Open era to have a daughter also play the US Open.[6] Mandlik played in the main draw of the US Open, defeating Tamara Zidanšek in three sets, before losing to eventual finalist Ons Jabeur, in straight sets, in the second round.[7]

2023: Australian Open and top 100 debuts

Mandlik qualified for the main draw at the French Open, making her debut but losing in the first round to Simona Waltert. [8] She made her debut at the Australian Open as a lucky loser, but was defeated in the first round by 27th seed Irina-Camelia Begu.[9] She reached the quarterfinals at the Osaka Open as a qualifier, losing to Lin Zhu. At the WTA 125 Reus Open, she reached the final, losing to Sorana Cîrstea. [10] At the Nottingham Open, she qualified for the main draw and defeated Viktoriya Tomova[11] and seventh seed Camila Giorgi to reach the quarterfinals,[12] where she lost to Alize Cornet.[13] As a result she reached the top 100 in the singles rankings on June 19, 2023, at a career-high of No. 97. She became the fifth American to reach this milestone and the ninth female player overall for the season.[14]

2024

Mandlik qualified for the main draws at the Libéma Open in s'Hertogenbosch, losing in the first round to Loïs Boisson, and at the Mérida Open, losing in the first round to Ajla Tomljanović. [10]

2025

Mandlik won her first W100 title at the Edmond Tennis Open, defeating Marina Stakusic in the final on October 13. [2]

Personal life

Mandlik is the daughter of Grand Slam champion Hana Mandlíková[15] and granddaughter of Olympic sprinter Vilém Mandlík, who died on October 6, 2023.[16]
