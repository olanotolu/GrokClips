Cyw

Cyw (Welsh for 'chick', pronounced [kɨʊ]) is a Welsh-language children's television block from S4C, which launched on 23 June 2008. Primarily aimed at children aged 3 to 6, Cyw airs daily in the mornings starting at 6am. It features a mix of original Welsh productions and international shows dubbed into Welsh. Programming is also available 24 hours a day on the S4C app and S4C Clic on-demand service.[1]

Channel Presentation

Channel presentation

Idents and graphics

The channel logo is built of the word Cyw in the S4C Sans font, the C being in red, the y in yellow and the w in blue.[9] Cyw is a non-commercial block, in a different format from its parent, S4C. Programme details are displayed in an apple tree-shaped device, with colors varying according to the Cyw palette. Idents feature characters such as Cyw and Llew, incorporating fun animations and shadows on colored backgrounds to maintain a playful, child-friendly presentation.[9]

Mascots

The channel originally featured six characters, created as part of the channel's global identity, which are used as short animated bumpers into programmes. The network mascots are the small titular female chick Cyw ("Chick"), a lion named Llew ("Lion"),[10] an elephant named Plwmp ("Direct/Blunt" or "Plump"),[11] a small fuchsia-coloured bird named Deryn ("Bird"),[10] a giraffe named Jangl, a dog named Bolgi ("Glutton",[10] and a play on the Welsh word for dog), and a three-legged female dachshund named Triog. Triog was added in 2018 as part of the channel's 10th anniversary animation series. On October 4, 2018, Hoho Entertainment signed a co-production deal with S4C to create a localised version of Cyw's segments, titled Chickpea and Friends, with the Cyw character being renamed as the titular Chickpea.[12] This series' VOD rights were later pre-sold to Canadian streaming site Kidoodle.TV in most countries and Tencent Video in China.[13] Cyw's characters were originally from Planed Plant Bach before it was replaced by Cyw.

Future

In a press release, S4C announced that the plan would be eventually for Cyw to launch as a standalone channel.[14] This was believed likely to occur during the switchover to DVB and DTT in the UK, which took place between 2007 and 2012. However, the launch never took place, and Cyw still remains as a block on S4C to this day. Stwnsh has since replaced Planed Plant, providing one hour of programmes every weekday evening. As of September 2025, S4C's new five-year strategy (2025–2030) emphasizes digital-first content to engage younger audiences, including expansions in children's programming through platforms like S4C Clic.[15]
