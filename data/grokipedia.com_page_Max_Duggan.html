Max Duggan

Maxwell Duggan (born March 12, 2001) is an American professional football quarterback for the Toronto Argonauts of the Canadian Football League (CFL). He played college football for the TCU Horned Frogs, winning several national awards after being part of the team that made it to the 2023 College Football Playoff National Championship game. After spending time on the Los Angeles Chargers' practice squad and roster in 2023 and 2024, he played for the St. Louis Battlehawks in the United Football League (UFL) during the 2025 season before signing with the Argonauts in August 2025. He was selected by the Chargers in the seventh round of the 2023 NFL draft.

Early life

Duggan was born on March 12, 2001, in Council Bluffs, Iowa.[3][4] His two older siblings, Sam and Megan, were both adopted from South Korea.[5] At Lewis Central High School, Duggan played football, baseball, basketball and ran track. On the football field, he was coached by his father and was four-year starter at quarterback.[4] As a senior, he passed for 2,130 yards and 24 touchdowns while adding another 1,223 yards and 25 touchdowns on the ground on his way to being named the 2018 Iowa Gatorade High School Player of the Year.[6] Rated as a 4-star prospect, Duggan was ranked as the top recruit in Iowa for the Class of 2019.[7] Despite scholarship offers from regional programs Nebraska, Iowa and Iowa State, national powers like Georgia, Ohio State and Penn State[7] as well as his favorite team from childhood, Notre Dame,[8] Duggan chose to play his college football at Texas Christian University in Fort Worth, Texas, announcing his commitment to the Horned Frogs via Twitter.[9] He graduated from Lewis Central a semester early in order to enroll at TCU in January 2019.[10]

Duggan's collegiate debut came in the Frogs' 2019 season opener, a 39â€“7 home victory over the Arkansas-Pine Bluff Golden Lions when he came on in relief of starter Alex Delton late in the first quarter, scoring his first career rushing touchdown on his first possession. His first career touchdown pass came in the second half on a 37-yard strike to Jalen Reagor.[11] He made his first career start three weeks later in a game against the SMU Mustangs, becoming just the second true freshman to start at quarterback under longtime TCU coach Gary Patterson.[12] In late October, he scored the game-winning touchdown on an 11-yard run with less than two minutes remaining as TCU upset the 15th-ranked Texas Longhorns, 37â€“27.[13] He ended his first season having thrown for a school freshman record 2,077 yards and 15 touchdowns as the Horned Frogs finished with a 5â€“7 record.[14][15]

Duggan's junior campaign was a frustrating time for the TCU program and him personally. He did throw for a career-best 346 yards and four touchdowns against the 4th-ranked Oklahoma Sooners,[23] but he was hampered by injuries for much of the season as he played through a broken bone and torn tendons in one of his feet.[16] In a late October road loss against the Kansas State Wildcats that dropped the Frogs' record to 3â€“5, Duggan was benched when his injuries severely limited his productivity. In August, the quarterback competition that lasted throughout the spring and summer ended when Dykes and offensive coordinator Garrett Riley informed Duggan that Morris would be the starter. Los Angeles Chargers

Duggan was selected by the Los Angeles Chargers in the seventh round with the 239th overall pick in the 2023 NFL draft.[50] He was waived on August 29, 2023, and re-signed to the practice squad.[51][52] On December 12, Duggan was signed to the active roster following a season-ending injury to starter Justin Herbert.[53] He was waived on December 18, and re-signed to the practice squad.[54] On December 22, Duggan was signed to the active roster.[55] On December 25, Duggan was waived and re-signed to the practice squad two days later.[56][57] He signed a reserve/future contract on January 11, 2024.[58] On August 19, he was released by the Chargers.[59]

St. Louis Battlehawks

On December 19, 2024, Duggan signed with the St. Louis Battlehawks of the United Football League (UFL).[60] Battlehawks coach Anthony Becht had sought a quarterback with NFL practice squad experience after deciding not to retain the rights to incumbent A. J. McCarron.[61] Duggan began the 2025 season as the second string quarterback behind Manny Wilkins until ascending to the starting position when Wilkins suffered a season-ending injury during Week 4.[62] Duggan started five regular season games for the Battlehawks (the lone exception being week 10 where Duggan was rested and backup Brandon Silvers started the game), as well as the 2025 XFL Conference Championship. He was replaced by backup Brandon Silvers in the second half of the championship, but Silvers suffered a season-ending shoulder injury during the game.[63] The Battlehawks lost the game 18â€“36 to the DC Defenders.[64] Duggan was released on August 15, 2025.[65]

Toronto Argonauts

On August 27, 2025, Duggan signed with the Toronto Argonauts of the Canadian Football League (CFL).[66]
