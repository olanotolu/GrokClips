Let's Dance (Slovak TV series)

Let's Dance (in English) is the Slovak version of the British reality TV competition Strictly Come Dancing and is part of the Dancing with the Stars franchise. Ten seasons have been broadcast by TV Markiza (1st in the autumn of 2006), hosted by Adela Vinczeová and Martin "Pyco" Rausch (both of them also hosted the program Slovensko hľadá SuperStar (Slovak version of Pop Idol) on STV in 2005 and 2006). An eleventh season has been announced for late 2025 or 2026. The winners of 1st season were Zuzana Fialová (a noted Slovak actress) and Peter Modrovský (a professional dancer). The winners of 2nd season were Michaela Čobejová (Slovak actress) and Tomáš Surovec (a professional dancer). The winners of 3rd season were Juraj Mokrý (Slovak actor and humorist) and Katarína Štumpfová (a professional dancer). The winners of 4th season were Nela Pocisková (Slovak actress and singer) and Peter Modrovský (a professional dancer). The winners of 5th season were Janka Hospodárová (Slovak TV presenter) and Matej Chren (a professional dancer). The winners of 6th season were Vladimír Kobielsky (Slovak actor) and Dominika Chrapeková (professional dancer). The winners of 7th season were Ján Koleník (Slovak actor) and Vanda Poláková (professional dancer). The winners of 8th season were Jana Kovalčíková (Slovak actress) and Vilém Šír (professional dancer). The winners of 9th season were Jakub Jablonský (Slovak actor) and Eliška Lenčešová (professional dancer). The winners of 10th season were Kristián Baran (Slovak actor) and Dominika Rošková (professional dancer).
